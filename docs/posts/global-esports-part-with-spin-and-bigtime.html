Global Esports part with Spin and BigTime

The 2023 regular season would not play so kindly for Global Esports. A cacophony of problems plagued the team, including visa issues for Indian players Ganesh "SkRossi" Gangadhar and Abhirup "Lightningfast" Choudhury in the opening weeks alongside complications with GE's seven-player roster. The team ultimately ended the season in eighth place with a 3-6 record. GE capped off the year with another first-round knockout, this time to Rex Regum Qeon Rex Regum Qeon Asia-Pacific Rank #15 Monyet Cahya Nugraha xffero David Monangin Lmemore Hagai Kristen Yesyurun Jemkin Maksim Batorov Estrella Park Gun (박건) in the LCQ. Following the loss, Spin was promoted to head coach in place of Eraser, who parted with the team.

Desiring greater results to make up for their lackluster debut season, Global Esports once more revamped their roster for 2024, splitting with five of their previous players while opting to keep only Lightningfast. Spin reunited with former TENSTAR members Russel "Russ" Mendes and Niko "Polvi" Polvinen, bringing alongside him tenured Pacific veterans Gary "blaZek1ng" Dastin and Benedict "Benkai" Tan.

Global Esports entered Kickoff desperate to find success amidst mountains of criticism. Renewed hopes were not enough to find the results desired, as two quick sweeps in the opening and deciding match by ZETA DIVISION ZETA DIVISION Japan Rank #11 Laz Ushida Koji yuran Yushin Hato hiroronn Hiroki Yanai Dep Yuma Hashimoto SugarZ3ro Shota Watanabe knocked GE out of contention for Madrid. Splits 1 and 2 yielded similar results, with GE finding only three wins combined to once again fall short of making an international event.

Just days after their final match of the year against Gen.G Gen.G Korea Rank #1 Munchkin Byeon Sang-beom (변상범) t3xture Kim Na-ra (김나라) Meteor Kim Tae-O (김태오) Lakia Kim Jong-min (김종민) Karon Kim Won-tae (김원태) , Spin announced that he had chosen to resign from Global Esports, with BigTime also leaving the team. Applications for the head coach position have since been publicly opened by the team for the next season.

Global Esports are now down to:

  • Russel "Russ" Mendes
  • Gary "blaZek1ng" Dastin
  • Abhirup "Lightningfast" Choudhury

  • Benedict "Benkai" Tan
