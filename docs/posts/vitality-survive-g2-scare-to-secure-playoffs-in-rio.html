Vitality survive G2 scare to secure playoffs in Rio

Vitality have secured a playoff appearance at IEM Rio after a three-map affair against a Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač-less G2.

Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire and company won 24 maps in a row before the match, a streak that ended on Mirage, but the world's undisputed number-one's series win streak has been extended to 18 games following the victory.

The series started on Mirage, where G2 ran rampant and secured a statement 13-5 victory to break Vitality's 24-map winning streak in dominant fashion, with their win aided by two pistol-round victories and crucial multi-kills by their rifling core.

Inferno was a much closer affair, one where G2 eventually found themselves with two map points on. Despite facing a rag-tag buy, Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García and company couldn't close it out, however, allowing Vitality to secure overtime and eventually win the map 16-13.

G2's didn't let off the gas on the Dust2 decider either, with temporary IGL SunPayus leading their CT side efforts with 12 kills in the half.

The lack of depth in the G2 stratbook showed in the dying moments, however, as a couple of uninspired calls meant they were left empty-handed after an otherwise impressive effort.

One streak ends, one lives on

After a 13-5 loss on Mirage, Vitality have to settle for 2nd in terms of longest-ever map win streaks at Big Events, 10 short of Ninjas in Pyjamas's record.

On the other hand, Vitality have now extended their series win-streak to 18 games in a row, a run that extends all the way back to January 24 at BLAST Bounty.

The loss against Falcons remains Vitality's only defeat in 2026 so far.
