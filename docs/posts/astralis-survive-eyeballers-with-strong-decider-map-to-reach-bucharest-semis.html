Astralis survive EYEBALLERS with strong decider map to reach Bucharest semis

Astralis were far superior on the Inferno decider after mounting a comeback on their Ancient pick to avoid elimination.

Astralis are through to the PGL Bucharest semi-finals after defeating EYEBALLERS 2-1 and continue their undefeated run in Romania after a clean 3-0 Swiss group stage and a hairy quarter-final victory over the Swedes.

Astralis won the first pistol round in the series on Mirage, but it was EYEBALLERS who showed a first glimpse of what was to come when the gun rounds turned around, stringing five in a row to take a 5-2 lead early on.

After taking a 7-5 lead at the half, Jesper "⁠JW⁠" Wecksell downed three in the second pistol round to put EYEBALLERS in a good position to win their map pick. The Swede then had the play of the match with an ace — capped by an AWP no-scope — to keep Astralis at bay in the final stretch.

In the end, EYEBALLERS secured their pick, 13-8, with JW atop the board with a 1.55 rating and +7.09% round swing. The series then moved to Astralis' Ancient.

EYEBALLERS continued to surprise on a map in which the Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen-led squad were the big favorites, but the Swedes were superior on the defense, going up 8-2 before dropping the final two before the switch.

Another pistol win put EYEBALLERS in a good place to run away with it, and a massive clutch by Max "⁠maxster⁠" Jansson in a 1v3 situation put Astralis against the wall as he and his teammates took an 11-4 lead.

Astralis weren't out yet, however, and started to mount a comeback, which included a crucial 2v2 to get within two, 11-9, followed by an anti-eco to make it a one-round game.

Short on ideas, several EYEBALLERS rushes hit walls and quickly overtime was in the cards. Astralis didn't forgive and secured their pick in extra rounds, 16-14.

Inferno was not as exciting as either of the other maps, as Astralis got off to a flying start on the CT side and EYEBALLERS had no answers. Astralis won the second pistol round after a 10-2 half and went on to make quick work of the Swedes to close out the series 13-2.
