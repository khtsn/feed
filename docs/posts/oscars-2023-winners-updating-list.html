March 12, 2023Updated 11:00 p.m. ET

The 95th Academy Awards are happening right now at the Dolby Theater in Los Angeles and are being broadcast on ABC. Jimmy Kimmel is the host.

Early victories went to “Guillermo del Toro’s Pinocchio,” which won for best animated feature; Ke Huy Quan and Jamie Lee Curtis, each first-time winners for supporting roles in “Everything Everywhere All at Once”; “Navalny,” which picked up the best documentary feature statuette; the costume designer Ruth Carter, who became the first Black woman to have won two Oscars (this time for “Black Panther: Wakanda Forever”); and “All Quiet on the Western Front,” which won several awards in the first half of the show, including for best international feature, production design and cinematography.

“Everything Everywhere All at Once,” the proudly weird sci-fi movie from the filmmaking duo of Daniel Kwan and Daniel Scheinert, went into the ceremony leading with the most nominations (11 total, including for best picture), followed by Martin McDonagh’s “The Banshees of Inisherin” and Edward Berger’s “All Quiet on the Western Front,” each of which had nine. The best picture race includes those three movies along with “Avatar: The Way of Water,” “Elvis,” “The Fabelmans,” “Tár,” “Top Gun: Maverick,” “Triangle of Sadness” and “Women Talking.”

Follow below for a full list of winners, which will be updated as announcements are made.
