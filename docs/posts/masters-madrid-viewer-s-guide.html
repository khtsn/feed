Masters Madrid viewer’s guide

The first Masters of 2024 begins on Thursday, March 14. It will offer eight teams from around the world an opportunity to advance in Champions qualification, as well as a share of the $500,000 USD prize pool.

Of the eight participants, three organizations will have a chance at joining FNATIC in an elite club of repeat international event winners. The initial portion of the tournament will be played with an eight team Swiss-style format. In this system, teams play against other teams with the same win/loss record. All matches will be best-of-three. After three rounds, the top four teams, i.e. teams with two victories, will move on to playoffs.

The winner of each region's Kickoff event was awarded a top seed at Madrid. They were drawn against one of the four runners-up, ensuring that all opening matches are cross-regional. The initial matchups are as follows:

Once a team has earned their second victory of the Swiss stage, they will qualify to playoffs. This stage of the event will feature a standard four-team double-elimination bracket. The teams that qualified without losing a series in the Swiss stage will be drawn against the two teams that had a 2-1 record.
