stressarN on making the Major: "I think I've never been happier in my life than that day"

SINNERS had an unusual road to the Major after sitting out the last few days of the VRS frenzy, which meant their fate was out of their hands as the invite date loomed.

The international squad finished in the invite places at the cut-off, however, meaning all five of their members are set to make their Major debuts on Tuesday.

"I think our progress on SINNERS, like players, was incredible," Jordan "⁠stressarN⁠" Manevski told HLTV at the IEM Cologne media day, but insisted the team won't be taking their foot off the gas after securing a Major appearance.

Listen to the interview here.

Congrats on making the Major. You're here in Cologne with SINNERS, so how are you feeling coming into such a tournament?

I mean, it's a pleasure to play in Cologne, of course. It's our first Major for three of our players. I mean, for five, but for three of us it's like the first time experiencing this kind of thing, like RMR before. It's a pleasure to come here, and we will show our potential.

And talking about that potential, you guys had a pretty good year. You won a few lower-tier LANs and also went to Atlanta at the end. I wanted to ask about those lower tier LANs. Do you feel like you reached the top of tier-two during this season?

Yeah. We showed it on these lower-tier LANs, that we are capable of beating very good teams, like tier-one teams. We showed it, especially at BC.Game Masters, the one that we won, we beat G2 and several other good teams. We are just improving and we are showing the fans who we are, and they're going to soon see who we actually are.

Atlanta as well was kind of your first tier one LAN, for most of you. How important is it to get one kind of tournament like that before the Major so you see how things work?

Of course, it's a pleasure as well to play a first tier-one event. It was a great experience, I must say, because we came there to do some preparation before the Major. We didn't prepare all our stuff, we were just preparing for EM Atlanta. It was a short preparation, but it was a really, really great experience. And I think we're gonna be showing most of that [preparation] at the Major.

To ask about your career, right, I remember a few years back I saw you playing the A1 Adria League, and that wasn't that far ago, right? How would you describe these last few years and the rise that you were on personally?

I mean, it's like a rollercoaster. I played very, very low tier tournaments. Then you're achieving your dream after so many years. I think hard work pays off, and I think our future is very bright, for everyone. We're gonna do very, very big things. I think every fan of SINNERS and me is gonna be very proud.

We're gonna do very, very big things. I think every fan of SINNERS and me is gonna be very proud

To also ask about your career in SINNERS. It's been one year, and it felt like a gradual progression. You entered your first international team, then you played some LANs, then you played your first tier-one LAN, and now you're at the Major. Did you expect it to go this well this quickly?

I mean, since signing for SINNERS, I was dreaming to get to the Major, of course. Me and kisserek were talking a lot, like, 'Hey, bro, let's go. Let's make beastik and SHOCK go to the Major, because they failed so many times when they were close.' It was a very great, great destination. And I think our progress on SINNERS, like players, was incredible. But we're not gonna go too far. Like 'We did, let's go. It's the Major, now everything is finished.' Now we're gonna just keep working.

How were those last few days before you qualified? Because, as you mentioned, the two Czechs went through a lot of heartbreak in their career so far. How was it watching from home? Because everyone else was playing and you guys were just chilling at home.

The last day I was watching with my friends outside. I was outside at dinner, and I'll never forget this moment. It was just pure happiness, I think I was never happier in my life than that day. I have many reactions, many clips of my reactions. I was just so happy and I started to cry that we even qualified for the Major. But shoutout to BIG because they saved us, and I think it was a really, really great experience.

Since signing for SINNERS, I was dreaming to get to the Major

You're playing FlyQuest right in your first game, which is an Australian team. Something you probably haven't faced a lot in your career. How are you feeling coming into that and what are the expectations for the entire team here?

I mean, every match is gonna be the same, not only FlyQuest, I feel like. Every match is important. They are great opponents and I think they will be ready as well. I think every match will be very hard and we need to expect the unexpected, of course. It's best-of-ones, but I think we're gonna get there and qualify for Stage 2.
