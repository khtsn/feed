Their recent form has seen Astralis climb to joint-third in the global VRS, but HooXi is refusing to get carried away with that fact. "I don't know who made this ranking but it's obviously like a full comedian or something, because we are obviously not the third-best team in the world."

With a grand final matchup on the horizon, HooXi sat down with HLTV for a chat about his team's run in Romania, their progression in recent times, and his expectations for the fast-approaching Major in Cologne.

You can listen to the full interview here.

Give me your thoughts on that series today?

We had a first map with a lot of technical issues that could have broken our momentum, but didn't on the CT side. That was probably also very frustrating for them, considering that it's way harder to set up a round on T when you don't have TeamSpeak than it is to just hold your spot on CT (laughs). That wasn't great obviously. Then I think we fell a bit into a trap of maybe forcing a bit too much on the CT side of Dust2.

I think they got a bit lucky on their CT side of Overpass, so they got maybe a few more rounds than they should. I think on CT side it was very comfortable, I think there were only just a few miscommunications or small timings that they got over the line a few times in some rounds, but in the end we pulled through so it was good.

Overall how do you rate your team's performance in this tournament so far?

I think in some games it has been very good, and in other games it has been the EYEBALLERS game (laughs). I think we cannot be proud of games like yesterday, we threw away a bunch of situations. We are struggling with some nerves maybe, it's the first time we make it this far and we probably also feel a bit like favorites for once, which is a new feeling that people have to adjust to.

For me it's not really about the result or making it far, it's about doing better than last tournament and not making the same mistakes

I've tried it before and jabbi has as well, but for the other guys it's not something they have a lot of experience with, and I think that is hurting us. We have to get through the motions and I think it's great that we get the opportunity before the Major to try this. Overall I'm super happy.

Did you talk about that before the tournament at all? The fact you were coming in as favorites or at least as one of the highest-ranked teams.

No not really. We probably should have, 20/20 hindsight, but I think that at least for me it's not really about the result or making it far, it's about doing better than last tournament and not making the same mistakes we have learned from already. I don't really care if we are favorites or huge underdogs or whatever, but I can see how it has an affect on people who haven't been through the same ride as me.

It looks from the outside at the very least that you are progressing and getting better with every tournament, do you feel that as well within the team?

Yeah definitely. I think that's also been the goal from the start, that we will not be perfect from the start and it's totally fine to make mistakes, as long as we learn from them. I think the boys are grinding hard and that's the most important piece of the recipe, that we are willing to work hard and have the same goal. I think that is paying off so far.

I don't really care if we are favorites or huge underdogs or whatever, but I can see how it has an affect on people who haven't been through the same ride as me

There's a little bit of chat because in the most recent VRS update, or in the live rankings, Astralis are up to third. How do you feel about that?

(laughs) I don't know who made this ranking but it's obviously like a full comedian or something, because we are obviously not the third-best team in the world right now. It's cool to be there but I also think we will get humbled when IEM Rio comes along (laughs). So I don't know, it's a funny thing but not something we talk about at all.

Looking forwards you obviously have a couple more tournaments beforehand, but the Major is coming up and it feels like the team is ramping up. Do you have an idea of what you want to achieve at the Major with this team?

No. I think it will be a lot like a learning experience, like this tournament. For ryu it's his first Major and that's always special, and some emotions you have to go through. I think that just in general as a team, the first six months I don't like to have result-based expectations. I think we will go and enjoy the Major and hopefully we will get some good games in along the way, and we see how far we make it. One day at a time.

So is it more a case of, after that Major, you will maybe sit down and look towards the next season and that's when you want to peak with this team?

Yeah. I mean I would obviously love to peak at the Major, but I think that right now we just… as I said, results are not really something I talk too much about. At some point we will have to set expectations for ourselves, but right now the expectations are more within the team, what is our base level. It doesn't really matter if we lose against a better team, as long as we don't lose to ourselves basically.
