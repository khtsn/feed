zonic on trophy pressure: "It would be nice for the guys to get that out of the way"

Falcons have made their second consecutive grand final since signing Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen in place of Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski, and they are set to come into the best-of-five match as massive favorites for the trophy.

"Both teams cannot be underestimated," Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen said about Falcons' potential opponents, MIBR and Legacy. "I think we have, as you say, learned that the hard way. I don't care who we meet. I just want Falcons to play good CS."

The Saudi organization has not won an event for over a year, and zonic admits the team would lift a weight off their shoulders with a win on Sunday.

"Of course it would help the team if we could get that trophy and get that out of the way because of not only the pressure from the community, but also with the team that we have and the history," he told HLTV after the semi-final.

Congratulations on the win, Danny. It's your second straight grand final after bringing in karrigan. Give me your thoughts on the series, first of all.

Obviously, it was a rough start, I would say. Also, previously we have had struggles in those games. We saw a bit of the old patterns, I would say, on the first map, but I think we really bounced back on the second and third maps, which is good to see. I'm really pleased to be in a second grand final in a row. And, yeah, looking forward to tomorrow.

It was getting a bit rough towards the end of Nuke, but then you pulled away with that force buy and a sneaky play from NiKo. It’s a bit of that karrigan bullshit effect, right?

 Yeah. I mean, of course I would have loved it if we could just have ended it quickly on Nuke. The amount of practice that we have had with karrigan is not that much. At the end of the day, we are in a position where, especially around playoff games, a win is a win. We don't really look at the beauty part of it. So, yeah, again, I'm really happy. We also know that MOUZ is, in general, really strong on the CT side across their map pool. So in MR12 it doesn't take long for the opponents to get momentum.

You began the series on the wrong foot. It was your third straight loss on Mirage. Is there a cause for concern? Is the approach to the map just not working out right now?

No, I think…  Of course, we have had to focus on some areas. Yes, Mirage has been going down a little bit. On the CT side, we have two new guys in a sense, karrigan on B, who has to connect with Maksim [kyousuke], and then also René [TeSeS] playing on A. Of course, it's something that takes time, and we had to prioritize where we wanted to put our focus. And one of the main focuses was on Dust2, which I think we have succeeded in, in some way. But, of course, at the end of the day, yes, mistakes will happen. This has been kind of the mantra we have been going through with the guys throughout these tournaments, that we are a new team. We have changed basically everything. And, yeah, it will take some time.

The last time you talked to us, it was still very early into karrigan's stint. Now that more time has passed, how would you say the team is adjusting to his system and how is he getting on with the players around him?

It's getting better day by day, but maybe we are making a bit more mistakes, but that has also to do with us being on the road for q uite some time. Yes, we got karrigan in and we changed the system, but we're also at the end of the season, and we have a Major coming up, so the boys are getting a bit fatigued, I would say, a bit tired now. That might be visible in some games where we could have played better.

But when it comes to the system and the way karrigan is running it, we're getting so much data, crucial data, that we can take with us, not only for the Major, but also for the season to come. So I'm really pleased to see, as I also said in Astana, the way karrigan is approaching the team, but also the respect players are giving him.

To start a series with a map where you didn't have much of a chance is never easy, especially when there is so much pressure around the team. What was the atmosphere like when you stepped outside? How did you manage to keep everyone composed and focused?

I think the approach, from my side at least, when it comes to these playoff games is something we talk about, of course. It's a bit of a mixed message from me to some of the players. Some of the players need to take a step back, not keep peeking, focus a bit more on discipline. But there are also some areas where some players need to relax a bit, just play the game and go for more duels and not think too much about, you know, push a smoke if they feel it, if they really trust their intuition.

And this is something where there's no clear message to the entire team where everyone is, like, afraid to take duels. It's just that some players are really eager to take them. And some players are a bit too scared. We have talked a lot about finding the balance in these games. And today they got the message, I would say, apart from the first map.

Most people would say that MOUZ was the hardest team you could face in this bracket. How are you going to approach this final to make sure you keep your feet on the ground and do not take this lightly?

I think there are no questions about the hunger of this team to lift the trophy. It doesn't matter who we play. It's up to us. I think, yes, when you look at the paper and all of this, yes, we are a new team. Didn't expect us to be in the grand finals of both [events], I would say. But yeah, we will go in focusing on ourselves for tomorrow, because I think this is the biggest obstacle, to be fair. This is the mindset that we have to go in with, that we dare to play, but that we also focus on the team in the difficult moments.

Looking ahead, it's Legacy or MIBR. You beat Legacy yesterday, while MIBR was the team that eliminated you from the Austin Major. Not a good match for you…

No comment. [laughs]

Both teams cannot be underestimated. I don't care who we meet. I just want Falcons to play good CS

Do you have any preferences?

No, not really. I know insani has been playing really well and MIBR is looking strong. Both teams cannot be underestimated. I think we have, as you say, learned that the hard way. I don't care who we meet. I just want Falcons to play good CS.

Getting a title here would be a massive boost for the team before the Major. This is something the team has been chasing since Bucharest 2025. The biggest question is: how will the team handle the pressure?

I've said a lot of times that the big hurdle is getting that trophy. Ilya [m0NESY] has been in many grand finals. We have been in many grand finals without succeeding. To be honest, I'm just focusing on one game at a time. If we don't lift the trophy tomorrow, yes, we will be disappointed, but we are going in the right direction. But yes, of course it would help the team if we could get that trophy and get that out of the way because of not only the pressure from the community — you know, being part of Falcons is a big thing —, but also with the team that we have and the history. Of course there's pressure, and it would be nice for the guys at least to get that type of pressure out of the way.
