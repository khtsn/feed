faveN: "If BIG would give me a 10-year contract I would probably sign it, because I'm really happy"

Josef "⁠faveN⁠" Baumann and BIG are on cloud nine on home soil at the IEM Cologne Major, after carrying the momentum from their historic 0-12 comeback at the end of Stage 1 into wins over paiN and MIBR in Stage 2.

The 26-year-old first joined BIG at the end of 2021, before being benched at the end of 2023 and going on to spend time with BLEED and 9INE.

This January, faveN got the call he had been waiting for, and the chance to re-join the German organization.

"Joining BIG was my biggest priority, because I know the org," he says. "I know what they changed inside the org, which I didn't like back then — and I also didn't do good stuff back then, just to be honest, not everything is BIG's fault — but then they changed, I changed, and I got two kids. I have less stress because it's a German organization, and it makes my life way easier."

It helps that the international lineup gelled instantly. Even playing lower-tier competition, the atmosphere was excellent. faveN has formed a bromance with Benjamin "⁠blameF⁠" Bremer, while head coach Sebastian "⁠xenn⁠" Hoch makes time for teambuilding activities during bootcamps.

Put together, faveN is happy. Really happy. "If they would give me a 10-year contract, I would probably sign it."

Listen to the interview here.

Okay, 2-1 now in Swiss. How does it feel after that?

It feels really good, especially because we were not the favorites to go 2-1. I think many people also put us 0-3 in pick 'ems. So, it feels really good to also show people that we can actually play proper CS against better teams, because some guys also said we didn't play good teams on the road here to Cologne, which is true, of course, I don't say it's not true. But beating paiN and MIBR, who are good teams, I think now we are starting to show what we can do.

This is the best team atmosphere I've ever been in. I think blameF and I, we just connect really well.

Has it been hard, even within the team, to know what your level is, considering the tournaments you were playing? Has it been difficult to work out how good you actually are?

Yeah, that's for sure. I mean, we always practiced against better teams, but it's practice. Our opponents practice maybe some other stuff than they would do in an official, so we can really never know how good we actually are. So it helps us, at this Major for example, to see where our biggest mistakes are, because these teams punish mistakes which t lower-tier teams don't do. So it's really helpful for us as well to play this Major.

It seems the team has a really good atmosphere. You guys really click as a kind of unit. Can you tell me a bit about what it's like compared to other teams, maybe?

Yeah, this is the team, I think, where this is the best atmosphere I've ever been in. I think blameF and I, I don't know how to say it, but we just connect really well. In 9INE I also had a really good atmosphere, but this team even beats it, I think, because we're all kind of the same in the head. We also think the same, mostly.

And of course, in every team, there will be maybe some bad moments. We also hate it when we lose and so on. But after a 30-minute talk, we fixed it and we are like brothers again, I would say.

blameF has helped faveN thrive in BIG

Yeah, and how nice is that to have? Because we know in international teams there can sometimes be the clash of culture and everything. I don't know who built the team but it's not normal to have this good an atmosphere, maybe.

Yeah, I think Basti, our coach xenn, is doing an insane job as well when we have bootcamps. He [makes us] do stuff together and so on. We also, when we first made this team, we played a lot of FACEIT together, just to get to know each other.

And we did some good team-building stuff. Even in the last bootcamp, like the Major bootcamp, we went for team runs together. I think I've never run in my life, to be honest, [laughs] but I did it three times in the bootcamp and it felt really good. Even though, let's say blameF is running a lot, and I'm not running a lot, he's just waiting for me, he's not pressuring me. And I think this is what helps a lot to know, 'okay, they don't judge me,' and this is how we feel in-game. If we do a mistake, we are not judging each other. We say, fuck it, we will talk it after the game. If it's really bad, we will fix it, if not, we can talk about it and so on.

In-game, no one is getting screamed at and tilted, because in my past teams, there were people who would be really full-tilt and scream at each other, but it's not like this here.

Today, it started with a 4k pistol round, where, I don't know, just sometimes my mouse just... does headshots.

And tell me a bit about your journey to this point, because when you left BIG the first time, I mean, this is a bit of a reversal in fortune, almost. Can you tell me a bit about what it was like before coming back? Did you always believe you would be back at Majors?

To be fair, after I left BIG, I still had this glimpse of hope, I would say, to be in Majors, but I knew I would not join a better team than BIG, to be honest. Joining BIG was my biggest priority as well, because I know the org, I know what they changed inside the org which I didn't like back then — and I also didn't do good stuff back then, just to be honest, not everything is BIG's fault — but then they changed, I changed, and I got two kids, that's a German organization. I have less stress because it's a German organization, and it makes my life way easier. I can actually only focus on CS.

Before that, in 9INE, for example, I needed to think about different stuff outside of the game, because it was not a German organization who can help me, because it's just not possible, because they're from Sweden, you know? So, BIG is, to be honest, if they would give me a 10-year contract, I would probably sign it, because I'm really happy, like, actually really happy.

In-game as well, I mean, you've had some really, really good moments, especially those kind of clutches and everything. Today as well, what do you think is behind your kind of increase in form, because you're not in the roles where that's easy all the time?

Yeah, like you said, I have some rougher roles, I would say, but in the end, I just do everything the team wants me [to], because I think we can win. Today, it started with a 4k pistol round, where, I don't know, just sometimes my mouse just... does headshots. I don't know myself why, but yeah, after the pistol round, I knew, okay, this will be a good game, and then I did another 3k on A, I think, which was important, and yeah, it just keeps rolling, and yeah.
