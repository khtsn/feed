FNATIC are the definite Champions favorites, but can NAVI join them?

The latter went from a title favorite to not even a title contender in four months.

Going into Champions 2023, there are two big questions for EMEA: First, can FNATIC keep up their performance, get the three-peat and win Champions? And second, can NAVI bounce back from their underwhelming recent performances?

It's no secret FNATIC are the isolated favorites to win Champions this year. The team has only faltered once this year, when they fell short of the VCT EMEA 2023 title. But, in return, they swept LOCK//IN and mopped the floor at Masters Tokyo.

The team has an absurd 44-8 map record and 20-1 match record this year. That one blemish is the VCT EMEA grand final they lost, but not even that has stopped FNATIC from topping every tournament's statistics sheet and claiming both of the year's MVPs. They were also the only team in the world to finish in the top four of every VCT event this year.

Three-peat time, FNATIC? (Photo by Liu YiCun/Riot Games)

NAVI sit nearly on the other end of the spectrum. While the team was considered one of the best teams in the world after their stellar 2022 years, even reaching the top four at LOCK//IN, their form quickly shrunk.

The ANGE1 and d00mbr0s -led squad barely reached Masters Tokyo, claiming the fourth seed. But they quickly returned home, with just one map win to their name in the two matches NAVI played.

In other hands, over a 12-month period, NAVI went from a team on the verge of being a title favorite to not even being a title contender. They sit far down in the community power rankings, and are the least-picked team to reach playoffs from Group D. But there's at least one positive to that.

“We are underdogs in [Champions]. I feel it makes us stronger, to play under pressure,” d00mbr0s said. “I think public opinion [of us] has changed and I think it's in our favor. We can use that for extra energy. I'm not scared of any team.”

d00mbr0s is convinced NAVI can win Champions. Others, maybe not so much. (Photo by: Hara Amorós/Riot Games/LVP)
