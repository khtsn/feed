As EMEA's last stand, BBL's elimination makes the region the first to be completely eliminated from play in Santiago, while G2 continues to push on as one of two remaining Americas teams.

The series was played out on BBL's pick of Haven and G2's pick of Breeze. G2 won Haven 13-5, and then went on to win Breeze 13-8.

BBL's pick of Haven sparked interest, as the map has long been a strong one in G2's map pool, with the team's record in 2026 being 4-0. In wanting to prove its own Haven against G2's, BBL went with its usual double-Duelist comp of Neon and Yoru, with Cypher, Sova, and Omen. G2, meanwhile, continued with its reinvented Phoenix-centric comp, with Yoru, Chamber, and Fade while IGL valyn was on Astra.

From the get-go, picking Haven against G2 looked to be a mistake. G2 had no issues playing around the map, exploiting a weak A site that often relied on Rosé . 10 straight G2 rounds gave the North American team a near-perfect finish on a supreme attack side, which was only interrupted by BBL earning one consolation round.
