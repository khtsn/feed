saadzin: "We never made [Major] playoffs, so we want it a lot"

Legacy came into Stage 2 as one of the favorites, but their road to the top-16 was far from straightforward.

The Brazilians needed five games to secure their spot, doing so with a dominant win against TYLOO in the 2-2 pool.

"To be honest, we got a little sad because of Jee. He is a very good friend of ours," Guilherme "⁠saadzin⁠" Pacheco told HLTV after the victory, and added that he hopes the confidence from this win will power the team through Stage 3 into the playoffs.

Listen to the interview here.

Congrats, you made it through after quite a straightforward win against TYLOO today. How do you feel after getting the first part of the Major done?

I'm feeling very happy, me and the guys. This win is very important for us, especially for me, because I feel that I had a bad start on the first day, in the best-of-ones, and also on the second day against Monte. So I think against M80 yesterday and today, playing well like that, it's very good for us to go to Stage 3 with that confidence. Everyone knows that Legacy loves to play aggressive games, we have to be confident to make things work.

Will the fact you won this quite easy help with that confidence? You previously had some very close games, but this one was kind of more comfortable. Will that help you when you go up against better teams in Stage 3?

I think that's a [bit of] overthinking. We have to think about this to play well. I think the first games we played were not bad, we played okay, but I think it was more about me. I remember that I played very badly in crucial rounds, so I think the most important thing now for Stage 3 is that I go with this mentality and this confidence.

The most important thing now for Stage 3 is that I go with this mentality and this confidence

Because, like everyone knows, AWP is the meta of the game, so if you are AWP and you are not getting too much impact, it will be a hard game. I think it will be very important to me and the guys, we are very happy.

You guys became kind of fan favorites in China during the recent tournaments, right? So how does it feel to eliminate their team? Do you have some sour feelings about that?

To be honest, we got a little sad because of Jee. He is a very good friend of ours, every time we see him, he's always smiling to us. We like him a lot. That side is a little sad, but it is what it is. This is the competition.

You also got an extra spot for the next Major for the Americas region with your win. Is it nice to help your region for the next Major? And yourself too, because now you have more spots.

It's very important, like you said. Many of the spots are for Europe, so we have to grind in America. We saw paiN today losing, so it was a little sad for us, but we also have to get this new spot. We wanted to go to the playoffs, last year and this year, we were just talking about, 'Guys, come on, let's reach the playoffs, let's reach the playoffs.' It will be a good chance for us this time.

You mentioned playoffs. You now have Stage 3, where all of the good teams will be. With your recent form, and your trophy win recently,you have proven that you belong among these teams. With Stage 2 now behind you, are you warmed up and ready to play these teams, and do you think that playoffs are what the goal is for you guys?

For us, the goal is for sure the playoffs. We never made it, so we want it a lot. It will be like [against] the best in the game, so it will be very good. We always talk about enjoying the game. There will be hard games, but I think the most important thing is to get happiness in playing. I hope that we play a good game, and I hope that I play well in Stage 3 too.
