Vitality outlast valiant G2 to make BLAST semis

Vitality have booked a semi-final berth at BLAST Rivals Season 1 by virtue of a 2-1 win over G2, picking up wins on Mirage (16-13) and Dust2 (13-3) either side of a loss on Overpass.

G2 will have to settle for starting their playoff campaign in the quarter-finals despite pushing Vitality hard on the first two maps, Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García in particular shining bright. Ultimately they were outlasted by Vitality, who sealed the deal on their best map.

SunPayus clutches up to prevent disaster

Vitality kicked off the series in imperious form on Mirage, dominating the CT side with ease as G2 struggled to get going. Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan in particular was in excellent form as Vitality sought to cast off their strugglesome performance against FUT, earning a commanding 10-2 lead.

A pistol win seemed to set Vitality on their way to an easy victory, but SunPayus had different ideas. The Spaniard was in superb form on the AWP, helping drag his team into the match as Vitality's offense stuttered and failed to gain any traction, sending the map into overtime.

MATYS and HeavyGod win unlikely 2v4

Vitality righted the ship in overtime, as they often do, Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire energy helping the team come out swinging for a clean T-side sweep, which they duly converted upon when the sides switched.

It was G2 who started Overpass off strongly, their CT side winning out an early force-buy war and punishing Vitality's struggling economy. Vitality's stars would not stay silent, however, finding miraculous plays in mid-rounds time and time again despite SunPayus' many openings, leading to a tied half.

ZywOo melts four with the Dualies

Despite the close score G2 were good value for their win in the second half, punishing Vitality's often passive approach to seal the map victory, helped along the way by an impressive 2v4 by Matúš "⁠MATYS⁠" Šimko and Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko.

After going toe to toe with Vitality across the first two maps, G2 simply couldn't keep up on Dust2. Vitality dominated from the first round and never looked back, sealing the series in comfortable fashion to earn a semi-final berth.
