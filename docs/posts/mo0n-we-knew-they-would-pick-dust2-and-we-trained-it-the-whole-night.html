mo0N: "We knew they would pick Dust2, and we trained it the whole night"

magic's miracle run at PGL Astana continued in the arena with a comeback victory on the Ancient decider against 9z in the quarter-finals, earning Abdurakhim "⁠mo0N⁠" Issa another match in front of his home crowd.

"This feeling is amazing," the Kazakh sniper told HLTV after the match. "I'm playing in my [home] arena for the first time in my first event. And... we are in semi-finals, I don't know (smiles). I'm just really happy; I wanted to win this game because I wanted to play two more matches in this arena, and that's it."

The young squad was the third-lowest-ranked team on the VRS coming into the tournament, which they had qualified for after recovering from 0-2 down in the best-of-five Europe qualifier final against K27, and are ensuring that opportunity doesn't go to waste.

"When we came here, the first games when we were 0-2, sFade8 and my coach, eksiver, they weren't sleeping," mo0N said. "They [were] just always training for the opponents, and we always have a game plan for these games. That's why I think we [made] playoffs.

"And also, yesterday they slept 10 hours because we had a one-day holiday, and today, they again sleep 30 minutes or one hour. And they did a very good game plan and we won this game because of them, only because of them."

Listen to the interview here.

Congratulations! A really big win in front of your home crowd, so what is going through your mind right now after that crazy series?

I don't know, this feeling is amazing. I can say it just in words like this. It's amazing that I'm playing in my [home] arena for the first time in my first event. And... we are in semi-finals, I don't know (smiles). I'm just really happy; I wanted to win this game because I wanted to play two more matches in this arena, and that's it.

You came into this game as the underdog, and you've been the underdogs this whole event. But walking on to the stage as underdogs, what was that like?

Yeah, every game we were like underdogs, from VRS points, and we're just always training a lot and winning games. I don't know how (laughs), but it's working. Now, I don't know, we were 0-2 and we just [qualified] for the playoffs 3-2, and after we didn't have any pressure from the arena. We're just playing our game, and that's it.

I've been playing LANs from when I was 12 years old, and I was winning money from here and living from this money

Usually when you have a team that is inexperienced like yours, that hasn't been on stage, they say they won't feel the pressure but they usually do. It seemed like you actually didn't, so tell me about that, what did you do to avoid feeling that?

Actually, if we say about me, I'm playing LAN tournaments in my home, in Almaty. I've been playing LANs from when I was 12 years old, and I was winning money from here and living from this money.

That's why I think I don't have pressure, and a lot of people who are from Europe, they're not playing a lot of LANs. They're just sitting at home and playing online games, but in my situation, I can't play online games because I have 100 ping. I'm just playing LAN tournaments with my friends, we are winning, and that's it. It's helping a lot for me.

I really want to play versus molodoy because we are from Kazakhstan

Talk to me about the series, it started on Dust2, their pick, but you blew them out. How did that happen and how were you so prepared?

We just knew that they would pick Dust2 because the last two games on Dust2, we lost 13-4 and 13-7. We had no chance. We knew they would pick Dust2, and we trained Dust2 the whole night.

After, we didn't have time for training Mirage, to [add] something, any ideas, and they just anti-stratted us. We're always going [into] stacks, and it was really hard for us. They just played good on Mirage, I can say that, they anti-stratted us very good. On Ancient, I think it was like a 50/50 map. It's our good map, and their good map also. We just came back on the T side, I don't know how, but we won this game and I'm really happy right now.

Before the game, the analyst desk was talking about sFade8 getting only 30 minutes of sleep last night, just talk to me about that and how crazy that is, winning the series after that.

When we came here, the first games when we were 0-2, sFade8 and my coach, eksiver, they weren't sleeping. They [were] just always training for the opponents, and we always have a game plan for these games. That's why I think we [made] playoffs.

And also, yesterday they slept 10 hours because we had a one-day holiday, and today, they again sleep 30 minutes or one hour. And they did a very good game plan and we won this game because of them, only because of them. I like them, I fucking love my team.

You're into the semi-finals. Tomorrow it'll be a tough game against the winner of FURIA vs. Falcons; a potential match against molodoy as well, so a bit of Kazakh on Kazakh action. How do you feel going into that game?

I really want to play versus molodoy because we are from Kazakhstan and I like to see... if he [beats] us, I don't care, I'd like to see him in the finals at home. And if we win, I'll also be happy because I can play in the finals at my home. That's it. I can just say 'Alga molodoy' right now.
