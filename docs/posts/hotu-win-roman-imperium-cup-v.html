HOTU win Roman Imperium Cup V

n0rb3r7 and company are on the precipice of the top 20 in VRS after the win over Gentle Mates.

HOTU have made it to 21st on the global Valve Standings after taking down Gentle Mates for the $5,929 first prize at Roman Imperium Cup V. BetBoom also claimed third over fnatic to bring the curtain down on the last day of play.

It was a statement event for 22-year-old rifler Dmitry "⁠mizu⁠" Kondratev, who posted a 1.42 rating in Portugal with six of his nine maps above a 1.58 rating.

The Russophone champions HOTU, counting David "⁠n0rb3r7⁠" Danielyan and coach Rustem "⁠mou⁠" Telepov among their members, are set to test themselves against top opposition at IEM Rio in April and still have a chance at qualifying for the Cologne Major.

HOTU won the final despite a 13-0 loss on map two to Gentle Mates, who had taken down Passion UA on Santino "⁠try⁠" Rigal's debut and then BetBoom to make the final.

It was n0rb3r7 who stepped up on the decider, after the Rio Major champion posted a 2.01 rating on his signature map of Overpass to take HOTU over the line.

The event was also notable for the debut of 100 Thieves, and their knocking out of pre-event favorites Ninjas in Pyjamas in the group stage. According to VRS expert Finn "Mischief" Farrer, that win netted the new team 118 points, but they will need three more LAN wins before they will have an official rank.
