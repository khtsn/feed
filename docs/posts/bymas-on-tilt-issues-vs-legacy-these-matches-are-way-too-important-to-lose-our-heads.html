Bymas on tilt issues vs. Legacy: "These matches are way too important to lose our heads"

Monte came in hot to the Cologne Major, beating BIG in their opener before taking G2 to triple overtime.

They were then matched against CAC winners Legacy and beat the Brazilians with a 13-11 scoreline to find themselves in the 2-1 pool after day two.

After the game against Legacy, Aurimas "⁠Bymas⁠" Pipiras told HLTV that he and his team are proud of their performances in Cologne and that they prove that they can "fight the best teams."

Listen to the interview here.

2-1 after day two, which is a pretty good result considering who you played. How do you feel after getting this best-of-one over the line against Legacy today?

A rollercoaster of emotions, we almost lost to ourselves. First of all, we won the pistol and boom, technical issues, which slowed down our momentum after we won the pistol. It sucks to have this long pause. We had some tilt issues in this match, which we're going to have to solve because during the Major, we cannot lose our heads.

These matches are way too important to lose our heads. And yeah, I feel like the game could have been even way better for us if we actually played our A game and we didn't tilt, but overall, 13-11 against a team like Legacy is awesome.

Do you think those tilt issues stemmed from the game yesterday where, against another great team, it got really close, but in the end you didn't do it. Did that dampen the spirits for today a bit, do you think?

No, I think it's not because of yesterday. It came out of nowhere, to be honest. We usually don't have any issues mentally in officials. Actually, we were very proud about yesterday, even though we should have even closed the game against G2. We were very, very happy that we are showing this game, and that the best teams should be scared of us, because we're here to fight.

We were very proud about yesterday, even though we should have closed the game against G2

You're the only guy from the team who has played a Major before. Especially in the first two games, you had a lot of impact, and it felt like you maybe made more plays than you usually do. Is that down to you feeling a bit more responsibility to help some of the other guys, or was it just a good day in the office for you?

I would say it's a good day in the office. The experience helps you to not be more scared and sometimes take the extra fights, but I would just say it's definitely a good day in the office.

Your IGL is probably the most inexperienced guy on the team, I guess you could say, but he has a very experienced coach standing behind him. At least from the outside, when I see you play, he's very vocal, he knows what he wants to do, and he doesn't really show any signs of inexperience or pressure. Do you feel that as well, and does that help the entire team stay grounded even considering it's a Major?

Yes. Even though our in-game leader is very young, at his first Major, and probably his like third or fourth tier-one LAN in career. It's definitely awesome that you don't feel that he's inexperienced. He's brave, he's taking the calls, he's not shutting down, he always has ideas, and just showing good signs of his potential. I believe he could become one of the best leaders in the game.

I believe he could become one of the best leaders in the game

When I spoke to you before the Major, you said that you're kind of dreaming of, or expecting, the playoffs. Now you've taken G2 so close, you've beaten Legacy, who are probably some of the two best teams in this stage alongside Spirit. Do you feel more confident that this is really something that you guys can achieve here?

Yes. I think day by day we are feeling way more and more confident to say. Before the tournament you come and you're just thinking, 'Guys, please let's not go out 0-3.' And now that we're already 2-1, it's a massive confidence boost for us. It just proves that we can fight the best teams, and we're not scared of anyone.
