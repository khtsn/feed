donk: "We all feel that we have really good chances against Vitality"

Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets is at the Major earlier than he is used to. The 19-year-old phenom will begin from Stage 2, a frightening prospect for his opponents considering that he averages a 1.45 rating in Majors.

With 160 hours in the past two weeks, and a 400 ELO lead to second in FACEIT's Season 8, donk is doing all he can to come into the Major ready.

As a team, however, preparation has been affected. Both of their co-coaches will miss the start of Cologne, leaving in-game leader Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev on his own in the server.

Fortunately, this is not the magixx of January. After Rio and Astana, Spirit's captain has taken on more responsibility and thrived. Adding even more now is a challenge, but one he can rise to.

"magixx has this charisma, and he's a very enjoyable guy," donk says. "Just look at him, just watch what he is doing, what he is saying. It's always fun. And basically, he's a smart guy."

donk, meanwhile, has a simple role: Frag. He has help, as the player with the most kills on blind opponents at Astana, but blind or not his opponents will struggle.

The question is if Spirit's new approach will work against CS2's top dogs. IEM Rio's grand final saw Vitality overcome Spirit, and a rematch is something all of Counter-Strike is anticipating.

Not least donk: "I think, and all of my teammates think, that we choked [in Rio], basically," he said. "We all feel that we have really good chances against them."
