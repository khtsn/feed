Valve release IEM Cologne Major stickers

Valve have released the stickers and Viewer Pass for the upcoming IEM Cologne Major, bringing a substantial amount of changes as compared to the usual structure.

Notably, Valve have done away with the previous system that required fans to unbox capsules in order to acquire stickers. Now, fans will use "tokens" to purchase individual stickers of any variety. The prices of individual stickers will be based on their relative demand, meaning that the price of a sticker will rise as more people purchase it.

To compensate for post-purchase decreases in price due to changes in demand, Valve have also implemented a partial refund system. When a sticker price drops by more than 25 tokens within 24 hours of a purchase, the purchaser will "get a refund of tokens for the total difference."

Souvenir weapons have also been changed in this iteration of Major items. Now, fans will have the ability to create their own Souvenir weapon from a weapon in their inventory by selecting a specific player and match, with the price for the Souvenir weapon determined by the value of the gold player and team stickers selected, as well as the rarity of the weapon being used for the craft.

Finally, the way that sticker revenue is allocated to teams has also been adjusted. Of the 50% of sticker revenue shared with teams, 5% will be retained by the tournament organizer, while the remaining 45% will be allocated to teams based on their placing in the Valve Regional Standings (VRS).

Accordingly, the top-ranked team will receive 2.85% of sticker revenues, descending to the bottom two ranked teams, who will each receive 0.72% of sticker revenues. Once the IEM Cologne Major concludes, all further sticker revenue will be allocated based on a team's placing at the Major, rather than by their VRS standings.
