zonic: "I can feel that I'm getting a bit tired; it's been some long years"*

*Update 14:38 : Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen has taken to X to clarify his comments, saying that he is as "tired as any other coach and player is after a long season."

"I’ve been doing this for 10 years straight with the expectation to win the biggest trophies and I love this life deep down to the bottom of my heart," he added, before pointing out that he spent just 35 days at home during the Spring season of 2025 out of 161 in total.

"With two kids, it is a tough balance and obviously something I need to discuss with my family 6-12 months before potentially negotiating another contract."

zonic has hinted at a possible retirement in an interview with Eefje "⁠sjokz⁠" Depoortere on ESL's broadcast after Falcons' victory at the IEM Cologne Major.

"I can feel that I'm getting a bit tired," the Dane said. "It's been some long years. The kids are getting older.

"I don't know [if I'll retire soon]. My wife is standing right there and it's always up to her, we take it season by season. But yeah, I'm getting a bit tired now."

The 39-year-old, who became the only person to win six Majors over the weekend, is the most successful coach in history after leading Astralis into their era before picking up Major trophies with Vitality and now Falcons.
