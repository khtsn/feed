The best highlights from BLAST Open Rotterdam group stage

9z coach Bruno "⁠BIT⁠" Fukuda Lima stopped by HLTV's microphone after his team beat MOUZ 2-1 and gave his thoughts on the match and its impact.

On beating MOUZ: "I'm really happy. We were confident about the match. I think we prepared well; we’ve been training well and playing some good matches in Brazil, but in the end, it’s hard to gauge our level when we hadn’t played here in Europe before. And even though we trained for two days, it’s still difficult — especially as our first match was against Vitality, and there’s no way we can match Vitality right now. I don’t think any team in the world could. There was no way to gauge our level because they were well above us in some areas.

But I think all of that really helped us come into today’s match better prepared, and that was really important for us to learn a lot of things, talk a lot, and watch the matches again. Yesterday we scrimmed Vitality as well, so I think all of that helped."

On the impact of this win:  "It's really important for us; we’re at a crucial stage right now. We need to pick up points to get into the invite zone for the upcoming tournaments. The timing of when we moved up in the rankings was a bit unfortunate.

Now, there aren't many invites being handed out, but it's important to stay in that invite range so we can get more opportunities. Even financially, so that we don't have to keep playing so many tournaments in Brazil because we’re constantly travelling back and forth, and that’s important for us."

On the goals for the rest of the tournament: "We're in a group… There's not much to say about it, is there? From the top 5 in the world, we have PARIVISION, Spirit, The MongolZ and Vitality. There’s not much to say, really. We’ll have to play like we did today, or even better, to beat these teams. We’ll see now. We’ll have to wait for the next match to see who we’re playing against."
