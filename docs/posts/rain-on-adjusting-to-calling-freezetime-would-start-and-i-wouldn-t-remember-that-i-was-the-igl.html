rain on adjusting to calling: "Freezetime would start and I wouldn't remember that I was the IGL"

You can watch the full interview here.

100 Thieves have gotten off to a flying start to life since putting together their ambitious project in the most-recent off-season, picking up a notable win over Ninjas in Pyjamas at Roman Imperium Cup V before making a run all the way to second-place at DraculaN Season 5, rocketing the team up to inside the top 40 of the live VRS within months of coming together.

Freshly-minted IGL Håvard "⁠rain⁠" Nygaard has been at the helm of this rapid rise, working with fellow veteran Nicolai "⁠device⁠" Reedtz to help bring their young core up to speed and battle their way through the gauntlet of VRS LANs. Ahead of their first matches at Roman Imperium, rain sat down with HLTV to talk through his first steps as a full-time caller and the process of building the new project at 100 Thieves.

rain joked about the difficulties he encountered when first adjusting to his new role, saying he would often forget he was the man to make the calls. "Freezetime would start and I wouldn't remember that I was the IGL," he laughed. "I would sit there and it was like oh shit, I have to make a call."

rain has enjoyed a positive start to life as an IGL

The 31-year-old then gave a more serious assessment of his biggest challenge as an IGL, citing mid-rounding as something he and the team need to work on. Specifically, he said this comes about as a result of the uniquely freeform and individualistic mid-rounding he became used to as being part of FaZe for so long. "I've been used to players just going for things, right? It's something that is maybe not lacking here, but we need to work on a little bit."

rain is not without help in his quest to improve as an IGL. In fact, he says ensuring he had the right support behind him was a condition of his taking up of the IGL mantle when joining 100 Thieves. "I was very clear on that from the start with 100 Thieves, that if I were to take this role, I would need a coaching staff that can help a lot, have a lot of influence on the team," he told HLTV. "Then I just try to carry out the vision on the server."

rain said he was positively surprised by how vocal sirah is in-game

Discussion also touched on some of the individual players in the team, with rain expressing his confidence in the work device is doing to get back to his best. "I see him working with the staff behind the scenes, trying to influence the way he plays, and I think he is doing everything he can to get back to it."

rain also spoke of playing with William "⁠sirah⁠" Kjærsgaard, calling it a "positive surprise." "He's very vocal, he knows what he wants… he will ask for stuff and is very vocal in the server, calls rotations and all this stuff, so it's been a very positive surprise in that sense."
