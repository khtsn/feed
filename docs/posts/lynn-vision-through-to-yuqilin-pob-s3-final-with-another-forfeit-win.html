Lynn Vision through to Yuqilin PoB S3 final with another forfeit win

LV won back-to-back playoff series by default

Lynn Vision are through to the grand final of the Yuqilin Pinnacle of Battle Season 3 after a forfeit victory against TYLOO, who could not play the match due to a scheduling clash with the XSE GangKui Cup Season 2 Finals.

This marks Lynn Vision's second forfeit win in the four-team double-elimination bracket. On Friday, they were awarded a walkover victory against Rare Atom, who forfeited the series following a dispute with the tournament organizer over the rules about lateness penalties.

These two wins have earned Lynn Vision 80 Valve points, pushing the team to fifth place in the Asia VRS and into the qualification zone for the Cologne Major.

SemperFi and The Huns have nothing left to play and are out of the race, while Chinggis Warriors can still play two more matches in the XSE GangKui Cup Season 2 Finals but have a 154-point gap to Lynn Vision, who will play the final of the Yuqilin PoB Season 3 event on Sunday.

With Saturday's 2-0 win against Change The Game, TYLOO are still alive on two fronts heading into Sunday. They could potentially play back-to-back matches in the XSE GangKui Cup Season 2 Finals before playing the Yuqilin PoB Season 3 final — opening the door to yet another scheduling clash.

The potential for abuse with Major spots on the line has drawn sharp criticism from community figures like Sudhen "⁠Bleh⁠" Wahengbam and Jordan "⁠Elfishguy⁠" Mays.

In a post on X, Lynn Vision admitted that "for us it is also very unbelievable that we qualify for Major in such a strange way."

"You may find it suspicious and we totally understand," the Chinese organization said. "The truth is teams just tried their best to gain more VRS points through the system, and we just happen to be the lucky guy who benefited most.

"The other teams could also join PoB and may benefit from the scheduling clash from the two events. As far as we know, the Huns and Chinggis Warriors played the open qualifier and withdrew later.

"The truth is after SemperFi lost to RA, the result is most likely concluded."

Lynn Vision also pushed back by seemingly calling into question the merit of THUNDER dOWNUNDER's qualification path.

The Australian team is currently ranked fourth in Asia after winning two local events, DFRAG Wildcard LAN Season 1 and ANZC Locals Brisbane 2026, highlighting how local LANs events can easily push teams up in the rankings — something HLTV mentioned back in September 2025.

"Where are you when teams are all farming their points in their own local lans?" Lynn Vision wrote.
