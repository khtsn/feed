The EVPs and All-Stars of PGL Cluj-Napoca 2026

ZywOo and flameZ stand head and shoulders above the rest, but there was still a clutch of players who did enough for EVPs.

PGL Cluj-Napoca confirmed that we remain in a Vitality era, and that the rise of PARIVISION is far more than just a flash in the pan.

It also saw a 30th MVP for Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut, who is being crowned as Counter-Strike's greatest ever player by more and more pundits as the months tick past and trophies are added to his cabinet.

Cluj was another tour de force for the Frenchman, and his brilliance is best illustrated by the fact that a 1.39 rating here is the 16th highest-rated Big Event rating of his career.

There was a challenge for the MVP, unlike in Krakow last time out, but ZywOo still finished with a 0.10 rating gap to Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan.

"I will always try to push myself harder when I see flameZ or ropz get closer," he said post-event. "It also pushes myself to be harder on myself and to be harder. I will always try to put a trial for them to make it harder for the MVP, we're always gonna win the tournament if they are playing like this, so it's good, of course."

Radar Chart stat glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • RW Δ: kill differential per round win

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan

flameZ had his best event of his career in Romania, averaging a 1.41 rating in playoffs with a 1.29 overall and 1.02 kills per round win. He was the player of the match in the grand final, too, with a 1.48 in the 3-0 win.

However, there is something telling about flameZ's best map being a 1.85-rated suffocation of Aurora — and him still being 0.01 rating short of player of the map ZywOo.

It's a tale flameZ will be used to by now, but we doubt he will mind having been given such a strong team and the host of trophies they have earned together.

Already a two-time seventh best player of the year, entry fragger flameZ has started 2026 up a gear and is one of only three players to have two awards from the first three events of the year.

Vitality were scary enough already, but with flameZ averaging a 1.25 rating this year, they are packing an even harder punch.

Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool

While lots of players stood out in Cluj, it remains clear that there was a chasm between ZywOo and flameZ and the rest of the chasing pack.

That left space for ropz, who was affected by illness and averaged a 1.00 in groups, room to climb all the way to third in this list (though his EVP will still be significantly weaker than that of flameZ for the top 20).

The main reason for the Estonian's placement was his playoffs, where he had a 1.21 rating and was player of the map on three occasions, including a 2.37 rating versus The MongolZ in semis where he finished the map on a +15.0% Swing.

Put together, that was an arena performance only his two teammates eclipsed.

Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás

torzsi may have made himself public enemy number one in Cluj-Napoca, infuriated by their boos, but he was having a very good tournament beforehand.

He was solid in groups, even putting up a fight against Vitality — more than you can say for most of this list, let alone the rest of the players at the event — and confirmed his EVP with a 1.96-rated Inferno and 125 ADR in the decider's first half against Natus Vincere.

Those two player of the map showings were crucial, but his overall stats still make for impressive reading, with a 1.17 rating vs top 10 teams, 0.78 kills per round, and 21.7% multi-kill rate.

After an EVP last year in the same arena, then, torzsi did enough to go back-to-back.

Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov

m0NESY's event rating of 1.33 was only beaten by ZywOo, and Falcons' sniper was crucial in pushing his team into the quarter-finals with a 1.39 in the group stage.

Seven of his ten maps had ratings above 1.18, and raw figures like 1.09 kills per round win and 78.6% KAST illustrate his impact and his consistency.

However, there were holes in his case that prevented him going any higher. Firstly, he was helped a lot by Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin and Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač in the group stage map wins (all three were above 1.35 in those six maps).

Secondly, while a 1.19 rating in the arena was enough to nudge him into EVP status considering the strength of his group stage, m0NESY did struggle on the decider (0.97 rating) and also lost control on Dust2 by going from a K-D of 22-4 to 26-16.

That meant he could only reach the middle of this EVP list.

Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač

NiKo follows just behind m0NESY after his 1.47-rated quarter-final helped him leapfrog kyousuke and make it into the EVP list.

Given that he was less impactful than his young star duo against FaZe and FURIA, it was important for his case that he was as strong as he was against PARIVISION, and he duly delivered.

Mirage was especially impressive, where NiKo posted a 2.42-rated, 154.2 ADR, first half to force PARIVISION on to a decider. Overall, he was at 1.47 in the arena, giving him a hefty push from third in his team into EVP territory.

After an MVP at Bounty, it's NiKo who joins ZywOo and flameZ on two awards so far in 2026 — and with it, wins his 52nd EVP.

Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman

While torzsi was better against the top teams, xertioN was MOUZ's leading man in their route to playoffs.

The Israeli opener had a 1.36 rating in the team's eight key map wins, and smothered any chance of a Natus Vincere fightback on the Mirage decider in the arena by going 10-3 with +18.1% Swing on the second half.

Figures like 108.2 damage per round win, and 83 ADR overall, are also important when assessing his impact on his team's run.

Usukhbayar "⁠910⁠" Banzragch

910 recorded eight maps (42%) above a 1.30 rating in Romania, as he continues to take the mantle of Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold as his team's new star player.

He averaged a whopping 1.40 rating in map wins, falling to 1.29 when you include the Vitality semi-final, and showed a strong level of consistency outside of the first group stage Vitality match.

Highlights included a 38-frag double OT affair against B8, a 2.00 rating against FURIA on Mirage in the arena, and putting up a fight against MOUZ in the third-place decider.

Despite his impact in pushing his team forward, five poor maps (albeit four of them against Vitality) reduced his rating against top 10 opponents to 1.01, which is why he could not go any higher.

Ivan "⁠zweih⁠" Gogin

zweih sneaks in at the end of the EVP list after leading PARIVISION's efforts in playoffs with three standout playoff maps against Falcons and MOUZ.

PARIVISION's incredibly tough run saw them play 20 of their 23 maps against top 10 opponents, with an average opponent rank per map of 5.7. They ended the event with a round win percentage of 48%, providing further context to explain their average team rating of 0.98 and why they need to be treated differently from teams with easier runs.

With EVP lists, it is necessary to isolate the maps that led to a team's placement and identify which players are pushing their team forward, allowing us to forgive poor moments when they do not result in the team's elimination.

zweih, therefore, with a 1.21 rating and 100.0 ADRW in map wins in series wins, and 1.33 in the four crucial arena victories, did just enough to earn a nod despite his 1.05 event rating and so much credit being shared out between the whole team.

Honorable mentions

Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar was just as good in aggregated map wins as 910 with a 1.45 rating there, but he was reliant on his demolition of 0-3 team paiN for some of those numbers. He also struggled badly in the third-place decider, when a good performance might have made his edge case an EVP as it did for 910.

Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko was good in map wins to get FURIA into playoffs, but dropped a 0.88 rating against The MongolZ in another disappointing finish to an event for the Kazakh sniper. Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato endured a similar fate, falling just short.

In groups, all of Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea, Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko, and Victor "⁠Staehr⁠" Staehr had fantastic events but did not pass the high bar for a group stage EVP.

All-Stars

ZywOo and flameZ lead the way for the All-Stars, but their dominance only left one other spot for a Vitality player in the five-man lineup.

In the end, we went with ropz over Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire to make room for Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali. Given that PARIVISION made the final with a 48% round win rate and their best player on a 1.05 rating, this is one occasion where intangibles such as a team's leadership have to be given credit for pulling them through so many close series and maps.

No apEX then allows ropz to beat NiKo as the best closer of the event, with his remarkable 8-0 and 100% record in 1on1 clutches over the tournament proof of his impact despite a slow start.

Finally, eighth EVP zweih was the only anchor to receive an award, and is rewarded for his 1.22 CT rating (0.17 above his position average of 1.05) with a spot rounding out the roster.
