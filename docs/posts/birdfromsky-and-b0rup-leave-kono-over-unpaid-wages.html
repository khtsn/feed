birdfromsky and b0RUP leave kONO over unpaid wages

Thomas "⁠birdfromsky⁠" Due-Frederiksen and Johannes "⁠b0RUP⁠" Borup have taken to X to announce that they are parting ways with kONO, claiming that the Ukrainian organization owes them four months' salary.

In a series of posts, b0RUP gave a lengthy account of his time under the Ukrainian organization since he, birdfromsky and Nicolas "⁠Keoz⁠" Dgus were picked up as a core from The Last Resort in August.

He explained that kONO put the roster on hold in December while they evaluated their next steps amid uncertainty about Arseniy "⁠cptkurtka023⁠" Derevinskiy's future. According to him, if the Ukrainian player were transferred to another organization, the team would find a replacement for him. Otherwise, the two Danes would be moved to the bench as the team would transition to an all-Ukrainian roster.

cptkurtka023 ended up joining Inner Circle in January, but a replacement was never signed, though Audric "⁠JACKZ⁠" Jug started playing for kONO as a stand-in.

"In December, the owner suggested terminating the contract immediately and paying out the remaining salary," b0RUP wrote. "I told him this would be acceptable, as it would amount to four months of full salary.

"During this discussion, it became clear that he believed players automatically receive half salary while benched, even if the contract does not state that. Our contract did not contain such a clause.

"During this period, there were also statements suggesting that the organization might declare bankruptcy and therefore would not pay anything regardless of the outcome.

"We attempted to find a compromise and even proposed accepting 50% of the owed amount, as Bird needed the money for daily living expenses. However, this proposal was not accepted."

The former HEROIC and Astralis member also confirmed that Keoz has gone through the same situation. On January 18, the Belgian player said that he had not been paid his December salary and that his attempts to resolve the matter privately had failed.

Keoz, who did not feature in kONO's latest match on March 5, also claimed that he had received "pressure, threats, and attempts to discredit me."

According to b0RUP, kONO offered him and birdfromsky 60 percent of the owed amount if they posted on social media "that an agreement had been reached and that keoz’s public statements about the situation were incorrect."

kONO have not yet commented on the Danish duo's claims. The Ukrainian organization, founded by professional footballer Yevhen Konoplyanka, has been silent on X since January 19, when its CEO, Oleh Shustenko, said that Keoz's claims that he was denied salary payments do "not correspond to reality."

"All obligations have always been fulfilled toward everyone," Shustenko stressed.
