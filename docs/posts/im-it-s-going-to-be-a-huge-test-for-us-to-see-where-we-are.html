iM: "It's going to be a huge test for us to see where we are"

Natus Vincere came into BLAST Open Rotterdam fresh off a title at ESL Pro League S23, and Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen's troops are showing no signs of slowing down.

The European outfit battled past B8, Aurora and Falcons in the group stage before defeating PARIVISION in the semi-final to set up a clash against Vitality in the grand final.

It will be the first match between the two teams since the Budapest Major, where Vitality ran out 2-0 winners after two 13-11 games.

"It's going to be a huge test for us to see where we are," he told HLTV after the win against PARIVISION. "And I hope we're going to put up a good fight and try to make a good impression."

You can listen to the interview here.

iM, a 2-1 victory, and you are through to the grand final. Give me your thoughts on the series.

It feels good to win, not going to lie. It feels good to beat good teams, to show that we can play at this level and on this stage. It was a great game, and now finally we can play Vitality.

For a lot of teams, PARIVISION are very hard to read, but it felt like on the first and the third maps especially, you had a very good read on them. Walk me through the preparation for this series.

We prepared like we usually prepare. Yes, I would say we had good reads, but also some fuck-ups. I remember we ran into some stacks on Inferno, for example, and even on Mirage, I think we ran into some small stacks and stuff like this. But overall, it was a good game for us, and I'm glad that we were on top of that.

There is not a lot of time for preparation. But I think we kind of know-ish how they play

I would say that one of the biggest issues for you in this tournament has been winning pistol rounds. You have a 36% win rate in this tournament, which is one of the lowest. Today you won only 2 out of 6. Have you identified the cause?

If I knew it, I would fix it, obviously. We saw that we are not winning pistols; sometimes we are joking about it, but also winning a game without pistols also feels nicer, I would say. But for sure in the future we need to fix it, because these pistols are like a huge bonus at the start of the game.

After that rough second map, you came out swinging now on Mirage. What did you talk about outside to make sure that you could reset and come into the last map with a new mentality?

I mean, we kind of fucked up on Inferno, like I was mentioning before. We were going into stacks and stuff like this, hurrying up when it wasn't needed… And yeah, Andrey [B1ad3] said to just follow our game plan, not hurry up, and think twice when we make a move. And then we just followed what we had planned for our CT side, and it worked pretty much very well.

Okay, now Vitality is the final boss. How do you think that you will stack up against them in a BO5?

I have no idea. I don't want to say anything about them, but I'm glad that we will play them in any game, not saying only about the final. I think it's going to be a nice game, and I'm just going to enjoy it, probably.

NAVI is known as a team that prepares heavily for its opponents. You have about 14 hours to prepare for that match. How do you think that will affect your preparation?

There is not a lot of time, like you said, for preparation. But I think we kind of know-ish how they play, because obviously they have been winning so much. And we have been watching them, how they play, how they are reacting, but overall, like you said, it's going to be the final boss. It's going to be a huge test for us to see where we are, it doesn't matter if you lose or win. And I hope we're going to put up a good fight and try to make a good impression.

And win?

And win, obviously.
