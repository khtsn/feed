G2, Monte, and Gentle Mates eliminated on BC Game Masters day two

Day two of BC Game Masters Championship Season 1 has concluded with the quarter-final stage, with BetBoom, HOTU, BIG, and SINNERS advancing to the semi-finals.

The results had a big impact on Major qualification for several teams. Monte's Stage 2 hopes were dashed by a loss to BetBoom, while HEROIC's loss to Tricked put a spanner in their Cologne Major hopes earlier in the day.

Elsewhere, HOTU kept their incredibly slim Major chances alive by beating Tricked in their quarter-final, while G2 suffered a shock loss to SINNERS on their debut tournament with Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz.

While their Major spot is not in doubt after the loss, the result will no doubt shake the confidence of the squad that recently went for a risky mid-season change.

"I think this tournament is practice still for us as well. We will be learning from all these small situations and build that routine for our team, which is the most important thing," Eetu "⁠sAw⁠" Saha told HLTV after the loss.

"I think in the end, like, the most important thing for us in these tournaments, like here in BC Game and then Roman Cup and Stake, it's gonna to improve ourselves for the bigger tournaments."

Meanwhile, SINNERS' upset keeps their slim chance of making the Major alive.

The biggest Major-qualification clash happened between BIG and Gentle Mates, with both squads starting the day with a 57% percent chance of making the Major as per Mischeif.

BIG got the job in two maps to rise up to 16th in the Global VRS, while Gentle Mates now require a good result at Stake Ranked to secure a spot in Cologne.
