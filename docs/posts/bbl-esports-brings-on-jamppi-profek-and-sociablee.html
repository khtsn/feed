BBL Esports brings on Jamppi, PROFEK, and sociablEE

UPDATE: This article has been updated as of October 24 to reflect the signings of PROFEK and sociablEE. This article will continue to be updated as BBL presumably announces the rest of their roster in the coming days leading up to the Red Bull Home Ground EMEA LCQ.

BBL Esports BBL Esports Europe Unranked Jamppi Elias Olkkonen sociablEE Volkan Yonal PROFEK Dawid Święć LewN Burak Alkan has signed Elias "Jamppi" Olkkonen as their in-game leader for VCT EMEA 2025, pending Riot approval. As of October 24, they have also announced the signings of Dawid "PROFEK" Święć and Volkan "sociablEE" Yonal.

"I am so happy to get this opportunity and represent BBL for next year," Jamppi commented. "To be part of this is an absolute honor."

Jamppi hails from Team Liquid after having been with the team all the way since February 2021. His accomplishments with the team include a top-four finish at Champions 2021, a top-eight placement at Champions 2022, and a first-place triumph in the 2023 EMEA League playoffs to win the regional crown. However, Liquid struggled in 2024 as it was the first year in the organization's history that they failed to qualify for an international event.

PROFEK recently played with GoNext Esports GoNext Esports Europe Unranked in Challengers League East. Excluding the league's Promotion Cup, in which his team did not participate, the controller player distinguished himself with a best-in-league 1.28 rating and 1.47 K:D ratio over the course of Split 2. GoNext claimed first place in the split to qualify for EMEA Ascension, where they exited in the group stage with a 2-2 record.

sociablEE has been a mainstay in the Turkish Valorant scene since 2021. He last competed with Fire Flux Esports Fire Flux Esports Europe Unranked lauress Toprak Kaynak Ruxic Uğur Güç Turko Mehmet Özen w0zzip Zafer Gungor in Challengers League Türkiye throughout 2024 as a flex. The team notably won Split 1 and later placed fifth in the Split 2 playoffs, missing out on Ascension. Individually, sociablEE ended Split 2 with a strong 1.17 rating and 1.20 K:D ratio.

BBL Esports spent the 2024 season in a middling position in the standings, finishing seventh overall. In both stages, the Turkish squad came just one placement shy of qualification to the EMEA playoffs after a five-way 3-3 tiebreaker in Stage 1 and a three-way 4-6 tiebreaker in Stage 2. Since then, BBL has parted with the entirety of their 2024 roster and picked up Burak "LewN" Alkan from Galatasaray Esports Galatasaray Esports Europe Unranked Padisah Onur Dönmez Docent Kadri Vatandaş Touven Can Eryıldız JrayN Ferhat Yılmaz in Challengers League Türkiye.

These arrivals to BBL mark the organization's first-ever signings of non-Turkish players and indicate a new international direction for the squad. This will also be Jamppi's first time serving in a formal in-game leader position. The new BBL roster will next compete in the EMEA LCQ of Red Bull Home Ground, which starts on Oct. 26.
