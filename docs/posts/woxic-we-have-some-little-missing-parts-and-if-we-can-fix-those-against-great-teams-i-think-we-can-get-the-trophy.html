woxic: "We have some little missing parts. And if we can fix those against great teams, I think we can get the trophy"

Aurora have booked a place at Rotterdam Ahoy

Aurora have punched their ticket to the playoff stages of BLAST Open Rotterdam following a 2-0 win against FURIA.

The Turkish team came back from 12-6 down on Dust2 to win the first map in overtime before coasting through Inferno.

"Communications-wise, mood-wise, nobody stepped down, even when we were down like that," Özgür "⁠woxic⁠" Eker told HLTV after the match. "So I think that's the key, the mentality, and if you have that, you can always make a comeback."

Aurora will have two days to rest and prepare for the group's upper final against Natus Vincere—a chance for revenge after the recent loss in the grand final of ESL Pro League 23.

"We could have won that final," he said. "But we made mistakes that we shouldn't make. We gave up 1v4 clutches, 1v3 clutches…

"What we can take from it is that we can play finals. We almost got the trophy. And we've started to be in great shape again. I think this final helped us have a great mentality for the start of this event."

A 2-0 win and you've secured a spot in the playoffs. What are your reactions to that series?

I think, when we were making the comeback on Dust2, I was feeling that we would beat them if we kept winning rounds like this. I wasn't sure about Inferno, 7-0, 8-0 lead, but maybe they got affected a bit because of the comeback, and when you are just going with a bad mood inside and coming back… But this is not the case too much for one of the best teams in the world.

But in the end, I'm happy that we were like ready for anything. And we were kind of feeling that we were just controlling the game all the time. Even when we were down 12-6 on Dust2, I think we were kind of controlling the game, but somehow we couldn't finish the rounds. We couldn't finish clutch rounds, or we were making little mistakes. We knew what they would do, but we were not handling it somehow. But in the end, a win is a win.

As you said, you were down 12-6 on Dust2 after that massive run from FURIA, 12-1. What was the secret behind that comeback?

I don't think there was so much of a secret. I think everybody just believed we could make the comeback, since we had done the same against Astralis and against.. I don't remember, but most of the teams that we were able to make these comebacks against, if we didn't step down from the game.

And I think, communications-wise, mood-wise, nobody stepped down, even when we were down like that. So I think that's the key, the mentality, and if you have that, you can always make a comeback. Maybe we can't do it, but I think having that mentality and going to the second map, winning it and going to the third one if we lose the first… I think 70% of it is just mentality.

The last time you faced them was in Krakow, and you lost to them 2-0. What were the main differences? Would you say you've improved as a team, that FURIA is not as strong as they were back then, or a bit of both?

Maybe a little bit of both, but I don't think they just started playing badly or anything. I think we have improved our teamplay and communication in key rounds, also making the mid-rounds calls to each other. I think people just started to take the lead sometimes, helping MAJ3R with, 'I have this in front of me.' These kinds of things.

When you're in front of people and you get the silver medal, it gives you so much pain for a while.

I think everyone is just staying on the server all the time. And I think our timings for executions are much better. When we are doing it as a unit. Our flash and smoke timings. I think that's what we have been working on. It is just paying off right now.

You came to this event after a second place in Stockholm. What were the main takeaways from the final against NAVI?

What I can take is that we could have won that final. But we made mistakes that we shouldn't make. We gave up 1v4 clutches, 1v3 clutches… What we can take from it is that we can play finals. We almost got the trophy. And we've started to be in great shape again. I think this final helped us have a great mentality for the start of this event. Because we came here as the finalists of the previous event.

This gives everyone, I think, some confidence. Personally, it has given me confidence because we were almost able to get a trophy. We have some little missing parts. And if we can fix those against great teams. I think we can get the trophy.

Are you looking forward to getting revenge against NAVI?

Yeah, obviously. Because when you're losing finals, it's always shit. It's always better to finish third or fourth, I think. Actually, third. You're playing one game, the event is over for you, you're in third place and you've won your last game. But in the final, when you're in front of people and you get the silver medal, it gives you so much pain for a while.

Aurora had to settle for second place at EPL after a loss to NAVI

But here, we didn't have that much time between events. We kind of couldn't stay sad because we cannot stay in the past. We need to move forward all the time. I think we've done it well. Otherwise, we would be showing bad performances. And maybe we would have been eliminated already if we were just stuck on feelings from the past. So I'm glad everyone is being professional about this. Because life goes on.

Every time, there are going to be wins and losses. But I want to win S-tier events, for sure. I hope we can do it. That's what we are working for every day.

From the outside, it feels like the team keeps improving. You were Top 16 in Bounty, top 6 in Krakow, top 8 in Cluj and then 2nd place in EPL. Do you feel this progression in the team?

Yeah, I think everyone sees this inside the team. That's why I said that today it was the key to victory. Because everyone is seeing that we are getting closer and closer. We will make it, today or tomorrow, basically.

Looking at your results here so far, the win against FaZe and now this one, how far do you think you can go?

Like you said, we played the finals in the previous event. So if we play the same way that we played in that event, and also looking at how we played against FaZe and FURIA, I think we can still reach the finals. That's the feeling I have.

It can be overconfidence, but I think beating one of the best teams in the world gives you this kind of confidence. And I think it's a good thing to have. You shouldn't feel like an underdog. Last event, we were in the final, now we've made the playoffs again. We've beaten one of the best teams. 'Let's go, guys, now for the playoffs.' This is a good feeling.
