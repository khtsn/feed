Freddy "⁠KRIMZ⁠" Johansson spoke to Esportstudion about his move to EYEBALLERS, and confessed that he was about to retire before the call came from Jesper "⁠JW⁠" Wecksell:

"The most important thing for me was to have fun again and then I enjoy it, I really enjoy it and then I can do a lot on the server," he said. "It was like a goal that I was very important to, that the socializing, that's the key. I've had fun in fnatic too, but not quite the same as it was a few years ago."

"I was going to retire, so it was a fun couple of conversations with Mr. Jesper Wecksell."

A patch went out overnight, updating Fachwerk, Boulder, Shelter, and El Dorado to their latest workshop version. "Various stability and security fixes" were also pushed.

fnatic have secured victory on debut with their new roster in ESL Challenger League Season 52 Europe Cup 1, beating SPARTA, BRUTE, and HOTU in the playoffs to earn $4,000 and a spot in the Season 52 Europe Finals.

Artem "⁠cairne⁠" Mushynskyi dominated the event with a 1.48 average rating over 12 maps, and was in terrifying form in the title decider as he posted a 2.24 rating in a series that included a 13-0 win on Ancient. The team's newest addition, 17-year-old Volodymyr "⁠mazay⁠" Iorhachov, ended the event averaging a 1.04 rating.
