FURIA, FUT, and FaZe suffer losses on day one of EPL S23 Stage 2

FalleN's troops suffered the biggest upset of the first day of action.

Day one of ESL Pro League Season 23 Stage 2 came to an end with almost all favorites progressing to the 1-0 pool, with FURIA suffering the biggest shock as they lost a game to B8 that finished way past midnight.

FURIA fall to B8

After a day of mostly expected results, the final match served up the biggest shock. B8 got the better of FURIA in a match that ended way past midnight, as Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo's troops fell on Mirage and Dust2 on either side of a confident 13-2 Overpass victory.

The majority-Brazilian team, who will play with Sid "⁠sidde⁠" Macedo if they are to qualify for the LAN stage due to FalleN having a personal commitment, haven't played an official for 14 days.

MOUZ recovered from an opening loss on Dust2 with impressive performances on Mirage and Inferno to secure their spot in the 1-0 pool, but their best individual moment came in the series opener.

Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás was forced into a 1vs4 retake of the B site in the final round of the half and delivered a breathtaking clutch featuring a no-scope, a USP one-tap, and a collateral AWP shot to close it out.

FaZe continue to flounder

FaZe's VRS ranking allowed them to skip Stage 1, but their first game of ESL Pro League S23 showed the European combine is far from rediscovering their mojo.

They got 13-1'd on their map pick, Dust2, meaning they are now 1-8 on their map picks in 2026 after choosing five different maps throughout the year already.

While some solace can be found in their Ancient performance, where they got 10 rounds despite losing both pistols, alarm bells are still loud and clear in the FaZe camp.
