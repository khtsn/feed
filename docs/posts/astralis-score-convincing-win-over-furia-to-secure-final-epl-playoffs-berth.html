Astralis score convincing win over FURIA to secure final EPL playoffs berth

Astralis have secured the final spot in the Annexet after a convincing 2-1 victory over FURIA in the final match of ESL Pro League Season 23 Stage 2 (13-4 Nuke, 9-13 Dust2, 13-4 Inferno).

The Danish organization began their Stage 2 campaign with a win over FUT, but were left staring elimination in the face after dropping series to MOUZ and Legacy. They earned a single chance to qualify for the playoffs after beating FaZe, and converted on the opportunity to deny FURIA a recovery from the 0-2 pool.

Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo's men struggled immensely in the online event, losing games to B8 and 3DMAX before struggling past HEROIC and looking more like themselves against paiN. But this series was more like the former pair than the latter, with FURIA marred by mistakes and questionable decisions that led to a blowout on two maps.

Astralis were in cruise control on Nuke, seemingly able to do no wrong as every default and execute played out to near perfection.

FURIA were repeatedly consigned to saves as Jakob "⁠jabbi⁠" Nygaard and Gytis "⁠ryu⁠" Glušauskas' openers and multi-kills tore apart the defenses, and salvaging two rounds near the end of the half did little to improve the mood as Victor "⁠Staehr⁠" Staehr and jabbi combined to win a 3v5 for a 10-2 scoreline before Astralis sealed victory early into the second.

Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen's men carried momentum into Dust2, converting a pistol win into a 3-0 start before Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko began to find impact with his AWP. FURIA kept the scoreline even at halftime and regained the lead after Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato and molodoy combined to win a 3v5 in the pistol, and the Brazilian rifler padded his stats a bit further with an anti-eco ace a few rounds later.

But the tale of the second half was really of molodoy, who repeatedly pulled his team out of holes and provided key openers (5-0 record) to help them cling on to life. That included a jumping AWP no scope on A to deny a comeback effort by Astralis, allowing FURIA to seal victory 13-9 and force an Inferno decider.

Hopes of a competitive map faded quickly when play resumed, though, with FURIA's pistol win spoiled by a fantastic quad kill by Staehr that turned a force round in Astralis' favor. The Dane continued to find high-impact multi-kills, helped by a few great calls by HooXi and mistakes by FURIA.

A failure to plant the bomb on a late A site hit and a sloppy B take into a three-strong stack added to a string of dismal rounds from FURIA, and they walked away from the map with just four rounds as Astralis romped to victory.
