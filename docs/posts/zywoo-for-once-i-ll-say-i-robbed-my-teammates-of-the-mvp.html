ZywOo: "For once, I'll say I robbed [my teammates of the MVP]"

Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan's admission that Vitality were "really tired" and "out of form" after winning BLAST Rivals was echoed by Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut when he spoke to HLTV in Fort Worth, who said that it was his "shittiest tournament individually" despite claiming the MVP.

The Frenchman has been in a few tight races this year, with flameZ in Cluj-Napoca, Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool in Rotterdam, and flameZ again here, but has still emerged with four medals from 2026 in just five months.

ZywOo claims BLAST Rivals MVP

"I thought it was ropz or flameZ who were going to get it," ZywOo laughed in the mixed zone. "But for once, I'll say I robbed them. ropz robbed me in Rotterdam, and now I rob him, so I'm happy for that."

A lack of practice in a week where a major patch was pushed might have hurt lesser teams, but Vitality rallied to extend their playoff map win streak to 27 regardless.

"We always have fuel," ZywOo says. "We say we're not here to waste our time. apEX says that every time we play a non-priority tournament for us, but every time we play a tournament, we're here to win it. It doesn't matter if you don't have energy or don't have the rest we need."

First of all, run me through the final. You start on Nuke and are 11-0 down, you get one before the half. How did you bring it back? Did you say anything at halftime? What was the rallying cry?

First of all, this T side was really rough for us. We couldn't trade, we couldn't do anything in the right timing, we couldn't launch any strats, and they did a good counter; in a way, they pushed Outside in a good timing, going Secret, T Red, under Silo, and always countered us. But, after 11-0, we were like, just get one round at a time.

We were kind of already thinking about the second map, like let's try to get our routine, get into the map, get into what we like to play. Of course, we're thinking about the comeback, but after 11-1, we just tried to get round by round, tried to get into the game individually and as a team.

After that, after 11-5, 11-6, 11-7, and we see we're playing pretty good, we're trying to play together, the comeback is getting slowly in our mind, and we managed to do that. Of course, after 11-1, we were just trying to focus on what we needed to bring for the second map, in terms of communication, of mindset, but after that, yeah, it was pretty clear that we play well on CT.

After you come back from a deficit like that, do you feel like you have psychologic edge? That you could get ahead now after that big win, crushing NAVI?

Yes and no, it depends, because also Anubis, we haven't practiced this map for three weeks, to be honest, so it was pretty rough. Even though we won Nuke, we knew it was going to be a rough map against NAVI. Even though we did the comeback and got into the mindset, we knew it was going to be hard anyway because of fucking Anubis.

But, of course, we knew as Vitality right now, we're pretty good whenever we get a comeback and when we get into these kinds of maps. We know we have a very good mentality, and we're prepared for that. We just need to play our game after that and not overthink. After the 11-1 comeback, we just needed to focus on what we needed to bring into the next one.

You closed it out on Dust2, a good map for you, it kind of pushed you over the edge to get the MVP, as well. You went 3-0, got the MVP, a good end to the tournament...

Yeah, of course. It was also my shittiest tournament, individually. I don't know how I got this, to be honest, I thought it was ropz or flameZ who were going to get it. But for once, I'll say I robbed them. [Laughs] ropz robbed me in Rotterdam, and now I rob him, so I'm happy for that.

Of course, I'm happy to get this one. We didn't practice for a week, and we didn't play as a team. We could see during this tournament that it was a bit rough for us in terms of everything, and we just need to get better in this way, even though we're not going to practice for Atlanta.

We need to be better in a way of playing together, being more ready as a team, and I think that right now, as Vitality, we can do anything we want; we're better than anyone, so we feel good.

We always have fuel. We say we're not here to waste our time. apEX says that every time we play a non-priority tournament for us, but every time we play a tournament, we're here to win it. It doesn't matter if you don't have energy or don't have the rest we need. We are Vitality, we're here to win every tournament, and in Atlanta, we'll be here for that.
