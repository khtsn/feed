Tomas "⁠tomaszin⁠" Corna, who averaged a 1.39 rating in the group, sat down with HLTV after the match against Gaimin Gladiators to discuss the results in Portugal, the recent changes, and the impact of next year's Major in Buenos Aires.

You and Gaimin Gladiators are in the VRS race against each other right now. What do you think of this rivalry after losing to them in such a tight game on Ancient?

The game was ours to win. We had five or six rounds to close out the map. We had it under control, and when we started to lose rounds, it became a snowball. We weren't starting out the rounds in the right way or making the best decisions, and they're an experienced team, so they made use of that.

We're also a new team, we've just had two practice days together, and we have a new IGL, but even then, I'm proud of the boys because we didn't stop fighting until the end, and we know that we could have won it, so we're calm.

I mean, we're upset now because we let it go, but at the same time, we could have won it, and that's reassuring. We had the game in our hands until the end, and having this happen to us now is good for us to learn and get experience from it.

Teams like yours and Gaimin Gladiators are working within this new VRS system, which can give an opportunity to smaller teams. Do you see your team as an inspiration for smaller teams?

Yeah, and a lot of tier-1 teams, like FaZe now, they're going to play DraculaN, and that makes the tier-2 and tier-3 tournaments more exciting. It's great. We like that. BESTIA is investing a lot in us so we can play a lot of tournaments abroad, so yeah, I think so in that sense.

Regarding the mental game, you're playing teams like Liquid and 100T. What's it like to play such big and legendary teams?

We're very focused on our own preparation, and the way we want to play, because we're a team that has just two practice days, so we're just trying to line up our vision of the game, our structure, our way of playing, and so the games against the best teams, we're still just trying to focus on ourselves, trying to get better round by round and map by map.

We don't really care who we play against because we know that if we play well, we have a good view of the game. We just try to focus on ourselves; we know that we can beat anyone if we play well.

What do you think can be improved in the Argentine CS scene until the Major next year? Do you think Argentine CS can be more popular than Brazilian CS someday?

I don't know if it will be more popular; Brazil has a huge population, much more than Argentina, but I think that 9z and us, and other teams right behind us, are doing a lot so that it's not just "Brazilian dominance."

I don't know if it'll be a lot more popular because of the number of people, but we're trying to inspire more amateur players and to get people to watch Counter-Strike. The Major in Buenos Aires will also help expand viewership in Argentina. Our way of adding to that is going as far as we can at events, so people see our name and our flag.

You have a new rifler, nacho. How is he integrating into the team?

He came into the team a couple of weeks ago, and he's adapting well. Now that we have changed our IGL and coach, we're approaching the game differently than we were before, but I think he's a smart guy, and he has a lot of firepower.

I've moved to some more active playmaking roles, and I think that with more experience as a team and in the roles I'm playing, I can have much more impact than I've had

He won us a lot of rounds today just with his individual skill. We're just trying to get everyone on the same page, but I think he's adapting well, and with more time as a team and with him, I think we'll go far.

Speaking of firepower, you have a lot of it, and you're known for having high impact. Is that an instinct you have, or is it something the team has structured around you?

I think it's something that developed little by little. I joined BESTIA, and I think that I started off pretty well, and they realized that there was potential there. The way we played before with Noktse, our previous IGL, was a bit more set regarding strats and defaults from spawn.

Now, with buda and shaRpy, our new coach, they're trying to give players more freedom to make decisions and take risks, which I think benefits me as a player. I've moved to some more active playmaking roles, and I think that with more experience as a team and in the roles I'm playing, I can have much more impact than I've had.

But yeah, I do think that I have a pretty good impact, especially in games when there's some pressure; those are my favorite games. But I have to continue gaining experience at bigger events, and as a team, we need to keep getting into the rhythm of things.
