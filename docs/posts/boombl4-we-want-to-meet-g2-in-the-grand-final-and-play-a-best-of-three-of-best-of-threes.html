Boombl4: "We want to meet G2 in the grand final and play a best-of-three of best-of-threes"

We caught up with Kirill "⁠Boombl4⁠" Mikhailov at Stake Ranked Episode 1 after BetBoom beat 9INE in the lower bracket to keep their lower bracket run in Barcelona alive while they sit inside of the Major invite spots ahead of the April 6 cut-off.

A small rivalry has developed between BetBoom and G2 as the two teams met at Roman Imperium Cup, where the Russians beat the Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač-led squad en route to the trophy, and were then knocked down by them to the lower bracket in Spain.

If G2 can make it past GamerLegion in the upper bracket final, and BetBoom can complete the lower bracket gauntlet, Boombl4 would like a third rematch, to decide in a third best-of-three who comes out on top in their tier-two VRS event tour.

First, however, BetBoom will face one of Ninjas in Pyjamas and HEROIC, two teams also fighting to leave their mark at Stake Ranked Episode 1 in order to get enough VRS points to qualify for the Major.

BetBoom may have a handicap playing with two stand-ins, but on the other hand, that handicap has also acted as a release valve for some of the pressure on the team to make the Major. "I think that for full teams, there's more pressure than us," Boombl4 admits, "so it's a bit easier."

You're in the race for a Major spot, and it's getting closer and closer now. How are you feeling about it?

We're trying not to think about the Major. We had three LANs in a row, and we're just trying to show the best result in every LAN, and that's all, without pressure because we're playing with two stand-ins. If we lose and don't get to the Major, it's unlucky for us because of the visa issue, but if we do it, we do it.

Did you expect you'd have some good results and be so far in the race with FL4MUS and zorte?

Before the first LAN with this lineup, we only had two practice days, and we played with zorte in the previous season, so we have a good understanding of how to play together. For FL4MUS, he's sitting on the bench, so he's really motivated, and he can give us firepower.

After winning Roman Imperium Cup, beating G2 and Bestia, some really good teams, as a mix, after that, we're going to the third tournament, and the teams here are really hard, but it's a great start, and it was unexpected.

Playing with these two stand-ins would affect your lineup if you do qualify. Have you even thought about what would happen if you do qualify for the Major?

Like FL4MUS said, we didn't talk about it, but we'll talk about it if we qualify. We don't need more pressure and other things for the players.

What do you make of your run here in the lower bracket? You beat G2 in Portugal and they got revenge here. How is it now in the LB?

They got their revenge after Roman Imperium Cup, and I think it's easier to get revenge on a mix with two stand-ins, but we had some fun because we want to meet them in the grand final and play a best-of-three of best-of-threes.

You can go up against Heroic or NIP now. Any thoughts or preferences on that?

For every team here, there's a bit of pressure, but like I said before, because we're here with two stand-ins, we're trying to get the best result, and I think that for full teams, there's more pressure than us, so it's a bit easier. We should play Counter-Strike, that's all.

Having the stand-ins, you think, is helping you with not feeling the pressure that some of the other teams are feeling?

I think so, yeah!
