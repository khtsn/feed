AntyVirus retires amidst matchfixing and cheating allegations

Miłosz "⁠AntyVirus⁠" Konieczka has announced he will "leave CS" in a "final post" on X, in which he responded to allegations of matchfixing and cheating that he and his former Oramond squad were accused of following an investigation by CYBERSHOKE's captain, Daniil "⁠alpha⁠" Demin, posted by the organization's Operations Manager the previous day.

CCT confirm investigation into former Oramond squad

"I'm done and tired of stuff going on," AntyVirus said in his post. "There is no point to fight with anybody here. For past 5 years I was trying my best to leave 'underground' and become professional player and for past 5 years i'm all the time accused of cheating or other stuff."

After the initial post, the Pole had called the accusations false and said he was fully willing to cooperate with ESIC. CCT confirmed to HLTV that it had begun an investigation into the former Oramond squad, now playing as DragonClaw.

"I was always trying my best to beat allegations," AntyVirus continued, "recording of POV with OBS, camera with full visibility on me, sometimes even 2 cameras, kernel level anticheat with some secure boot type shit etc. and it was not enough to convince people at anytime."

AntyVirus also tried to refute the evidence put forward by alpha, wherein the Pole allegedly provided his and his aunt's address to receive shipment of Direct Memory Access (DMA) cheat hardware, claiming his address was known by many as "throughout many years I won a lot of cups with PC equipment stuff like keyboards, mouses, energy-drinks etc."

He also claimed voice chat evidence of him asking how to use DMA cheats, which alpha claimed was heard and verified by several Polish players including Paweł "⁠innocent⁠" Mocek and Martin "⁠zur1s⁠" Sláma, could be "created in minutes or even seconds" by AI as he is a public person with voice clips online, or that "it's not hard to ask somebody from Poland with poor accent and tell him to 'say this phrase.'"

AntyVirus is not the only member of DragonClaw who has been embroiled in controversy, with ENCE previously issuing a statement in 2025 about competitive integrity concerns ahead of a match against M1, which featured several of the same players.

In a post on X following AntyVirus' announcement, alpha stated that the same source who originally contacted him with evidence, whom he calls "Person X," told him the plan is for AntyVirus to take all the blame on himself, and implored for the investigation into the rest of the team to continue.
