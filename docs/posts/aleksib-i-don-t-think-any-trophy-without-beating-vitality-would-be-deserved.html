Aleksib: "I don't think any trophy without beating Vitality would be deserved"

The Finn believes NAVI has what it takes to reach every grand final left this season, and keeps the hope alive that a victory over Vitality is possible.

Natus Vincere came out the victors over a struggling FaZe in the Group B upper bracket semi-final at BLAST Rivals.

After the series, in-game leader Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen took some time to talk to HLTV about the feat, where he sees the team, who are second in both the HLTV and VRS rankings, in the lead-up to the Major, and Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri's impressive growth.

"I think that we definitely think that we're contenders to reach the grand final in all of the remaining tournaments," the Finn said. "The real test is always Vitality, but I do think that we have what it takes to beat any other team."

Listen to the interview here.

First day here in Fort Worth, first victory against FaZe, 2-0, but a bit hairy there at the end. FaZe are going through some stuff now, but yesterday, speaking to Twistzz, he said that FaZe vs. NAVI is always a fun match. What did you make of that opener here?

We tried our best to make the best preparation we could, thinking about their old positions on their old rosters. Where we think they're going to play on certain maps, and how they're going to approach the game, especially on CT and T.

We understand that Twistzz is the IGL, and maybe they want to utilize some players, and we were just going back and forth with what we felt was about to happen.

To be honest, the exact same things happened on Anubis, and on Ancient, so I think that went well for us. Other than that, we knew that they're a fresh team, they have stuff that maybe we couldn't prepare against, that they're going to try to play a bit different CS than they played with karrigan...

As you said, the second map got dicey, but in the end, we just showed that it's not easy to close the game against us, and once we got the money into our control, I felt like we were controlling the game.

You say it was a bit hard to make a read on a team making changes and so on. Did anything catch you off-guard or surprise you at all?

Not really. I wouldn't say anything surprised us. I just think that we had some sloppy rounds on the T side of Ancient and, to be honest, they ended up playing better in some rounds. Maybe we got surprised by that because Ancient T side for us is like a comfort zone. So they put us to the test, really, but in the end, we just managed to play a lot better on the CT side.

We're always going back to the drawing board and always trying our best to analyze our mistakes and what other teams are doing to make Vitality struggle

Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen

You're now second in both the HLTV and VRS rankings. How good of a shape do you see this NAVI in, and what potential do you have going into the final stretch of the season with the biggest tournament on the horizon?

I think that we definitely think that we're contenders to reach the grand final in all of the remaining tournaments. The real test is always Vitality, but I do think that we have what it takes to beat any other team, and I'm pretty sure we have beaten any other team when it comes down to the rest of the competition.

I'm not saying we can't lose to them; all I'm saying is that we understand as a team where we are. We're still a step behind Vitality, but we can put a match against anyone else, and that's a good thing to know because you go into these tournaments, these group stage games, playoff games, and you're feeling confident.

At the end of the day, we would be happy to lift a trophy, but I don't think any trophy without beating Vitality would be deserved, in a way. So we really want that, and we want to make ourselves feel that we deserve it.

You say you can beat anyone but Vitality, and make every final. Are they that impossible? Their presence looms so large over everybody. What do you make of that effect they have of making other teams afraid of them?

We saw that Falcons won against them, and now we saw here that in the first game, FUT took them to a very close series, and honestly, FUT could have maybe even won here today.

So we understand that other teams are making them struggle, and we understand that we have what it takes to beat them, but I'm not going out there and saying that it's easy to beat them since we haven't won a map in a while against them.

The thing is, we're always going back to the drawing board and always trying our best to analyze our mistakes and what other teams are doing to make them struggle. And you can't lose hope.

I just think that whenever we have the chance to play against them, we're going to take it and we're going to enjoy it because that's the peak of CS, that's something that I think our whole team is excited for because, as I told you, that's the real test and where you get to enjoy the best CS.

If we take this BLAST Rivals, for example, it's a tournament that Vitality have come to after winning a Grand Slam, going deep into a lot of tournaments, and being away from home a lot. Do you think that if you can get a victory against them here, it can break that image of them being a brick wall that you can't get through?

Yeah, any win against them will be like a goal achieved. But at the same time, if we make it happen at this tournament, it helps us for the next two, because we're sharing the same tournaments.

Our challenge is to bring them down and, if you manage to do that, you can be what it takes to be number one.

Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen

In the end, everything will come down to the Major, and I think that pressure-wise it will be different, even for them; they will want to keep winning and keep the streak alive, but as I told you, I think that any chance we have against them, we're going to take it, and hopefully we're going to come out on top and keep it that way.

I've been doing HLTV Prospects, and I wanted to follow up a bit with a guy like makazze because he was on it last season. Could you tell me, being the in-game leader and captain of the team, how you've seen him make progress over these months that he's been playing in tier one and stepping up and being a contributor to the team?

I think that he's doing just super. He's a hard-working guy, and he's been doing super well in the server when it comes down to group stage games, playoff games, especially, and that's a thing that as the IGL of the team, and you understand that when you go into high-pressure matches, or on the stage, you understand that your rookie will likely put up numbers that other players can't.

It's a very nice feeling, and it brings your team even more confidence. Seeing him play and laugh, and just the way he's playing situations in the game, you see that he's fearless, and he's bringing it to all of the rest of us, and that's a super nice sign for a rookie.

There are only some rookies I've ever played with that I could say the same thing when it comes down to... the first playoff he played was Cologne, and ever since that, he always wants to go back to the stage and show what he's capable of, so yeah, just happy to play with him.

You're an experienced guy, you've won some of the biggest tournaments. Looking at this team now, and comparing it to teams you've been on in the past, where do you think the potential or limit for this team is?

The potential for us is to be number one. Whenever I play on a team like this, and I know what we were capable of previously, and I know that we lifted a trophy this year — even if it wasn't against the best competition —, but we understand that we've reached grand finals this season.... It's a bit hard to think when you take everything into consideration, like we have a team in the scene that, right now, could be or is the best Counter-Strike team there ever was.

Our challenge is to bring them down and, if you manage to do that, you can be what it takes to be number one. That's our goal, that's our challenge, and I always believe in my team wherever we play, whenever we play, and you just need to keep believing and go forward.
