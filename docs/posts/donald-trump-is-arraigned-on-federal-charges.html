Donald Trump Is Arraigned on Federal Charges

Newsletter

The Evening

Also, Cormac McCarthy dies at 89. Here’s the latest at the end of Tuesday.

June 13, 2023, 5:56 p.m. ET

Former President Donald Trump pleaded not guilty in a federal court in Miami this afternoon to criminal charges related to his handling of classified documents. It was the first time in the country’s history that a president, current or former, was booked on federal charges.

Inside the courtroom, Trump — wearing a dark suit and a red tie — sat with his arms crossed at the defendant’s table while the magistrate judge overseeing the hearing described the indictment. It includes 37 counts, lodged against Trump last week by the special counsel, Jack Smith, that he kept and refused to return sensitive government documents after leaving office.

Outside the courtroom, a few hundred people — both Trump supporters and anti-Trump protesters — turned the typically mundane grounds of the courthouse into a circuslike atmosphere, with scattered arguments and chants of “Lock him up!” “We were all just sitting outside while a U.S. president — one of only a few dozen in history — was inside a federal courthouse being arraigned,” my colleague Nicholas Bogel-Burroughs said from Miami. “It was all pretty remarkable.”

Tonight, Trump is traveling to his golf club in Bedminster, N.J., where he plans to deliver a statement about his indictment. Follow along for updates.

Still, a trial could be months away: My colleague Charlie Savage said that Trump and his legal team might attempt to delay the trial until after the presidential election in November 2024. Trump is currently the leading candidate for the Republican nomination.

“It is possible that he will be president again, or that another Republican nominee will be president,” Charlie said. Once a new president takes office, he said, that person could end the case against him.

Inflation cooled to a two-year low

The hike in consumer prices slowed again in May, climbing at a 4 percent annual rate, according to data released today. That was the slowest pace in more than two years and slightly lower than expected.

But not everything about the new inflation numbers was rosy: The so-called core index, which strips out food and fuel prices, remained stubbornly high at 5.3 percent. My colleague Jeanna Smialek told me that this report “reconfirmed” a widely held prediction that the Fed will pause its rate hikes tomorrow before potentially hiking them again next month.

More data: Food prices ticked higher, but rent increases slowed. The trend of post-pandemic “revenge spending” on hotels, flights and recreation, which pushed prices higher, is shifting back to normal.

Russia targets Zelensky’s hometown

At least 11 people were killed in the central Ukrainian city of Kryvyi Rih today after Russian strikes hit a warehouse and an apartment building. The city, which is the hometown of Ukraine’s president, Volodymyr Zelensky, was already grappling with the aftermath of the Kakhovka dam disaster.

Acclaimed novelist Cormac McCarthy died at 89

Cormac McCarthy, the reclusive writer of Appalachia and the American Southwest, whose fiction took a dark view of the human condition, died today at his home in Santa Fe, N.M. His acclaimed books, which include “All the Pretty Horses,” “The Road” and “No Country for Old Men,” explore a bleak world of violence and outsiders.

Read his full obituary. Also, check out some recently uncovered interviews McCarthy gave as a younger writer, when he was less guarded than in his later years.

One last Beatles song?

More than 40 years after John Lennon was fatally shot outside his New York apartment, his voice is set to be featured in a new Beatles song.

That’s because Paul McCartney said he used a decades-old demo with Lennon’s voice — and artificial intelligence — to create a new track that would come out later this year. McCartney hasn’t announced the title of the song or offered any clues about its lyrics, but there’s speculation that the tune might be “Now and Then,” which Lennon composed and recorded as a demo in the late 1970s.

Another champion could be crowned tonight

A day after the N.B.A. championship went to the Denver Nuggets, who earned their first title in franchise history with a win over the Miami Heat, it could be the N.H.L.’s turn to hand out a trophy. The Stanley Cup could come to a close tonight with a win by the Vegas Golden Knights over the Florida Panthers — but the team from Miami is hoping to extend the series to a sixth game on Friday.

What is this? A handbag for ants?

In recent years, it has been trendy to carry around a tiny luxury purse. Some high-end handbags hardly fit more than a credit card or two inside. Now, the creative collective known as MSCHF (pronounced “mischief”) is taking that impractical trend to its limits.

The group has designed and created a specklike rendition of Louis Vuitton’s OnTheGo tote that is smaller than a grain of sea salt and narrow enough to pass through the eye of a needle. The good news? You can’t lose your keys in the Microscopic Handbag, as it’s called. The bad news? You could lose the bag itself in a light breeze.

Have an absurd evening.
