Spinx: "Everyone is suffering because of the Vitality era, every [title] you can get is important"

MOUZ made nine Big Event semi-finals in a row in 2025, yet CS Asia Championships will be only their fourth so far in 2026.

It will be their second in two events with Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius and Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze on board, however, marking an uptick in form since the mid-season changes.

Given their improvement, a weaker team list at CAC represents a rare chance for MOUZ to take a trophy home in the Vitality era and Lotan "⁠Spinx⁠" Giladi is more than aware of the opportunity.

"Everyone is suffering a bit because of the Vitality era, Spinx explained. "Not a lot of tier one teams are winning tournaments at the moment, so every one you can get is super important."

The Israeli lurker also touched on jL's future, with his trial coming to an end after Shanghai. "It's not like I decide," Spinx said, but he did call jL "a very good player" and a "super nice guy."

Listen to the interview here.

You guys are through to the semi-finals after two long days. Today you had to beat paiN and B8 back to back. Give me your thoughts.

Yeah, a lot of best-of-threes in one day. It's kind of new for some of us. For xelex it's normal because he came from tier two or tier three. Tier one, we're not so used to it, but I'm happy we managed to show some good level and stay focused throughout the day. The energy goes up, down, up, down, it's hard to play so many games in a row but I'm happy we managed to win all of them. We can get to the semis, just enjoying the moment.

He doesn't have the easiest role ever, but I think he's doing well adapting after coming back from a break

Lotan "⁠Spinx⁠" Giladi on jL

You lost against TYLOO, and then against NRG you lost map one, was that a wake up call? And why do you think the team got a slow start?

We definitely got a slow start. We lost to TYLOO, best-of-ones are really tricky, we should have played much better on this one but BO1, it's life in CS2 at the moment. Against NRG, I don't think we played our best. We had to wake up a bit, get our energy going, get our communication going, we didn't start from 100%. We managed to build it up through the tournament so far and it's been refreshing for us to go to the semis with more games.

Now Falcons, you haven't played them since Krakow, you have two different players and they have a new IGL. How are you going to approach this match, and what kind of expectations do you have for it?

We are going to approach it the same as any match. Whether it's Falcons, NRG, or Vitality, the preparation will always be the same, nothing special. Falcons are one of the best teams in the world, they made it to the final in Astana, obviously a good team with a lot of individuals who can pop off. We need to show that our individuals can pop off and just play with good team play.

After the Major, a decision needs to be made about jL. Do you think he's a a good fit for the team. Today he had this amazing 1v3 clutch that kept you in Ancient. How do you look at his performances, and his impact not only inside of the game but outside too?

I really like jL. He's a super nice guy, funny guy, he brings a good vibe and he's enjoying the moment a lot. As a player he's a very good player, he doesn't have the easiest role ever but I think he's doing well adapting after coming back from some break. What will happen after the Major, I can't tell, it's also not like I decide. It's all of us and the organization and everything. I really enjoy the company with him, I'm sure he will be good no matter where he goes, us or someone else.

It feels like the team that wins your semi-final will be the tournament favorites. How important will it be for a team like yours, with a new player like xelex, to win a tournament. It's something you guys haven't done in a while.

I think it's super important, but it's super important for any team, winning a tournament. Everyone is suffering a bit because of the Vitality era, not a lot of tier one teams are winning tournaments at the moment so every one you can get is super important. About being favorites if we beat Falcons, I think if you are in the grand final you can be a bit of a favorite but it's never a full favorite. The other team also made it to the final, they showed a good level throughout the tournament, so we will see how it goes.

And if you go all the way here, it would be a nice boost for the Major, right?

Of course, a tournament win before the Major is super important. It helped us to win the Major in Paris in 2023, when we won IEM Rio before it, it's a full confidence boost and for sure an energy boost for the Major.
