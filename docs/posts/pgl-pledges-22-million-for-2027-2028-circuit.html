PGL pledges $22 million for 2027-2028 circuit

PGL has announced its planned incentive system for 2027 and 2028, titled the "PGL CS2 Tier 1 Program."

In order to encourage teams to take part in PGL events, the tournament operator has committed to offering at least $11 million to teams each year, a hefty increase compared to around $5 million per year in 2025-2026.

Of this amount, $6 million will be allocated to the prize pool, with $3 million dedicated to players and $3 million to organizations.

The other $5 million each year will come from a Valve Regional Standings (VRS) invite bonus and an annual viewership incentive.

The former system, worth $300,000 to $400,000 per event, will reward the five highest-ranked teams, up to the top 12 of the Global VRS, who accept a VRS invitation to a PGL event.

The annual viewership incentive, similarly to ESL's current scheme, assigns points to 12 teams at each event based on the average cumulative viewership their matches bring. At the end of the year, teams will have their points multiplied by the number of PGL events they attended, with the top 16 point-earners then going on to take a portion of the $2.8 million pool.

PGL also reaffirmed its commitment to hosting six events in 2027, and at least six events in 2028, though the tournament operator noted that "additional tier one events may be added to the 2028 calendar" contingent on their growth. Previously, the TO announced eight events for 2028.
