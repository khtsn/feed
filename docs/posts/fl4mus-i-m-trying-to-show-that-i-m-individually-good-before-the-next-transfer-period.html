FL4MUS: "I'm trying to show that I'm individually good before the next transfer [period]"

BetBoom headed into IEM Atlanta with former Virtus.pro rifler Timur "⁠FL4MUS⁠" Marev in tow, a result of continued visa issues for Daniil "⁠d1Ledez⁠" Kustov as the IEM Cologne Major draws near.

FL4MUS, who has already helped BetBoom clinch a place at the Major, has now helped the team to secure a playoff berth in IEM Atlanta. The team took down B8 in a narrow opening matchup, before stunning tournament favorites Vitality 2-1 to earn their place on stage.

After his team's monumental win over Vitality, FL4MUS spoke with HLTV regarding securing a playoff berth, the thought of a potential comeback from Vitality on the Nuke decider, and the status of BetBoom's starting lineup for Cologne.

Or you can listen to the interview here.

Huge win over world number one Vitality, and you're headed to the playoffs. How are you feeling?

First of all, that was my first time to win against Vitality. I personally played them four or five times, I think this was the fifth time, and it feels really great to finally beat them at least once. Also we qualified for the playoffs, and it's going to be my first stage playoffs. Because when I was playing for VP, we went once to the playoffs, and my visa got wasted and I couldn't play in Katowice.

After that, we went for another playoff in PGL or some tournament, and the first game of the playoff wasn't in an arena, and that was going to be my first game in front of people since the Shanghai Major, so I'm really happy that it happened. I also could have been playing better, but at least I played good.

You guys looked very strong on that first half of Nuke, going up 10-2; what was going right for you?

Those things... I don't know. In my opinion, everyone was very calm, and everyone was reacting to what they see on their monitors, and telling each other what to do, creating a great plan for each other to play together. Always trying to play together, always trying to do something not stupid. Sometimes we got lucky, sometimes we just did a great move, someone did a multikill, and there weren't much mistakes on that Nuke game. That is probably the reason why we won. Everyone played fantastic individually, and that's the reason as well.

Did you start to get worried about a Vitality comeback?

I wasn't worried, but the NAVI game, when Vitality won from 1-11, it was in my head, but it's like... nah, not really. I wasn't worried about that. We just don't have pressure. If we lose, they're Vitality. If we're gonna lose, nothing's gonna happen, but if we win, everything's gonna be great.

It doesn't matter what type of team you play in, English-speaking or Russian-speaking, it matters how you will fit in the team

Timur "⁠FL4MUS⁠" Marev

Now you have won, and you're in the top six. You've beaten Vitality, the favorites for the event. Do you feel like you have a shot at winning this whole event?

I don't know, I don't like to make predictions. There is always a chance. If you're asking if there is a chance, there is. Nothing more than that I can give you.

Looking a bit bigger picture, you've bounced back and forth between English and Russian-speaking teams. Do you have a preference between the two?

I will say when I was playing in GamerLegion, I think everything stacked up pretty well. The mentality of the team, the chemistry, was working pretty well. Everything was going fine. For example, if I take my time in OG, it just didn't happen. After that time, I was thinking that it was something that I wanted to play in a Russian team or something, but this is not true. It doesn't matter what type of team you play in, English-speaking or Russian-speaking, for me personally, I don't think it matters. It matters how you will fit in the team, and how the players are going to be you, and how you're going to be to the players, and also the coaching staff, etc.

How do you feel you're fitting into this team, especially because you're just here on loan?

I cannot tell. I'm not playing in my positions, I'm just playing some anchor, just doing my work to help the guys out to achieve great results. Also, I'm just trying to show individually before the next transfer [period], just to show that I'm individually good, that I want to play, and that I can play. I think I'm doing not bad at this.

Obviously, BetBoom's starting lineup for the Major is a bit up in the air. Do you have any updates on the situation?

I'm a substitute for the Major. If someone will not get a visa, I will fly. If everyone gets a visa, I will not. I do have a visa, it just depends on the main roster, because I'm not in the main roster. If someone from the main roster is not getting a visa, then I'm going.
