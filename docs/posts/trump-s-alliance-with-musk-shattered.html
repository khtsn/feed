Trump’s Alliance With Musk Shattered

Briefing|Trump’s Alliance With Musk Shattered

https://www.nytimes.com/2025/06/05/briefing/trumps-alliance-with-musk-shattered.html

Over the last year, President Trump and Elon Musk have been among the world’s most powerful allies. Together, they worked to return Trump to the White House and radically reshape the federal government. Less than a week ago, they sang each other’s praises.

Then, today, over the course of just a few hours, the alliance between Trump and Musk dissolved into open acrimony. The two men hurled personal attacks at each other over matters both significant and petty. Even by the Trump administration’s standards, it was a remarkable moment.

The dispute ostensibly centered on the president’s signature domestic policy bill, which Musk has attacked as a “disgusting abomination,” but the mudslinging quickly escalated. During a White House meeting with Germany’s leader, Friedrich Merz, Trump criticized Musk: “I’m very disappointed in Elon,” the president said. “I’ve helped Elon a lot.”

Musk shot back at Trump: “Without me, Trump would have lost the election,” he wrote on X. “Such ingratitude.” In follow-up posts, Musk suggested that he would leave the Republican Party, accused Trump of involvement with convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein and backed a call for the president’s impeachment.

Justices backed a straight woman in a discrimination case

The Supreme Court today unanimously ruled in favor of a straight woman who twice lost positions to gay workers. The justices said that a lower court had been wrong to require her to meet a higher burden in proving workplace discrimination because she was a member of a majority group.

The U.S. and China agreed to revive trade talks

Xi Jinping, China’s leader, spoke to Trump over the phone this morning about the souring trade relationship between the two countries. It was the first time the leaders were known to have spoken directly since Trump returned to office in January. After the call, China and the U.S. said they would meet again in hopes of breaking an impasse over tariffs and global supplies of rare earth minerals.

In related news, U.S. imports of foreign made goods fell sharply in April compared with the previous month.

A study suggested an effective way to deter rhino poachers

Vast sums have been poured into militarized efforts to combat rhinoceros poaching, but it has produced little results. Instead, according to a study published today, a relatively affordable and straightforward tactic seems to be most effective at preventing their killing: removing their horns.

It takes about 18 months for the horns to grow back. The research suggested that, as a result, poaching declined 78 percent.

Want to get your hands on an Hermès Birkin bag? Good luck. The bags are so expensive and so exclusive that it is often considered folly to even try.

Do you see craters of bumps?

A Japanese spacecraft called Resilience recently captured the above photograph of the moon’s south pole. When some people look at the image, they see craters; most readers see bumps. Let us know which one you see.

This variance is known as the crater illusion. It occurs because our brains have limited information about the source and the direction of light in an image. In reality, the moon is pocked with craters, not bumps.
