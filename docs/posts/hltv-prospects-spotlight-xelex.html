HLTV Prospects spotlight: xelex

The IEM Cologne Major has hit the top 16 mark, and the best teams in the world have descended upon the city on the banks of the Rhine to fight for it all. Just about half of the 31 rookies at the Major are still alive, but only Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici and Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze (#4 HLTV Prospects) are yet to play their first match starting in Stage 3.

We sat down with MOUZ's newest addition, xelex, who just turned 18 years old and became one of the top youngsters in our latest prospect update after being called up from the academy, to learn more about him as he gets ready to make his Major debut at the most important event of his young career so far, IEM Cologne.

As far as Counter-Strike tropes go, the one of the younger brother being introduced to the game early on and excelling beyond their older siblings may be the most recurrent, and xelex's start is exactly that. "The big brother story," the young Hungarian tells HLTV succinctly.

That first contact with the game was in 2017, briefly, and it wasn't until late 2019 and early 2020, after xelex got a new pc, that he really started to put in the hours. It wasn't only Counter-Strike at first. Like Krabeni, who admitted to playing a lot of Fortnite early on, xelex was the same. " I just played a lot of games," he says, "like Fortnite, which was the trend back then."

It wasn't until the end of 2021 that xelex started to focus more on Counter-Strike. First, with the goal of reaching the Global Elite. Then, in 2022, and with that goal out of the way, the FACEIT grind began to reach Level 10. By the end of the year, he could also check that bucket list item, and then went on to reach 3,800 elo.

He gave my name to NXT, put me twice as his Bold Prediction, so I appreciate him, and I was happy both of the times I got it

xelex had no particular intentions of becoming a professional player at the time. It happened in two ways. Gradually, then suddenly. Attila Marton, who would go on to become xelex's agent, reached out proposing that he join a local team. "I tried out and liked it because it was fully different than FACEIT PUG matches," he recalls, "and that's when it started with team CS."

He played ESEA Intermediate and was enjoying his time playing on a team, but it was when Áron "⁠Aaron⁠" Homoki and Henrich "⁠sl3nd⁠" Hevesi reached out in August of 2024 that things really took off. "They wanted to play FACEIT with me and see who I am because I was top three in the Hungarian leaderboard," he recalls.

What really set xelex's career in motion came next, when torzsi was also invited to join in and play some games with them. MOUZ's AWPer didn't skip a beat and gave the youngster's name to Tobias "⁠TOBIZ⁠" Theo, who was at the time working on the new MOUZ NXT roster during a rebuild of the famed academy team.

torzsi has played a big role in helping xelex start his career

MOUZ NXT's coach wasted no time and, by September of 2024, xelex was invited to try out for the academy roster. "I was very nervous when I was trying out with NXT because I had a very bad PC," he recalls. "They didn't know I was playing with 100 FPS, maybe 120, and that's how I had to play Mid on Ancient. I got smashed in the first tryout, I remember, and then I stepped up on Anubis, and I dropped like 20-something kills."

TOBIZ admired xelex's ability to bounce back after a bad game and, after watching him play some more, signed the Hungarian player to MOUZ NXT. Then, upon finding out that he was having issues with his rig, the skipper made sure that the equipment issue was righted. "When we first started to practice Nuke, that's when I told TOBIZ, because I was going to be the Outside player, and if they broke the smokes, I'd have 90 FPS," xelex says, looking back. "So yeah, it was funny."

xelex credits much of his development to his coaches in the NXT system. First with TOBIZ, and later with Sergey "⁠lmbt⁠" Bezhanov. He also mentions the importance of the team's headquarters in Hamburg for bootcamps and the staff around the team.

It's just human feelings, you can't just not be nervous. But now, as the games go on and everything gets better and better, I'm feeling less and less nervous

Not only was torzsi instrumental in getting xelex into one of the best talent factories in Counter-Strike, torzsi's patronage also included back-to-back shoutouts when he was named a Top 20 Player of the Year in 2024 and 2025, making xelex his Bold Prediction twice. "I think he will get his chance sooner or later because he has a lot of potential," torzsi said in 2024. "He is getting better and better in MOUZ NXT, and I am excited about his development," he added in 2025. "I think he has a great future in front of him if he keeps working and improving!"

The 18-year-old doesn't take such help for granted. "I appreciate it so much," he says. "He helped me get into the CS scene. He gave my name to NXT, put me twice as his Bold Prediction, so I appreciate him, and I was happy both of the times I got it. I was just happy, to be honest, that I could see myself there. When you're a guy who has been watching CS for a few years, when you see yourself there, it's a different kind of feeling."

xelex got his first taste of top-tier Counter-Strike at the beginning of 2025 when he was called up to the main team as a stand-in when Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek was benched and, due to IEM Katowice roster restrictions, Lotan "⁠Spinx⁠" Giladi was not yet allowed to join the team. "If you just check the event, zero maps won, so it wasn't the best, but it was good," he reminisces.

"I felt nervous because it's a fully different level of CS," he adds. "It's the same, LAN, but you want to prove yourself and be good, so you put a kind of pressure on yourself. You don't want to do that, but it's hard, it's just human feelings, you can't just not be nervous. But now, as the games go on and everything gets better and better, I'm feeling less and less nervous. It will go away with time, and it will be better, for sure."

xelex quickly became the academy team's golden boy, blending a mix of aggression on the CT side with late-round situational play on the T side, and averaging a 1.18 rating to show for it. Even then, he didn't always feel like he was going to get the call-up to the main team until recently, admitting that he was starting to feel stagnant in the academy roster.

It's a full switch for me because I have 9,600 hours in the game, but never played them at a high level, so it's a new story, new opportunities and challenges for me, and I'm looking forward to it

"NXT was kind of set up for me, like endrounds, afterplants, that kind of thing, he says. "I don't want to sound cocky, but I think I was the best on that team in the late rounds, winning the afterplants, so it wouldn't make sense to just send me in first to just die." That's the way the system was built, and it worked.

"I didn't expect it, to be honest," xelex says of the sudden way in which he was called up before PGL Astana. "I thought it would be after the Major that I could maybe get a chance on the main team. Maybe even in winter. It just came in a very surprising way and, of course, I was shocked and very happy that I could already play the Cologne Major with them. It's crazy to say, even. I was fully surprised and in shock — very happy about it."

Now, he's facing a new challenge as he adapts to a new map and new Anchor positions that he wasn't used to playing in the academy. "I hate that map," he says of Inferno. "It's just not for me. It's all about util, and I just don't like it. But I'm open to new positions, it's a full switch for me because I have 9,600 hours in the game, but never played them at a high level, so it's a new story, new opportunities and challenges for me, and I'm looking forward to it. To max it out as I did in the other positions. I want to be very good at it and maybe even the best."

The two-way player xelex was on NXT with aggressive CT sides and high clutching on T side is no longer the mold, as his opening attempts in his first two events with the main team have seen the numbers go from 23% to 15.7%, while his clutch points have decreased from 1.02 to 0.35. If his dopelganger was Benjamin "⁠blameF⁠" Bremer before, in the top right of the above scatterplot, his role is much different now.

So far, xelex has had two runs at PGL Astana and CS Asia Championship, with a discrete 1.01 rating at the first and a much higher 1.18 at the second. At these two events, he's had the chance to play against some of the teams he will potentially face in Cologne, such as G2, 9z, Aurora, Spirit, B8, or Falcons.

He is also interested in playing Vitality. "I want to see what this is all about," he says. "I want to see how they play. I played them twice with NXT, but it's a different thing because you're playing in an academy, and it's fully different. I really want to see how we would play them now with this roster after some time to practice, and we'll be getting closer to each other in a way. I want to see how we can play against them because, who knows, maybe we can beat them."

The #4 prospect's optimism may clash with expectations as MOUZ enter the event with Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin back from the bench during a transitional period before the off-season, but he already has some goals set for the future ahead. "To be as consistent as I can," he says. "To reach a point where I can say that I can at least keep between a 1.10 and 1.15 rating consistently, and be there every single time for my team and never let them down.

"Of course, there will be days when it just doesn't work out, but I want to achieve the most consistent level that I can. That's the main goal for me, my individual goal. Maybe get some trophies, that would be nice, but my main goal is to stay as consistent as I can, and then I will probably make some other ones."
