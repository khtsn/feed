jL: "I should theoretically be back [to playing] in the summer if everything works out"

Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius is shaking off the rust from missing the last nine months of competition as he is playing for Liquid at the Roman Imperium Cup VI as a stand-in for Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz, who missed the event due to travel issues.

Earlier on Friday, the Israeli star joined G2 in a swap deal that will see Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa move in the opposite direction.

jL sat down with HLTV before the start of Liquid's group stage run to talk about life on NAVI's bench, his IEM Krakow analyst stint, and his goals for the future. He revealed that he expects to be back in competition in the summer "if everything is correct and it works out."

"If that doesn't work out, I'll stick to streaming, creating content, and keeping myself informed like I am right now," he added.

jL went on to put in a 1.24 rating as Liquid scraped through to the playoffs in Vila Nova de Gaia after a two-way tie with BESTIA for second place.

You're playing on LAN for the first time since June. What have you been up to during this hiatus?

I've been getting to know myself, be a better husband, learn how to cook, learn how to manage my time a little bit better, I got a puppy, a lot of things happened. I learned drifting... Of course, I'm not the best, but I know how to drift. I've been getting into cars. There are a lot of hobbies I've tried out that I couldn't while I was a pro.

You went on the desk at IEM Krakow, a tough job, and you were critical of yourself. After some time to sit on it, what are your biggest takeaways from that experience?

The number one takeaway is to not judge myself in the moment and not compare myself to other analysts. My best work doesn't come from being the best analyst. My best work comes from being a funny dude, giving some insights that some people haven't heard before... Because I'm a pro and should give those behind-the-scenes insights, but I just analyzed the most obvious things.

We already had YNk to do that, and dupreeh was also really good at analyzing what people see. Of course, he was giving some behind-the-scenes, and I think that I lacked a lot of relatable stuff to the community with the pro scene. That was my biggest takeaway. I should not criticize myself and not compare myself to other analysts.

This hiatus from playing has now gone for over half a year. Have you come to any conclusions on what you want your next steps to be professionally?

I don't want to get into too much detail, but I should theoretically be back in the summer if everything is correct and it works out. Then I should be back in tier-1, but if that doesn't work out I'll stick to streaming, creating content, and keeping myself informed like I am right now. I don't feel like I've been away from the game or not at my peak performance.

Of course, I'm not playing my best; I'm not in my 2024 or 2025 version, I'm a little bit below that, but you value yourself by the competition you're playing against, and if I'm not playing against tier-1 opponents, I cannot give myself a fair assessment, but right now I feel like I'm in good shape and I'll keep it that way.

What have you missed the most and enjoyed the most during your time away from the server?

Number one is time with my wife. Today we're actually celebrating six years. Not really celebrating, I'm in Portugal helping my boys in Liquid, but then also doing hobbies that may seem dangerous when you're a pro, where you can hurt your hands, or just exploring myself, like learning how to cook, how to schedule time, or how to assemble IKEA furniture.

There are a lot of things you miss as a pro player because either somebody else does it for you or you just don't pay attention to it. You save our time because you know you have to practice or deathmatch, so you'll throw that burden on someone else and just focus on the game. I feel like as a pro player you have to do that to be the best, but on the other hand, I want to try to live a normal life.

We were playing a 10v10 CS2 match with content creators and mid-game flashie messaged me like, 'SOS, SOS, SOS'

How did the offer to play with Liquid come about? What was your thought process in accepting this gig?

We were playing a 10v10 CS2 match with content creators and mid-game flashie messaged me like "SOS, SOS, SOS," so I answered him and he told me that they need a stand in because Guy [NertZ] couldn't make it to his flight, so I was like, 'OK, let me think about it, let me speak to my wife about it, and I'll give you an answer.' That same day, I gave him the answer that we're good to go, we got the green light from NAVI, and that's how it all happened.

First of all, I wanted to play again competitively. It's not tier-1, of course, but I'm still very happy to be here. I'm enjoying the LAN so far. Shoutout to the SAW guys, it's a very beautiful arena. They have good PCs, good monitors, and it doesn't feel a lot different from a tier-1 LAN besides not having practice rooms. I'm enjoying it. There are people watching the games, even the group stage games, and for me it's a very positive experience.

Regarding Liquid, do you think it could turn into something long-term?

No, no. Not now. I mean, theoretically, maybe you could, but no.

What is it like to see flashie again and hang out with him? What was your relationship with him like in NAVI?

It was amazing. Our relationship was more of best friends, he's a very close friend of mine. Back then, in NAVI, he was the analyst, but now he's the coach, he's the big boss, so I have to give him some more respect. I can't call him names anymore, I gotta respect the coach...
