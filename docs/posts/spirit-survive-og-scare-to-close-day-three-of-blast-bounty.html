The biggest team in action on the third day of BLAST Bounty was Spirit, and the current top-ranked team in the global VRS were pushed all the way by a valiant OG squad.

Cache was an entirely one-sided affair in favour of Spirit but OG came roaring back on Dust2, taking advantage of a turgid offense by Spirit to head into halftime with a 7-5 lead.

adamb destroys Spirit with ballsy push

Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets began to activate on Spirit's defense to give his team more of a foothold in the map, but a combination of some poorly organized Spirit retakes and excellent calls by OG were enough to send the series to a decider.

It seemed normal service had been resumed on Ancient as Spirit raced out to a comfortable 9-3 lead off the back of their CT side, only for Adam "⁠adamb⁠" Ångström to come alive in spectacular fashion in the second half, an audacious push out of A the highlight of a stellar performance that helped push the game into OT.

OG kept the game alive into OT with well-coordinated mid-rounds and some vital multi-frags by Casper "⁠cadiaN⁠" Møller and adamb, but Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich's late-map revival and some improved offensive play by Spirit were just enough to keep the upset at bay.

The upset of the day came in the very first game as FOKUS took down Aurora to eliminate the new-look international roster.

FOKUS stun Aurora to eliminate them from BLAST Bounty

FOKUS were sporting a revamped squad themselves, having recently brought in Paavo "⁠podi⁠" Heiskanen and Jacob "⁠jocab⁠" Nerheden, and they picked up a statement victory that included a crushing 13-4 win on the decider of Dust2.

FUT ensured a winning start to life with xfl0ud amongst their number by defeating SINNERS in 2-1 fashion, recovering from a loss on Dust2 by sweeping their opponents aside on Ancient and Mirage.

xfl0ud was the player of the game in his first outing for FUT, the Turkish rifler putting up an outrageous 2.24-rated performance on Ancient before also top-fragging on the decider of Mirage.

The performance of their new man will be pleasing for FUT and in particular coach András "⁠coolio⁠" Fercsák. coolio spoke on broadcast after the match and revealed that the decision to part ways with Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea was "50-50[...] it's not only [that] he wanted to leave." The 26-year-old stated that the team felt they "were missing a guy who was speaking in the middle of the map," and that xfl0ud was "able to communicate more."

paiN picked up the other victory of the day in Bounty, getting past a Gentle Mates squad sporting Joey "⁠CRUC1AL⁠" Steusel, but Spanish side Gentle Mates could have made the series closer had they capitalized on the impact of their stand-in on Inferno.

CRUC1AL picked up a pistol ace and further multi-kills early on in the second-half of the opening map, giving his team a chance to build some momentum, but they were unable to capitalize and eventually slipped to a meek series defeat on Nuke.
