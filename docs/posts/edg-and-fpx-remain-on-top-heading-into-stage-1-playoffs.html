Notably, FunPlus Phoenix are an undefeated 5-0 after beating EDG 2-1 in the regular season. That was EDG's only loss, finishing the regular season at 4-1. The region was top-heavy in Stage 1, as the top two teams finished with a +50 round differential or better. Three different teams qualified for playoffs with a negative round differential.

Three teams qualified with three wins, with Nova Esports as the only playoff team with a losing 2-4 record.

Due to the parity of Stage 1, the best match was between EDward Gaming and FunPlus Phoenix in week four. Each team dominated a map, showing off how these teams seem to be in a different tier in comparison to the rest of the region. It began with a 13-4 on Breeze for EDG, a close 13-11 win for FPX on Bind, and a 13-4 for FPX on Ascent to close it out.

Qu "Life" Donghao (瞿东豪) had one of his best VCT games so far, dropping 58 kills across the series. Not only that, but he had 48 of those kills in the final two maps. While his teammate Kale "Autumn" Dunne played Yoru on Breeze, Life played Yoru on Ascent and completely shut down EDG. He ended that map with 339 ACS, the highest across all players in the series.

This series also proved a key weakness for EDG: predictability. Despite the potential of the roster, EDG ran the default Ascent composition with Jett, KAY/O, Killjoy, and Omen. FPX countered everything EDG threw at them with Viper, Yoru, and Fade to disrupt any planned executes.

FPX has always been creative with their ideas, but this win over EDG proved that it was more than just a gimmick if they could execute it right. Now both of these teams will have to take their momentum and get to Shanghai though the playoffs.

For standout players in the region, there will always be one duelist that stands out in China. Zheng "ZmjjKK" Yongkang (郑永康), better known as KangKang, remains a top duelist in the world. His ability to play both Jett and Raze at a high level while flexing over to the KAY/O when needed is crucial to their map pool. His stats in China were absurd, with an average of 285.9 ACS, a 1.42 K:D ratio, and one kill per round. EDG rightly design their composition to play around KangKang, a strategy that has netted them regional success.

At the same time, two players from FPX deserve their respect after helping the team go undefeated and topple EDG. First off, Life has proven himself one of the few duelists who can fight KangKang blow for blow, second in ACS at 259.3 and a 1.30 K:D.

Another sneaky improvement that was key to FPX was Liang "Lysoar" Youhao (梁优好). His ACS might not be as high as the two duelists higher on this list, but his K:D of 1.54 led the region. As a controller for FPX, he also finished fourth in KPR at 0.84. Statistically, he was the best regional controller main in Stage 1, able to stay alive longest and get plenty of impactful kills across their matches.

The top three teams from both groups qualified to the Stage 1 playoffs.

Prize Distribution

Each team gets a circuit point per match win.

  • 7th-8th = Bilibili Gaming - 2 points
  • 7th-8th = TYLOO - 2 points
  • 9th-10th = Wolves Esports - 2 points
  • 9th-10th = Titan Esports Club - 2 points
  • 11th = JD Gaming - 1 point

All regions have a playoff format where the top teams from each group receive a bye to the double elimination portion of the tournament. The first round between the other teams is single elimination, where the winners move to the double elimination bracket.

This means Trace Esports will face against All Gamers, and Dragon Ranger Gaming will face Nova Esports. The respective winners will face FPX and EDG, where the loser of the first lower bracket round will be the final team not to make Shanghai.

The VCT China 2024 Stage 1 playoff format.

FPX and EDG have two chances each to make Shanghai, but one still can fall out if they go winless. The sole winner of the tournament will receive an extra three circuit points, but the top three all make it to Masters Shanghai.
