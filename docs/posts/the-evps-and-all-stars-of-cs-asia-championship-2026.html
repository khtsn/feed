The EVPs and All-Stars of CS Asia Championship 2026

kyousuke earns back-to-back EVPs for the first time as a Falcons player.

Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto is a huge reason why Legacy are being christened LegaCAC.

He was MVP and champion in 2025, and was even better this year as he put Falcons to the sword in the grand final. He was the player of the map four times in the playoffs out of nine maps, where he averaged a 1.40 rating.

After making the top 30 last year, his sights will be set on getting into the top 20 for the first time and he has set himself a springboard to do just that.

While CAC is only a medium event it still matters, and latto's 1.30 rating under new coach Alan "⁠adrrr⁠" Riveros is a hell of a warning ahead of the IEM Cologne Major.

Radar Chart Stat Glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • Swing in RW Δ: round swing in round wins

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin

After 10 months without an EVP, kyousuke has two in the space of a few weeks after taking his playoff game to the next level.

How much credit Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen deserves for that is debatable, but the numbers speak for themselves. kyousuke had a 1.03 playoff rating under Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski, and after two events sits at a 1.25 in the new era.

A switch has flipped, and kyousuke was by far Falcons' best arena player at CAC with a 1.41 rating in the grand final and 1.15 in the semi.

Such a strong playoffs, and 1.07 KPRW, meant he was not a million miles off his first MVP. The task is now to sustain this form as the pressure ramps up for the Cologne Major.

Felipe "⁠insani⁠" Yuji

insani, even without being in the final, gets immense credit for CAC after a mindboggling 1.40 rating en route to fourth place for MIBR.

He posted 1.24 KPRW, a 1.76 rating in map wins, and 98.2 ADR over the event to ease his way to a third career EVP.

A 3.28 rating against B8 was his best map of all time and the new record at MVP events since the start of CS:GO. Considering his +21.4% Swing and 164 ADR, that's a record he's likely to keep for a while.

But it was not his only insane peak; PARIVISION were subjected to a 199.9 ADR first half in the round prior as insani went on to a 2.38 rating there too.

After putting up a fight in semis, he cooled down for the MOUZ third-place decider but it was still a confidence booster and a half ahead of Cologne's Stage 1.

Eduardo "⁠dumau⁠" Wolkmer

dumau slots in behind insani for his first EVP of the year having come close at IEM Atlanta.

He had solid impact in both map wins (1.28 rating) and playoffs (1.15 rating), while 107 ADR in round wins is only just behind latto.

The last two maps of the grand final were his playground, as he starred on home map Mirage and snuffed out any hope of a Falcons comeback with his 4k running up Mid on Dust2.

Together, he and latto rule this roost.

Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman

This in-game leading business has done wonders for xertioN's form, as he joins kyousuke in earning back-to-back EVPs for the first time this year.

The gap behind him was smaller than in Astana as other players chipped in, but xertioN was still player of the map five times in Shanghai, including both maps of the quarter-final against B8.

38% of his maps ended above the 1.60 mark, the highest of anyone at the event, which demonstrates just how frequently he delivered map-winning peaks.

A quiet semi-final prevented him from going any higher, but it was more than enough for his third EVP of the year.

Usukhbayar "⁠910⁠" Banzragch

910 has been the shining light in an otherwise dreary year for The MongolZ, and did so again here despite a quarter-final exit.

He contributed to all five series with a map over the 1.28 mark in every match The MongolZ played, a level of consistency that was crucial in the team even making playoffs.

+3.08% swing is the highest of any player that made it to playoffs, as was his +0.22 kill differential per round, so a team-high 1.09 rating in the quarter-finals was enough to confirm his EVP.

Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov

m0NESY is the final EVP, having led Falcons in groups with a 1.30 rating and just squeezing over the line in the arena.

Two maps were key to his case, Dust2 against MOUZ where he posted a +7.43% swing in the 9-3 first half, and his 22-9 K-D Nuke (1.34 rating) in the grand final's opener.

That was enough to get him to EVP status after three great peaks in groups, but there is no doubt that m0NESY left China disappointed after a 1.00 K/D in the last three maps of the event as he lost his eighth grand final in a row.

Honorable mentions

Vinicius "⁠n1ssim⁠" Pereira showed a good floor in China, and had a great semi-final, but was held back from an EVP by a 1.10 rating in map wins.

Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač also had a great semi-final, and was closest to an EVP out of the honorable mentions, but he needed more than that after a fairly quiet group stage to reach EVP status and then had a disastrous 0.85 rating in the grand final.

Young gun Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze also just missed out after posting 1.00 KPRW and a good arena game against Falcons, but fell short due to only having three true peaks (he was bottom of his team in the 9-3 first half of Overpass in the third-place decider).

Andrii "⁠npl⁠" Kukharskyi had a ridiculous 1.33 KPRW but was below those three, after middling series against The MongolZ and MOUZ scuppered his chances having had a great start.

Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali's 1.25 rating was of little solace to a PARIVISION squad in free fall, but he still deserves a shoutout.

All-Star

Several openers made it to EVP status but kyousuke's great final means he takes the opener spot over insani, xertioN, and dumau.

910 and m0NESY both had decent cases to be the event's best AWPer, but we plumped for the consistency of the Mongolian.

n1ssim and MVP latto will play as wingers like they do in Legacy, the former taking the anchor spot easily over René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen and Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius.

Finally, Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan takes up the mantle as in-game leader after the second notable LAN victory of his career, overcoming a tough start to his Legacy career at the perfect time.
