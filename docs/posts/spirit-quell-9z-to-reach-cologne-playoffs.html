Spirit quell 9z to reach Cologne playoffs

After only getting four rounds against Spirit in Stage 2, 9z put up a valiant effort in the rematch.

Spirit are the second team to secure a place in the playoffs of the IEM Cologne Major, capping off their flawless Stage 3 run with a win over 9z. With this result, Spirit have managed to reach the Lanxess Arena with an immaculate 6-0 record across Stages 2 and 3, dropping just two maps along the way.

Spirit and 9z previously clashed in the 2-0 pool in Stage 2, albeit with a dramatically different outcome. There, Spirit embarrassed 9z, as Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets posted a jaw-dropping 2.55 series rating, allowing the South Americans to claim just four rounds across two maps.

"I feel like that this one was probably one of the hardest games of my life," Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev said after the game. "They played super impressively. They fucked us for all the mistakes we did, a huge amount of mistakes, they were capitalizing on it. Crazy game."

"There was a low amount of faith in me; it was extremely difficult, and it felt like I was trolling the game super hard. Overall, we managed to somehow win, and I have no idea how. How is this possible? I felt like I lost three maps today."

Spirit roared ahead on the CT side of 9z's pick of Overpass, quickly accruing a 9-3 lead despite missing out on the opening pistol round. Despite their stellar start to the map, Spirit struggled to find much headway on the offense, with a donk multikill earning them one of the three rounds they managed to earn on the T side.

All five of 9z's members were finding output in the second half of Overpass, as compared to just donk and Dmitry "⁠sh1ro⁠" Sokolov for Spirit. The South Americans fought all the way back to 12-12, and with the momentum firmly on their side managed to lock down their pick 16-12.

Riding the high of their Overpass comeback, 9z hit the ground running on Spirit's pick of Mirage, cruising to a 9-3 scoreline on the defense. Though they snagged the second pistol to reach double-digit rounds, 9z allowed the game to slip from their grasp in the second half, as Spirit began piecing together a comeback.

9z managed to reach series point, but back-to-back high-impact rounds from Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich allowed Spirit to force overtime, where they soon stormed ahead to a 16-13 win to force the Dust2 decider.

Neither side was able to run away with the game in the first half of Dust2, but 9z managed to break away from the 6-6 halftime score with an 11-6 lead on the defense off the back of a pistol victory and conversion. The South Americans' momentum stalled once again, however, as they garnered just one more round while Spirit clawed their way back into the map, forcing overtime. A 1v2 from Franco "⁠dgt⁠" Garcia gave 9z a lifeline, but it did not stop Spirit from completing their comeback 16-14 and taking the series 2-1.
