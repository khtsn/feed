Betclic part ways with Bogdan

The Polish coach is open to a new challenge after two years with the organization.

Betclic have announced the dismissal of head coach Jędrzej "⁠Bogdan⁠" Rokita, ending a long association that stretched back to August 2024, when the Portuguese/Polish organization entered the game.

During Bogdan's tenure, Betclic most notably qualified for PGL Bucharest 2025, where they managed to beat The MongolZ and Astralis in a 9th-11th place finish.

The Polish team managed to bounce back from the departures of Jakub "⁠jcobbb⁠" Pietruszewski and Milan "⁠hypex⁠" Polowiec and won Parken Challenger Championship Season 2 in March before their form dipped as the season wore on.

Betclic started cleaning house last month with the departure of Bartosz "⁠eskyy⁠" Tomaszewski. The organization then cut ties with Piotr "⁠nawrot⁠" Nawrocki, who had been part of the team's coaching staff since April, benched Adrian "⁠Dr3nquu⁠" Wrześniewski and released AWPer Olek "⁠hades⁠" Miskiewicz and analyst Michał "⁠misho⁠" Staniszewski.

The Polish team now has:

Patryk "⁠Demho⁠" Tomaszewski

Adrian "⁠Dr3nquu⁠" Wrześniewski (benched)
