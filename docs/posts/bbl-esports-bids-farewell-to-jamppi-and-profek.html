BBL Esports bids farewell to Jamppi and PROFEK

BBL Esports BBL Esports Europe Rank #5 sociablEE Volkan Yonal LewN Burak Alkan has parted ways with Elias "Jamppi" Olkkonen and PROFEK.

Both players first joined BBL for the 2025 season as part of the Turkish organization's first international roster, with Jamppi making his debut as an official in-game leader. Previously, Jamppi played for Team Liquid Team Liquid Europe Rank #6 nAts Ayaz Akhmetshin kamo Kamil Frąckowiak for almost four years, while PROFEK was one of the most promising rookie Controller talents in EMEA after an impressive performance with GoNext Esports GoNext Esports Inactive demek Dawid Madrak Czester Nikolas Żarów Kozok Kamil Kozioł NeFi Filip Pawlak in Challengers League East.

The new BBL lineup demonstrated themselves as a potential contender early on, achieving the organization's best-ever results in VCT EMEA. They began their season with a top-six finish in Kickoff and later came one win away from qualifying to Masters Toronto with a fourth-place result in Stage 1.

In the Stage 2 playoffs, BBL once again found themselves one win away from international qualification in the lower final against GIANTX, where only the winner would be awarded the final slot at Champions Paris. With the stakes higher than ever, a 0-3 heartbreak loss once again barred the squad from the global stage despite them placing third in the bracket due to FNATIC and Team Heretics both having more Championship points.

Jamppi and PROFEK are the second and third active players to depart BBL after Martin "MAGNUM" Penkov departed earlier this month, signaling another fresh start ahead for the team.
