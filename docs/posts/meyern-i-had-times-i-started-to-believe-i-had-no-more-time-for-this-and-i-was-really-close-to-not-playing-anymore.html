meyern: "I had times I started to believe I had no more time for this, and I was really close to not playing anymore"

9z erupted after a convincing win over The MongolZ put them through to playoffs after two failed attempts

HLTV spoke to Ignacio "⁠meyern⁠" Meyer after 9z completed their dream run to the IEM Cologne Major playoffs, making history as the first Latin American team to make it to the top-eight stage.

The Argentine, who is one of the first two players from his country to advance to Major quarter-finals along with Luciano "⁠luchov⁠" Herrera, talked about what it means for him and the team.

"I'm so happy. It was tough days. Everyone was so strong mentally. We had a tough loss against Spirit, we had the match in our hands, and it was really difficult for us to come back the next day," he said. "But today was different."

"It means a lot for me. I had some times where I started to believe I had no more time for this, and I was really close to not playing anymore," the AWPer went on. "Luckily, I still believed in myself and I had this opportunity to play in this team. I have friends beside me and I'm living a dream right now."

Congratulations. Tell me your first impressions after making the playoffs here.

I'm so happy. It was tough days. Everyone was so strong mentally. We had a tough loss against Spirit, we had the match in our hands, and it was really difficult for us to come back the next day. But today is different. I think everyone reset, and I'm so happy with this.

As you say, after Spirit, it was so close and you could have closed it there, and then you had a difficult loss to Aurora the next day. What was the talk like behind the scenes to make sure that you guys are ready for today?

We talked about a lot of things. Yesterday, it was a lot more in the game. We failed in a lot of things, more micro, and we also had things in the mental... We didn't reset at all. Today we had a good sleep, we were in a good mood, and everyone wanted this so much. I think the most important thing was that we stayed together and everyone played really good today.

You showed some great Counter-Strike throughout this stage, beating Vitality, almost beating Spirit, now beating another top team in The MongolZ. You come in as this pretty inexperienced team. What was different about this stage, this tournament, to be able to hit such a high level?

We knew we had the level to compete against these teams, but we weren't ready to have the same consistency as them. I think when we won some matches and knew we can do it, everyone stopped thinking about the opponent. We just focus on our thing and try to get better and better day by day. Now we know we can beat this type of teams and we want more. We know we can get more results.

You're the first Argentine players to make it to Major playoffs along with luchov. What does that mean to you?

It means a lot for me. I had some times where I started to believe I had no more time for this, and I was really close to not playing anymore. Luckily, I still believed in myself and I had this opportunity to play in this team. I have friends beside me and I'm living a dream right now (laughs).

You're saying you want more. The playoffs are ahead, where there's going to be a big stage, big pressure. How far do you think you can go?

First of all, we want to enjoy this. We're playing good CS, so we don't have overthink. We need to play our game, still focus on the things we are talking about every day, and believe. We have to believe because the CS we're playing is good and we can beat everyone.
