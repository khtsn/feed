Live updates from IEM Cologne Major semi-finals

René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen briefly chatted with Eefje "⁠sjokz⁠" Depoortere ahead of his team's semifinal against Spirit to share some thoughts.

On ending Vitality game late: "It was a rough night's sleep, really. Your body is full of caffeine and adrenaline, so it was tough to fall asleep. But I'm all good, and I think everyone is feeling the same. If you don't have energy for a Major semifinal, something is wrong."

On karrigan's leadership: "He makes everyone put the team first, the team above everything. If we have a bad game individually with any of these superstars, we have to be able to look past it and help the team as much as we can. I think this is helping our bottom level right now. Every game we play is really close, and he's there, mentally strong, making us feel like that, and pulling us over these lines every single time. Everyone just has a lot of belief in him."

On his recent performance: "I'm really happy. karrigan came into the team and put direct lines on how he wants to use me. I have a very good understanding of what he wants and how to utilize myself in this roster. It's very helpful to me, and I feel I've developed a lot in this team, and I'm just trying to make myself the best for this team possible. Right now, it's going pretty well, I would say, but I have to keep it up."

Falcons in-game leader Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen took a second to speak with Eefje "⁠sjokz⁠" Depoortere on the broadcast heading into his team's semi-final against Spirit.

On anti-stratting Vitality: "It's always a balance of how much you want to anti-strat. Vitality are really good at what they do, and even when we have advantages, they came back in the end on Dust2 winning 3 on 5. Of course, there was some preparation; we had a few days, and we were well-prepared for the maps that came along in the BO3."

On the impact of the day ending late: "It was very late when I got back to the hotel, prepping a little bit before going to bed. I like to prep the day before, but I had to do some extra prep this morning, but also making sure to not overprep and just continuing our streak. I think we found a good balance for today."

On Spirit matchup: "We knew the Astana final would be tough; the way Spirit played in Astana was great. We had a close map against them on Dust2, it was a very fun game, the first map of the final. I think we are more prepared now; we are a team that will learn from every game we play, every win and also every loss we have. After yesterday, it's always going to be interesting, and I think we're in for a really good game."

Ahead of his semi-final series against Falcons, Spirit captain Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev briefly caught up with Tres "⁠stunna⁠" Saranthus on the broadcast.

On G2 game: "This one was kinda hard, not going to lie. I thought that the 9z series was the hardest I've played, but there's always something harder in the future, like the G2 match. We have shown the poor CT side, but on T side we kinda caught the momentum, and when we made the game towards the score like 8-8, we were playing just fine. In overtime, I feel like it went down to the experience, because the game is getting exhausting for both teams, and it depends on who is capable of keeping their cool."

On Falcons matchup: "I don't want a 25-22 game again, but we have to give our best to save Counter-Strike."

Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli spoke with Eefje "⁠sjokz⁠" Depoortere after the match and gave his thoughts on the series and his takeaways from the Major.

On what went wrong: "A lot of things, to be honest. We didn’t show anything. We played very poorly. I’m very heartbroken for that. I want to apologize to everyone here, our fans, our loved ones. We didn’t fight. This is what makes me very sad. It’s not the face that we showed. This is our demons. The way we played was our demons. Today, the demons beat the angel."

On his thoughts about the team's semi-final run at the Major: "As a five-Turkish team, we came as far as we could at this Major. We tried our best. We sometimes showed good Counter-Strike; sometimes not. I really gave my best today. It was not enough. I want to congratulate FURIA; they played really well today. That’s the end of the story. We will see for the future."

While at the desk, Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo also gave his thoughts on the impact of a third Cologne victory for his legacy.

On whether it will be hard for others to match his legacy if he wins his third Cologne: "Definitely. Of course it's cool, with the statistics, but for me, what’s more important is to bring another team to the top. That’s much more important to me. Having the chance to play a final with yuurih and KSCERATO… winning a Major is reaching the top of the competition. Everyone dreams of this. This is the tournament people remember. I have been playing this game for a long time, right? What do people say? ‘FalleN has two Majors.’ That’s what they say. That’s what matters the most.

"It will be a hell of a match. We will have Falcons or Spirit coming in. What can I say? Those teams are super strong. What they delivered yesterday was incredible. Hopefully, we’re going to be able to deliver a great match as well."

Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo stopped by the analyst desk and gave his thoughts on FURIA's current patch of form and what the team did to bounce back after a series of mixed results.

On whether this was the best version of FURIA of the event so far: Definitely. We played very good CS today. It was not easy. There were some key moments that brought the match back to us. We were losing Dust2, then I gave KSCERATO a hero AK, he gets four kills, and then we were managing to find those mid-round situations. So many entries from YEKINDAR as well. The whole team was playing very well. We are playing the Counter-Strike that made us win last year. A Counter-Strike where we're connecting the dots very fast, with a good understanding of how we can help each other. I feel like we're playing good CS right now.

On the team's improvements throughout the season: I have been playing for so long that I can identify when you actually suffer from success. I have seen that in many teams I have been through. It’s just something that happens. And I guess we suffered a bit from that at the beginning of the year. A bit of a mentality shift, some point of views that you had to deal with on a daily basis. And I think go over that, we learned some lessons from that and we could recover, play the way we want to play and only focus on the game. It was important to go through this stage, to grow and create more chemistry. Now it’s paying off, and we’re getting to the same spot we were in last year.

In a post-match interview, Mareks "⁠YEKINDAR⁠" Gaļinskis said that FURIA have their sights on winning the Cologne Major for Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo, who recently announced that he will retire from the game at the end of the year.

"When he announced his retirement, it was a very sad day, not only for Brazilian CS or for our camp. It was a sad day for Counter-Strike. Of couse, a man of his figure deserves to go out with a bang. It would be great, it would be amazing. He would be the first to win Cologne three times. That's our goal."
