sScary returns to competitive play under FunPlus Phoenix banner

FunPlus Phoenix FunPlus Phoenix China Rank #14 BerLIN Zhang Bolin (張柏霖) Autumn Kale Dunne Life Qu Donghao (瞿东豪) AAAAY Zhang Yang (张杨) sScary Nutchapon Matarat has announced the signing of Nutchapon "sScary" Matarat, marking the Thai star's return to competition after he previously retired in January due to a shoulder injury.

Before his retirement, sScary was a longstanding veteran of the Southeast Asia scene, attending multiple Masters and Champions events across 2021 and 2022 as part of X10 Esports X10 Esports Inactive Vera Rodman Yap Tempz Pierre Heng RedKoh Danial Hakim wayne Wayne Chang Egoist Javier Chua and XERXIA Esports XERXIA Esports Asia-Pacific Rank #22 .

In 2023, sScary made the jump to BLEED BLEED Inactive in Challengers Malaysia/Singapore. The team put up a dominant regional performance, only losing one match the entire year before moving on to Pacific Ascension, where sScary played a crucial role in BLEED winning the tournament and becoming the first team to ascend to VCT Pacific in the partnerships era.

sScary's legendary 1v4 clutch sealed the first map in BLEED's Ascension grand final victory.

Despite their dominance in Challengers, BLEED struggled in VCT, undergoing multiple roster moves and only securing one win over the course of the year in Stage 1. BLEED was later removed from VCT during the offseason; sScary soon issued an LFT announcement but later retired.

sScary now makes his return to VCT with FPX in his first foray into VCT China. He reunites with his former coach Nikola "LEGIJA" Ninić from his time on BLEED and is the first to join the team in the offseason following the recent departure of Wang "yosemite" Lei (王磊) to Wolves Esports Wolves Esports China Rank #5 Satoshi Fei Zhixian (费郅贤) SiuFatBB Pong Gaa Hei (庞加曦) jowa Jordan Wang yosemite Wang Lei (王磊) Spring Liu Jiunting (刘俊霆) . After a successful 2024 season that saw them qualify for all three international events and secure their first international match win, FPX had a more tumultuous 2025, hovering around the middle of the pack in China and failing to return to the international stage over the course of the year.
