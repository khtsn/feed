Lake on Major chances: "We just gotta iron some stuff out, and I think we'll make a good run"

M80 have been eliminated from the CS Asia Championships in 9th-12th place following a 2-1 loss to MOUZ in a series where their 13-10 Inferno win was overshadowed by heavy defeats on Overpass and Mirage.

Speaking to HLTV after the match, Mason "⁠Lake⁠" Sanderson had positives to take from the team's run in Shanghai, which also included a BO1 victory against paiN and a close series against Falcons.

"I think we need to iron out some more maps against these types of teams and get more comfortable playing these types of games, but I'm not necessarily the most disappointed in how we played at this tournament," he said.

Looking ahead, Lake lamented that M80 will have to go back to North America to play in the ECL Finals — a distraction from their preparation for the Major, where he hopes the team will do one better than in Budapest.

"I think we should be able to get out of Stage 1, even Stage 2," he said. "I believe in the team. I think we can do big things. We've just got to iron out some stuff between now and the Major, and I think we'll make a good run."

A 2-1 defeat and you're out of the tournament. You had a good showing on Inferno but you weren't really in it on Overpass or Mirage. Give me your thoughts on the series as a whole.

The series as a whole wasn't very good. Obviously Inferno, even at the end, was a bit scary. A bit shitty, for sure. Overpass didn't go anywhere where we wanted it to go. I think just a little bit too much pressure on ourselves, playing a bit weirder than we normally do, missing some utility that we don't normally miss. So just one of those things.

What can you take away from this event? You beat paiN and you had a very close series against Falcons. So I guess there are positives to take from this one.

Yeah, there are definitely some positives to take, especially if we look back at how we were playing a week ago. We can be like, "You had a few okay close games," and not be too harsh on ourselves. But obviously it's been a little bit rough, especially with Atlanta, so just trying to look forward to going to the Major.

Back in Atlanta, you said that one of your goals was to play as many games as possible against top 20 teams to shake off the tier-1 LAN rust before the Major. Do you think you were successful at that?

Yeah, a little bit. I think we need to iron out some more maps against these types of teams and get more comfortable playing these types of games, but I'm not necessarily the most disappointed in how we played at this tournament. I think it definitely could've been better. You know, maybe win over Falcons if we'd cleaned some things up, maybe even in a different scenario we play better here, but it's okay. You know, look forward to the next tournament.

Looking back at the series against Falcons, do you think it was a wasted opportunity to get a win against a team like that?

I definitely think so a little bit. I think we kind of let it slip away, especially with our really good starts. But it's just one of those things that kind of happens. Gotta move on, gotta push forward. But obviously you want to win against a team like that.

I wanted to ask about the preparation for the Major. Are you still going to go back to NA to play in the ECL Finals?

Yeah, we do unfortunately have to play that shit.

And how do you think that will affect your preparation for the Major?

You can't really let it affect you too much. It's just kind of one of those things. It's life. You have to make it happen. So we're going to go back, play for three days, and then try to get a few days in before the Major.

What do you make of your chances at the Major? In Budapest, you were one win away from reaching Stage 3.

I think we should be able to get out of Stage 1, even Stage 2. I believe in the team. I think we can do big things. We've just got to iron some stuff out between now and the Major, and I think we'll make a good run.
