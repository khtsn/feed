fame on K27's Prospects: "Sometimes when we play pracs, I see crazy moments. I haven't seen that in a long time"

Petr "⁠fame⁠" Bolyshev joined K27 for a short-term loan until the end of May, and thus received the opportunity to return to a tier-one for the first time since IEM Chengdu 2025.

While results didn't go the way of fame and his young teammates, as they were eliminated with a 0-3 record, the Russian rifler still enjoyed the experience.

"I really liked this experience because all the guys are very good players, very good characters, and people," he told HLTV after the team's elimination from PGL Astana.

"We practiced two weeks very hard, like from the morning to the night, and I missed this feeling, it was great."

Listen to the interview here.

Out of Astana with a 0-3 scoreline. Tell me what's going through your mind after that.

It's kind of sad, but it's not like we... Maybe we should have won these two games against Gentle Mates and magic, but they were close games. Kind of sad, but I think it was still a good result in terms of experience for the guys.

They have a lot of moments to fix, a lot of matches to watch, to make some fixes in our game. So I think it's great in terms of experience, but kind of a sad result, 0-3, when we could have easily won yesterday's game. Today's game was kind of close as well on the second map, but it is what it is.

For you, this is a short-term loan until the end of May, and mostly for Astana. What did you come into this team with? What was the goal for yourself?

My goal was to come to a tier-one event, to show myself, because I'm sitting on the bench and I have nothing to do. I'm just playing some FACEITs, playing some qualifiers with friends, and it's a nice opportunity for me to be back on LANs. If I show myself well, maybe some teams are going to have interest in me. So I think it's good for me, for K27, and for VP, and for everyone.

My goal was to come to a tier-one event, to show myself, because I'm sitting on the bench and I have nothing to do

Tell me about being on the bench. What's the situation with you and Virtus.Pro?

It was like a mutual thing between the team and the organization. We had some big problems because of the results, because of the mental stuff, and we kind of needed a break. We decided with the organization to be benched, and they take a new roster and try to play with this lineup.

What about your future plans? You said you were hoping to come out here and show what you had, right? So what are your future plans looking like in the next couple of months?

I think I'm going to go back to Moscow first of all, then I go home to chill with my family if we don't have something to play with K27 until the end of May. Afterwards I will come back, I will play individually, and I'm going to be ready if someone invites me or something. I'm just going to wait for the news from VP, if someone wants to take me to play, and I'm going to be ready for this moment.

I will play individually, and I'm going to be ready if someone invites me or something

Obviously, as I said, it's a 0-3 exit, so not the result you would have wanted, but what was the experience playing with K27 and the players here like?

Yeah, I really liked this experience because all the guys are very good players, very good characters, and people. It was very funny, and I kind of missed that feeling. We practiced two weeks very hard, like from the morning to the night, and I missed this feeling, it was great.

I really miss this tier-one event feeling as well, so it was nice. [There is still a lot of work to do] for the guys, so I hope I can help them with my experience. I try to say new things to them, try to help in a mental way… I hope I can help them, and I hope they will step up after that.

I hope I can help them, and I hope they will step up after that

I'm not sure if you follow our prospects list, but three of the players on K27 are on it, I think qw1nk1, kashl1d, and xeedo. What did you think of them after playing alongside them?

Yeah, they're very strong players, as I said. If they continue to grow up as players, as people, they will be good players in the future. They have a bright future for sure, and they are very good individual players. Sometimes when we play pracs, I see crazy moments. I haven't seen that in a long time, and it's kind of a good feeling. I think they have a bright future.

Is there anything from any of the three of them that particularly stood out for you?

In the game, I see crazy highlights from them, and sometimes I was shocked, like: 'How can he kill like this?' In the case of individual performance, I was surprised in a positive way. It was a great feeling to play with these guys.

Outside of server performance, how about the attitude and the mental side of things? What do you think of them as players?

They're still young guys. I have a feeling like I have a big age gap, but it's not that big. I think it's great that they have good chemistry, because they're all young, and they have their own team jokes. They like to laugh and all this stuff, and I felt like I'm a young player as well, like I was in K23, for example. It's good vibes.
