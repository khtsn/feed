The Styles Desk

  • Dec. 5, 2024Updated 9:18 a.m. ET

Style manifested in many forms over the last 12 months, as well as in many places: on the campaign trail and red carpets, at stadiums and zoos. The 63 names on this list, which are arranged in no particular order, emerged as those that may come to mind when asked to picture 2024.

Of course, style is subjective — not to mention a subject of heated discussion, which is partly why this group of people, characters and things made the list this year. Each made us look, think and talk about the clothes we wear, the ways we live or the approaches we take to personal style and self-expression. — Anthony Rotunno, fashion news editor

Colman Domingo

The best part of the actor’s awards season fashion? Rarely if ever did he wear a standard tuxedo.

Kim Yeji

The South Korean sharpshooter came to the Olympics with a stuffed elephant and eyewear out of “Mad Max” — and left with a silver medal.

Mary Todd Lincoln in ‘Oh, Mary!’

In this election year, no living first lady managed to capture attention quite like the campy version of Mrs. Lincoln played by Cole Escola.

Hasan Minhaj

The comedian did not shy away from the scuttlebutt generated by a critical New Yorker article; he leveraged it to make a new Netflix special and to land the October digital cover of Esquire.

Jools Lebron

With a few TikTok videos, the content creator made the world a more demure place.

Cynthia Erivo and Ariana Grande

The “Wicked” stars, in their steadfast commitment to wearing pink and green, raised the bar for dressing the part.

Chappell Roan

Her personas — Lady Liberty, Joan of Arc, swans white and black, to name just a few — were as unforgettable as the pop star’s hit songs and unfiltered exchanges with the press.

Daniel Craig

Any lingering associations with James Bond were shed by the actor when he appeared as a floppy-haired fop in a Loewe campaign and starred in the gritty romantic drama “Queer.”

Alex Cooper

She parlayed her hit podcast, “Call Her Daddy,” into a media company — a feat made more impressive knowing that she often works in sweats.

Kordell Beckham

Of all the dramatic whirlwinds on the latest season of “Love Island,” the model spinning around shirtless with his hardtop suitcase was by far the most entertaining.

Demi Moore

While promoting films like “The Substance” and TV shows like “Feud,” the actress made a habit of appearing with a delightful accessory on her arm: her Chihuahua, Pilaf.

Shohei Ohtani

In the first year of his contract with the Los Angeles Dodgers, the superstar two-way player helped carry the team to a World Series title; became the first player in major league history to have 50 home runs and 50 stolen bases in the same season; and was named the National League’s Most Valuable Player. And he hasn’t even pitched yet.

Charli XCX

The singer’s influence can be summed up in two words: “Brat” green.

Miranda July

Her book “All Fours” shaped a new genre: the perimenopause novel.

Christina Applegate

Few if any moments at the 75th Emmy Awards in January — or the 76th Emmy Awards in September — drew as many laughs and tears as the actress’s remarks at the earlier ceremony about her life since being diagnosed with multiple sclerosis.

Phaedra Parks

The former “Real Housewife” wore Scottish garb on “The Traitors” just as memorably as she did beaded and fringed bodysuits on “Dancing With the Stars.”

Gabbriette

The model-influencer born Gabbriette Bechtel cemented her status as an “it” girl when she appeared in Charli XCX’s “360” music video (and was name dropped in the lyrics); starred in campaigns for Marc Jacobs and Y-3; and got engaged to Matty Healy, the musician and former Taylor Swift flame.

Myha’la

After three seasons in HBO’s “Industry,” which was recently renewed for a fourth, the actress has established herself as one of Generation Z’s stars with one-word names.

Babe Paley, Slim Keith, C.Z. Guest and Lee Radziwill in ‘Feud: Capote vs. the Swans’

Watching the quartet of society women — played by Naomi Watts, Diane Lane, Chloë Sevigny and Calista Flockhart — attend balls and have lunches dressed to the nines was a glamorous trip back to a bygone era.

Jack Schlossberg

The only grandson of President John F. Kennedy burst onto the political scene (and social media feeds), propelled by a combination of his grandfather’s looks and a personality like that of another relative: Edith Bouvier Beale, or Little Edie.

Radhika Merchant and Anant Ambani

A red carpet. Emeralds roughly the size of Popsicles. Rihanna. Their pre-wedding celebration and nuptials had it all.

Beyoncé

She became inextricably linked to 10-gallon hats and Western boots with the release of “Cowboy Carter.”

Cooper Koch and Nicholas Alexander Chavez

Some of their scenes in the docudrama, “Monsters: The Lyle and Erik Menendez Story,” were hard to watch. But onscreen and off, the actors themselves were very easy to look at.

Angel Reese

Her decision to skip her senior year at L.S.U. to enter the W.N.B.A. draft (where she was picked by the Chicago Sky) was as remarkable as where she chose to reveal it: Vogue.

Sandra Hüller

The German actress prefers to keep a low profile. Roles in two buzzy films prevented her from doing so this awards season, which came as a delight to fashion followers who appreciated her daring red-carpet style.

Marcello Hernandez

Some might know the “Saturday Night Live” actor better by a name he went by in skits that traveled widely online: Domingo.

Representative Jasmine Crockett of Texas

A prime-time speech at the Democratic National Convention, impassioned exchanges on Capitol Hill and a penchant for wearing statement jewelry all made the freshman congresswoman one to watch.

Stephen Nedoroscik

The bespectacled American gymnast known as “pommel horse guy” had some watching his every move at the Olympics, whether he was breathing meditatively on the sidelines or performing the routine that helped his team win a bronze medal.

Moo Deng

She bit. She screamed. She conquered hearts the world over.

Reneé Rapp

Of all her performances — onscreen in “Mean Girls,” onstage at music festivals like Lollapalooza — the most notable may have been the off-script and often subversive interviews she gave to promote her projects.

Dawn Staley

The coach of the University of South Carolina’s women’s basketball team was among the first to be noticed in “Everyone Watches Women’s Sports” apparel that was later worn by Jason Sudeikis and featured on “The Tonight Show.”

Olivia Nuzzi

Not since Marilyn Monroe has a blonde’s relationship with a Kennedy invited as much speculation as the one that the journalist disclosed having with a member of that family.

Ramy Youssef

At the Oscars, the Muslim actor showed that statement-making outfits don’t have to involve loud clothing. His formal wear for the event, held on the first night of Ramadan, included a black thobe that he originally wore at his wedding.

Tyler, the Creator

The rapper started the year with his first collaboration with Louis Vuitton and ended it with the 10th installment of his music festival, Camp Flog Gnaw.

Christie Raleigh Crossley

Criticism of her participation in the Paralympics did not deter the American swimmer with neurological impairments from taking home five medals, two of them gold.

Mark Eydelshteyn

Those who don’t know the actor as a breakout star of “Anora” might know him for another distinction: He has been called the Russian Timothée Chalamet.

Ayo Edebiri

The actress solidified her standing as a red-carpet star because of the way she wore various attire — sculptural dresses, swingy suits, sequins evoking koi fish — with a natural effervescence.

Apple Martin

After her debut at Le Bal des Débutantes in Paris, the daughter of Chris Martin and Gwyneth Paltrow became the Apple of the internet’s eye.

Ellie the Elephant

As the New York Liberty played its way to its first W.N.B.A. championship, Ellie, the team’s mascot, twerked her way to glory, picking up accolades like a Glamour Women of the Year award as she did.

Dylan Bachelet

The roguishly handsome 20-something contestant brought some heat of a different variety to the latest season of “The Great British Baking Show.”

Jean Smart

Winning a second Emmy for her role in “Hacks” was yet another highlight of the actress’s third act for the ages.

Bronny and LeBron James

Not all nepotism babies make history like Bronny did in October, when he appeared alongside LeBron, his father and Los Angeles Lakers teammate, at the first N.B.A. game since the league’s creation in which a father-son duo shared the court.

Nicola Coughlan

The “Bridgerton” actress showed that sex appeal and eye-catching clothes can be enjoyed by anybody.

Kristin Juszczyk

Her football-jersey-inspired jackets won the approval of Taylor Swift — and earned Ms. Juszczyk, the wife of the San Francisco 49ers fullback Kyle Juszczyk, a licensing deal with the N.F.L.

Adele

The singer concluded her Las Vegas residency after performing 100 shows and wearing more than 50 LBDs — long black dresses, that is.

Liz Cheney

Though her overtures to fellow Republicans to put country over party failed to generate enough votes for Vice President Kamala Harris in the presidential election, they nevertheless were to some an expression of patriotism.

Tyla

Not everyone can make feathered wings and mirrored hot pants look flattering. Even fewer can pull off a skintight gown made with sand, like the singer did at the Met Gala.

Phyllis Kao

The sale of a stegosaurus fossil by the Sotheby’s vice president for client strategy became a must-see event because of her captivating performance at the auction podium.

Campbell and Jett Puckett

Bantering about her “fire” clothing brought the couple — known online as Pookie and Jett — social media stardom (and some backlash, too).

Flavor Flav

His journey from rapper to reality star to women’s water polo hype man offered a lesson in reinvention, as well as another chance to appreciate his fondness for wearing clocks.

Zendaya

On press tours for “Dune” and “Challengers,” the actress dazzled red-carpet watchers with outfits nodding to the films’ themes. At the Met Gala, she doubled down by walking the carpet in two different couture looks.

Patrick Zweig and Art Donaldson in ‘Challengers’

No duo has made the thought of being a churro quite as titillating as the friends turned tennis rivals played by Josh O’Connor and Mike Faist.

The Seine

After a glow-up to the tune of roughly 1.5 billion euros, the river in Paris welcomed swimmers that included the city’s mayor and Olympic athletes.

Larry David

Among the reflections prompted by the series finale of “Curb Your Enthusiasm”: that its star and creator might be the most unsung fashion critic of our time.
