NRG eliminate B8 from BLAST Open Rotterdam

Grim and oSee led the charge for NRG against B8

NRG have knocked B8 out of contention from BLAST Open Rotterdam after two maps to move on to the second round of the Group A lower bracket.

The series was a worrying one for B8 from the get-go, as it kicked off on their Inferno map pick, and even though they won the pistol, a second-round forcebuy from NRG led to the American team going on a nine-round streak on the attack.

Michael "⁠Grim⁠" Wince was all over Daniil "⁠s1zzi⁠" Vinnyk, swinging and killing the AWPer in the apartments on multiple occasions before the half came to an end at 9-3.

Nick "⁠nitr0⁠" Cannella downed three in the second pistol round to kickstart a three-round streak that got NRG to map point, and even though B8 stopped the inevitable three times, they were taken down 13-6 in the end.

Ancient developed in a similar fashion, with NRG getting off to a large lead early on by posting a dominating 10-2 at the half with Grim and Josh "⁠oSee⁠" Ohm leading the charge.

B8 tried to fight back after losing the second pistol round, but once again the comeback was nothing more than cosmetic as Grim won a 1v3 to hit match point after which NRG went on to win 13-9.

The stakes will get higher in the second round of the lower bracket, as NRG will face the loser of Aurora vs. FURIA to continue their run in the group stage at the BLAST Studio.
