HooXi: "When we discuss the rounds outside of the game, everyone knows what's right to do, but we're just not doing them in the moment"

Astralis' time in Cologne is already over after a stunning collapse against paiN.

After two best-of-one defeats to 9z and TYLOO, Astralis then lost a third consecutive Nuke against paiN to start the series 1-0 down with their confidence shattered.

But even then, they were not prepared to go 0-12 down on their own pick of Overpass, ringing the alarm bells right at the end of the season.

paiN end Astralis' Major with dominant victory

"It felt like we had to fight really hard for every round," Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen told HLTV after the game, "and it felt like our opponents always got two or three easy rounds.

The international new era for Astralis started well after they broke into the top ten in VRS, but this result means they will be lucky to make the top twenty by the end of the Major, putting next season's schedule under immense pressure.

Listen to the interview here.

Okay, obviously a tough one to take. Can you just give me your immediate reaction?

I don't feel like we ever really, after the GamerLegion game, got into the flow. It just didn't feel good to play. It felt very hard. It felt like we had to fight really hard for every round, and it felt like our opponents always got two or three easy rounds. So it just felt like it was one kill and then everything crumbled. So I mean, when you feel like you always have to give it your all to get a round and then the opponent just kind of falls into a round, I don't know.

What do you think that's down to? There was so much optimism around the team early in the year, but it seems like those kind of rounds keep happening. Is it the inexperience, do you think?

I mean, if I had the answer, honestly, I would tell you, but it's a lot of, honestly, amateur decisions. Especially on Nuke, our CT side has been horrible. It's just very obvious things... I feel like it's hard because when we sit and discuss the rounds outside of the game, everyone knows what's right to do, but we're just not doing them in the moment. And I'm not really sure what that comes down to. Honestly, I don't know. I don't have the answer for you.

CT side, it's a lot on the individual. But it's not like it's only that, we just went 12-0 down on T side, right? I have no clue what happened, like I'm still in shock

This Major was so important because of the top 12 VRS coming up and everything. Was that something you knew about going into this? Was it a big focus point at the Major to try and get those points?

No, I don't look at this VRS ranking. I feel like it's so... everyone has a take all the time. And if you win it's the best thing in the world and you basically secure the next three Majors. And if you lose, it's a disaster and you will never play a tournament again. So at this point, I just ignore it.

Yeah, okay. ESL broadcast the clip where you were asking the team to kind of take a bit of responsibility mid-round. Is that something you feel like has been lacking this tournament?

Yeah, definitely. I feel like the personal responsibility for your spots is like... I don't know, it's gone out the window a lot. Especially on CT side. We played Nuke a lot, and I have a very isolated spot where it's hard to take charge of the round.

If something happens outside the Ramp, it's hard, as a Hut player to, to control. And I feel like I shouldn't but, at the same time, when nothing is being said or the initiative is not there, then I feel like I have to. But then it's very hard to actually call the right thing because I don't have the actual big picture, I just have what is being communicated. And it's always easier to make a decision from what you see than what you hear.

So, yeah. I mean, I think there was a lot that's also, you know, sometimes we were too easy to read. Sometimes we were too one-dimensional and that's again. CT side, it's a lot on the individual. But it's not like it's only that, we just went 12-0 down on T side, right? I have no clue what happened, like I'm still in shock. I feel like no matter what they did, they got the kill. And the few times we had a good situation to actually win the round, we just froze up and didn't take any initiative. So yeah. I don't know.
