torzsi: "We got ten wins with the new lineup in the last two weeks, and we're proud of that"

MOUZ ended their 2026 CS Asia Championships run on a positive note after beating MIBR 2-0 in the third-place decider. It's the second consecutive bronze medal for the European side, who also finished third at PGL Astana.

"There is a lot of role changing, a new IGL, new players, and I think we're happy with what we showed here," Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás said after the match. "We got ten wins with the new lineup in the last two weeks, and we're proud of that."

The Hungarian AWPer gave his thoughts on how he's working to bounce back from a rough patch of form, Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze's seamless transition to tier-1 CS, and the team's goals for the Cologne Major, where Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin will be back in the lineup.

"I'm really looking forward to playing with Brollan again," he said. "I think he's also a really good guy, a really good teammate. I hope he will show how confident he can be when he doesn't have to think a lot about the game and he can play as a player because xertioN will lead the team and he can just focus on aiming. I think he's really good at it."

torzsi, you beat MIBR to get the bronze medal here at CAC. It's a small consolation prize after not making the final. How would you describe your run in this tournament?

I think the tournament run was pretty good. I think as a team we reached our goals in the last two weeks, especially with Astana and also here. We had some expectations and we reached them, to be honest. There is a lot of role changing, a new IGL, new players, and I think we're happy with what we showed here. We got ten wins with the new lineup in the last two weeks, and we're proud of that.

We are, of course, still disappointed with the game we played against Spirit in Astana. But I think we showed a better game against Falcons, [we showed] that we can play better versus a top-3 team. Overall, this tournament started rough. We had to go to the lower bracket, play two BO3s in a day, two days in a row, and we are happy that we managed that. I think it shows a good mental resilience, that we can do that. Overall, I think it was a good experience.

There was that anti-force where they surprised you on the upper site and then that round where you didn't spot NiKo going down and then he caught two players by surprise.

 To be honest, we haven't had too much time to talk about that game because we had to prepare for MIBR insani, and we rested a little bit after the game. But we said that we felt like on Dust2 we had a good chance as well. For example, there is one round, first buy round, I think, they are B splitting and we are three on B and we have a good chance, or when theywere short popping and we caught them. We were in good positions, but kyousuke played amazingly. I think there were, like, three rounds where he gets a double or triple entry. When you play against these teams, they will sometimes... They are superstars, they will have these rounds. We have to give it to them.

Nuke came down a little bit to details, you know? Also the second round of the game, I accidentally dropped a smoke between boxes and we couldn't pick it up, the round is delayed and we lose the anti-eco and then it's a 10-2 half. It was a little bit awkward. But I'm proud of what we showed on the CT side because I think we were in a really good spot. We lose the A rush. I think we knew they were playing fast and we still got caught. Just details, you know?

It's still unclear what is going to happen after the Major when it comes to jL. How would you say he did in these two events?

Yeah, it's still unclear what will happen. I would say that I'm really happy with what we've shown, with what Justin [jL] and Adrian [xelex] showed. I think they are great players. xelex is coming from NXT, playing his first tier-1 events, and he just showed amazing performances. Also, he's really, really calm. Even when we got to the stage in Astana, he stayed really calm and understood the game really well. I think he's a really smart kid, and I think he has a great future ahead of him. Justin as well. I think he's really funny to be around. We can rely on him in, like, some situations when the pressure gets to us. He has a lot of experience, just like Spinx. So I think it's good for the team. Maybe he didn't feel the best here because he had a lot of off time since NAVI and he needs some time to get back. But I would say I'm proud of them.

You played a major role in getting xelex on the NXT team. What does it feel like to see him play like this and get a shot like this? As a fellow Hungarian, does that make you proud?

 Yeah, it makes me proud. Actually, I dreamed of him. I told him yesterday that I dreamt that he became one of the best players in the world and I'm just next to him like a proud brother. I think he has a good chance to become a really good player. I think he's really smart. He's already really good. I think he's really excited for the Major. Unfortunately, no sticker for him yet, but hopefully [there will be one] for the next one.

I'm also really looking forward to playing with Brollan again. I think he's also a really good guy, a really good teammate. I hope he will show how confident he can be when he doesn't have to think a lot about the game and he can play as a player because xertioN will lead the team and he can just focus on aiming. I think he's really good at it.

It's no secret that this year has been rough for you individually. Before Astana, you had a sequence of three bad individual events. What was it like for you to go through that period? What are you doing to get back into form? Are you comfortable in xertioN's system?

I definitely feel really confident in xertioN's system, and I just want to give some props to him and sycrone. I think they are building a system where everybody can shine at the moment. And xertioN is leading the team in a way where he's trying to use all the pieces all the time. I feel really confident in it and activated.

As you said, I'm having a rough period. I'm working on it a lot. I think it was a lot on my mental and exhaustion. I feel like I'm slowly coming back. Of course, Astana was still no the best, but it was better than before, and now I think this tournament was also better. I had some really good games. I'm really happy with that, and I think it was a nice build-up for the Major.

And now it's back to the drawing board for the Major, where Brollan will be playing instead of jL. How are you going to prepare for the Major? You're starting in Stage 3, so that means you will have a bit more than two weeks to prepare.

As I said, I'm really happy to play with Brollan again. We will have some preparation, we will have some practice. We will go home, we will have some days off, then we will have a little bit of practice at home and then we will have a boot camp. From the bootcamp we shuttle to Cologne. I really hope we can see some MOUZ fans in the Cologne arena.

And given the circumstances, what are the goals for the Major? What is the minimum target, in your opinion?

 As a team, we haven't set a goal yet. I think it will come with a good team talk. We will set some goals together. Individually, I really want to reach the playoffs and give Adrian his first Major playoff experience. I think he deserves it. Hopefully, get some wins in the playoffs. But of course, the upcoming weeks will be a little bit rough, like the last month was, with the roster changes, so it's hard to set a goal at the moment.
