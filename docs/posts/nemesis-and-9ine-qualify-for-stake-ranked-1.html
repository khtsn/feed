Nemesis and 9INE qualify for Stake Ranked 1

kraghen was the third-highest rated player of the qualifier

Nemesis and 9INE have successfully made their way through the closed qualifier for Stake Ranked Episode 1, rounding out the team list for the $100,000 LAN event set to go down in April in Barcelona, Spain. The pair join the likes of G2, HEROIC and Ninjas in Pyjamas at the inagural Stake Ranked LAN.

The result is a particularly impressive one for Nemesis, who secured their LAN berth despite being one of the lowest-ranked teams participating in the qualifier.

The majority-Russian side first took out EYEBALLERS before then besting SINNERS, denting the latter's Major qualification hopes with a hard-fought 2-0 victory that included an overtime brawl on Ancient.

Last but not least Nemesis defeated BIG, one of the in-form teams of the moment, in another closely contested series, needing a big performance from Ivan "⁠SELLTER⁠" Miroshnikov to seal the deal in overtime on Mirage.

The Polish-Danish combine enjoyed a relatively smooth run throughout, beating Tricked, fnatic and an in-form FOKUS without dropping a single map.

9INE will no doubt be happy with the performance of new signing flayy, who was their second-highest rated player across the event and a force to be reckoned with in their wins over fnatic and FOKUS. Tobias "⁠kraghen⁠" Kragh Jensen was the man of the hour, however, posting an impressive 1.31 rating over six maps.

VRS implications

Whilst the VRS impact of the qualifier itself was relatively minimal, with Nemesis and 9INE seeing modest point gains thanks to their success, there are some implications relating to the LAN spots that were on offer.

9INE are likely too far off the pace to qualify for IEM Cologne Major at this point, but they have at least given themselves a huge chunk of VRS points to play for by making it to the LAN. They would likely need to win it to have a shot at Cologne qualification, but regardless they can put themselves in a better spot for next season.

SINNERS' defeat in this qualifier was a significant blow to their qualification chances for Cologne and combined with other teams' improved chances, they are now very likely to fall short. BIG also dented their hopes with their failure against Nemesis.
