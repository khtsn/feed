fer: "I can't handle the player life nowadays, so I told them I could practice for a month and play the Major"

Fernando "⁠fer⁠" Alvarenga will return to a Major for the first time since IEM Rio in 2022 after agreeing to a short-term deal with Gaimin Gladiators.

The legendary Brazilian has already debuted for the squad at BC Game Masters Championship Season 2, where Gaimin Gladiators lost against Acend and Inner Circle, but fer says that is mainly down to a lack of practice.

"We have like two weeks of practice only, and it's kind of hard to have a really good connection inside the server, but I think we made many mistakes," fer said about their early exit.

"We played badly this tournament. Now let's practice more, because the Major is coming and we have to do better."

The Brazilians now have just over a week to prepare for their IEM Cologne Stage 1 opener against BetBoom.

How is it to go back to a Major after so many years? Do you feel nervous about that return after being away for a while?

Well, to be honest, I don't feel much pressure. I have been playing since 2011 so I kind of know how to lose and how to win, so there is no pressure for me. Of course playing the Major is a super special tournament, and I'm replacing a really good player that is a friend of mine as well, that's felps. I was retired for almost three years so I know that it's hard to adjust to playing how the meta is going, but I don't feel much pressure.

I'm not nervous, I'm just waiting for the tournament. We are going to try to do our best and I know that we have like a few weeks to practice, so it's kind of hard to be at a really high level… But I'll try to do my best individually, and the team is creating a really good atmosphere. Let's see.

Do the recent examples like karrigan and FalleN influence your decision? Do you believe that the prime is whatever you decide the prime is, and age doesn't matter?

Well, I don't think that age matters to be honest, but I know that when you get older and older you have more things to do in life, and you have some [different] priorities in life. For us Brazilians it's kind of hard because we have to leave our country to play. For example karrigan, he has a girlfriend, he has his family, he can have a kid, and he still lives in his house. He can practice from the house and after the practice he's with his family and his girlfriend.

When you get older and older, you have more things to do in life

Fernando "⁠fer⁠" Alvarenga

For us in Brazil it's different, because we have to leave our family and girlfriend and stuff, so it's kind of hard to have a life. For us, when we get older we're just focusing on doing other things in life, like having a family, having a baby or whatever, so Counter-Strike hurts that a lot in this situation. But yeah, I don't think age matters.

This is your first official tournament with Gaimin Gladiators. How do you feel about these first games? You had an interesting overtime, so for sure this was not the result you expected, but it's a new lineup. What do you think you bring to the table that felps perhaps couldn't?

I don't like to compare myself with felps because I don't know the results they were having in practice and tournaments, to be honest. We just lost our two matches here and I don't think that we played our game that we were playing in practice. I know that we didn't practice much because we have like two weeks of practicing only, and it's kind of hard to have a really good connection inside the server, but I think we made many mistakes. I don't know if we were nervous playing, but we just got wrecked in the first match of the tournament.

Do you believe that the felps replacement was a necessary decision? Did you think your team in particular needs more of your style of playing, because you have different styles, so do you think you fit better?

I don't think so, I have the same play style as felps, but I don't know what was going on in the team with felps and what was their decision to replace him. They just told me that they have a spot in the team to play the Major, and I was like okay. I cannot handle the player life nowadays so I told them that I could practice for a month and play the Major. I think felps is a really good player, I played with him in the past and we won a lot of tournaments together, and I really like his playstyle. I don't know his mind nowadays, I don't know if he's as focused in the game like he was in the past when we played together.

If it was a team decision, I didn't have any idea what was going on in the team with felps. I think felps is a really really good player, like inside the server, of course he plays like really aggressive, and sometimes people [aren't that happy] with aggressive players, even more when you are not putting results in the server. But I don't know if it was about results in the server that they have to replace felps, or if it was something else outside the server, I don't know that.

I don't know if it was about results in the server that they have to replace felps, or if it was something else outside the server

Since you are a veteran of CS and you have seen everything, how do you see the CS2 tournaments now? Do you think the pro player's life is more comfortable now for the younger players, or do you think it's even more difficult because they have an excessive amount of tournaments and all this VRS points pressure. Do you think it's more difficult for the young people now?

For the older people, the people that play for many years, it's kind of hard because there are a lot of tournaments every week. So it's kind of hard for the old players, but for the younger players of course it's 100% better nowadays because they have more salary, they have more sponsors coming on. When I started playing we didn't even receive the money, we were playing in the US and receiving $300 as a salary, so for the young players it's really, really good.

When we started playing the only thing we wanted was to play tournaments and play more and more hours of CS, so for them it's really good. It's easier nowadays to join a team with a big structure, sponsorship, and salary, so they can live from CS. They can come to their parents like, 'Okay, so I want to be like a ZywOo or a FalleN.' When I started playing I couldn't tell my parents that I want to be this guy, because there wasn't a Brazilian player doing something outside the country, so for younger players for sure it's a dream for them.

In these years of retirement, semi-retirement. We know this life is very exhausting for family life, as you said. What have you learned outside the server that maybe now can help you be a better player and better teammate?

The thing that I learned outside the server is that you have to enjoy what you do. When I stopped playing it was because I wasn't enjoying that much. I played a lot of tournaments and stuff and I left my family for many years, so for me it was hard. I wasn't enjoying playing that much and I wasn't enjoying life with my family, I wasn't enjoying life, so the two things I did in my life was study and play Counter-Strike.

There are a lot of countries that I just know the hotel, the arena, and the airport, so it was kind of exhausting

Fernando "⁠fer⁠" Alvarenga

In life you have to have more things to do, more things to explore, so my feeling at that time was like, 'Dude, the time is coming, you are getting older and older and you did nothing in your life but Counter-Strike and study.' We did travel a lot to play tournaments and in my time we won a lot of tournaments, so we didn't have much time to explore the cities and stuff. There are a lot of countries that I just know the hotel, the arena, and the airport, so it was kind of exhausting.
