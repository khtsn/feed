IEM Atlanta semi-finals day as it happened

On the feeling in NAVI to stunna on stage: "It's just confidence. All the individuals are feeling their moves. We gained some valuable experience about playing on stage, and we're playing some situations perfectly."

On upcoming final: "We understand how good we are right now, and I think tomorrow will be a good day."

Speaking to STYKO on the map pool: "We are a mix with FL4MUS, so every map can be shaky. It's a lot of space to create some new calls, this is a good opportunity for me to be a free caller. Magnojez and zorte are trying to help me with what they can do and structure a call with me."

On practice: "We are practicing with one roster and playing the tournamment with the second. We don't understand what is happening, but we have the opportunity to win some tournaments and we will try to."

Speaking to STYKO, on the decider: "On Inferno, it's always chaos, it's about IQ and adrenaline. Some bad decisions can affect the team. The economy, when you lose momentum, makes it worse."

On the third place decider: "We have a game tomorrow, it's a great opportunity to play in front of people and get experience for the Major and CAC."

On unlocking their potential: "There is more room for the A game. Sometimes we reach down to the C game, because of the emotions, we lose focus, we have conversations after losing or winning and we can do better after. It's just about time."

Speaking to the desk post-game: "The experience that we got. Going deep into events, PR and hypex get more time on stage, that's really important against good tier one teams.

"Everyone is able to control emotions more now, we're more disciplined than before when we choked. We have composure now, and know what to do in the crucial moments."

Talking to STYKO, on nerves: "Its' got better. On the stage, I can feel the difference from last year. I'm just happy that I can feel the experience this young, it's going to help me.'

On Legacy: "We can smell a bit of the Brazilian vibe. I love Brazil, but they have a very specific playstyle. paiN, and FURIA, are the only teams playing a bit different."

On his own form: "I always have a hard start to events, I'm figuring out why. After the first map, I get into the zone and feel way better."

On the crowd: "It's the Snax effect. Thanks guys, for cheering for us."

GamerLegion made top-four at Rivals, and they've done the same here after beating paiN.

The last one didn't go too well against Vitality, but Legacy offer a different flavor as an opponent here.

The cores have met just once before, in Bucharest last year, and GL came out on top.

Before Atlanta, Vitality had won five events in a row, made top four at 21 events in a row, and won a whopping 28 playoff maps in a row.

But after taking some well-earned time off, a weakened Vitality could not keep up any of those three streaks at IEM Atlanta, and lost their quarter-final against NAVI yesterday.

With a look at the bracket, you'd think that makes NAVI's path to the trophy straightforward but their three rivals are here for a reason. You just might not have predicted it.
