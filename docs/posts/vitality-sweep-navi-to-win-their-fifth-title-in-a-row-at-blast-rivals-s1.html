Vitality sweep NAVI to win their fifth title in a row at BLAST Rivals S1

Vitality's superb start to 2026 shows no sign of slowing down after they beat Natus Vincere 3-0 in the BLAST Rivals Season 1 grand final.

Holes were poked in the world number one's armor in Texas, with FUT and G2 taking map wins in groups and Natus Vincere taking them close on maps one (where NAVI led 11-0 only to lose in overtime) and two of the grand final, but Vitality came through that adversity with their invincible reputation intact.

They have now won 27 playoff maps and 15 grand final maps in a row this year, and the curtain raiser of BLAST Bounty remains the only trophy that did not go home with Vitality to Paris at events they attended.

Things could have gone differently in Texas' grand final, not least on Nuke before the 0-11 comeback but also on Anubis, where Natus Vincere led 11-9 only for Vitality to win the last four rounds in a row to win 13-11.

But against a team as indomitable as Vitality you have to make the most of those opportunities, and Natus Vincere didn't get a sniff on Dust2. Nuke and Anubis were where the final was decided.

And all things said and done, the fact remains that Natus Vincere have lost 12 maps in a row against Vitality in a run that spans nearly two years since IEM Cologne 2024's grand final.

That they are the world number two team, and still cannot leave a mark on the number one, is a sign that this era has plenty of road to run just yet.

The 11-0 comeback

At Big Events in CS2 Vitality had never gone down 10-0, let alone 11-0, but Natus Vincere were able to do exactly that as they stormed out of the gates on Nuke.

Truth be told, it was not as convincing as that makes it seem: NAVI were 4-0 in clutches in that run, and Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri's deaths Outside did lead to a good few 4v4s. But the fact remains that Andrey "⁠B1ad3⁠" Gorodenskiy's troops were in a position where 99% of teams had gone on to win in regulation.

Clip: Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool's 4HP 1v1 that helped turn the final

That's now 98%, after Natus Vincere allowed Vitality to get themselves back into the game. In the second half, ropz, Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut, and William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman all won crucial 1on1 clutches as Vitality went flawless on their own CT side. ropz hadn't even won a single clutch in Fort Worth before the final, and was having a quiet event, but three maps later he had won seven and was lifting another trophy.

Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire said on stage he picked Nuke because he "knew what they would do" on T side, and it certainly looked like it as Vitality suffocated their opponents with utility and smoke spam in the yard. But as good as Vitality were, Natus Vincere went outside with themselves to blame for not converting such a dominant lead.

Another streak to keep up for Vitality

Vitality's remarkable streak in playoffs has been extended further in Texas, and they head to Atlanta having won 27 playoff maps in a row since IEM Krakow's grand final.

That puts them ahead of all the era-defining teams we checked except NIP, who won 36 maps in a row in tracked playoffs in 2012-13.

It's easy to focus on the close games along the way if you are one of their opponents, and believe that this playoff streak — just like their overall map win streak, and Dust2 run — is there to be broken.

But regardless of records, the sheer relentlessness of Vitality is convincing more and more people that we are watching the greatest team of all time.

In 2025, BLAST Rivals Season 1 gave Vitality their fifth title in a row. In 2026, it has done the same. Last year, they went and won two more at Dallas and the Major, and you can bet that apEX, just a few minutes after the trophy lift, is already starting to think about IEM Atlanta.
