Vitality can further their claim as the greatest team of all time should they win today, not only because they would be adding another trophy to a cabinet already overflowing, but also because they would win a second ESL Grand Slam.

"We'll have pressure for sure. It's the Grand Slam" Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan told HLTV in an interview yesterday. "Tomorrow is a great opportunity for us, we'll come and give a lot of energy. It's just about ourselves, if we don't lose to ourselves, it's going to go well for us."

Should they claim the Grand Slam, Vitality would be the only team to have done so twice, and be the only back-to-back Grand Slam winners. It would be yet another accolade for the squad to add to their resume.

The cores of Falcons and FURIA have plenty of recent history together, having faced off in seven series over the course of the last year or so.

FURIA have the edge overall, a 4-3 series record and a 12-8 map record, but plenty of these series have been close, going to three maps and featuring overtimes.

The more recent history is firmly in Falcons favor, however. Falcons have won both matchups between the two sides this year, squeaking out hard-fought wins at PGL Cluj-Napoca and BLAST Open Rotterdam.

Can Falcons clinch a threepeat, or will FURIA answer back today?

The final day of IEM Rio is upon us, which means a best-of-five grand final is coming up to decide who will take home the coveted trophy. Before that, however, the hometown heroes will be hoping to seal the deal on third place.
