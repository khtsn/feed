HexT: "I think Cxzi brings some things Peeping was lacking"

HexT tells HLTV what Wildcard's latest additions have added to the team following their BC Game Masters victory.

Wildcard have been in a rebuilding phase across 2026, steadily swapping out the entire roster that represented the organization the previous year. That rebuild has begun to pay off, the team enjoying a streak of tournaments that has seen them pick up three top placings regionally before grabbing a trophy at BC Game Masters Championship Season 2.

As a result of their grind, Wildcard now sit inside the top 30 of the global VRS, having started life with this core outside the top 100. "It wasn't really intimidating," Jadan "⁠HexT⁠" Postma said of the VRS grind after the team's winning effort in Portugal. "I knew we weren't going to get invites[...] we can just buy our way into events and play those smaller events. This one isn't really that small, but we can play these events and work our way up. It has helped us a lot, for sure."

With a strong run of form behind him and a real chance to qualify for the next Major ahead, HexT spoke to HLTV at BC Game Masters Championship to chat about the victory, being the top team in NA, and what the additions of Nemanja "⁠nEMANHA⁠" Đukić and Danny "⁠Cxzi⁠" Strzelczyk have brought to the team.

It was smooth sailing for you guys here, the team's synergy was really felt on stage. What was the missing element in your team before? Was it the addition of Cxzi?

I don't know what the missing element was. I think Dani [Cxzi] brings some things that Jaxon [Peeping] was lacking. Nothing against him, of course, he just needs to grow, he's still young, and Dani has some experience from Passion UA, so I think he brought that. Honestly, it was just a different environment, a different feel in the team. I think it gave us more confidence.

Before nEMANHA joined, it was said that the team lacked a traditional IGL, as no one claimed the role outright. What's the biggest structural improvement he has brought to the Wildcard strategy?

I personally like the way that nEMANHA calls. It's very loose, it's not very set. He wants you to take initiative and make plays. That's one of the best ways you can call as an IGL. However, I think it does have its downsides. If people are off that day, it makes everything a lot harder, but he brings a lot of passion to the team, and I think that the way he calls is magical.

He brings a lot of passion to the team, and I think that the way he calls is magical

Jadan "⁠HexT⁠" Postma on nEMANHA

Wildcard restarted from the bottom of the VRS. Was that intimidating or motivating the team to work harder to conquer those points?

When I joined, they already had some points from FRAG in Miami, and I knew we weren't going to make the first Major, so our eyes were always set on the Singapore Major. It wasn't really intimidating. I knew we weren't going to get invites. I knew we'd have to play some LANs like Fragadelphia, especially with the VRS system now, we can just buy our way into events and play those smaller events. This one isn't really that small, but we can play these events and work our way up. It has helped us a lot, for sure.

You are the number one NA team right now. How does that feel? Can you make people forget Liquid for a day?

Liquid are definitely having their struggles right now. There's like three teams in North America, so being number one doesn't really mean anything. It changes by the day, typically, so it doesn't really mean anything. That's not really our goal. We're looking at the world ranking, for sure, which we're getting now. Liquid is just definitely having struggles, and they need to make some changes, for sure.

There's like three teams in North America, so being number one doesn't really mean anything

Jadan "⁠HexT⁠" Postma

It's pretty much confirmed by now that you'll be getting an EWC invite. What are your expectations for that since it will be your first S-Tier event?

Expectations... I don't know. We've been playing pretty well in practice, but we haven't really been playing any of the top five teams. Our small bootcamp here was like four or five days, and the rest was just in NA. Expectations are just to take it game by game. If we can keep it up like today, then I think we'll have a good result, but now it's obviously top teams, it's going to be not too long after the break, so we'll see how that goes.
