Floods in Spain’s Valencia Region Kill Dozens

Europe|Flash Floods in Spain Leave More Than 50 Dead

https://www.nytimes.com/2024/10/30/world/europe/spain-floods-valencia.html

Over 1,000 soldiers from Spain’s emergency response units were deployed to the devastated areas in the eastern part of the country.

Oct. 30, 2024, 5:02 a.m. ET

At least 51 people have died after flash floods in eastern Spain swept away cars, turned village streets into rivers and disrupted rail lines and highways, according to the local authorities.

Emergency services in the eastern region of Valencia confirmed the death toll.

Rainstorms on Tuesday caused flooding in a wide area of southern and eastern Spain. A high-speed train with nearly 300 people on board derailed near Málaga, although the rail authorities said no one was hurt. High-speed train service between Valencia city and Madrid was interrupted, as were several commuter lines.

Floods of mud-colored water tumbled vehicles down streets at frightening speeds. Pieces of wood swirled with household articles.

The police and rescue services used helicopters to lift people from their homes and cars. Over 1,000 soldiers from Spain’s emergency response units were deployed to the devastated areas.

Spain’s central government set up a crisis committee to help coordinate rescue efforts.

Storms were forecast to continue through Thursday, according to Spain’s national weather service.

Spain has experienced similar fall storms in recent years. It is still recovering from a severe drought this year. Scientists say increased episodes of extreme weather are most likely linked to climate change.
