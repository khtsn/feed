Europe gains one Major slot for PGL Singapore at the expense of the Americas

The conclusion of the IEM Cologne Major's second stage has settled the changes to regional slot allocation for the next major, PGL Singapore, where Europe will receive one more invite at the expense of the Americas.

The IEM Cologne Major distribution was:

  • Europe: 17 invites (6 in Stage 1 + 5 in Stage 2 + 6 in Stage 3)

  • Americas: 10 invites (6 + 3 + 1)

  • Asia: 5 (4 + 0 + 1)

Now, with 12 European teams in Stage 3, that means the reigning region will get as many invites (plus the 6 base Stage 1 invites) to the next Major, while the Americas, with only 3 teams in Stage 3, will get that many added teams to their base 6. Asia, with just The MongolZ left in the running, adds 1 to their Stage 1 base of 4 invited teams.

The new allocation is:

  • Europe: 18

  • Americas: 9

  • Asia: 5

Now, the IEM Cologne Major's Stage 3 will decide where these future invites will be seeded, as teams making the playoffs in Germany will grant their region a direct berth into Stage 3 at PGL Singapore.
