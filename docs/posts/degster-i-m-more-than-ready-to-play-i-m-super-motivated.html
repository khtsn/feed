degster: "I'm more than ready to play; I'm super motivated"

The Russian AWPer has shed some light on his forced inactivity.

Former Spirit and Falcons AWPer Abdul "⁠degster⁠" Gasanov has said that he is in shape and ready to immediately settle into a new team despite not playing any tier-1 competition in a year.

The 24-year-old discussed a wide range of topics in an interview with Duncan "⁠Thorin⁠" Shields, from his surprise departure from Spirit in 2022 after a semi-final run at the Antwerp Major to his time on OG and HEROIC.

He also offered his perspective on his benching from Falcons in April 2025. Signed by the Saudi organization at the start of that year alongside HEROIC teammates Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski and René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen, degster spent less than four months in the starting lineup before making way for Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov.

While the Russian AWPer struggled for form during most of his Falcons spell, his time on the team ended on a memorable note as he earned an MVP medal in the title-winning campaign at PGL Bucharest.

"I understand Falcons," he said. "I don't have anything bad to say. I even invited NiKo to talk about it [the reported interest in m0NESY] in our first bootcamp. The only thing I didn't expect was for it to happen before the [Austin] Major.

"Was I bad in CS2 when I came back? Yes, I was. But I was grinding. And I was also grinding with my teammates. We go to Falcons, and this first Major was 100% my Major."

The biggest surprise for many is how degster seems to have completely fallen off the radar, even after becoming a free agent in December.

According to him, a move to a "tier-1 team" when he was on Falcons' bench didn't materialize because the team in question "didn't have the money". Some "top 20, top-15" teams also made inquiries, though he noted that there weren't deep discussions.

"Some of them were, for example, to stand in," he said. "There was a really good chance to stand in for HEROIC. It was like nine months ago. They contacted me to play for them right at the same time when I already had tickets to visit my family in Dagestan.

"If you remove that tier-1 offer, there was no offer at all that was super serious, in my eyes."

When asked if he had a message for prospective teams, degster argued that it would take him very little time to shake off the rust.

"I'm not just available; I'm still in shape," he said. "I'm still capable of playing. I still grind.

"After I left Falcons — just to show you how ready I was —, vacation? I'm saying, 'We're not going anywhere. I need to be in shape for when I join my next team.'

"I'm ready. I don't need any time to join any team and get into shape. Yes, you can say that I'm out of the meta. I feel it. I'm not an idiot. You can watch demos, but if you're not playing, you're missing small things, especially if you're an AWPer.

"But in my eyes, that's not the biggest problem to deal with. I know myself, I know how capable I am.

"I'm more than ready to play. I'm super motivated to play."
