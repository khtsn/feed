woxic: "The first time for me to play in LANXESS, the feeling is amazing"

Özgür "⁠woxic⁠" Eker stopped by HLTV's microphone after Aurora completed their qualification to the IEM Cologne Major playoffs with a comfortable 2-0 win over 9z.

The AWPer was the first to note that the qualification will mark his first appearance on the LANXESS Arena stage, after his failed bids with MOUZ in 2019 and Aurora and Eternal Fire in the past two years.

woxic's Cologne placings

IEM Cologne 2025 - 9th-12th

"This is the first time I'll reach the German crowd," he said with a smile. "The feeling is amazing, and thank you to my teammates that nobody stepped down, nobody spoke with bad emotions to each other, everybody was just believing in each other."

Below you can listen to or read the interview in full, in which he also talks about his momentum-stopping 1v4 clutch on Dust2 and how Aurora managed to shut down an otherwise very competitive 9z on the way to the playoffs.

You can listen to the interview on HLTV's dedicated Spotify channel.

Congratulations on making it to playoffs. I just wanted to ask you about that clutch. That must be somewhere pretty high up there in terms of your best plays.

Yeah. When I killed the guy inside the smoke, I just said, 'okay, it's a free site now.' But he was too close, the third guy, and right after I saw him, he just went back again and he didn't put some kind of balls. I can understand that he just didn't want to lose the round, so he wanted to play with his teammates, but I think he had an opportunity to kill me.

When he gave me that chance, and I was kinda feeling it today, that it was our day, every piece of the puzzle just connected to each other and I won it.

It was very one-sided today. You saw 9z beat Vitality and almost beat Spirit. What kind of mindset did you go into this match with, knowing they've been so competitive here?

Yeah. Also we watched their previous games in Stage 2 as well while we were doing a bootcamp. We were watching their games and they were really showing great Counter-Strike individually and also with teamplay.

When they beat Vitality... I think they lost to Spirit because of some small clutches and little mistakes, otherwise they would not have even met with us, if they didn't do it yesterday. They really have big potential and they are really playing good, actually, but this is kinda our day and we did really good preparation. We have been watching their games and everybody of us was feeling confident to play today.

Everybody's main focus was to go into the quarter-final, not giving any opportunity for 2-2. Tomorrow, the other team also going home if they lose, the games can be so tough, and that's why I wanted to give everything to go as soon as we can.

What did you see about them that you learned from or that you could exploit?

We saw they like to play like a group, swinging Short or whatever, the map is not important. They like to do little fakes and go together. They are having really good individuals and they're really good about the pushes as well, for the A rushes on Nuke and stuff.

They had good points, but we were kinda ready for it. We knew they would do that, we were three-four guys close to inner and we got the right rounds when they made them, otherwise we would have lost some rounds and the game wouldn't have been so easy. We kinda understood what can happen, we expected it, and that's why we countered them. Otherwise they could have used their own power.

Aurora were able to shut down a 9z side that Vitality and Spirit struggled against

But I think they played a long game yesterday, three overtime maps, and also here, they had to wait, like us, but we had good sleep, we didn't play, we were watching their games. Here, the MongolZ game against Monte went to the third game, so they have been waiting more and more... It's hard for them.

Tell me about the run overall. A convincing 3-1 outside of that Spirit game. How do you evaluate the run to the playoffs?

I wanna talk about the Spirit game. I think we should have won that if I gave 10% of what I did in this game. My coach said that if I gave 10% in that game, doesn't matter, we were not about to win the game, but personally, inside of myself, I couldn't do shit. Things were going unlucky for me in the second map. I started really good on Dust2, but in the next games I was just lost inside the maps.

That's why that happened. But I think qualification in the Major quarter-final is a really big thing. And also, this is the first time for me to play in LANXESS as well. I've been here three or four times with my former teams, but this is the first time I'll reach the German crowd.

The feeling is amazing, and thank you to my teammates that nobody stepped down, nobody spoke with bad emotions to each other, everyone was just believing in each other. If you have a bad game, doesn't matter, tomorrow you're gonna make it. Everyone was in this kind of mindset to each other, so I'm glad that they were like this. This is the result.

soulfly has "unleashed the beast" in his rookie performance

soulfly had a great performance through Stage 3. What did you think about his rookie performance?

He's kinda unleashed the beast, or otherwise I don't know if it was just some lucky games, but I don't think so. He really showed what he's capable of, communication-wise and shooting-wise, repositioning, and helping teammates.

He's just getting better and better because he wasn't playing on this level before. It's not easy to get used to it, but I'm glad he was able to show in the most-watched tournament in the world. Let's see how it's gonna be for the quarter-final from everyone as an individual.

How are you feeling about that? The narrative has been that you struggle when the lights come on and on the stage. It's a lot of pressure to play in Major playoffs - how do you think you can handle it?

I don't know. There was a crowd here on the A stream and we didn't feel any pressure, but of course it's gonna be more thousands there. Let's see. There's no secret behind it. If we are feeling good, shooting good, it doesn't matter. It's not gonna affect us. When things are going bad inside the server for us, that's when it's starting to affect us. If we are like this today, I think it's not gonna affect and we can show our real potential in the arena for the people.
