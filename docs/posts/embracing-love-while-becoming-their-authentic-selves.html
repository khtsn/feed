Embracing Love While Becoming Their Authentic Selves

Spencer Hatcher and Crystal Carmichael met in 2012 through a dating app, and at the time, both were going through transitions in their lives.

Spencer Ter Hatcher and Crystal Marie Carmichael can’t quite agree on who noticed whom first. They first connected on the dating app Plenty of Fish on Sept. 5, 2012, and “I reached out first,” Ms. Carmichael said. “His blue eyes really drew me in. He claims he saw me first.”

Mr. Hatcher said he was taken by Ms. Carmichael’s profile, including the fact that she had children because he had always wanted them. But one thing gave him pause: “There was so much Jesus in her posts.”

“He thought I was secretly trying to convert him,” Ms. Carmichael said. “At this time, Spencer was presenting as a female, so he thought I was pretending to be a lesbian to convert his gay soul,” said Ms. Carmichael, who had just ended a 10-year marriage to a man and “was seriously questioning” her own sexual identity.

Still, the two texted “nonstop,” Ms. Carmichael said, and made plans to meet for milkshakes at a Carl’s Jr. in Albuquerque the following Tuesday after her volleyball practice.

“We talked about everything, including where I played volleyball,” said Ms. Carmichael, which was at a “very conservative mega church” that she occasionally attended at the time.

Before the date was over, Ms. Carmichael received a call from a family member. “He told me the cops were on the way to his house because his shotgun accidentally went off in the house when he was trying to scare his roommate,” she said.

No plans for a second date were made. Mr. Hatcher said, “I did initially recognize a few possible red flags,” from everything Ms. Carmichael shared about her life and the call she received, “but more than anything I think I realized my heart wasn’t ready.”

“He thought I was completely psycho,” Ms. Carmichael said.

The two texted and Mr. Hatcher told her he had plans to move out of state and didn’t want to start a relationship. “I cried to my best friend because I truly knew that he was my soul mate,” Ms. Carmichael said. “I needed to be with him.”

The two stayed in touch. “I sent Bible verses and songs every day,” Ms. Carmichael said. Mr. Hatcher found the gesture endearing. “We grew closer and closer,” Mr. Hatcher said.

Her persistence paid off. “The more I learned about her, the more the initial crazy date made sense” — she was dealing with a lot of stress and was the person many people came to with problems. “She had these situations because she has such a good heart,” Mr. Hatcher said. “It clicked that this is a trait I’m actually looking for.”

On Oct. 1, 2012, “We made it official that we’re going to date exclusively,” Ms. Carmichael said. “It just morphed. Every evening we were together.”

They were hesitant to tell Ms. Carmichael’s twins from her previous marriage — a son and daughter — that they were dating, unsure it would stick; the children were just 5 at the time. “We would do things with the kids, but we weren’t being affectionate in front of them,” Ms. Carmichael said.

In early December 2012, Mr. Hatcher moved into Ms. Carmichael’s house in the North Valley, N.M., and they told the twins they were a couple. In June 2013, they bought a house together in Los Lunas, about 30 minutes south of Albuquerque. They now live in Rio Rancho, north of Albuquerque in a home they bought in May 2023.

On Oct. 1, 2013, the two went to the Melting Pot restaurant to celebrate their one-year anniversary. On the way, they happened to stop at a red light about a block away. “I looked up and took a picture and made a joke about Jared’s, the jewelry store,” Ms. Carmichael said. “I posted it on social media and said, ‘Look, he went to Jared’s.’”

Ms. Carmichael had no idea what Mr. Hatcher had planned for that night.

After dinner, he handed her a glass jar with 99 clear stones in it. “Each stone had a memory from the past year. He asked me to pull a few out and read them, so I did,” Ms. Carmichael said. The stones recounted their first “fancy date” at a steakhouse; taking the children to a pumpkin patch; milkshakes at Carl’s Jr.; and their new house.

He then handed one to her that he pulled from his pocket. It read, “Getting engaged.” He then took out a white gold and diamond ring, got down on one knee and proposed. “I immediately cried and said, ‘Yes,’” Ms. Carmichael said. “The Melting Pot took our picture and gave us a printout that I still cherish today.”

Mr. Hatcher, 42, grew up in Albuquerque and works as an acquisition program specialist with the federal government. He has a master’s degree in software engineering and database technology and an M.B.A. from Regis University in Denver. A previous marriage ended in divorce. He has also been in one previous civil union.

Ms. Carmichael, 40, is a fourth and fifth grade special education teacher for Albuquerque Public Schools and was also raised in Albuquerque. She has a bachelor’s degree in elementary education and special education from Grand Canyon University in Phoenix.

Not long after their engagement, the two began having problems. Their pastor recommended a therapist. “A few months in, we made the realization that many of the issues were because Spencer was not able to be his true self,” Ms. Carmichael said.

Mr. Hatcher said that before he ever met anyone who was transgender and realized that was what he was, he had always felt “a deep self-loathing for my body and appearance.” Once he had a name for it in his 20s, he felt as if he had to simply hide it.

But in therapy, he said, he was able to embrace his identity and explore what living his true self meant. “I was still terrified to come out to others,” Mr. Hatcher said. “But it got to the point where I either had to give up on life or just bite the bullet and be me. I chose the latter and couldn’t be happier.”

Mr. Hatcher began his transition by changing his name. Then he pursued hormone therapy and eventually had two gender affirming surgeries. “During this we also found out that Spencer has multiple sclerosis and had to learn how to live with this disease,” Ms. Carmichael said. “Never once during this process did I question my love for him.”

The two were wed at Vegas Weddings, a wedding venue, on Jan. 2, 2024 by Lynn Marie Goya, the county clerk of Clark County, Nev., with 30 people in attendance. Roger Bennett, who was associated with the venue, performed the ceremony. “Vegas is our special place. We often spend family Thanksgiving there and vacation there. We love the city,” Ms. Carmichael said.

The couple chose their favorite color combination of red and black for the wedding décor. Mr. Hatcher’s best friend, Shannon Ellefson, was his “best woman” and Ms. Carmichael’s best friend, Valene Urbán, who died in 2014, was honored with blue flowers in the bridal bouquets.

During the wedding, the couple had a unity sand ceremony with their children. “Our children have only known Spencer as their dad and have navigated these changes as well with ease,” Ms. Carmichael said.

For the processional, his parents walked down the aisle and then sat on the bride’s side as a “sign of accepting the union,” Ms. Carmichael said. “The parents of my best friend who passed sat on his side to accept the union.” Ms. Carmichael and Mr. Hatcher processed together, “rather than the traditional ‘giving the bride away,’” Ms. Carmichael said.

“We have both transitioned so much and have been through so much during our relationship that we know our love is so true and so strong,” Mr. Hatcher said. “Nothing will break us.”

Pure Vegas On Jan. 1, “For our bachelorette/bachelor party, Spencer was pulled onstage by the girls at the Fantasy Show and had to dress up and dance like Elvis,” Ms. Carmichael said. After the wedding, “We went to Freemont Street and I was pulled on the bar to dance at Hogs and Heifers. Then we met a celebrity and I rode a mechanical penis. I think the only thing missing to make it a more Vegas wedding was a tiger. And believe me, we looked for one,” Ms. Carmichael said.

Let’s Dance “Our first dance was literally like only the second time we have ever danced together so I loved that moment. Crystal hates dancing so having everyone’s eyes on us plus me staring into her eyes was magical,” Mr. Hatcher said.

Magic Moment The couple streamed the ceremony for friends and family who could not attend, allowing the couple to relive some of the best moments of the night. One of those, Mr. Hatcher said, was when, “I hugged my parents after coming down the aisle and my dad said, ‘I’m so proud of you Angel.’ Our online streaming audience caught that and shared that moment with me.”

Double Date The couple chose Jan. 2 in honor of Mr. Hatcher’s grandmother who died in August 2021. “That is her birthday and now we can celebrate both occasions and honor her every year,” Ms. Carmichael said.
