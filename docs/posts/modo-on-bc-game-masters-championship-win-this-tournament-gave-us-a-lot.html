MoDo on BC Game Masters Championship win: "This tournament gave us a lot"

SINNERS are not letting the Major invite cutoff change their mindset now that they're back in the race.

SINNERS showed a glimmer of what they are capable at Parken Challenge, with a third-place finish behind GamerLegion and illwill. Now, they've one-upped themselves by first beating the favorites, G2, and then winning it all at the first BC Game Masters Championship with a 2-1 victory over BIG in the grand final.

"The mentality should be the same as we had for this tournament." Mădălin-Andrei "⁠MoDo⁠" Mirea told HLTV after the victory in Romania, which lifts the spirits of a team that suffered in the mud of online play. "Just beat everybody, play our game, and just enjoy it."

This is a huge result for the team and organization, the first LAN trophy outside of Czechia in over six years. What does that mean to you?

It's amazing for the org, and it's amazing for us because basically... we were sad in the last period because we kinda fucked up some tournament online, and it was not possible to dodge them because we'd already accepted the invite. We were sad, but it was also good because we had a period where we had time to practice for this LAN, and we did everything we could for this to happen.

For example, we had a bad Overpass before, and we put some weeks [of practice] into it just to try and pick it. We had good results in practice, we never lost a game in like 20 games or something. We tried really hard on this map, and I'm happy we could pick it and feel comfortable on it.

It was a troubling period for SINNERS for a while. Lots of roster changes, rough results, but things have started turning around this year. What's different?

I don't know. I'm new here, only been here three months, but I'm pretty sure they had a Czech team and they were struggling. We all know that in national teams, it can go both ways. Either it can go bad or good, but... I'm happy that they decided to go international, that I'm here to help the boys, and we're here to lift this trophy.

You beat G2, which was a huge win and netted you a lot of VRS points, as well as BESTIA, BetBoom, and BIG for the trophy. What are your thoughts on your run here overall, and beating G2 in their debut with NertZ?

Speaking about the G2 game, we said to ourselves that we don't have any expectations about this game. We just need to play our game like we usually do, like in practice, and we just did it.

It's also like... I would say... easier to play? Like, I would choose to play G2 over BetBoom, for example, where they can play this tier-one CS and we can understand what they are doing and we can expect some things. But overall, I'm really happy about this win. It was huge for us, and it's amazing.

The VRS cut-off for the Major is in less than two weeks. This run has put you in contention for an invite, so what does that mean to you and how does it feel?

It's amazing, but we had a discussion with the team. We were up there on the invites after the Parken [event] we played in Denmark, but we fucked up online again. And we decided, all of us, that we don't need to think about the Major. We just need to take it game by game and fuck this, just play our game and if we qualify for the Major, we qualify for the Major. But don't lose to ourselves, this is the worst feeling.

You have another LAN to play in a few days and a lot of teams fighting for those last Major spots. What is your mentality like heading into this last week before the cut-off, other than 'fuck it, we play our game'?

It's amazing that we're here at this point, this tournament gave us a lot, but the mentality should be the same as we had for this tournament. Just beat everybody, play our game, and just enjoy it because we are here to enjoy the game. We can sometimes forget this, that we are just CS2 players and we are really lucky to be here and we should enjoy it more sometimes.
