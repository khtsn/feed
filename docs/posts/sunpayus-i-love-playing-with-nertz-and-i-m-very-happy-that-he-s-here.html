SunPayus: "I love playing with NertZ, and I'm very happy that he's here"

G2 continue their run through the Stake Ranked Episode 1 upper bracket after defeating BetBoom in the second day of play, dishing out revenge against the Russians for their loss at the Roman Imperium Cup.

The tournament in Barcelona is G2's third on a tier-two event tour after signing Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz, serving as a first contact with their new rifler, and helping the team find out what they have to work on before getting back to business at IEM Rio.

NertZ: "I have full confidence in the players and the system we have"

"It's all mental at the end of the day, and the stronger we can get mentally from these events, the better it will be for us in the future," Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García says about these events. "CS2 is a very mental game right now, and that's the most important thing for us."

The Spaniard also opened up about his struggles with the AWP, which he says is not what it used to be ever since CS2 came out, and how he is taking a more hybrid approach. "on T, I don't take it unless I feel like the team needs the AWP," he says, "I prefer rifling in that case."

I spoke with NertZ yesterday, and he was talking about the importance of him being on the same page as the AWPer, you in this case, in the main pack. How is it for you to play with someone like him, with whom you get along so well?

The thing is, we get along very well both inside and outside of the game. We laugh a lot outside of the game, and that helps us be able to communicate well inside the game.

If we say something to each other, we'll have full confidence, and there will be no doubt because we see everything the same way. So for, me that's great, of course, and I love playing with him, and I'm very happy that he's here.

I've been having consistency issues for a long time now, although I do think that the AWP role isn't what it used to be with CS2

You've been up and down with G2, and haven't been at your best. Do you think that playing with someone you're on the same page with will help get the best out of you?

I think so. We'll need some time because we're not there yet with NertZ, there's still a lot we have to work through, but I can become better with him [on the team]. I've been having consistency issues for a long time now, although I do think that the AWP role isn't what it used to be with CS2. That's just a reality.

Every AWPer has good and bad games; ZywOo doesn't count, he's different. But in general, I'm happy, although I know that I can do more. I just need to fix things with the team, become stronger mentally, smile, and keep moving forward.

Speaking of ZywOo, as someone who plays more with the rifle, is that something you like to do more as well? Be more hybrid?

Yeah, I'm more hybrid these days. I play the AWP more on CT sides, and depending on the map, but on T, I don't take it unless I feel like the team needs the AWP — I prefer rifling in that case.

We're using these tournaments to get some game time, to see what mistakes we're making, and what we need to fix

I overheard HeavyGod cussing in Spanish during the match today. Have you been teaching him some bad words?

No, no! Puta madre was something Mario used to say sometimes if something didn't work out, and it just stuck with HeavyGod. [Laughs] You know, in Spain we say joder.

These tier-two VRS tournaments before the Major cut-off are filled with tension for a lot of teams fighting for qualification, but for you guys, it's to get some games with NertZ. Your spot is assured, so what's it like playing these events?

It's very important for us to go and win, and we've learned a lot. At our first tournament, when we lost to SINNERS, our expectations were really high, and we were really serious. Teams here are fighting for their lives, and for them beating us is not only that; it's really, really good for them because of the points in the ranking and everything.

We know we're favorites, but the reality is that we have to take everything with a grain of salt. We have a new player, and we're using these tournaments to get some game time, to see what mistakes we're making and what we need to fix.

It's also it's a big test for us mentally, and I have to say that I can see us getting better and better. Like today, we lost a full eco on Overpass, and we just brushed it aside, moved on, and won one ourselves later on.

It's all mental at the end of the day, and the stronger we can get mentally from these events, the better it will be for us in the future. I think that CS2 is a very mental game right now, and that's the most important thing for us.

When you finish with these VRS tournaments and get back to the routine, how do you see this team doing? NertZ is very hyped right now and really believes in the team and that you can win a title this year. Did he pass on that hype to you at all?

He's the new guy, so he's a bit more excited. I'm excited with the team, as well, but I'm going a bit more step by step. Also, because of how I see myself, I've been inconsistent, so I just want to focus on doing better than the day before.

As long as I feel like I'm getting better than the day before, that I've learned something I didn't know yesterday, then I'm happy. That's the way I see it. If we lose and we know what happened, then OK. If we lose some rounds, there's a lot we can learn, but it's especially about the mental part. I'm taking it very day by day, and he's a bit more hyped. [Laughs]

How about the team's system, playing in it, and being comfortable as an AWPer. How is that going?

I won't say we're going to change drastically, but there should be some changes coming. huNter- has taken a lot of responsibility on his shoulders during these months. It's something I've spoken with him, and with sAw, and he should be delegating some responsibility. Not to change anything big, but just some details that could make us a more consistent team.

Would that be giving players a bit more freedom, or how do you envision this?

We need to understand what the other team is doing better, and then allow players to take a bit more responsibility to make calls and tell huNter-, 'Hey, this is happening in my area, and we should do this or that.'

No IGL in the world can win a game calling alone; he needs feedback, help, movement, people to take space on their side of the map to help people on the other side of the map... Sometimes you can win a round as an IGL without even calling. You get a couple of entries, you know what's happening around the rest of the map, and the round is over.

huNter- also plays on the flanks sometimes, making it even more important for the main pack to make some calls, right?

Yeah, of course, that's very necessary. I don't know how the position changes will go in the future, so that he can call comfortably. But yeah, I try to help a lot, and we're all trying to give feedback and help him play it out. It'll come little by little. These are our first events with NertZ, and we're seeing how it's going with him and working on our mentality.

I was listening to huNter- in his post-game interview, and he said it feels like he's been IGLing for five years, but it has only been one. How have you seen his evolution as an IGL?

Oh, it's great. Being an IGL is so hard; you have to be so aware of so much going on, and I think he's doing it so well. With time and experience, I'm sure he'll get even better, and it'll flow better, but I think his transition has been so good.

He feels like it has been five years because the amount of work he does with sAw is crazy, so I'm sure it feels like he crammed all of those years into just one.

What would you say is his strong point as an IGL?

He's an IGL who can put up numbers, which is really hard.

You got revenge on BetBoom from Roman Imperium Cup. What was it like getting back at them?

They're a good team, even with the stand-ins. I don't know who they will play with if they qualify, but they're playing well. They're not an easy team to play; I was very surprised by them. We made a lot of mistakes on Anubis...

For the time we've had with NertZ, it's hard to have a complete map pool and not make mistakes on any side. We had some solutions on Anubis, and we would communicate them, but we couldn't execute them. Now I'm glad we beat them on Overpass because I know they're not bad on it, and we beat them there.
