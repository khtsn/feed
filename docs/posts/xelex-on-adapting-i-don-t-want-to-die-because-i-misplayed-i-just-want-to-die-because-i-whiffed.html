xelex on adapting: "I don't want to die because I misplayed, I just want to die because I whiffed"

17-year-old MOUZ NXT graduate Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze is bound for his first big arena at PGL Astana in his debut event as a full-time member of the organization's main roster.

After MOUZ beat Aurora to secure a Barys Arena berth, the Hungarian rifler said he was happy to reach playoffs at his first event, but admitted he is still adjusting to new situations and positions that have come with his promotion.

"I just sometimes need to still get used to those situations that I'm in, and I feel like I am overall adapting good to everybody else in the team," he said. "Like I can adapt in situations if we have to go 3v3 or whatever; it's just for, in my positions to have the best reactions and everything, that is what I still need the practice on. But it will come, so that's fine."

xelex also praised Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman's leadership, calling him "an absolutely crazy IGL," and spoke about his hopes heading into the arena. "I have no idea how I will play in the arena," he said. "I'm hoping for the best, to be honest, and I will just try to focus on my game and not the people outside because there is no point."

Congratulations, your first big arena appearance in your first proper event with MOUZ. What's going through your mind, what're your thoughts on this run so far?

I'm very happy about everything, to be honest. We had not that much practice, and I think playoffs at the first event is pretty good. Even though some games I couldn't come with my best individual level, I still have to get used to it a little bit, to my new positions, but I'm very happy that I can play in a big arena like here in Astana. I'm just happy.

A tough game against Aurora at the end. Just give me your thoughts on that matchup.

Dust2 was... I was getting fucked on B, to be honest (laughs). I just need to get used to it because it is pretty hard to hold it when it is a B split, and with Spinx, we are still trying to find the chemistry between us. After that, on T side, I came back, so I was very happy about that.

On Nuke, I don't know (smiles). Nothing was working for me, but I was happy that at least I was getting traded, we were winning the rounds, so I'm happy about that. And after all of that, in the end game, I was coming back a little bit with a few frags. I'm just happy that we won it in double overtime and we didn't go to a third map because I didn't kill.

How do you feel like you're adapting to the team so far?

I think it's going pretty good, to be honest. For me, it's all about being... like, I don't want to die because I misplayed something, I just want to die because I whiffed, you know? (laughs) If it that is the reason, then I'm fine with it.

I just sometimes need to still get used to those situations that I'm in, and I feel like I am overall adapting good to everybody else in the team. Like I can adapt in situations if we have to go 3v3 or whatever; it's just for in my positions to have the best reactions and everything, that is what I still need the practice on. But it will come, so that's fine.

You obviously also have a bit of a Hungarian connection with torzsi. How does that play into things and what has that been like?

If we are two alive, then of course we usually talk in Hungarian, but sometimes it is hard to switch because you're so much in the game and [speaking] in English, so sometimes you just forget. But sometimes we switch, and it is very nice to have someone that you can talk with any time in your own language.

The many LANs that we played [in NXT] helped me a lot, and I just earned a lot of experience

How has your time in NXT prepared you for this jump up in competition and joining the main team?

My first half year in NXT, we didn't really have any HLTV matches, and I didn't even have an HLTV profile; I was playing on ESEA. After that, the run that we had in the last half year, from last year August til March this year, that was like 240 matches in HLTV, so we had a really crazy grind.

The many LANs that we played helped me a lot, and I just earned a lot of experience. Even though it was tier-two/tier-three events, I still feel like I needed those HLTV games so that I could get used to those. And yeah, I feel like I'm adapting pretty fast overall, in everything.

You come into MOUZ at a time of change for them as well. xertioN is taking over as leader, jL is in for a couple of events. What are your thoughts on the players and how the team is overall?

xertioN is an absolutely crazy IGL (smiles), like he's very good at it, you know? It's my first time having that kind of an IGL who, every single time, can come up with something in every moment. That really helps the game and makes it a little bit easier, when you have someone who can always give you directions on where to go.

Justin, [jL], he is a very funny guy, to be honest (laughs). He is such a vibe, and I'm having a lot of fun with him. He's just bringing a good vibe in the team. Lotan [Spinx], just a good guy too, there is not much to say, but I kinda like him. He has his own things you know, he's just a good guy. Adam [torzsi], the usual, always bringing the energy, yelling, screaming, so that's nice. We have good energy in the team so I'm happy about that.

How about sycrone, how do you feel about him as a coach and what he brings? How has he helped you so far?

He's, of course, a pretty good coach. Overall, it's more of... I get some feedback on my positions, of course, but it's more me asking about where I feel like I'm a little bit inexperienced, I would say. But yeah, he is working a lot with xertioN, like they were working their asses off in the past two weeks, so I feel like the game plan and everything they are working on with Xyp9x is absolutely crazy. It's very good; they are both great.

I'm hoping for the best, to be honest, and I will just try to focus on my game and not the people outside because there is no point

Lastly, speaking of your experience: not only were you inexperienced at big events, but you're inexperienced when it comes to big stages, and that's where you're headed next. So what do you think about going into that arena and how you're going to perform there?

I feel like the biggest change for me was to come here and just play, even if they are studio games. I just had to get used to this LAN experience, that I'm playing with them here and everything, but I have no idea how I will play in the arena.

I'm hoping for the best, to be honest, and I will just try to focus on my game and not the people outside because there is no point. I'm just hoping for the best and I will try to do the best that I can, and hopefully it will go in a good way.
