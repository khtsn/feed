The Canadian spoke to us about losing karrigan to Falcons. "I don't think it was handled the best way," he said. "When you know people on a personal level, and you have history together, these types of situations hurt a lot more than what the outside community probably sees."

Twistzz also spoke about the opporuntiy to take the reins alongside coach Niclas "⁠enkay J⁠" Krumhorn. "Some players have only played under one or two IGLs in their career," he said, "and now they have a third opportunity with a new captain and a new coach, so I think it's a breath of fresh air so far."

FaZe will play at BLAST Rising and then go to IEM Atlanta in a quick two-tournament tour in the USA. "It is special," Twistzz said of these two events. "It's filled with opportunities to make people excited about FaZe again."

Let's start with karrigan leaving and the way it happened. After having such a bad season ending dramatically, the Major qualification part of it at least, and his rapid exit to Falcons. Do you think it happened in the best possible way?

Luckily the team hasn't put so much thought into what exactly has happened. We just moved on pretty fast, and we've been practicing, so it's good. How the situation went down? I don't think it was handled the best way. I think there are always better ways of going about this stuff, possibly more mature ways. So yeah, it's a bit disappointing to see how it did happen in the end. That's all I can say.

Especially with someone who built his legacy with the organization. He won trophies with TSM and MOUZ, but once it goes down in history, karrigan will be remembered as a FaZe player. Do you think this taints it in a way?

I'm not really sure. He's a legendary player, no doubt about it, a Hall of Fame player at the end of his career. Like I said, it doesn't matter how great you are, your reputation or whatever, if the community will see it one way, it's different, but when you know people on a personal level, and you have history together, these types of situations hurt a lot more than what the outside community probably sees. But yeah, that's all I can say about it.

I think there are always better ways of going about this stuff, possibly more mature ways

This brings forth a big moment for FaZe. Probably a big rebuild moment for FaZe. NEO is also gone, and Enkay J is here now. You'll be a big veteran voice in this, so how are you seeing this next FaZe and this moment that FaZe is in?

It's exciting. It's a brand new start for the organization in Counter-Strike. We have very good pieces here that I think have a different opportunity. Some players have only played under one or two IGLs in their career, and now they have a third opportunity with a new captain and a new coach, so I think it's a breath of fresh air so far.

I'm interested in the organization getting more involved. They haven't really had much say in past decisions. I wasn't here for what happened last year, but I know that Eddie [Han] has been really hard at work. He gets to be more of a General Manager now and do that, so I'm excited to see what happens next.

What do you make of your first impressions with Enkay J? Have you had some time to chat with him? How is that relationship building up?

Enkay has been great. He's a lot more like the past coaches that I've worked with. He's a bubbly personality, more fun, more of a leadership vibe. He has opinions, he comes from the German Counter-Strike scene, and there's a lot of discipline and principles built there, so I think he's been very helpful for me. Not only for me, I think for the whole team so far.

He's a bubbly personality, more fun, more of a leadership vibe

You're playing here with a stand-in, and for the next events, as well. Have you had time to meet and practice a bit? What do you make of Neityu coming in?

We've had a week of practice with Ryan. I'd say it has been good. I don't want to say it's gone really well or really bad. It's just been good. It's a good start for a new team, and given all of the variables that have changed for us, I'd say it has been good. I'm looking forward to playing, and everyone is excited to be at the event together and put our best foot forward.

There are a lot of links to maybe frozen looking elsewhere. Is that something that maybe worries you? Do you hope he'll stay and be a pillar in this rebuild? Have you chatted? Is there anything you can share about a possible future for this team?

I love David. I think he's an amazing player, an amazing teammate, and he's been an amazing friend since I returned to the org. I hope that he wants to be here for the rebuild and be a pillar alongside me and the other OGs here. I value him a lot, and at the moment I think his mind is with FaZe, but at the end of the day, it's the player's decisions and comfort. FaZe is not the type of organization to trap players, so I think if he's ever unhappy or happy, he's going to let the organization know.

Moving on from the Major disappointment, you still have some tournaments this season. How are you facing this now? What are you trying to get out of an event like BLAST Rivals?

We're a huge underdog at the event. What we're looking for is one or two upsets, particularly for our VRS ranking, but I think it would just be a huge confidence boost right now. That's kind of how it goes here; it's a learning process to see what we can punish, how much we can punish, where we can punish, what our map pool is like, how people are reacting to playing better teams, and using this event as all of this footage and review we can have for the next week. Then we go into Atlanta feeling really strong, that's kind of how I picture the timeline, but of course, anything can happen in CS. But yeah, I think that this event is a huge growth point for this current five.

With the team going through turmoil, you talk about a growth point for this five. Is there a bit of a feeling of unease because you don't know what's coming next? Is it hard to look forward to this end of the season, knowing that there's probably going to be movement in the off-season?

I'm not sure. I take a lot of responsibility and pride at the moment. If I can be a great captain and mesh the team together, then maybe people start to see more real potential in themselves and more belief. I really think it's in my hands to make this five work. So yeah, I take that as a personal challenge.

I really think it's in my hands to make this five work. So yeah, I take that as a personal challenge.

You're starting against NAVI, a team that seems to have gone in an opposite direction from you guys. They were a bit shaky at the beginning and then have been growing as they prepare for the Major. How do you envision this match-up between the two teams as they are now in their current forms?

No matter the level of either team, I think FaZe vs. NAVI makes for a spectacular match-up. Funnily enough, both teams find a kind of pleasure in playing each other. It's fun. I think the players match up well against each other. All of B1ad3's past rosters and everything, it has always been competitive, so I look forward to playing them. It's always a pleasure to play the top five teams.

You have a couple of events in the US. You're Canadian, but the org is American, and of course, you're a part of the scene in general, even if from different countries. Is there anything special about it for you?

It is special. It's filled with opportunities to make people excited about FaZe again. That's an objective, as well. I do think I represent the North American region, so for me it's important to make it to the arenas here and in Atlanta.
