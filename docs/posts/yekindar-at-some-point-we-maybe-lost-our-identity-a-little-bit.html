YEKINDAR: "At some point we maybe lost our identity a little bit"

FURIA are one win away from earning Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko a home arena berth at PGL Astana after convincing wins over Monte and HEROIC, showing a return to form after getting bounced in last place from BLAST Rivals Fort Worth.

"Honestly, I don't know what happened, but after BLAST Rivals it clicked," Mareks "⁠YEKINDAR⁠" Gaļinskis told HLTV after beating HEROIC. "Even when we played practice after getting eliminated 0-2, we were owning. Some things, some little things... we needed something so small that was missing, and that loss gave us a lot so we're not very sad about that."

The Latvian explained how the team is rediscovering its identity after some moments where they were "burning the kitchen," but admitted — while referencing Rihanna — that there is still much work to be done.

"Sometimes you look at Vitality and you see how Vitality is playing, and you think like, 'Oh, we can play like that,'" he said. "But we have different players, stylistically completely different players, and I think at some point we maybe lost our identity a little bit. This was something that we've been focusing on lately."

Mareks, you're 2-0 after that. A little close in the end, but you're only one match from the arena now. How does that feel?

Obviously it feels great, because this season has been a little bit on the rougher side for us in terms of reinventing ourselves. Many individuals in the team are trying to find a new pattern and new beliefs on how to play because we definitely... we saw last season that we can be better in some aspects, and we tried to change it.

And at some point, I think we forgot how close all the games last year were, and maybe got a little bit too stressed about the rounds, that we were not playing perfectly or whatever, and this is something we're focusing on now, especially after the BLAST Rivals exit.

We needed something so small that was missing, and that [Rivals] loss gave us a lot so we're not very sad about that

Honestly, I don't know what happened, but after BLAST Rivals it clicked. Even when we played practice after getting eliminated 0-2, we were owning. Some things, some little things... we needed something so small that was missing, and that loss gave us a lot so we're not very sad about that. And of course, we're happy to be in Kazakhstan, the homeland of Danil [molodoy], and try to support him.

Would you say that the Rivals exit was a bit of a kick in the ass in that way, then?

No, I wouldn't... I think the kicks in the ass were a little bit before. The Rotterdams and the EPLs and all these things. I feel like Rivals, we kinda... we got a little bit unlucky because I feel like how GamerLegion and FaZe played was so unorthodox that... you're going to play these games like maybe one time a season, or something.

It was very hard to play against them, because for example, GamerLegion on Mirage was playing very proactive CT side, pushing Ramp, pushing B, and they were getting their calls right. And FaZe, for example, if you remember the Nuke game, Twistzz was getting so many timings in Main (laughs) which usually doesn't happen in our game.

That's why, for us, we kinda talked it out, we focused on the areas of what we need to change and how we need to adapt. I feel like everybody kinda came to the same conclusion, that we just need people to make decisions for themselves and focus on their areas, and of course shoot, individually. I'm not saying somebody specific, everybody sometimes is having bad games and whatever, and we're trying to find the consistency.

Talking about fixing the system, I know even last year you mentioned to me that you weren't playing perfectly, there was stuff that you wanted to change even back then but just didn't have time. You've had some time now, but you've talked about maybe overdoing it a bit. Can you expand on what you've been working through and how you've dealt with it now?

Yeah, I mean I'm not going to go into details about what specific things are where, but there are definitely like, burning the kitchen moments a little bit at some point. I feel like the difference is that sometimes you look at Vitality and you see how Vitality is playing, and you think like, 'Oh, we can play like that.' But we have different players, stylistically completely different players, and I think at some point we maybe lost our identity a little bit.

This was something that we've been focusing on lately, and even though we won two games here pretty comfortably, it's still not the end of the road and we have to still work, work, work. (Laughs) As Rihanna said it, 'Work, work, work' (laughs).

molodoy struggled a bit in Rio, struggled at Rivals, but he really seems like he is back to his best here. Talk to me about that and what you're from him.

Yeah, the thing is, with Danil, he... I feel like at some point, he wanted to do a little bit of everything, and he was trying out new styles, new approaches, and new philosophies. And basically, we were kinda adapting our system to what he wanted and whatever, until he finally, after Rivals, realized his very strong traits that we can utilize.

And sometimes, there are going to be hard games where it's going to be harder to utilize his strong traits, and I feel like right now he is focusing a lot on, instead of being an independent one player in the team, he is focusing a lot more on being a part the system and truly part of the team. Because last year, there were many moments of individualism — there still are now, but the difference is that the teamwork part is a lot better now.

I'm focusing on sponging out all the information that I can in these last couple of months [with FalleN]

Going back to Rio, we haven't talked to you since FalleN announced that he would be retiring at the end of the year. How did you take that?

Dude, this guy is telling that he is going to retire like every fucking event, bro (laughs). One thing is, everybody started writing on social media about how FalleN is a legend and whatever, and I kinda took it a little bit of... I didn't write anything because, well, I have half a year still to play with him, and when it's going to be his last game, then we're going to talk about it.

But obviously, FalleN has been such an inspirational figure for many, not only our team. Of course molodoy, who got out of nowhere into a tier-one team, me, who had a rough year in Liquid or whatever And of course, you can learn a lot and everything, but specifically for me, I'm focusing on sponging out all the information that I can in these last couple of months. Because it's very rare that you can actually meet and be so close with a person who has so much experience, and I'm not only talking about in the game, but also outside of the game. He's truly an inspirational figure.

When he does retire, it's going to leave you without an in-game leader. You've had that role before, so where do you see yourself fitting in when that does eventually happen, and how are you trying to plan for that eventuality?

Yeah, listen, there are a couple of things that can happen: one, FalleN doesn't retire (laughs). The second one depends on who is available on the market during that time; is there a player who fits FalleN's roles and he can also IGL, or is there a player who fits FalleN's roles but he cannot IGL? If there is an IGL who is playing rotation roles and if anybody in the team is willing to switch and go anchor.

So there are many variables that can change during that time, and of course we're trying to figure out and plan ahead, but it's very hard because it's still half a year to go. And in half a year, anything can change.

There are chances for the plebs now

Lastly, there is no Vitality here, the team that has been everyone's boogeyman for half this year and most of the last as well. Without them here, but still having challengers like Falcons, like Spirit, what do you make of where FURIA stands in the field here?

I don't think anybody in the team was thinking about this. I feel like we always prefer to play against Vitality if we can because after every Vitality game that we've played, we came out with so much knowledge and so much experience that we gained that it just makes you better. In the long run, I feel like maybe losing an event or two, but becoming a better team, is just better. But of course, yeah, there are chances for the plebs now (smiles), for the normal teams and whatever.

But Rio was a very great sign for us, because after the Krakow final, I don't believe we played against them, and IEM Rio was the semi-final we played against them and we were very close. Honestly, there were specific details that changed the outcome of the game, and these details are what helped us to beat them last year, for example. Truly, to beat Vitality you have to have a good day, and they have to have a little bit of a bad day, you know?

But every time we learn, and it's truly amazing that people were talking about the Astralis era and how it will be for ages and they're the best team in the world, and now suddenly there's Vitality, right? So this is something that, in my opinion, is very good for the game that's developing other teams, other organizations, and just helps the scene. It's very cool.

If you're only thinking about how to beat Vitality, you're going to lose to everybody else, and this is what we don't want

Does not having them here leave a bit of an open door for you?

Nah. I mean, the thing is, yeah they are very good and they're winning every event or whatever, but I don't think other teams are so much behind them. Like for example, Falcons, we lost to them in the third-place decider in Rio, and they're a very good team. Now, with karrigan, I don't know yet how they're going to be adapting. Spirit, of course, they needed time and now they have the time, so they are also on an upward trajectory.

So there are definitely still teams that you have to, not necessarily worry about, but you have to respect. Even Monte or HEROIC, who we played, those are young teams, and we've lost to many of these young teams. Even BLAST Rivals against GamerLegion, for example. I don't know if FaZe is considered a young team or not (smiles). But nowadays, in CS, if you're only thinking about how to beat Vitality, you're going to lose to everybody else, and this is what we don't want.
