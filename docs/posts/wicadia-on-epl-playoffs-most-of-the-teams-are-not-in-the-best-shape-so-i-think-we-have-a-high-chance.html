Wicadia on EPL playoffs: "Most of the teams are not in the best shape, so I think we have a high chance"

Aurora are set to embark on their ESL Pro League playoff campaign, earned through a successful 3-1 run through the previous stage of the competition, which included victories over G2 and their upcoming quarter-finals opponents, Legacy.

Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın is confident of his team's chances at the LAN event in Sweden, and whilst he acknowledges Legacy's virtues, he still feels ready to take them on. "They have some really crazy stuff and their aim is really sharp, so it's always going to be an exhausting and long game. I think if we just keep our mentality good and keep fighting, we will manage to beat them."

HLTV caught up with Wicadia on the media day ahead of the EPL playoffs to chat about his team's performance in the previous stage, his confidence in facing a field absent of Vitality, and the adaptation of teammate Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici.

How are you feeling about your team's form after the group stage?

I think until this moment we played good Counter-Strike, and we didn't have pressure because it was an online event. It's not going to be such a big arena, so I think we will keep playing good and hopefully we're going to do some good stuff.

How do Aurora feel about playing online, is it comfortable?

Yes. Online is always more comfortable than LAN, but for us it's a bit better.

Just in general how are you feeling about Aurora's year so far? You added a new player in the off-season, how do you feel about the way the team is playing?

We added soulfly and I think he is adapting fast and well, he's a really good player and vocal in-game also. I think when he's fully adapted, it's going to be great for us.

How long do you think that will be, before you are confident that he is at top level with Aurora?

Not so long I think, maybe one or two months, something like this.

You have the ESL Pro League playoffs coming up, and Vitality are not in attendance. Does this give you extra confidence it's a tournament you can go and win?

I'm not gonna lie, yes, because Vitality and Falcons are not here. Most of the teams I think right now are in low form, they are not so good, not in the best shape, so I think we have a high chance in this one.

I think they are playing much crazier. arT is really doing some loose moves and that kind of stuff

Who do you see as your biggest rival for this tournament?

Spirit and MOUZ.

You've got Legacy as your next opponents, and they are a team you have played quite a bit in recent times. You played them last year in the final in Bucharest, and you played them in this group stage now. How are you feeling going into that game?

They are a really hard team to play against, they have some really crazy stuff and their aim is really sharp, so it's always going to be an exhausting and long game. I think if we just keep our mentality good and keep fighting, we will manage to beat them.

They've changed IGL since you played them last year, did you see much difference in the way they play?

Yes, I think they are playing much crazier. arT is really doing some loose moves and that kind of stuff, and sometimes it works, you know?

How do you feel playing against that more crazy loose style? Do you prefer playing against that, or a more structured team?

I think the second one.

Looking forward to the rest of the year, did you set goals for your team, considering you changed a player in the off-season? What are your aims for this year as a team?

We are trying to go step by step actually. Our first goal was to reach playoffs, every single tournament we have this, and then after playoffs we are setting a new goal, like semi-finals or finals. We go like this, we didn't set a goal from the beginning of the year.
