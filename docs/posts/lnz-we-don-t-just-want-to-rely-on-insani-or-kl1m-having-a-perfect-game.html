LNZ: "We don't just want to rely on insani or kl1m having a perfect game"

MIBR have recovered from their day one shock loss to THUNDER dOWNUNDER by picking up hardfought wins against TYLOO and Liquid.

They now sit 2-1 ahead of their first best-of-three tomorrow, comfortable reading for a team that has high hopes after a top-four finish at CAC.

Captain Linus "⁠LNZ⁠" Holtäng is hoping for a run to Stage 3, having just missed out on that mark when he was at HEROIC in Austin, but there is a cloud over the team after news broke that Klimentii "⁠kl1m⁠" Krivosheev's loan deal from G2 was set to expire.

For all of their good form, this may be the last time we see this quintet together. Losing kl1m would also leave LNZ as the only international player on the team.

But the Swede presented as unbothered by the prospect: "I'm not worried at all, at least, about what's going to happen [to me]. If he stays or if they choose a Brazilian player, I don't really mind. For me, I just want the team to be the best it can be, right?"

Listen to the interview here.

Okay, I saw your reaction, felt like a good win then. Tell me how it feels.

It feels amazing. I think we had a rough first game here versus the THUNDER dOWNUNDER team. But I'm really happy we could bounce back with two wins in a row after that. One game now for advancing, and yeah, that feels good.

What do you think happened against THUNDER dOWNUNDER? Is it just the classic Day 1 Major, just not really warmed up yet?

Maybe not that we weren't warmed up, but we were maybe not ready to play the proactive series that we usually play. I felt like we kind of fell into our shell a bit, and we didn't play proactively at all on the Inferno CT. And yeah, if you lose the economic control of the half, then it's kind of rough. So I think that's just mainly what happened.

I'm not worried at all, at least, about what's going to happen. If he stays or if they choose a Brazilian player, I don't really mind.

And then today, of course, I mean, kl1m was outstanding. What's it like trying to kind of get the best out of him and insani at the same time?

I mean, it's maybe not the focus, like how to get the most out of him and both at the same time. But we try to always put them both in situations where they can thrive. But not specifically them, but just in general. And I think today was maybe more him just taking the game in his own hands and maybe not me actually doing so much for him. Which was really nice to see. I'm really happy for him that he could play so well today.

And I have to ask, obviously there's rumors about his future now, because we know his loan finishes at the end of this major. What's kind of your view from within the team? Do you know anything about the chances of him staying?

Yeah, obviously I cannot say so much about it. It's more for like MIBR and G2 to agree on, right? And for MIBR to choose what direction they want to go in, right? Because he was signed last year, right? And after that, they signed a completely different IGL and coach. And they have to evaluate what's fitting the team best, right? And what that is, I don't know.

Do you think it affects your future at all in terms of the international direction? Is that something that's been communicated to you in terms of... Because this team's gathering steam now, it'd kind of be a shame to see you all break up.

I'm not worried at all, at least, about what's going to happen. If he stays or if they choose a Brazilian player, I don't really mind. For me, I just want the team to be the best it can be. And yeah, I have now nothing more to add about it.

We both want us to play this proactive CS and show balls when we play

I also did want to ask about LETN1 and the system that you guys are bringing into this team. Does it really feel like you've got that buy-in? Because that's kind of how it looks.

Yeah, I felt like we've got a lot of buy-in from the players. It feels really like we have the trust from everyone, which is really nice to have as two leaders. And I think the system that maybe I brought with my calling is actually not that different from HEROIC, because I kind of have my way how I like it. And it's also what they brought me in to do. So I'm really happy that it also works with LETN1, that he can also contribute on how I like to do things, but still bring what he likes, you know?

How different do you think you guys actually see the game then? Because what's that kind of synergy like, do you think?

It's hard to say. I think we are very similar, but also very different in many ways, which is very hard to explain. But I think we both want us to play this proactive CS and show balls when we play. And we want all five to contribute as much as possible. And we don't just want to rely on insani or kl1m having a perfect game. You know, we both see the game that we need all five players to contribute to be a good and consistent team.

Last question on expectations for the major. We know you've had some good results recently. Does that kind of change things coming in? What would you be happy with at the end?

I want to at least make it to stage three, because last year when I was in Austin, my last major that I played, then I only made it to stage two. So that's my personal goal to at least make it there. But we didn't set any like result goals, but I think everyone of us knows that if we show our best CS that we've done the last couple of weeks in CAC and etc., we can make it really far and upset some really good teams. So that's what I'm hoping for.
