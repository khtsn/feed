NRG gets revenge, sends Paper Rex to lower bracket

NRG NRG North America Rank #1 keiko Georgio Sanassy brawk Brock Somerhalder mada Adam Pampuch skuba Logan Jenkins Ethan Ethan Arnold clinched its spot in the VCT Masters Santiago Upper Final, earning a four-day rest thanks to a revenge 2-0 victory over Paper Rex Paper Rex Asia-Pacific Rank #1 invy Adrian Jiggs Reyes Jinggg Wang Jing Jie f0rsakeN Jason Susanto d4v41 Khalish Rusyaidee something Ilya Petrov who defeated them in the Swiss Stage.

The series was played out on NRG's pick of Pearl and Paper Rex's pick of Bind. NRG won Pearl 14-12 and then Bind 13-11, sealing a tight series sweep.

In a chess match of sorts, both teams adjusted their comps for this match, reinventing themselves to get an edge over the other. That chess match began on Pearl, where PRX moved away from its unique comp of Yoru, Raze, Veto, and Fade in favor of a shift of roles, which introduced a Vyse, Sova, and a Fade. Meanwhile, NRG stuck with its solo Waylay comp.

Early on, the series was back-and-forth, but NRG looked to have a better grip on the map. From down 3-2, NRG went on a run of four straight round wins. While NRG looked to spread the defense thin, Paper Rex looked to play more proactively, frequently playing up in A Main to feed Jinggg orbs, cycling his Phoenix Run It Back ultimate, a useful tool for retakes. something's Outlaw also looked to exploit NRG's tendencies to buy light sheilds, but returning Outlaw fire kept those attempts relatively at bay. Momentum kept changing through the end of the first half, as PRX clawed back two of the last three rounds to enter halftime down just 7-5.

PRX appeared to start the second half with all of the momentum, with a would-be bonus round being the only thing stopping the Pacific powerhouse through the first five rounds. From there, though, NRG entered cruise control. Well-coordinated retakes and solid anchors earned NRG another run of four rounds to reach match point, but PRX again did not go down without a fight. Well-coordinated execs, with late-round play consisting of wraps through mid and reliance on post-plant utility, helped PRX force overtime. Once in OT, NRG rolled, doing well to maintain man advantages after picking up first bloods.

NRG drops just one player on the final round of the map.

On PRX's pick of Bind, both teams adjusted their comps in an attempt to paint white-out over the opposing team's scouting report. PRX moved away from its Veto comp to field its third different Bind comp in four appearances this year, a double-Initiator, double-Controller comp with Gekko-Skye and Brimstone-Harbor. NRG got into reinventing, too, opting away from its solo Waylay comp on the map for a Yoru-Raze double-Duelist pairing, with Brimstone and Viper the Controllers.

Paper Rex started the series with pistol and anti-eco rounds, forcing a timeout from NRG head coach bonkar , who seemed to have identified an issue. As is standard for the head coach, NRG's play improved following the timeout, as NRG won six of the next seven rounds, tightening the seams on its attack to enter halftime up 8-4.

NRG won the pistol round to start its defense half, but disaster struck soon after. A thrifty round, won with four Classics and a Bucky, helped PRX spark a run of four straight rounds to pull to within one round. The next five rounds were once again tight, resulting in an 11-11 deadlock. With the score tied, skuba put an exclamation mark on what was already playing out to be a big day for him with a resounding 4K.

skuba with four of his most pivotal kills in the series to reach match point.

On match point, a chaotic cross-map round played out, with teammate mada crowned the hero with a 4K of his own to close things out.
