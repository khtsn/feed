Since qualifying for the Major, SINNERS have continued to grind out online events, and they are now undertaking their sole notable LAN outing ahead of the Major in IEM Atlanta.

The team's Atlanta campaign did not kick off as they may have hoped, as SINNERS took a map off of GamerLegion before suffering back-to-back map losses following disappointing CT-side showings. SINNERS now have their backs to the wall, preparing to take on Passion UA for a shot at keeping their IEM Atlanta run alive.

After his team's loss to GamerLegion, SINNERS captain Sebastian "⁠beastik⁠" Daňo spoke with HLTV regarding the team's difficulties on the defense during the series, the transition from calling in Czech to English, the team's recent Major qualification, and the recent retirement of Czech veteran and former teammate Tomáš "⁠oskar⁠" Šťastný.

Commiserations on the loss. On Inferno and Nuke, you guys had very difficult CT-sides. What do you think the issue was there?

I don't know to be honest, and on Inferno I have actually no clue what was happening, because I felt like we started on B, did our things, what we actually do on B, and then we got killed by REZ. Every round, I felt like they had openings from him or Tauson. It was very weird, I don't think we were not playing our game, I just don't know what happened, to be honest.

On Nuke, I have a bit of an idea of what was happening; we lost so many 5v2s, we knew what was happening. I don't know how we can lose so many rounds in such a fashion, to be honest, and we almost lost a full eco or whatever it was. Today, the CT sides, I feel like we couldn't communicate properly, and it cost us so many rounds, and I don't know if we were a bit afraid to play or stressed or whatever it was, but I felt like it was not us on CT sides at all.

That we are at a Major means nothing for me, I want to have some deep run, or I will not be that satisfied with us only qualifying with the Major

Sebastian "⁠beastik⁠" Daňo

You guys do not have many opportunities to play LANs; you've been with SINNERS for a few years, and this is one of the biggest you've been to, and your teammates don't have many more either. Do you think that maybe played a role in the loss?

I don't think so, to be honest. Actually, with the lineup we have, I feel like we're way better on LAN, and we're actually travelling to some tier-two LANs, and we are getting some of the experience. [IEM Atlanta] is nothing really special to be honest; we are not playing on stage or anything, it's still a studio LAN, so I feel like it's very comparable to tier-two LANs that we were playing before. I don't know, to be honest, I cannot say.

You guys recently just qualified for the Major, and for you especially, you've come so close with SINNERS so many times. How does it feel to finally lock down a Major spot?

It feels very good, but at the same time, it feels weird, because it happened online, and it didn't happen on LAN, so we were lacking some of these real emotions that we have on LAN actually, and it would be a crazy party if we made it on LAN. We were lacking some of this, but I'm very happy because it was our goal that we were grinding for for so many years, especially with SHOCK, he was doing a lot for it, and every time we were so close, but it didn't work out. I'm happy that it worked out, but the job is not done. That we are at a Major means nothing for me, I want to have some deep run, or I will not be that satisfied with us only qualifying with the Major.

I feel like it was way easier than I expected, and I think it's way better to be honest than talking in Czech

Sebastian "⁠beastik⁠" Daňo on calling in English

One big difference between qualifying this time around and your attempts in the past is that SINNERS is now an international English-speaking lineup. For you as a captain, what has that transition been like?

I expected it to be a bit more difficult than it actually is. I've been calling six years in Czech, so I was a bit afraid of my English level and how I'm going to call in these stressful situations. I feel like it was way easier than I expected, and I think it's way better to be honest than talking in Czech. We also brought international people with different mentalities and different energy than we had with the Czech. Czechs, we are mostly like, I would mostly say, a bit dead inside. We like to tilt a bit.

It was sometimes hard to make it work with Czech people, and these guys are quite opposite. When I'm tilting, they hype me up a bit, so it's way easier for me also, since I put myself into these star role positions where I have way more impact on the game, not just anchoring and waiting to be killed. They gave me the trust, and I feel like it's the best thing that could have happened to me.

When you say calling in English is better than calling in Czech, do you mean it's because you have access to international players? What do you mean specifically?

Specifically, it's easier for me because we have a lot of keywords that we were not using in Czech; maybe if we were using a bit more keywords in Czech it would have been easier for me. But I was a type of guy who was talking a lot, and explaining the details; sometimes that cost me some life or lost focus while calling.

Now, it's like we have a stage one, and how we want to proceed to stage two, and how we want to end the game, just by keywords. So I can just tell them the keyword, and they know what they should do, and they'll sometimes just say, 'I can do this, and I'll do this,' and they're adapting to what I say so I can focus a bit more on my individual level.

Looking ahead, you'll be facing off against Passion UA in an elimination match. Any thoughts on that game?

I don't watch their games that much, but I don't think they're a better team than GamerLegion, so if we lose against them, it would be a pretty bad showing, for me especially. I expected us to at least fight for the playoffs or something, so for me, it would be very disappointing.

We are looking forward to playing against them, because we haven't played against them at all I think, I don't remember at least. It will be nice to play against a team that we've never played actually, because with GamerLegion we've played them a couple times already, so it's sometimes 50/50 how these best-of-threes go. So, yeah, that's what I expect.

I feel like he's one of the greatest to ever touch the game, and I still believe if he was still playing he would still be really good in some team

Sebastian "⁠beastik⁠" Daňo on oskar's retirement

You have a couple weeks off after this event before the Major. What is the game plan? Is there a bootcamp planned out?

Yeah, we are definitely bootcamping before the Major. At least seven or eight days we will go full grind to prepare for the bootcamp, and then give our stratbook a bit of a fresh look, because I feel like we are not changing stuff that much, and we might be a bit readable, to some teams that are actually prepping a lot. The main goal is to just have some rest after America, and then just go into the grind again and make a deep run at the Major.

Last thing I wanted to ask you about. Not too long ago, longtime teammate of yours and legend of the Czech scene, oskar, announced his retirement. I just wanted to know if you had any thoughts to share about that announcement, and maybe the impact he's had on your career.

Yeah, I mean oskar was one of my idols. I was following him since 1.6, since I was 12 or 13 years old. He was my idol, one of those guys who you look up to because they made it, they were so good, I remember every one of his fragmovies that he had on YouTube, and I was like his biggest fan, watching his every stream, subscribing and everything.

He's a big inspiration for every Czech player, and I'm kinda sad he made the decision, but I think it was... you know he had his age, and it was inevitable at some point, so it had to come, and unfortunately he didn't have any offers. I feel like he's one of the greatest to ever touch the game, and I still believe if he was still playing he would still be really good in some team. Unfortunately, he made maybe some decisions that maybe cost him some of these teams. That's unfortunate, but he's our idol.
