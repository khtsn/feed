B1ad3: "With the kinds of mistakes we did, we don't deserve to be in the top eight"

Natus Vincere missed out on the Cologne Major playoffs after a tough draw that put them against Spirit, Legacy, The MongolZ, Falcons, and G2, but Andrey "⁠B1ad3⁠" Gorodenskiy isn't looking to find excuses for their early exit.

"We had all the chances versus Falcons, all the chances versus G2 today, but if you're not closing the rounds with an advantage, it means you're not ready to play [the playoffs]."

When asked about the future of the team, Natus Vincere's coach admitted that the organization will look to the transfer market to build something "equal" to Vitality.

"It's a transfer season, agents will come to us and make offers," he told HLTV after the loss against G2. "I will see with the organization and try to build a plan to be stronger next season."

Listen to the interview on our Spotify channel.

Commiserations. Two very tough losses in the last couple of days, I'm sure. Tell me your initial impressions on not making the playoffs here.

Very sad. We were looking forward to going to playoffs, we played good CS in the last two months. But I think, unfortunately, with the kinds of mistakes we did, we don't deserve to be in the top eight. It's obvious because we played all the good teams, we had all the chances versus Falcons, all the chances versus G2 today, but if you're not closing the rounds with an advantage, it means you're not ready to play this.

The Major is the biggest event, all top teams coming there, and if there are any strong teams, they will be here at the Major. It always finalizes a really strong top-eight, and I think we were not good enough to get there. We were still showing good CS, obviously, but not good enough to be top eight.

We were looking forward to going to playoffs, we played good CS in the last two months

How difficult is it considering just a few details could have made the difference yesterday and today?

The Major always creates high pressure and you need to be able to play your game in high-pressure situations, that's all I can say.

We also saw you quite frustrated. Is that also because of the high pressure?

Yeah. Whenever I'm frustrated, it's usually because something we discussed several times, we don't do. That's usually why I'm frustrated. For example, during the timeout I can say something like 'on Inferno, guys, focus B, please, focus B, protect B, don't think about anything else' and we're stacking four A for no reason. At this moment you don't know what else you could do and you're desperate, basically (chuckles).

Whenever I'm frustrated, it's usually because something we discussed several times, we don't do

Is this individual mistakes, macro mistakes, communication? What was it that they couldn't make the right decisions?

A little bit of each thing. You mean today or yesterday?

Throughout both of the tough losses.

Yesterday we should have closed the game. We pussied out. We didn't play where we should have played, like in some situations where you see an advantage, you need to use it, and not be scared to do it. Versus these kinds of teams like Falcons, you don't release this pressure, you must keep the pressure on them constantly and they must have discomfort playing. We didn't do it when we could and they jumped back.

Yesterday there were also high-pressure situations in OT of Anubis with an advantage, where you need to play more clearly, have more clarity and more synergy. For this synergy you need to speak vivid so that the guy can understand what you want from him, and also to build micro plans. This is all basics of tier-one CS, but it's not easy to do. We were struggling on all of these parts in some situations.

When you play really good, strong teams against each other, it's not something obvious happening. It's always small margins. And today on Mirage we lost 5v3 and one 4v2 or something like this, where we needed to create these plans and to be decisive in what to do. We were just a little bit passive, waiting for their mistake. It's basic stuff, but all teams do it, choke, or not being confident enough that my move will be correct, all this stuff.

This is what I mean by saying high-pressure situations. You need to be able to play your own game that you show in practice or other games. We couldn't, in my opinion.

You've always seen Majors as this big telling point of where the future of the roster lies. You were saying how in the lead-up to this Major you were showing improved form and grabbed a couple of wins along the way. How big of a setback is it not to make playoffs here and continue that progressions with the off-season coming up in mind? How much can you say about the future of this roster?

I think we will definitely become stronger next season, but I don't know yet or cannot say how. I'm pretty sure we will be much stronger next season. I will think about the strategy, overall, of the next season. Who knows, maybe we need changes.

If we cannot do this with this lineup, we will think about other options, and maybe we will find what we can do

Like I always say to you on HLTV, every season, every good tier-one team wants to build a legacy, to be a big trophy team, a constant trophy contender. And to be able to do this, organizations put a goal to do this. If we cannot do this with this lineup, we will think about other options, and maybe we will find what we can do.

I cannot say now that we wanna do it. I need to sit down to watch through demos we had. It's a transfer season, agents will come to us and make offers, and I will see with the organization and try to build a plan to be stronger next season.

Do you think there's going to be a lot of options out there? It feels like everybody's going to be looking out for changes this time.

I hope so, I hope so. We know there's Vitality building something unbelievable, if they win this third Major. You need to think you want to be one of the teams that win trophies when Vitality is not there, or you want to build something equal? Obviously, you want to build the same, so if we will see this option, we will always use it.
