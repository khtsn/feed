Hurricane Milton: What We Know About the Damage and Flooding in Florida

Still, officials in the region said it had escaped catastrophe. In Orlando, Milton left its mark in the form of downed street poles and trees. More than 120,000 customers were still without power in Orange County, which includes Orlando, as of Thursday evening.

Some areas of Central Florida saw 10 to 15 inches of rain, the National Weather Service said, adding that it could take days for the water in rivers and flooded areas to subside.

There were damaging tornadoes on the east coast.

Tornadoes spawned by Milton killed at least five people in St. Lucie County on the eastern side of the state and damaged dozens of homes in Martin County to the south of St. Lucie.

At least three of the victims resided at Spanish Lakes Country Club Village, according to the victims’ family members, a retirement community of mobile homes north of Fort Pierce. Officials said the rest of the victims’ identifications were not publicly available.

Many coastal communities on this side of the peninsula were also still reeling from the deluge of rain and surging tides on Thursday.

Officials in Volusia County, which includes Daytona Beach, were still trying to reach people who were left stranded in homes in areas cut off by flooded roadways. Also in that county, the sheriff’s office confirmed that two people died after trees fell on homes during the storm.
