NAVI move past new-look FaZe to make playoffs

Twistzz's debut as the new in-game leader of FaZe was soured with a 0-2 defeat.

Natus Vincere are through to the playoffs of BLAST Rivals Fort Worth after taking down FaZe 2-0 in their opening match. The EPL S23 champions were made to work for the victory after a comfortable win on Anubis to start the series, relying on excellence from Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri to claw back from a 5-10 deficit on Ancient to scrape victory in two maps.

"I feel like every interview this," Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen said while laughing in a post-match interview when asked about praising makazze for his performance, "and I think it's warranted. He's playing tremendous, no matter if it's the group stage or the playoffs."

FaZe's pick of Anubis, a map they were 0-4 on in the past six months, went as one might expect. Despite Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken taking over captaincy duties and the team likely putting in work on the map, little of their efforts showed in a lopsided first half.

Excellent teamplay from Natus Vincere and a few multi-kills from Valeriy "⁠b1t⁠" Vakhovskiy let them romp to an 11-1 lead at the break, and though FaZe looked better on the offense, the deficit left them no room to pull off a comeback.

Ancient was, predictably, a battle of Middle control. makazze and Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan both found high-impact multi-kills in the first half to gain the upper hand there, but NAVI's late-round efforts fell short as Twistzz and David "⁠frozen⁠" Čerňanský both had their moments to help FaZe lead 7-5 at the break.

A pistol win and first gun round conversion had FaZe knocking at the door of a third map, but makazze again proved a menace once guns came back up for Natus Vincere. The Kosovar rifler was nigh-unkillable in early duels, ending the map 8-3 in openers, and that made all the difference in powering an 8-1 run for NAVI to seize a 13-11 win.
