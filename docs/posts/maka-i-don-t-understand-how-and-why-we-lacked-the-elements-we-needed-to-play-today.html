Maka: "I don't understand how and why we lacked the elements we needed to play today"

3DMAX arrived in Rio looking to carry momentum forward from their fourth-place finish at PGL Bucharest, but rather than show the form they had in their latter group stage matches and initial playoff games, their campaign in Brazil ended in a similar whimper as to how the team played in the third-place decider against The MongolZ.

"I don't understand how and why we lack the elements we needed to play today, again," Bryan "⁠Maka⁠" Canda told HLTV after the match. "It happened against The MongolZ, it happened again today. Maybe it's too much pressure, too much stress, or maybe not enough. I don't know. It's hard."

The French team managed to win just two of the last 21 rounds of the series, securing only one in the second half of Inferno and losing 13 straight on Dust2 after winning the pistol. Maka explained that the team is working to implement a system that will help them find consistent results, and with a month off until their next appearance at CS Asia Championships, they'll have plenty of time to iron things out.

"Once NBK- and wasiNk came back, we tried to implement that, and this is something we believe in," he said. "It's hard to implement consistently, and that's what happens when you're out of the system and out of your mind. You're not in the right mindset to play the game, and you just get wrecked by those kinds of teams."

Listen to the interview here.

Commiserations, that was a brutal way to go out: two rounds out of the last 21 in the series. What is going through your mind after that?

Yeah, it's brutal, as you said. It's really hard to swallow this one. I felt like we went out of the tournament the same way we did in Bucharest, when we got smashed by The MongolZ.

I don't know, I don't understand how and why we lacked the elements we needed to play today, again. It happened against The MongolZ, it happened again today. Maybe it's too much pressure, too much stress, or maybe not enough. I don't know. It's hard.

The last couple of events where we struggled, I think it was a mixture of a lack of work and a lack of a system

That kind of seems like the story for 3DMAX this year, a couple of results that you feel like you should get, but they slip away. What has the conversation in the team been like with trying to correct this?I know you said it's hard to pinpoint the issues, but what are you exploring?

We're trying to build on a specific system we want to have with the coaches. Having the right person focus on the right thing, and I think we delievered most of the time when we respected that. Especially in Bucharest. The last couple of events where we struggled, I think it was a mixture of a lack of work and a lack of a system.

Once NBK- and wasiNk came back, we tried to implement that, and this is something we believe in. It's hard to implement consistently, and that's what happens when you're out of the system and out of your mind. You're not in the right mindset to play the game, and you just get wrecked by those kinds of teams.

You swapped in-game leaders, and that changes everything with the system too. Do you think that was a mistake, or that it's made it really hard to fix in such a short amount of time?

No, I think this was the right thing to do, definitely. We had a lot of technical points [where] we struggled a lot in the last six months. We didn't work properly and we didn't have the right system to rely on. Even though we had two good results, I think it was just built on momentum and not on something we can rely on.

It felt like Bucharest was more composed after we had our win against Voca, we understood the way we wanted to play, and until Astralis it was fine. Then against The MongolZ we collapsed, and today we collapsed again against G2.

Since we don't have the Major, I think the coaching staff will also look into the way we want to approach the player break

You also played at Bucharest, placed fourth there, and then travelled straight here. Did that contribute to your form here?

I don't think so. I could have said that when we went to Chengdu; it was really hard to go from Saudi Arabia to Serbia to China, back-to-back in China. But no, for now, I think we had a lot of travel, a lot of work, and it was not one of the things that put us in such bad form.

Speaking about needing some time to iron out that system, you now have a month off until CAC. How are you going to spend that time to try and fix these issues?

The management and coaching staff will have a look into it. For now, we need some rest. Just a couple of days, maybe less than a week, and we are getting back on track in maybe one week to work on all of this and watch why we have these kind of rough games.

Maybe we'll have a bootcamp, this is what the coach will decide. Since we don't have the Major, I think they will also look into the way we want to approach the player break because obviously, not having a tournament during June makes it different for us to head into the second part of the season.
