Following his team's elimination from IEM Atlanta, David "⁠frozen⁠" Čerňanský spoke with HLTV regarding FaZe's performance in the event, the new structure being implemented by Twistzz and enkay J, and his evaluation of Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen's choice to depart FaZe for Falcons.

Or you can listen to the interview here.

Commiserations on the loss, 0-2 to Vitality and out of IEM Atlanta. Can you evaluate how you feel about the team's performance throughout this event?

I mean, the first game was disappointing. I don't think we showed up at all, all of us, in lots of aspects. First day, we didn't really show up, which put us in the lower bracket, where we have to fight with our backs against the wall one more time. We won against NRG, which, in my eyes, is a team that we should beat any day.

You know, meeting Vitality is a little unfortunate in the lower bracket. On the first map, we definitely showed a great effort. I would even say that we could have won the first map, for sure. On the second map, I think we started to become a little more chaotic than it had to be. Some rounds... it's just the number-one team; if you do mistakes in the rounds, they will take it away from you. There were many rounds where they still had one or two players, that might've changed the outcome of the half, but it didn't. We played the number-one team, you make mistakes, they will punish you and capitalize on it.

When you say you guys were going to face off against Vitality, did you feel like they might be beatable after they lost to BetBoom?

I mean, I was looking forward to playing Vitality. Especially in the state we are in at the moment, you don't meet the top three or top five teams that often. So, playing Vitality, in my eyes, I was looking forward to it, to see where we stand, and how we're going to do. As you said, yesterday they lost to BetBoom, (laughs) they've been on a fucking long winning streak at the moment, so there for sure has to be a time where they become a little bit tired and become beatable. I think today was the day. I would say on the first map, we played much better than they did, to be honest.

The old FaZe, and the whole structure that we used to have, is gone

You've been working with Twistzz IGL and enkay J as coach for a little bit now. How would you evaluate the structure that they've brought in? Is it substantially different from what karrigan had before, or is it a continuation of that?

I mean, I would just that the old FaZe, and the whole structure that we used to have, is gone. It's a different generation of FaZe, everything we do, how we are. It's a completely new team, it is new.

You've spent a lot of your tier-one career with karrigan as your captain. How does it feel personally to be playing without him?

I played most of my career with karrigan, but I also had my time in MOUZ without karrigan. What we have going on now kinda reminds me a little bit of that time. I wouldn't say it's new. New environment, new people, new things to get used to, but that's just life.

Last karrigan question; you guys had a vlog that came out yesterday...

(laughs)

...and you had a comment where you said that you disagreed with karrigan's decision to go to a new team. Did you believe that if he had stayed, you could have turned things around?

Hard to say. I think the spot we were in, the position we were in with the results that we were showing, honestly, I feel like anyone who would have got a Falcons offer would have left at that point. Hard to say what would've happened if karrigan would stay. I don't even want to think about it, you know? I wish karrigan the very best.

You can see that some of the wins we got are way more convincing than they used to be

You've got Stake up next, about a week and a half before that. What's preparation gonna look like for Stake?

I mean, rewatching the games for sure. The games we played here, the games we played in Fort Worth, and some practices that we played in boot camp in between. Gotta reevaluate, see what we are missing, what things we need to improve on. We have one and a half weeks, as you said, probably a few days off between that. Back to work.

Do you feel like you guys are on the right trajectory?

Yes. You can see that some of the wins we got are way more convincing than they used to be. We need to sharpen a few things, we need to be always ready from the get-go, we cannot forget the little details. Just practice what we preach.
