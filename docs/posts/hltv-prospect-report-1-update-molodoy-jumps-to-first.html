Viva first joined Xi Lai as their Initiator in December between their Ascension win and the start of the 2025 season. While they had a middling Kickoff performance, Xi Lai proved their potential to be one of China's top teams with a near-perfect Stage 1 run that culminated in them lifting their first tier 1 trophy. As China's first seed, they exited their first international event of Masters Toronto 0-2.

Xi Lai later returned to the international stage for Champions Paris as the region's fourth seed. While they did not qualify for playoffs, a win over Sentinels gave them a higher placement than fellow Ascension squad Dragon Ranger Gaming, allowing them to automatically retain their VCT spot. deLb played for All Gamers for the past two years as their primary Sentinel, although he has also been seen flexing to various agents across the Duelist, Controller, and Initiator roles in that time. In 2025, All Gamers did not find their first win of the year until Stage 2, where they defeated Wolves Esports. They managed to collect a few more wins from there, qualifying to playoffs and exiting in the top six after being eliminated by Xi Lai in the lower bracket.

After capturing global attention with major upsets back at Champions Seoul, Trace has had a more turbulent 2025 season. Since their short-lived 0-2 appearance at Masters Bangkok, the team has not been able to return to the global stage, hovering around the middle of the pack in China with early playoff exits in both stages.

Trace is set to compete in the ongoing China Evolution Series: Epilogue offseason event and will play their first match with their new roster tomorrow against Titan Esports Club. Their announced six-man roster for the event includes Viva and deLb but notably omits Song "FengF" Xuefeng (宋学锋), although the team has not made any official announcements regarding his status on the team at this time.
