Viewer's guide to final pre-Major invite LANs

As the IEM Cologne Major invitation deadline looms, slated for Tuesday, April 6th, a slew of teams looking to secure their spots at the IEM Cologne Major will be spending the next two weeks at local LANs across the globe hunting for Valve Regional Standings (VRS) points.

Teams just on the cusp of a Major invite, including BIG, Gentle Mates, FaZe, Passion UA, and BetBoom, will be among those hopping from event to event, looking to secure prize money and LAN wins to push them up in the regional standings. Even big names that have effectively already secured a Major invitation, such as G2 and Aurora, are also set to take part in local LANs, setting their sights on Stage 2 or Stage 3 invites.

To help keep up with the onslaught of LANs, HLTV has assembled a handy guide, aggregating information on some of the more notable events taking place in the buildup to the invitation deadline.

ROG JOURNEY Spring 2026

Location: Stockholm, Sweden

Date: March 23-28

Prize pool: $30,000

Notable teams:

Kicking off the spree of local LANs is ROG JOURNEY Spring 2026, running over the span of six days, from March 23-28. M80 and GamerLegion are the most prominent teams in attendance, with the former side looking to cement their Major invite while the latter vies for a direct spot in Stage 2.

BC Game Masters Championship Season 1

Location: Bucharest, Romania

Date: March 24-26

Prize pool: $50,000

Notable teams:

The day after ROG Journey Spring 2026 begins, BC Game Masters Championship Season 1 will get underway in Bucharest, Romania. This event features numerous squads in the hunt for a Major spot, including HEROIC, Gentle Mates, Monte, BetBoom, Passion UA, BIG, and BESTIA, meaning that many Major dreams could meet their end here. The Romanian LAN will also be G2's first with Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz as they look to record five matches with the Israeli rifler before the invitation deadline.

FERJEE In House 2026

Location: Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Date: March 26-30

Prize pool: $28,140

Notable teams:

One of the largest local LANs set to take place in the Americas over the next two weeks will be FERJEE In House 2026, hosted in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. The tournament features most of the region's top teams, many of whom, namely Legacy, paiN, 9z, and MIBR have all but secured a Major spot. As such, this event will mostly be important for teams on the outer edge of the cutoff, such as Gaimin Gladiators, Imperial, and American squad Marsborne.

Roman Imperium Cup VII

Location: Vila Nova de Gaia, Portugal

Date: March 28-30

Prize pool: $12,000

Notable teams:

Roman Imperium Cup VIII in Vila Nova de Gaia, Portugal, will have a number of Major hopefuls from across the globe. BIG and HEROIC will be aiming to clinch spots from the European VRS, while Fluxo and BESTIA vie for one of the 10 Americas spots.

DraculaN Season 6

Location: Bucharest, Romania

Date: March 29 - April 2

Prize pool: $15,000

Notable teams:

The sixth iteration of DraculaN, taking place in Bucharest, Romania, is set to feature several top sides. Aurora and Liquid will be hoping for an extra boost in the VRS to get into an invitation to a higher stage of the Major, with the latter also trying to record more matches with Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa, while FaZe, Monte, and Passion UA will all be looking just to make the Major at all.

Parken Challenger Championship Season 3

Location: Copenhagen, Denmark

Date: March 31 - April 2

Prize pool: $11,518

Notable teams:

Parken Challenger Championship Season 3 in Copenhagen, Denmark, does not feature a long list of notable teams due to its overlapping dates with DraculaN, but it does have a handful of sides with a fair shot at Major qualification, such as B8 and BESTIA.

Stake Ranked Episode 1

Location: Barcelona, Spain

Date: April 1-4

Prize pool: $100,000

Notable teams:

Stake Ranked Episode 1 in Barcelona, Spain, features the largest prize pool of the various pre-Major local LANs, with a whopping $100,000 up for grabs at the eight-team event. Gentle Mates, Ninjas in Pyjamas, HEROIC, and GamerLegion will be looking to lock in their Cologne Major spots here, while BetBoom, Nemesis, and 9INE will be making last-ditch efforts to sneak into Stage 1. Ninjas in Pyjamas in particular will want to capitalize on this outing, as this is currently their only outing ahead of the deadline, and they are far from guaranteed a spot at the Major after a last-place BLAST Open Rotterdam finish.

XSE GangKui Cup Season 2 Finals

Location: Shanghai, China

Date: April 1-5

Prize pool: $15,000

Notable teams:

With few LAN opportunities throughout the year in Asia, the XSE GangKui Cup Season 2 Finals in Shanghai, China will be critical in deciding the allocation of the region's five Major spots. FlyQuest are already primed for a Major invite, but TYLOO, The Huns, and Rare Atom have by no means guaranteed their place, having to compete with SemperFi and THUNDER dOWNUNDER for spots in the region.

BetBoom Circuit X Mayhem São Paulo 2026

Location: São Paulo, Brazil

Date: April 1-5

Prize pool: $50,000

Notable teams: (Note: Signed up, but have yet to pay registration fee)

Following the conclusion of FERJEE In House, many of the very same teams will be headed down to São Paulo for BetBoom Circuit X Mayhem, where another $50,000 will be up for grabs ahead of the invitation deadline. The event will serve as the last opportunity for Imperial, Gaimin Gladiators, RED Canids, and Marsborne to clinch a Major spot.

MPKBK CIS LAN Season 4

Location: Moscow, Russia

Date: April 2-5

Prize pool: $50,000

Notable teams:

While Season 4 of MPKBK CIS LAN does not feature a long list of sides in Major contention, this event will be particularly important for K27. The Russian side, who won the last iteration of MPKBK CIS LAN, have been on a steady climb up the VRS, and a LAN win here could help push them across the finish line into the IEM Cologne Major.

Urban Riga Open 4

Location: Riga, Latvia

Date: April 3-5

Prize pool: $20,000

Notable teams:

FaZe (registered but unpaid)

Ending the day before the Major invitation deadline, Urban Riga Open 4 in Riga, Latvia, will be the absolute last chance for teams looking for a Cologne berth. The Latvian event will cap off BIG and BESTIA's lengthy local LAN tour across Europe, and will also give notable sides FaZe and Passion UA a potential lifeline for Major qualification. Sign-ups remain open until March 26, so other Major-hungry teams could potentially join in on the action in Riga.

HLC Belgrade PRO 2026

Location: Belgrade, Serbia

Date: April 3-5

Prize pool: $30,000

Notable teams: (Note: Responded to sign-up form, but not necessarily confirmed)

Rounding out the notable pre-Major LANs is Relog's HLC Belgrade PRO 2026. Originally slated to take place in Cyprus, the 16-team event was shifted to Belgrade, Serbia, as a result of security concerns. Though the team list has not been confirmed, the registration form has received interest from a wide range of names, including Liquid, Natus Vincere, Passion UA, and FaZe.
