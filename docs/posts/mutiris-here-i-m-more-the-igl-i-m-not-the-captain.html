MUTiRiS: "Here I'm more the IGL, I'm not the captain"

BC.Game endured a difficult campaign at PGL Bucharest, their single series win coming in a matchup against a Voca side fielding a last-minute stand-in. The tournament was reflective of BC.Game's form in general since the squad's initial promising run at IEM Krakow, which has seen the team slide to outside the top 50 of the VRS.

FOKUS eliminate BC.Game from Bucharest

"We just need to be better, that's the answer," Christopher "⁠MUTiRiS⁠" Fernandes admitted to HLTV when reflecting on his team's form, before expressing regret about the PGL Bucharest campaign specifically. "For us individually, I can speak for myself, it's a shame that we just came here and did not show a proper level."

The torrid string of results, compounded by a PGL Bucharest exit at the hands of FOKUS, will leave the future of the lineup very much in question. It's a question MUTiRiS found himself unable to answer. "When it comes to this place it's like sometimes I don't really know what is next. For me that's really difficult, I think that TaZ knows it, the organization knows it."

MUTiRiS spoke with HLTV after his team's loss to FOKUS to discuss their PGL Bucharest campaign, what exactly is going wrong for the team, and the interpersonal dynamics within the squad.

Give me your reflections on this tournament overall for your team.

Well I think that we didn't come with so much expectation. It's hard because when you come here, it's a reflection of pracs. I think that we are not finding consistency, different plans, misunderstood in a lot of situations. At this level all those little things make the difference and I think we are suffering a little bit of that.

What's the plan going forward to improve on those things?

My plan was always the same, it was hard work, playing a lot, if something is not working in a map we need to try different stuff. Always work, work, work. I think that there is no magic about this, it's all about we need to improve individually and especially as a team. I think that nowadays everyone plays very good Counter-Strike, if you don't win the key rounds or the rounds that you need to win, you are going to suffer. That's what is happening to us.

I think that we are not finding consistency, different plans, misunderstood in a lot of situations

Christopher "⁠MUTiRiS⁠" Fernandes

You took a break from tournaments to work on the chemistry, at least that's what was said. What kind of things were you doing during that break?

It's what I said, we were working, we were trying to improve on some different maps, because since the beginning we have some maps that we are kinda struggling. But of course, I can tell you from experience, that when you need to focus on one map or two, it's okay that you can bounce back, but when you are focusing so much on so many maps, it gets hard and it's hard to get the answers.

With that tournament break, whose decision was it? Was it the organization, was it the coach, who made that call?

Everything here is decided by the organization and the coach. For me it's just we take the call, we just need to follow what they are saying basically.

We go to another tournament, we have travels, people are getting sick again. It feels like a curse sometimes

Christopher "⁠MUTiRiS⁠" Fernandes

Do you think the break helped?

I think so, because we had so many unlucky situations in these months, people are getting sick. So we are travelling home from a tournament, we give some days off for people to breathe a little bit, when we come back people are getting sick. We go to another tournament, we have travels, people are getting sick again. It feels like a curse sometimes, but we tried as much as we can.

I think it's not about… sometimes you feel the chemistry, and that's what we are lacking. I think it's in our eyes, it's in everybody's eyes, so everyone needs to think about this and we need to find the answers, or I don't know what is next.

You had quite a good start with this BC.Game lineup in Krakow and got some good wins over some good teams. Why do you think you weren't able to continue with that?

I think that at the beginning we worked very hard, everything was still very fresh, and with some time some frustrations [showed]. We got worse on some maps just because we didn't play them so much; Nuke was our best map, after that in pracs we started to not prac it so much, and then we had difficulties on Nuke. It's CS, it's not easy when it's like that.

We just need to be better, that's the answer. I've been playing for so many years and there are no excuses, it's just we were not good enough. For us individually, I can speak for myself, it's a shame that we just came here and did not show a proper level.

I think that sometimes it's needed, those fights, sometimes it's too much and maybe we go out of the game

Christopher "⁠MUTiRiS⁠" Fernandes

A lot gets made, especially within the community, about the frustrations we see on player cams. We see people maybe having a debate, let's say. Do you think more is made of that than needs to be?

To be honest back in the days in Portugal especially, we were like that, we were always blaming each other or fighting. I think that sometimes it's needed, those fights, sometimes it's too much and maybe we go out of the game. We are trying to find the balance and it's like we are knowing each other, so everyone has different backgrounds, everyone reacts in different ways. Of course it's not the ideal place, but it's not so dramatic as people see sometimes.

For me it's like everyone needs adapting basically, and we are trying, but sometimes it's hard. Sometimes it doesn't bother us, these fights. We just need to find the balance and that's it.

When you are the captain you make some decisions, you have more voice, here I am more the IGL and I am more following some things

Christopher "⁠MUTiRiS⁠" Fernandes

You mentioned it a little bit earlier, but do you know what the future looks like for this BC.Game roster, particularly into next season? Because I think that's the question everyone is going to be asking.

To be honest I don't know. I'm gonna say this: here I'm more the IGL, I'm not the captain. That's how I feel inside. When you are the captain you make some decisions, you have more voice, here I am more the IGL and I am more following some things.

When BC contracted me I felt honoured because it's good to know that an organization wants you. I'm 33-years-old so I want to compete, but when it comes to this place it's like sometimes I don't really know what is next. For me that's really difficult, I think that TaZ knows it, the organization knows it, and like I said, I don't know what is coming next. Let's see the future, I don't really know.
