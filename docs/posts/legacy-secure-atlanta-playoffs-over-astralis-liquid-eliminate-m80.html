Legacy secure Atlanta playoffs over Astralis; Liquid eliminate M80

Legacy have secured a spot in the IEM Atlanta playoffs with a 2-0 over Astralis, knocking the European squad to the lower bracket against SINNERS.

The series kicked off on Legacy's Dust2 pick, and it was the Brazilians who set the pace after a Eduardo "⁠dumau⁠" Wolkmer ace turned a 2v5 early on to keep the momentum going in a six-round streak that turned an early 0-2 deficit around.

It was dumau who topped the board with a 1.59 rating — and he got a second ace, albeit against a full eco — as Legacy took off after losing the first three in the second half to a 13-8 victory, but Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto was a close second with a 1.58.

Astralis did not fare better on their map pick, where once again an early two-round lead did not bear fruit, as Legacy locked down the CT side of Nuke. Once again, it was the latto show, as he ended the map with a 1.71 rating in the 13-6 victory

The derby of teams with American cores between M80 and Liquid kicked off on Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek and company's Dust2 pick, but M80 held it down to start things by winning six of the first seven rounds played on the way to an 8-4 half.

The second half was all Michael "⁠Swisher⁠" Schmid, as he posted a +26.04% swing, 2.79-rated half in the five rounds it took M80 to close out the map 13-4, including a big 1v2 situation on the B site.

Swisher kept going on Inferno with a huge quad-kill round as Liquid tried to tie it up early on in the fourth round, but after the M80 player's highlight, the early lead went from 2-1 to 4-1.

Liquid fought their way back to a 5-5, but a quad-kill 1v3 by Elias "⁠s1n⁠" Stein got M80 back ahead and, going up against pistols, the lead was extended to 7-5 at the switch.

Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa then won a huge 1v3 to kick off the second half, which got Liquid in position to start a comeback in what ended up being a dominating half that they closed out 13-8.

Having prevailed on Inferno, Liquid took to Ancient in great shape and, after winning the T-side pistol round, went on to take an early 5-0 lead which was reduced to a 7-5 at the half.

After switching sides, Liquid won the second pistol round, this time on the defense, and didn't look back, winning out 13-5 to secure a spot in the lower bracket semi-finals, where they will face GamerLegion for survival.
