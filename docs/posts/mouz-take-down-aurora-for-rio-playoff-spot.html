MOUZ take down Aurora for Rio playoff spot

MOUZ have clinched their spot in the playoffs of IEM Rio, taking down Turkish side Aurora in a straightforward two-map affair on Dust2 (13-4) and Mirage (13-8).

MOUZ will now have the opportunity to head directly to the semi-finals, depending on the outcome of their upcoming series against hometown heroes FURIA. A win would send them straight to Saturday's semi-finals, while a loss would pit them against Group A's lower-bracket winner in the quarter-finals.

The series kicked off on Aurora's map pick of Dust2, a map which MOUZ posted a lackluster 31% win rate across 13 games played in the last three months prior to this series. Despite snagging the pistol round, MOUZ had a difficult start, fumbling to Aurora's Glocks and Deagles in the second round to give the Turkish side an early 3-1 lead.

Once MOUZ's economy stabilized, however, they did not let up. Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin and Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás put together an exquisite A-site defense throughout the half, posting four and five multikills on the CT side, respectively. Aurora failed to break into any bombsites after their early lead, culminating in a disappointing 3-9 half as they headed into their own CT side.

Aurora clinched the second pistol thanks to a 10 second defuse from Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici, but their comeback hopes were dashed after MOUZ won out the second-round force, culminating in a 13-4 map win for Brollan's men shortly thereafter.

Aurora had a better go of things in the first half of MOUZ's pick of Mirage, going back and forth with MOUZ throughout the half before a Brollan quad kill consigned Aurora to a 5-7 deficit heading into the T-side.

MOUZ ran away with things to start the second half, getting within touching distance of series point with a four-round streak fueled by a pistol round win. Aurora finally started getting rounds on the board once MOUZ hit series point, stifling MOUZ's economy, but the international side managed to get it over the line with a lackluster buy following a 1v2 4k from Brollan.
