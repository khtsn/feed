B8 clinch last Stage 3 berth over BIG in Cologne

s1zzi toppd the board with a 1.49 rating

B8 secured a top 16 spot at the IEM Cologne Major after beating BIG in the final Stage 2 decider match, with #32 HLTV Prospect Danylo "⁠s1zzi⁠" Vinnyk posting 2.01 and 1.51-rated performances on the two maps the Ukrainians won.

The series kicked off on B8's Ancient pick, but BIG were quick to set up an early lead, 5-1, in what would become a strong CT side. Benjamin "⁠blameF⁠" Bremer's 16 kills led the way to an 8-4 scoreline at the switch.

B8 won the first two rounds of the second half to shorten the distance, but it wouldn't mean much as BIG picked right up with guns in play to steal the map 13-7.

BIG weren't able to repeat their first map showing on Overpass, where it was s1zzi's time to shine with the AWP. Four kills in the fourth round against a forcebuy, and one three rounds later against a full buy kept the Ukrainians in the driver's seat. The 16-year-old then capped a fantastic 19-kill half with an ace against pistols and a last-round triple that made it 11-1.

BIG opened up the second half with five in a row, but the comeback was only cosmetic as B8 stole the map back and forced the decider, 13-6.

It all came down to Dust2, and BIG took advantage of starting on the CT by going 6-0, but this time B8 clawed back with a five round streak on the attack before giving up the last round of the half to trail by two.

s1zzi downed three in pistols and four in the anti-eco for B8 to tie it up, 7-7, and from there on out the Ukrainians continued to dominate the half on the defense with an ace by Artem "⁠kensizor⁠" Kapran putting in the final nail in the BIG coffin, 13-8.
