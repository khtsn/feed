Ben Sasse Will Step Down as President of University of Florida

U.S.|Ben Sasse Will Step Down as President of University of Florida

https://www.nytimes.com/2024/07/18/us/ben-sasse-university-of-florida.html

The former senator from Nebraska cited the health of his wife, who has been diagnosed with epilepsy.

Ben Sasse, the president of the University of Florida and a former Republican senator from Nebraska, announced Thursday that he was resigning his position so he could dedicate more time and attention to his ailing wife.

Dr. Sasse, 52, took office in early February 2023. His resignation will be effective July 31. Mori Hosseini, the chair of the university’s board of trustees, said in a separate announcement that the board would work quickly to appoint an interim president and would begin the search for a new president.

In a long and heartfelt message on X, Dr. Sasse said that his wife, Melissa, who had an aneurysm and a series of strokes in 2007, was in recent months diagnosed with epilepsy and had been “struggling with a new batch of memory issues.”

Although his two daughters are in college, his son is just turning 13, he said in the post.

“I’ve got two spectacular callings in life right now: First, I’m a husband and dad,” Dr. Sasse wrote. “Second, I’ve been blessed to serve as president of the best dang public university in America — Go Gators! — and I’ve loved the challenge of giving this university everything I’ve got. But here’s the bottom line: Those callings are significantly at odds with each other right now.”

Dr. Sasse said that his family would continue living in Gainesville, Fla., and that he would remain as president emeritus and as a professor continuing to teach classes.

Of his students and colleagues, he said, “You touched our hearts and made this more than a job — you made it our community. That’s why we’re not going anywhere.”

Dr. Sasse, who has degrees from Harvard, St. John’s College and Yale, was a U.S. senator from Nebraska from 2015 to 2023.

Dr. Sasse was appointed president of the University of Florida in November 2022, and he ran the college at a very partisan time. Elected officials in Florida have taken a strong hand in steering curriculums. In May 2023, Gov. Ron DeSantis, a Republican, signed Senate Bill 266 “to prohibit institutions from spending federal or state dollars on discriminatory initiatives, such as so called ‘diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI)’ programs,” according to an official announcement.

The governor’s announcement said the bill “takes several steps to prevent woke ideologies from continuing to co-opt our state universities and state colleges.” It would prohibit curriculums that were “based on theories that systemic racism, sexism, oppression, and privilege are inherent in the institutions of the United States and were created to maintain social, political, and economic inequities.”

In the Senate, Dr. Sasse was reliably conservative but also a critic of Donald Trump.

In February 2021, Dr. Sasse was one of only seven Republicans in the Senate who voted to convict Mr. Trump on a charge of incitement of insurrection in his second impeachment trial. Mr. Trump was acquitted by the Senate.

A year earlier, Dr. Sasse had joined almost all of the Republican senators in voting to acquit Mr. Trump during his first impeachment trial.
