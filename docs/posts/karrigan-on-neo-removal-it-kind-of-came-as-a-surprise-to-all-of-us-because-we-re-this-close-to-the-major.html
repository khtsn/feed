karrigan on NEO removal: "It kind of came as a surprise to all of us because we're this close to the Major"

FaZe entered 2026 full of optimism after their grand-final appearance at the Budapest Major, but their year so far has been far from satisfactory. "I have to be honest with you, completely shit," Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen described FaZe's year so far during BLAST Rotterdam's media day.

FaZe have won just three of their 12 matches so far this year, and the string of results ended Filip "⁠NEO⁠" Kubski's three-year stint on the roster as FaZe attempt to change the course of the struggling squad.

FaZe part ways with NEO

Ahead of BLAST Open Rotterdam, karrigan sat down with HLTV for a lengthy chat about NEO's removal, FaZe's struggles, and their upcoming DraculaN appearance.

We're here for BLAST Rotterdam media day. I wanted to start off with what was announced yesterday, the departure of NEO after three years. What happened and why did you guys decide to move on from him after the start of the season?

I think the results have been lacking this year. Obviously that means FaZe looked into the team, and they came to the conclusion that this is a direction they want to try. It kind of came as a surprise to all of us because we're this close to the Major, but I think FaZe explained what direction they wanted to go and try to figure out a permanent solution.

I think it's more a signal in some way to tell the players that they're safer now, but we need better results. I think everyone in the team knows it's time to step an extra percentage up. I don't think per se NEO was a problem, we as a team have not performed, and everybody is part of the problem. And with how FaZe is tackling it, it's how they want to do it. We all expected maybe something could happen, but it probably still surprised every one of us.

For an IGL, it's very important to have a strong connection with the coach, and with you saying that this was a decision made by the organization, it came be a surprise to everyone. How would you describe your relationship with NEO, and what kind of coach do you think that the team needs to get to work with you?

I think my relationship with Filip has always been and always will be good. I think I remember calling him into the team back then three years ago, he was making his bike in the garage. I was ready to tackle this journey, and what a journey it has been. I think he has been supporting the team really well, it was big shoes to fill from 2023 with RobbaN suddenly stepping down after winning the Grand Slam as a team.

For the permanent coach, FaZe is probably looking at somebody who maybe has more experience, can be more structured and harder

For the permanent coach, FaZe is probably looking at somebody who maybe has more experience, can be more structured and harder. I can believe that some of the attributes that FaZe may be looking at are those that Filip could lack, but it's also hard to figure out your coaching style when it's your first gig within a team that came from winning, came to a new game, did great, and then did really bad.

The past year has been really rough with roster changes back and forth and performance not as we all want them to be, so like I said, I think this is FaZe's way to try a different approach because we had Filip as a coach in the past three years.

Are you going to be involved in the decision-making process of the new coach or not at all? As in, will FaZe come to you, 'We have like three or five names?' Do you have any input? How is that going to work?

Obviously, FaZe wants me to kind of have a talk once they have narrowed down the coaches they want. They still believe it's important that the coach and a strong vocal IGL like me are on the same page. I still think FaZe trust me and value my vision, but they are still making their decision on their own on what they think is the best long-term. I can't be thinking five years ahead, that's FaZe's part. I can think every season, every year, about where I am and what I want to do, and they respect that. For the final decisions, it's up to them in the end.

I still think FaZe trust me and value my vision

But I think for them it's obviously important that we share some kind of same vision, or that me and the new coach have different attributes that can help the team. Because in the end, FaZe wants the team to win, and lately we haven't won anything at all. We don't even [have] enough games to qualify for the Major right now. We will see how they're going to tackle this. I'm not going to be directly involved, but I can imagine they want me to approve some of the names they might be bringing forward.

This has obviously been a very rough start to the season, three wins and nine losses. What do you make of these first months of the season?

I have to be honest with you, completely shit, I think that's where my head is at. Coming from the Major, [having] high hopes, and I remember going on vacation and just wanting to play the next tournament. We were finally in a momentum, gaining something from the victories we had.

To obviously go completely opposite at the beginning of the season… Maybe some individual level dropped, team cohesion dropped a bit from where we were at the Major, but one thing is that we have been fighting.

We have put extra days in, maybe even too much. It's a fine balance when you want to grind more, but in the end, you also need to be mentally strong because you are going through a rough period. So to be honest, not at all good enough, and I think everyone on the team understands that. That's why Filip is not on the team anymore, because the results in some ways are not acceptable.

If I knew what the issues were, I don't think we would have them anymore

If you had to pinpoint the issues, what would you say are the biggest flaws and the biggest issues right now?

If I knew what the issues were, I don't think we would have them anymore. I think it's a classic moment of, you look at the games in isolation, and then once the tournament is over, you look at the big picture on what was the reason why we didn't win. Different things happen in different stages of the game.

I think if you actually look at the PARIVISION and the Falcons games in Cluj, you could make the case that if we were 2% sharper, we would have won both games 2-0, and we end up losing the third map really badly. Then we ended up losing to Astralis without a chance.

You're sitting and wondering about where the team from the two first maps of those games is, then you suddenly can be 3-0, right? So I think it's about different things. It can be energy, communication, individual, the classic meme, everything can be wrong. But we as a team have tried to add a little more structure and give space to people to call themselves in the mid-round and create an opening, what they're comfortable with.

We're going to see how that can work out, maybe that can create some stability. But if the confidence comes back, I think we can play different brands of Counter-Strike, we just need to make sure that when we're in this rough period, we are playing to the best of our abilities and not throwing advantageous positions. It has been a huge issue for us this year, with advantageous positions or 5on4s.

Since the start of the season, it's been setback after setback. You're getting blows left and right, and then you still have to find the strength to get up the next day and go back to work, right? The fire that you had at the Major doesn't seem to be there, at least not yet. What is the mood like in the camp?

I think the mood is decent. I've been in a not-so-bad situation before, but in a much worse mood. We are all trying to stay tunnelvisioned on what we want to do, trying to ignore the things around the team. Obviously, there is individual pressure on different players, me included, as our performances as individuals have not been good enough.

Obviously, there is individual pressure on different players, me included, as our performances as individuals have not been good enough

To top that, the team performance has not been good. There's a lot of pressure on the team's shoulders. We're trying to make sure we don't have inside pressure in the team, and everybody has to handle their own pressure their own way. For me, it's just important that we come with as much fire as we can, we bring the most energy we have on that day, because it is a rough situation, a rough period.

But the more you accept it, the easier you can move into the game and try to see it as a challenge. You can, for example, for the Aurora game we're playing... Some might say we need to win it, but I see it as a challenge to show ourselves that we can win it. The moment you make this mentality switch, it doesn't become about whether you need to win it, but you have a chance to show the world that you are capable of winning it, and I think that's where we need to see ourselves.

We cannot see ourselves as a top-ten team right now, because we are not. It's similar to that. But do we have the pieces, and did we show three months ago that we can beat a lot of teams? Yes. And we need to find that again. That happens only in certain scenarios, and we can then build on that. Because right now, we don't have much to build from this year, and that's the problem we have as a team. When you don't have a strong foundation of what we know can work really well, then everything is basically sometimes wrong in different scenarios of games.

Obviously, there is individual pressure on different players, me included, as our performances as individuals have not been good enough

After this, you have DraculaN, which is quite an interesting change for the team. You need to get those LAN wins, and there's a huge upside in attending those LAN events, but at the same time, if you lose to a team that is ranked lower, you lose a lot of points. How do you think that the team will deal with that pressure? Because you're playing a lot of games against teams you know pretty much nothing about, and if you lose to them, you lose a lot of points. And at this point, you need to get points and not lose them, right?

Yeah, I think it's a really good sentence for DraculaN. I think I would rather be in control of the car than being a passenger, and hope other teams won't make it to the Major. If I'm driving the car, I'm in control, and I'm in control of how I can perform that day. I'm in control of how I lead that team that day.

It's going to be tough, sure, but what I'm looking forward to is that I'm slightly tired of tier-one events. You know everything is perfect, you know how the admins are, you know you can ask for a coffee, you know when the water's coming. You have a player lounge, a shuttle… When you go to DraculaN, you pay to be there.

It reminds me of that moment where you have to grind, and that's the moment where I'm in my career. I have to prove myself in a different scenario, in a different stage. I welcome that. And I accept that as a privilege that I'm still here, and I still find a chance to be at the Major. If it's gonna go badly, at least I was there doing my best and in control. The worst thing I would do is if we're doing decently in BLAST, then go home and sit home for two weeks, hoping certain teams are losing. I went through that one time with the RMR and FaZe.

It was very hard to sit home, watch, and pray for other teams to lose. Then you jinx it and they win. So yes, DraculaN, it's gonna be tough, but I welcome the challenge, and I'm looking forward to getting down in the mud, as we say, and just make sure that even though it's ugly, we make it to the Major. Let's see at the Major, maybe we can make a small journey again and a small run. Who knows? It is FaZe after all at the Major, but let's get there first.

DraculaN, it's gonna be tough, but I welcome the challenge, and I'm looking forward to getting down in the mud

It's quite interesting that you say that about the tier-two tournaments, because I was with rain two weeks ago, and he was saying the exact same thing, he was very excited to play those tournaments. I wanted to ask about his journey now as an IGL with 100 Thieves. Are you following the team? What do you think about his work with the team so far?

I think he has made a good alliance with gla1ve and device. I think I know device very well from my past, I know he's a very vocal AWPer, which will help him a lot. At the same time, I think gla1ve has a good way of leading teams and is going to take some capabilities that rain maybe doesn't have yet, that he has to grow into. But I think what rain has is unique. He's a very calm player, and I think he's going to have quite a journey in front of him, and I'm looking forward to following it. I know how good of a teammate he is and what value he brings to a team, that's really important.

After all, everybody says that rain is their best teammate. He also needs to find himself in that role [in terms of] when he's going to be harsh about mistakes, when he's going to let it go, you know? It's always easy to be a teammate when you don't have to correct everyone's mistakes.

You've had many ups and downs in your career, but this feels like one of the most challenging periods, at least in terms of how long it has gone for. It's been almost two years since you last won a trophy. Are you still confident that you can turn things around?

I think so. When you are in the storm, I feel like I'm in the middle of the hurricane now, it's very hard to see which way is out. But I also had a very rough 2021 when I joined FaZe, all of us coming and waiting for ropz to join. But there, you could see the light at the end of the tunnel, that ropz coming in at some point would turn the team around, because they knew what ropz could bring to the team. Here we are in the middle of a storm, right? We are now without a coach, we have to see when the coach comes in, what value he brings, what things are good for us, what things are bad for us.

So you can say there's something happening, but I believe that what we did at the Major…. Sometimes a fluke win can help you stabilize things and maybe you can't replicate it, but at least you can get close to replicating it. That's why I still believe, because I have seen how good we can be. When you have seen it, you just know there is a way that this switch turned on. It's like turning on the lights. And it doesn't turn on, but you know it turned on yesterday, and it blinks for a second. That's what I kind of felt like we showed at the Major.

That's why I still believe, because I have seen how good we can be. When you have seen it, you just know there is a way that this switch turned on

Maybe it's a fluke, maybe it's not, but us performing that well as a team and individually, then I know it's in there. Now we just need to make sure we all can perform at the same time, because that's the beauty of Counter-Strike, right? We don't have a specific top-three player in the world that when four of us have a bad day, he can carry our ass. We need to perform as a team. It's my job to make the best foundation for us to perform on the bad days, because on the good days, I still think we can challenge teams. But obviously, we haven't had many of those lately.
