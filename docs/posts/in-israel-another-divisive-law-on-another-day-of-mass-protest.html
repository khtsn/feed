In Israel, Another Divisive Law on Another Day of Mass Protest

Amid a national crisis over a planned judicial overhaul, Parliament approved a bill protecting the prime minister from being declared unfit to govern.

March 23, 2023Updated 4:16 p.m. ET

Hours after his coalition passed a divisive law making it harder to remove him from office, Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu of Israel vowed in a dramatic televised speech on Thursday night to proceed with an even more contentious judicial overhaul that has divided the country, set off unrest in the military and led to mass protests in the streets.

Speaking on prime time television, Mr. Netanyahu promised to go ahead next week with plans to give the government greater control over appointments to the Supreme Court — emphatically squashing rumors that had swirled throughout the day that he was about to back down.

The speech followed a dramatic day in which thousands of protesters demonstrated across Israel against the plan, leading to confrontations with right-wing supporters of Mr. Netanyahu and clashes with police, who sprayed protesters with water cannon.

The address came just minutes after the defense minister, Yoav Gallant, met with Mr. Netanyahu to warn him about the effect the turmoil has had on the military, amid speculation that Mr. Gallant was about to speak out against the plan himself.

And all this came against the backdrop of a vote in Parliament earlier in the day, in which coalition lawmakers voted to make it more difficult to declare prime ministers incapacitated and remove them from office. The new law takes the power to remove a prime minister out of the hands of the attorney general and the court and grants it instead to Parliament.

Critics said the bill was aimed at protecting Mr. Netanyahu, who is on trial for corruption.
