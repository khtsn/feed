Boombl4: "It's not the biggest tournament in my life, but it's still great emotions"

BetBoom are one of the squads travelling all around the world in search of crucial VRS points in order to qualify for the Cologne Major, and Kirill "⁠Boombl4⁠" Mikhailov have just made a huge step in the right direction by winning Radio Popular Roman Imperium Cup VII despite fielding two stand-ins due to visa issues.

The Russian squad battled past SINNERS, G2, and BESTIA during their playoff run, but BetBoom don't have long to rest on their laurels.

"From the first flight, we have nine flights in two weeks, so it is really hard and stressful for the body and mentally," admitted Boombl4, whose team is already travelling to Barcelona for Stake Ranked Episode 1.

"But we understand that we should focus on these two weeks, and afterwards we can have some chill time."

Congratulations on the win. You didn't drop a map against BESTIA, so how does it feel to lift the trophy?

It's great emotions. It was a long, long time with no trophies, and of course it's not the biggest tournament in my life, but it's still great emotions because we have two stand-ins and it is a really important LAN tournament for VRS points to get to the Major.

Of course it's not the biggest tournament in my life, but it's still great emotions

It's a great feeling because three best-of-threes in one day is really, really hard, but we still did it and everyone is happy right now. We have one more LAN tournament in a few days, and we will try our best. We have great emotions to go through the next LAN.

This is also a massive leap in the Major invite race. Given you're so close now, do you feel any pressure with these VRS points? Is the Major at the back of your mind, or are you worried?

You know, we remember last season, we were watching a lot about the VRS points and we were feeling stressed. Right now, with this season, we're trying to not think a lot about VRS points and the Major race. Of course, we understand, but we're still trying to focus on the game and play every round like a team. We have the visa issues so we're playing with two stand-ins, and if we fail, it's still okay because we are not a full team here.

You talked about it a bit, but considering how close you were to playing in Austin, do you feel this is your moment to finally make it?

Yeah, the last season we were at 18th place, and 17 teams were going to the Major. We lost the Moscow final, LanDaLan, to PARIVISION, and they went through, so it was really unlucky and this match was really hard, it was 2-3 in a best-of-five final. But this is how it's going, and we'll just try our best in the last tournament and that's all.

From the first flight, we have nine flights in two weeks, so it is really hard and stressful for the body and mentally

You fly tomorrow to Stake. Are you worried about tiredness at all with all these tournaments? Do you think this achievement today will help you carry the stamina further?

Of course, it's a little bit hard to play every day and it's a lot of flights. I think from the first flight, we have nine flights in two weeks, so it is really hard and stressful for the body and mentally. But still, we understand that we should focus on these two weeks and afterwards we can have some chill time, so it's five or six more days to focus up and we will try our best.
