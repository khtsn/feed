Israel’s Raid on Jenin, in Photos

Israel’s most forceful incursion into the occupied West Bank for years has drawn to an end, the country’s military said on Wednesday.

Beginning around 1 a.m. Monday and heralded by intense drone strikes, the operation sent about 1,000 troops into the narrow streets and alleyways of the Jenin refugee camp, accompanied by armored vehicles, in what Israeli officials described as an effort to root out armed militants after months of growing violence.

The incursion killed at least 12 Palestinians, left one Israeli soldier dead and led thousands to flee their homes.

On Tuesday, eight Israelis were injured in a car-ramming and stabbing attack in Tel Aviv. On Wednesday, Israel said that it had intercepted five rockets fired from the Gaza Strip and that its fighter jets had struck sites in Gaza in response. The events raised fears of an escalation of tit-for-tat violence.

This year has been one of the deadliest for Palestinians in the West Bank in more than a decade, with more than 150 killed over the past six months. Most died in armed clashes during Israeli military raids or while carrying out attacks, though some were civilian bystanders. It has also been one of the deadliest years for Israelis in some time, with at least 24 killed in Arab attacks, nearly all civilians.

Israeli military officials said that more than 50 shooting attacks against Israeli targets had been carried out in the past six months from the Jenin area, a stronghold for Palestinian militant groups, and that the operation there aimed to “break the safe-haven mind-set” of the camp. A member of the local committee that administers the camp said that the incursion had transformed it into “a war zone in the full meaning of the word.”
