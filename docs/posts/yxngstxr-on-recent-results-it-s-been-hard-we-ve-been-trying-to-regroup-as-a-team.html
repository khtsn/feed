yxngstxr on recent results: "It's been hard, we've been trying to regroup as a team"

The Swede remains positive about advancing to Stage 2.

HEROIC had a bad start to their IEM Cologne campaign, losing to Lynn Vision and Sharks in two close best-of-ones to find themselves in the 0-2 pool after day one.

While they stayed alive after beating Gaimin Gladiators, Simon "⁠yxngstxr⁠" Boije and company still require two straight wins to make it to Stage 2, but the Swede is adamant his team has what it takes.

"I have no doubt in my teammates, I have no doubt in the team and the coaching staff behind us, that we will make it to Stage 2," he told HLTV after the win against the Brazilians.

Their next test will come against Liquid in the 1-2 pool.

Congrats, your first win here after some close best-of-ones. Today was quite comfortable, I guess you could say, so how does it feel to finally get a W on the board?

We've been talking about it in the team, like the last win we got was actually in Astana, first game against Aurora, and then after we had some rough matches. It's been hard, we've been trying to regroup as a team, talk about the losses and what we can do moving forward. The two best-of-ones we lost, they were close games, we didn't think too much about it. We still, on paper, should have won, but they were… I don't know, we were not prepared. But I'm finally happy to win this game.

Those two best-of-ones were very close, but you ended up losing them. Do you think that's also down to a lack of confidence given the results that you mentioned, considering it's been a while since you won a game?

I mean, going out 1-3 in Astana and 0-2 in Stake doesn't help us a lot with the confidence before the Major. Personally, I was a bit sick, so I haven't been able to put the best [performances in], but now I'm on the better side. Not the greatest start [to the Major], but I don't think it's like a lack of confidence. I feel like it's more like individual mistakes and mistakes as a team, but I'm happy we showed that we can still win.

To ask about you personally. You're obviously AWPing in this team, which is not something that you did before, but it's been going on for quite a few months, right? Do you feel comfortable with the weapon now?

With the weapon, of course I feel comfortable. It's more like the different scenarios. When I was a rifler, I was playing the same scenarios over and over again, but now when I have a new position and especially the new scenarios, it's getting a bit uncomfortable. But day by day I'm playing \more], so it's more comfortable day by day.

How do you feel about being put into this role? Do you like it overall? Is this something that, you know, you could see in your future in another team or in the future of HEROIC as well?

AWPing is super fun and it's an important role. You're the playmaker, compared to being a support-anchor before. Now I can choose my own destiny, choose my own fate, do the peeks I want, do the calls I want, so it's definitely more suitable role-wise I think. But who knows what the future will hold, you know?

About this tournament here. You need two more wins to get through, and given your ranking and the names on the team, I guess you should be expected to make it through. Do you feel that you have what it takes to make it to Stage 2 or maybe even further?

If you look at it, we're 0-2, but at the end of the day, it's just two best-of-ones, and if you lose some crucial rounds in the best-of-ones, it can be over from that round. I have no doubt in my teammates, I have no doubt in the team, and the coaching staff behind us, that we will make it to Stage 2. I feel very great personally, and I have no doubt about it that we can easily make it.
