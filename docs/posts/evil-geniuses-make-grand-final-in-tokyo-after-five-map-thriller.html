The best of five series began on EG's first map pick of Fracture. Prior to the series, EG sported a 4-0 undefeated record on the map at Masters: Tokyo. EG's dominance continued against Paper Rex as they took the first pistol. Despite a strong start from Paper Rex in the first gun rounds, the Pacific champions eventually fell flat against EG's defence.

EG held a 9-3 lead after halftime and were poised to take the first map of the series. Despite strong rounds from Paper Rex with wins in both the second pistol and first gun round, EG handled the situation comfortably to close the map out 13-7 and take a 1-0 lead. Demon1 and Ethan excelled in the first map, recording higher than 1.30 ratings.

Paper Rex regained their composure once on their own map pick of Lotus. After losing the pistol, PRX took seven rounds in a row to hold a 7-2 lead, their signature hyper-aggressive style of play performing wonders against EG. However, a slight recovery from EG came late into the half as EG's A retakes secured them three rounds to end the half with a slight 7-5 deficit.

A pistol win from Paper Rex put them in a good position to take the map, with an 11-8 lead coming quickly after. However, an attacking Operator from Boostio flourished against the ever-aggressive Paper Rex to keep the score close. An excellent trap play towards A from Paper Rex combined with a lack of bullets in the clip on C0M pushed Paper Rex onto map point. Evil Geniuses swiftly secured overtime thanks to some excellent midrounding from Boostio in the 24th round. An incredible performance from Jinggg in the following two rounds ended EG's hopes of a comeback as Paper Rex secured Lotus 14-12.

Jinggg forced his team into a map victory in overtime

EG's second map pick of Split began similarly to Fracture, with EG taking suffocating control of the game. With help from a 1v3 from mindfreak, Paper Rex came back slightly into the half to trail at 8-4 going into halftime after being 7-1 down.

A pistol and anti-eco win, thanks to a hero play and following 4k from f0rsaken, gave Paper Rex a lifeline in the second half. With four rounds in a row and another clutch from mindfreak, the score was all tied up at 8-8. An incredible performance from f0rsaken aided in Paper Rex completing the comeback and taking the map 13-10. EG took just two rounds after swapping to the offence as Paper Rex climbed their way back into the game after initially being down 7-1 in the first half. This gave Paper Rex the lead in the best-of-five series as EG trailed behind 2-1.

f0rsaken excelled on the Split defence

Paper Rex were now at series point heading into Pearl and a successful force buy in the second round after a pistol loss gave the underdogs considerable momentum. The substitute of cgrs proved pivotal for Paper Rex's performance as the Thai player provided both successful lurks and hero plays for his team. Despite this, EG had their own heroes to thank for keeping the game close. Both Boostio and jawgemo provided moments of brilliance to keep the score narrow. With the rest of EG coming into their own late in the half they took a slight lead into halftime 7-5.

EG won the second pistol on Pearl to give themselves a significant boost in momentum as the conversion of the anti-eco resulted in a 9-5 lead. EG proved dominant on the offence as Paper Rex could muster up just a single round before EG took Pearl 13-6 and evened up the series 2-2.

The gruelling series came down to the fifth map of Ascent and began with an incredible unbelievable 4K from cgrs. A 1v2 clutch from d4v41 in the bonus round gave Paper Rex a 3-0 lead before EG answered with a low buy win of their own that shifted the momentum of the map. The score was quickly tied up at 3-3. EG began to run away with the game on their attack side winning every round bar one following PRX's initial three.

A pistol win and anti-eco conversion put EG at a 10-4 lead. The Americans seemed destined to close out the series after seeing how Paper Rex's defence had crumbled. The map was closed out shortly after 13-8 for EG to take home the series 3-2.

Evil Geniuses are set to rematch Fnatic in the grand finals for a shot at the trophy on Sunday, June 25.

Paper Rex has been eliminated from Masters: Tokyo in third place and will play next at Champions 2023.
