bodyy: "The vision in 3DMAX diverged from every perspective"

After his departure from 3DMAX, Alexandre "⁠bodyy⁠" Pianaro didn't take long to find a new home in OG on a loan and get back into action at HyperX Roman Imperium Cup VI.

Despite their short time together, they made their debut with the Frenchman count, making a run to second place in Portugal after beating the likes of BC.Game and Liquid, only narrowly missing out on the title after a close decider with Ninjas in Pyjamas.

After the grand final, bodyy heaped praise on Casper "⁠cadiaN⁠" Møller's leadership and credited the coaching staff behind him with the deep run.

"I really enjoy the way Casper is calling, in the way he lets some freedom to the players but also the detailing he has from tier-one experience and being ice-cold in all these tournaments," he said.

At the end, they needed something new to get a new start and make the Major

"It's very important to play like that on LAN, and I think that's why we got that far after playing two or three days of practice. [...] All the game plans that were made from the coaching staff and the IGL were pretty great," the 29-year-old went on.

bodyy also spoke about why his time with 3DMAX ended. "Our end of 2025 was not the greatest," he began. "We'd been patching it up all season long to make the team work. At the end, they needed something new, something fresh to get a new start with and make the Major.

"The vision in the team diverged from every perspective, from every direction. I was not aligning with the way they were seeing the future, and we just decided to part ways."

You can read the interview in full below:

cadiaN mentioned yesterday that when you speak, it's "really good words" and that your calm energy fits well with the Swedes. In this tournament, how did you find the balance between micro-managing every detail of the game and letting the system run for itself?

I'm here to help the team out and adapt to their system, first of all. I came in to fill a role that they need to be filled. I think we have a good balance with cadiaN because he micro-manages a lot and he also has really good ideas, but also in mid-rounds, where I think he shines the most.

He's super active and tells pretty much everybody what to do. He can count on me as an experienced player to be able to complement his calls and make sure that there's as few details left to the side.

cadiaN is a really good in-game leader in terms of macro detailing and making sure that all those moments are ice-cold

What is it like to play with another AWP-IGL? Last time you played with one was with Maka in 3DMAX. How are these experiences different or similar to each other? What lessons did you get from these?

I don't know if it's too much about an AWPing IGL. It's more about styles. I really enjoy the way that Casper is calling, in the way that he lets some freedom to the players to be able to do what they do best, but also the detailing that he has from tier-one experience and being ice-cold in all these tournaments.

I think that's really valued in this team. I don't know how much the players value what he's bringing to the table, but he's a really good in-game leader in terms of macro detailing and making sure that all those moments are ice-cold and not stressed. It's very important to play like that on LAN, and I think that's why we got that far after playing two or three days of practice with me.

Although you sadly lost this trophy, you mentioned the goal was to climb the VRS ranking and fight for the Major spot, and it was a pretty good tournament nevertheless. Do you think it will elevate OG's stamina for the rest of the road?

I think it gives everybody a lot of confidence. It's the first tournament together, and obviously losing in the final, but also making it to the top two, this is pretty good for the VRS and for the vibe of the team.

Playing the first tournament together and going as far is pretty good. I'm pretty sure we could have won that final, but we lack practice, we lack repetition, and we lost kind of the vibe after the first map, playing all these games every day and being kind of tired of it. But overall, I think it's a pretty good showing for us and we're gonna have some time to practice before the next tournament.

You are facing teams with wild energy and who take risks. How did your experience help counter all this chaos from these young teams? Do you prefer to go with the flow or stick to the basics?

I'd say we like to the stick to the flow, but we also have ways to counter those kinds of teams. Today it failed pretty badly, I would say, mostly on Nuke, but all the game plans that were made from the coaching staff and the IGL were pretty great. Regarding the little time for practice, the preparation actually made us have a direction to follow and cadiaN just had to adjust mid-rounds and make sure the plan was actually followed.

My input into that is that I've also been an IGL in international teams and also in France. I think it's good to share visions and values with the team and make sure that I also have this understanding of the game where there are so many ways to get upset. My style helps in that everybody sticks to the plan. I also move forward to that and make sure the OG style is being set in stone.

I'm happy to play with the boys, I think we had a great showing overall, and I'm glad for the future and what's happening next in the following month.

Our end of 2025 was not the greatest. We'd been patching it up all season

Do you hope this is a start of something more? Are you just enjoying the ride, or do you see yourself becoming a member of OG more permanently?

I think we all really enjoy the time we spend together, and the boys are amazing people. I've been having a hell of a time with the guys and being back with my former teammate, Lambert. Let's see what the future holds for us and for me, but I'm having a very good time and playing these tournaments.

You spoke about Lambert - you worked together before in LDLC. How is it working with him again now, and what's changed since then?

Lamby, Lambert, was the IGL in the previous team I played with. He took a lot of experience as a coach in the past two years, I think, and it's a great showing now with the great game plans that we had and also what he can make for the team to be structured. I can see really good structure in this team.

I think his input in how he's outside of the game is really helping the team as well. He brings such good energy, also the assistant coach, killazoo. Both of them work really well together. Overall, I'm really happy to be working with him again. It was super easy to get into a team that was held by someone I played with before.

Moving back to 3DMAX, what happened that led to your departure from the team? How did it feel to be replaced by misutaaa?

I think our end of 2025 was not the greatest. We'd been patching it up all season long to try to make the team work and try to use everybody to the best of their abilities. At the end, we needed and they needed something new, something fresh to get a new start with and make the Major. That was the main goal.

I would say the vision in the team diverged from every perspective, from every direction. I was not aligning with the way they were seeing the future, and we just decided to part ways.
