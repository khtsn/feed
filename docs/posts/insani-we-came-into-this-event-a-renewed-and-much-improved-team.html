insani: "We came into this event a renewed and much-improved team"

MIBR got their CS Asia Championships campaign off to a winning start with a 13-9 victory over 3DMAX, avenging their 2-1 defeat to the French side at PGL Bucharest last month.

After the match, Felipe "⁠insani⁠" Yuji sat down with HLTV for a quick interview to discuss the preparation for this event, the team's international transition, and the upcoming clash with PARIVISION.

Congratulations on the win, insani. It was very important to start the tournament with a win in a BO1 game. You had a fantastic T side, then things got a bit hairy in the second half, but you managed to pull through in the end. Give me your thoughts on the match.

I think it was a very important match for our confidence. We hadn't played any matches in like a month and a half, so it's good to begin the tournament like this, with a really important win. Anubis is a map where we feel confident, we tweaked our CT side and it has become something we're very confident in. This game was a reflection of that, we managed to get 11 rounds on it.

I think the Anubis meta has changed a bit. Perhaps the map is getting a bit more CT-sided now. We struggled a bit at the start of the T side but we managed to stay calm, and we closed it out.

How important was it to secure this win and get revenge on 3DMAX for the loss in Bucharest?

Absolutely, it is good to beat a team that has eliminated you in the past. I think that if you look back at that match, we had the game in our hands, and we ended up allowing the comeback on Ancient; otherwise, we would have defeated them 2-0. But I think that getting revenge or wanting to face 3DMAX doesn't change anything. We will face any team head-on.

This is your first event since PGL Bucharest. What was your preparation for this event like?

We had a very intensive and productive bootcamp. We had long talks, and we perfected a lot of things in our playbook. We came into this event a renewed and much-improved team.

How do you look at these first four months with the international lineup? What has changed, in your opinion?

Just like every other new team, we need to adapt. We brought in foreign players and more inexperienced players. We're trying to adapt our communication. For example, this is the first time that venomzera is communicating in English. We got a new coach and a new captain. So the whole project and the team's mindset have changed.

So it's about getting to know and working with each other. I think we've had some good tournaments. We've had some slip-ups, events where we haven't done so well, like at FERJEE in Rio. But overall, I'm feeling good, I'm very happy with the team, and I'm confident that things will go well in the future.

I wanted to get your thoughts on kl1m, one of the most promising players in the scene. Would you say he is one of the best AWPers you've played with?

Yes, kl1m is very good individually. He plays really well, his gameplay is really good. I think he's still a bit inexperienced, but we're working every day on that. He's a very good player, and one of the few I've seen with this kind of gameplay.

What are your expectations for this event? How important would it be to make a good run here before the Major?

We're in the final stages before the Major, this is where we will put our bootcamp to the test, so to speak. So it's very important for us to play some good matches here and to keep our energy and confidence high so we can come in hot at the Major. But it's good not to put too much stock into this event, either. We need to stay grounded. So I think that our main focus is to play good CS, which we know we can do.
