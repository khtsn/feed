sAw: "I know what NertZ can bring to the team when you give him the right resources and put him in the good positions"

We caught up with G2's coach Eetu "⁠sAw⁠" Saha after he and his team won Stake Ranked Episode 1, putting an end to a road trip that took them to three tier-two events across Europe to secure Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz as a fixture in the starting five before the Major cut-off.

"I'm just happy that we were able to show progress in these tournaments, and to get the win here is the cherry on top," the Finn told HLTV after the victory on Sunday.

Now, G2 are getting ready to return to the routine as they prepare for a trip to Brazil, where they will kick off a series of consecutive big events at IEM Rio, followed by BLAST Rivals in Forth Worth, and finally PGL Astana before finally having some time to bootcamp before the IEM Cologne Major.

You've completed the gauntlet of tier-two events before the Major cut-off. How was that experience?

It was two weeks on the road, starting from the BC Game tournament to the Roman Imperium Cup and then coming here for Stake. We knew it was going to be tough, traveling tournament to tournament, and knowing that we were making a player change right before. We had like one week of practice with NertZ at home, and coming into these tournaments, we wanted to approach it as practice, but on the other hand, we don't have practice rooms from the tournament, so we were basically without PCs besides the officials.

We just had to improvise, talk here and there, go to my room and watch on the laptop to try and improve what we can... I think we were able to do it, you can see it results-wise, but also fix some things on some maps here and there. Like, we lost to SINNERS at the BC Game tournament, we looked through the match to see what went wrong there, and then we lost to BetBoom at the Roman Imperium Cup, and here we were able to play some better CS.

Still, [we have] some issues here and there, but I think it's to be expected. I'm just happy that we were able to show progress in these tournaments, and to get the win here is the cherry on top. It wasn't our expectation to win all of the tournaments; it was more to get the matches in with NertZ, make sure he's in the roster for the Major cut-off, and also to secure at least Stage 2, which I think is clear now. In those ways, it's a successful two weeks.

You couldn't practice on the road, and both SunPayus and NertZ told me that there are some changes expected to happen when you can properly sit down and practice. Can you give maybe a bit of a preview of what you're thinking about?

Yeah, it's a balance between fitting NertZ in with some of the positions that I know how we have to use him from the past experiences in ENCE and HEROIC, but it's also going to be a balance of mixing in MATYS and huNter- with their positions.

Some of the maps, we had to just go with the old positions and put NertZ into Malbs' positions, and in some other maps we were able to make more changes, already, like on Dust2 CT where you can see NertZ is playing B Rotation and MATYS is playing Long. Also on Mirage, we did some shifts, but it's still a process because it's going to be Rio now in a week, and then after Rio, we have Rivals, and then Astana. It's all coming pretty fast.

So, realistically, our biggest space to practice is going to be before the Major when we're going to do a bootcamp and everything. We have to see what maps we want to adjust now before Rio, and what we'll do after, and it depends on how deep you go in those tournaments, how much practice you'll have in between.

With the travel and everything, now being away two weeks, we also have to manage energy levels, so I think that the rest of the season is going to be tough to adjust everything you would want to adjust for the long term. I mean, we need to do it step by step, anyway. On some maps, we'll kind of go with what we have, and make it the best we can, and we'll see.

NertZ and MATYS are a pretty good one-two aggressive team, or could be, and then usually you'd have the AWPer as the third person in the pack. But that would leave huNter- in more lurking positions, but he's the IGL. Do you think that's a possibility, and having your IGL roam a bit more? How do you see those pieces falling in together?

One benefit we got with NertZ is that he's vocal, so he's able to help either by being in the pack, or being the lurker, so that's already going to benefit huNter-, like, when he's in the pack he knows that he has a lurker who can talk from his side of the map, and then on the opposite side...

It's a bit mixed right now, but on the opposite side it can be sometimes — especially when it's set strats —, we can mix it where huNter- can lurk, and in this way we can also make some changes with our positions, so the look from the opponents will be more distracting or harder to read in the way that, well, if they see huNter- somewhere, is he the lurker or is he with the pack now?

It's not maybe so... I wouldn't say structured, but it's a bit more free-flowing at the moment as we're trying to figure out exactly everything because, as I said, some maps we did more one-to-one [with Malbs' roles] and others we mixed more. In the long run, it's going to even out more. The main structure is still that huNter- is in the pack, and he's not an IGL-lurker, but in some rounds, we're able to do it.

[NertZ] can bring a lot of stuff, and I think it can put us on the next level

Another one of the pieces is SunPayus, who has been struggling to find his game ever since the release of CS2. You've known him since back since the ENCE days. What can you do to make him confident again and get him playing to the level you've seen in the past?

The player change now, bringing on NertZ, it helps on two levels. One being the structure opening up a bit more as we have more voices in the team, and we can play a bit more free-flowing, which will then also open it for SunPayus because then he can work with the AWP, and we can have more trust in the team in defaults and more open rounds, going for some picks.

Then, on the other hand, there's the mental aspect of getting NertZ on the team, whom he knows well and likes to play with. Especially right now, at the beginning, it's like a honeymoon phase, so I think it'll help SunPayus on those two levels. Then I think that, on top of that, it's just going to be time, basically. Trying to work on what's working and trying to stop doing what's not working so well.

Looking at the CS landscape right now, it's Vitality on top and then a big scramble beneath that, and then some struggling teams below. Where do you see G2 in this constellation right now?

Talking about being in the second group, which would be reaching playoffs and potentially fighting for trophies, I think we were really close to it all of the time. So far, with this team, we have missed six event playoffs by one match. So if you change that one match, and a lot of them were close, so if you change that one match, it's playoffs. Then it's already there, fighting for the trophy.

We were able to show it in London, but it's a one-off when you look at the big picture, so I think we were close, but the thing is that now with NertZ, I think we can get some extra percentage. Is it going to be enough? Time will tell, but I think it's looking good so far. I know what NertZ can bring to the team when you give him the right resources and put him in the good positions. He can bring a lot of stuff, and I think it can put us on the next level.

Obviously, Vitality is above everyone and what everyone is aiming for, but on the other hand, when you see other teams struggling, maybe that gives us a shot to get into that group and then slowly get into the next position. That's basically the goal for now, as well as just getting NertZ implemented in the next tournaments, which is going to be a busy time.

You're flying to Rio in less than a week, so how can you crunch it all in?

It's going to be a couple of off days to reset after these tournaments during these couple of weeks, and give people time with their families, friends, and everything. We'll have some practice days before Rio where we can put our focus into a couple of maps and get those up to speed.

After this tournament, you can see Dust2 and Ancient being in pretty good shape, but we still need to figure out things on the other ones. Also, just play them more. Play in practice, play in officials. That's ultimately what we need, the repetition, because we can look at it, we can talk about it, but in the end, we just need the reps in a well.

You beat BetBoom in the best-of-three of best-of-threes. How was it to get the final one on them?

It feels good, yeah. They beat us in the first one, then we beat them in the next one, and then I saw in an interview that they would be hungry coming for us again. Also, looking at this tournament, I had a feeling that we would play them again in the final. I think they were the strongest team besides us, and it was a good fight. They play a good, aggressive, reactive CS, and it can be a bit tough to handle.

In this grand final, there were also so many of these half buys or low buys that they were able to win, but then we got one back, and it's going back and forth. It's a bit of a chaotic match, but I'm happy we came out on top in the match-ups, and of course, winning the grand final in this tournament.
