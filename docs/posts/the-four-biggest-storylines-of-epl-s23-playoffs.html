The four biggest storylines of EPL S23 playoffs

Eight teams in vastly different circumstances are set to clash tomorrow in the quarter-finals.

Events outside of packed arenas may not be the most exciting for players and viewers alike, but many of these playoff-bound sides have a lot to play for in this ESL Pro League as the season builds towards the IEM Cologne Major. While this iteration may not feature the world's top eight teams, this season's LAN playoffs host an interesting bunch.

Sides more familiar with playoff appearances like Spirit, MOUZ, and The MongolZ will be on the prowl for the event's trophy in the absence of Vitality, and they will be joined by several teams who have been on the rise in recent months and are now looking to leave their mark, such as Legacy, FUT, and Astralis.

Who will take the crown in Vitality's absence?

IEM Krakow and PGL Cluj-Napoca champions Vitality are noticeably missing from this season of EPL, opting to take a nearly month-long break from competition between their title-winning campaign at PGL Cluj-Napoca and BLAST Open Rotterdam, which kicks off on March 18th. Now, three months into the year, a new team will join Vitality and PARIVISION as winners of a notable event in 2026.

Spirit and MOUZ are undoubtedly the chief favorites for the title in Vitality's absence. Both teams racked up flawless 3-0 records through Stage 2, with Spirit moving past 3DMAX, B8, and G2, while MOUZ bested HEROIC, Astralis, and The MongolZ.

MOUZ have yet to reach a grand final in 2026

These squads both came away with trophies last year, but they have yet to make a grand final in 2026. Spirit fell to eventual champions PARIVISION at BLAST Bounty, the opening event of the year, in the quarter-finals, and were shipped off to the third-place decider at IEM Krakow after a semi-final loss to FURIA. MOUZ were on the losing side of that decider in Krakow after falling to Vitality in the semi-finals, and similarly ended up in the PGL Cluj-Napoca third-place decider following a loss to PARIVISION.

Spirit and MOUZ, situated on opposite sides of the bracket, will each be hoping to capitalize on Vitality's vacation. Spirit will face off against Astralis in the latter side's first playoff appearance since going international, while MOUZ take on FUT. With Vitality slated to play the remainder of the season's top events barring PGL Astana, Spirit and MOUZ will want to jump on one of their best opportunities to hoist a trophy in the first half of the year.

Legacy stick the landing with arT

Legacy were beginning to look like a dangerous team towards the end of 2025, clinching the trophy at the CS Asia Championships alongside a runner-up finish at PGL Masters Bucharest. Unfortunately, the inexperienced Brazilian side were unable to keep that momentum up into the StarLadder Budapest Major, crashing out in Stage 1 despite being the second-highest-ranked team in attendance.

Legacy struggled to recapture their winning form after returning from the player break, notching a disappointing third-place finish at BetBoom Rush B Summit Season 1 in Brazil and a dead-last departure from IEM Krakow. These results culminated in the team's first change to the starting five in over a year, bringing on former FURIA captain Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan in place of IGL Lucas "⁠lux⁠" Meneghini.

saadzin shared that he was surprised by Legacy's debut with arT

arT's first outing with his new team has already netted him his best result since departing FURIA in April 2024. Legacy cruised through Stage 1 3-0 after taking down Ninjas in Pyjamas, G2, and PARIVISION, before marching on through Stage 2 while recording a single loss to Aurora.

"We had the expectation to start well, but not like this, very, very well." Guilherme "⁠saadzin⁠" Pacheco told HLTV ahead of the playoffs. "We were thinking about getting someone who can realise the potential of the team, you know?

"We got very surprised because we were not expecting to get so good so fast. It was very nice, we are just happy with him and just want to do more and be even better in the playoffs."

Legacy now have a golden opportunity in front of them. Unburdened by expectations in their debut event with their new captain, Legacy will now be preparing to face Aurora once again, this time in the quarter-finals. Even if the Turks come out on top once more, Legacy are no doubt pleased with the way arT's tenure on the team has started.

FUT finally break into the playoffs

The young international side of FUT have been on a slow and steady grind towards the top since their time with NAVI Junior, and EPL Season 23 has given them their next big milestone.

Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija's troops ended 2025 slogging their way through online competitions and smaller local LANs, with their most notable result being a quarter-final finish at CS Asia Championships. Now, FUT are cashing in on 2026, having earned a plethora of invitations to top events thanks to their rise through the Valve Regional Standings (VRS).

FUT's impressive rise continues at ESL Pro League

FUT have gotten tantalizingly close to playoffs before. The young side missed out on a LAN playoff berth in their first big outing at BLAST Bounty after a hard-fought loss against FURIA. They got revenge against Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo's men at IEM Krakow, but bowed out in 9-12th place after losses to G2 and Astralis, and once again came within touching distance of a playoff appearance at PGL Cluj-Napoca, fighting their way to a 2-2 record before faltering to eventual finalists PARIVISION in yet another close series.

Freed from the shackles of an academy, FUT have broken through

This time around, FUT managed to get it over the line. Starting in Stage 1, FUT battled past M80, the new-look Passion UA, and Astralis to move on to the next stage with a flawless record. They stumbled against Astralis and 3DMAX in Stage 2, but wins over HEROIC and B8 put them one win away from playoffs, which they managed to secure after a three-map series against G2.

After months of hard work, FUT will now finally get a taste of the playoffs of a big event. They will take on a daunting quarter-final opponent in MOUZ, but FUT should by no means be scared; the young squad caught Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin's men off guard in their last meeting in Cluj-Napoca with their style of "in your face" Counter-Strike. FUT will be looking to make good on their first big playoff showing, while also preparing themselves for the future possibility of playing in a packed arena game.

International Astralis notch their first big result

While FUT's push into the playoffs came after months and months of grinding, the newly English-speaking Astralis lineup have already secured their first playoff berth two months into the team's existence.

Love "⁠phzy⁠" Smidebrant and Gytis "⁠ryu⁠" Glušauskas got within touching distance of the quarter-finals in their BLAST Bounty debut, pushing past fnatic in their opening game before ultimately being trounced by eventual champs PARIVISION. Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen's men had more opportunities to get in reps at IEM Krakow, clearing the first stage with wins over paiN and GamerLegion and a loss to NRG. They started the next stage with a tough loss to Spirit, but made a hearty run through the lower bracket, securing revenge against PARIVISION and eliminating FUT, before being sent packing one game away from the arena after a narrow loss to FURIA.

Astralis have earned their best result yet with phzy and ryu

Astralis lost some of their momentum at PGL Cluj-Napoca, garnering just a single win over 3DMAX before bowing out 1-3, but they have more than made up for it at EPL. Astralis marched through Stage 1 with a 3-1 record, moving past Monte, HEROIC, and 3DMAX, while suffering a loss to FUT. They had a more up-and-down run through the second stage, culminating in back-to-back wins over FaZe and FURIA while on the verge of elimination in order to book their place in the playoffs.

Staehr on ryu and phzy: "I'm very surprised in a good way"

Despite the freshness and substantive changes made to the lineup, Victor "⁠Staehr⁠" Staehr expressed confidence in the team. "I think everyone in this team believes we can reach the playoffs, because I think, when we play good, we can play up to some of the best teams in the world," the rifler shared at PGL Cluj-Napoca.

"Normally, I don't expect much from people communication-wise when they come from international teams. I think a lot of international teams in tier 2, they just play for the aim and play individually, but [phzy and ryu] actually have very good vocal strengths. I was very surprised in a good way, so that was amazing."

The new lineup has lived up to their belief in reaching playoffs, in just their second month as a unit, a promising sign for a team that wants to be competitive at the most prestigious events.
