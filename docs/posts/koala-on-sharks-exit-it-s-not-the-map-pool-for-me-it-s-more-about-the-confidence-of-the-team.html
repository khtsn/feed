koala on Sharks' exit: "It's not the map pool for me, it's more about the confidence of the team"

Sharks arrived at Cologne knowing that they would be underdogs, but the manner of their exit was still disappointing for João "⁠koala⁠" Pfeffer.

"Right now it's a bit sad because we didn't play one match well," he explains, pointing to their slow start against HEROIC before they suffered three losses in a row.

A 2-0 record on home map Nuke was proof that, when comfortable, Sharks could compete, but they leave Germany winless on the other four maps they played as a reminder of the improvement required at this level.

Commiserations on the loss, what's going through your head after having your first Major?

It's good to play the Major, of course, but how it ends, for me right now it's a bit sad because we didn't play one match well. At least the first map against TYLOO, it was a good map. When we won HEROIC we didn't play well on Nuke, but we won because our Nuke is strong, but we only played one map well, in my opinion, in the entire Major.

So it's sad because we put so much expectation, we wanted to play well and prove ourselves to the world. We are not a tier-S team yet, but it's like a tier 2, challenging the best teams in the scene. But it didn't happen, so it's really frustrating after all.

It's not the map pool for me, it's more about the confidence of the team

What do you think is the difference between you guys on Nuke and the other maps? Because I think it was 2-0 Nuke this event, but then kind of struggling on the other ones, so what do you think that's down to?

Nuke we know how to play [laughs]. It's a bit more complicated than how I said it, but on Nuke we know how to prepare the game well. We can play against a lot of metas, teams like TYLOO don't play like the other teams we play, so we can't change our tactics or something like that, just change some details or something like that to counter them, and it works perfectly.

For me, the door guy on T side, the lurker, it's good because I always change something that works against the team, so it's better for us.

But in the other maps I think we don't have those type of things. We know how to play, but we don't have the solutions as well as we have on Nuke, so it's like if our game, the basics like trade, communication, entries, knowing the right times for bans, etc, we don't play well. So it's the most different thing about Nuke and the other maps.

So what's the main learning you guys are going to take from this then? Because it's your first international event of this calibre and everything. So is it the map pool, is it something else? What's the main takeaway?

I don't think it's the map pool. Inferno and Anubis were supposed to be good maps for us, but we didn't play well on these maps. It's a difficult team, but to be honest, we didn't play, it's our B game at least, these two maps. So it's not the map pool for me, it's more about the confidence of the team.

It's a common thing for us, we know that when we jump to another stage in the tournaments, like a bigger tournament, we don't play well in the first try, it's a common thing. It's sad because you know what's going to happen, we challenge not to get this, but it happens all the time. So in the finals we don't play as well as when we play in other stages of the playoffs. It's a thing that we need to improve more, our mental in these types of stages and tournaments.

t's normal for teams like us that don't have that pace of playing so many tier-one tournaments, to not go well the first try

We know it's your first major and everything, do you feel like you could hang at this level? Because individually we saw the same stuff as what we'd see in Brazil or online from you guys. Individually how did you feel against players like JamYoung?

Yeah. For me at least, I made not a good tournament for myself, but not a bad one. So I didn't think like, 'oh they are so smart or they are so good with their aim.' No I just thought they were more intelligent when it comes to decisions in mid rounds. The pace is more fast, just that, nothing after all.

Brazil is not like before. You see Gaimin Gladiators and us getting [eliminated] so fast you think we are bad, but there are a lot of teams in Brazil, like four teams now, 9z too. It's a strong scene now, it's not that different.

But when you play just against some types of teams, Brazilian teams, that have this slower pace, it's easier for us. And when we come here and challenge other teams that have another pace, it's more difficult. So it's normal for teams like us that don't have that pace of playing so many tier-one tournaments, to not go well the first try.
