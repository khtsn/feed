Swisher: "I feel like JBa was told what to do and not why on Wildcard; we're trying to get him to be more independent"

All three of the Americans on M80 finished Stage 1 above 1.20 ratings, with JBa posting his best rating since signing.

Restored into rotator roles, Michael "⁠Swisher⁠" Schmid has been enjoying himself in 2026 with a 1.16 rating on LAN this term.

He's not the only one on M80 having good time though, with Mason "⁠Lake⁠" Sanderson and Josh "⁠JBa⁠" Barutt joining him above the 1.20 rating mark as the team passed Stage 1 with a 3-1 record.

With new analyst Luca "⁠Munstur⁠" Ioanițiu, formerly of Passion UA, the team are better prepared than ever as they look to prove themselves against tougher opposition in Stage 2.

Things can still go wrong — Swisher called the B8 game "F-tier" — but with two Germans playing on home soil, the team would be forgiven for dreaming of making Stage 3 (or 4) for the first time as an organization in order to benefit from a home crowd.

Okay, congrats on getting through to stage 2. I mean, that comeback on Inferno was pretty good. What's your immediate reaction to the game?

T-side didn't go the way we wanted. Like, the pistol round and the force buy after, it was like flashbacks of the B8 game, right? I felt like we threw both of them again in the same fashion. To be a top player and to be a top team, you can't be babied

Michael "⁠Swisher⁠" Schmid on improving JBa's game

I just wanted to ask about JBA, because, I mean, we've all seen the kind of FACEIT stats and everything about how hard he's been grinding. I think he was grinding in China as well, someone told me. Tell me a bit about how he's kind of stepping up now, because it's all three of you Americans are above 1.2 ratings now.

The biggest thing for him has been trying to get him into our system and, like, into the team play system. Sometimes he gets, I think we saw it after player break, or we had like a three-week break after Dracula or something, where it's like the bad habits come back a little bit from the spam pugs.

So it's like, we're trying to get him to be like, 'like, yeah, like, this is the pug mentality, but, but come on, like, get back into this for a match.' Like, 'don't re-peek this.' And things like that. But he always pugs. He's like almost pugging with slaxz- 24-7 every day.

What's the kind of balance then for a young player like that, between the kind of, the confidence and kind of overcoaching them out of some of that? What's the kind of, the way you guys see it within the team?

It's a little difficult because he just has a little bit different view of the game sometimes. And he just didn't get taught, I feel like, when he was younger with Wildcard too much. That he was more so told what to do, not like why, in a sense.

And we've been trying to help get him to be like feeling the game more and getting him into a sense of like, 'Josh, you have the power to do your own things and make your own calls.' And we can't baby you in a sense. To be a top player and to be a top team, you can't be babied and things like that. So it's like, we're trying to get him to be more in the motions, to be more self-independent.

I mentioned as well, you being on 1.2 right now, that's kind of pretty normal for you this year. What do you think is behind that? New roles maybe?

What's going on? I mean, a few new roles. I feel like I was always kind of, I've always been like the guy to get thrown around a little bit to whatever, like the site that's struggling and I've never been one to complain. I was, I think on Dust2, for example, I went from B anchor to A anchor to now A rotate.

I've been kind of been thrown around everywhere to try to figure out our problems on maps. I'm just a super selfless guy. And I think that we've decided that me in rotation roles has been a little bit better for us. And we gave it a try this year. And so far so good. It was what I played originally was rotator roles before I joined M80. I think just on top of that, I've just been shooting well. I couldn't tell you why, but I just have.
