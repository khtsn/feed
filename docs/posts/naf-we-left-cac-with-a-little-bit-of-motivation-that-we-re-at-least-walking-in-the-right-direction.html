NAF: "We left CAC with a little bit of motivation that we're at least walking in the right direction"

Liquid's 2026 has been a dreary one, but the Major is a chance to rewrite all narratives and the team got off to a good start with a win over BIG.

For a team that has a 4-10 record in best-of-ones in the last twelve months, it's an especially good sign to kick off with a 13-10 win.

siuhy: "It's a very tough moment for the team"

After struggling so badly in 2026, beating 3DMAX and coming close against The MongolZ was seen as a decent result at CAC as Keith "⁠NAF⁠" Markovic and Liquid search desperately for some green shoots. A win against BIG now can add to what they hope is a slow-build of momentum.

"Team Liquid is a fantastic organization, you know, so it's super disappointing to be kind of letting them down with how bad of a string of events in the past year have been," NAF said as he reflected on the year so far.

"But I think we're slowly getting there, hopefully striving for greatness. The goal is just to get as many points as we possibly can."

Okay NAF, must feel good to get started with a win. Tell me how it feels.

Yes, of course, it's always nice to start off strong, I feel like that's something we're not used to. If I recall, I think in the Budapest Major we were going down 0-1 or 0-2 in the start usually for the most part. So it feels good, so yeah, just got to keep going forward.

I think your best of one record is 4-10 in the last twelve months or something, it's something unfortunate. Did the team come into this with any kind of different approach, I mean obviously it's hard to do that, but are you aware of that?

Yeah, no, I mean it's something we've brought up in the past where we're starting out not so well in the best of ones. But saying one thing and doing it and taking action is one different thing as well. I don't know, we came in, we feel prepared, we feel comfortable, so we're just happy that we're able to start off strong and we just hope to keep it going.

Was it also nice to get started with a nice 7-5 T side as well, because we know you guys have struggled a bit on T side this year, so was it kind of encouraging to get started like that?

Oh yeah, of course, I mean T side Nuke, you want to start with the ball moving pretty fast. We're able to win the pistol round, which helps. I always thought throughout the years of playing that when teams start their first game, some people might feel a little cold, and with playing as CTs you need to be more sharp, whereas T's can be more mobile and just spamming their Glock. So I think it worked in our favour and I have zero issues with starting T sides when it's the first game, so it felt good to start off strong and I think it helped a lot of course.

Team Liquid is a fantastic organization, you know, so it's super disappointing to be kind of letting them down

And of course, it's good to start with a win as well, but can you tell me a bit about your preparation, and obviously, in the officials, maybe it wasn't helping that much, but do you guys feel prepared coming in?

Yeah, of course, like you mentioned, BIG hasn't played like an official in like five weeks, so they could surprise in every factor, but at the same time, they could feel a little cold when it comes to playing on the main stages and the tournaments, so overall we're just doing our best with what we got. Of course it's been pretty tough for Liquid the past year, so we just need to find our stride. I think with our previous tournament at CAC we had good games versus 3DMAX, and then there was a chance we even beat The MongolZ, so we left that tournament with a little bit of motivation that we're at least walking in the right direction, you know, so.

And then we had a good bootcamp before here, so we just got to keep it rolling and yeah, we're confident, we're preparing and doing everything that we can, because Liquid deserves to be at the top, so we need to just keep working.
