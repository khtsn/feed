The MongolZ send pistol-less Liquid home

Liquid end malbsMd's debut event with a 1-13 record in pistol rounds, and went 0-4 vs The MongolZ.

The MongolZ are still fighting for a playoff berth at BLAST Open after knocking Liquid out of proceedings.

In their debut with Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa, Liquid managed to beat Ninjas in Pyjamas two days ago but could not build on that win against The MongolZ as their struggles in pistol rounds continued.

Over their three series in Copenhagen, Liquid lost 13 of their 14 pistol rounds, and failed to convert the one that they did win.

It speaks to their credit then, that so many of their maps were close and it was the same story today. Ancient went to overtime as Liquid fought back from 4-10 down, while Mirage reached 10-11 before The MongolZ converted it 13-10.

But in a worrying sign reminiscent of past iterations, key clutch moments went against Liquid. On Ancient, at 14-14, Garidmagnai "⁠bLitz⁠" Byambasuren won a backbreaking 1v3. Then on Mirage, Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold was the hero with three clutch wins on his own, including one in round 22.

Those key moments, added to their dire pistol record, combine to create a sour taste in the mouth; Liquid know this game could have easily gone the other way.

"We look a lot better," Jonathan "⁠EliGE⁠" Jablonowski reflected post-game, after a series where he posted 103.2 ADR. "We almost beat MongolZ, who are a really strong team. We lost in OT, and any of those rounds could have gone our way. Against Spirit it was really close too. Close isn't good enough but we got Mario for this event.

"The feel of the team feels like we are on a good path. We're moving forward, we're getting the things that we want, focusing on the things that we need to. This year, so many things have prevented us from focusing on the game, getting better, getting protocols, meta strats, and all the other things other teams have."

The MongolZ, meanwhile, will face the winner of 9z and Spirit for a spot in playoffs.
