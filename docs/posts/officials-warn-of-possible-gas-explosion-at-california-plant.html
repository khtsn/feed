Officials Warn of Possible Gas Explosion at California Plant

U.S.|Officials Warn of Possible Gas Explosion at California Plant

https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/22/us/california-chemical-leak-evacuation-orange-county.html

Evacuation orders were issued to residents in parts of Orange County after a chemical leak that was initially reported on Thursday grew more serious.

May 22, 2026Updated 5:26 p.m. ET

Residents in parts of Orange County, Calif., were under evacuation orders on Friday after a gas tank with toxic chemicals leaked and was at risk of exploding.

The Orange County Fire Authority responded to reports of an industrial chemical leak at an aerospace manufacturing plant in Garden Grove, about 35 miles south of Los Angeles, on Thursday afternoon. Firefighters at the scene found vapor emanating from a tank containing methyl methacrylate, an industrial chemical used in plastics and manufacturing.

“We have a tank that is actively in crisis,” Craig Covey, an incident commander with the fire department, said in a video update on Friday. “The tank that is in the biggest crisis is in fact unable to be secured and mitigated.”

Mr. Covey said the tank will either fail and spill up to 7,000 gallons of toxic chemicals, or it will blow up and affect nearby tanks that also have toxic chemicals.

Evacuation orders were first issued on Thursday as firefighters used hoses to cool the tank, which had overheated. Those orders were later lifted and residents were advised that they could return home on Thursday evening after the leak had been mitigated.

By Friday morning, however, residents were again told to leave their homes after responders discovered damage to a valve on the tank. The authorities urged residents to comply with the evacuation orders, which had expanded to cover a wider area.

The episode occurred at GKN Aerospace, which builds engines and landing gear. A company spokeswoman said in a statement on Friday that “the situation at our Garden Grove site remains ongoing, and we are fully focused on working with emergency services and the relevant authorities to ensure the safety of our employees and the local community.”

More than a dozen schools were closed, and two evacuation centers had been coordinated.
