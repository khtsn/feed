‘Imminent’ Risk of Dam Failure Prompts Evacuation in Illinois

Residents in Nashville, Ill., were told to evacuate their homes on Tuesday amid heavy rain and the risk of a dam failure.

July 16, 2024, 11:56 a.m. ET

Residents in Washington County, Ill., were ordered to evacuate their homes on Tuesday morning because of the “imminent” risk of a dam failure as strong storms moved through the area.

The Washington County Emergency Management Agency told residents in Nashville, Ill., about 55 miles southeast of St. Louis, to evacuate, warning that “the failure of the Nashville dam is imminent.”

About 3,200 people live in Nashville. It was unclear how many were in the evacuation area.

Portions of south-central Illinois, including Washington County, were under a flash flood warning on Tuesday morning, as strong storms brought heavy rainfall. The National Weather Service in St. Louis said that 2 to 5 inches of rain had fallen in some areas, with an additional 1 to 3 inches expected.

The storms on Tuesday came after severe weather on Monday night in the Chicago area, where there were reports of tornadoes and damaging winds.

The heavy rainfall in south-central Illinois, the Weather Service said, could bring “life-threatening flash flooding” to creeks, highways and streets across the area.

“Move immediately to higher ground,” the Weather Service said.
