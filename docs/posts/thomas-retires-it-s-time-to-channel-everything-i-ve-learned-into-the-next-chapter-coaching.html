Thomas retires: "It's time to channel everything I've learned into the next chapter: coaching"

Thomas "⁠Thomas⁠" Utting has announced his retirement as a player to focus on taking his learnings as an in-game leader and applying them to the next step in his career as a coach.

"After nearly a decade competing in both CS:GO and CS2, going from the UK scene to the Majors stage, I've decided it's time to channel everything I've learned into the next chapter: coaching," Thomas announced.

"I've been fortunate to experience this game from every angle. Breaking through with Endpoint, competing internationally, and most importantly, representing the UK at BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 with ITB, reaching 5th-8th place from a run that started in the open qualifiers."

That quarter-final finish at a Major was Thomas' biggest achievement in a career that spanned nearly a decade from his first matches recorded on HLTV with CeX in 2016 to his final matches with Nemesis in late 2025, with both British and international rosters.

"This decision has been heavy on my mind for over a year now," Thomas continues, "I personally don’t have the ability to compete as a player anymore. I have way more enjoyment now helping a team from a coaching position, which I've been lucky enough to work with plenty of great coaches throughout my time.

"Competing has been an amazing chapter of my life, but going forward, I want to start a new one which is focused on supporting IGLs and developing players for teams, something which I've always strived for in UKCS and international teams, but have found it harder while playing as an IGL."

Thomas also stated that in order to continue developing the skills he learned as an in-game leader, he is looking for opportunities as a head coach, assistant coach, or analyst.
