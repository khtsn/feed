Gen.G conquer Leviatán in 2-1 win

Gen.G Gen.G Korea Rank #1 Munchkin Byeon Sang-beom (변상범) t3xture Kim Na-ra (김나라) Meteor Kim Tae-O (김태오) Lakia Kim Jong-min (김종민) Karon Kim Won-tae (김원태) defeated Leviatán Leviatán Latin America South Rank #1 tex Ian Botsch kiNgg Francisco Aravena Mazino Roberto Rivas aspas Erick Santos C0M Corbin Lee 2-1 in their debut match at Masters Shanghai, with the Masters Madrid finalists getting their first win of the Swiss stage. Leviatán looked strong on their map pick, but Gen.G rallied back to win the next two maps convincingly.

The map veto began with Leviatán picking Icebox and Gen.G picking Lotus, leaving Breeze as the potential third map.

Icebox was a battle of mid-rounding, with Leviatán coming out on top thanks to their pistol round wins and good damage done on bonus rounds. Add onto that a 21-kill performance from Erick "aspas" Santos, and Gen.G had too much on their plate to deal with. A 7-5 lead turned into a 13-7 win for Leviatán.

Lotus continued the pistol success for Leviatán, but Gen.G had a strong defense and an even stronger attack. The duelist duel swung towards Kim "t3xture" Na-ra (김나라) when Raze came into the field, dropping 26 kills in only 18 rounds. There was another 7-5 half, this time in favor of Gen.G, and a perfect attack half ended the map 13-5 and took the series to map three.

Breeze brought some chaos to a relatively structured game, and Gen.G were thriving in the chaos thanks to their Yoru pick. Despite losing another early pistol, the Madrid finalists won multiple rounds after dying first, with the momentum shift being a 2v4 win on A site. Gen.G entered the half up 8-4, and Leviatán struggled against the aggression from the double duelist defense. The map ended 13-7, and the series went to Gen.G 2-1.
