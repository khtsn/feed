Brollan: "It was just a lot of details; we were not on the same page"

"You can't lose, basically," the Swedish captain said about being in the lower bracket. "I think it's positive in a way."

MOUZ are down to the lower bracket in Copenhagen

MOUZ find themselves with their backs against the wall just one match into BLAST Open Rotterdam, falling 0-2 to The MongolZ in what was their first-ever series defeat to the Mongolian side after ten consecutive map wins.

After a highly contested Nuke game that went the distance, MOUZ were nowhere to be seen on their map pick of Inferno, where Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin was the only player to hit double digits as his team was trounced 13-3.

"I think they punished us very hard, especially on Inferno," the Swedish captain told HLTV after the match. "They had a good game plan by pushing down mid, which we were not ready for.

"Overall, I think we did not play the best game, of course. We made a lot of mistakes in our defaults. We did not trade properly. We had bad late-round executes, and we got multi-killed all the time. I think for sure they played the best series they have ever played against us.

"Today, it was just a lot of details; we were not on the same page. I would say we need to talk about the details, and we just need to improve from it. We have one day off now, so we need to talk about the game and what we should do. And try to be more ready for the next games.

"We are struggling a little bit in the mid-round at the moment. We will just try to talk about it more and try to improve as much as we can."

Brollan admitted that the loss to FUT was a bitter pill to swallow

BLAST Open Rotterdam offers MOUZ an opportunity to rebound from their latest setback, a quarter-final exit at ESL Pro League 23. Without teams like Vitality, Falcons or FURIA in the bracket, MOUZ were regarded as a strong favorite heading into the playoffs, only to fall to FUT in a close three-map affair.

"Of course, it affected us a lot," he said. "There was a lot of talk that we were the favorites and were going to win the event. Especially for me: it was in Sweden, and my whole family was there, and I wanted to prove more. I took it hard.

"But shit happens sometimes. You just need to move on. You have another tournament the following week. But especially in tier-one, you need to move on instantly from losses. Of course, you can be sad for one or two days, but then you need to reset for the next tournament. And I'd say we've done it really well."

The recent losses to PARIVISION, FUT and The MongolZ are a worrying sign for a team that began the season looking to build on the consistency shown in 2025, when they won PGL Cluj-Napoca and were at least top four at 11 of the other 13 events they attended with Lotan "⁠Spinx⁠" Giladi.

Asked why his team has struggled to find the same level of consistency in 2026, Brollan said: "It's a good question. I don't know.

"We need to talk about how we lost today. I think against FUT, we saw it was an energy thing. They really wanted it more than us, and we could hear it. When we played, they were in our faces, and they wanted to win. Of course, we wanted to win. We were the favorites; we had a little bit more pressure on ourselves.

"Today, of course we were ready. We had a lot of confidence in beating them. We had been beating them a lot in the past. Sometimes, they are going to show their dominance in the game."

MOUZ have a mountain to climb to make it to Rotterdam Ahoy. If they move past 9z in their next match, they will then have to survive two more elimination series, including a clash against the loser of Spirit vs PARIVISION.
