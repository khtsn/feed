FaZe stun G2 to reach semis in Fort Worth

FaZe are through to the semi-finals at BLAST Rivals Season 1 following a 2-1 victory over G2 in a high-octane series, setting up a rematch against Natus Vincere on Saturday.

The win showcased what makes FaZe such an entertaining team to watch, with great clutches, messy rounds, and a never-say-die attitude that saw them win a deluge of man disadvantages and survive three series points on Mirage.

"When we got to this event, I said I wanted to get one or two upsets, and we just got two of them," Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken said after the match. "I feel like anything else we do here is the cherry on top. But I really think we can make a push for that trophy.

"I think we have a good feeling of how NAVI play. To be honest, the first time we played them, we were very nervous, we played scared, and it wasn't us. The past two series, we played more like… You know, FaZe. This is how we should play."

A highly entertaining first half of Dust2 ended in a 6-6 stalemate because of some unlikely rounds going FaZe's way. G2 twice lost their grip on the game, once in an anti-eco round and then in a 5v2 situation, with Helvijs "⁠broky⁠" Saukants at the heart of the action on both occasions, much to the delight of the crowd.

FaZe retook the lead after switching to the CT side and used that momentum to reach double digits. Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García breathed new life into G2 with a series of big plays (though in the end he was left ruing a wasted 1v1 against a low-health Jakub "⁠jcobbb⁠" Pietruszewski) before FaZe finished the game off by winning the last two rounds of regulation.

G2 came out guns blazing on Ancient and cruised to an 8-0 lead on the T side of Ancient. FaZe still mustered three rounds in the half, but any momentum they had quickly evaporated as G2 increased their lead after halftime.

Down 4-11, FaZe refused to throw in the towel and enjoyed a spell of dominance. A 1v2 clutch by Twistzz gave them even more confidence, but their comeback try fell short in the end.

David "⁠frozen⁠" Čerňanský fired FaZe into the lead on Mirage with a crisp 4K in the pistol round, but it wouldn't last. After a hectic period of play when neither team managed to stamp their authority on proceedings, G2 put their foot down and secured a 7-5 lead.

G2 extended their lead with a clean pistol round before Ryan "⁠Neityu⁠" Aubry showed nerves of steel in a 1v2 clutch on the B site. Their respite proved brief, however, as G2 quickly retook control of the game, hitting series point off the back of a clutch from Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko.

FaZe hit back in the following round, and then, when all hope seemed lost, Twistzz and frozen pulled off a miraculous 2v5 clutch. That round altered the course of the game and swung the momentum back FaZe's favour, ultimately pushing them to win the game in overtime.
