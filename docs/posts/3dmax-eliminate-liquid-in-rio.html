3DMAX eliminate Liquid in Rio

Lucky produced an excellent performance on Ancient

Liquid kicked off Nuke with a halftime lead thanks to a solid CT side, stretching their lead to 11-4 with a converted pistol victory on their offense. 3DMAX's historically strong Nuke defense then kicked in, the Frenchmen repeatedly fending off late-round resurgences to drag the game to OT.

Having already squandered an 11-4 lead in the map, Liquid proceeded to squander a 4v2 advantage in the final round of overtime to grand 3DMAX the lead in the series.

Ancient was Lucas "⁠Lucky⁠" Chastang's map, the French rifler putting Liquid to the sword to ensure a straightforward win for his team. Liquid manage to scramble together a decent CT side but were dire on offense, something Lucky and 3DMAX duly took advantage of to secure the series.

Liquid's poor form continues

Liquid's level was questionable before the addition of Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa, and there is little sign that their form is due to improve any time soon. The team scraped into the Major more by virtue of other team's failures than their own success, and now they have suffered the ignominy of another early tournament exit.

The T sides were unconvincing across the series, Liquid struggled to get late-round scenarios over the line, and lost Nuke despite dominating in the opening kill department. A lot of work is necessary ahead of the team's Major campaign in a couple of months time.
