FaZe's Major hopes collapse after DraculaN elimination by fnatic

FaZe's chances of making IEM Cologne Major are dead and buried after the team's early elimination at DraculaN Season 6, where they managed a single win over aimclub before losses to Passion UA and fnatic.

In order to have any hope of rescuing Major attendance FaZe would need to skip PGL Bucharest in order to attend another VRS LAN that concludes prior to the Major invite cut-off. When asked by HLTV in the wake of their elimination at DraculaN, FaZe said they planned to attend PGL Bucharest, thus killing their Major chances.

After kicking off their DraculaN campaign with a win over aimclub, FaZe were sent to the lower bracket with a hard-fought series loss at the hands of Passion UA. There fnatic awaited them, with FaZe's Major hopes firmly on the line.

FaZe were off the pace from the start of Anubis, struggling to get going on the favoured T side despite some Deagle heroics early doors by David "⁠frozen⁠" Čerňanský. Once the sides switched fnatic dominated, easily strolling to a 13-6 victory off the back of a fantastic performance by IGL Rodion "⁠fEAR⁠" Smyk.

On their own pick of Overpass FaZe weren't any better, unable to handle fnatic's retakes in the first half before finding themselves rolled over by an up-tempo offense in the second half, consigning FaZe to elimination.

FaZe's elimination from DraculaN and the end of their Major qualification hopes is the culmination of what has been a terrible season for the European combine. The team have only won four series all year thus far, one of those being against aimclub, and have been one of the first teams eliminated from every tournament they have attended.

Should they ultimately miss IEM Cologne Major, it would mean FaZe would be absent from a Counter-Strike Major after 16 appearances in a row, and a terrible fall following their appearance in the grand final last time around at StarLadder Budapest Major.
