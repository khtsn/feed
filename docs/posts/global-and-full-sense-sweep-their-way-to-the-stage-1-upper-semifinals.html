Global and FULL SENSE sweep their way to the Stage 1 upper semifinals

VCT 2026: Pacific Stage 1

1 day ago

The playoffs of the VCT Pacific Stage 1 playoffs began on Thursday, with four teams battling it out for a chance at either of the group leaders or facing elimination in the lower bracket.

Here's how the first day of action played out:

In Global Esports' playoff debut, a squad consisting of three players making their first-ever playoffs at the VCT level helped excel above Pacific playoff regulars Rex Regum Qeon. The matchup was also the first time that head coaches FrosT and Jovi went head to head, as the two former TALON TALON Inactive Killua Tanate Teerasawad thyy Anupong Preamsak Crws Thanamethk Mahatthananuyut JitBoyS Jittana Nokngam primmie Papaphat Sriprapha coaches duked it out on their new teams.

The match started on GE's Breeze, a map that RRQ had not played once in Stage 1 after going 0-3 on it in Kickoff. In a shifting of roles for RRQ, Monyet was moved off of Neon and onto Harbor for a Viper-Harbor comp, while Jemkin assumed Duelist duties on Neon. The rest of the comp was rounded out with a Sova-KAY/O pairing, while Global took on a less conventional comp. With Viper as the solo Controller, GE had a double-Sentinel comp with xavi8k on Sage and Autumn on Chamber. RRQ began on attack, and the map was back-and-forth to begin. No team won more than two rounds in a row, with RRQ holding a slim 7-5 lead entering halftime.

PatMen doing PatMen things.

Once Global got onto its attack side, the game began to flow smoothly. Using Sage's Barrier Orb and Slow Orb to delay retakes, Global pushed forward, using a run of five straight rounds to claim a 12-9 lead before closing out the map 13-11.

RRQ's map pick of Pearl appeared to be a counter-pick to GE. Through the Stage 1 group stage, GE banned it four times and played it just once, losing to Paper Rex Paper Rex Asia-Pacific Rank #1 invy Adrian Jiggs Reyes Jinggg Wang Jing Jie f0rsakeN Jason Susanto d4v41 Khalish Rusyaidee something Ilya Petrov . While RRQ opted for a Killjoy-Chamber double-Sentinel comp with Jemkin on Chamber and Monyet back on Neon, GE went with a double-Duelist look that would have been hard to predict, with Autumn on Phoenix for the first time with GE. RRQ, opting for Skye instead of Fade, appeared to struggle on attack against the more proactive GE.

Both teams were knotted at six rounds apiece entering the second half, which played host to hero play after hero play. As clutches, crucial ults, and seemingly unwinnable rounds flipped, Global appeared to be the team with more magic, winning out the gunfights and putting itself in a position to win as long as the bomb went down. In ten post-plants, RRQ only succeeded in defusing two. GE closed out the map 13-10, sending RRQ down to the lower bracket and earning themselves a rematch against Paper Rex.

In the second match of the day, FULL SENSE went up against Kiwoom DRX and claimed a clean 2-0, claiming the series advantage over them after splitting two games in Kickoff. Heading into this match, FULL SENSE was without head coach THEELOVEFAMILY , who stepped away for a necessary surgical procedure. KRX also had a personnel change, with yong moved to the bench in favor of Hermes , who was benched after KRX (known then as DRX) finished sixth at Kickoff.

Play opened on FS's pick of Lotus, a map that KRX played four times through Kickoff and FS played just once. Both teams fielded similar comps with Omen, Vyse, and Raze, but while KRX opted for Neon and Fade, FS went with Jett and Skye. After playing four maps of defense-heavy Lotus in the group stage, KRX wrapped up its attack half up 7-5, which already stood to be its best attack-side production this split on the map. Once FS got on attack, it was clear that seven was not enough. The Thai team took total control of the map. Led by JitBoyS , who had 19 of his 26 kills on attack, FULL SENSE dropped just two rounds for the rest of the map, winning 13-9.

This guy was playing Sentinel just a few months ago, by the way.

KRX picked Fracture, a map it had played three times this Stage and won twice with two different comps. Against FS, they went with the Raze-Neon comp, which featured Breach and Cypher. Meanwhile, FULL SENSE went with primmie on Phoenix and JitBoyS on Neon while opting for a Fade instead of Breach as the solo Initiator.

Fracture, despite its final score suggesting a tighter finish, was a wash. FULL SENSE ended its attack half with a 9-3 lead, an expected result on a map that is considered attacker-sided. After winning its third pistol of the day and converting on the bonus, the score quickly became 12-3 in their favor. A force-buy from KRX started to snowball some momentum for the side, but FULL SENSE wrapped things up soon after 13-7.
