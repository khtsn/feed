Global Esports parts with yoman

Global Esports Global Esports Asia-Pacific Rank #24 UdoTan Go Kyung-won (고경원) Kr1stal Savva Fedorov Papi Federico Evangelista xavi8k Xavier Juan Autumn Kale Dunne Deryeon Derrick Yee has bid farewell to Chae "yoman" Young-moon (채영문).

After Global began their 2025 season with a 9th-12th finish in Kickoff and a 9th-10th finish in Stage 1, yoman was added to the roster along with Joseph "ban" Seungmin Oh as part of a midseason changeup for the squad. Despite the changes, Global proved unable to reverse their fortunes in Stage 2, ending the group stage 1-4 and failing to qualify for playoffs.

Before Global Esports, yoman served a brief stint with Gen.G Gen.G Korea Rank #3 Ash Ha Hyun-cheol (하현철) Karon Kim Won-tae (김원태) t3xture Kim Na-ra (김나라) at the beginning of the year for Kickoff, where they placed third. The Initiator player also previously played with the pre-Ascension iteration of Nongshim RedForce Nongshim RedForce Korea Rank #1 Xross Jeonghwan (정환) Rb Goo Sang-min (구상민) Francis Kim Mu-bin (김무빈) Dambi Lee Hyuk-kyu (이혁규) Ivy Park Sung-hyeon (박성현) in 2024 and served as the in-game leader for Rare Atom Rare Atom China Rank #5 Guran Zhou Minyan (周铭言) nnono Midnight Wang Yingjie (王英杰) in 2023 as part of their China Ascension runner-up run.

This announcement comes after a slew of other changes for Global Esports; since the beginning of the offseason, the team has parted with Donnie "Elevated" Chell and ban and added Kale "Autumn" Dunne from FunPlus Phoenix, Xavier "xavi8k" Juan from NAOS, and Hector "FrosT" Rosario from TALON.
