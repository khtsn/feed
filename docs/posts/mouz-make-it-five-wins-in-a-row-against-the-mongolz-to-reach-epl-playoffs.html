MOUZ make it five wins in a row against The MongolZ to reach EPL playoffs

Spinx ends Stage 2 with a 1.48 rating, the highest of anyone in the Swiss stage.

MOUZ have extended their win-streak against The MongolZ to five matches, ten maps, and 458 days with victory on day three of ESL Pro League.

The win makes Dennis "⁠sycrone⁠" Nielsen's troops the first team to book a spot in the 2200-seater Annexet arena, and MOUZ will enter the Stockholm stage as the favorites given Spirit's shakey form and after FURIA revealed that Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo will skip playoffs.

With Vitality not even at the event, it is as good a chance as MOUZ will have for their first title since PGL Cluj-Napoca more than twelve months ago.

And in today's series, they certainly looked like favorites with 13-1 and 13-11 wins over The MongolZ.

After shakey moments on map one of matches against Astralis and HEROIC, where 9-2 and 7-3 leads were given up, MOUZ were back at their well-oiled best as their teamplay and good reads barely gave The MongolZ a sniff on Ancient.

Overpass, despite a strong 4-1 start on the T side for MOUZ, needed all 24 rounds after they were caught out by some contact plays from The MongolZ but the win was eventually completed 13-11.

The MongolZ can take some confidence from a decent Overpass debut with Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar, and still have two chances to make it to the arena themselves — and will at least have a chance for revenge in their Rotterdam opener.

But for MOUZ, and skipper Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin, Stockholm awaits. "I don't want to have high expectations, too much pressure on ourselves, there's still good teams, FURIA and Spirit," Brollan said post-game.

"I want to prove to everyone and ourselves that we are capable of winning a big event."

With a 3-0 group stage, his family attending the home arena, and having won his last two Swedish LANs, Brollan may well get his wish.

Spinx tops the board in Stage 2

So often, we talk about team efforts within MOUZ.

But Lotan "⁠Spinx⁠" Giladi, already the highest-rated player of the event going into the match, improved his score to a 1.48 rating after topping the board once again with 91.3 ADR and a 1.53 rating in the 2-0 win.

His multi-kills were both impactful and flashy, putting his late-round prowess on display by turning two key 3v5s MOUZ's way. His 4k on Overpass was particularly showstopping, a display of individual brilliance his side will need to see more of in the arena.
