At Least 6 Killed in Storm That Leaves More Than 700,000 Without Power

U.S.|Storm Brings Widespread Freezing Rain, Leaving More Than 900,000 Without Power

https://www.nytimes.com/2025/03/30/us/ice-storm-michigan-ontario.html

A spring storm that brought freezing rain downed trees and snapped power lines in Indiana, Michigan, Minnesota and Ontario.

Published March 30, 2025Updated March 31, 2025, 12:13 p.m. ET

Nearly 900,000 electricity customers in Indiana, Michigan, Wisconsin and Ontario, Canada’s most populous province, were without power on Monday morning after a spring storm brought freezing rain and sleet to the Great Lakes region over the weekend.

The storm also brought the threat of tornadoes to parts of the Southeast and Mid-Atlantic, and put a large swath of the area under severe thunderstorm warnings on Monday.

The National Weather Service warned of severe weather and heavy rainfall from the Mid-Atlantic to the central Gulf Coast on Monday.

A National Weather Service office in Michigan posted photos on social media of trees weighed down with icicles. Accumulations of ice there ranged from half an inch to nearly an inch.

On Sunday night, the Weather Service also warned for continued icing across northern sections of Michigan, “with significant amounts of mixed precipitation through tonight across eastern upper Michigan” as well as thunderstorms in the area.

In Ontario, images on social media showed downed and ice-encased trees, some of which were making roads impassable.

More than a million people across parts of Michigan, Minnesota, New York, Vermont and Wisconsin were under an ice storm warning late Sunday morning through the evening.

In Michigan, state officials activated the State Emergency Operations Center after the ice storm downed trees and power lines, making some roads impassable in the northern part of the state.

This ice storm produced more ice than usual, said Harold Dippman, a meteorologist at the Weather Service office in Gaylord, Mich. A typical one in the region produces up to a quarter of an inch of ice.

The storm was also lasting longer than usual. A typical one lasts six to 12 hours, Mr. Dippman said, this one lasted multiple days.

In Michigan, about 337,000 customers were without power on Monday morning, according to the monitoring site poweroutage.us, with around 58,000 customers without power in neighboring Wisconsin, and more than 53,000 without power in Indiana.

In Ontario, almost 400,000 customers were without power on Monday morning, according to poweroutage.com. The outages, concentrated in central and eastern Ontario, were largely caused by ice that weighed down tree branches, Hydro One, Ontario’s main power transmission company, said on its website.

In the Northeast, temperatures edged up on Sunday, ending an icing threat in the southern and western Adirondacks, Upper Hudson Valley, southern Vermont and northern Berkshire County in Massachusetts, according to the Weather Service.

Livia Albeck-Ripka and Johnny Diaz contributed reporting.
