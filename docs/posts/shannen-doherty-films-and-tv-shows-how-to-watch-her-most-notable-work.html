Shannen Doherty Films and TV Shows: How to Watch Her Most Notable Work

The actress, who died on Saturday, was best known for “Beverly Hills, 90210,” but she wasn’t only a teen idol.

Shannen Doherty died Saturday, at 53, after a long battle with cancer. She left behind a legacy as a touchstone of 1990s television thanks to her role as the Midwestern new girl Brenda Walsh in “Beverly Hills, 90210.” But before she became a teen TV idol — as well as a tabloid fixation — she warmed hearts as a child in “Little House on the Prairie.”

Here’s where to stream some of Doherty’s most notable work, including, of course, “Beverly Hills, 90210.”

‘Little House on the Prairie’

Years before Doherty began shaking things up at West Beverly Hills High School, she did so in “Little House on the Prairie.” Doherty joined the long-running series as Jenny Wilder, the niece of Melissa Gilbert’s Laura Ingalls Wilder and her husband, Almanzo Wilder, played by Dean Butler. Doherty appeared as Jenny, a pigtail-wearing girl whose father dies, throughout the ninth and final season of the show, and in subsequent “Little House” TV movies.

Stream it on Peacock.

‘Heathers’

The first sign of Doherty’s star power came with the release of “Heathers,” the pitch-black comedy directed by Michael Lehmann and written by Daniel Waters. Doherty plays Heather Duke, a member of the title clique of mean girls who terrorize their high school and all share the same name. (Well, except for the protagonist, Veronica Sawyer, played by Winona Ryder.) When the lead Heather, Heather Chandler (Kim Walker), is murdered by a new kid in school, J.D. (Christian Slater) — a death that is passed off as a suicide with help from Veronica —Doherty’s Heather sees a path to power. She claims her dead friend’s red scrunchie as her crown, but her status obsession also makes her an easy target for J.D.’s manipulations.

‘Beverly Hills, 90210’

Doherty remained best known for “Beverly Hills, 90210,” the influential teen series that debuted on Fox in 1990, created by Darren Star and executive produced by Aaron Spelling. Doherty played Brenda Walsh, a Beverly Hills newcomer who arrives with her twin brother, Brandon (Jason Priestley). Doherty told The New York Times in 2008: “Whereas her brother would feel a little more confident and secure, and just fit in automatically, I think for Brenda there was always a little more of, ‘Is this the place for me?’ I think she probably struggled through that, subconsciously, throughout the show.” Brenda’s relationship with the cool guy Dylan McKay (Luke Perry) was one of TV’s defining romances of the 1990s. Doherty left “90210” after four seasons, but would later reprise her role in the 2000s reboot.

Stream on Paramount+ and Pluto TV.

‘Mallrats’

One of Doherty’s first roles after she left “Beverly Hills, 90210,” was in “Mallrats,” Kevin Smith’s shaggy ensemble comedy about young people who hang around a mall. In an early scene, Doherty’s character, Rene, serves a withering breakup to Brodie Bruce (Jason Lee), tired of unsatisfying sex with a guy who only wants to play video games. In a tribute to Doherty on Instagram, Smith wrote that she was the only reason that the movie was greenlit. “Shannen was one of the most famous people on the planet — and the fact that she picked Mallrats as her first follow-up to 90210 *made* that movie happen,” he wrote.

Buy or rent on most major platforms.

‘Nowhere’

Given that the director Gregg Araki described his film “Nowhere” as “‘Beverly Hills, 90210’ on acid,” it made sense that Doherty would make a brief appearance. With her hair teased high, she shows up alongside Rose McGowan — who would later replace her on “Charmed” — and Traci Lords as a trio of “Val-Chicks” who gossip at a bus stop before being obliterated by an alien.

Stream it on the Criterion Channel.

‘Charmed’

Doherty eventually returned to the Aaron Spelling empire with “Charmed,” co-starring alongside Alyssa Milano and Holly Marie Combs in this WB series about three magical sisters. She played Prue Halliwell, gifted with the power of telekinesis, but as with “Beverly Hills, 90210,” her time on the show was relatively short. (There were reports of tension between her and Milano.) Prue was killed off in the third season finale. Doherty also directed three episodes of the series, including the one in which her character is murdered.

‘Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back’

Doherty returned to the Kevin Smith universe with a cameo in “Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back.” She played herself in a sequence set on the Miramax lot, where she is starring in a “Scream” movie and faces off against Ghostface. The director Wes Craven also makes an appearance.

Stream on Pluto TV.
