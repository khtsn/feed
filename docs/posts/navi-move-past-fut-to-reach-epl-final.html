NAVI move past FUT to reach EPL final

NAVI beat their former academy squad in a high-octane series with a deluge of highlights.

iM was the star as NAVI beat FUT in three maps

Natus Vincere have secured a spot in the grand final of ESL Pro League 23 with a hard-fought 2-1 win over FUT.

NAVI cruised on Mirage but left it late to win the series over their former academy squad. They let an 8-4 lead on Ancient slip away as FUT pulled off numerous clutches on the T side. Things looked bleak for NAVI on Dust2 as they found themselves down 6-2, but then Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri rescued his team with a heroic ace clutch.

"We trolled on Ancient," makazze said after the match. "It could have been easier. We should have closed it. But sometimes, it doesn't happen. It's fine."

In the grand final, their first at a Big Event since IEM Rio 2024, Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen's charges will take on the winner of Astralis vs Aurora.

Heading into the series, FUT head coach András "⁠coolio⁠" Fercsák had said that if his team got off to a strong start, they could get into the heads of Natus Vincere's players.

But what happened was the exact opposite: FUT were outclassed and outsmarted in the entire first half of Mirage, when they couldn't win a single opening duel. Their only T rounds came off the back of straight A executes on low buys as NAVI cruised to a 10-2 lead, led by Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan and Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov, who combined for 30 frags.

The Romanian added to NAVI's commanding lead with three frags in the ensuing pistol and two in the conversion as the team overcame a 3v5 disadvantage. Dmytro "⁠dem0n⁠" Myroshnychenko bought FUT some time with a 1v2 clutch, but the respite proved brief as NAVI quickly finished the game off.

The onslaught continued on Ancient, where Natus Vincere again came out guns blazing. They shut down any chances of FUT stringing rounds together as they raced to an 8-4 lead, with Valeriy "⁠b1t⁠" Vakhovskiy turning heads with a fantastic triple-kill spraydown.

The tables turned in the second half as FUT took the game by the scruff of the neck. NAVI had plenty of opportunities to put the series to rest but lost a barrage of man advantages, including two 4v2 situations, with Nikita "⁠cmtry⁠" Samolotov closing the map out with a 1v2 clutch.

The Ukrainian AWPer picked up where he had left off, delivering a 4K in the pistol round to put FUT in the lead. The young squad was in control of proceedings until a spectacular ace clutch from makazze powered Natus Vincere to a run of four rounds as the half ended in a 6-6 stalemate.

NAVI carried that momentum into the second half and kept a cool head after losing the conversion to a highlight-of-the-year contender from Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija, who pulled off a magnificent Scout ace. FUT had no gas left in the tank to mount a comeback, with iM providing the finishing blow with a quad-kill in the last round.
