Vitality stifle FURIA to book Rio grand final spot

Vitality have secured a place in the grand final of IEM Rio, besting crowd favorites FURIA in a 2-0 affair with wins on Overpass (13-10) and Ancient (13-10).

The veto for this series took an unorthodox direction, as Vitality and FURIA both normally keep Ancient as their permaban, but both sides allowed it through the first set of bans, giving FURIA the opportunity to select it as a punish pick. This is not the first time that FURIA have pulled off this gambit, having previously bested Vitality 13-11 on Ancient in the grand final of IEM Chengdu en route to their trophy hoist there.

With this victory, Vitality have now put themselves within touching distance of their second ESL Grand Slam. Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's men clinched their first set of gold bars in April of last year, and now, after wins at IEM Dallas, ESL Pro League Season 22, and IEM Krakow, Vitality need just one more series victory to be the first organization in Counter-Strike history to earn two Grand Slams, and back-to-back at that.

Vitality set their sights on making history in Rio

The final hurdle standing in Vitality's way of a second Grand Slam is Spirit, who have marched all the way to the grand final of IEM Rio despite missing coach Sergey "⁠hally⁠" Shavaev for this outing. This all-important series will serve as the first meeting between the two teams in 2026, having last clashed in the semi-finals of the StarLadder Budapest Major. Elsewhere, FURIA will still have another opportunity to score a win on stage in the third-place decider series against Falcons.

The second semi-final of the day got underway on Vitality's map pick of Overpass, though it was FURIA who came out with a strong early start. B-site duo Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato and Yuri "⁠yuurih⁠" Santos helped spearhead a solid 6-0 lead for FURIA, but Vitality composed themselves in the latter portion of the half thanks to the efforts of Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool and Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut to end the half down only 5-7.

With a pistol-round win under their belts, Vitality hit the ground running on the defense. Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan, who went the entire first half without a kill, found his footing with a triple-kill flank that got Vitality in the lead for the first time on Overpass. apEX's men marched on to match point, and although FURIA were able to convert on two man-disadvantage situations, Vitality still managed to close out their pick 13-10.

FURIA's pick of Ancient was a hectic affair; Vitality kicked things off on the CT side, picking up the opening pistol round, but the two sides took turns trading streaks of rounds throughout the first half. flameZ recovered from his weak start on Overpass with a solid showing holding down Middle on the defense, helping to keep things tied 6-6 at the side swap.

Vitality bagged another pistol round to kick off their T-side, but struggled to build an early lead, with FURIA stringing together three rounds in a row to reclaim the lead. Control of the map swung back into Vitality's favor after a 5v3 fumble from FURIA and an impressive showing from ZywOo. Vitality's momentum was briefly stopped by a 1v2 from yuurih, but it was not enough to prevent Vitality from locking down the map 13-10 and the series 2-0.
