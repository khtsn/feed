EliGE: "It always felt like whatever direction that we went, we weren't fully going into it"

Liquid's sorry season suffered a sorry end at the Cologne Major, as they failed to make it out of Stage 1.

It was a particularly tough term for Jonathan "⁠EliGE⁠" Jablonowski, who has fallen to a 1.04 rating so far this year after a 1.16 in 2024 and a 1.10 in 2025.

"Even since joining Liquid," EliGE explains, "I've had a lot of self-confidence issues, having a lot more doubts in myself, and kind of building that back. While also not trying to overstep, because there was a lot of different voices, a lot of different ideas of how we wanted to approach the game on this team."

That difficulty in establishing one vision of the game has consistently held Liquid back according to their 28-year-old opener. Time was "wasted" as the team changed direction time after time.

FlyQuest stamp out Liquid to qualify for Stage 2

But EliGE points to a different issue when it came to today's bout with FlyQuest, and it wasn't letting Anubis through the veto: "I feel like this was more of the pressure and nerves getting to us a little bit, and not trusting in the things that we have been doing."

Whatever the problem, Liquid go into the summer break 37th in VRS, and needing major surgery to take the organization back to its previously lofty heights.

Listen to the interview here.

Commiserations. What's your immediate reaction to going out at this stage?

Just super disappointed. I think that we had what it takes, and we've been putting in a lot of work, and not having that show anything, like not having any of that work amount to a good result, obviously feels bad. Like I said before the event, Stage 2 would have been okay, Stage 3 would have been good for us, and Stage 1 is obviously bad. Overall, really disappointed.

If a core has played Overpass, then it's hard to float that

I think it's come up a few times with a lot of different players, in terms of the practice to officials difference. Obviously, it's hard right now, but what do you think that is down to?

I'd say this particular BO3 was a little bit different than what we've said throughout the season, or last season even. Typically, we play really well in practice, and everything is flowing and things like that. But I think that this series, it is a 2-2 game. We haven't been having a great season, and it just felt like there were a lot of nerves, looking for more of the easy answers of how to get the round wins on Anubis.

In the second one, after we lost both pistols, unfortunately, it ended up being 11-4. It's just hard to come back from that. We were put into a tough spot, after we lost the first map so hard, and we lost both pistols, and we couldn't really get anything going. We weren't able to string any rounds together, so I feel like this was more of the pressure and nerves getting to us a little bit, and not trusting in the things that we have been doing.

Was there a chance you could have floated Overpass? It just seemed like Anubis was such a strong map for them, and it just deflated you guys in a way, visibly.

Well, it's a good question for sure. I wasn't really part of the veto process, but it is a risk. I remember I was looking at it, and they have an Overpass in the last three months. At least the way that I look at things... this might be a little bit too much for the future, but whatever.

But if a core has played Overpass, let's just say that as a core, like Liquid, we have never played Overpass, but them as a core, maybe they've played as a core on Overpass, then it's hard to float that. Because they might be more willing, and people will also get more desperate, especially 2-2 at the Major, maybe some crazy things will happen.

But it's not like we didn't feel good about our Anubis. I mean, we played well against good teams, like tier-one teams in scrims coming into this. But as I said, when we get into the game, and things are getting hard, I think that we just had nerves kind of take over for us in this.

We also kind of expected Ancient to be played, but they ended up getting Anubis and Ancient for it. But we were confident in most of our map pool coming into this, so it's not like them picking Anubis made everyone feel like we didn't have a good chance. We all felt like whatever the map pool was going to be, whether it was going to be Anubis, Ancient, whichever one first, we still felt like we could win.

Even since joining Liquid, where I've had a lot of self-confidence issues

And on the season overall, I mean, it's obviously hard when you have a different kind of win-loss to normal, but what do you think has been tough for you individually this season?

I think it's been kind of a roller coaster, even since joining Liquid, where I've had a lot of self-confidence issues, I'd say, having a lot more doubts in myself and kind of building that back while also not trying to overstep. Because there was a lot of different voices, a lot of different ideas of how we wanted to approach the game on this team ever since I joined, essentially. And we went in multiple different directions. I kind of just wanted [laughs] everyone to be happy.

So whatever direction we went, I just wanted to just do that, but it always felt like whatever direction that we went, we weren't fully going into it. So it kind of felt like there was a lot of wasted time in that regard. Like I said, even since the beginning, where we were half doing this, half doing that, trying to make this kind of work with that and this kind of working with that thing.

So I think it was just overall very split. Even in the last month, we fully went another direction, which is fine. But I think that we just split our direction a little bit too much. And that's why our identity as a team has been changing a lot. And that's also a little bit hard for me because I always feel the most comfortable when we're all kind of knowing what we're doing with the structure, everyone's doing what we want. And when we're changing so many things, so often structure-wise, from the top, it's a little bit more difficult for me.

And pile that on with, like I said, with the self-confidence, even coming into it, it was hard to get my footing. But fortunately for me, I feel better on an individual level in the past month and a half. I feel super good about myself mechanically and what I can do. But it's just been hard to show that consistently.

And just the last question, because it comes back to the team always, right, and all those voices and everything. Is that fixable without a roster change? Is that where this is leading?

Well, like I said, I think it's just picking a direction and sticking to it because I think that many things can work. I've been on many, many different rosters and lineups where we played differently, even going from FaZe to here.

In the very beginning, it was very different, but I think anything can work as long as everyone believes in it and we do it. But if there's doubt and we're not fully doing it... I think that in the last month, we were fully in a certain direction, and I think that was good for us. And that's why during this stage, any interviews that we've had, we all felt good about what we're doing, because we're all doing something together and we know it's going to be like this.

But, you know, when you do that, you don't have as many reps as the other teams, maybe our playbook is going to be more limited. So there's always going to be downsides to it. But, you know, at least I felt good that we were all going in a certain direction together, even if it wasn't the best for everybody.

So I think that to answer your question, I don't know, that's not really up for me to decide [laughs]. But, you know, I'm happy that we're going in a direction together because I think that when we split off as many times as we did, it honestly might have been like four or five times, and it's just hard to be a top consistent team like that.
