FL4MUS on BetBoom's roster if they qualify for the Major: "We didn't discuss who will play"

BetBoom are in the midst of a campaign to qualify for the IEM Cologne Major despite playing a gauntlet of LANs with Timur "⁠FL4MUS⁠" Marev and Aleksandr "⁠zorte⁠" Zagodyrenko as stand-ins ahead of the April 6 ranking cut-off.

Visa issues affecting Daniil "⁠d1Ledez⁠" Kustov and Artem "⁠ArtFr0st⁠" Kharitonov sowed doubt into the team's Major hopes, but they have surged into contention after winning Roman Imperium Cup VII, placing third at BC Game Masters Championship, and starting Stake Ranked Episode 1 with a win over HEROIC.

It is an immense turn of fate for FL4MUS, who found himself benched from OG — who are well outside of Major invite range — in early March.

"It's a big chance to prove that I can play in a lot of positions because almost all of the positions I'm playing are not mine," he told HLTV after the win in Barcelona. "It's also very good practice for me to see the game from different perspectives because I'm playing on different sides of the map, and I'm focusing on getting better from game to game."

But uncertainty still looms surrounding who BetBoom will field at the Major proper, as its qualified lineup will include both stand-ins.

"Either me or zorte will play at the Major because you can have only one stand-in," FL4MUS said. "We didn't discuss that, and to be honest, I don't really care because I'm not playing for the organization. It's their decision."

You opened against Heroic and got a big win coming here straight from playing in Portugal. What has it been like playing these tournaments with BetBoom and running around helping the team get these VRS points?

I'm feeling great with the team. We started off a bit slowly during the first tournament we played in Romania, where we took third place. I knew it was a great start, but in Portugal it was kind of hard because instantly after the flight, the next day, we started to play again, and we started badly.

We won one game, then we lost two, and it ended up that we got into the playoffs. We beat Wildcard by one round, and that was the reason we got into the playoffs and won a tournament. It was a pretty big win for us in terms of confidence.

So we flew here again, and three players on our team are sick. We flew yesterday because the day before we were winning the tournament, but overall it feels pretty good. I'm happy to play again at a good tournament against good teams and show good results. I'm happy to be here.

It has been a bit of a rough ride going back to Virtus.pro. There was that period when you were deconstructing your game and relearning how to play, and the team struggled, and you were benched pretty quickly after joining them. What do you make of that period in VP?

I've been fixing some personal problems, some with health, others even like my mentality... I was trying to get back my fire, if you know what I mean. It's not about my personal fire, but my fire in the game. My instincts. I've been trying to get them back. I've been watching lots of demos, playing a lot of DM, and I've been working a lot.

Do you think that playing in the system in VP took away that fire?

Not really. I don't think so. Overall, it was a bad time. I couldn't play with my fire since I didn't have the positions. Same with BetBoom, on CT I have a much worse rating because I'm playing on d1Ledez's positions, which I don't really like. I'm just doing my role.

It was the same in Virtus.pro, but there was lots of pressure, and that made some things more complicated. You couldn't go on your phone because lots of bettors and other people were just hating on you and stuff, and everybody wants results. I want results, and I'm putting extra pressure on myself because I want to be good, I want to play good, I want to have good statistics.

Now I think I just chilled. I understand everything that has been happening, and I'm very thankful for that time that happened because every experience is experience. It doesn't matter if it's a bad one or a good one. It made me in some way better, and I'm thankful for that, for the organizations that I have been playing with before, and the players I've been playing with before.

You had a brief moment with OG, which was also a flash in the pan. What happened there? Why was it so quick? What went wrong?

I just didn't sync up with the players and the coach. We had a different vision of how the game works. I cannot say anything bad about the team, it's just that the way I was playing didn't match up with the way OG want to play, and that was the main reason I was removed from the active roster.

I wish them all the best and hope they achieve great things because the players are amazing, and the coaching staff, the organization, everything was pretty good, and I wish them nothing but the best.

It's a very great opportunity for me to get better and also to show myself to the world

You've been standing in with BetBoom long enough that if the team does make the Major, you'll be on the roster for the Major. Have you spoken about who would play or not play if you do qualify for the Major?

We didn't discuss it, but either me or zorte will play at the Major because you can have only one stand-in. We didn't discuss that, and to be honest, I don't really care because I'm not playing for the organization. It's their decision. I'd be happy to play at the Major, but we'll see what happens, and I'll try my best to help them qualify for the Major.

If I have the chance, I'll be very, very happy, but we didn't discuss it. There was no time for it; we have games every day, tournament after tournament. We need the VRS points to get to the Major...

With all of these games, and all of this travel, I'm sure you do get tired, but there's also the excitement of the VRS race, getting the points, and as a stand-in, getting this opportunity to play. What are you making of it, and how is it for you personally to get to be in this?

I'm happy that I got a chance after I moved out of OG. For me, it's a big chance to prove that I can play in a lot of positions because almost all of the positions I'm playing are not mine. It's also very good practice for me to see the game from different perspectives because I'm playing on different sides of the map, and I'm focusing on getting better from game to game. It's a very great opportunity for me to get better and also to show myself to the world.
