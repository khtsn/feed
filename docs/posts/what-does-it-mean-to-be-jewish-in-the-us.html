Wordle 653 5/6

⬜⬜⬜🟨⬜ STYLE

This is my second ever Wordle. Until this day, I have ignored the green and yellow squares on my Twitter timelines and group chats. But earlier today, after I completed Wordle number one, I thought I’d found a new daily hobby.

Then I did Wordle number two — this Wordle — and it zapped my enthusiasm completely. What is a FLORA? I was sure I had it when I submitted FLOOR, and the annoyance of that not being right, much less the word being FLORA — I imagine veteran Wordle players are familiar with this particular type of frustration. It pained me.

A part of me wants to go all in — Googling strategies about initial words and the likelihood of certain consonants — just to make sure this never happens again. But there’s another part that wants to give up, retiring from Wordle before the frustration sucks me in.

I also have an admission: I Googled “five letter words that start with FLOR.”

I’m not familiar with the rules. Is that allowed?

Today’s word is FLORA. According to Webster’s New World College Dictionary, it’s a noun referring to the defining plant life of a specific era or region.
