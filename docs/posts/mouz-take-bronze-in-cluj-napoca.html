MOUZ take bronze in Cluj-Napoca

Last year's champions secured a top-three finish over The MongolZ in this year's stacked tournament.

MOUZ beat The MongolZ 2-0 in the PGL Cluj-Napoca third-place decider, securing the lowest rung on the podium ahead of the grand final pitting PARIVISION and Vitality.

The series kicked off on The MongolZ's Mirage pick, where the AWPers made plenty of noise with a 1.38 rating for Usukhbayar "⁠910⁠" Banzragch and a 1.33 for Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás. But it was Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin, with a 1.44 rating and +6.48% swing, who made the difference. The Swedish in-game leader had seven multi-kill rounds, including a 1v2 clutch that kept his team in the driver's seat to take a 7-5 lead at the half.

MOUZ then excelled on the T side, taking their two-round lead and extending it to 11-5 before The MongolZ could get a successful defense in. That would be it, though, as MOUZ ran away to a confident 13-6 victory to tip the series balance in their favor ahead of their map pick.

Brollan also came guns blazing on Inferno, winning a 1v3 clutch after an Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold triple to keep his MOUZ ahead 5-3 on the attack. The first half ended up going 8-4 to MOUZ, but ended up being a more competitive map than Mirage.

After the switch, The MongolZ kicked into gear, winning five rounds in a row to take the lead, 9-8, before back-and-forth rounds made it 10-10. In the end, though, it was MOUZ who ran out ahead, breaking the Mongolian squad's economy and hitting match point, 12-10.

The MongolZ had a chance to keep it going with a 3v4 afterplant situation on the A site, but a strong retake by MOUZ ending in a last-second defuse ended it then and there.

Now that the third and fourth places have been sorted, a new champion is ready to be crowned in Cluj-Napoca when Vitality and PARIVISION go head-to-head in the best-of-five grand final.
