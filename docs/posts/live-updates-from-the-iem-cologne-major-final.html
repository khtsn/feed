FalleN seeks to make history with third Cologne victory

vs.

Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo is after his third title in the Cathedral of Counter-Strike, a feat nobody else has achieved. It is also where he won his second and last Major nearly a decade ago, at ESL One Cologne 2016.

If the Brazilian veteran were to win today, it would make the time between winning Major championships the longest in history, at 9 years and 346 days.

"I feel like we have what it takes to win here," FalleN told HLTV before the playoffs kicked off. "It's not going to be easy. We're going to need a lot of things, including luck in our favor, but we can do this."

Could the final break viewership records?

vs.

The IEM Cologne semi-final between Falcons and Spirit hit 2,200,426 peak viewers, according to Esports Charts, making it the second-highest peak in the history of the game surpassing the 2,113,610 peak during the PGL Antwerp quarter-finals.

The current peak viewership record resides with the PGL Stockholm Major grand final, in which Oleksandr "⁠s1mple⁠" Kostyliev and Natus Vincere beat Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač and G2 with a peak viewership of 2,748,434 viewers.

Can NiKo end his unenviable streak at the Majors?

vs.

Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač is the unenviable co-holder of a record for most Majors played without a title, having attended 17 of them without lifting silverware. The other is Jonathan "⁠EliGE⁠" Jablonowski.

Today, NiKo has the chance to curb that statistic at 16 Major attendances without a victory and make EliGE the sole holder, or keep the race with the American rifler alive.

NiKo's Major lowlights over the years include Cloud9's comeback on Inferno against FaZe at ELEAGUE Boston in 2018, alongside Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen, and the missed Desert Eagle shots on Nuke at PGL Stockholm in 2021 that could have helped shift the final in his G2's favor by forcing a third map that never came.

The elders will break a record no matter who wins

vs.

Regardless of who wins the grand final today, we will have a new oldest Major champion, with Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo vying for it on one side and Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen on the other.

karrigan had held the title at one point, when he won PGL Antwerp at 32 years and 38 days of age. At that time, he dethroned Danylo "⁠Zeus⁠" Teslenko, who lifted the PGL Major Kraków 2017 trophy at 29 years and 289 days old.

The current holder is Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire, who became the oldest Major winner at BLAST.tv Austin at 32 years and 121 days old and then beat his own record at StarLadder Budapest at 32 years and 293 days.

If karrigan were to break the record now, he would do it at 36 years and 68 days old, while FalleN would do it at 35 years and 22 days.
