RRQ move past GE in the Pacific LCQ

RRQ showed promise towards the end of the regular season while GE comes off of the recent departure and retirement of WRONSKI .

Despite a strong start from RRQ on Global's map pick of Split, a resurgence in form from Monyet gave his team five. On the other hand, RRQ's slight lead was powered by frequent multikills from Lmemore and xffero .

After a successful force buy from Global following a pistol round win, RRQ held control of the server with multiple retake victories. Four rounds in a row towards the tail end of the map gave RRQ a 12-7 lead that proved insurmountable for Global. RRQ prevented a GE comeback from solidifying and took the first map 13-10. The Philippine duelist duo of EJAY and Emman excelled for RRQ with both players ending the map with greater than 150 ADR.

The second map of Ascent brought with it a surprising Phoenix pick from t3xture and 2ge subbing in for Emman. GE initially appeared to be turning things around from their loss on Split, however, they frequently struggled to utilize Phoenix effectively with just a single ultimate coming out in the first half. GE's individuals were their saving grace as both Monyet and t3xture provided hero plays to keep the score even coming into halftime.

In the second half, RRQ kept composure and control of the game after conceding the pistol. With seven of the final nine rounds, RRQ took Ascent 13-10 and the series 2-0. Yet again RRQ's Philippine duo, this time with 2ge in place of Emman, were by far the stars for their team. As the only two players on their team with greater than 1.00 ratings despite the map win, the two players put their LCQ fates in their own hands.
