Official: BCG complete roster with mzinho

BC.Game has announced the signing of Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold to complete its roster, confirming the move after it was reported by HLTV on Wednesday.

Sources: BC.Game set sights on mzinho

The 19-year-old rifler is the final piece of the organization's rebuild after it made Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold's loan from The MongolZ permanent and signed Emil "⁠Magisk⁠" Reif as its new in-game leader.

The move reunites mzinho with his "lifetime friend" Senzu after the latter was benched by The MongolZ in a shock move in October 2025. Speaking on BC.Game CEO Ali Muhanned's stream, mzinho said Senzu "did a lot of convincing" to get him to join the team, and confirmed he would move to Europe to play with the team.

It will be mzinho's first international excursion after spending his career playing in domestic teams and experiencing a breakout with The MongolZ in 2024. The Mongolian rifler played at five Majors during his three-year tenure, notably finishing as runners-up at BLAST.tv Austin Major and winning a historic first Big Event trophy for Mongolia at Esports World Cup 2025.

His departure from The MongolZ comes as a big blow to the Mongolian roster, who had been struggling for results since losing Senzu and will have limited options to pick from in Mongolia.

BC.Game now have a long way to go to achieve Muhanned's wish of breaking into the top five in the world by the end of the year with its new roster, as the team will start fresh on the VRS with a new core.
