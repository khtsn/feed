The EVPs and All-Stars of IEM Krakow 2026

A few years ago, IEM Winter (held in Katowice) was supposed to be Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut's bogey event, spawning memes about air quality and group stage exits in 2019, 2020, 2021 (online), 2022, and 2024.

But that narrative has been thoroughly shattered now with back-to-back MVPs for the Frenchman in Poland. 2026, in new location Krakow, even saw him break his personal Big Event rating record with a 1.59 beating out his previous 1.59-rated event at Pro League in the decimals.

Fittingly, he has a full stats radar (meaning he was in the top 5% of players in every metric) to go with that record. It was difficult to choose which maps would even make it onto his highlight section; he posted 53 frags against Aurora in groups, had a 1.66 rating in the grand final, 1.68 in the semi-final, and 58% of his maps were rated above 1.60.

Having broken the record with a fourth Player of the Year title in 2025, it's a terrifying way for ZywOo to begin 2026.

Radar Chart stat glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • RW Δ: kill differential per round win

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets

donk's quest to become the first player to earn a semi-final MVP is still underway because of ZywOo's greatness, but his 1.43 rating in the tournament proper (excluding the third place decider) makes this a very strong EVP indeed.

As usual, he was extraordinary in round wins (1.23 KPRW, though ZywOo's 1.26 puts him in second here for once) and map wins leading Spirit to semis (1.57 rating), while he finished the semi-final defeat with 108 ADR and a 1.43 rating.

Myroslav "⁠zont1x⁠" Plakhotia admitted in Malta that Spirit's roster changes over the last nine months have been made with the express intention of winning trophies to keep their superstar happy, and the pressure will only increase with each remarkable donk event rating.

Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato

KSCERATO fell quiet in the semi and grand final, but he was FURIA's leading man in their run to the top four and finished with a 1.43 rating in map wins (1.27 including the entire final).

In the run through The MongolZ, Natus Vincere, Astralis, and Aurora, KSCERATO did not drop below a 1.37 rating in map wins and that contribution has been rewarded with the second EVP.

The 1.80-rated demolition of Aurora in the arena was particularly impressive, and the full picture of eight huge performances that pushed FURIA forward tick a lot of boxes when it comes to EVP criteria.

After missing out on any super-elite EVPs in 2025, it's a strong start to 2026 from Brazil's finest player.

Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan

flameZ slots into third place after being Vitality's second star in Poland with a 1.23 rating overall and 1.24 in the grand final.

Whilst he only had one huge peak against top opposition (the 1.59 rating on Mirage against FURIA, which Vitality lost 11-13), his consistency was fantastic (83% of maps above 1.00 rating) and his KPRW (0.99) also proves the impact of his output.

Given ZywOo's dominance, it was sometimes hard for the entry fragger to stand out, and his 1.20 rating in map wins is the second-lowest of our EVPs. But his high floor, solid output (80 ADR), and strong final meant his award was never in doubt.

Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko

Alongside KSCERATO, molodoy led the charge for FURIA into the grand final with a 1.33 rating in the map wins that led them there.

His semi-final, where he posted 54 frags in two overtime maps against Spirit (1.44 rating on Mirage, 1.96 on Nuke), was the pièce de résistance that confirmed his EVP, a monumental peak coming at a time his team needed it.

Before he struggled in the final, he had a 1.16 rating, which shows just how poor the final was (44-64 K-D, 0.75 rating) but also how valuable he had been up to that point. Given that EVPs are always about how far your individual excellence pushes your team forward, a poor grand final was never going to take that away.

Dmitry "⁠sh1ro⁠" Sokolov

sh1ro has the opposite story to molodoy, where his raw statistics end up looking fantastic but he remains below because of where his impact arrived.

After the semi-final (which molodoy dominated over sh1ro directly), Spirit's sniper was at a 1.19 rating, just 0.03 above a molodoy who had performed better in playoffs. sh1ro then boosted his rating to 1.28 in the third-place decider, which are not treated as playoff games by HLTV given the lack of stakes.

He was also not as important as a 1.28 rating implies when it came to pushing Spirit forward, as his 1.15 rating in map wins leading to the semi-final indicates. His best map before the third-place match was even in a 13-8 loss to G2 in the group stage.

sh1ro's statistical excellence (1.08 KPRW, 0.85 KPR), and dominance of a top-five team in MOUZ in that third-place decider (1.88 rating), still push him over the edge for an EVP but that is why he remains fairly low on the list.

Lotan "⁠Spinx⁠" Giladi

We have spoken a lot about pushing your team forward in this article (and every EVP article), and that key criterion is why Spinx (1.32 rating in map wins) did enough for an EVP.

In MOUZ's eight big map wins, he was above a 1.15 rating in all, and he confirmed the EVP with a strong 1.43-rated quarter-final against G2 in the arena.

His impact was also clear in razor-tight 16-14 and 19-17 wins over Falcons to decide who even made it to playoffs, topping his team's side of the scoreboard with a 1.18 rating and +1.78% swing.

Like molodoy's poor final, then, Spinx's disappearance in the semi-final and third-place decider could not undo the work he had done beforehand to ensure MOUZ were even in those matches.

Honorable mentions

Four-time 2025 super-elite EVP Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool just misses out this time, after posting just 0.82 KPRW and taking more of a back-seat in Vitality's run (1.14 in map wins) in the groups (0.87 KPRW) and grand final (0.87 rating), with a strong semi-final impressive but the exception to the rule.

Mareks "⁠YEKINDAR⁠" Gaļinskis's strong playoff performances were key to his Top 20 placing in 2025, and he was en route for something similar here with a big map in each of the quarter, semi, and grand final. But he nearly cost his team against Astralis in the 7-8th place match, and his overall playoff rating (1.09) was not enough for an EVP given two of his teammates did more in the run to the final.

No super-elite EVP this time for ropz

Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás was just behind Spinx in MOUZ's run, but was less consistent before the semi-final and just as bad in the final two matches of their campaign.

Matúš "⁠MATYS⁠" Šimko was the only G2 player above 1.00 in the quarter-final, but credit for their run had to be split between the entire team which meant he was not too close to an EVP.

Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici recovered from a disastrous debut at Bounty to be Aurora's most valuable player in Krakow, standing out in groups during a run where the team played the four best teams in the world.

Maxim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin came close to a Cologne-style group stage EVP, but this time around there were holes in his case (like a 0.39 rating against Aurora) and he was less dominant within his team, too. Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov also posted a rating above 1.20, but got plenty of that from the series against NRG and also misses out on an EVP.

Further down the list, Filip "⁠Graviti⁠" Brankovic's 1.30 rating deserves a mention, but he struggled to maintain his form from the FaZe game in the two following series.

All-Stars

ZywOo and donk take their usual places in the all-star lineup, with Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire at the helm.

KSCERATO beat out Spinx and ropz to be the side's closer, while the interesting discussion came in choosing our anchor.

It was a quiet event for finalists William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman and Yuri "⁠yuurih⁠" Santos, making it a shoot-out between zont1x, Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko, and soulfly.

In the end, soulfly's 1.19 rating during Aurora's incredibly tough run against three top five teams through groups meant he got the nod with none of that trio having much impact in playoffs.
