Congratulations, you're through to the playoffs. A pretty convincing win in the end, so tell me how you're feeling right now since I'm sure it's nice to be confirmed to be going through heading into the last day of groups.

Thank you. I'm feeling really good, and also as you said, it was a confident win and I think we needed that as a team. Especially after the results lately, so it gives us a lot going into the playoffs, for sure. Also, with the Dust2 win, it was really convincing and we finally showed how we can play on that [map]. Yeah, I'm just happy overall and looking forward for the arena here.

It looked a lot better than yesterday, which almost ended up in a loss to Legacy where you were down 6-10 on map two before you came back. What did you do between yesterday and today to reset in this kind of fashion?

We didn't do too much to honest. Yesterday, I would say we were just really slow at painting the picture for our teammates and for ourselves because we didn't play officials for three weeks or something, and we were a little bit... out of shape. Not necessarily out of shape in-game, but just like, it's an official, the temperature, how you go into the game, i was just not there for us, and it cost us the game on Dust2. As the game [went on] versus Legacy, we built into it, and after Inferno CT side we played really good and on Mirage we kept it up to close the game down. Today, we continued that, and it showed.

Talking about mistakes is definitely needed [...] but at the same time I think we overfocused on it and it got into our heads

As you said earlier, it's been a tough period of results for you, and sycrone after Rotterdam said it was a bit of a 'wake up call.' What was the talk like after Rotterdam, did you feel like it was a wake up call for all of you as well?

Yeah, definitely. We talked a lot in the last year about where we can improve, try to chase Vitality, and try to chase the trophies, and I think maybe in the last couple of months we got into a bad spiral where we were always overanalyzing stuff. Talking about mistakes and where we can improve all the time, which is definitely needed in a team where you want to achieve greatness, but at the same time I think we overfocused on it and it got into our heads. We didn't really highlight what we are good at, where we do good stuff, we always picked at the really small things, and it went into a lot of spiraling. It was not doing good for us and we needed a little bit of a reset, so we are trying to take more responsibility, not focus on too many details at this point, and just look at the bigger picture and things like that.

" I think it was just a funny rumor with Magisk, it's not true at all," torzsi says

He also said that you were lacking energy. Is that part of the same problem?

Yes, I think it came with the spiral. As I said, when you are focused on too many [things], too many mistakes, I think it's difficult to be energetic and positive all the time, and I think it came with that a lot. That's a part where we are focusing at the moment, yes.

This was a bit of a break for you to reset, and as you said, you've worked on trying to iron out some of these ways that you're approaching the game. As part of that, obviously MOUZ have been hunting for a title for a while, so do you think that this break and this reset is what you need to actually get over that hump? Or is there something more you still need?

It's a good question. I wouldn't say that... I mean, of course we are here for the trophy and we want to win, but maybe at this moment we are not there. We know that lately, as we highlighted, we were not the best and we had some bad results, and we need to build it back again. We just want to take it step by step and, as I said, focus on the good things that we are good at and build it up for Cologne. Of course we want to win and we are here to lift the trophy, but at the same time, we are also aware that maybe we are not in our best shape at the moment and maybe we need some time to rebuild the stuff we had and get into the best shape of us. But definitely, this win versus Aurora gives us good confidence.

Last time we played against FURIA, and we kinda smashed them in the playoffs, so I'm not sure if we will be welcomed in this arena

Whenever teams have these kind of periods, the rumor mill is always churning, stuff starts getting said like with this Aurora-molodoy thing that came out, and then obviously karrigan to Falcons thing. But there was also something about your team, about Magisk potentially coming in, so is there anything you can comment about that? Is there anything actually to it?

(smiles) I don't think there is anything to comment about (laughs). I think it was just a funny rumor with Magisk, it's not true at all (laughs). It's nothing interesting.

Last time you were in Rio you ended as the runner-up, so talk to me about going into the arena and how you hope to make a difference this time around.

I think last time we played against FURIA and we kinda smashed them in the playoffs, so I'm not sure if we will be welcomed in this arena, you know? (laughs) But I'm really looking forward to it because playing in front of the Brazilian crowd is amazing, I think they're really passionate and... yeah, they like to be loud and aggressive, they cheer a lot, and we are looking forward to being in the playoffs again because we missed it last time [in Rotterdam]. We are just excited.

Before you go there, you're going to play FURIA in the upper bracket final seeding match first. So what are you hoping for in that game? If you beat them there too and then you go into the arena, the crowd is going to be even more against you than before, right?

Yeah, definitely. I think we will have a good game versus FURIA. We played some practices versus each other and it was really good practice. Both teams are really competitive and they are also in really good shape, so it will be a challenge for us at the moment. But as I said, this win gave us a lot of confidence going into tomorrow, and we will see where we are. It's just a bonus game to make it to the semi-finals.
