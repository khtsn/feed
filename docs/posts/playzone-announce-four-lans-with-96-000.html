PLAYzone announce four LANs with ~$96,000

The Czech operator will run two open events and two VRS events in Prague and Brno.

fnatic won the Conquest of Prague in 2025

PLAYzone has announced its roadmap for 2026, with four tournaments and nearly $100,000 in prizes to be given out. The Czech tournament organizer has its eyes set on becoming a staple of the tier-two circuit with two VRS LANs and two open cups.

The first LAN will be Tipsport Conquest of Prague, which PLAYzone already ran in 2025 with a grand final featuring fnatic and ENCE. This year, it will start with four online qualifiers, including one for a local team, before it moves to a LAN final with 12 teams invited via VRS. It will take place at the Vodafone PLAYZone Arena in Prague on May 22-24 with a ~$35,000 prize pool.

Two buy-in open cups for 12 teams from around the world will follow, also taking place at the Vodafone PLAYzone Arena on August 5-9 and September 23-27. Each tournament will feature a ~$13,000 prize pool. Eight teams will start in the group stage, while the four highest-ranked according to the VRS will start straight from the playoffs.

For all of the fans out there clamoring for IEM Brno, PLAYzone is offering the next best thing, Tipsport RUSH: Brno, an event like the aforementioned Conquest of Prague, but taking place at the BVV Brno exhibition center during the Czech Championship Gaming Festival on November 20-22. It will have 12 teams invited via the VRS ranking and four joining from online qualifiers, and will once again feature a ~$35,000 prize pool.
