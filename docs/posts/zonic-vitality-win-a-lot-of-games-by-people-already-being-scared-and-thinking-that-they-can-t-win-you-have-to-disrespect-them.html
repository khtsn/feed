zonic: "Vitality win a lot of games by people already being scared and thinking that they can't win. You have to disrespect them"

The Danish skipper spoke about beating Vitality and regulating how many PUGs his players play.

Falcons had a huge moment on Wednesday, after already qualifying for the IEM Rio playoffs on Tuesday, when they beat Vitality to break up an 18-game winning streak that the Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire-led squad had going into their fourth tournament in a row.

"You have to disrespect them, take chances, take risks, and go for fights," Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen says. "Even though it's ZywOo around the corner, you have to just swing him and get the trades up."

Falcons' skipper also spoke a bit more in-depth about the FACEIT ban instilled in the team, which works to both keep players hungry by not overplaying, as well as keeping them from picking up too many bad habits outside of the team environment.

Congratulations on a big win over Vitality. You seem to be one of the only teams that can beat them right now, so talk me through that game. It got really tight on Nuke, and m0NESY had an amazing series.

Obviously, they are the best team in the world for a reason. You know, it's funny when you look at the veto and how it's going to go down, you always go on HLTV, and you check their map stats, there are like five maps that are like 100% [winrate]. It reminds me a little bit of 2018 [Astralis].

[It was] a tough game, of course, you have to play your A-game to beat them. I think it was only on Dust2 where I felt that we really dropped, but overall, we played really solid on the first map and especially on Nuke. But it's just not easy, you know? Even though we got a really strong start and got a good half on Nuke, they started to come back and... yeah. It was a good game, and hopefully also a good game for the spectators.

You were 4-10 down on Mirage, and you came back with nine rounds in a row. Talk to me about that, it seemed like you just couldn't lose in that run.

Throughout this tournament, we have been really good communication-wise, making decisions, playing after-plants and retakes — playing them properly. On T side Mirage, it felt like we went a little bit back. People were not... we were respecting them too much, and against a team like that, I think they win a lot of games by people already being scared and thinking that they can't win the game. If you do that against Vitality, you're dead.

You have to disrespect them, take chances, take risks, and go for fights. Even though it's ZywOo around the corner, you have to just swing him and get the trades up. I felt that was something that I addressed at least in several of my talks with the players, both after the first map and also after the second, and in some of my timeouts, as well, that we have to dare to play.

We were a little bit scared in some of these situations, a bit too hesitant, and it was good to see that our CT sides prevailed today, maybe apart from Dust2. But we got off to a rough start, and then towards the end it was also a bit rough, but overall the CT sides were really good today. I'm proud of the guys.

When Nuke was coming down to the wire, you had this 5v3 and 4v2 you lost. Were you getting flashbacks a little at any point?

Yeah (laughs). Do you mean the round where NiKo and kyxsan died in the Window, the one we lost?

No, the one on A where it was [ropz] and mezii.

Yes, I mean, of course. It's something that we have been working on a lot in these situations. Advantage situations, being a bit after them, trying to work on it. Also, outside of practice, talk about different scenarios, and... yeah.

Overall, it's getting better and better and, of course, against Vitality, if you just miss that a little bit... I'm not expecting us to be perfect, but it's better, and this I'm proud of. But as long as we keep this trajectory and know that against a team like Vitality, if you just give them a little bit, a guy like ZywOo will take everything.

I know you obviously can't talk about the karrigan report or anything, but does it help in a way that the team might be feeling less pressure from that at this event?

I think we have the same pressure. Rumors are rumors, and I'm just here to focus with these five amazing players at this tournament and hopefully see that the hard work that we also do behind the scenes is paying off. We showcased that against the best team in the world now, and now the next thing is about the playoffs, which has also been a topic for us. We'll see how we go there.

NiKo told me that you really wanted to play Vitality because you hadn't really gotten a proper chance [recently]. You played them with NucleonZ as a stand-in at Bounty, but the last time [at full strength] was at Rivals last year. So playing them now and getting this result, what does it mean for you?

Hopefully, it gives you a lot of confidence, and knowing that we know we can do it. Some of the teams that we have lost to previously are good teams, teams that you shouldn't underestimate, but also teams that we see ourselves beating on a good day.

Maybe the exception is Vitality, who are the best team, so here we definitely need to play our A-game, but it gives you confidence knowing that you are one of the only teams, at least, who can beat them. So this is a really good boost that I hope we can take with us for the playoffs.

Why do you think that is? Why are you one of the only teams that seem to be able to beat them?

I think obviously, firepower is a topic that we match them in, when it comes to that. But I also think that maybe, at least on paper — I don't say that they fear us, but they also know that they have to play their A-game. They are one of the best lineups, but also because the roles are fitting, they have one of the greatest in-game leaders, who is really good at working with them, and it's... I'm really proud of the guys, I just have to say that.

I think they respect us, respect our players and how we work, and they have a history with us. I think this is also something that maybe Dan [apEX], I'm not sure, but I know Dan from the past, and he didn't like going up against G2. He really wanted to beat them, and I think he has [felt] the same here sometimes. But yeah, if it had an impact, I'm not sure, but I think he really wants to beat us, and sometimes, that's not a positive.

The rule is, just for everyone to understand: One week before an event, we ban FACEITs

Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen

Talk to me about m0NESY's form, he's been incredible this tournament. He's always been consistently good, but he looks like he's smurfing with his stats here. First of all, is that FACEIT ban still in effect? Is it making a difference?

I think he will definitely disagree with me (laughs). The only thing is that, when I looked at how much they played — especially him and kyousuke, and also TeSeS to an extent —, I think they overplayed. Sometimes you need to have a break, sometimes you need to get that enjoyment around Counter-Strike.

If you're just sitting and playing, I don't know, I think in a little bit under one month — this is where I did the tracking — a guy like kyousuke had played 130 FACEIT games, and this is including us practicing quite heavily. And I know he's young, he has to play a lot, and this I'm fine with. So I'm not after him too much. [With] him it's a little bit more in the discipline department, but when it comes to m0NESY and TeSeS, I think they have been overplaying a bit at the start of the season, and sometimes you need a break.

I also know Vitality and how they work, and this is also something I implemented with them back then, that it's really good to have a break where we have these pit stops of five days, where we take those days off. I'm really after them that they shouldn't spend that time playing FACEITs and all of that, so for now we will keep this.

The rule is, just for everyone to understand: One week before an event, we ban FACEITs. But from what I see now in the practice room also, they're barely playing at all now (laughs), so maybe at one point I also need to have a talk with them that maybe they need to play a little bit more because these are usually the talks I've had in my previous teams, being after dupreeh and Magisk, you know, 'Ay, we have to grind a little bit more guys.' But this issue, I definitely don't have here.

It seems to be working for m0NESY.

Yeah, and I think this was maybe my only concern, that he would drop individually and then blame it on me, saying, 'Hey, this is because I'm not playing outside of practice.' They can play DM, but ultimately, it was to get them to feel a little bit more hunger for the game because I think they overplayed.

The second part was the discipline. I think you're creating some bad habits by playing a lot of FACEITs, and this I wanted to take down, and so far, so good. We have also improved on the whole radar... thing (smiles) that people have known us for.

A bit of relief too then for you, since his form didn't drop off?

Yes, definitely. I know what he's capable of. He's also a player who likes it when the vibes are good on the team, he knows what we're doing, and I think he has also benefited from having a bit of a break, and also seeing that the team is improving.

We're also trying to look at him, trying to set him up. Every time we're prepping, I'm always coming with suggestions for him, giving him feedback about what he has been doing lately, if he wants to change it up, but ultimately it's always his call.

Lastly, this win put you straight through to the semis, so you have an extra day off. You'll miss playing in front of the crowd for one day, but you have extra time to prep and a bye, so I'm sure you'll take that. How are you going to approach the next days until you get to the semis?

We will take one day off tomorrow, for sure. Then we might have some content or media day either with Falcons or with ESL. I think we're going to go out tonight, and then we will start practicing a little bit. It's maybe a little bit of a tough region, but hopefully we can practice against some of the teams here that went out already, but we'll see.

We'll take it day by day now. I need to have a meeting with my staff, and also take the temperature of the players and see where we are because it has been some long weeks that we have been grinding.
