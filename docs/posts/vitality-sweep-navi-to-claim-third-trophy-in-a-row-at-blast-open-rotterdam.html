Vitality sweep NAVI to claim third trophy in a row at BLAST Open Rotterdam

Vitality have claimed their third Big Event win on the bounce, sweeping Natus Vincere 3-0 to claim the BLAST Open Rotterdam title with wins on Inferno (13-7), Anubis (13-10) and Dust2 (13-10).

With their trophy lift Vitality extended their current map winstreak to 22, series winstreak to 16, and further emphasised their case as the best Counter-Strike team of all time.

Whilst it was ultimately a comfortable series win for Vitality, Natus Vincere had chances to make the match more competitive. Natus Vincere lost to numerous bad buys across the series and lost clutch after clutch, partly down to Vitality's brilliance, but also partly due to their own profligacy.

ropz posts disgusting 3K to shut down the rotations

Natus Vincere kicked off the series by stealing away a CT-side force-buy win early on Inferno and giving themselves an excellent chance for a strong start, only to lose to a Vitality half-buy further down the line, squandering the opportunity.

The half became scrappy after that, both teams benefitting from individual highlight plays from their stars as they traded rounds back and forth, but Vitality squeaked ahead for a 7-5 lead.

The second half was a more straightforward affair, Natus Vincere losing to another bad buy after winning the pistol and letting Vitality run away with things. Vitality's defense was resolute, they read the offense well, and comfortably sealed a 1-0 series lead.

makazze 3K gets NAVI on the board on Anubis

Vitality stormed out to an early lead on Anubis only for Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri to pop up with massive impact, back-to-back 3Ks giving Natus Vincere the chance to build up a head of steam on their defense. Once again Natus Vincere fell to a bad buy, however, allowing Vitality to seal a comfortable 8-4 lead.

Another boon was thrown away by Natus Vincere in the second half as they lost to the force-buy after winning the pistol, which Vitality happily took and ran with to an 11-5 lead. Natus Vincere's offense then began to come online, strong calls ensuring a string of easy round wins, only for Vitality's stars to make the difference.

A creative boost earned Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut an opening kill, which Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan converted in a 2v3 retake after a slow map, before Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool and William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman wiped the board in the final round to claim a 13-10 map win.

ropz picks up incredible all-headshot Glock 4K to open Dust2

ropz set the tone early on Dust2 with a fabulous all-headshot 4K on the Glock, paving the way for a dominant offensive half for his team. Natus Vincere squandered another pistol win to a force-buy in the second half, and despite a valiant attempt at the comeback, they could not hold off Vitality's 3-0 series sweep.
