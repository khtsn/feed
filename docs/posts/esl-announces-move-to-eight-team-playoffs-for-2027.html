ESL announces move to eight-team playoffs for 2027

IEM Winter will return to Krakow to kick off 2027

ESL has announced a return to eight-team playoffs as the standard for their events from 2027 onwards. All playoff matches will also be played in front of a live audience.

Starting with the now-confirmed IEM Krakow 2027, that means two additional playoff matches instead of the lower bracket finals and upper bracket seeding matches usually seen at ESL's premier events, though further format details were not forthcoming.

Krakow will also have four Wildcard invites in Stage 1, which ESL says will only be used "rarely." They go on: "Our thought process in adding these Wildcard Invites is to enable teams to be in a position to make post-Major changes which would change their three-player core, but still maintain a world-class lineup and not have to start completely from scratch."

The $1,250,000 prize pool has been redistributed slightly based on the playoff change, with more of a top-heavy emphasis. In the group stage, the figures have only been tweaked slightly for simplification ($23,000 to $20,000 for 9-12th, $4500 to $5000 for 17-20th).

The operator also committed to taking their Pro Tour to three new cities by the end of 2027, "across three different continents," as part of their seven-event offering for the year. The Esports World Cup will be an eighth ESL-run event, announced separately last September.

The announcement additionally confirmed that 2027 will begin for ESL at the sophomore IEM Krakow in late January, while the "footprint" of the two ESL Pro League campaigns has been reduced to match Season 24.

The German company also confirmed details regarding its Annual Club Incentive for next year, with an offering of $10,450,000. Including the Grand Slam, the payout may rise to $12,450,000. That compares to PGL's offer of $11,000,000, and BLAST's $10,000,000.

Playoff viewership has also been added as a factor behind the incentive, where previously only group stage figures were used. This should help the high seeds, who might have to wait until the arena to play similarly-ranked opposition.

ESL's offering in 2027 is now:

IEM Krakow: Jan 27 - Feb 7

EWC: July 16 - Aug 1
