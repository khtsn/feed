A national surge in drive-throughs, an interview with Barbra Streisand and a beginner’s guide to looking at the universe.

The New York Times

Nov. 10, 2023

Times editors have handpicked 10 stories for you to enjoy. Welcome to The Weekender.

_____

Hungry (but Not for Human Contact), Americans Head for the Drive-Through

A national fixture is enjoying a fresh surge as post-pandemic customers crave speed and solitude. Restaurants are responding with a raft of innovations.

_____

A Beginner’s Guide to Looking at the Universe

The James Webb telescope has revealed light where once we saw only darkness. Our view of the universe will never be the same.

_____

Most Americans Still Commute Every Day. Here’s How That Experience Has Changed.

Since the beginning of the pandemic, commutes have changed in ways both good and bad. Take a data-driven dive into what’s happened.

_____

Barbra Streisand Is Ready to Tell All. Pull Up a Seat.

At home in California, Streisand talks about her new memoir, discussing the movies and men of her life and her determination to control her own art.

_____

In This Country, Most People Rent for Life. Is That Really What They Want?

At a time when many young people in places like California and New York can’t see a path to buying a home, Switzerland offers a glimpse of a post-ownership society.

_____

Cricket Gives a Nation Bowed by Violence a Reason to Stand Tall

Afghanistan’s cricket team has won big games and many fans in an international competition, in a stark contrast to the pariah status of its government.

_____

The Youngest Senior

Moving to Florida for early retirement, a native New Jerseyan in her 50s is finding her third act to be the most fun in a community where the average age is 79.

_____

How to Hijack a Quarter of a Million Dollars in Rare Japanese Kit Kats

The long, strange criminal trail of one stolen load of valuable sweets.

_____

Are ‘Elf’ and ‘Love Actually’ the Last Holiday Classics We’ll Ever Get?

The two comedies opened on the same date in 2003 and have stood the test of time. A changing Hollywood landscape might make another such day impossible.

_____

What’s the Easiest Houseplant? Many Experts Will Give You One Answer.

Bromeliads tend to have an “easy disposition” that allows them to flourish in low light with infrequent watering.
