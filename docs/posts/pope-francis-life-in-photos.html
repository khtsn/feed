The life of Pope Francis, the first Latin American and first Jesuit to serve as the Roman Catholic Church’s pontiff, lasted nearly nine decades and spanned several continents.

A pope chosen from “almost the ends of the Earth,” as he said upon his selection in 2013, Francis was a relentless advocate for migrants and marginalized people, as well as for the environment.

His papacy promoted inclusivity, embracing survivors of sexual abuse by clergy members; L.G.B.T.Q. Catholics; Muslim and Orthodox clerics; and leaders of developing nations, where he believed the church’s future was rooted.

Francis also tried to facilitate peace in Ukraine and more broadly on a planet that he often said was living a world war fought piecemeal.

He died on Monday at age 88.

Pope Francis appeared on the balcony of St. Peter’s Basilica on March 13, 2013, after being elected by the conclave of cardinals.

Pilgrims crowded onto Copacabana Beach in Rio de Janeiro for a Mass celebrated by Francis in July 2013. His humble attitude earned him immediate favor from the public, and he quickly became a major global figure.

Francis praying for peace at the Western Wall, Judaism’s holiest shrine, in the Old City of Jerusalem in 2014, while holding a wishing note to place in the cracks of its ancient stones.

Attendees gathering for a Mass celebrated by Francis in the Plaza de la Revolución in Havana in 2015. Although his popularity declined somewhat over the years, his speaking out against violence and war resonated with the general public.

Well-wishers greeting the pope in Manila in 2015.

Francis talking with journalists on a flight from Manila to Rome in 2015, in one of the impromptu in-flight news conferences that were a regular feature of his papacy.

A line of people waving as Francis traveled past an informal settlement on the outskirts of Nairobi, Kenya, in 2015. While visiting Africa, he urged local leaders to seek peace and aimed to bring global attention to their needs.

The pope in Philadelphia in 2015. Although welcoming crowds greeted him on his trip to the United States, he faced resistance from conservatives during his papacy.

Addressing a joint session of Congress in Washington in 2015 as Vice President Joseph R. Biden Jr. and House Speaker John Boehner, both Catholics, clapped behind him.

Members of Congress welcomed Francis to the Speaker’s Balcony of the U.S. Capitol in 2015.

Addressing the United Nations General Assembly in New York in 2015.

In this photograph provided by the Greek prime minister’s office, Francis met migrants at the Moria refugee camp on Lesbos in 2016.

Embracing Pope Emeritus Benedict XVI at the Vatican in 2017. Francis tried to bring order to the Vatican, an institution that was troubled with power struggles and shocked by Benedict’s resignation in 2013.

President Barham Salih of Iraq greeting the pope at the presidential palace in Baghdad in 2021.

Waving flags and taking pictures in the Church of Immaculate Conception in Qaraqosh, Iraq, where Francis celebrated a Mass in 2021.

Celebrating Good Friday from an empty St. Peter’s Square in 2021, as much of the world imposed social-distancing measures to stop the spread of Covid-19.

A moment of reflection in 2022 at a cemetery where children from residential schools were buried in Maskwacis, Canada. Francis continued to travel in his later years even as knee problems and sciatica led him to use a cane and then a wheelchair.

Crowds gathered outside the airport to welcome the pope to Juba, South Sudan, in 2023.

Francis leaving the airport after arriving in Juba.

Celebrating the funeral Mass for Benedict XVI in St. Peter’s Square in 2023.

Visiting a school near Vanimo, Papua New Guinea, in 2024. The Asia-Pacific trip, an 11-day tour, was the longest and most complicated of his tenure.

Francis arriving to say Mass in Jakarta, Indonesia, in 2024.

A crowd waiting to welcome Francis in Dili, East Timor, in 2024.

Waving to the faithful in Dili.
