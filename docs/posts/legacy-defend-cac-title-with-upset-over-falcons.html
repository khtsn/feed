Legacy defend CAC title with upset over Falcons

After two hard-fought maps to open the series, Legacy took control and romped to the trophy in emphatic fashion.

+5

Legacy have defended their CS Asia Championships in dramatic fashion, upsetting pre-tournament favorites Falcons with a convincing 3-1 grand final victory to secure back-to-back trophies in Shanghai.

The match was a fascinating and hard-fought battle across the first two maps, both teams finding success in the macro and micro sense, leading to a 1-1 series score. Falcons then waned badly, however, particularly on the T side, whilst Legacy went from strength to strength.

Legacy have now bagged their first international trophy since adding Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan, and sport overall strong results in international competition, adding a top-eight at ESL Pro League, a third-place at IEM Atlanta and now a trophy at CAC to their resume.

Meanwhile Falcons are left to settle for another second place finish, their second in two events since bringing Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen into the fold. Their issues in the business end of tournaments have yet to be rectified, as they have now been defeated in seven grand final matchups in a row.

Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač went missing in this final in a bad way, Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov's impact waned as the series wore on, leaving only Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin to walk away with any real credit. Questions will be asked of their T sides as well, as they were so poor offensively across the final two maps.

The series kicked off with Falcons securing victory in a hard-fought Nuke, despite struggling on the favored CT side.

karrigan and company won the CT pistol but squandered it by dying on info pushes in the following round, only to hit right back with a stack on the B site. Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto then grabbed control of the half for his team with an ace clutch on an A hit, granting his team a runway to a 5-2 lead.

latto signs off his ace with smart 1v2

Another low-buy win ensured Falcons avoided a disaster half, after which NiKo and kyousuke provided impact rounds to get their team to a 6-6 score.

The second half continued the back-and-forth nature of the map, a latto multi-kill giving Legacy a good start on their defense before Falcons answered back with a clean A execute.

m0NESY triple makes it four T rounds in a row as Falcons win 13-11

Mixed results for a Legacy double-AWP setup saw Falcons tie the map at 9-9 before Legacy stretched into the lead off an audacious arT push, but Falcons had the last laugh, their star trio popping up with the individual plays needed to get Nuke over the line.

Legacy righted the ship after losing their pick of Nuke, racing out to a convincing lead to start Ancient. latto pulled off a tasty 1v3 to open his team's CT side and Falcons' offense struggled, unable to get much traction as Legacy flew out to a 7-1 advantage. NiKo provided the lifeline his team needed, a 3K round allowing them to post two more offensive rounds.

NiKo flank ends in disaster after latto 180

A pistol victory gave Falcons the chance to push the score to 6-9 on their T side, then they recovered after an A execute caught them off guard to run the score all the way back to 9-10. Unfortunately for Falcons, a bad miss by NiKo in an advantageous round gave Legacy a foot in the door and latto stepped up in an incredible way to capitalize, racking up a series of multi-kills to push his team over the line.

After two close maps, Legacy rose to the occasion and steamrolled the rest of the grand final. Falcons' offensive halves were a problem in particular, seeming disjointed and short of ideas, whilst Legacy's star duo of Eduardo "⁠dumau⁠" Wolkmer and latto provided play after play to put Falcons to the sword.
