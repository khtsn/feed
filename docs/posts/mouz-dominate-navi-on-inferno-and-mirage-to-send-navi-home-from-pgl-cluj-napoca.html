MOUZ dominate NAVI on Inferno and Mirage to send NAVI home from PGL Cluj-Napoca

MOUZ have booked a semi-final berth at PGL Cluj-Napoca by virtue of a 2-1 victory over Natus Vincere, bouncing back from a loss on Ancient (10-13) with comfortable wins on Inferno (13-4) and Mirage (13-6).

With their victory MOUZ have given themselves a good shot of reaching the grand final by setting up a semi-final bout with PARIVISION, whom they comfortably beat in the group stage.

Coming into this series it was clear Natus Vincere would have the crowd at their backs, due to the presence of Romanian Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan among their number, and there was a real chance MOUZ would shrink in the face of the hostile environment. Instead, they rose to the occasion.

MOUZ kicked off the series by racing out to a convincing lead on the CT side of Ancient, doing so largely off the back of Lotan "⁠Spinx⁠" Giladi. The rifler found key multi-kills in mid and late-round situations, even when his team were at a disadvantage, putting MOUZ in control by halftime.

Brollan's miraculous multi-kill nearly wins the 2v5, but iM sticks the defuse for map point

Natus Vincere were, however, even better on their CT side, powered by an unlikely star in the form of Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen. The IGL found multiple opening kills to put his team in the driver's seat of rounds, and they duly delivered on a number of retakes to clinch Ancient in close fashion.

Inferno was an entirely one-sided affair in favour of MOUZ, who emphasised their proficiency on the map. Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás' excellent AK-47 entry work drove forward MOUZ's slick attacks, which Natus Vincere struggled to handle, with Spinx ready to close any rounds that needed tidying up.

torzsi and Spinx combine to keep MOUZ rolling

Natus Vincere relied on a Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov AWP ace to help them cobble together a few CT rounds late in the half, before MOUZ easily converted the map on their own defense.

MOUZ continued their dominance into the first half of Mirage, picking up a force-buy in the second round of their offense to get themselves rolling early. Natus Vincere could only manage isolated round wins as Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin and torzsi put them to the sword.

torzsi embraces the role of villain after sick flick clinches the clutch

A Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri multi-kill in their CT pistol gave Natus Vincere a glimmer of hope, but some slick A executes and strong fragging from Brollan and Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman ensured an easy conversion for MOUZ.

MOUZ embrace the role of villain

MOUZ have often wilted in arena settings but today seemed to thrive in the hostile environment. torzsi and Spinx could be seen taunting the crowd after round wins, and torzsi told the crowd to boo louder in his post-game interview on the stage.

If MOUZ can continue to harness this energy moving forward, maybe they can shake off their arena struggles and claim back-to-back PGL Cluj-Napoca titles.

NAVI's map pool struggles continue

In their recent matches, Natus Vincere's map pool has become a bit of a problem. They lost their map pick more often than not across the group stage, and whilst they managed to clinch Ancient in this series, the veto was still weighed against them from the outset.

The loss of Train and the collapse of their Mirage have left Natus Vincere without a consistent home to call their own, and it is something they will need to address moving forward if they are to compete in the deep end of tournaments.
