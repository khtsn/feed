IEM Cologne Major shatters CS viewership records

The German event broke records across all three major audience benchmarks.

Falcons' 3-0 victory over FURIA in the grand final of the 2026 IEM Cologne Major had a viewership peak of 2,751,121, making it the most popular match in Counter-Strike history, according to data firm Esports Charts.

The series surpassed the record set during the Bo3 grand final of the 2021 PGL Stockholm Major between G2 and Natus Vincere, which had a peak of 2,748,434 viewers.

IEM Cologne had already cracked the two million concurrent viewer mark twice before the final — during the quarter-final between G2 and Spirit, and the semi-final between Spirit and Falcons. It marked the first time in CS history that a non-final had crossed that threshold.

Esports Charts added that the Cologne Major completed "an unprecedented sweep" of Counter-Strike's audience benchmarks. Beyond peak viewership, the event also set records for average viewership (627,863) and total hours watched (100,301,009).

The data firm adds that the final numbers might still be higher as it continues to update the viewership figures.
