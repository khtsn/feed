soulfly on crowd pressure: "We were scared to do some moves, people become more silent than normal, and I think we fixed that"

Aurora showed mental fortitude in a two-map win over The MongolZ to clinch the final playoff berth at PGL Astana, recovering from deficits on both maps to make the Barys Arena after faltering on their first attempt against MOUZ.

"The MOUZ game kinda [gave] a slap to everyone, we slapped each other, 'What the fuck are we doing?'" Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici told HLTV after the match. "And then today, that slap worked."

Aurora come alive late to make Astana playoffs over The MongolZ

The Turkish rifler said that, in a team talk, Aurora reflected on that loss by saying they played their "D-game," and believe that if they can play their A-game, they can win the event. A rematch against MOUZ in the quarter-finals will give Aurora a chance to prove those words true, as well as show they can overcome the demons that have often plagued them under the bright lights.

"When you play in front of maybe 10k [people], sometimes we feel pressure from the crowd or we're scared to do some moves, and people become a little bit more silent than normal," soulfly said when reflecting on his team's prior issues. "So that was the problem that we have, and I think we also fixed that, I believe. We're going to see."

Listen to the interview here.

Congrats, into the playoffs. A tight game against The MongolZ, what's going through you mind after that?

We're feeling pretty good. After Rio, we're trying to find our best version, individually as a team, and what we have done today was pretty good and everybody was happy. The analysis was really good, and the pre-game plan was good as well. On Overpass everything worked for me, and MAJ3R and the others were really good, and we ended up winning.

You said after Rio you were trying to find yourself a bit, what has that process been like?

We had a lot of talks out of the game. We're trying to... like, we all trust each other, and it's sometimes nice to talk honestly with each other, you know? Like for example, if I have something to say to Wicadia and I'm openly saying to him to [meet in the] middle, you know?

And we don't have any problem about anything out of the game; we like each other and we are hanging out together. We eat dinner, we eat breakfast together, so basically we're gaining this team atmosphere. We're bringing it back.

sometimes we had some... some people are scared to take responsibilities in the game

Yeah, as we know, eating breakfast together [for good atmosphere] is a requirement for teams (soulfly laughs), we had that whole drama with Falcons. You make it into the playoffs often, but sometimes once you're in the arena, it has been a bit of a struggle. What have you been doing to get over that?

Honestly, on all the stages we struggle, as you said, but I think we got over it after the EPL final. We played many times, and I feel like sometimes we had some... some people are scared to take responsibilities in the game.

It doesn't matter who, but sometimes me, sometimes, like everybody. We should remember all the time that we are playing the same game, just in front of a crowd, so nothing changed for us. We need to believe that and play like that.

When you say that sometimes people are struggling to take responsibility, in what way?

Like when you play in front of maybe 10k [people], sometimes we feel pressure from the crowd or we're scared to do some moves, and people become a little bit more silent than normal. So that was the problem that we have, and I think we also fixed that, I believe. We're going to see.

Here, you had that close loss against MOUZ, and now you're going into the arena after getting this win. How much confidence does this win add when you're going into the arena and there are going to be a lot of big teams there?

After the MOUZ game, we had a talk, that we played our D-game, you know? So we just said, 'Okay, if we are playing our D-game, and if we show up with our A-game, we can win the tournament.' The MOUZ game kinda [gave] a slap to everyone, we slapped each other, 'What the fuck are we doing?' And then today, that slap worked.
