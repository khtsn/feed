Xizt: "For sure this is a setback"

Ninjas in Pyjamas have been sent packing from the CS Asia Championships in last place following a 2-1 defeat to Lynn Vision.

It's a bitter pill for the Ninjas, who had shown positive signs at the CCT Global Finals, the team's first event with Martin "⁠stavn⁠" Lund as the new AWPer.

"We're making things hard for ourselves, so for sure this is a setback," Richard "⁠Xizt⁠" Landström told HLTV after the match. "We expected to at least get out of the groups."

After failing to make the Major, Ninjas in Pyjamas will turn their focus to Stake Ranked Episode 2 — their final opportunity to salvage some success from a disappointing season.

"I think what I want is to see us play more relaxed," he said. "I want to see us be more decisive in what we want to do.

"Obviously now the plan is to practice as much as we can before we fly back to Europe, and hopefully we can end the season on a high note."

A 2-1 defeat, and NIP is out of the tournament in last place. Give me your thoughts on this series.

Starting with Dust2, obviously we lost both pistols, but I still feel like our understanding of the map isn't the best right now. It hasn't been so for a while, actually. And then I feel like in general, especially on Ancient, we have a really hard time using advantages to our advantage, we can't really close out those… We're getting entries, or we're trading 4-on-3s. I think we even lost a 4v1 today where we decided to smoke long instead of just smoking the bomb. We're just making things a bit harder than they should be. But, yeah, Lynn Vision played well today, especially in the pistol rounds. They just hit a lot harder as well. Props to them.

I wanted to ask you about what happened on Dust2. A few rounds into the game, there was a pause, and you were arguing with the admin. stavn didn’t seem happy. What happened?

I think there was a graffiti put somewhere on the map. I don't know if it impacted anything. We just wanted to make sure that the graffiti was removed before we continued playing so it wouldn't create any confusion for us and for them, obviously. Nothing major, I would say.

But do you have any idea how that happened?

I have no idea.

The third place in the CCT Global Finals seemed to suggest that you were taking steps in the right direction after bringing in stavn. Do you look at the results here as a setback?

For sure. I think against B8 we won both pistols and lost two half-buys. It's just that we're making things hard for ourselves, so for sure this is a setback. We expected to at least get out of the groups.

How would you say that stavn is settling into the role? Today he had a great showing on Nuke but he also has some maps where he doesn’t have a lot of impact. Do you see that as something normal for someone who has just transitioned to a new role?

For sure you could see that in the Nuke game today and also in CCT, I think he had a good tournament there. I think it's more like as a team we tend to stress a lot. We're not really listening that well to each other, and I think it obviously affects everyone on the server, especially here at this tournament. It was really bad.

You now have Stake Ranked coming up. Your opportunity to finish the season on a high note. What are the goals for that event? What do you want to see from the team there?

I think what I want is to see us play more relaxed. I want to see us be more decisive in what we want to do. Obviously now the plan is to practice as much as we can before we fly back to Europe, and hopefully we can end the season on a high note.
