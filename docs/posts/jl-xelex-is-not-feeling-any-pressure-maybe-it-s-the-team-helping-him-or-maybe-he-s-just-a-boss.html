jL: "xelex is not feeling any pressure; maybe it's the team helping him, or maybe he's just a boss"

Although the team's communication got more chaotic on stage, jL says he is happy his team is communicating more rather than less.

MOUZ repeated their group stage victory over Aurora in the playoffs of PGL Astana to advance to the semi-finals, with another stellar series by Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman delivering his team a victory in their debut arena match with Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze and Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius.

After the match, HLTV caught up with the Lithuanian rifler, who explained how the team's comms became more chaotic on stage.

"Some freezetimes, holy shit, we had like four or five guys speaking, and I was like, 'Okay,'" jL said while laughing. "So sometimes I just typed in the chat, 'I dropped a smoke,' you know what I mean? (laughs) Like I didn't want to flood the comms too much. But it's good, I'd rather have people communicating more than under-communicating."

jL also praised rookie xelex, saying he "felt no difference from him between the group stage and the stage game," said he was surprised by how well xertioN is playing with how much he has to think about, and shared his thoughts on Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici's comments about Aurora playing their D-game in their previous matchup and the Turkish team getting over their stage woes.

Listen to the inteview here.

First stage match with the boys, and you got it done 2-0. A rematch against Aurora as well, so give me your thoughts on that and how it compared to the group stage game you played.

Compared to the group stage game, I feel like we played pretty much the same, besides Dust2 CT side. I feel like I could've been more vocal on the Dust2 CT side and... I wasn't playing too well, I'll be honest. But then the rest, I feel like Dust2 T side was good, both sides of Nuke were good. Of course we had some rounds here and there, but I just feel like we played solid CS.

How was your first time on the stage with the team?

It was good! It was enjoyable, there was... I felt the upshift in the comms, but other then that, I feel like we handled it well and we still played as a team.

They were a little bit more chaotic with the comms [on stage]

Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius

When you say you felt the upshift in the comms, what do you mean? You told me before the team is very vocal on CT side especially, so what was the difference?

They were a little bit more chaotic with the comms. Some freezetimes, holy shit, we had like four or five guys speaking, and I was like, 'Okay,' (laughs), so sometimes I just typed in the chat, 'I dropped a smoke,' you know what I mean? (laughs) Like I didn't want to flood the comms too much. But it's good, I'd rather have people communicating more than under-communicating.

soulfly told me after they qualified for the playoffs that they felt they played their D-game when they played against you (jL smiles). What kind of game do you think they played today, and any thoughts on his comments?

(smiles) Hmm... I mean, to be honest, I didn't look into it too far. Of course, a team can have their opinions, but I don't think we played our A-game or even B-game against them when we played in the group stage. I feel like we had a lot rounds where we just gave it away to them. On Dust2, I feel like they were very fortunate on how they split B, so that's why they got a lot of rounds.

But I don't think they played their D-game, I feel like it was... of course they were below their level, but... Yeah, I read soulfly's interview as well afterwards, and he said they kind of fixed the... I don't want to quote him too bad, but fixed their choking problems on stage, and now it should be all better.

But a funny thing happened, I thought about this [during the game], and then I pressed tab, and then I see him 1-10, and I'm like, 'Okay.' (smiles) It was a little bit funny to me, no hate towards him. People can have bad games, but I hope they do overcome it because it was a close game, and if they are better at that, they probably could take a map.

Talk me through that Dust2 comeback a bit, it was eight rounds in a row at the end. Tell me about that and xertioN, again, having another insane series.

Yeah, I feel like insane series for him is the default now; I haven't seen him have a bad series. The CT side, we understood that we were not connected with each other, we needed some more pre-planning, we needed some more active calls, setups on sites, Long, whatever. We just tried to do it on T side as well, and I feel like the ping-pong was very good, like the information between players, between sites, was a lot better on T side.

We understand how the map works. We had a call to pop Short, but then one of Aurora's guys went from Middle to CT to A, with full sound, and we were like, 'Okay, let's split B,' and then we just beat them on B. Maybe they were too stressed, but I don't know, we just played with what they gave us. They were very vocal with how they play, and it didn't work out for them.

He's not feeling any pressure, and maybe it's the team helping him, maybe we're just tight like that, or maybe he's just a boss (laughs)

Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius on xelex

How was xelex on stage? First experience for him, a rookie, so what was he like next to you?

I felt no difference from him between the group stage and the stage game. Of course, leading up to the game he was a little bit more excited, and even during the game he was a little bit more excited than usual, but his comms were good, his plays were good, he was still going for the plays.

Like even on Nuke T side, he kills the AWP behind Red, and I would expect a rookie to chill, you know? You got the entry now freeze, but he goes and kills Main, and then later in the round he also kills someone else, and I was like, 'Damn, okay.' He's not feeling any pressure, and maybe it's the team helping him, maybe we're just tight like that, or maybe he's just a boss (laughs).

When I talked to you last, it was before you played any officials. What do you think of how the team is playing now? Especially xertioN, I want to get your thoughts on him and how he's calling and playing. Does he call around himself, is that [helping] him play so well?

He's not calling around himself, definitely, but when you're the in-game leader, it's a little bit easier to play because you know everything, you know the macro. He's very into the game; I'm very surprised he's still shooting that good while he's still thinking about a lot of stuff.

And it's not like he calls around himself, he's just playing good. Honestly, I'm surprised. To be honest, like any game that we play, I'm like, 'Okay, xertioN is not going to have a good game, so we'll have to step up,' but every single game he's having a good game so... (smiles) props to him.

No complaining from you!

No! Like keeping shooting (laughs), I'll play late, it's fine.

From a 'if you want to win the match confidently' [perspective], I'd like to play against G2 [...] but I want to play Spirit because they have a good run against me; that's the ego talking

Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius

Lastly, you're into the semis now, and you're going to play against the winner of G2-Spirit. Thoughts on that matchup, who you'd like to play, how you feel like that game might go?

Coming from a 'if you want to win the match confidently' [perspective], I'd like to play against G2. I feel like we're just better than them, we understand how they play, and it's easier to play against a team like G2, for example, than Spirit. Because Spirit always has this x-factor, even if you have the correct reads, they have donk and he can shoot well. And every single half-buy he has an AK, so every single round you have to work for the map.

Of course I'd rather play G2, but I want to play Spirit because... I mean, Spirit do have a good run against me, and maybe if I join MOUZ, it's going to change. So I'd like to play Spirit, that's the ego talking.
