Passion UA move Grim to the bench

The American player is expected to make a move to NRG as Senzu takes over in the starting five.

Passion UA has announced that Michael "⁠Grim⁠" Wince has been benched, an expected move after acquiring Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold on loan, a move sparked after interest from NRG in acquiring the 25-year-old.

"When Mike [Grim] got the offer from NRG, or when NRG came to buy Mike, that obviously changed things and sped up everything that we had to do and made us act a lot faster on getting new transfers and new players," coach Tiaan "⁠T.c⁠" Coertzen told HLTV in an interview at Roman Imperium V.

"So that's pretty much what sparked all of it, I can say, is the interest from NRG in Grim, and we just had to act fast."

Grim has been a key component of the Complexity core that joined Passion UA in August of 2025, posting a 1.09 rating during his years with the North American organization and 1.14 since joining the latter, but the allure of returning to North America made the NRG proposal a hard one to contest. "We've played together for three, four years now. There's no bad blood or anything like this," T.c said.

"And for Mike, the move also makes sense because he wants to be on an American team so he can be home more. He has family and his girlfriend back on America. So, honestly, the best case scenario for him is that he can go to an American team, where he can be at home more and he can actually practice in NA."

Passion UA's first event with Senzu will be Stage 1 of ESL Pro League, which kicks off online on March 1.
