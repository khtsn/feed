npl: "We've worked a lot and it pays off; it's great to see"

B8 beat The MongolZ to secure a playoff spot

B8 have locked up a spot in the playoff stage of the CS Asia Championships following a 13-9 success over Ninjas in Pyjamas and a 2-1 win against The MongolZ.

It was a great day at the office for the young Ukrainian squad, and Andrii "⁠npl⁠" Kukharskyi believes his team has a realistic shot at the title.

"I'd say that we can actually win this tournament if we continue to play like this," he told HLTV after the match.

"It's too cocky to put it like this, that we can win the tournament, because you have Falcons, you have MOUZ, even though they have two new players and are not in the best shape. But still, I feel that my team can win this tournament, and we will try to do it."

npl also shared his thoughts on Danylo "⁠s1zzi⁠" Vinnyk's meteoric rise, the recent results at IEM Atlanta, and the team's steady improvements as we approach the Major.

Listen to the interview here.

Congratulations on making the playoffs here at CAC. You looked strong today against NIP and The MongolZ. Give me your thoughts on these two matches.

We just played well as a team. We made a small number of mistakes. We made some, but not a lot of important ones. I'm feeling great.

And how does it feel to get revenge on The MongolZ, after they beat you in Cluj-Napoca. Back then, you said the team "didn't want to fix mistakes during the tournament." Today, you came out on top. What was different about this match?

If you look at how we played back then, I'd say we were forgetting our rounds, we weren't doing our things properly. Overall, we just played bad. We've worked a lot, as I said before in a previous tournament, maybe Atlanta, I don't remember well, that we had a very long and hard period of training, ten days, with only one day off. We've worked a lot, and it pays off. It's great to see.

You came into this event straight from Atlanta, where you notably beat FUT and you had a three-map series against BetBoom. What are your main takeaways from that event?

I'm happy overall. We had like a 50/50 match against BetBoom, but they had an insane tournament from their perspective. They showed a very great performance, so it's not bad that we lost to them. And we lost to Vitality, but that's obviously, like... We're not on their level right now. We can fight with them, but on a good day, you know?

Back at PGL Bucharest, you told us that s1zzi was getting better with every tournament. In Rio and in Atlanta, he started to show his true level. He's starting to show his potential. How do you look at the progress that he has made in just a couple of months with the team?

He has made a huge amount of progress. I'm happy to see that. Obviously, he's my friend, and I'm happy to see him showing a good level. Obviously, he's not up there yet in terms of tier-1 snipers, but he's taking huge steps, and I'm happy to see that. As you said, we saw in those tournaments a good level of performance from him, but I'd say that was like 50% of his performance.

When he's working with the coach and with the team, I can see that he's very confident. He's not shy. We're helping him a lot. So I'm happy with his progress.

You're now already qualified for the playoffs regardless of what happens in the next match. What sort of expectations did you have coming into this tournament, and how far do you think that you can go here?

My expectation was to proceed to the playoffs and show good performance there as a team. And I'd say that we can actually win this tournament if we continue to play like this. It's too cocky to put it like this, that we can win the tournament, because you have Falcons, you have MOUZ, even though they have two new players and are not in the best shape. But still, I feel that my team can win this tournament, and we will try to do it.

Your team seems to be getting into a good rhythm at the perfect time, with the Major around the corner. Are you happy with the way that the team is playing right now, this close to the major? And what is the secret?

There is no big secret. We're just working a lot. We're young players, and we obviously cannot play well on every map. And when we are working, when we are practicing our weak maps, I can feel we are getting better on them every week and we understand how to play, macro-wise and micro-wise, which positions we have to take, where we have to fight. That's it.
