Aurora make Cluj playoffs after 2-10 comeback vs Astralis

If the narrative around Aurora is one of a team failing in playoffs, one around Astralis might be building around falling just short of the arena placings.

The Danish-majority team placed 7-8th at IEM Krakow and have now been eliminated one game shy of playoffs in Cluj. It was in damning fashion, too, after failing to convert their 10-2 lead on Dust2 and another 7-13 loss on Overpass.

The writing seemed to be on the wall for Astralis after they lost home map Overpass, but Victor "⁠Staehr⁠" Staehr's 20-frag CT side led to a 10-2 lead on Dust2 and both teams must have been planning for an Inferno decider.

Somehow, however, Aurora were able to build back into the match, with some great holds on B from Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici as Astralis tried to escape an oppressive Özgür "⁠woxic⁠" Eker AWP. Only a few rounds were close, and Aurora had highlights like Ismailcan "⁠XANTARES⁠" Dörtkardeş's 4k ready when required.

"CT side, I want to point my finger to woxic," soulfly told James "⁠BanKs⁠" Banks post-game. "I was kind of down, I had no fights on T side, but [he said], 'if someone is going to do this comeback, it's us.'"

It was fitting that woxic lit the fire for the comeback, after he had crushed Astralis' game plan on Overpass, almost single-handedly. Time after time, woxic would disrupt Astralis in their set-up phase in the A Bathrooms, and he ended the first half with a 1.98 rating.

woxic's clutch on the T side of Dust2 also ensured Aurora got two rounds, at least, and he ended the series as the player of the match with a 1.46 rating and +5.1% Swing.

Aurora now set their sights on another arena match, and soulfly is confident they can show a better face than in Krakow: "I know what mistakes I made in the last previous stage," he said post-game.

"We will have some plan, I also need to feel comfortable on the stage. The Krakow arena was incredible, I could hear the voices, but this time I am more experienced and can do a little bit better."

HooXi: "We have to continue working on not losing to ourselves"

"Tough day at the office," Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen said in his own post-game chat with BanKs. "We wanted to take another step at this event and had the chances to do it. it's tough."

Astralis' international lineup have shown promise but after two events, they are still yet to jump the final hurdle into playoffs. It will also be concerning to HooXi that the team have suffered two losses in a row on home map Overpass, after FUT beat them 13-7 in round 3.

An event to forget for 0.83-rated jabbi

The form of Jakob "⁠jabbi⁠" Nygaard — who had a 1.18 LAN rating before this event in 2026 — in Cluj was particularly disappointing, and he ends the event with 0.54 KPR and a 0.83 rating.

"We have to continue working on not losing to ourselves." HooXi concluded. "We need to keep ourselves to a higher standard. There was a lot of frustration we could not reset from."
