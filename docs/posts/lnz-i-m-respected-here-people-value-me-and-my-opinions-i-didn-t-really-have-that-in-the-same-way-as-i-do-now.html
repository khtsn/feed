LNZ: "I'm respected here, people value me and my opinions. I didn't really have that in the same way as I do now"

MIBR recently followed in FURIA's footsteps, bringing in players from the European scene to supplement their Brazilian lineup. The early signs have been promising, as MIBR have won a couple of regional tournaments in Brazil before kicking off their PGL Bucharest campaign by coming back from a loss to Astralis with victories over BC.Game and Legacy.

Linus "⁠LNZ⁠" Holtäng made the trip over from Europe to serve as the team's IGL at the start of the year and the Swede has plenty good to say about his decision. "I felt like this was a chance a needed to take," he told HLTV. "I'm really happy that I took this chance and I'm just happy to keep playing now."

Whilst MIBR have enjoyed regional success since LNZ's addition, they haven't had much opportunity to show their worth internationally, something the 23-year-old is well aware of. "The goal is to get experience, mostly in Europe, because the pracs in Brazil don't really make sense for us to play, and just try to make as deep runs as we can."

With MIBR making their way to a 2-1 record in their first international outing with LNZ, HLTV sat down with the IGL to discuss his time in the team so far, his thoughts on rising star Klimentii "⁠kl1m⁠" Krivosheev, and the differences between playing in Brazil versus Europe.

Thoughts on that series? It seemed like there was a lot of fire in that game.

Yeah, it was a really nice game to win. A Brazilian derby, which is my first in a tournament like this. It was very fun to play and I think we were all very excited to win this game. I'm really happy we showed good energy and good spirit, I felt like everyone contributed to this game as a team, so it was nice.

Like you say that was your first Brazilian derby, so I wanted to ask what it's been like playing on a Brazilian team? You've had a few months to settle in now.

I wouldn't say it's too different, because I still have my style and our coach, LETN1, is still European as well, so I feel like we work like a European team. But I think playing in Brazil is a bit different, it's a lot more explosiveness, it's different kind of styles and it's hard to adapt to. People always say that in Brazil you can lose to anyone, and I've really experienced that now.

You spent a bit of time playing in Brazil and now you've come back to Europe, so what's the difference like? Is it tough to switch that mentality?

I honestly feel like it's in a way easier to play these teams, because they're not in your face as much. I also feel like Legacy is a team that is in your face. In a way it's easier, not easier to win but just easier to play. I feel like we still need a lot of games here to get more experience in the bigger tournaments and get the groove a bit, because we haven't been playing any big LANs so far, except for this one.

venomzera is someone who maybe struggles with English, but you hear him scream a lot and you see the passion, and I just love to play with people who show that kind of energy.

What's it like playing with Brazilian players, who are generally known for being quite passionate and loud in game? What's that been like for you?

I love it, I think it's very nice to play with. For example, venomzera is someone who maybe struggles with English, but you hear him scream a lot and you see the passion, and I just love to play with people who show that kind of energy because it really builds into the group as a whole, both on and off the server. I really love it.

Have there been any culture shocks, any difficulties for you playing with players who are culturally very different?

I don't think so actually, I think it's more about the experience that the players in our team have. I've had players recently with more experience, and in Europe there's a different sort of standard of what you should know in any team, and not. It's just maybe a struggle to make the inexperienced players play in a system that works in Europe, but maybe it doesn't always work that way. It's hard sometimes but I think we're doing it good.

How's the language been? Like you say, venomzera maybe struggles a bit with his English.

It's a lot better now than it was in the start, so I don't really feel like it's too big of a struggle for us. I think it's been going a lot better and hopefully it can be even better.

How has playing in MIBR measured up compared to your expectations before?

I think I wasn't expecting anything in particular, I just felt like this was a chance I needed to take, for the options I had after the benching in HEROIC. So far I'm happy that I took it because I feel like I'm very respected here, people value me and my opinions, which is also making me grow more. I didn't really have that in the same way as I do now, so I'm really happy that I took this chance and I'm just happy to keep playing now.

I think he's probably not the best I've played with mechanically, but one of them. He has insane mechanics

I wanted to ask about kl1m because he's somebody who turned a few heads on G2's academy, and has settled in really quickly, which I think for a young player who's playing in a team outside of his region is quite impressive. What have you thought of him since he joined?

I think he's probably not the best I've played with mechanically, but one of them. He has insane mechanics, sometimes you spec him and you're like 'how does he kill the way he does,' and I think it's something special about him. I think he, as a player, just needs to have more experience on a higher level, to learn how to dictate the game when you're an AWPer and control your team a bit, but that's something we're working on and talking with him about. So hopefully it can be as good as we want it to be, right? But I think he's doing an amazing job.

Looking forwards for this MIBR lineup, what are the goals and expectations for you moving forwards?

One goal for us when I joined was to make the Major and now we did it. Barely, but we did it and that's all that matters. I think now the goal is to get experience, mostly in Europe, because the pracs in Brazil don't really make sense for us to play, and just try to make as deep runs as we can.

I think you cannot make too many expectations when you're not playing all the big tournaments, so that's maybe the first goal, to play all of them and be within the top 20 regularly. After than I think we just want to show we can actually do some upsets, and not go out last in every event.
