LEVIATÁN takes down NRG 2-1 to secure playoff berth

LEVIATÁN LEVIATÁN North America Rank #4 spike Rodrigo Lombardi blowz Guilherme Oliviera Neon Bruno Rodríguez Sato Eduardo Kenzo Nagahama Sato kiNgg Francisco Aravena defeated NRG NRG North America Rank #2 keiko Georgio Sanassy brawk Brock Somerhalder mada Adam Pampuch skuba Logan Jenkins Ethan Ethan Arnold 2-1 in the second 1-0 match of the Masters London Swiss stage to book their tickets to playoffs.

The two Americas representatives hadn't met in Stage 1 after running through opposite sides of the regional playoff bracket, but they did encounter each other in a recent EWC qualifier match that went 2-0 the way of NRG. This time, however, the LATAM squad came out on top, guaranteeing a top eight finish at four of their players' first international event.

The match began on LEVIATÁN's pick of Ascent. LEV continued to pay little mind to the Neon nerf, sticking to the same Neon-Phoenix lineup that they used previously. NRG went for a Jett-Phoenix composition with Ethan on a somewhat unusual Astra pick.

Sato came out of the gates swinging—the Phoenix player pulled off an ace in round three and a 1v1 4K in round six to get LEV off to a 5-1 lead. NRG managed a decent recovery after a timeout, punishing LEV's slow attack to close the gap to 7-5 in favor of LEV at the half.

Sato mows down everyone in his path to win the bonus.

Over on defense, LEV made quick work of NRG, deploying their signature aggressive set strats for early picks and locking down the sites afterwards for a 13-7 win. Sato continued to dominate, ending with a 23/12/6 KDA, 317 ACS, and a 1.66 rating.

Lotus was the next map, where LEV brought out a new composition with Jett, Raze, and Skye versus NRG's standard Viper-Vyse. LEV found some purchase until brawk 's Odin and mada 's Operator began to shut them down at every turn, leading to an 8-4 defensive half for NRG. A double pistol win followed by a 2v3 from brawk and Ethan in the second half ensured a dominant 13-5 victory for NRG.

The decider map of Pearl pitted LEV's signature Neon-Phoenix against NRG's double-drone. The first half was a tight race. keiko clutched two impressive 1v3s for NRG while spike spearheaded several clinical retakes for LEV, leading to a narrow 7-5 lead for LEV at halftime.

In the final half of the series, kiNgg came alive, acing with a Ghost in the round 14 anti-eco and winning a crucial 1v2 in round 16 to spur his side on to a 13-8 finish. spike topped the scoreboard with 24 kills and 303 ACS; kiNgg trailed close behind with 22 kills and 256 ACS.

With this win, LEV are the second team in the Swiss stage to earn a playoff spot. NRG will have one more chance to join them in a decider versus a to-be-determined opponent on Wednesday, June 10.
