makazze: "Now that I won a tier-one event, I'm writing history"

makazze hopes his success can inspire a new wave of talent from his country.

Not only did Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri earn his first-ever HLTV MVP award with his performances at ESL Pro League S23, he also became the first player from his nation to earn the accolade.

What's more impressive is that the young rifler did so relatively quickly after joining the tier-one scene, having earned the callup to NAVI's main squad less than a year ago.

makazze hopes his success can inspire future players from his country. "To be honest it feels good," he said of being the first of his countrymen to win a HLTV MVP. "I hope more talents will come. I hope they will be motivated."

HLTV caught up with makazze after he won his historic MVP medal to talk about what it means for him and what the ESL Pro League trophy means for his team.

How are you feeling?

I'm feeling good.

And how does it feel to win the HLTV MVP as well?

It's feeling amazing to be honest, I hope many more will come (laughs).

How does it feel to win one so soon after joining NAVI? You haven't been here too long in the grand scheme of things, and it's your first tier-one team, so it must feel good to hit such a level so quickly?

I mean it's feeling good because we were not showing our level in past tournaments, and when we came here and got to the semi-finals, we knew that we should win. We were confident, we just played our game and we won. I'm happy.

I think in playoffs I played a little bit different, I played with more heart and more confidence

Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri

You stepped it up in a big way compared to the group stage online, why do you think you were able to perform so much better, particularly in these last two games?

I think in playoffs I played a little bit different, I played with more heart and more confidence. So maybe it's the playoffs, I don't know?

A bit of a LAN buff maybe?

Yeah, maybe also.

So you're the first person from your country to win a HLTV MVP medal, how does that feel for you personally?

It feels good to be honest because it kind of looks like I'm writing history. Before it was Bad News Eagles, they wrote history going to the Major three times, and now that I won a tier-one event, it's kinda I'm writing history, so I'm feeling good.

You and Krabeni on FUT are leading a new wave from your country. How does that feel?

To be honest it feels good, and I hope more talents will come. I hope they will be motivated.

Looking forward to the rest of NAVI's season, does this tournament win give you some confidence that you can go on and do even better?

I think 100% it will give us more confidence, because we needed a tournament like this to boost us our confidence. I think this is that chance.
