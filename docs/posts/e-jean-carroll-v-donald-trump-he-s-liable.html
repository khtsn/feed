To the Editor:

Re “Trump Is Found Liable for Sexual Abuse and Defamation” (nytimes.com, May 9):

It is an important day, even if it has little effect on the finances of Donald Trump, or on those who will forever believe he walks on water.

The aura of invincibility — his “I could stand in the middle of Fifth Avenue and shoot somebody and wouldn’t lose any voters” mantra that has largely proved accurate through all of his life, even through two impeachments, even with the multiple pending investigations bearing down — has now been badly damaged.

If E. Jean Carroll can convince a jury that this defendant can be held accountable, then who is to suggest that criminal prosecutions are not capable of reaching the same results?

If those who find every aspect of Donald Trump reprehensible were recently buoyed by his criminal indictment in the Stormy Daniels matter, today brings a sense of satisfaction. And more than a little relief that the wheels of justice still turn, even for Mr. Trump.

Robert S. Nussbaum

To the Editor:

Donald Trump has shown his cowardice yet again. With no bone spurs to hide behind, Mr. Trump chose to use social media messages, over which he has total control, rather than to meet his accuser, a quietly dignified 79-year-old woman, in a courtroom where the jurors could size him up.

Actions speak louder than words, and Mr. Trump’s actions show that despite his big talk he was no match for E. Jean Carroll and the truth.

David Baldwin

To the Editor:

The question now becomes will the Republican Party finally repudiate a former president found guilty in a civil case of sexual abuse and defamation by a Manhattan jury of his peers? Or is the Republican Party going to wait for criminal indictments of Donald Trump in Georgia and by a special counsel, Jack Smith, before it decides that our country deserves better?

Does the Republican Party really want to renominate a former president who refused to accept the results of the last election and did not participate in a peaceful transfer of power? Our democracy deserves better!

Jack Nargundkar

To the Editor:

It’s worth noting that the jury that found Donald Trump liable for both sexual abuse and defamation of E. Jean Carroll in just a few hours of deliberation was majority male: six men and three women.

My guess is Mr. Trump’s legal team thought that more men on the jury would benefit him, but apparently not.

Lois Berkowitz
