Spinx: "We believe that one win can change everything"

MOUZ head into ESL Pro League playoffs as favorites to win the entire thing, given the absence of Vitality and the inability of FURIA to qualify for the playoffs. Many feel it's a watershed moment for MOUZ, the chance for them to secure an import arena LAN win.

"Of course since Vitality isn't in the tournament maybe you have a better chance, but still, in this level of Counter-Strike, we could lose to FUT tomorrow," Lotan "⁠Spinx⁠" Giladi told HLTV, keeping a level head despite the context surrounding his team's upcoming playoff run.

He does, however, appreciate the importance of winning an event. "This one win will give us a step ahead more than almost anything, and it will help us a lot for the next tournaments."

Spinx also spoke with HLTV about the upcoming matchup with FUT, the state of the scene and his team's goals for the rest of the year.

Listen to the interview here.

Let's look at the playoffs. You've got FUT coming up, they gave you a pretty good game in Cluj. How are you feeling about that matchup?

They're a young team, a young upcoming team. They have pretty good individuals, they're shooting good. We all can see the love they have for the game, it's really nice. We are definitely the favorites to win the game, but we don't underestimate them. They're a pretty good team, so we'll see how it goes. But as I said, we're still the heavy favorites.

In general, looking at these playoffs, we've got no Vitality in attendance. That must get you feeling like its got to be your trophy, right?

(laughs) Of course, there are people talking in the media and you feel the vibe that since Vitality is not here, you maybe win this one. Which there is some truth to it of course, we lost to Vitality so many times, but also in Cluj we lost to PARIVISION. Maybe we would lose to Vitality in the grand final, but we didn't even make it there.

Of course since Vitality isn't in the tournament maybe you have a better chance, but still, in this level of Counter-Strike, we could lose to FUT tomorrow. We could lose to Spirit in the grand final, we can lose to any team. It's part of CS, so we just need to keep focused and play 100%, and if we manage to do that we have a good chance. But just because Vitality is not here doesn't mean, 'Ah, MOUZ will win the trophy.' We'll see.

We can all agree that right now, Vitality is a bit ahead of everyone else, and in Counter-Strike, a bit ahead is a lot

Is there any added pressure because Vitality aren't here and it is a little bit of a better chance to win the trophy? Does that add a little bit of pressure for the team?

Hmm... maybe. We didn't talk about it. I can't speak for others, but I don't think about it myself because even if Vitality is not here, there are also other great teams. We lost to Spirit in the grand final of Cologne, so if we play them in the grand final of ESL Pro League, what's the difference, you know? But the reason is I think the media is getting this vibe of something. We didn't speak about it as a team so I can't speak for others, but it is flying around.

Like you were saying, you can lose to any team, there are lots of good teams out there in the scene. Does it feel to you like there is Vitality, and then a big group of good teams under them who, if they have a good day, they can beat each other or lose to each other?

Yeah, I think it's the same for everyone. We can all agree that right now, Vitality is a bit ahead of everyone else, and in Counter-Strike, a bit ahead is a lot. When we looked at the games against them, we can always talk about the details. 'Ah, we fucked up this round, maybe if we won it it's a different game,' but Counter-Strike is a lot about the details and against Vitality you need to minimize them as much as possible. They're also such a good team that they make you make mistakes and this kind of stuff. So I do agree that right now, we have a lot of good teams in the scene, but Vitality is still a bit in front against any team.

The loss in Cluj-Napoca against PARIVISION was really hard. We had our chance there to change the storyline a bit, and again after so many times, we didn't

Looking at the year so far for MOUZ, obviously it's been the same old MOUZ, top fours, always in those semis (Spinx laughs), but not able to get over the line. How do you feel about the team's year so far?

This year so far, the loss in Cluj-Napoca against PARIVISION was really hard. We had our chance there to change the storyline a bit, and again after so many times, we didn't. It's really hard mentally, for me, for the players, for everyone. To keep making it to the semis, or even the finals or whatever, and keep losing, being so close, yet so far... it's not easy. But it is a sports game, someone needs to do it, and we keep grinding. We're still in every game, it doesn't matter how hard it is that we fucked up again. The next day we still look at it, we try to think what we can do better. It's sport, it's life. It's not always easy.

Do you think that one win would make a huge difference? If you did get over the line in that one tournament, do you think it could be like flipping a switch for MOUZ, and suddenly the floodgates open?

Definitely. We believe that one win can change everything, confidence-wise, but mostly mentality-wise, that we can do it, we did it. This one win will give us a step ahead more than almost anything, and it will help us a lot for the next tournaments. Either it's a win in ESL Pro League, studio, arena, I don't think there is much of a difference for us, but just a win. But we need it to be an important tournament, if it's half-and-half, it's not the best. Just an important win against the best teams will help us a lot.

We believe that one win can change everything, confidence-wise, but mostly mentality-wise, that we can do it

For this year overall for MOUZ, when you talk about the goals, is it just to win trophies? Is there not really anything else?

Yes and no. Of course, no matter what tournament we go to, if we lose in the finals — even if we go to a Major or whatever big tournament it is — against any team, against Vitality, Spirit, any team we play, it will be a disappointment because we've been here, we've done it, we want to move on.

At the same time, every tournament we go to, you can't just think to yourself, 'Okay, I'm going for the trophy.' There is still a process to do, so the first goal is to qualify for the playoffs, and then you have each game at a time, you don't know the bracket. Maybe you don't have the best day, the other team is insane, or maybe you have a really good day but the other team also has a really good day. It's Counter-Strike, after all.

So first of all, the goal is to make it to the playoffs, then each game at a time. But I can tell you that every tournament that we are falling short, semis, third-place deciders, grand final, whatever it is, we are leaving the tournament pretty sad.
