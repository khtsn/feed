BIT: "Somehow, we always make history with Vitality"

9z pulled off an immense upset on the second day of Stage 3 at the IEM Cologne Major, beating Vitality in a three-map series to advance to the 2-0 pool and sit just one match away from making the playoffs.

The Hispanic-American team dominated domestically in the early part of the year, and eliminated MOUZ in their first Big Event outing at BLAST Open Rotterdam before bowing out with one-sided losses to Vitality and Spirit. The team bounced back by reaching the quarter-finals at PGL Astana, where Bruno "⁠BIT⁠" Fukuda Lima said the losses in Rotterdam were a valuable learning lesson, and those teachings were on display as 9z got their revenge on the reigning Major champions in Germany.

"This shows that our work is paying off," BIT told HLTV after the win. "This is very important in Counter-Strike, to understand that you can win, and right now, I think we all know we can win against any team, that's why we beat Vitality. That's very important."

The Brazilian coach was also happy to add another big win for 9z over Vitality, having beaten the team previously at IEM Dallas 2024 to advance straight to the semis and in the Round of 16 in BLAST Spring Showdown 2021.

Listen to the interview here.

Congratulations, a big win over Vitality, and now you're 2-0. Just tell me about your feelings after that match, you told me in Astana that you learned a lot from that Vitality game you played in Rotterdam, and it kind of showed here.

Every match that we play, we learn something. The match against Spirit was a big lesson for us, especially because we didn't show up at all. So it's a very special moment.

I think 9z, in general, have a good history against Vitality. We beat them when I played, then the guys beat them in Dallas. Somehow, we always make history with Vitality, so that's another history we are making here. Yeah, super happy, but we have to keep working because in the end, what matters is the spot in the playoffs.

Back when you played them in Rotterdam, it was really one-sided for them, and you said that you learned a lot [from it]. What did you bring into this match specifically? Because obviously different maps this time around. So what was your approach? How did it change?

I think there's not a big change. I think just game by game, we kind of learned something. Maybe not a huge change, not the way we play, just maybe mental, maybe how we approach the game, maybe how we see them. There's a lot of small stuff that we can improve.

On Inferno, we had our chances, and there are some rounds, like small details matter a lot in this kind of match. Like maybe throwing two smokes at the same time in the same place, and then you lose one smoke for the after-plant, and all this kind of stuff. I think when you start winning against this team and you feel like you can win, maybe you're going to be more relaxed with comms. You're not going to be stressed to make a decision; you're going to be calm when talking. I think everything counts when you're playing the best team in the world.

A lot of times we're maybe one step ahead sometimes, and that's important to play against those guys, to have courage

As you mentioned, some of those situations do get stressful. The game did get close in the end. Talk to me about the mentality from you guys to close it out and just getting this win.

Everyone played very good individually; as a team, we played very well. We are helping each other a lot, we are proposing [ideas], on the T side we're not afraid to take the risk. I think a lot of times we're maybe one step ahead sometimes, and that's important to play against those guys, to have courage. You're trying to play the game, not just reacting to what they do and stuff like this. It was a good day for us (smiles).

And a pretty good event from you overall so far. I mean, you went through the last stage, now you're 2-0 here, one game away from the playoffs. What would making it to the arena mean to you? It's been quite the little trajectory upward for you recently.

This was a huge victory for us. In the end, what matters is the [playoff] spot. Doesn't matter if we go 3-1, 3-0, 3-2. Our goal is to go to the playoffs and try our best afterward. So we have to keep working hard, keep studying, keep talking, maybe try to find something new.

There are a lot of things we can do now, but we need to keep playing, we need to do better on the stage if we got to the stage because in Astana we lost in the first match. There are a lot of things we can talk about (laughs), but in the end, what matters is if we get this spot or not.

How important was playing that match on the stage in Astana for you to potentially prepare for one here? How much has that helped you before such a potentially big appearance?

There are some things in Counter-Strike that you cannot learn by talking [about it], you have to live the moment. You have to play on the stage to maybe be a little bit better the next time. There are a lot of situations in Counter-Strike that you have to live to get better for the next time. In Astana, we played magic, and they played very good. They also had a very good tournament at Stake, but everything helps for the next time we're going to be in the stage. Hopefully, it's going to be at the Major.

This is very important in Counter-Strike, to understand that you can win, and right now, I think we all know we can win against any team, that's why we beat Vitality

Last question, in Astana at the start of the event, you told me that because you didn't get to play a lot of these big events, you were using that as a test to see what your level was against these good teams at these big events. How do you feel about that now? Where do you stand?

I mean, game by game, every time we win against any team — it could be MOUZ, it could be Falcons, it could be PARIVISION, it could be HEROIC — every time it shows that we can play at this level. This shows that our work is paying off. This is very important in Counter-Strike, to understand that you can win, and right now, I think we all know we can win against any team, that's why we beat Vitality. That's very important.
