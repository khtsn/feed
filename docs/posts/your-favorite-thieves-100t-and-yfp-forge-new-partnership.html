"Under the partnership, 100 Thieves will lend its brand, creative direction, and fans to amplify the roster throughout Stage 3, while YFP continues to lead the team's day-to-day competition and player development," 100T's press release shared. "The two organizations will collaborate on co-branded creative, social amplification, and content built around the players, giving the roster a larger stage and connecting 100 Thieves' community to the next generation of NA VALORANT talent."

YFP has been competing in Valorant since the game's earliest days in 2021, and has steadily grown in stature throughout the years. In 2024, the team finished second in Americas Stage 3, and more recently finished 5th-6th in Stage 2 earlier this year.
