mezii: "apEX is used to being the villain, so we know what to expect coming into this arena against FURIA"

Grand Slam-chasing Vitality have shown a rare sign of weakness in Rio, at least by their lofty standards, after losing a map to G2 and missing out on direct semi-final qualification after a loss against Falcons.

Once the playoffs rolled around, Vitality were back to their best. They comfortably dispatched No. 2 team in the world Natus Vincere by losing a total of 10 rounds across the series, meaning just two games stand between them and the Grand Slam.

They are set to meet FURIA in the semi-final, who are sure to receive loud support from the crowd, especially after Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo's retirement announcement yesterday, but William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman says Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire has taught them to deal with the boos.

"He's a captain who leads by example and we're kinda used to it now, we're used to the boos in the crowd, we're used to crowds being against us," he said.

mezii also spoke about the game against Natus Vincere and the loss to Falcons earlier in the tournament.
