BLG's clean sweep is deceiving, as the team went into the final stretches of regulation on both Sunset and Icebox. The former boiled down to a more consistent effort from Bilibili who, despite not accruing huge round streaks, slowly inched their way to a 13-9 win.

Despite the similar 13-7 scoreline, Icebox played out differently as the scoreline was tied at 7-7 shortly after switching sides. Two rounds later, an ace from Yosemite at the back of the A site unlocked the game for his side, allowing Bilibili to chain six rounds on their way to victory.

Yosemite's ace was far from the only stat making him stand out, as the flex player capped at 328 ACS on Icebox, the highest of the match and the highest of the map by over 100. Teammate whzy had a similar performance on Sunset, albeit while accruing two more kills at 23 and far fewer assists, scoring four on Sunset to Yosemite's 10 on Icebox.

Who cares if it's against an eco?

With this win, Bilibili maintain their flawless record of Champions attendance, and are the only team to do so alongside EDward Gaming.

BLG can finally breathe a sigh of relief this year. After Champions and reaching the VCT last year, the team's road has been full of bumps, as the squad was unable to reach playoffs in both Kickoff and Stage 1. In Stage 2, the team has gone on a near-flawless run.

The only pebble in their shoe in the second half of the season is EDward Gaming, who bested them 2-0 in a tight-knit affair. And that's exactly who Bilibili are facing tomorrow in the upper semifinals.

Historically, BLG have always been Robin to EDG's Batman. Only twice in seven encounters — three Bo5s — have the former managed to win a map against the latter, and never have they secured a full victory. But there's always been something on the line: elimination, Champions qualification, a VCT slot.

Now that they're only fighting for regional gold, who knows what could happen.
