lauNX ominous about FUT's future: "Many things can happen"

Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea briefly stopped by HLTV's microphone moments after FUT were eliminated from the IEM Cologne Major with a 2-3 record in Stage 3 at the hands of BetBoom.

The team struggled in the deciding match after making the climb back from 0-2, something the Romanian rifler attributed to the pressure. "It's not a good feeling, especially because we felt very confident in this veto, we felt we had it, to be honest, but I don't know. It was too much pressure and we couldn't handle it," he said.

When asked about the season as a whole, lauNX reflected positively on his time with the team, during which FUT became a regular fixture at tier-one and grabbed a PGL Bucharest title.

I don't know how these guys will be in the next two or three Majors, but for sure some of them will lift the trophy

"I'm not feeling that sad. What we did, I know in the back of my head that it's very crazy what we achieved in a year together. I'm playing together with them for a year, and we achieved what others can't do in five years," the 21-year-old went on.

But heading into the off-season, an ominous cloud hangs over FUT, whose players have consistently been touted as some of the biggest talents in the scene and as being in line for an upgrade for some of the big dogs.

Last time around, they withstood the pressure from the bigger organizations, but that may change heading into the upcoming break. "Many things can happen, especially when the big guys are coming with crazy offers," lauNX commented.

"I have no comment because then if I say something and then the opposite happens, I might get backlash for it, so I don't wanna say anything."

Commiserations. Tell me your first reaction to being out of the Major.

It's tough. It's bad. It's weird. I don't know how to explain it, to be honest. It's not a good feeling, especially because we felt very confident in this veto, we felt we had it, to be honest, but I don't know. It was too much pressure and we couldn't handle it.

Can you tell me about that, because even from the comms, the coach had to rally you guys up on the first map when you were down badly early on. What was the problem?

Honestly, I don't have a clear answer. There are so many mixed feelings right now. Again, I think it's because of the pressure. We played to win, and I think that's the problem. When you play to win, that's the moment you make a lot of mistakes, you crumble under the pressure and you just lose it.

You put yourselves in a difficult position after going 0-2 to do the climb back. How would you evaluate this whole run?

I think for such a young team we did a great job. No one can take it away from us. We grinded all the way from the slums of VRS through tournaments, through local LANs. We went to tier-one, we even won in PGL.

I think what we did here, it's still great. Of course, it's sad we didn't make it to the playoffs, but in the end we need to be realistic. We're such a young team and 10 years ago you couldn't even imagine it like this, that so many young kids could just come up and take over the playoffs in the Major. So I think we did good, but again, it hurts, because we could have made the playoffs, but from here I think we can only go up.

How do you feel in retrospect compared to the whole rest of season? I think a lot of people expected you to be in the playoffs. Does it feel like a setback heading into the off-season?

I don't think we can call it a setback because I think for us as a team, we did what we wanted to do. We went into the Major and we went into Stage 3 flawless. We almost made it to the playoffs and it's great.

I don't know, at least I'm not feeling that sad. What we did, I know in the back of my head that it's very crazy what we achieved in a year together. I'm playing together with them for a year, and we achieved what others can't do in five years.

Of course it's a bit sad and tough, but I don't think we should be depressed about it or anything, because for them it's their first Major and they're one inch away from making it into the playoffs. I don't know how these guys will be in the next two or three Majors, but for sure some of them will lift the trophy. Pretty sure.

At the beginning of the season you were talking about being able to withstand the pressure from other organizations, from the big teams coming to take your players. How do you feel about the future of the team past this off-season?

I mean, I don't know. We'll have to see because now it's tough to say. Many things can happen, especially when the big guys are coming with crazy offers. I don't know what's gonna happen.

I have no comment because then if I say something and then the opposite happens, I might get backlash for it, so I don't wanna say anything.
