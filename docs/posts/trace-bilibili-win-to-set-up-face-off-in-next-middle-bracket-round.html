The first map was Split, a first for both teams in this event. Both teams opted into double-Duelist compositions, with Trace going for Waylay and Neon and JDG going for a more standard Raze and Yoru.

Trace opened with a 4-0 start on their attack side, with Kai on Neon leaning into Judge purchases for his entry kills. JDG recovered, stringing together three rounds in a row to get back to 4-3, eventually taking the half to 7-5 in favor of Trace off the back of a low buy win in round 10.

JDG won the second pistol, but Trace won the eco round immediately after and utilized the economic advantage to extend their lead to 9-6 and then 12-6. JDG swung back in with overwhelmingly fast site hits, bringing the score all the way back to 12-11, but Trace managed to close out the map in regulation and took Split 13-11.
