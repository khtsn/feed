tN1R on reason for his uptick in form: "It's really a lot of work on my mentality"

Spirit swept Falcons 3-0 in the grand final of PGL Astana to lift their first trophy in exactly nine months, with Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets walking away with the MVP medal after averaging a 1.61 rating — among the top four of his career-best LANs.

But it was not just the donk show in Kazakhstan, and the elevation of Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich since IEM Rio has played a key part in the team living up to what was expected after the Belarusian star moved over from HEROIC late last year.

"In Rio, I got a lot of energy and a lot of boost from my team, and also from the arena," tN1R told HLTV after lifting the trophy in Astana. "I got this confidence, and after this, I'm just trying to keep it and work on the stuff."

Working with the team's sports psychologist has helped that mentality immensely, and helped tN1R remain refreshed.

"Always when I have some thoughts or something like that, I can talk with him about all this stuff," he said. "And then I'm just refreshing my mind, and it's easier for me to work and communicate with my teammates and stuff like that."

Listen to the interview here.

tN1R, congratulations, your first trophy in Spirit and a big moment for you. What's going through your mind right now after that game?

Right now, I have only emotions, I don't have some thoughts or something like that. I'm really happy because we did a very great job, and I really am very respectful for everyone because everyone helped me to improve myself. It's very important to win a trophy after such a long period before the Major.

Obviously it was a rough period at the start of the team for you, but you've been in really good form recently. Tell me about what has changed for you, why are you in such good form now?

To be honest, I think it's really a lot of work on my mentality. About how I'm feeling on the server or something like that because in Rio, I got a lot of energy and a lot of boost from my team, and also from the arena. I got this confidence, and after this, I'm just trying to keep it and work on the stuff.

In this tournament, you went 3-0 in the Swiss stage, but a couple of games were close. Once you reached the playoffs, you were kind of blowing everyone out until this final. Walk me through that, how it felt to power up throughout the tournament.

I think it's also working like that when you come to the tournament, you need some games to improve your confidence in the tournament and during a competitive match. Of course, in the group stage, you can play a lot of matches because you [need] to win three matches, and of course you have a lot of time to work on your stuff also, like fix some mistakes or something like that. And you are getting more confidence step by step.

I don't know, he's just god (smiles)

How about this final? It started out really close on the first map, and then after that you kind of took control. Talk to me about that and why it was it pretty easy for you after that initial start.

If we're talking about the first map, we had a very bad start in the first half. Me and Danya donk, we really had a very bad start. But after the pistol round when I was just hiding and kill a lot of people, I'm just starting to feel my confidence. Then, Danya also started [getting] frags, and after that we just came back good. And also, all my teammates did a great job (laughs). But in general, yeah, sometimes when you have a very bad start, it really looks like we don't control our map, you know?

But if we're talking about the second map, Mirage, we knew they would pick it and we are ready to play it against them because they are really a very new team and don't have a lot of stuff right now. And the third map, we were also in full control of the map, but maybe it's a little bit about we want to finish the game faster, so we started trolling anti-force rounds. But it's fine, we just won, and that's it.

You spoke earlier about your mentality, how that's changed and helped you come back a little bit. You're also working with a different sports psychologist in the team, I saw in one of your vlogs, so how much has he been helping you specifically?

He also has a good part in working with my mentality because always when I have some thoughts or something like that, I can talk with him about all this stuff. And then I'm just refreshing my mind, and it's easier for me to work and communicate with my teammates and stuff like that.

Obviously donk got MVP on stage, he had a crazy tournament. What was it like playing with him here, how come he elevated so much?

I don't know, he's just god (smiles).

As we leave Astana, we're headed towards the Major. How much did this trophy help you gain confidence for that with how good Vitality has been doing recently?

This trophy will give a lot of confidence for everyone, and also we will start the Major from the second stage. We will be more warmed up for the third stage.
