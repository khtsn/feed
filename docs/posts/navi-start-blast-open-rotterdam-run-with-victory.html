NAVI start BLAST Open Rotterdam run with victory

The EPL champions got off to a good start at the BLAST studio with a 2-1 win over B8.

Aleksib had clutch moments on Ancient, but NAVI couldn't make it a clean 2-0

Natus Vincere, fresh off of lifting the trophy at ESL Pro League Season 23 in Stockholm, have kicked off their BLAST Open Rotterdam journey with a 2-1 victory over B8.

The series kicked off on B8's map pick, and the Ukrainian squad got off to a flying start by winning the first pistol round and getting off to a 0-3 start on the attack, but Natus Vincere didn't let it get away and settled once the gun rounds came about to take a 7-5 lead at the half.

B8 once again won the pistol round to regain the lead after four straight rounds, 7-9 but fell off as Natus Vincere pressed with guns in play and kept the Ukrainians under constant economic pressure to go on and win it out 13-9.

B8's 16-year-old Daniil "⁠s1zzi⁠" Vinnyk struggled in the first map, tallying only one AWP kill and bottoming out at 0.51 rating with 11 total kills and -9.70% round swing.

The series went to Ancient, where Natus Vincere had it in their hands to close it out 2-0 on their pick, but failing to double up after winning the pistol round put them behind 1-3 early on.

A four-kill 1v2 clutch by Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan put Natus Vincere back ahead in the ninth round, but the two teams went to the half at a stalemate.

Artem "⁠kensizor⁠" Kapran downed three with glocks to put B8 ahead, and they kept a lead all the way to 12-9, but failed to win out after a crucial 1v2 afterplant by Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen.

The Finnish in-game leader had another big 1v2 in the first set of overtime, but it took two for the two teams to break the tie as B8 edged past Natus Vincere with a Andrii "⁠npl⁠" Kukharskyi triple to hit map point and a Alexey "⁠alex666⁠" Yarmoshchuk 1v2 clutch in the final round, making it 19-17.

s1zzi, who had struggled hard on Mirage, turned it around on Ancient, and was in the trio that made it happen for B8 on Ancient with a 1.26 rating.

The series ended in Dust2, the decider map, and B8 once again dominated the pistol rounds, winning both of them, but outside of that they had little to show for as Natus Vincere got off to a large lead on the attack, 8-4, and didn't let up after the switch, winning five out of six gun rounds to secure the series with a 13-7 scoreline.
