ESIC issues interim suspension to jmqa

The Esports Integirty Commission (ESIC) has issued an interim suspension to Savelii "⁠jmqa⁠" Bragin pending its investigation into "suspected breaches of the ESIC Anti-Corruption Code arising from communications attributed to Mr Bragin."

The communications in question stem from a screenshot shared on X by Eclipse player Rasmus "⁠Pihl⁠" Pihl, which shows the Dane being approached by user jmqa6479 on Discord with the offer to throw matches.

"The available material includes direct communications in which Mr Bragin appears to solicit or encourage another Participant to engage in conduct inconsistent with the principles of fair competition," ESIC's statement reads.

The announcement stresses that the suspension is a "protective measure and does not constitute a final determination of guilt."

In the meantime, ESIC has launched the investigation "requiring the production of communications, financial records and other materials relevant to the investigation."

jmqa is best known for his time with Quantum Bellator Fire, who made a surprise run to the ELEAGUE Major 2018 quarter-finals with a lineup that also featured Kirill "⁠Boombl4⁠" Mikhailov.

He has been teamless since 2021, and his last match on record on HLTV is from September 2024.
