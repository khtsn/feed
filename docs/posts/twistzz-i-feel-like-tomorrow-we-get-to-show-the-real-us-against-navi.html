Twistzz: "I feel like tomorrow we get to show the real us against [NAVI]"

FaZe will get the chance to rematch Natus Vincere after upsetting FURIA in the group stage and beating G2 in the quarter-finals when they take to the Dickies Arena stage for the second time on Saturday.

"NAVI is a very tough opponent, but when we played them the first time, I felt like we were nervous," Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken told HLTV after defeating G2. "We had some first-event jitters."

This victory was also big for the Canadian captain because he got it in front of the Texan crowd. "I haven't played in front of a North American crowd in three years," he added, "So... Yeah, it's a massive motivation."

Listen to the interview here.

Winning the pistols and clutches on Dust2 got you a huge win in the first map. Run me a bit through that, getting ahead in the series, did that spark something in you guys?

Yeah, we felt pretty prepared for Dust2, but we got caught off-guard by their early gun rounds and their decision to push Top Mid and be very aggressive. A 6-6 half is the most we could have hoped for, even with the pistol. Three gun rounds is okay.

It's very important to get an early lead in the series. Dust2 was our pick, and it's important to win it. A lot of clutches and situations went our way, but I think that the teamwork so far has been brilliant, and I'm proud to see it.

I got a bit emotional after the FURIA win, realizing that we're getting ur first crowd in America

You got Mirage in overtime, frozen had a huge map, and all of you guys had big moments, the OGs, as you called them. How was it to get that very FaZe victory?

I'm not going to lie, it was looking a bit dire. I felt like people started to feel the pressure a bit, and started to get more quiet. We just had to push really hard, start the rounds with good openings, and towards the end of the half I kind of figured them out. They were leaving Ramp open quite often, and we were using that to gather information for all of our rounds.

And yeah, it's frozen. I mean, I feel like he should always be in the conversation for being one of the best players in the world. David [frozen] is super important for us, and it makes me super happy to see him have big games like this. Mirage was so exciting. You know, as a competitor, these are the most fun games to play.

And you did it here in front of the American crowd cheering for you guys. Was it nice to have that?

Yeah, of course. It was such a motivation. I got a bit emotional after the FURIA win, realizing that we're getting our first crowd in America. I haven't played in front of a North American crowd in three years, so... Yeah, it's a massive motivation.

Up next is NAVI. You opened against them, and they got the 2-0 win. Do you have a bit more magic left in the tank for tomorrow?

I'm happy with the two upsets we've gotten so far, I'm not going to lie, but I really believe we can make a push further into this event, further into the grand final.

NAVI is a very tough opponent, but when we played them the first time, I felt like we were nervous, we had some first event jitters, and we were so excited to show what we've been working on... Now I feel like tomorrow we get to show the real us against them.

I'm getting the same communication and the same decision-making, so props to him

When you say the real us, what do you mean exactly?

The Counter-Strike we played against FURIA and G2. We didn't really get to do that against NAVI because we were nervous to make the moves and do the right things that we talked about in practice; that's what I mean.

It's been a while since you've been on a stage, since Budapest. How was it to get back up there in front of a crowd?

It's the most exciting environment, you know? This is what FaZe fans live for, and this is what the players live for. You better bet that frozen, broky, and I, this is where we want to be every event.

We want to play in front of a crowd, that's where the team gets elevated, and Kuba [jcobbb] deserves more, he deserves to see more of the stage, and it was Neityu's first time in a big arena. What an excellent performance by him.

How did you see him do in his first [stage] match?

I didn't feel like I was getting a different version of him. I feel like I'm getting the same communication and the same decision-making, so props to him.
