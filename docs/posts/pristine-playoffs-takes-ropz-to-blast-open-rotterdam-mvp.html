Pristine playoffs takes ropz to BLAST Open Rotterdam MVP

Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool has earned the BLAST Open Rotterdam HLTV x 1XBET Most Valuable Player (MVP) award, becoming the first-ever Vitality player not named Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut to win the medal.

It was from behind, too, after he took a back seat for the first two group stage matches with ZywOo and then Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan in sensational form.

But in the playoffs, there was no question who added the most value to Vitality's trophy lift. The Estonian was the clear player of the map on three occasions, tied William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman on map two of the grand final for a fourth peak, and his fifth-best playoff map was still a 1.28 rating with a 97.0 ADR first half.

Overall, ropz averaged a 1.54 rating in the arena, with 1.20 kills per round win. That compares to 1.28 for both ZywOo (0.97 KPRW) and flameZ (0.95 KPRW).

Even when you add in the seeding match against PARIVISION, ropz remains clearly top of his team at a 1.42. Against top 10 opposition, ropz and ZywOo both had six EVP+ maps, with flameZ on five, but ropz's peaks all came deeper into the tournament. That explains how it was possible for ropz to leapfrog the still superlative performances of flameZ and ZywOo.

He was also helped by the fact that ZywOo had no player of the map showings in the arena. flameZ led the race before the final, not ZywOo, but ended Championship Sunday with just a 1.01 rating and a negative K-D. That left a vacuum, which ropz soared into with a carry performance that took his team over the line.

ropz has come close before, notably in StarLadder Budapest, but this time he was impactful in each and every playoff map to ensure the medal went home with him. It's the lurker's sixth career medal, first since CAC 2023, and a frightening reminder of his ability to deliver under the lights time and time again.
