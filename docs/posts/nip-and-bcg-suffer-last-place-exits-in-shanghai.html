Ninjas in Pyjamas's disappointing season continued with a loss at the hands of Lynn Vision, the two teams contesting an incredibly scrappy series that was low on quality in a macro sense, but featured some strong individual plays from both teams.

Those individual plays helped Ninjas in Pyjamas overcome a very poor start on Dust2, Artem "⁠cairne⁠" Mushynskyi bagging an ace clutch before Marco "⁠Snappi⁠" Pfeiffer pulled off a 1v3, only for Lynn Vision to seal the deal on their T side thanks to Lizhi "⁠Starry⁠" Ye's impact.

Nuke was an even more chaotic map, Lynn Vision often finding themselves lost in the mid-round and unable to read what was happening on the map. As a result they opted for a very brawly approach, but Ninjas in Pyjamas weathered the storm to send the series to a decider. In said decider Ninjas in Pyjamas found themselves completely outclassed on Ancient, Lynn Vision's aggressive and fast-paced approach running them over with ease.

Whilst Ninjas in Pyjamas can point to the fact they are bedding in a new player in the form of Martin "⁠stavn⁠" Lund, questions will be asked in the player break if they finish their season with another poor showing at Stake Ranked Episode 2.

Over on the B stream, paiN ran away to an 11-3 lead early on on Dust2, but the scoreline could have been even more one-sided if not for a couple of Oleksandr "⁠s1mple⁠" Kostyliev hero rounds. While BC.Game got a five-round streak near the end of the map to make the scoreline appear closer, paiN's win was never in doubt.

Anubis was an equally straightforward win for paiN. BC.Game kept it close in the early rounds thanks to a few force-buy victories, including one courtesy of a 3k by s1mple's unarmored Deagle, before going completely missing after taking a 4-3 lead.

The Brazilians won ten rounds in a row to secure a 13-4 win and stay alive at the tournament, with their next test coming against TYLOO later on Thursday.
