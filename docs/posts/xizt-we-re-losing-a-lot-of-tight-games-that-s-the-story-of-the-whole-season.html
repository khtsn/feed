Xizt: "We're losing a lot of tight games — that's the story of the whole season"

Ninjas in Pyjamas are out of the running at Stake Ranked Episode 1 after a lower-bracket loss to HEROIC, and thus their Major run has come to a conclusion ahead of the April 6 cut-off.

We spoke to head coach Richard "⁠Xizt⁠" Landström, to hear his thoughts after the knock-out blow in Barcelona, and what the takeaways can be for a young team still in the process of adjusting to a higher level of play.

You can listen to the interview, here.

Tough loss against Heroic in the lower bracket. End of the lower race for you guys, sadly. Give me the good, the bad, and the ugly.

The good is that I feel like we have really strong players. We're lacking a lot of experience, so the bad is that we're playing really badly in the clutch situations. In advantages, we have a tendency to overpeek a lot, and we're losing a lot of tight games — that's the story of the whole season, basically.

I expected way more this season. At the start of the season we had a really good situation with Krakow, and we managed to qualify for BLAST Rotterdam. I feel like we had good strategy, but just couldn't close out games when it mattered the most.

You bring up overpeeking and overaggresion, which is something Snappi brought up when you exited Rotterdam. What can you do to get more discipline from your younger guys, especially?

It comes down to them fixing it, right? We're on their asses in practice, we talk about this during every review, right? Your actions will have consequences, but sadly, we'll have to learn it the hard way. We just need to be better when it comes to it, that's it.

Do you think something as heartbreaking as falling just short of a Major can be one of those moments to learn and say, 'Hey, look at what happened because of the decisions you made?'

Hopefully, yes. I do think we're lacking experience in the team. We basically have three guys who haven't played that much tier-one, so if we want to have the chance of breaking into tier-one next season, these are the things we have to fix. We can win against Gentle Mates while making these mistakes, but a good team like PARIVISION at BLAST will punish us every time.

Then, of course, playing against GamerLegion and HEROIC, who I assume have less pressure because they were basically qualified before our game... it's tough.

It'll sting for a little while, but you'll eventually put it behind you, and the season keeps going. How are you going to face what's coming from now to the summer?

It's time to take a few steps back and look at the overview. We have some time to work on the team, basically, to reflect a little bit, and make a strategy on how to improve the team. That's what we have to do now.
