sAw: "[SunPayus] needs to find his form again; that comes from not overthinking"

sAw addressed SunPayus' up-and-down performances at the Cologne Major

After a narrow loss to Falcons to open up Stage 3, G2 came on the winning side of another close series against FUT, taking revenge on the young squad for the loss earlier in the tournament.

HLTV spoke to Eetu "⁠sAw⁠" Saha shortly after the match to ask him about the approach to such a matchup against a team that is both a familiar opponent and one well-known for its aggressiveness and unpredictability.

"Knowing their style, they're not gonna exactly let you do what you have planned, so it's gonna go into reactions and XvX situations, which is always gonna be tough," sAw said. "It's about switching some things while keeping the balance of doing the things we are comfortable with."

He's not 100% yet; I think he was way better in the later days of HEROIC and first days of Liquid, so there's still potential there

The Finnish coach also addressed the criticism towards Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García, who dipped deep into the red against Falcons in the opening round and in their earlier FUT clash, admitting the Spaniard has had a tough time so far in Cologne.

"It sucks, it sucks for him, but in the end I think he is in the right positions most of the time," sAw conceded. "It comes down to playing with your comfort, having confidence in yourself, and in the end it's hitting your shots. He needs to find his form again, like what he had these last couple of events before coming here, but I think that comes from not overthinking in the end."

It's not the first time you're playing FUT here and another close best-of-three after a close loss last time around. How do you approach such a matchup, having played them so recently, and especially with them being such a dynamic team?

You don't really do it differently compared to other teams, but of course you know their style, and when you have this recent matchup, it puts a bit of a twist into everything. It starts with the map pool — this time they picked Ancient instead of Anubis and Mirage being the last map.

Some changes here and there, mind games happening. Knowing their style being this aggressive team, they're not gonna exactly let you do what you have planned, so it's gonna go into reactions and XvX situations, which is always gonna be tough.

They're a good, young team with good firepower, and a tough matchup for many teams it looks like. I'm happy we came on the winning side and got our revenge this time because it's been close matches.

How do you approach it from the prep side? You're talking about that it's more about reactions, situations... Is it difficult to prepare for those situations?

It's about switching some things, trying to see what we need to change without changing too much, keeping the balance of doing the things we are comfortable with, but then at the same time trying to switch up some small things, where we are trying to end on the map.

Also trying to control their reactions, in a way. People will have tendencies in reactions, some small things are repeating here and there, so trying to play against that, and then of course not overthinking it. Let's take Mirage as an example, you can't be holding for B pushes, underground pushes, A pushes and Ramp pushes and top Mid pushes, everything at once, and then trying to finish the rounds together as a team.

It's about deciding something, making the decisions, being decisive individually, and being focused on your crosshair in a way that you're ready for these pushes as well. You can't get sloppy, you can't be looking at your radar when there's people pushing at you. It's simple things like that.

The overview is pretty much the same as most teams. You're trying to make sure you're good at the first timings, second timings, late-rounds, everything, that stays mostly the same.

SunPayus has been catching quite a bit of flak. He had quite a big uptick in performance towards the beginning of the year and now he's gone back to being up and down. What is that down to?

It's hard to say because maybe it was even the Animgraph update, maybe something boosted AWPers a bit, or he found a bit more form and comfort in the team.

For sure, this has been a hard event for him at the Major and it sucks, it sucks for him, but in the end I think he is in the right positions most of the time. It's not like he's on the wrong side on the maps when the other team is hitting the map, so I think it comes down playing with your comfort, having confidence in yourself, and then in the end it's hitting your shots. It comes down to that. I don't think there should be any overthinking about it.

Of course it's tough when you have high-pressure matches coming, also now it's this Swiss stage with BO3s every day, so it's gonna drain your energy. In the end, I think he needs to find his form again, like what he had these last couple of events before coming here, but I think that comes from not overthinking in the end.

Tell me about NertZ as well. We spoke to you a couple of months ago when you brought him in, and at the time you were talking about it bringing some sort of an upgrade in terms of firepower, but you weren't sure how much of a difference it would be. What do you think about how his development has gone?

It's been good overall. We understood bringing him mid-season that it was gonna be tough with practice and him learning everything we have. Of course in firepower, sure, an upgrade, or I don't even know, because Malbs also has really good firepower, but more like in being vocal and understanding the game and calling for the team from these active positions that he has, both T and CT side.

That's the main factor where he has been improving, calling-wise, understanding what rounds we have, how we can use anti-strat, everything. That's been the process he's been learning. I think he's doing a really good job, to be honest. He's finding more and more of his form.

He's not 100% yet, he's more like 80-90%. I think he was way better in the later days of HEROIC and first days of Liquid, so there's still potential there. When he joined, my estimation was more like that it takes 3-6 months. Now we are at the three-month mork, and he's doing really good, so I think he can find more consistency over the next season.

Of course, even this Major, every match is in some ways a learning point, some small things happening here and there, he's getting more comfortable, and he's getting more responsibility and control. It's growing all the time and I'm happy with that.

You've played a lot of close games so far, but showed a lot of competitiveness against some of the teams that people are expecting to be in the playoffs [in Falcons and FUT]. How are you feeling about your chances to pull through to the next stage?

I think we have good chances. Whenever we play top teams, it's close matches most of the time. The sad thing is, most of the times we have ended up on the losing side. But I think this is our chances to prove it wrong, or change the course.

We will get to play all good teams. I think tomorrow we will play a top team again, and most likely to make playoffs we will have to continue all the way, so... I think it's our chance. I'm confident that we can turn it around because of small improvements we have done in terms of roles, NertZ coming in, all these small things. As long as that adds up and we can show it in the officials, I think we can make it.
