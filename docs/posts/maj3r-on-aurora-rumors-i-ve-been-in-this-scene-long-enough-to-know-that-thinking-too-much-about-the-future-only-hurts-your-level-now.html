MAJ3R on Aurora rumors: "I’ve been in this scene long enough to know that thinking too much about the future only hurts your level now"

Aurora bounced back from an opening loss to HEROIC at PGL Astana by beating The Huns and PARIVISION to reach the 2-1 pool, after which Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli admitted in a written interview with HLTV that the team is still working to find consistency.

"I think sometimes we rely too much on momentum instead of building a stable identity every game," he said. "The goal now is not to chase perfect CS, but to become more disciplined and reliable under pressure."

The 35-year-old in-game leader also spoke about rumors pointing to Aurora exploring an international pivot during the off-season, stating that he is focusing on the present and using it as motivation to prove himself. With top challengers like Vitality and Natus Vincere not in attendance, MAJ3R added that his team isn't underestimating anyone.

"I never like looking at brackets and saying, 'This team is missing, so it’s easier,'" he said. "That mentality is dangerous. [...] We know our ceiling is high enough to beat any teams, but potential means nothing if you don’t show it consistently on the server."

A bit of a slow start to Astana with the HEROIC loss, but you’re 2-1 now after beating PARIVISION. What are your thoughts on your run so far?

I think we started below our level, especially individually and in communication, but the good thing is we reacted well instead of collapsing mentally. These tournaments are about adapting quickly. We still have a lot to improve, but I prefer a team that grows during an event rather than one that peaks too early.

You mentioned in an interview with BanKs that you feel like you’re in the same position as last year, and results have been pretty up and down. Why do you think that is, and what needs to change?

At the top level, consistency is the hardest thing in CS right now. The level between teams is very close, and small details decide matches. I think sometimes we rely too much on momentum instead of building a stable identity every game. The goal now is not to chase perfect CS, but to become more disciplined and reliable under pressure.

The analyst desk spoke about the team’s attitude looking a bit deflated this event. What can you say about that?

I understand why people can see it like that from the outside, but internally all good. Sometimes, frustration can look like low energy. The important thing is that everyone still cares, everyone still wants solutions, and we work every day to fix our mistakes and be a better team.

There have been rumors about Aurora already exploring changes for the off-season, with a potential switch to an international lineup. How are you handling that with a few events and the Major still left to play?

In esports, there are always rumors. But as players, our job is to focus on the present. I’ve been in this scene long enough to know that thinking too much about the future only hurts your level now. I don’t have information about our future and myself, but I try to make my team play good CS every day. I’m just focusing on the present.

Does it add any stress?

Pressure is normal. If you play for important organizations and big trophies, pressure will always exist. The key is whether pressure controls you or motivates you. Personally, I still enjoy proving myself after all these years, and I feel great.

We know our ceiling is high enough to beat any teams, but potential means nothing if you don’t show it consistently

Most of your losses at notable events this year have come against the top teams like Vitality, NAVI, and MOUZ. A few have been to the likes of FUT, FURIA, and PARIVISION, but with two of your main challengers absent in Astana, what do you think about your team’s overall chance to win the event?

I never like looking at brackets and saying, 'This team is missing, so it’s easier.' That mentality is dangerous. If you underestimate teams in modern CS, you lose immediately. We know our ceiling is high enough to beat any teams, but potential means nothing if you don’t show it consistently on the server.
