KSCERATO: "No one remembers second place"

HLTV spoke to Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato after FURIA clinched their spot in the IEM Cologne Major grand final with a convincing win over Aurora.

The rifler brushed off the achievement of making it to his first Major final, making it clear the job isn't done. "It would mean a lot more if we win. No one remembers second place, so let's focus up for the next match and do our best."

And as much as they may hold true, the clichés didn't end there, as KSCERATO spoke in a similar vein about the potential grand final opponent between Falcons and Spirit. "It doesn't matter. As my father used to say, who wants to win doesn't choose the enemy."

He provided some insight on adjustments in the one-sided affair against Aurora, whom he called his "arkadaşes" (Turkish for "friend"): "It was pretty tough for me in the beginning [of Nuke]. I said, 'FalleN, it's not working door, they are fucking me,' so we changed a little bit and it started working."

You can watch the interview in full below:

Congratulations, your first Major final. What does that mean to you?

It means a lot for me, means a lot for me, but it would mean a lot [more] if we win. That's what matters. No one remembers second place, so let's focus up for the next match and do our best.

A very convincing result today, what was behind that?

We played very well and we knew exactly what they were doing as well. Also, we won some clutches, some good rotations we did, so I'm really happy we are playing at this kind of a level against a good team. Aurora's been very good, they are my friends, my arkadaşes [Editor's note: Turkish for friend], so thank you so much.

You made a lot of a good mid-round decisions on Nuke after losing the openings. Who was behind those calls?

We had the plan to do some door hits, but unfortunately they were playing against door, the Main guy spamming, smoke on top of hut to peek doors, so it was pretty tough for me in the beginning. I said, 'FalleN, it's not working door, they are fucking me,' so we changed a little bit and we started working.

Tomorrow, Spirit or Falcons. You lost the last games to both of them, but Falcons have been a tougher opponent this year. Is there a preference? Does it matter who you're going to play?

It doesn't matter. As my father used to say, who wants to win doesn't choose the enemy.

In the introduction to the semi-final, you had a lot to say about your father too.

I'm a bit emotional right now, but I know that he's really happy upstairs. Also my grandpa, they died in the last year.
