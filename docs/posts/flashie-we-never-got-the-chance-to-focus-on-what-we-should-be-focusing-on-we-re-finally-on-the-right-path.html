flashie: "We never got the chance to focus on what we should be focusing on; we're finally on the right path"

Liquid live to fight another day at BLAST Open Rotterdam after beating Ninjas in Pyjamas 2-1 in Group B's lower bracket. Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek's troops rebounded from a 13-3 thrashing on Nuke as they picked up an overtime win on Mirage before completing the turnaround with a 13-9 success on Ancient.

It was Liquid's first victory since signing Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa, who played an instrumental role on Mirage with a team-high 1.28 rating.

"He's open to new ideas, and he also has ideas of his own," Viktor "⁠flashie⁠" Tamás Bea said of malbsMd. "But I would say that despite having really good comms in the game — he has amazing comms — he's not the one who is trying to control the game around him.

"And that's exactly what we needed because previously we had kind of too many voices in the team."

With the Guatemalan star on the roster, Liquid are poised to re-enter the Americas VRS and qualify for the Cologne Major. The move has earned Liquid much criticism, but flashie rejected the notion that it was made with the Major in mind.

"That was just a bonus, kind of," he said. "We brought in Mario because we believe that we can be a better team with him. And about the criticism, obviously we see it and we are aware of it, but we cannot focus on it too much. Otherwise, it's going to overshadow our motivation and potential of this team."

flashie, a 2-1 win and you're still alive in the tournament. Nuke was very rough, but you played much better on Mirage and then Ancient. Give me your thoughts on the series.

We started very slowly on Nuke CT. We knew what to expect from them as they are a team that likes to run a lot of scripts and a lot of set rounds. But unfortunately, we couldn't really show up in the first half of the map. And obviously, we didn't have many rounds to play on the T side, either. Obviously, with the lack of time, we didn't have time to iron out everything. And Nuke is a map that punishes you if you're not aware of the details. And that's what happened.

On Mirage, I felt like we had a good chance to close out the game in regulation. We had one or two rounds that we shouldn't have lost. I would say that that was the map for us to get into the game. And then on Ancient, I think we were in the flow much more. And I think we were in control of the game, despite losing, I think, both pistols on Ancient.

Looking at today's result and also the way that you played against Spirit, it seems like the potential is there, clearly. Even against Spirit, the second map was very close. Now it's about putting in the work and ironing out a lot of details, right?

Yeah, I think this season our struggles have been dealing with the stand-in situations and just ironing out the internal things in the team. And I felt like we never got a real chance to focus on what we should be focusing on — and that is developing the team and focusing on CS. And now we are finally on the right path. And I think with some time, I think we will be just stronger and stronger. And we will try to make it a very good curve for us to develop.

What has it been like to work with malbs? He's someone who came into the team with some confidence issues, and now he has to adjust to new positions and a new system. But you could see on Mirage the kind of impact that he can have.

Yeah, I would say I have known him for a few days only, so I can't say much. But he seems to me that he's a really open guy, open to feedback. He's open to new ideas, and he also has ideas of his own. But I would say that despite having really good comms in the game — he has amazing comms — he's not the one who is trying to control the game around him.

Coming into the tournament, you only had a few days of practice with malbs. Given those circumstances, what kind of goals did you set?

We basically just wanted to make sure that we could say that we were competitive in this event. That means not just in the rounds, but just with our attitude, constantly pushing for something and just having good rounds against good teams. And this is a really good opportunity for us to actually mash Mario into our team because we play tier-1 teams, right? So the goal was kind of just to be competitive, I would say.

And I guess you're happy with what you've seen so far, right?

Yeah, definitely. I think we showed a good fight against Spirit. I think it could have gone a different way if we had a little bit more extra. And against NIP, I think we showed a great game.
