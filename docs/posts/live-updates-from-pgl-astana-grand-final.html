karrigan: "We're not ready for a BO5 final"

Live

Master of managing the expectations publicly, Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen admitted the team hasn't had enough time to get the map pool up to standard for a best-of-five series.

"Obviously with eight or nine days of practice coming into this event, we're not ready for a BO5 final, right?" he told HLTV yesterday. "There are going to be mistakes, we have to understand that, and there is fatigue as well in a BO5 and we don't have a system."

So how will he try to make up for that?

"I maybe need to try and simplify some stuff for tomorrow for my own individual level, because that is going to be a key factor for tomorrow as well against a really heavy-firepower team like Spirit."

Live

Ahead of the grand final, the race to the special MVP medal will realistically come down to one player on each team.

A special medal awaits the best player of PGL Astana

Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets has a lead that looks insurmountable regardless of the result, averaging some rídiculous numbers in Spirit's undefeated run to the grand final, including a 1.95-rated semi-final against MOUZ.

It would take a disappearance to some degree for donk to give it up at this point, and although that is not unheard of, only Vitality have ever really managed to limit him in the finals.

His rival is none other than Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov, who has been instrumental in nearly all key maps for Falcons, especially the overtime affairs against The MongolZ and FURIA as well as the semi-final against magic.
