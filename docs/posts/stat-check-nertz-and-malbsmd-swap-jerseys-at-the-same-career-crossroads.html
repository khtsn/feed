Stat Check: NertZ and malbsMd swap jerseys at the same career crossroads

A straight swap should help both riflers rediscover their lost form.

"I didn't see myself leaving G2 this quickly."

That was Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa in his first interview as a Liquid player, and a sharp reminder of the reversal in fortune the 23-year-old has experienced since he made it into the top 20 in 2024.

malbsMd: "I want to be the malbs that I once was, and I'm hoping to find myself again"

There is no doubt that the organisation's decision to trade him for Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz will have hurt his pride, but malbsMd knew his form simply wasn't good enough. When asked why G2 could not kick on after BLAST London, he said "it was because of me not performing in the roles I was playing back then, because I also switched to anchor positions at the end."

And Eetu "⁠sAw⁠" Saha had more than enough reason to enact that role change. This is the era of Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets and all of his copycats, where systems are built around aggressive star rifles just as much, if not more than, star AWPers. Under that lens, malbsMd simply wasn't delivering the bacon.

The interesting part about the malbsMd-NertZ trade is in its symmetry. Both are aggressive riflers, who took on map control roles at the start of 2025 in the expectation that it would take their games to the next level.

Instead, they floundered. NertZ joined Liquid after posting a 1.18 rating at the back end of 2024, while malbsMd was on a 1.11. As both switched from being aggressive lurkers into star map control riflers, NertZ fell to a 1.05 and malbsMd to a 1.06.

NertZ entries less than malbsMd

They are different players, but both were misprofiled at the start of 2025. NertZ is a classic Marco "⁠Snappi⁠" Pfeiffer and sAw star, best used as a solo playmaker and not a bombsite entry. When Liquid signed him to be their opener after a brief look at some opening kill statistics, they missed that crucial distinction.

malbsMd, meanwhile, was thrust into all of Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač's positions and expected to pick up the slack on mid-round calling. In hindsight, we can see that this was not his game. But even at the time, it was a big ask for a player who played off his instinct and mechanical ability rather than natural macro understanding.

For both players, confidence dissipated rapidly. And no wonder, in roles where space has to be taken regardless of whether you're 0-5 in opening kills or not. NertZ could no longer take duels on his own terms, and malbsMd wasn't able to focus on his crosshair.

Both players then had a bit of a reset. Jonathan "⁠EliGE⁠" Jablonowski's arrival to Liquid changed NertZ's roles, while malbsMd shifted back to the extremities on some maps as Matúš "⁠MATYS⁠" Šimko settled into G2. But once confidence has gone, it is not easy to flick the lights back on.

A fresh start for both players, as they trade places, is therefore a chance for a true reset. malbsMd puts it simply: "They got a more balanced player in NertZ, and Liquid got a more aggressive player in me. So I think it's a good balance."

malbsMd is the bomb-site entry Liquid have lacked since Mareks "⁠YEKINDAR⁠" Gaļinskis's departure. His partnership with EliGE is a promising one, which should help the North American fire as a second-in star rather than the tip of the spear. It's no coincidence that EliGE's best form has come alongside willing entries like Epitacio "⁠TACO⁠" de Melo, Jake "⁠Stewie2K⁠" Yip, and Michael "⁠Grim⁠" Wince.

Role changes will be required on T side, where EliGE and malbsMd are both used to map control positions, but it should not be too large an issue. The Guatemalan is capable of being explosive from extremity positions or the pack, so whether he starts from Silo or Outside on Nuke is not a huge issue. The profiles still fit.

CT side, it will be interesting to see if Liquid take this chance to put Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek back in rotator positions and leave malbsMd as a turret. Given their issues with NertZ over this exact issue, it would not be too big of a surprise if siuhy took more control this time, which is reflected in our predictions here.

Long-term G2 target NertZ, if his confidence can be restored with sAw, can be the resource-heavy star rifler that G2 have lacked. Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko's impact is limited by his positions, MATYS is naturally selfless rather than a star, and Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García is no longer a star sniper. A happy again NertZ can add an active CT voice and some T side unpredictability to the roster in a way that malbsMd could not once his confidence had been knocked.

MATYS' style lends itself to being the malbsMd to EliGE's NertZ; this is a win-win for the organizations. Both now have complementary rifler structures of a high trade-volume pack player (malbsMd, MATYS), a lone wolf playmaker (EliGE, NertZ), and an overqualified stable anchor-lurker (Keith "⁠NAF⁠" Markovic, HeavyGod). It is far more than a cynical VRS grab, as EliGE insisted in an interview with Hotspawn.

The NertZ-SunPayus-sAw axis is a proven one, and familiarity will ease NertZ's arrival. G2 is his best chance to prove that his time in Liquid did not count, and NertZ must take it.

Might NertZ be G2's lone wolf?

In terms of positions, it is harder to predict how G2 will use MATYS and NertZ so we have not done a full prediction. But expect the duo to share traditional control positions on T side, with NertZ keeping spots like Mid on Ancient and MATYS being the primary Mid player on Mirage.

On CT, Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač has a similar dilemma to siuhy when it comes to the choice of himself or MATYS in rotate positions. Most maps will still sort themselves out, like Overpass or Ancient, but it will be interesting to see if NertZ's ability as a mid-round caller will lead to huNter- taking some more anchor roles than we are used to — or if the ever-selfless MATYS takes one for the team as usual.

So far, then, we have been very positive about this move. There is genuine hope that malbsMd and NertZ can both improve in form once they settle into their new squads.

But it would be negligent not to zoom out and consider the whole picture. In Liquid's case especially, swapping NertZ for malbsMd is the equivalent of adding some sugar to a dish that you have already added too much chilli to. Yes, it will help, but the damage has been done.

The team has been less than the sum of its parts for years. Good players frequently look like poor ones in Liquid, and NertZ is just the latest example. Justin "⁠jks⁠" Savage, Robert "⁠Patsi⁠" Isyanov, Felipe "⁠skullz⁠" Medeiros, and Casper "⁠cadiaN⁠" Møller have not been the same since joining. YEKINDAR and Aleks "⁠Rainwaker⁠" Petrov are only just returning to form, while Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken surely regrets his 2023 decision to leave FaZe.

The case for the prosecution here is easy. Roland "⁠ultimate⁠" Tomkowiak is not good enough. siuhy is struggling as a captain without Dennis "⁠sycrone⁠" Nielsen, evidenced by how often the team rush late-round situations and their awful T side statistics.

Viktor "⁠flashie⁠" Tamás Bea is clearly intelligent, but are his ideas being enacted on the server properly, and if they aren't, why aren't they? Plus, when things are going wrong, EliGE and NAF are not personalities that lead to easy comebacks. malbsMd, a bit of a tilter himself, is not going to help in that regard either.

The malbsMd-EliGE-NAF trio makes sense on paper, in the server, and allows a spot in the Americas VRS. But let's be clear: It would not be a surprise to see more changes to Liquid down the line, even as soon as the Major. This is no band of brothers.

G2 are in a stronger position, and their recent struggles are not on the same planet as Liquid's. London proved that they can win big titles, even over Vitality. They have a stable base to build on but even here, signing an out-of-touch NertZ is not a surefire success in a world where firepower is the buzzword of the day. Players of the calibre of NiKo and Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov are not replaceable, and G2 will miss them for some time yet.

These two organizations are institutions in Counter-Strike. Between them, Liquid and G2 have won 16 Big Events, attended 31 Majors, and won over $9,745,515 in prize money.

This trade deal makes both teams well-balanced, solid, international rosters under the helm of respected coaches. But behemoths like these two should not be satisfied with anywhere outside the top five of the VRS.

NertZ and SunPayus' reunion comes in a far bigger organization than ENCE

MOUZ have proved that even overperforming your player talent, with top four finish after top four finish, is still not enough to be respected in the community. It is all or nothing, and trophies are the only way to stave off the waves of memes and criticism. That might not be fair, but it is the reality massive esports organizations like G2 and Liquid are built to thrive in.

A sensible trade like this is a step in the right direction. But the sad truth for NertZ and malbsMd is that even if they improve their new sides, there will be a demand for more. At places like these, the shadow of old trophy-laden rosters looms large and that is the challenge both now have to accept.

Their last chapters were disappointments. Neither can afford two duds in a row.
