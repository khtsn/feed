Following his team's elimination from IEM Atlanta, Vitality rifler William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman sat down with HLTV to discuss the team's performance throughout the American event, the quarter-final loss to Natus Vincere, and the team's plan to combat exhaustion ahead of the IEM Cologne Major.

Or you can listen to the interview here.

You guys are out of IEM Atlanta after a difficult quarter-final match against NAVI. Tell me how you're feeling about the team's performance this event.

Of course, always disappointed to lose. Especially the way we lost, it could've been easily 2-0 to us as well. Losing is never fun, we always want to be winning. But at the same time, it's been a long time on the road. These tournaments, even though we had these back-to-back tournaments, Fort Worth to Atlanta, bit of a gap in between, but still, at the same time, it's being away from home, and then even before that having Rio, with a few days at home, and then we're back out on the road.

It's been a long stretch of events where it's been pretty exhausting for all of us. At some point, it was gonna happen where we tapered off and didn't perform at our best. Now the goal is to just get some rest at home, get some days to recover, and then back to bootcamp and prepare for the Major. That's the big goal.

About the NAVI game in particular, I'm curious about Inferno. B1ad3 highlighted letting Inferno through as maybe a mistake from you guys. Why do you think the map went the way that it did? Were the individuals just not online?

Yeah, I mean, the individuals weren't the best, but at the same time, they played a good game. We were happy to play Inferno, even though it's not something we've been super active on in the map pool at the moment, but at the same time we're comfortable on the map. I just think yesterday, whether it was Inferno or another map, it could have easily gone the same way. I think we just ran out of energy, ran out of the extra push to try and make it further.

At the end of the day, they played a better game on Inferno. The individuals were shooting very well, they had a good game plan. When it got to it, we just ran out of a bit of steam and gas at the end, just to kinda get it over the line. If it's Inferno or another map, Nuke, whatever, I think it could've easily gone the same way as well. I don't think it was the map choice. I think the tournament has kinda gotten to that point in time where we had nothing left. We tried to give as much as we could, but we just couldn't get it over the line.

Even though we have these five days between Fort Worth and here, it's still time away from home. It's not the same recovery as when you get to go home

A big narrative around you guys this event has been around the lack of practice, but it sounds like extra practice would have only done so much and you guys are just more drained than anything else. Is that an apt assessment?

Yeah, I think it's a bit of both. Of course, apEX and the coach are still trying to work during the event to add new things, but there's only so much you can add during the event without practice, on what works and what doesn't work. Of course, we needed practice just to get the reps in on some maps. With Anubis and Inferno, we hadn't practiced so much, and we tried to, but at the same time, there's not so much time to practice. So, in terms of repetitions and adding new things, we needed that practice.

But also, the amount of time on the road... it's not so much getting burnout from the game, I think it's just the travel burnt us out a bit and exhausted us a little bit. Even though we have these five days between Fort Worth and here, yes we did our own things and had that time to relax, it's still time away from home. It's not the same recovery as when you get to go home, be with family and friends and stuff. You're still away, still travelling. Even though you're trying to take your mind off the game, you're still away for work and you're still thinking about CS, what we need to add, and what we need to do differently. In that sense, it's still exhausting as well.

I think, of course, it would have been nice to get as much practice in and get some time to add new things, but at the same time, the travel thing is probably the biggest thing that has exhausted us a little too much.

How important do you think it is to have this wake-up call right before the Major?

I think it's important, of course, it's a wake-up call, but at the same time, we kinda knew it would have to come at some point, that maybe we would take a dip. If we had won here, and lifted the trophy here, we probably would have said to ourselves, what the fuck is happening? We just won Fort Worth with not the best preparation or practice, and if were to win here it'd be getting a bit too much now. Of course, it's good; now we get a couple extra days at home to just recover, rest, and then come back to practice.

It's a wake-up call, but at the same time, we had to expect this was going to happen, that we weren't going to keep winning events with no preparation and no practice, still exhausted. It would have been crazy to win here. I think it's still fine as well; now we've got the time to prepare for the Major. That's the main goal. We know we're gonna come back even more hungry, and even more motivated to make sure that no team can beat us again.

So the plan for the Major is a little bit of rest into a bootcamp?

Yeah, so we have time off when we get home, and then we have a few days of practice at home, and then a bootcamp in Paris, then over to Cologne for the tournament. As usual, for us before the Majors, we always get that bootcamp in, it's really important for us, to make sure we're all prepared fully and know exactly the plan, and all the preparation going into the tournament.

Even though now I want to go home and rest, the mind is still running about CS, and just so motivated to come back and get the win now. I think it's going to be a crazy Major, super exciting to play. Cologne is one of the best tournaments to play, and for it to be a Major as well, it's going to be crazy.
