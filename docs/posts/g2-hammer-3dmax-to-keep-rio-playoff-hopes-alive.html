G2 hammer 3DMAX to keep Rio playoff hopes alive

The international team will have to survive another elimination match to reach the quarter-finals.

G2 were too much for 3DMAX to handle in Rio

G2 are through to Group A's lower bracket final at IEM Rio with a quick 2-0 victory over 3DMAX, who have been sent packing in 9th-12th place. The international team, which is without Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač for this event, is now one series away from qualifying for the playoffs of the Brazilian tournament.

It was simply a poor day at the office for 3DMAX, who were on the back foot throughout the series, save for a short stretch on Inferno. They will now have a month off to prepare for their next tournament, the CAC Asia Championships in Shanghai.

3DMAX bounced back from an early deficit on Inferno but lost their grip on the game as the first half wore on, with G2 taking a 7-5 lead thanks to a series of multi-kills from Matúš "⁠MATYS⁠" Šimko and important frags from stand-in Vilius "⁠tAk⁠" Keserauskas.

Things then went from bad to worse for 3DMAX as they wasted a 5v3 advantage in the ensuing pistol. They could muster only one round in the half — by denying Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz a 1v3 clutch — as their defense was picked apart time and time again by G2.

More misery was to come for 3DMAX, who won the first pistol on Dust2 but were then overrun by NertZ in the conversion. That sparked a dominant 13-0 run from G2, punctuated by two clutches from Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García.
