Legacy coach barred from remainder of Cologne Major after communication infraction

Legacy coach Alan "⁠adrrr⁠" Riveros has been barred from standing behind the Brazilian side for the remainder of IEM Cologne after he was repeatedly found to communicate with the team outside of the designated time windows, ESL has announced.

The coach had received "multiple warnings" before his final infraction came in Legacy's final timeout in the match against PARIVISION. He gave a thumbs up to one of the players after the tactical timeout ended, he admitted in an interview on Gaules' stream.

"After reviewing repeated violations of the Major ruleset, the decision has been made that adr will no longer be permitted to coach the team for the remainder of the event's matches," ESL's statement reads.

"adr received multiple warnings for interacting with Legacy players during rounds, with the most recent incident involving the use of hand signals to confirm a strategy during a match."

At Valve-sanctioned tournaments, communication between coaches and teams has been strictly limited to the designated time windows in the tactical timeouts and breaks between halves since PGL Stockholm 2021.

From Valve's Major Supplemental Rulebook

Coaches are not to communicate with (i.e., be seen, heard, or felt by) the players by any means at any time with the exception of warmup, half-time, and tactical timeouts.

This is also covered in the IEM Cologne rulebook, which lists an ejection from a match as one form of punishment following a repeated infraction, as well as "more severe" consequences in the event of the incident "relevantly impacting the game."

From the IEM Cologne rulebook, section 7.6 Coaching

The coach is not allowed to communicate in any other way including, but not limited to, shouting or non-verbal communication (e.g. touching) with players outside of the specified time windows.

Any coach found to be in breach of the communications restrictions outlined in this section will receive one (1) warning from the referees. This warning stays in place for the duration of the event. If this rule is breached again, or the first instance is severe enough, the coach will be ejected from the game and forfeits their right to coach for the remainder of the match.

If the incident has clearly and relevantly impacted the game, the consequences for the coach and/or the team will be accordingly more severe.
