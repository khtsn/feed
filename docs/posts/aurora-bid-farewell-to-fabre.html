Aurora bid farewell to Fabre

Aurora have announced that they have parted ways with head coach Sezgin "⁠Fabre⁠" Kalaycı, marking an end to his long tenure in charge of the squad.

The 36-year-old joined the Turkish team in November 2022, when it still played under Eternal Fire, and helped turn it into a regular playoff contender. In November 2025, he guided Aurora to victory at PGL Masters Bucharest.

In a video posted on X, Fabre revealed that he informed Aurora's management and the players during ESL Pro League 23 of his wish to become inactive. He coached his final game for the team at the IEM Cologne Major, where the Turkish outfit was eliminated by FURIA in the semi-finals.

"The reason for my decision to take a break actually has many reasons," he said. "But, in summary, it's to gather some energy. To get some rest.

"I have been actively coaching for five years. You know the CS schedule. I thought I needed to take a break.

"I have some minor health issues. Nothing serious. To deal with those, I thought I needed a break for a few months."

Fabre also opened the door to a new coaching challenge in the future. "Right now, I have no intention of leaving this job," he said. "I just want to take a break."

Fabre's exit comes amid rumors that Aurora are moving toward an international roster, with former GamerLegion head coach Ashley "⁠ash⁠" Battye believed to be on the verge of taking over.

Shortly after the team's elimination in Cologne, Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli admitted that the future looked uncertain.

"As a five-Turkish team, we came as far as we could at this Major," he said. "We tried our best. We sometimes showed good Counter-Strike; sometimes not. I really gave my best today. It was not enough. I want to congratulate FURIA; they played really well today. That’s the end of the story. We will see for the future."
