NAVI came into the series after a disappointing string of play following a joint last-place finish at Masters: Tokyo and a second-place finish at the EMEA LCQ. Liquid will likewise hope to recover from a Tokyo run that failed to live up to expectations. A top-six finish at the event and a 1-2 series record was an unfortunate turn of events for the EMEA League victors.

Liquid got off to a hot start on their opponent's map pick of Fracture. A 4-0 lead and Jamppi delivering the first ace of the tournament were promising signs for Liquid coming into the series. NAVI managed to recover late in the half with cNed 's Operator providing solid site holds and SUYGETSU delivering at every opportunity. NAVI could be proud of having ended the half at a 6-6 scoreline, despite the initial deficit.

NAVI displayed dominance once moving to the attack side, despite losing another pistol, ANGE1 's squad took six out of seven rounds in a row to lead 12-9. All 24 rounds were required for NAVI to take the map and lead 1-0 in the series. Both SUYGETSU and cNed delivered for NAVI, while Liquid can take solace in a strong performance from nAts .

Despite a map loss, Jamppi delivered the first ace at Champions.

NAVI once again excelled coming onto the gun rounds. After losing their third pistol, NAVI took five rounds straight to lead 5-2 before Liquid could recover. Some excellent mid-round calls from Redgar and first bloods from Jamppi on Chamber kept the map close. Liquid took five rounds of their own to end the half with a narrow 7-5 lead at halftime.

NAVI's first pistol win of the series was immediately answered back by a thrifty win from Liquid. The thrifty would quickly prove to be a slight hiccup in NAVI's journey to a map victory with the black and yellow taking five in a row before Liquid could respond. Sayf and Redgar combined with heroic plays to bring Liquid back into the map and string together three rounds to tie the score at 11-11. NAVI's single chance at closing the series in regulation was denied by a stick on the defuse from nAts to force overtime.

Four sets of overtime were required for NAVI to eventually close out the map 17-15 and the series 2-0. Despite nAts' heroics, repeated impact from SUYGETSU and ANGE1 was too much for Liquid to handle.
