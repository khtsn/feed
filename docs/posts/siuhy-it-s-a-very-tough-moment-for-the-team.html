Not long after his team's elimination from IEM Atlanta, Liquid captain Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek spoke with HLTV regarding Liquid's disappointing performance in Georgia, the team's plan as CAC and the IEM Cologne Major loom, and the positive impacts that malbsMd has made on the team.

0-2 loss, out of IEM Atlanta. Just tell me what's going through your head right now.

A lot of disappointment. We had three weeks at home where we were able to practice and put in a lot of hours and perfect the things that we wanted to perfect on the server, work on a lot of those things that we were coming short in previously. We came into this tournament with our heads held high, despite the previous results and previous tournaments, some tournaments where we bomb out without even winning a game.

We came in with our heads held high after those three weeks of practice. We worked on a lot of maps, and then we come here, and we just fail to show any of that, especially on our own map picks. First two times we picked Dust2; it was our best map in practice, and we failed to show it on the server here during official games. A lot of disappointment, not really sure what else I can say. I feel a little bit helpless in this situation.

Do you have any idea why there's such a disconnect between what you guys are doing in practice and what you're doing in the server?

Something that comes to my mind... obviously, we haven't been doing good for a long time now. Naturally, a person loses a lot of confidence, and Counter-Strike is a game where you need confidence to play against any team. Some even want it more than others on the server, and that's why you have upsets happening, that these lower teams want to beat these better teams, and they're hungry for that.

I feel like, for us, that confidence dropped throughout the last couple of months because of the results. Now, when we come into a tournament, although home practice is going fine, we come to a tournament and it feels like we're kinda back to maybe reminding ourselves that in the last couple of tournaments we bombed out and we're still not playing good. That's kinda what it feels like, and that's the only thing that comes to my head for now.

Maybe we just need that one good win, one confident win, where we do everything perfectly, and it's not a scrappy win, and maybe that brings us up

You guys have CAC in China right around the corner, and then the Major more or less right after that. What is the priority for you heading into China and then straight to the Major?

I wanna say being mentally there. It's a very tough moment for the team, for all players. They all feel that we're not doing the best. Being mentally there, even though the situation, we still have two tournaments where anything can happen.

Maybe we just need that one good win, one confident win, where we do everything perfectly, and it's not a scrappy win, and maybe that brings us up. You never know in Counter-Strike, things can turn around in a millisecond, in one round. Just be mentally there for the team, and do the best we can in the last two tournaments of the season.

You've had malbsMd in your ranks for about two months now. How do you feel about him now that he's been slotted into the team? What is he bringing to the structure?

I think it was a really good change for us. malbsMd is the type of player that we needed on the team. He's very selfless, he knows how to coordinate the players around him to also make a play for himself. He has a really good balance between that, and I said it before, but previously we didn't have a balance between aggressive style and passive style. Now, I feel since malbsMd came in, he's kinda filling in that gap, and that balance is there now. That's really good to see, I think he's slotting in, I think he's an amazing player.

It's been really good to play with him, and he's also bringing in a lot of ideas to the game, so it's not like he's just slotting in playing our system, I think we're also listening to him, which is important for him. I think he's a very smart player in general. A lot of things we changed based on his view. I think he fits us really good.
