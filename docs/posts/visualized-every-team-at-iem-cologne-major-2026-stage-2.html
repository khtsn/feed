Spirit

Quote: Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich after winning PGL Astana: "To be honest, I think it's really a lot of work on my mentality. About how I'm feeling on the server or something like that because in Rio, I got a lot of energy and a lot of boost from my team, and also from the arena. I got this confidence, and after this, I'm just trying to keep it and work on the stuff."

Stat: Dmitry "⁠S0tF1k⁠" Forostyanko has earned praise for Spirit's improved flash assists but Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev should garner plenty of credit. Spirit have averaged 0.28 flash assists per round with S0tF1k standing behind the team, compared to 0.25 with Sergey "⁠hally⁠" Shavaev this year, but the big jump is from 0.18 in 2025 when Leonid "⁠chopper⁠" Vishnyakov was in-game leader. S0tF1k should still get credit, but more for some new T side proactivity.

Fact: Spirit are 17-2 on Dust2 this year; their two losses have come to Vitality (13-5) and Astralis (13-6). But their round win percentage of 59.2% still pales in comparison to Vitality's 65.2%.

So here I can help the kids; I can, you know, make the things they want to happen come true, right? The things they’re aiming for in their careers. I feel really close to them and that gives me a sense of comfort, it makes me work with a smile, I get to play the CS I need to play, I do what I have to do. So I think it’s been a really different and very good experience."

Stat: Alan "⁠adrrr⁠" Riveros's arrival as the new coach has gone under the radar, but Legacy's improvement has been stark. With the new manager, they have event placings of 1st, 3rd, and 1st, while they are 8-1 on Mirage and have Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto averaging a 1.30 rating.

Fact: After their last-minute invite to Austin, Legacy ended up 9-11th just shy of playoffs. But they unravelled in Budapest under the weight of higher expectations and exited in Stage 1. How will they fare this time, ninth on our power ranking after their win at CAC?
