Chr1zN: "In Cluj we weren't ready for it [...] now it's going to be much better"

HEROIC kicked off their campaign at PGL Astana with a 2-0 win over Aurora, made all the sweeter for Yasin "⁠xfl0ud⁠" Koç over his former Eternal Fire teammates, delivering in their return to top-tier LAN competition after playing almost entirely in the tier-two circuit after PGL Cluj-Napoca in February.

"The last time when we played in Cluj, we weren't... I'd say, ready for it," Christoffer "⁠Chr1zN⁠" Storgaard told HLTV after the match. "There was too much change, and a lot of stuff was uncertain with the team and so on. Now that there is a bit more calm and everyone has gotten the month to practice and actually feel each other out, it's going to be much better."

Certainty around the team finishing the season with Simon "⁠yxngstxr⁠" Boije has gone a long way in helping that cause, as has the relief of pressure from having a spot at the Cologne Major secured.

"I feel like everyone was so focused on it, especially because last time HEROIC didn't manage to qualify, so it was a big goal for us, of course," Chr1zN said. "And therefore, every match probably meant like 10% more than it does now in a sense."

Congratulations, a big win over Aurora to start your run in Astana. You took it in two but it got a little close in the end, so what are you thinking after that game?

I think we played really good, really solid. We had a very good game plan coming into it. Everything felt like it fell into place, especially on Nuke. The T side, maybe we went too much off-script at one point and they got back into it, but other than that, I think we did good. Dust2 also here, I feel like we had rounds where we could close it earlier.

So I'm very proud of the boys, I think they played really well. Aurora is tough, and I know the other guys were very hyped, especially xfl0ud for this matchup, so it meant a lot. I'm really happy.

It has been a bit of a tier-two LAN grind for you; you haven't played at a big LAN like this since Cluj-Napoca in February, so it has been a while. How does it feel to be back?

It's nice. It's very different (laughs) than playing tier-two LANs. It's very good. The last time when we played in Cluj, we weren't... I'd say, ready for it. There was too much change, and a lot of stuff was uncertain with the team and so on. Now that there is a bit more calm and everyone has gotten the month to practice and actually feel each other out, it's going to be much better.

I also said before the matchup here that we were going to beat Aurora because... I don't know, I just feel like when we've been practicing right now, it seems like it's good. Everyone is chiming in and it's nice.

For everyone I can feel it, that it gives a bit more certainty, and that's nice

Talk to me about that uncertainty a bit. Obviously you've had these last few months where yxngstxr has been standing in despite being on the bench, and now you know you're going to conclude the season with him. Does it help to have that stability and know how you're going to proceed for the rest of the season?

Definitely. Of course, not knowing if Simon [yxngstxr] is coming or going, if Alkaren is coming back or not, was hard. Also like, how much time do we dedicate to this project? So yeah, it's been tough, but it's also nice knowing now that okay, this is how we're going to finish the season off, and then we will see what happens after, of course. But definitely, for everyone I can feel it, that it gives a bit more certainty, and that's nice.

On the Alkaren note, I mean, we always have to ask at this point (Chr1zN laughs). Any update on that situation?

No comment (smiles).

Okay. When you've been at a couple of these LAN appearances, you're pretty good against the teams around you, but you have struggled a bit against the better teams. What have you changed? Is it the stability you're talking about that has helped turn that around here?

To be honest, I just think we needed time. I feel like, as I said, we weren't ready. It was too big of a jump for us in our progress, and also with the curveball of Simon playing the AWP certainly didn't help with that. So I just think we needed time.

Last time, to be honest, there was a big gap in the level, and this time around it's not going to seem as big. Which, of course, it shouldn't either. It was just about that, to be honest, because everyone is individually more than skilled enough to play and win against people at this level. We know what we are capable of.

This tournament means a lot for our VRS and how we're going to manage invites even after the Major

Touching on the last couple of results — you ended up qualifying for the Major, and it is always a little bit uncertain until it happens. No matter, like you see 99% (Chr1zN laughs), but you're never really sure until it's locked in, right? TOBIZ told us there was a lot of pressure in that whole period leading up to it, so how is it to have some relief now and that pressure off of you?

Definitely. Like, I didn't even mention it, but of course the Major pressure, yeah, it's so hard. And yeah, as you mentioned, like it's 99%, but then you go ahead and you lose to Acend and Tricked, and suddenly it doesn't look like 99% anymore, you know? (laughs) You can jump a lot in the rankings, but yeah, it definitely helps a lot.

I feel like everyone was so focused on it, especially because last time HEROIC didn't manage to qualify, so it was a big goal for us, of course. And therefore, every match probably meant like 10% more than it does now in a sense. It's good to have the Major and know that we're going to be in Cologne.

A little bit after you joined HEROIC, you said that it was your goal to bring them back to the top. It's not been ideal with this start to the year with the roster, but how do you think you're handling that goal?

When I said this, I knew that of course it's going to be a long and hard-fought process. It's not easy, especially in this era of CS, where everyone is so good and competitive. It's about building on to each other, brick by brick, every day, and I think this tournament means a lot for our VRS and how we're going to manage invites even after the Major and whatnot. Right now, this is just our goal, trying to do as well here as possible, hopefully find ourselves in playoffs, and then we'll take it from there.
