Brollan: "MOUZ have been very direct that I could have a future here"

Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin was benched by MOUZ in the wake of IEM Rio as the organization overhauled its roster, handing leadership duties to Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman and promoting academy prospect Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze. But with only one roster substitution available for the IEM Cologne Major, Brollan was still set to play one last event with his former team, and they began their Major campaign with a win over Legacy.

It may not be his swansong as much remains up in the air heading into the off-season, he told HLTV after the match, with his contract expiring after the Major and a surprising possibility to stay on as a rifler if both parties think it works out.

"It could be a future here for sure," Brollan said. "They have been very direct that I could have a future here, which I'm thankful for, and I'm trying to do my best to have it. Then we'll see if I want to stay or if they want to keep me, et cetera."

The Swede is playing some of the roles he occupied before transitioning to in-game leading, making it a little easier, and has been trying to turn the "IGL brain" off while trying to adjust to xertioN calling instead.

"It was pretty weird in the beginning," he said. "In recent times, I've always come into a team and I have a new IGL, basically, but now it was playing with my former teammate and he's leading now instead of me, so it was pretty weird [...] with him calling out strats to me instead of me calling strats to him."

Listen to the interview here.

Started off with a win in Cologne. Is it a bit odd for you, coming back into the team? How did it feel on the server?

It feels good, of course. A lot of new stuff coming in, I'm not the leader anymore, so it's just a lot of new things. Maybe the last practice week, it felt a little bit off for a few days, but I'm getting used to it still to try and play in the rifle role. We'll see how it goes, but I think it will be better each day that will come.

I [am] playing basically the same T side roles as I did before I became the in-game leader, [...] and I felt pretty confident in those roles

A lot has changed, you're not in-game leading anymore, xelex is in the team. How different is it right now?

Of course it feels a lot different, but at the same time, I [am] playing basically the same T side roles as I did before I became the in-game leader. Maybe I have some new positions on CT side, like Ramp on Nuke for example, and maybe some others I can't really tell, but it's just a lot of new things I'm trying to improve on. But yeah, basically the T sides have some new positions, but it was pretty much the same as I did before and I felt pretty confident in those roles back in the days.

You've been on the sidelines for a bit, so what have you been doing during this time?

The first one and a half or two weeks, I tried to just be relaxed and not do anything to be honest. Tried to keep my routines up and these things, and I was talking with our performance coach as well, that I tried to have a little bit more off time than usual.

Then coming back to the FACEIT grind and these things, I was playing FACEIT for maybe a month. I tried to keep my individual form up and maybe get the IGL brain out from the head a little bit, try to focus on destroying people in some way. That's my main goal right now, and just try to relax and of course be with my family and friends.

Did the benching come as a surprise to you at all?

Yeah, it was.

It came as a surprise because it was the middle of the season

What happened with that, then?

I know that it was a rough period of course, but it came as a surprise because it was the middle of the season, I think that was the only [thing]. Maybe we're not the same page, and the staff wanted to basically change the entire leader role. I think it was fine of course, and I was expecting it, but it could be with my contract going out soon as well.

Then, with xelex coming into the team, I think they wanted to test him as well, so it was a mix of everything. But it was pretty surprising, especially as it was the middle of the season, right? That was mainly it.

With you being out, xertioN took over as in-game leader. What was it like playing with him in that role?

It was pretty weird in the beginning. In recent times, I've always come into a team and I have a new IGL, basically, but now it was playing with my former teammate and he's leading now instead of me, so it was pretty weird. But when they said Dori [xertioN] is going to lead the team, I could easily see how he can do it, but it's just pretty sad that he's maybe going [away] from the space creator [role] because I think he's insane on that as well.

But yeah, basically the first practice days were pretty different and pretty weird, and I maybe had the FACEIT habits still in me, but also I actually think he's doing a great job. His mid-rounds and directions is really good for the team.

When you say it was a little bit weird, a bit different, can you expand more on that?

It was not weird in the sense of calling, it was just weird with him calling out strats to me instead of me calling strats to him. It was in these strats. But he's always trying to lead as much as possible on CT side, so it was nothing different on that side, but it's just weird coming into the same team but he's calling to you and I'm not calling to him. That was the most different.

It's maybe a lot of question marks for everyone, but it's a lot of question marks for me as well

You touched on it a bit, just about your future. You said your contract is running out, I think you told STYKO after the Major, but it seems you're not really sure what will happen afterward. Do you have any idea of what you'd like? Would you want to continue being an in-game leader, could your future be with MOUZ, any thoughts on that?

It could be a future here for sure. They have been very direct that I could have a future here, which I'm thankful for, and I'm trying to do my best to have it. Then we'll see if I want to stay or if they want to keep me, et cetera. About leading or rifling, like I don't really know... I try to think about it daily, to be honest, it's a really hard decision.

But both roles are really fun, and I think I'm doing a great job as a leader, but at the same time, I'm a really good secondary lurker, or lurker positions, or maybe like a rifler — basically in these positions I'm playing right now. We'll see how it will be, but it's maybe a lot of question marks for everyone, but it's a lot of question marks for me as well. We'll see how it goes, if I perform well, how the team is performing, and what's on the market as well.

Sometimes it can be really hard to turn off the in-game leader brain; we've seen other players experience that. Is that going to be difficult for you?

There are a lot of people who say, 'IGL brain, you need to turn off,' and these things. I maybe have a little bit still, but I think it was really easy, to be honest. I don't think it was a hard thing for me, especially because sometimes I was just trying to talk too much as a leader, but now I'm trying to have my communication at a medium [level].

But I'm trying to get the IGL brain out of my head, basically, and just try to focus on my crosshair a little bit. I know what I liked to do before as a rifler, so I tried to keep doing that. It worked so far and the practices have been going pretty good so far.

Lastly, you were benched before the Major, but it was always known you were going to come back for this event. You started 1-0, so how do you feel about MOUZ's overall run and what the potential for the Major is, as it still is an interim lineup?

Yeah, I've seen a lot of people don't have us in the playoffs, for example, in pickems or whatever it is, and truly I understand it because they don't know where we are, basically. There are a lot of good teams, and everyone is really good nowadays, especially in Stage 3, so I think it's very hard for people to predict where we are.

But I feel like we are really confident, and it was not... I think Spinx said in the ESL podcast or whatever it was that we haven't changed much, and I think that's true. I'm basically coming into the system, and I was really prepared to come into the system as well and try to talk with xertioN a lot for it as well. We'll see, of course we'll try to take it one game at a time, but our main goal is playoffs for sure.
