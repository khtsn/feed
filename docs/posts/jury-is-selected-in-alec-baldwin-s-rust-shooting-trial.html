Jury Is Selected in Alec Baldwin’s ‘Rust’ Shooting Trial

Mr. Baldwin, who was charged with involuntary manslaughter in the fatal shooting of a cinematographer, has pleaded not guilty.

July 9, 2024Updated 6:34 p.m. ET

Picking an impartial jury to hear the trial of Alec Baldwin, a movie and television star who inspires strong feelings from both his fans and his detractors, is a particular challenge. So as jury selection began on Tuesday, Judge Mary Marlowe Sommer started things off by turning a standard question on its head.

“How many of you have not seen or heard anything about this case from any source whatsoever?” she asked a pool of 70 potential jurors.

Only a couple indicated that they had not.

“I don’t have cable or internet or anything like that,” one outlier noted.

The case, as a vast majority of prospective jurors had heard, involves the shooting death of the cinematographer Halyna Hutchins on the set of the film “Rust” in 2021. She was killed when a gun that Mr. Baldwin, one of the film’s stars, was rehearsing with fired a live round.

The prosecution argues that Mr. Baldwin, 66, was criminally negligent, and he was charged with involuntary manslaughter. He has denied responsibility, saying that he had no reason to believe that a gun on a film set would be loaded with a live round, and that he had been told the gun was “cold,” meaning it should have been impossible to fire.

A jury of 16 was sworn in on Tuesday afternoon. Twelve will ultimately decide the case, and four will serve as alternates.

The potential jurors were asked about their feelings on guns (many owned them, while one declared, “I hate them”), whether they had connections to the film industry or whether there was anything that would make it difficult for them to serve on the jury (one woman brought up her daily medical marijuana use as a potential factor).

Alex Spiro, a defense lawyer, sought to gauge how potential jurors view Mr. Baldwin. “Maybe he played a role in a movie you didn’t like, maybe he did a comedy routine or an imitation you don’t like,” he said, possibly alluding to Mr. Baldwin’s impressions of former President Donald J. Trump on “Saturday Night Live.” He asked, “Does anyone have a view that would cause you to lean against him in some way?”

Wearing a gray suit and thick-framed eyeglasses and sitting across the room from the jury pool, Mr. Baldwin largely sat back and watched, sometimes taking notes or speaking to his lawyer. In the back row of the courtroom sat his wife, Hilaria Baldwin, and one of his brothers, the actor Stephen Baldwin, according to a pool report from the one journalist permitted inside. (During a delay in the proceedings, while the court was dealing with technical issues, Mr. Baldwin’s Instagram account shared an out-of-the-blue political suggestion, “Sheldon Whitehouse for Vice President,” without elaborating.)

A couple of potential jurors said from the outset that they did not think they would be able to judge the case objectively. “I have read the case extensively and looked at it and reviewed it and I don’t feel that I can be fair,” one said.

Kari T. Morrissey, the lead prosecutor in the case, questioned jurors on whether their media consumption on the case had shaped their opinions on it, or whether they had formed positions on whether Mr. Baldwin should have been prosecuted at all. “We’ve had some different camps in terms of the public,” she noted.

“Is there anything about your life experiences or maybe the media you’ve been exposed to that may cause you issues?” she asked.

Mr. Baldwin’s lawyers have railed against the prosecution as a misguided bid to secure the conviction of a celebrity in a high-profile case. Though Mr. Baldwin has sometimes addressed the case publicly since the fatal shooting, he has been silent on the case in the lead-up to trial, letting his legal team do the talking.

“There’s a man who is sitting here who has his day in court now, finally,” Mr. Spiro told the jurors.

Opening arguments are likely to begin on Wednesday, and the trial is expected to last about two weeks.
