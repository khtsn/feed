ScrunK: "You can tell [Senzu] has a champion's mentality"

BC.Game's interim in-game leader had nothing but praise for the team's new addition.

To fill the gaps left behind in their roster, BC.Game elected to bring on former The MongolZ rifler Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold on loan, while moving assistant coach Robin "⁠ScrunK⁠" Röpke into the in-game leader position on a temporary basis.

After his team's elimination from the event, assistant coach turned in-game leader ScrunK spoke with HLTV regarding returning to the server as a captain, the difficulties the team faced against B8, and what it has been like to work alongside new addition Senzu.

Or you can listen to the interview here.

Very difficult start to that series, 13-2 loss on Overpass. What do you think went wrong for the team on the first map?

I mean, we started good winning the pistol, and then we fucked up the second round, we didn't execute it properly. That kinda got into our head already, and I don't know, maybe it's the time or whatever, but we are not really there yet, because in these games, if you lose momentum pretty early it's gonna be hard sometimes to bounce back. Then, when people are not on point when it comes to communication and everything, which could be just it's the first map and early in the game. Then it was rough to bounce back, because we weren't really aligned, people were doing a lot of solo peaks, not trading, etc. Usually, we are better at this. It was just an off game for everybody, I feel like, and it's hard when you lose momentum that early.

Were those communication issues something you targeted going into Mirage?

Definitely. We knew if we lose Mirage it's going to be our last map of the tournament, so we're gonna give it everything, not that we didn't try to do it before. We just talked about that we need to step it up a lot, we have no chance winning like this, and on Mirage I feel like we did, even though on CT-side we also made some mistakes we usually shouldn't do. (laughs) I feel like it's so sad to say but we got activated in the fourth half of two maps, and it kinda sucks, because is you play like we did on T-side Mirage I think we would win both maps. But, we didn't, and they played really good, so kudos to them, they deserved the win today and we didn't.

What were the expectations like for this event, with Senzu coming in on loan and you jumping back into the server to in-game lead? How far did you think you'd be able to go?

Expectations is a strong word, maybe the goal is to get playoffs, but what can we expect from this lineup? We don't know what to expect from ourselves, we can't predict how I'm going to be calling for the first time after a year, especially the first time playing with these kinds of players, which is also different. When I was playing tier-two teams before, it was a one-man show, micromanaging here and everything, but I don't need to tell s1mple or electroNic what to do, so for me it's also different. For me there weren't really expectations, but obviously, you want to win and get to the playoffs. We felt like we could do it, especially with Senzu, and s1mple as you saw yesterday, I think you also saw how crazy good they are. When you have these kinds of players on your team, you always want to go as far as possible.

I mean, let's be realistic, if I don't hit double-digits kill every map, that's not going to happen for me to keep playing

Robin "⁠ScrunK⁠" Röpke

What has it been like working with Senzu so far?

It's been absolutely great. He came in, super nice guy, super humble as well. Eager to get good, and you can tell he has a champion's mentality, he wants to win. Good things to say about the guy.

Obviously, he's just on loan right now; do you have any idea if there's any expectation that this might turn into something long-term?

I don't know any of this. I'm just coaching, playing, whatever it is they want me to do at the moment.

Right now, you're in-game leading; as far as your fifth goes, is there a possibility you could keep the role, could it go to electroNic, or is the org committed to finding a new leader?

I mean, let's be realistic, if I don't hit double-digits kill every map, that's not going to happen for me to keep playing. We have China coming up, who knows what's going to happen, maybe something clicks, and we start playing like prime Vitality. Who knows? (laughs) For me, I just focus on my job ahead, and doing the best I can as an in-game leader, and what the future will bring. I feel like BC.Game will check out the market and decide what's the best option for them.

CAC is the last event of the year for you guys, and it's quite a ways away. How are you going to spend your time to try and fix some of these issues heading into CAC?

I mean, it's a lot of talking, doing theory. We didn't have much pracc, so we don't have a lot of protocols, we haven't talked about how we want to solve some situations in the game. Just talking a lot more, and getting on the same page.
