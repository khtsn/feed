ZywOo: "We won four trophies already [this year] and we just feel like we cannot stop"

It was back to normal for Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut as he took his 31st MVP award at IEM Rio 2026.

Having just missed out on the medal after Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool's pristine playoffs in Rotterdam — the first time a teammate ever beat him to the MVP — ZywOo admitted his ego got "a bit hurt."

ZywOo claims the MVP at IEM Rio 2026

But the ever-modest Frenchman insisted that feeling passed with time: "If people are playing good it means we are winning as well, so I'm really happy for that. I will never get mad or angry about someone in my team to be above me or something, it means that we are also a good team, so I'm just happy."

In Rio, though, ZywOo made sure that there was no doubt about the destination of the custom medal. Dominance is an understatement for how he performed in the Farmasi Arena, with a 2.53 rating against NAVI, kicking off a relentless campaign to the title.

Congratulations, winner of IEM Rio and your 31st MVP medal. Usually, you say the MVP medal is a bonus, you'll take it, but last time you lost out for the first time. Does it feel better to get it this time then?

Yeah, of course it's feeling better. Like, we joke in the team that I got scammed by Robin [ropz] for this last MVP, but of course it's a small joke between us. We want everyone to perform in the team. Also, I'm making jokes about it, like if flameZ, or even apEX, or anyone in the team can get an MVP, it means they deserve it as well. So... of course I got hurt a bit because I was not used to it, but right now, I'm just making jokes about it.

You say you got hurt by it, did you use that as some fire?

It's not hurt, but you know, my ego. Like when you play good and you don't get the thing you deserved, or not deserved but you wanted, my ego got a bit hurt. But after like 30 seconds I don't really care about it, so I was just really happy for ropz to get it, and also to flameZ for the insane tournament he did.

So after all that time where you said, 'Ah, it's just the MVP,' there was a little bit of something to it of you wanting to get it in general.

Not too much, to be honest. I don't really think about it, like if people are playing good it means we are winning as well, so I'm really happy for that. I will never get mad or angry about someone in my team to be above me or something, it means that we are also a good team, so I'm just happy.

veryone still cannot believe that we are playing [this well]

Obviously a big moment with getting the Grand Slam done on your first chance. There was a lot of conversation about wanting to get it done before Cologne, so talk to me about that and becoming the first team to win two of them.

It's incredible to be honest, I don't think we expected anything to happen for Vitality like this, and now we're obviously winning two Grand Slams. I'm feeling really happy, really great, feeling like we are... to be honest, it's a lot of emotions, like a lot of things happened. Everyone still cannot believe that we are playing that good, and we just want to keep going in this way.

Keep going, and it's not because we won the Grand Slam, Rio, two Majors with this team that we're over. We don't know what is going to be next, maybe tomorrow it's going to be over for Vitality (smiles), in a way, we don't know if we're going to win the next tournament. So we just want to keep going in this way, keep going to win trophies, to be one of the best, and we'll see.

Was it really important to get it done this early so there's some pressure off Cologne?

Yeah, of course, less pressure for Cologne. It's pretty much the same as last year, like Melbourne was a lot of pressure for us. I think it was a bit less pressure for us at this tournament because we're used to it, and we knew what we were going to fight for.

As we move forward in the season, you actually have a chance to win another Grand Slam this year if you win Atlanta, Cologne, EPL, and IEM China. I'm guessing you haven't talked about it yet, but is that going to be a goal?

Of course. I mean, people on Twitter or I don't know, someone in the team talked about already, but for us it's not really in our minds yet. The next step is going to Cologne because it's going to be the next really big step at the Major, so obviously we are not really focused on if we have a Grand Slam to do again this year. Because it's so much time, so much preparation, so many things to do before being able to win the Grand Slam, so we're not really focusing about this now.

It's pretty weird to be winning that many trophies. I feel it's just a copy and paste from last year

A 3-0 in the end. That means in your last [five] best-of-fives, you've lost two maps total, so just talk to me about that dominance.

To be honest, when we are playing like this, even though we did a lot of mistakes today, we still played good as a team. Our mentality has been really good lately, so when we are like this, when we are playing this good... to be honest, it could be Spirit, Falcons, or anybody else in front of us, and we're going to beat them. Today they faced a great Vitality, so it was tough for anybody to face us tonight. Now, we just play good, we felt like we had to do something for the Grand Slam, to play with this pressure in a way, and I think we did great with this.

It's the first time you played them since the Budapest Major semis, and it's obviously a different roster for them now. What did you notice that was different this time around?

The difference today, I will say tN1R. tN1R played insanely good, he had some crucial rounds where sometimes we could... maybe not smash them, but he turned the round really quick. And the way magixx is calling makes them play a bit better as a team, but yeah, I think it's also tough. He's still a young in-game leader, so I want to see later what he is going to improve, what he is going to bring into the team, because it's still too early to talk about it. Being in-game leader, you need at least a good one year to see what you can improve and bring into the team, so let's see in the future.

Fort Worth next, and you are just continuing to win all these trophies. Are you looking to match what you had last year, and what is the setup like going into this next event?

To be honest, I don't know (laughs), like it's pretty weird to be winning that many trophies. I feel it's just a copy and paste from last year (smiles). We did a bit better in the BLAST semi-final, but yeah, it's pretty much the same. We won four trophies already and we just feel like we cannot stop, to be honest. We are so good as a team, so good as humans together, and we can keep going like that... not forever, but for a long time. We're going to just keep going for that, play good, and lift trophies.
