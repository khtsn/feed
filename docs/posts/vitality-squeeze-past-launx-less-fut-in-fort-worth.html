Vitality squeeze past lauNX-less FUT in Fort Worth

Vitality got their BLAST Rivals Season 1 campaign off to a winning start with a hard-fought 2-1 victory against FUT, assuring themselves of a spot in the playoffs at the Dickies Arena regardless of the outcome of their next match.

Playing with head coach András "⁠coolio⁠" Fercsák instead of Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea, who is unavailable for this tournament due to health reasons, FUT exceeded all expectations by taking Dust2, snapping Vitality's 17-game win streak on the map, and nearly pulling off a stunning comeback from 11-1 down on Nuke.

The PGL Bucharest champions will have another chance to advance from the group stage on Thursday, against the loser of Astralis vs G2.

"We played really badly," Vitality head coach Rémy "⁠XTQZZZ⁠" Quoniam said after the match. "Not disciplined at all. We threw away many rounds on Nuke and Dust2. We didn't play together. It was too individual. It was a really bad game.

"It's still a win, but not happy at all."

William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman led the charge on the CT side of Mirage, posting a stunning 2.25 rating and +16.43% round swing as Vitality raced to a 9-3 lead after shutting down any chances of FUT stringing rounds together.

Vitality's dominance continued early in the second half as they bounced back from a pistol-round loss and quickly finished off the game.

After a quiet performance on Mirage (by his lofty standards), Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut began to show flashes of impact play on Dust2. He went 16-6 (2.04 rating and +8.66% round swing) in a highly entertaining first half that ended in a 6-6 stalemate, prompting Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire to give his team an angry verbal reprimand at halftime.

It didn't produce the desired results, however, as FUT reached map point on the back of two streaks of three rounds. Vitality still tried to force overtime but fell at the final hurdle in a 5v5 retake attempt, suffering their first loss on the map since November 14, 2025.

Vitality entered Nuke with fire in their bellies and went on a dominant 11-0 run on the CT side after losing the pistol. FUT refused to throw in the towel and kept eating away at the deficit, only to fall in the last round of regulation after a costly mistake in a 5v3 situation against a low buy a few rounds earlier.
