Falcons beat MOUZ in Shanghai to make second consecutive grand final

Falcons have qualified for the CS Asia Championships grand final after getting the better of MOUZ in a three-map series (Mirage 7-13, Dust 13-7, Nuke 13-10).

The victory nets Falcons their second grand final in a row since signing Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen, with the winner of MIBR vs. Legacy set to meet them in the best-of-five trophy clash in what is a fantastic chance for the Saudi organization to end their trophy drought.

MOUZ will aim to secure their second third-place finish in a row on Sunday against the loser of the aforementioned Brazilian derby.

Falcons got off to a good start in the series thanks to a 1vs2 clutch by Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač in the second round, giving them a chance to go 4-1 up before MOUZ could answer. Their reply was strong, however, with Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman's troops winning the last seven rounds of the half to take a commanding lead into the second half.

René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen's pistol-round clutch gave Falcons a ray of hope early on in the second half, but a force-buy victory for MOUZ meant they could breeze to a very comfortable 13-7 win.

Falcons then quickly proved why they picked Dust2, as Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin led the way in an incredibly one-sided start. The star-studded roster ran away to an 11-3 lead, and while they had some issues closing out, they never allowed MOUZ to get close and tied up the series with a 13-7 win.

The Falcons domination kept going on Nuke. They couldn't put a foot wrong on the CT side, with NiKo securing two clutches while TeSeS (2.04 CT rating) and kyousuke (1.47) locked down the upper site and Ramp.

Falcons once again had some issues closing it out, but two half-buy victories netted them four match points before an impactful NiKo lurk secured them a spot in the grand final.
