Vitality beat MOUZ to go 2-1 in Stage 3

Vitality are one victory away from the IEM Cologne Major playoffs following a 2-1 win against MOUZ, who are now staring at elimination from the event.

It was a much-needed win for Vitality after the surprising loss to 9z on Friday. But their masterclass performance on the decider map of Inferno doesn't mask how vulnerable they looked at times in the other two maps, especially on Dust2, where they are now on a two-game losing streak.

"We were a bit slow on the rotations, and sometimes it catches us off-guard," Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan said after the match. "I don't know if it's nerves from the Major or… It happens. Those mistakes are crucial, and some of the rounds we lost, MOUZ did really good plays and got really good headshots.

"But we're still very confident on Dust2. It's really small mistakes that led us to losing this map. But we're still confident."

A 1v2 clutch from Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut in the first round of Mirage kick-started a dominant run from Vitality, who established a 5-0 lead on the CT side. The rest of the half was all about MOUZ's dynamic offense as they were able to cancel out the deficit, led by Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman, who boasted a +10.42% round swing.

Vitality put a stop to MOUZ's momentum with a much-needed pistol and followed that up with a streak of rounds. Just like in the first half, MOUZ responded with a run of their own before Vitality killed the game off with some impactful late-round fragging by William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman.

The closely contested nature continued on Dust2, this time with Vitality bouncing back from an early deficit to restore parity before half-time.

ZywOo fired Vitality into the lead with a Dual Berettas quad-kill in the pistol round, only for them to lose the anti-force as Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin picked up three quick frags with a Galil on A. Vitality went missing for the rest of the half as MOUZ calmly put the game to bed on the back of two successful 2v3 post-plants.

ZywOo and Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool stole the show during the first half of Inferno, combining for 33 frags to help Vitality to an 8-4 lead. They carried that momentum into the second half, winning five rounds in a row to close out the series.
