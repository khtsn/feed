BIT: "It's good to be here, honestly we never know how good we're going to be because we're not used to playing those teams"

9z's coach is hoping to get a better sense of how his team fares outside of South America over the next few events.

9z claimed the second and final upset at the tail end of the first day of PGL Astana by way of demolishing PARIVISION 2-0 in their Swiss stage opener, an effort led by an otherworldly CT effort by Luciano "⁠luchov⁠" Herrera on Inferno (24-7 K-D, 207.9 ADR, 3.73 CT rating) and a 2.32-rated Ancient by Franco "⁠dgt⁠" Garcia.

"I feel like we have a good matchup against them because we played against them in the Thunderpick qualifier last year, and we also beat them 2-0," Bruno "⁠BIT⁠" Fukuda Lima told HLTV after the match. "It was not easy, but it was a good match as well. I don't know, I feel like we have a good read of their games, and also when the players are playing like this individually, it helps a lot (smiles)."

The win adds to a growing resume of strong results for the Argentine organization, which secured a Stage 2 invite to the IEM Cologne Major after lifting several trophies in South America since the start of the year. That also earned the team invites to tier-one events, and they made an impression at their first big outing at BLAST Open Rotterdam by eliminating MOUZ, despite suffering blowout losses on either side to Spirit and Vitality.

"We beat MOUZ, so it was a very good showing that we can beat those teams," BIT said. "But at the same time, we played Vitality and Spirit and we had no chance against them. So it was a very good feeling, but at the same it was like, 'Oh, maybe we are not as close as we were thinking.' We just came back and we kept grinding CS as much as we can."

A big win to start off your run in Astana. An insane showing for luchov on Inferno, and then dgt on Ancient. I saw it on your face, you were really happy, so just tell me how you're feeling after that?

Yeah, it feels very good. It obviously is a very important win for the team, especially at the beginning of the tournament. Having a good game like this, especially individually, the guys played very great. meyern played great on the CT side of Ancient; dgt on Ancient as well. luchov on Inferno, yeah, it was crazy (laughs). 30 bombs, I guess? I'm not sure, I think he killed 32 or something?

He was even 27-4 on CT side.

Yeah (laughs), I was just behind him and every round he was getting two kills, four kills, it was insane. Yeah, of course it's important for the confidence, for the tournament, for the VRS, for everything. It was a very, very good win for us.

A very one-sided win, too. Why was it so dominant for you?

I don't know. I feel like we have a good matchup against them because we played against them in the Thunderpick qualifier last year, and we also beat them 2-0. It was not easy, but it was a good match as well. I don't know, I feel like we have a good read of their games, and also when the players are playing like this individually, it helps a lot (smiles).

Bruno "⁠BIT⁠" Fukuda Lima

In general, it has been a really strong year for the team. You won a few LANs in South America, and then got the chance in Rotterdam. You beat MOUZ, but obviously a very hard path with Vitality and Spirit too. When you left that event, what did you take away from it to come into this one?

When we left BLAST, it was kind of mixed feelings because first of all, we beat MOUZ, so it was a very good showing that we can beat those teams. But at the same time, we played Vitality and Spirit and we had no chance against them. So it was a very good feeling, but at the same it was like, 'Oh, maybe we are not as close as we were thinking.' We just came back and we kept grinding CS as much as we can; we took some break as well.

So it's good to be here, honestly every time we come here we never know how good we're going to be because we're not used to playing those teams. So right now, we are in a good path to keep attending those tournaments, and right now we have a lot of tournaments to play. We have the Major, we have PGL Astana, we have a tournament in China; we have a lot of tournaments, so I think we can at least know what our level is because you need to play those teams to understand.

I saw some criticism about you making it straight to Stage 2 of the Major by playing mostly in SA, but I'm sure you would've wanted these opportunities to play more [abroad] too. So I just wanted to give you a chance to comment on that, playing so much in SA and not getting these chances now to play in these events.

I mean, the VRS is a complex ranking, you know? (laughs) I think we had a good path and a good read on the ranking, on how to build the schedule and everything. What to play, what not to play, but in the end, what matters is if you win or lose. So I think we deserve it because we won a lot of matches, a lot of tournaments, and fortunately in Brazil right now we have a lot of big tournaments with big prize pools as well, so it helps a lot.

But yeah, it's not like... I think we showed a good game in BLAST, and we also went to Brazil and played Legacy, we beat Legacy, we beat paiN, we beat Imperial, we beat all the teams besides FURIA that we never played. I think we beat all the Brazilian teams, so I think it was at least deserved on this side.

Talk to me about the path of the team overall and how you've seen them improve over time. You've been behind them coaching for a while, but there were some changes at the start of the year, so talk to me about the progress and what you're seeing.

First of all, the team just clicked together. The players get along very well, and that helps the team a lot because we have been together for 24 hours every day. We play a lot together, we have been traveling together a lot, so it helps a lot that the team gets along very well. I think that helps inside the game. Everyone is trusting each other, which is very hard.

I talked to the guys a lot about this because... I think sometimes, it's hard to keep this mood, especially when you are together all the time. Maybe you lose some matches, maybe you are staying together for a long time, and it's never easy. It's easy when you are a new team, but it's never easy in the long [run], so this is something we are trying to take care of a lot as the coaching staff, me and exp.

I think we are doing good just because of this, and of course we have a good captain, we are doing very good prep for the matches, and having the confidence to win matches helps a lot. Because when you are losing a lot of matches, you lose confidence, and we have to enjoy this moment that we are going through and that is helping a lot.

On one side, you're winning those matches and those LANs in South America, and then you're coming to an event that's bigger now. You had those results in Rotterdam we talked about, and now you've gotten this win over PARIVISION, but there are a lot of other big teams here too. So what are you hoping for 9z to be able to do here?

You always hope to win, but it's very hard to have those high expectations in your mind because in the end, there are so many different teams and so many different styles right now, in Europe especially.

There are teams from everywhere here, so I think we just need to keep grinding, keep playing game by game, and if we keep playing good like this, the way we played today as a team, as individuals, I think the results are going to show up. There is nothing we can do about the results, you know? The maximum we can do is grind as much as we can, and maybe it's going to show or not. I think we're grinding a lot, and that's helping our team.

You touched on it briefly, in terms of making the Major, you said it is deserved. How do you feel overall about going straight to Stage 2 and managing to go to Cologne later this year?

It's a very good feeling, especially because 9z was out of the Major for maybe four years, I think?

Yeah, 2022.

Yeah, it was a very long time since 9z qualified for the last Major, so it feels good to come back to the team and make it to the Major, help the guys to go back to a Major. We know that this is important not only for the players, but for the org as well, to maybe have big investments. The org is trying to do as much as they can for the team, like paying for bootcamps, paying buyouts. We bought luchov from BESTIA (smiles), which was a huge signing for us, we got dgt, so...

It's never cheap in South America for those buyouts either, I'm sure.

(laughs) Yeah, it's never cheap and it's never easy as well, because luchov was in the rival of 9z, so it was a very hard signing. But I think it's very good because everything is working right now, and it feels good to be in the Major, it feels good to be in Stage 2, because we're going to have a little bit more time to prepare, maybe go to Europe. We have some Stake tournaments before the Major and some days to practice before, so I think that is when we have to be at our peak.
