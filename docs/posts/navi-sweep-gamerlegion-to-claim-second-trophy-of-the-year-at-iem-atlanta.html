NAVI sweep GamerLegion to claim second trophy of the year at IEM Atlanta

Despite growing into the series as it wore on, GamerLegion couldn't handle the experience and depth offered by NAVI.

Natus Vincere have claimed the IEM Atlanta 2026 trophy in emphatic fashion, sweeping GamerLegion aside 3-0 to secure their second title of the year, after winning ESL Pro League in March.

Natus Vincere had already made a bold statement in this tournament by besting Vitality in the quarter-finals, breaking a multi-year streak of defeats at the hands of the best team in the world. Leaving without a trophy would, however, have soured the taste of said victory.

On the other side of the server were a young GamerLegion lineup who had shown immense growth across the event, with Milan "⁠hypex⁠" Polowiec in particular impressing with his improved level.

Ultimately Natus Vincere were too much for GamerLegion to handle. Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov continued his incredible event with consistent fragging output, Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen spent much of the series calling rings around his opposite number, and Natus Vincere were ultimately deserved winners.

Mirage was an entirely one-sided affair to start the series, GamerLegion finding themselves entirely outclassed on their first map pick.

After Natus Vincere got the better of the force-buy war early on their CT side, they never looked back, beating away GamerLegion's varied attacks despite the unreal efforts of Fredrik "⁠REZ⁠" Sterner, sealing the deal with ease after the sides switched.

b1t cleans up a dangerous round with a robotic 3K

Things were far more interesting on Anubis as GamerLegion put forth stiffer resistance. GamerLegion managed to get the better of the early force-buy war this time around and made things scrappy, relying on individual playmaking from the likes of hypex and REZ. Natus Vincere had a stellar w0nderful to fall back on, however, who was enough to seal a six-round defensive haul.

GamerLegion picked up their CT pistol and answered back after Natus Vincere's first round win, setting the map up for an interesting finish, only for Aleksib to have the last laugh. Natus Vincere began to completely outplay GamerLegion on the map, rocking back and forth between points of attack and leaving GamerLegion bamboozled, racking up six rounds on the bounce to seal a 2-0 series lead.

hypex runs through flames to pull off insane 1v3

The start of Nuke saw GamerLegion come out swinging on their offense, running out to a 4-0 lead thanks to fast-paced attacks that left Natus Vincere reeling. Natus Vincere adjusted, however, finding more success in dispelling the faster plays and seeming the more comfortable team in the slower rounds, leading to a tied halftime score.

Aleksib must have thought he had set Natus Vincere on the path to victory with a classy clutch on the T pistol, but Sebastian "⁠Tauson⁠" Tauson Lindelof found impact with a wacky 1v2 clutch to finish off his team's force-buy, and GamerLegion ran with the momentum. The likes of hypex and Oldřich "⁠PR⁠" Nový grew in confidence and stature and started to beat Natus Vincere back with ease, leading the team all the way to 12 rounds.

Aleksib posts classy 1v2 clutch in key pistol round

GamerLegion couldn't take their chance. Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri came alive on Natus Vincere's offense, finding four opening kills in a row to give his team the room to make their playbook shine. Five round wins in a row sent the map to OT, where Natus Vincere's experience shone through as they sealed the IEM Atlanta trophy.

The Cologne Major race heats up

Natus Vincere made a real statement with their campaign at IEM Atlanta, and not just by securing the trophy. They defeated Vitality along the way, breaking a long multi-year streak of losses to Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire and company.

With FURIA seeming on the upswing, Spirit having won PGL Astana and Falcons looking impressive with Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen among their number, something is brewing ahead of the IEM Cologne Major.

At one point, it would have seemed ridiculous to expect anyone but Vitality to win the first Major of the year. Now, there is a world where multiple teams might be able to give them a run for their money, one of them being the winners here today, Natus Vincere.
