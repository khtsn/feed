HeavyGod: "NertZ maybe wasn't the best in Liquid, but I knew what he was capable of and I always told him that"

G2's anchor spoke about how NertZ's voice and style are already impacting the team.

G2 trounced Monte in the penultimate group stage match of PGL Astana to secure a Barys Arena berth, getting the job done in the 2-2 pool after staring elimination the face following losses to MOUZ and The MongolZ.

"Two days ago we had not the best start, and we wanted to have a good start and fighting spirit," Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko told HLTV after his team qualified for the playoffs. "I'm really happy with how we played, and big applause to huNter- because he was calling really good and NertZ was helping a lot with mid-round calls and this stuff."

The team is still working to find consistency after the addition of HeavyGod's former Endpoint teammate, Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz, and has been hamstrung by limited practice after Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač missed IEM Rio due to injury. But the Israeli rifler is already finding his voice in the team and working to integrate his own style, putting his dismal stint in Liquid behind.

"[NertZ is] doing really good, and I'm really happy with the way he is developing as well as a player because in Liquid, he maybe wasn't the best, but I knew what he was capable of and I always told him that," HeavyGod said. "I'm happy to have him by my side."

Listen to the interview here.

Congratulations, into the playoffs with a pretty dominant win over Monte to get it done. First of all, give me your thoughts on that match and how it went.

We were well prepared against what they were going to do, and we had good energy. This is really important for us, to have good energy, because two days ago we had not the best start, and we wanted to have a good start and fighting spirit.

I'm really happy with how we played, and big applause to huNter- because he was calling really good and NertZ was helping a lot with mid-round calls and this stuff. It was really good, and I enjoyed playing it.

You got it done on your last try here, so not the cleanest run from you. How do you look back on the whole run?

I mean a win is a win, a qualify is a qualify, that's how I always see it. Obviously we want it cleanly, but if we don't get it cleanly, it's whatever. Our focus is just to improve, get a good connection with each other, and develop more as a team because we didn't have so many practice days and it's a bit tough. It's a learning process for us because we have a short time together with NertZ and he's a different player.

When you're taking some of these losses, what has the conversation been like when you go back into the practice room afterward?

I mean, every day we're having something new (laughs). It's always like this in the beginning when you change a bit of stuff, but basically, it depends really on what we had the mistakes on or what we need to focus on, really. Like, as I said, the fighting spirit, or maybe some strats that we didn't do correctly. It's always small stuff; it can never be perfect and it will never be, so you just learn and try to fix the maximum that you can.

As you touched on, you haven't had a ton of time with huNter- because he was out and NertZ just came in, which has changed up the dynamic a bit. How do you feel about where the team is now in terms of consistency and the amount of time you've had together to practice?

I think we're working on it still, to find the consistency, and as I said, we need to develop ourselves as a team. It's really important to do that as a unit, and it's going to take time for us to build it because Rome wasn't built in a day, as a lot of people like to say (smiles). We just grind a lot, we have a lot of discussions about CS, and it's really important because we want to grind a lot and be better as a team.

We are really good friends outside of the game, and... yeah, I mean, he's really aggressive, sometimes maybe he's crazy (smiles), and he's good

Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko on NertZ

Let's talk about NertZ a bit. Obviously, a bit of a reunion for you as you played on Endpoint together, the Israeli connection as well. What has it been like having him in the team?

We are really good friends outside of the game, and... yeah, I mean, he's really aggressive, sometimes maybe he's crazy (smiles), and he's good. He has a lot of ideas and obviously he has a different vision in the game, and also a big voice. He also wants to do stuff his way, so it's really good that he can implement his playstyle in our structure and get some help from the staff and huNter-.

He's doing really good, and I'm really happy with the way he is developing as well as a player because in Liquid, he maybe wasn't the best, but I knew what he was capable of and I always told him that. I'm happy to have him by my side.

You say he has a bit of a different vision. Has him coming into the team changed your style in any way?

He's maybe more vocal, I would say, and then he can control a bit more. He likes to do it, and that's what is helping us a bit more than before. Yeah, I'm just happy.

Whenever I've seen G2 at events, you're always laughing, loud, having some fun. It seems like NertZ has really added to that atmosphere. How much has he helped keep the mood up throughout these events, even when you had a stand-in and whatnot?

He is a funny guy; he likes to troll. I think all of us, we are a team that trolls a lot and we are doing a lot of jokes and stuff. He is a happy guy in general, and it's good that he can add this to our team. It's not like previously we didn't have fun or something, but he's also doing it really good and we're just happy and like to do jokes (laughs).

It seemed like G2 was on the up for a wihle, coming a little close to making some deeper runs, and then NertZ came in and you've had a bit of a reset. How far do you think you are from being able to win titles, obviously with some challenges like NAVI and Vitality being the main contenders right now?

Yeah, I mean, obviously we always want to be title contenders and to win trophies. How far are we? I don't know, I cannot say because, as I said, it's a building process and it's really hard for me to know. We're just trying to develop and do our best, and obviously we want to be there as soon as possible, but we don't want to hit our peak so fast and then drop down. We want it to be consistent and always fight for trophies.

I do want to take you back to the FaZe loss at Rivals. People thought that you'd be able to make a deeper run there, but obviously the way that ended was unfortunate. Can you just talk to me about that game and what you took away from it?

We took away a lot of stuff from that game. We were handling situations wrong in the game, and we didn't... shoot properly, I would say. It's also a shooting game in the end (smiles), and maybe we weren't fully focused as well at the beginning, or mid-game. It was different each map. So we really took a lot from this, like if it's strats we need to fix, if it's priority voices, like a lot of stuff in the game.

When you say you weren't handling situations right, what do you mean?

Like, I don't know, maybe overpeeking a bit or just talking on top of each other, just small stuff that can change a lot.

Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko on huNter-'s evolution as IGL

You're going into the arena now, so a big chance for you to get some redemption from that Rivals run. What are your thoughts going into that with teams like Falcons and Spirit as challengers as well? What do you make of your chances in the arena?

First of all, I'm excited to play in the arena in front of the Kazakh people; I think they're amazing and they're showing insane amounts of support. I don't know, I'm not really thinking about it or I don't think we will think about it; we'll just take it one game at a time. We just want to grind it and do our best.

Lastly, I also wanted to ask about huNter-'s evolution as an IGL. How do you think he's faring about being in the role for a while?

Obviously, it's taking time to be an IGL like the big ones, but I think he's doing a good job. Slowly he's developing, he's also changing a bit of his vision, and he's also adapting to us a bit to us with the players and our style. He can do a lot of stuff, and that's amazing because he knows how to help everyone in the server and off the server, and also how to adapt [well] with the calls and the plays that people want to do.
