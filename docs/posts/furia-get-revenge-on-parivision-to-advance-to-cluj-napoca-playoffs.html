FURIA get revenge on PARIVISION to advance to Cluj-Napoca playoffs

FURIA are through to the playoffs of PGL Cluj-Napoca after outlasting PARIVISION in a three-map series (16-14 Dust2, 14-16 Inferno, 13-8 Mirage).

It was a taste of revenge for the Brazilian-majority team after they fell 0-2 to Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali's side in the semi-final of BLAST Bounty Finals at the start of the year, and a victory that came by way of a revival from Mareks "⁠YEKINDAR⁠" Gaļinskis on the Mirage decider.

The teams traded labored overtime wins on each other's picks of Dust2 and Inferno with mistakes, solid offensive calling, and scrappy mid-rounds aplenty spread across both sides, but the Latvian rifler's impact on the decider and FURIA's experience and tenacity when the pressure was on made all the difference while PARIVISION withered away, leaving them one last chance to qualify for the playoffs against FUT.

FURIA were humbled early on Dust2, only squeaking out a pistol win due to time after a Emil "⁠nota⁠" Moskvitin quad kill and then running headfirst into a full B stack to lose the ensuing round to pistols.

That momentum swap let PARIVISION dominate early for a 5-1 lead before back-to-back double openers from YEKINDAR turned the balance, allowing FURIA to tie the game by the break.

A second pistol win and conversion then gave FURIA the lead for the first time since the start of the map, but meticulous offensive calling by Jame that punished FURIA for overextending in a few mid-rounds swung the pendulum again as PARIVISION won six of the next eight to hit map point first, 12-10.

FURIA were up against the ropes, but managed to force overtime in round 24 after Yuri "⁠yuurih⁠" Santos made up for a quiet map with a pivotal 1v2 clutch to stop his team from squandering a 5v3. The solution was found in overtime as Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo pulling out a secondary AWP shut down PARIVISION's map control efforts, with two double kills on the CT side helping FURIA scrape a 16-14 victory on their opponent's pick.

A near-identical scenario unfolded on Inferno, where a pistol ace from Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko net FURIA the first two rounds, but a fumbled round to time and a few excellent site stacks let PARIVISION reply with five of their own. FURIA salvaged five by the break, but continued to lose out in opening duels as PARIVISION won the second pistol to go up 9-5 early after the swap.

Brutal utility in Banana and a spectacular recovery from YEKINDAR, who at one point was 1-7 in opening duels, were key in FURIA getting back into the map. But PARIVISION were unrelenting in their efforts to attack B and managed to force overtime after calling for a half-buy despite winning the round at 11-10, and a costly overpeek by yuurih in Pit in OT let PARIVISION turn a 3v5 and eventually march to victory.

FURIA's struggles in openers reared again on Mirage, and their pistol and conversion only offered small salvation with PARIVISION trading out efficiently for a 4-2 lead. Multi-kills from YEKINDAR and FalleN broke that slide and let FURIA tie at 6-6 as they swapped to the attack, and they didn't let up from there.

PARIVISION's riflers struggled immensely in the second half, with nota in particular going missing, while YEKINDAR transcended with multiple high-impact multi-frags. The Latvian took the game into his own hands and led FURIA's charge to victory, with just two rounds slipping away in an otherwise dominant offensive streak.
