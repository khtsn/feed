FlyQuest came into the Cologne Major with limited practice and found themselves in the 1-2 pool after the second day of action.

They then comfortably won the domestic derby against THUNDER dOWNUNDER before securing a dominant 2-0 victory over Liquid to lock in their spot in Stage 2, equalling their result from Budapest.

"Beating Liquid pretty convincingly is going to help us build our confidence," Joshua "⁠INS⁠" Potter told HLTV after qualifying. "I think with the limited practice time we have, we're very happy with how we've performed so far."

Congrats on making it through, it ended up being a pretty straightforward win for you guys. I wanted to first ask about the veto, because Anubis was pretty unexpected, at least for me. Do you feel the same, and how happy were you when you saw it?

I mean, it was very unexpected. We didn't even prepare for it, because we thought there's no way they would let us pick it considering we both have the Overpass ban. We were very happy to be able to pick that and just play on a comfortable map for us as the first map.

About the entire stage, you guys had some really close best-of-ones, right, and then yesterday you beat your countrymen. Do you feel like you're leveling up as the stage goes by? Since you haven't had a lot of tier-one experience and international experience this year yet.

Even in the best-of-ones against GamerLegion and NRG, we were in those games until the end. It was just those crucial moments where we made some mistakes, and we kind of lost from there. I think beating THUNDER dOWNUNDER pretty convincingly, and beating Liquid pretty convincingly is going to help us build our confidence.
