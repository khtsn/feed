Falcons win two 13-11 maps to secure third place in Rio

m0NESY and NiKo topped the board for Falcons

Falcons won the third-place decider match at IEM Rio in a close series against FURIA, which needed two maps, Dust2 and Mirage, but both went down to the wire.

The series kicked off on Dust2 with FURIA picking up the pistol and conversion on their T side to start 3-0 up. When guns came up Falcons took over, however, René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen driving their defense forward with his aggressive manoeuvres and, alongside Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov, guiding Falcons to a convincing CT half, with Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato the only man really offering resistance.

With the sides switched FURIA once again started in the ascendancy but began to lag when the guns came up. An impactful round from Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko's hero AWP and Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo's USP on a bad buy gave them some hope, but it was too little too late as Falcons found the two rounds needed to close the map.

Falcons took an early lead on the T side of Mirage and looked well on their way to a dominant second map, but FURIA came alive with a four round streak to make it 5-5 before a Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač deagle quad put Falcons back in the driver's seat at the half, 7-5.

Falcons got up to an 11-7 lead heading into the final stretch of the map, well in control, but FURIA had one last burst. A fumbled flash by m0NESY in a set play up 11-9 opened the door for FURIA to come back, as they tied the game at 11-11, but a last-second defuse by NiKo the following round set up match point, which was converted when m0NESY denied KSCERATO a quad-kill in a 1v1 clutch in the final round of regulation.
