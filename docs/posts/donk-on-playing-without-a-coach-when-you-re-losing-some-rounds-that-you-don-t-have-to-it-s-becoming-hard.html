donk on playing without a coach: "When you're losing some rounds that you don't have to, it's becoming hard"

Spirit still have a flawless record in Cologne after progressing through Stage 2 and 3 without losing a match under Jab "⁠Jab Jabich⁠" Jabich's leadership, but Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets is aware that not having a coach could present some difficulties to the Russian squad.

"There is no one who can take a pause and say what happened, what went wrong and how we can come back," he told HLTV at the IEM Cologne media day.

"You're losing control. The best example of this was Overpass against 9z. We lost the comeback and we lost our minds, I would say."

Despite this, the Russian superstar is confident Spirit can bring their best game to the LANXESS, where they are set to face off against G2 in the first round of the playoffs.

Watch the interview on HLTV's YouTube channel.

Congratulations on making it to the playoffs. You were a lot more tested in Stage 3 compared to Stage 2, and especially that 9z game towards the end. What was different in that game especially?

I think the main reason why the game was so tough for us is that we were pretty tired. We didn't have a day off for like 20 days or maybe more. We had praccs, some theory things, and after we came here we had officials, officials, officials, and in Stage 3 we had two best-of-threes.

In the Aurora match you could see that it was still tough even though we won like 13-4, 13-5 on second and third map. We still lost Dust2, which is the best map in our pool. I think the main reason is just our level of fatigue, how hard we were grinding this month.

How different is it to play without a coach, somebody with an overarching view of what's going on?

When you dominate matches or maps, when you win the pistol, the first gunround, the second gunround, and it's a 6-0 score, it's nothing hard. You feel that you control the game and you feel good in terms of dynamic and everything is great. But when you're losing some rounds that you don't have to lose, or you're throwing or something like that, it's becoming hard.

There is no one who can take a pause and say what happened, what went wrong and how we can come back. You're losing control. The coach has to take timeouts when he feels something is going wrong and the players are losing control of the game. The best example of this was Overpass against 9z. We lost the comeback and we lost our minds, I would say.

Couldn't find answers.

Yeah, you're right. We just couldn't find answers and a coach would have probably helped us, but we don't have one.

Does it mean you have to play a bit more simplistic? How does it change your game?

I think it's more about the middle of the game. If you have some problems, you have to play more simple. Yeah, we can take a pause and call something difficult to win the round, but now it's not our work, it's not our mission, I would say, and it's more about the coach.

We are trying our best, and sometimes, like in the match against Aurora, we were taking timeouts. I took the first timeout and zont1x took the second timeout because we all felt like, 'oh, what's happening, we are losing our best map.' We're trying our best.

Going into the playoffs, you have a very familiar matchup first in G2. It's been very one-sided this year. What do you think makes it that, and how confident are you heading into the playoffs with that being the first matchup?

I think we've never had problems against this G2 roster. We only lost to them one time online for [BLAST] London and that's it. We're good in terms of understanding them.

We don't have some expectations that we will 100% beat them because it's playoff of the Major and everything can happen. The last Major, we had lost to Falcons 0-7 or 0-8 and we beat them in the first round of playoffs, so it can happen to us.
