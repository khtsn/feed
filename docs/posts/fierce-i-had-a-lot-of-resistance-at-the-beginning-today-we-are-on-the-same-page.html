Fierce: "I had a lot of resistance at the beginning; today, we are on the same page"

BetBoom completed a shocking run to the IEM Cologne Major playoffs with a dominant 2-0 victory over FUT in the 2-2 pool, managing to make it to the LANXESS despite playing the Major with two stand-ins.

"We were joking around that we can add two more players next season, so we can mix it up any time and just come to the tournaments and do some damage," Artem "⁠Fierce⁠" Ivanov told HLTV with a smile on his face after making the playoffs.

The 36-year-old coach, who only joined the team in January, talked about how the team has developed during his tenure, despite the stand-in situation, and the kind of voice he has within the team.

"Today, it's pretty simple; we are on the same page," he said. "Boombl4 is super experienced, and Magnojez is younger, like super young. But first of all, he is talented, and he is a hard worker. He understands my vision. It was not that simple at the beginning, because I had a lot of resistance. Like I tell them something, they want to argue, they want to discuss every. Fucking. Time (laughs)."

`Congratulations, first of all, and what a run. Tell me what's going through your mind after making this run at the Major with stand-ins and everything around it.

Thank you. We were joking around that we can add two more players next season, so we can mix it up any time and just come to the tournaments and do some damage (smiles). Gladly, we have our core, like our main core, and we practiced a lot. We have a lot of [educational review], like when you discuss some mistakes you made.

Like, I'm not that glad; obviously, I'm super happy to play in the arena. This is the first time I'll play in the arena as a coach, and... I never played as a player as well (laughs), back in the days. That's cool, that's cool, let's see what we can do.

Talk to me about how it felt coming into this Major with the stand-ins and still managing to make this run. It's been uncertain for you, so how did you actually do it from your side? How did you work with the players so quickly and get them on board?

It's super hard to play against the top teams from all over the world, especially S-tier. I understand that I have players who are pretty aggressive with good aim. Basically, I'm trying to play around it.

Basically, we try to play as simple as we can, and I'm always trying to focus on the communication and the mental stuff. That we should definitely come into every match trying to beat the enemy, and not [playing] not to lose. For example, in this match, we were pretty good, but as we got closer to the win, the more intense TeamSpeak got, like...

Like more chaotic?

Yeah! Chaotic.

I have my own cup, and when it overflows, I can be very tough. You know, like Bad FalleN? I can turn into this man, but most of the time, I'm pretty understanding

You only joined this team five months ago, so how has it been working with them? You've had stand-in after stand-in recently, but just in general, how has it been working with Boombl4 and this team?

Today, it's pretty simple; we are on the same page. Boombl4 is super experienced, and Magnojez is younger, like super young. But first of all, he is talented, and he is a hard worker. He understands my vision. It was not that simple at the beginning, because I had a lot of resistance. Like I tell them something, they want to argue, they want to discuss every. Fucking. Time (laughs).

The first two months were pretty hard, but I know how to work with all this resistance; I'm pretty calm with this. I have my own cup, and when it overflows, I can be very tough. I have my own voice, like from the big guy. You know, like Bad FalleN? Like with the glasses? I can turn into this man, but most of the time I'm pretty understanding.

Did you have to turn into that bad man during this Major run at all?

A couple of times, a couple of times (smiles). In the practice room, I just wanted to add some noise on the phone, like when guys are yelling and going crazy in the practice room when I'm trying to say something, I just turn it on. It's like a horn, and that's it. It means silence (smiles).

Making this Major run is pretty unexpected, I'm sure it is for you as well. You played with different stand-ins over the last couple of months, so where does this leave BetBoom after this Major? You played pretty well with FL4MUS, and it's a bit uncertain with the visa issues, so do you have any thoughts about that yet or are you waiting until after the Major to decide?

We postponed this thing to, not after the Major, but after the tournament in China, XSE. In a month, we will see. I'm not sure about the future.

Heading into the playoffs, the LANXESS, it's a big stage, and Major playoffs at that. In your first match, you're now guaranteed to play Aurora, so what are you thoughts on that game?

It's going to be hard. Any team is super skilled, so I don't know. We need to practice some maps that we didn't play yet at the tournament, for sure, and that's it. We'll just try to beat them, I don't know what to say (laughs).

Also, with the way the results have gone today, no matter if NAVI or G2 win, your side of the bracket will have FURIA and 9z, too. Spirit, Falcons, Vitality, and NAVI or G2 will be on the other side, so how you feel about your side of the bracket overall? A chance for you to actually make the final, maybe?

I don't even know who we are going to meet in the future, in the playoffs, next game, next game, I don't care. Like, I have a manager, or analyst, they come to me and they say, 'We play mhmm at this time,' and that's it. I don't know, I don't have any feelings (laughs).
