Short news: Week 18

João "⁠story⁠" Vieira was the guest on the latest episode of SAW's podcast, and he revealed that FlyQuest have had a hard time finding stability since he joined the team at the start of the year.

The Oceanic team has participated in only four tournaments in 2026.

"We've had some visa issues," he said. "We've barely practiced in Europe. Or rather, we've barely practiced as a team. We've had like three or four weeks of practice, and I'm including our practice time in China. For a team that wants to be competitive, that's nothing.

"I tell them sometimes, 'Right now, we're in survival mode. Then, what happens, happens.'"

Falcons have released their final vlog with Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski, and the Macedonian in-game leader was shown being honest about his struggles in a team talk after the semi-final:

"Every game we played in playoffs this year, it feels like we're not playing the same way," he said. "You cannot tell me that three days ago we win 13-4, 13-4, and now we do not know how to play. It's just something in our heads that stops us from doing it. We don't know how to make good individual decisions in these games and that's the biggest problem for me.

He continued: "I'm sorry if I didn't bring too much this event, but it's hard to find... I wouldn't call it motivation, it's hard to play.

"Some of the games were shit, some T sides were, I can take responsibility for that but it's hard to play after the news and what I have gone through. I hope you don't have to go through it. I haven't slept more than five hours. I can tell you that. It's hard to function."

aimclub have announced that they and Eduard "⁠Ed1m⁠" Nelÿ Ichim have "decided to go in different directions," and as such he has parted ways with the team.

"We’re thankful for the work, commitment, and moments we shared, and we wish him all the best in his next chapter," aimclub's announcement read.

Ed1m had been with aimclub for nearly a year, averaging a 0.88 rating during his tenure.

M80 are now 24th in the global VRS after beating Wildcard 2-0 in the grand final of Thunderpick World Championship 2026 North America on Sunday.

It was a chaotic eight-team playoff bracket as only two out of seven matches were played—the remaining five ended in forfeits, with teams unable to field eligible lineups.

M80 will attend IEM Atlanta and the CS Asia Championships before travelling to Europe for the Cologne Major.
