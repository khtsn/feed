Maka: "It's been quite some months that we've struggled in terms of confidence"

3DMAX have endured a difficult few weeks, a string of underperformances in tier-two LANs leaving them short of IEM Cologne Major qualification. Their struggles carried over to their current PGL Bucharest run, where their opening two games saw them suffer an upset loss to FOKUS and barely scrape by a Voca squad playing with a stand-in.

"It's quite a shame to struggle that much against that kind of team," team captain Bryan "⁠Maka⁠" Canda admitted in the wake of his team's win over Voca. "Confidence has been really low lately, the team is struggling in almost every aspect and fundamental we have in the game. We try to rock on."

In the midst of 3DMAX's PGL Bucharest campaign, Maka spent some time with HLTV to talk about his team's struggles, the pain of not making the Major, and the thinking behind the recent changes to the coaching and playing staff at 3DMAX.

What are your thoughts on that match?

I'd say it's quite a shame to struggle that much against that kind of team. Confidence has been really low lately, the team is struggling in almost every aspect and fundamental we have in the game. We try to rock on. It's a shame that we struggled that much, but in the end it's a win. We have to keep that as a foundation for the next few games we have, and take the pretty good positivity we can have for this game to put it in the other games.

How much of that confidence is because you missed out on the Major?

I think it's been quite some months that we've struggled in terms of confidence. The system has changed a lot since bodyy got moved, you know? I've picked up the IGL role back for one month, we have new coaching staff, the system is pretty brand new, I would say.

Players tend to get back to the old habits we had with the old roster, and I think that's what makes us very low in terms of Counter-Strike, and that's put us in a bad spot mentally. We have pretty low confidence when we make a lot of mistakes. I think not making the Major is a pain in the ass, especially for us when we had like three Majors in a row in the Legends Stage. Not making it is really painful.

3DMAX was the first to make those changes, it was not a team thing... From their perspective it was the best for the team, so I respect that

When you made those changes, what was the idea behind them? Why did you decide to make those changes?

I think first of all, 3DMAX was the first to make those changes, it was not a team thing. They decided to cut bodyy and cut YouKnow, to bring in a new coaching staff, and to put me back on the IGL role. From their perspective it was the best for the team, so I respect that, and I am really happy that 3DMAX trusts me in that way.

That was the main reason I think this change happened, because we... in some way, we didn't find our identity the last months, like three or four months, that we played, and that's why we made those changes.

Do you think the timing of the changes was a bit difficult? Because they happened in the middle of the season and just before the Major was being decided.

Yeah it was, definitely. We just made the changes and we kicked off Pro League instantly, like a couple of days after, so it has been mentally really hard for me to get back on the IGL role, because it demands a lot of work and attention. I think it put me in exhaustion for a couple of weeks. Of course not the best in the end, when you are playing really important games.

It has been mentally really hard for me to get back on the IGL role, because it demands a lot of work and attention. I think it put me in exhaustion for a couple of weeks

Moving forward, what are you hoping the changes can do for you as a team? What are you hoping they can help you improve?

I'm pretty sure we can improve instantly quite fast because we showed that at ESL Pro League, that we can show really great CS. I'm pretty sure there are not tonnes of teams in the world that can show the CS that we can show at some point, but we can also show really low level CS, and we have to figure out why. We have some ideas why we struggle to show the same CS every time, so we work on that. NBK and wasiNk are helping a lot, as is Alex, our second coach, getting back on track, confidence, and having trust in ourselves and in our teammates.

How has misutaaa settled into your team?

Pretty well actually. He doesn't need that much space to perform, and he is really insane. He's been one of the most insane players I've seen mechanically; when he's on his A-game, everything is great for him. In his roles he's really insane, so he fits pretty well in the team. Then as a human, he's a great guy.

He's been one of the most insane players I've seen mechanically

What are 3DMAX's plans for the future? What are you looking to improve upon and where does the team go from here.

Hopefully we are able to make playoffs in some tournaments, like this one. We hope to make it, even though we are not in the best shape, I'm pretty sure we can get back to our shape. So we can get fewer tournaments in our schedule, fewer schedule conflicts, more time to rest and more time to work on ourselves.

I think in the end it's all about balance. If you have a good balance between being good at performance, then we can choose the right tournaments we want to play, which will be the best for us. Just tournament by tournament, we have Bucharest and Rio, we have got to make the best of it, and we will see how the season is for Singapore.
