TaZ: "It was very important to show the community and the players that we want to do better and are willing to make moves to improve"

The CS Asia Championships represents BC.Game's last chance to salvage something from a disastrous season. After the double benching of Christopher "⁠MUTiRiS⁠" Fernandes and António "⁠aragornN⁠" Barbosa, the IGL role remains vacant, with analyst Robin "⁠ScrunK⁠" Röpke set to play his second event with the team as an analyst.

Shortly before the start of the event, Wiktor "⁠TaZ⁠" Wojtas sat down with HLTV to discuss the loan signing of Mongolian star Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold, MUTiRiS' comments about not feeling empowered to captain the side, and the profile of IGL he is looking for.

"I believe that with the addition of Senzu, the team has what it takes to challenge the top teams on a shooting level," he said. "The next step is to find a leader to make it the full package."

What is the mood like in the team after not just the results in Atlanta but also the recent changes?

I think that the recent changes have given us a spark of life. As I said in my previous interviews, I feel that Senzu is a great addition to the team. He's great not only in the game but also outside of the game. The mood in the team is definitely better. Also, we decided to play with ScrunK, and it was going well in our slightly limited practice. Robin is familiar with our tactics, he's familiar with the play style of the players, so that was the main reason why we decided to go with him short-term.

This is your second event with Senzu. What are your first impressions of him?

He is a great kid. I knew what to expect from an in-game perspective, but what really impressed me was his personality. I think that he has a bright future ahead of him if he keeps working hard. I really enjoy his company in the team, and I hope it will be for the long run.

I wanted to go back to the changes that you made in the team when you benched MUTiRiS and aragornN. Obviously, the results weren't great, and I guess the atmosphere inside the team wasn't great either. But to make those changes during the season without a clear plan — that's why you're playing with ScrunK now — is never ideal. So I wanted to ask you about the rationale behind those changes during this particular moment of the season.

I think that it was very important for us, as BC.Game, to show the community and also show the players that we want to do better, and that we are willing to make moves to improve. I like to give players time to build both in-game and outside of it, but unfortunately, it didn't work with the previous roster, although we really tried.

 To get the SAW core, which allowed you to get invites into tournaments, the organization had to spend, I imagine, a significant amount of money. And then to make changes three, four months after getting the core, that was a sign that the vision and the project that the organization had in mind didn't come to fruition, right? How would you rate those months with the team? Why do you think that the team was not able to achieve the goals that the organization had for the team?

Unfortunately, the vision that you have for a team is not always going to fit the reality. We had many challenges, and I think locking tier-1 tournaments [so soon] was a mistake. The team needs time to develop, players need time to get to know each other, and you also need time to get the best out of every individual. The short time frame built a lot of expectations and put pressure on individuals. Some handled it better, some worse. The reality was that we were not able to meet the goals, and we needed a change.

I wanted to ask about the interview that MUTiRiS gave us during his last event where he said that he never felt like the captain of the team, that he was more like just the IGL. Why do you think that he gave those comments, and what was your relationship with him like?

My relationship with MUTiRiS was always good. And it's hard for me to give you good insight on why he gave such an interview and why it sounded that way. I can assure you that whenever we have an IGL on a team, I do my best to give him support and empower him to become a captain of the team. I believe that the strongest team you can build is when your IGL is also a captain, but it's not always like that in teams, right? You cannot make a leader. You are either a leader or not. It's also about the personality and how much you want to put on the line and build relationships with players or overcome the obstacles that are in front of you. We've definitely had a lot more than in other teams, but I know one thing: the bigger the obstacle, the bigger the prize.

TaZ said he did his best to give MUTiRiS the power to be the team's captain

You've now been with the team for four months. Has it been harder than you expected to assemble a team that works as a unit? And how would you compare the challenges that you have faced so far with what you faced, for example, with G2?

BC.Game is definitely a different kind of beast than G2 was. The players are way different. The approach is also slightly different. G2 had a lot of structure already, and players knew each other very well. With BC, you needed to build everything from scratch. With that said, I believe that with the addition of Senzu, the team has what it takes to challenge the top teams on a shooting level. The next step is to find a leader to make it the full package.

What kind of player do you have in mind for the role? Someone who has a lot of charisma, maybe experience as well?

I don't think experience is the number-one factor, but definitely what you need from the IGL and the captain of a team with s1mple and electroNic is someone that will not break under pressure and that will be the presence outside and inside of the server. You look at the person who's leading the game to be an example. If he's working hard, if he's sure of his calls, everyone is getting better. If he shows weakness or disbelief, the whole team will crumble.

Do you already have some names in mind?

There are some names in mind. We've also had some talks with certain players, and I can tell you that I feel that there are a few candidates that would fit the team.

We are not a tier-1 team in the ranking, and we are not a tier-1 team on the server. What we have is tier-1 potential. With the current lineup and a proper IGL, we can improve this situation very fast

And who's going to be making the call when it comes to picking the new IGL? Is it you and the CEO? Are you also getting input from the other players?

We are definitely talking with the players, and my connection with the CEO of BC is very good. We've been in close talks whenever we've made decisions since I joined, so I feel happy about it. In the end it will always be BC's call if they want to sign a player or not, if they can sign a player or not. But they are listening to my input, and I'm happy with how it works.

Before you picked SAW's core, BCG talked to other teams as well, you tried to get other cores. And even though you have s1mple, you have electroNic, the team has yourself as well, a very experienced coach, it seems that it's hard to convince players to join this project. Considering that, and the fact that you need an IGL, which is one of the biggest and most important roles out there, what kind of challenges do you envision in trying to make this team function and get an IGL that is the right fit for the kind of team that you have?

The main challenge is to understand our position. We are not a tier-1 team in the ranking, and we are not a tier-1 team on the server. What we have is tier-1 potential. With the current lineup and a proper IGL, we can improve this situation very fast, but first, we need to acknowledge the reality, Hard work and trust are what will make this team function. It's no different from other teams. I'm sure we can make it work as long as we fully believe in each other.

Back in Atlanta, s1mple and ScrunK said that they didn't really know what to expect, given the circumstances, but that they hoped the team would show some good CS. It didn't go well, even though you played some close maps, like the last one against B8.  What kind of expectations do you have for this event?

 I expect us to do much better than we did in Atlanta. I think that we can play much better than what we showed against B8, especially on the first map. Also, when you look at the Vitality match, even though the scoreline wasn't that great, on both maps we lost a force buy that changed the outcome of the games. And I just believe that if we step up in this event, we can surprise a lot of teams. Let's see what will happen. We open with a BO1, and that's never easy for the team that is the favorite.
