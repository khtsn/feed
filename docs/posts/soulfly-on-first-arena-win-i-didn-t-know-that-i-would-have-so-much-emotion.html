soulfly on first arena win: "I didn't know that I would have so much emotion"

Aurora have secured their best placement of the year thus far at ESL Pro League, guaranteeing at least a top-four finish with a narrow 2-1 victory over Legacy.

For their latest addition Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici it was his first-ever win in an arena setting, and it was one that was hard-earned. "In TeamSpeak it was a little bit chaotic," the young rifler admitted to HLTV after his team secured their victory. "I have to focus on what my players are saying. I'm trying to adapt to them, but it's really hard when three people are talking in TeamSpeak"

soulfly gave HLTV his thoughts on a tough quarter-final win, his team's confidence moving forward at ESL Pro League, and how well he has settled in at Aurora.

You can listen to the interview here.

Congratulations on the win, what are your first thoughts after that series?

It feels really good. It's my first stage win and I didn't know that after the win I would have so much emotion, and I can't imagine if I play good on stage how I'm going to react, I don't know. So it feels really good.

Was it a difficult series for your first stage win? It was back and forth with long streaks for both teams.

In TeamSpeak it was a little bit chaotic, so I didn't focus that much in the game, because I have to focus on what my players are saying. I'm trying to adapt to them, but it's really hard when three people are talking in TeamSpeak. Sometimes from the outside it looks like I'm dying like a bot, but I'm trying to listen and understand. Yeah it was difficult, but we managed to win.

What happened on Overpass? Because the first map you won pretty comfortably, and were leading 6-0 on the T side of Overpass, but it all just seemed to fall apart.

I don't know honestly. We did some individual mistakes. We were playing really good, but I don't know really what happened. I did some mistake one round, I went short before they came. What they told me is that I need to take short with them, but I took a decision by myself to go there, and I died. One round I did [a mistake], one round somebody else did [a mistake], which is why it ended up 6-6.

What was the atmosphere like in the team during that second map and afterwards, because on the player cams it looked like people were getting quite frustrated?

I was really disappointed in myself because I could have done much better, this is not how I usually play, so everybody was trying to hype me up, saying 'it's okay, no worries.' Fabre told us 'guys, step it up,' so I started believing, and everybody started believing, and we managed to do it at the end of the day.

We are not that confident after this win because even though we won, we made many mistakes, we didn't win clean

Considering this game was so close, does it give you more confidence that you managed to come back and get through it, or does it make you feel like you're maybe a little bit out of form?

We are not that confident after this win because even though we won, we made many mistakes, we didn't win clean. It was chaotic, with the mentality side as well, but still we get a little bit confident from this, and for the next game I think it will be better.

Now that you've had a little bit of time on Aurora, do you feel comfortable?

Honestly what the team wants me to do fits me so much, going entry, leading as the first guy. On CT side it's a new role and still I'm trying to adapt, but at the end of the day, with the roles, they're happy and I'm happy, so I really feel comfortable at the moment.
