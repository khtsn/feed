On the ground at IEM Cologne Major Stage 2

Speaking to HLTV post-game: "It was very fun, especially to play with MATYS and the AK. I think we were having a rough time on Inferno before. Individually, I would say it was hard for me.

"But for me, we changed a lot of things. We had bootcamp and we did some things so that I will feel more comfortable. We said what we need to do so that MATYS, also, will feel comfortable, and this is something that I'm happy about. I think it was an important win for us in Inferno.

"I don't know the rating, but I think like I played well, finally, I would say in this map, I hope I will keep playing like this."

On preparing: "Nothing. I didn't know I was going to the Major. I was not already playing CS. When I knew that I was coming, I played for one or two days and that was it."

On being in the 'shop window': "I'm playing a lot of new positions and showing a good result, in my opinion. I'm just trying to do work. If one player plays well in all of the positions it shows their understanding of the game."

On Boombl4: "He's doing really great. His calls, his reads, are insane. I enjoy playing with him."

Felipe "⁠insani⁠" Yuji stopped by for a quick chat with us after MIBR's elimination. The team still have XSE Pro League to play, but have an uncertain future as Klimentii "⁠kl1m⁠" Krivosheev's loan expires.

On what went wrong: "We lost rounds, and we didn't win them, that's a loss. We might have lost some crucial rounds, economy wise it was a fucked game, we didn't have any chances. We could have played better, of course it's a bad feeling now we're out of the Major but we need to keep our heads up."

On whether it's frustrating to post 99 ADR only to lose: "Of course."

On underperforming expectations: "If I'm a player, I have my dreams, I have my goals, I have my own expectations. If you go out in Stage 2 I can say that we didn't go through our expectations. We could do better for sure, I'm happy we showed some good CS in Stage 1 in some games, but this result shows we have a lot of work to do."

On the future without kl1m: "We're going to keep working. The season's not over for us. I don't know what's going to happen with the team, but we just need to keep going."

Speaking to HLTV, when asked why favorable rounds keep going against Astralis:"I mean, if I had the answer, honestly, I would tell you, but it's a lot of, honestly, amateur decisions. Especially on Nuke, our CT side has been horrible. It's just very obvious things...

"I feel like it's hard because when we sit and discuss the rounds outside of the game, everyone knows what's right to do, but we're just not doing them in the moment. And I'm not really sure what that comes down to. Honestly, I don't know. I don't have the answer for you."

We caught up with Rodrigo "⁠biguzera⁠" Bittencourt for a post-game chat:

On the win over FlyQuest, and breaking their Anubis streak: "They are a very difficult team to play against on Anubis, so yeah, we're happy that this side was doing well."

On coming in cold yesterday: "It's hard to say because we never know if it was because we were kind of cold or just we played bad. We want to win those matches, but at the same time, we know we have good opponents on the other side, and we have to respect them also. And yeah, it was a tough match. I think we didn't play well, and we got punished by them."

On picking saffee over nqz: ""It was [going to be] saffee for a long time. We chose him to be the AWPer here, and yeah, it's him because I think we'll be better with him. I think he's the kind of person that you want inside the team, you know? He plays for the team. He's more proactive, you know? He speaks more during the game. So yeah, I think that's why."

On the rest of the stage: "We really want to go through, but at the same time, we know that here we have a lot of good teams also. But I know how capable we are, and I'm pretty sure that we can go through. So expectations is so high, always. I never thought before, like, go home or go to vacation. I just want to go through and win and more matches as possible."

"It's definitely awesome that you don't feel that he's inexperienced. He's brave, he's taking the calls, he's not shutting down, he always has ideas, and just showing good signs of his potential," Aurimas "⁠Bymas⁠" Pipiras told HLTV after their victory against Legacy.

"I believe he could become one of the best leaders in the game."

Speaking post-game: "It feels really good, especially because we were not the favorites to go 2-1. I think many people also did 0-3 to us, in pick 'ems, so it feels really good to show people that we can actually play proper CS against better teams.

"Because also some guys said we didn't play good teams under the road here to Cologne, which is true, of course. I don't say it's not true, but beating paiN and MIBR, who are good teams, I think now we are starting to show what we can do."
