On the ground at IEM Cologne Major Stage 2

"It's definitely awesome that you don't feel that he's inexperienced. He's brave, he's taking the calls, he's not shutting down, he always has ideas, and just showing good signs of his potential," Aurimas "⁠Bymas⁠" Pipiras told HLTV after their victory against Legacy.

"I believe he could become one of the best leaders in the game."

Speaking post-game: "It feels really good, especially because we were not the favorites to go 2-1. I think many people also did 0-3 to us, in pick 'ems, so it feels really good to show people that we can actually play proper CS against better teams.

"Because also some guys said we didn't play good teams under the road here to Cologne, which is true, of course. I don't say it's not true, but beating paiN and MIBR, who are good teams, I think now we are starting to show what we can do."

Some minor drama is surrounding BIG's game against paiN, where the Brazilians had to start on T side after a glitch in the veto system.

"If I said it didn't hinder us, I'd be lying, and I would never do that. It hindered us not only mentally, but also in terms of preparation. If we had started as Terrorists, we wouldn't have played Overpass, a strong map for them," said Rodrigo "⁠biguzera⁠" Bittencourt in an interview with Dust2 Brazil.

ESL's response to Dust2.com.br was as follows: "The veto system is exactly the same as the one used in many previous ESL events. Our setup is always rigorously tested before the start of the championship. It is the team's responsibility to complete the veto process; in this specific case, the side was simply not chosen, and therefore the sides were determined randomly."

Speaking to HLTV: "Immediate reaction? Holy fuck, I'm happy that this map actually ended. I think if... I did throw some important rounds onto me to be honest, like that made a big difference I would say, but I still managed to come back mentally. And we did as a team, like we were losing 15-18, right, and it's not easy, especially like when these games matter a lot.

Now we are one BO3 away to qualifying for Stage 3, so I think we showed insane mental and balls when it matters the most, and we came back on top as a team, as a better team, I think that's why we won."
