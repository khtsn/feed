We spoke to Dongkai "⁠Jee⁠" Ji through a translator after TYLOO's elimination.

On exiting in Stage 2 again: "Compared to previous Majors, in Stage 2, we had a good start. Going 2-1, after the first two days, is pretty good for us. When it came to BO3, we kind of lacked map pool and individual skill against the better teams. In general, the teams in Stage 2 this year are stronger than previous Majors."

If playing more international events help get better results: "Actually the main problem is that we didn't have time for a bootcamp before the Major. We had to play a lot of tournaments, online and on LAN, just to keep up the VRS because of the ecosystem right now. Especially for the Major, it's important to have a bootcamp to be prepared for the Major. Considering we didn't have a bootcamp, I'm pretty satisified with the outcome here."

On Zero: "Zero is a really talented player. He's only played the game for 4-5k hours until now. He has a really good personality, I'm really happy that he's in TYLOO."

On beating TYLOO after the match to HLTV: "To be honest, we got a little sad because of Jee. LJee is a very good friend of us. Every time we see him, he's always smiling to us, so we like him a lot. That side is a little sad, but it is what it is. This is the competition."

Rafael "⁠saffee⁠" Costa spoke to HLTV after their deciding loss, touching on the reasons for the exit and his uncertain future.

On the series against Monte: "It was so tough. I believe on Nuke we just couldn't do our game. Then on Dust2, I believe we got back on track, but after winning the pistol, we lost the break round. We tried to come back as well, it was really close, and I believe it could have gone to us, but it happens. Just really painful to lose.

On 0-2 start: "It's how the Major works. Having those best-of-one on the same day, if you have an off day, you're probably going to lose two games, and that's how it is. It sucks, to be honest, but it is what it is. You can't complain."

On his future: "I don't know yet. I was just focused on the Major and hoping to make it to Stage 3 and the playoffs. I'm just happy to be here, happy to be back competing, happy to be in such a stage at a Major. I'm just happy and grateful to be here, and let's see what the future holds.

Talking about AZUWU post-game to HLTV: "Our coach has been working a lot with him. So, you know, we all want Azu to do well. He's like in the star roles, he's our entry. He's a super important person for the team.

"I've been looking at how I can activate him, but I think a lot of it comes down to him. Especially in Astana, but then some past games he was probably giving away entries too easily, not making things hard enough for the opponents.

"We spoke a lot about that and he's really up like he stepped up a lot on this. Of course, he's shooting hard as well. That always helps.

"But as for him personally, I don't know. He's actually ill right now so maybe that's the solution, just being ill and then he's just he's just wrecking everyone."
