The MongolZ win 10 rounds straight to make Bucharest semis over PARIVISION

The MongolZ overcame PARIVISION in PGL Bucharest's last quarter-final matchup after securing a reverse-sweep victory (6-13 Dust2, 13-8 Mirage, 13-9 Ancient) with a 10-round spree on the offense of the Ancient decider.

The Mongolian team struggled at the onset of the series, all but taking themselves out of contention on Mirage with two dreadful round losses, and looked out of sorts after a masterclass T side by Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali had PARIVISION up 9-3 on the Ancient decider.

But The MongolZ turned the map on its head with a resurgent offense of their own, taking over in the second half by dodging a few site stacks and after PARIVISION dropped numerous advantages to steal away the victory.

The fashion in which the series ended will leave PARIVISION with much to think about with a month off until their next tournament appearance at PGL Astana, with costly errors on the CT side of Ancient putting all of Jame's hard work to waste.

Dastan "⁠dastan⁠" Akbayev had been transparent about his frustration at witnessing his players' repeated mistakes in recent matches, and there were several throughout this series. But early on, it was PARIVISION who benefited most from mistakes the other way.

The MongolZ squandered two critical rounds in the first half of Dust2, one to five Glocks and another to three Glocks, a P250, and Deagle, losing all hope of building any momentum in what became a lopsided 8-4 half for the Russian team. Losing to a force after winning the pistol only added to The MongolZ's misery, and PARIVISION seized upon that to romp to a 13-6 victory.

The MongolZ regained their footing on Mirage, but it was not without errors as they ran time down too low after an early spree of rounds to hand Jame a quad-kill on B. That helped PARIVISION get into the fight, but multi-kill rounds from Sodbayar "⁠Techno⁠" Munkhbold let The MongolZ hold on to a slim lead at the break.

The writing seemed to be on the wall after a spectacular Deagle quad-kill by Andrey "⁠BELCHONOKK⁠" Yasinskiy turned another second-round force-buy PARIVISION's way and let them take the lead, but this time, The MongolZ were bailed out by a 1v3 clutch from Techno. That, paired with several multi-kills from Usukhbayar "⁠910⁠" Banzragch's AWP, ensured control didn't slip out of the Mongolian team's hands, and they went on a five-round run to force an Ancient decider.

A masterclass offense from Jame resulted in a blowout first half, where the Russian captain called circles around The MongolZ round after round for a 9-3 lead. Somber faces and frustration were clear on The MongolZ's faces during the side swap, but the comeback that followed left even Techno with disbelief on his face in the post-match interview.

PARIVISION dropped numerous advantages and failed to record a single CT side round win, including giving up a 1v2 to Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar after The MongolZ ran into a stacked bombsite, allowing players to slip away from a stack in the next, and then losing a 4v2 by leaving cobrazera unchecked in Donut. Entries out Middle by Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold kept the streak going, and PARIVISION were left with no chance to recover as The MongolZ sealed victory with their 10th round win in a row.
