TOBIZ: "The last month, we've been in a state of pure pressure"

HEROIC were eliminated in the Stake Ranked Episode 1 lower-bracket final, but their spot at the Major should be secure nonetheless according to Mischief's latest predictions.

Right after the loss to BetBoom, which HEROIC went into with a spot at the Major just about secured no matter the outcome, coach Tobias "⁠TOBIZ⁠" Theo still wasn't sold. "One day you're 95 percent and one day you're 56, and I've seen the simulation before, and it wasn't that nice to follow last season," he told HLTV.

The Dane also spoke to HLTV about waiting for Alimzhan "⁠Alkaren⁠" Bitimbay's visa issues to be solved and how the team will play with Simon "⁠yxngstxr⁠" Boije even in Astana if Alkaren can't make a full-time return, and how playing a lot of high-pressure matches can help his young team gain experience quickly.

You can listen to the interview here.

Tough loss against BetBoom. It felt pretty winnable for you. How heartbreaking is it to end your run here in this way?

I'm kind of at a loss for words because, as you say, we had it in the bag, and then we started to crumble a little bit. We started to do things we didn't talk about beforehand, and it becomes weird. We get into late-round situations where they already read us, and yeah, it's just not good enough in the end.

If it was pressure... I don't think it was pressure... I think it was just bad individually. Disappointing, but we can take some good rounds away from the match, even if it's hard right now. I think we played a good Ancient, the CT side, especially. Nuke was a bit too much, individually, and we suffered from that. Here, if we get one more trade or win one duel, then we also win this game here.

So yeah, it's a sour feeling right now, especially with everything on the line. You don't know if you're qualified for the Major, you're out of this tournament, and the next couple of days will be nerve-racking to follow.

We could not avoid it because it comes up on the timeline, and we need to talk about it and address the pressure, so we deal with it instead of not talking about it

At least it seems like you're in a pretty good position, everything considered. Maybe not 100% safe, but a lot of things have to go very wrong. Do you at least take a bit of consolation in that?

Yeah, we knew that after the NIP game, everybody was focused on it. We could not avoid it because it comes up on the timeline, and we need to talk about it and address the pressure, so we deal with it instead of not talking about it.

We were 98 percent [secure] before this game here, and we had a talk about how we can do it ourselves and not rely on everybody else. I think the chances are pretty good, but you never know.

One day you're 95 percent and one day you're 56, and I've seen the simulation before, and it wasn't that nice to follow last season, so I rather just wait for the official invites to come in, hopefully.

We're still waiting for Alim's [Alkaren] situation overall, and until there's news about that, we'll keep playing with Simon [yxngstxr], even though we're playing in Astana[

Ending here is also a big moment for the roster with yxngstxr? Is he going to keep playing with the team? You're going to Astana, so Alkaren wouldn't have any issues playing there if you chose that. What's the thought process there?

We're still waiting for Alim's [Alkaren] situation overall, and until there's news about that, we'll keep playing with Simon [yxngstxr], even though we're playing in Astana.

From a VRS perspective, it doesn't make too much sense only having him for X amount of games, you know? So we'll have to hope that he can come back in full force before we start to use him. But hopefully we'll meet him there.

What do you make of the place the team is in overall? Being at least a team that's fighting to be in the Major, and will probably be in the Major. Are you hitting some goals, especially doing it with yxngstxr as a stand-in? Where are you as a team and with the development of this roster?

It has been a pretty rough four months since we got susp and the whole Alim situation started. I think our pressure and expectations lowered, of course, and it got put aside from the original goals, but we also want to maintain the highest level that we can, and I think we have world-class players.

The rounds we're losing, the games we're losing, they're often close, and even in Pro League, for example, or other tournaments, it has never been the case where we're getting blown out of the server. We're in a good spot, and ideally, when everything settles, we're in a position where we're playing for the Major every single time.

And I would rather qualify early so we don't have these, like... yeah, it has been a hectic month where one or two games are very costly, and it just can't happen on this level here. But you see everybody else is struggling as well when we play these smaller events. Everybody can beat everybody, and it's cool for the viewers, but not as a team because you have to be on your toes every single time.

The team is fairly inexperienced, so going through moments like this and through this sort of tension and pressure can be good, as well, if you take it from a developmental perspective.

Yeah, of course. The last month, we've been in a state of pure pressure. Every single game that we're playing, we're feeling pressure, and I know that everybody else is also feeling that, and hopefully it can help us in closing out games.

I think that BetBoom just had more experience in the late rounds here with what they needed to do in order to close out the rounds, and we were maybe a bit tunnel-visioned and not as creative as maybe other games where we are in a position to close out a third map against a team that has been doing very well recently.

So yeah, hopefully it will help us in the future. It's a good experience, and we take that with us. So yeah, we'll take it!
