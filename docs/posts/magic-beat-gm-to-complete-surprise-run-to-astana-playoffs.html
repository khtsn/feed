magic beat GM to complete surprise run to Astana playoffs

magic have secured the sixth spot in PGL Astana playoffs after locking in their third victory in the Swiss group stage, over Gentle Mates.

The win completes a surprise run from the Russophone squad, who came into the event as the fourth-lowest-ranked team and got off to a 0-2 start following losses to The MongolZ and Monte.

But they then beat three teams ranked above them on the VRS in regional rivals K27, HEROIC, and Gentle Mates in the decider to lock in their first appearance on a big stage at the Barys Arena from Friday.

"It means a lot for me. From 0-2 to 3-2 is amazing. I'm playing in my country, in the arena, and I have a lot of support," local hero Abdurakhim "⁠mo0N⁠" Issa said on the broadcast in reaction to the playoffs qualification.

For the little international experience on magic, they locked in their playoffs appearance in style.

Mirage wasn't much to write home about from Gentle Mates, who were obliterated on their CT side by a very calm and controlled magic offense spearheaded by Vitaliy "⁠sFade8⁠" Marushka.

But when the match went to Inferno, all hell broke loose with magic playing high-octane, balls-to-the-wall Counter-Strike full of aggressive pushes on timings, last-second turns, big individual moments and clutches.

Andrey "⁠AW⁠" Anisimov was key on the CT side as he played with full confidence in aggression on Banana, at times going as far as to push the bottom Banana smoke on a pop flash from behind to catch Gentle Mates off-guard.

In the end, the map was only resolved in overtime with Gentle Mates recovering on their own defense, but there magic reigned supreme off of more individual moments, scoring two clutches and a big triple to send the Spaniards home.
