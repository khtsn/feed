Aurora come alive late to make Astana playoffs over The MongolZ

woxic won three crucial clutches on map one

Aurora came from behind twice to beat The MongolZ 2-0 and advance to the quarter-finals of PGL Astana.

In the face of a supernova performance from Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold, Aurora came back from 2-7 down on Overpass to win their map pick 13-11. On Mirage, Aurora led initially but lost both pistols and were losing 10-9 before wrestling the game back into their control.

Such mental fortitude is nice to see for Aurora, who Özgür "⁠woxic⁠" Eker said had hired a new sports psychologist before the event, as they look to take their ceiling to the next level.

On the flipside, not making playoffs is a huge disappointment for The MongolZ, who are yet to reach the same heights they hit with Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold in 2025 since parting ways with their star rifler.

Truth be told, it was a tough draw here: All five of their matches were against teams that made playoffs, and the three losses were to top opposition in the form of Falcons, Spirit, and Aurora.

But The MongolZ are also in that bucket, and had plenty of opportunities both against Falcons and in the series today against Aurora to convert leads into match victories.

Overpass was particularly tough as a 7-2 lead turned against the Mongolians. mzinho posted 19 frags on CT side, with 147 ADR, but woxic won two 1on1s to close the half 5-7. woxic then won another clutch, a 1on2, to start the CT side well for Aurora and deny a mzinho 4k.

woxic wins his third clutch in a row

The Overpass pick itself was a strong call from Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli and company; the two teams had not played it against each other despite having 30 maps of history between the cores. But in the end, it paid off for Aurora as Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici joined woxic with some strong impact late on the CT side.

"Last 22 matches, they choose to ban Overpass 18 times," woxic said post-game to James "⁠BanKs⁠" Banks. "If we lost this, it would have been a mental issue, on their worst map. But God wanted it this way."

On Mirage, Sodbayar "⁠Techno⁠" Munkhbold took over from mzinho as The MongolZ's hero with a 1.72 rating, and helped them launch a comeback of their own from 3-8 to 10-9.

But in the gun rounds, life was harder for The MongolZ, and Aurora ended both halves strongly with Ismailcan "⁠XANTARES⁠" Dörtkardeş back in form to secure the 2-0 victory.
