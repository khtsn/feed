AntyVirus banned by Akros for "competitive integrity violation"

The Polish player recently retired from competitive CS amid allegations of match-fixing and cheating.

Akros Anti-cheat has confirmed to HLTV that it has banned Miłosz "⁠AntyVirus⁠" Konieczka "for a competitive integrity violation." The Polish player is embroiled in controversy after an investigation by CYBERSHOKE captain Daniil "⁠alpha⁠" Demin pointed to possible match-fixing and cheating by the Oramond/DragonClaw squad.

"Our investigation showed technical indicators in alignment with the public accusations against the player for using DMA [Direct Memory Access] hardware," Akros told HLTV.

Contacted by HLTV, the Esports Integrity Commission (ESIC) confirmed that it is presently investigating the matter.

AntyVirus retires amidst matchfixing and cheating allegations

Earlier this month, CCT told HLTV that it had launched an investigation into the DragonClaw squad, which is currently ranked 116th in the world. On June 8, NODWIN announced that the Polish team had been suspended from the Clutch Series "pending an ongoing investigation involving their roster."

AntyVirus announced on June 2 that he was retiring from Counter-Strike, explaining that he was fed up with having to defend his name after five years of nonstop "accusations of cheating and other stuff."

He also attempted to refute some of the evidence compiled by alpha and to justify some of the controversial clips about his team that have surfaced online.

DragonClaw forfeited their CCT Europe Series 3 match against Bushido Wildcats on June 2 — the same day AntyVirus announced his retirement — as they were unable to field a full roster. The team then withdrew from the CCT 2026 Europe Series 4 Closed Qualifier without playing a single match.
