BetBoom upset Vitality to make Atlanta playoffs

BetBoom have booked a spot in the playoffs of IEM Atlanta in spectacular fashion, besting Vitality 2-1 to send the best team in the world down to the lower bracket in Group A.

Much has been made of the break Vitality took ahead of this event, with pictures of the players enjoying time off doing the rounds on social media. BetBoom meanwhile have undergone changes in recent times, swapping in Aleksandr "⁠zorte⁠" Zagodyrenko and fielding Timur "⁠FL4MUS⁠" Marev in place of Daniil "⁠d1Ledez⁠" Kustov.

Despite their time off Vitality were in solid form to dispel a strong Oleksandr "⁠s1mple⁠" Kostyliev performance in their opener, and considering BetBoom's roster being in flux, it seemed this result would be a foregone conclusion.

Vitality pull off unlikely 3v4 retake

BetBoom's proactive and aggressive approach was too much for Vitality on Anubis, leading the Russian squad to an 8-4 CT side. Vitality slowly warmed into the map, helped by some Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut heroics on low-buy rounds, but it was too little, too late, granting BetBoom a lead in the series.

It seemed Vitality had righted the ship by the time Overpass came around. They outplayed BetBoom consistently on their offensive half and weathered a mini-resurgence by BetBoom when the sides switched, sending the series to Nuke.

ZywOo and mezii convert 2v5

A Vitality T-side pistol win kicked off Nuke and it felt like a quick close to the series was coming, but BetBoom answered back quickly with a force-buy win and set about a dominant defensive half. The underdogs completely shut down Vitality Outside and forced them into desperate Upper pops and Ramp takes, powered by smart rotations by Kirill "⁠Magnojez⁠" Rodnov and high-impact AWPing from zorte.

Vitality were not to go down without a fight, however, and they grabbed a force-buy win after losing the pistol on their CT side to give themselves some room to work with. It took some more immense impact from Magnojez, first with a hero rifle and then in a 2v5 post-plant, to seal the deal and grant BetBoom a memorable upset win.

BetBoom pull off 2v5 to win the series

Questions will be asked of Vitality as they were comfortably beaten on two of these maps, and it wasn't just down to individual rustiness. BetBoom's aggressive, trade-heavy approach was a struggle for Vitality to deal with, and Vitality's offensive halves were sloppy, on Nuke in particular.

BetBoom can now look forward to a matchup with the winner of paiN vs. FUT for a spot in the semi-finals, whilst Vitality will begin their lower bracket campaign against FaZe
