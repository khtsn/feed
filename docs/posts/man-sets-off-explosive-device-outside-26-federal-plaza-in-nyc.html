Man Sets Off Explosive Device Outside 26 Federal Plaza in NYC

New York|Man Sets Off Explosive Device Outside Federal Building in Manhattan

https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/20/nyregion/nyc-explosion-federal-plaza-manhattan.html

A man was taken into custody on Monday after the police said he set a fire, ignited fireworks and waved a gun at passers-by outside a building in Lower Manhattan that houses the federal immigration agency and courts.

Officials did not release the man’s name or indicate a motive. The New York Police Department said the incident happened around 8:30 a.m., and a gun was recovered from the scene.

The Fire Department said three people suffered minor injuries that were not life-threatening. One of them was taken to NewYork-Presbyterian Lower Manhattan Hospital, while the other two refused medical attention, officials said.

“This morning an individual deployed an incendiary device outside of 26 Federal Plaza,” the Federal Bureau of Investigation said in a statement. “The individual has been taken into custody.”

In a statement, Mayor Zohran Mamdani called the incident “deeply disturbing.”

“I’m relieved no one was seriously injured and that a suspect is in custody,” he said, adding that his team was monitoring and supporting the federal investigation. “Our administration will continue to ensure that every New Yorker is safe in their city — and hold accountable any who threaten that.”

The building, which houses federal immigration courts and the local field office of Immigration and Customs Enforcement, has become a flashpoint since President Trump returned to power last year. As Mr. Trump dedicated more resources to immigration enforcement across the country, protests at ICE facilities across the nation surged.

Protesters and elected Democratic officials have focused their efforts in New York City on the building at 26 Federal Plaza in Lower Manhattan. Some were arrested in September while seeking access to cells inside the building where ICE holds asylum seekers and immigrants. The police had briefly evacuated the building a month earlier after officials discovered envelopes containing white powder.

At the scene late Monday morning, a pull-cart left behind had a poster affixed to the front that said, “ICE OFF OUR STREETS.” The suspect was seen pulling the cart, which contained fireworks and two rifles, according to two government officials who requested anonymity because they were not authorized to discuss the case.

A video circulating on X showed a man lobbing an object into bright orange flames on the ground outside of the building, followed by an explosion. Officers ran toward the man and appeared to have wrestled him down near the street corner.

A second video posted to X by Freedom News TV, an independent news outlet, showed officers running toward the man as a cloud of smoke billowed out from the building.

A couple hours later, a gaggle of investigators were clustered on the Worth Street side of the building near a large, boxy police truck emblazoned with the words “Bomb Squad.” Two people wore white hazardous material suits.

Police officers in uniform surrounded the scene, closing off Worth Street with white and yellow crime scene tape between Lafayette and Broadway. The incident did not appear to cause any significant disruptions at the building. Immigration lawyers on the inside said it was operating normally.

Chelsia Rose Marcius, Luis Ferré-Sadurní, Nate Schweber, Emma Goldberg and Hurubie Meko contributed reporting.
