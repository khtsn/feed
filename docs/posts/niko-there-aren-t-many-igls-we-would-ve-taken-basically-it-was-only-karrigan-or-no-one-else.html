NiKo: "There aren't many IGLs we would've taken, basically, it was only karrigan or no one else"

Falcons squeezed past The MongolZ in the 2-1 pool at PGL Astana to earn a playoffs berth in Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen's debut event, the Dane's first arena of the year after a strugglesome period on FaZe.

"It definitely should be a good confidence boost for him now," Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač told HLTV after the match. "Now he can kind of breathe a little bit; I think he was under a lot of pressure, I think a lot of people have huge expectations from him, and also from us, with this move."

karrigan interview: Leaving FaZe, joining Falcons, and first impressions

The star-studded squad has been bereft of trophies since adding Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov, and pulled the trigger on a leadership change after IEM Rio in hopes of turning their fate around.

"The team was going in the wrong direction, we could feel that something was off, and it was very hard to get out of it," NiKo said, "The management felt the change was necessary in order for us to have a higher chance of doing better at the Major."

But the Bosnian also stressed the team will need time to adjust to karrigan's system, which is putting more onus on its players to communicate and take initiative so the Dane can call as he is used to.

"Today, I think we have seen that when we start struggling a little bit, that maybe players shut down a little bit and then maybe Finn is a bit left in between; he doesn't necessarily have enough resources to call the way he likes calling," he said. "So this will take a little bit of time, but so far it has been pretty good for us."

Congrats, you made the playoffs. A bit of a hard one against The MongolZ in the end, but you got it done. Give me your thoughts after that game.

Yeah, it was a very tough game. Super happy that we won in the end; we showed a lot of resilience. It was messy, I think we kinda let them come back into the game on Inferno, and then they kinda took the momentum all the way to Mirage, and they were really, really on point and sharp at the start of the Mirage CT side.

But overall, super happy to avoid the 2-2 game and just to have an extra day off where we can kinda rewatch all the games that we have played here, and just keep on improving with Finn [karrigan] now in the team. Hopefully show a bit better CS in the playoffs.

It definitely should be a good confidence boost for him now. Now he can kind of breathe a little bit; I think he was under a lot of pressure

How has it been with Finn in the team?

Honestly, it's been very good so far. We are slowly getting used to his system because it's very different from many IGLs out there, so we need time, but so far so good. We don't expect, from ourselves at least, to go out of this event and become [anywhere] near close to Vitality immediately, so we need time, we need more practice. Changing the IGL is the hardest role in the team that you can change; it's not just a regular player, you just change the whole system basically, so it will take us time.

Even though I played with Finn eight years ago, I am a different person, he is a different person, so there are still new things that we are learning about each other. He didn't know how I was behaving in these eight years, how I've matured and everything, and also for him, the way he is also doing things differently. But so far, so good.

Obviously, the first playoffs for him this year as well, so it definitely should be a good confidence boost for him now. Now he can kind of breathe a little bit; I think he was under a lot of pressure. I think a lot of people have huge expectations from him, and also from us, with this move, so now, as I said, a couple of days to relax and improve a bit.

You mentioned his system being different. Can you tell me more about that and how it has been for you trying to adjust to it?

Yeah, I mean when I say different, it's just that he's playing much more versatile, much more free, in the rounds. Fakes and all of these things, the rounds are way more open, the players have to constantly be close 100% in their game. We all have to contribute with the communication, with the moves, with the initiative, what we see on the map, and stuff like this. This is something that the players will have to get used to, and we have to go in each game thinking, 'How can I help Finn? What can we do better individually?', and so on.

Today, I think we have seen that when we start struggling a little bit, that maybe players shut down a little bit and then maybe Finn is a bit left in between; he doesn't necessarily have enough resources to call the way he likes calling. So this will take a little bit of time, but so far it has been pretty good for us. I think for us, we... it's going to sound bad, but we enjoy playing this way, so far. It's something that we wanted to achieve even before, and we believed that this was the right move and this change will help us to get to another level.

You said that sometimes he might struggle where to go with things when people aren't at a 100%. How are you adapting around that right now, what is the solution you've found so far?

I wouldn't say... I think the only half that we have struggled was in the first game we played on Dust2, where we struggled on the first map. But other than that, it wasn't... I think right now, he wants to do too well, as well, like he really wants to be on top of everything. But it is just a learning process for us, and I am trying to help him as much as I can.

Today, when it was maybe a bit rough on Mirage, I suggested a couple of things and stuff like this because I saw that we all kind of shut down a little bit, and we kind of gave him all the space that he can call, but he needs people to help him. This is something that we will keep working on and keep improving.

You mentioned that you've evolved since you last played with him, he's evolved as well, it's been eight years. Can you tell me more about how you've changed, what sort of differences you're noticing between how the dynamic has evolved?

I'll speak for myself, I'm just looking at the bigger picture way more, and I'm always suggesting first. I'm never like, pushing my ideas to him; I've always tried to help him, but I'll only take the space that he has given me, basically. So far, it has been working very well, obviously it is still early days, but this is something that I have learned throughout the years and I believe that I didn't have many issues with the IGLs (laughs) in the past. Probably the community thinks otherwise.

But for me, Finn needs full responsibility of his calling, and he will get all the support that he needs from myself, but also from all the other guys. We want to make sure that he is as comfortable as possible, and we will give him all the resources that he needs. We all want to make this project work.

The team was going in the wrong direction, we could feel that something was off, and it was very hard to get out of it. The management felt the change was necessary

When we talked in [Rio], I'd asked about the timing of the change and it happening so close to the Major with only a couple of events to play, and you said you'd be able to tell me a little bit more about that timing here. So just wanted to give you a chance to share your thoughts on that.

Yeah... I probably had different thoughts back then in Rio (smiles), but for us... the team was going in the wrong direction, we could feel that something was off, and it was very hard to get out of it. The management felt the change was necessary in order for us to have a higher chance of doing better at the Major.

We all know the timing is tricky, but we also know that if you wait a little bit longer, they might have started rebuilding the FaZe [roster] and then he would have stayed and so on. There are not many IGLs that we would have taken, basically, it was only Finn or no one else. So for the Falcons, it just seemed like the right opportunity to jump on this. And with the performances that we have had, no one was happy and satisfied with it, and we all believe that this change will improve our chances at the Major.

You're through to the playoffs now as well, and as you mentioned, karrigan's first playoffs of the year. You also told BanKs that some people are expecting you to win this event, but you don't know if that is necessarily the same expectation you have. What is your hope going into the arena now?

Yeah, I mean for us, definitely the expectation is not to win, or the reality is not to win, because as I said, we need time. I think I saw some odds somewhere, like either on HLTV or on X, that we are the favorites to win this event, and for me that is crazy. I understand why people have these expectations with the players that we have, and we have been... well actually, this season, we haven't even been close to winning a trophy, so we will need time, obviously.

It has been a long time since we have been on the same stage on the same team together, and I think he is a very special player when it comes to the stage. He takes the team to another level

We know that there is always a chance, and also Finn, I think he is pretty good in the early stages of the team as well, so we are going to try to take advantage of that. For us, it's mostly collecting the data from all of these games and trying for everyone to fit Finn's system so we have enough data for both this event and also the one in Shanghai. Then we will have a smaller bootcamp right before the Major where we can actually see what things are working, what we have to change, what we have to improve on, and then after that we can slowly start having higher expectations.

But so far, happy with the playoffs, and it also depends on who we play and so on. I'm just looking forward to playing with Finn on the stage; it's going to be a different vibe. It has been a long time since we have been on the same stage on the same team together, and I think he is a very special player when it comes to the stage. He takes the team to another level, and I think now it's just for us to build on that confidence that he has on the stage and see what damage we can do here.

Are you enjoying the sirens?

Yeah, we do (laughs). We all started laughing as soon as we heard it. He brings a special kind of atmosphere in the team, and it's something that we have also been missing before.
