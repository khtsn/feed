FlyQuest stamp out Liquid to qualify for Stage 2

INS led the way to a convincing 2-0 victory

FlyQuest have advanced to Stage 2 at IEM Cologne and eliminated Liquid in the process after stamping out the Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek-led side in a 2-0 whitewash series.

The Australian squad barely skipped a beat from the moment Anubis began with them starting on the CT side. Liquid played the map in an official for the first time in three months, refusing to float Overpass against a FlyQuest team that doesn't play it, and it showed.

Racking up an early lead, FlyQuest were brimming with confidence, making all the right moves on the defense while João "⁠story⁠" Vieira and Joshua "⁠INS⁠" Potter did the heavy lifting in the fragging department.

The first map went by the wayside for Liquid as they only managed two rounds on the T side, entering Inferno in a somber mood that quickly turned from bad to worse, as they started off badly on the offense once again.

On the other side, INS continued to put up numbers alongside Justin "⁠jks⁠" Savage, while Declan "⁠Vexite⁠" Portelli was playing third fiddle and putting salt in the wound in between rounds, shouting at their sour-faced opponents.

The score was soon 12-4 as Liquid gave up an anti-eco to make matters even worse, and although they put up a last-ditch effort to save face, FlyQuest soon emerged victors after overpowering the A bombsite with a simple execute.

A Stage 2 qualification comes as a big boon to a FlyQuest side that has struggled of late. They got off to the Major 1-2 after three closely-contested best-of-ones, but leveled up in one-sided series against local rivals from THUNDER dOWNUNDER and Liquid to secure their spot in the next Swiss group.

That renewed confidence will be needed as the competition goes up several notches in Stage 2, where the likes of Spirit, Legacy, FUT and G2 await.

On the other hand, Liquid's downfall continues after they barely made it into the IEM Cologne Major in the first place thanks to changing regions to the Americas at the last minute with the swap of Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz and Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa with G2.

Like FlyQuest they started out 1-2 in the best-of-ones and got back into contention with a convincing win over HEROIC, but ended up going out with a whimper after two lackluster T sides in the decider.
