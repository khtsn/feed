We caught up with Vitality's in-game leader Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire after he and his teammates beat GamerLegion 2-0 in the semi-finals at BLAST Rivals 2026 Season 1, securing a grand final appearance where they will face Natus Vincere in the best-of-five final.

Top team Vitality and ranking runners up Natus Vincere have gone up against each other twice this year: in the BLAST Open Rotterdam grand final, where the apEX-led squad won 3-0, and in the IEM Rio quarter-finals, where the map record was extended to 5-0.

"I'm never sure if I'm comfortable playing against NAVI," apEX told HLTV. "Of course the stats show it, but in my head, I'm never like that. It's another game, it's a new opportunity for us to win."

The Frenchman admitted that the team is not the as rested as they could be heading into the season's stretch, but his hunger for winning is intact. "We can create something so big if we keep going," the captain said. "I just want to win as much as possible, so I try to give my teammates the same context and tell them to give a lot."

Listen to the interview here.

You just beat GamerLegion to reach another grand final, but tell me a bit about that Overpass, though. You had some struggles to close it out, that CT side... What happened?

Lately we're fucking strong on every CT map overall, but Overpass is the one we struggled [with] here. We struggled against G2 after going 6-6 in the first side, we lost that one. Here we were up 7-5 on the T side, and we fucked it up a bit.

They won one Deagle round that put them back in the game, from 10-4 to 10-5, so it would have changed a bit. They started to play more on B, and we struggled to catch them and to kill them. It was not the easiest one.

We keep repeating it, we haven't played that much practice, and Overpass is a map we were struggling a bit before this tournament, so yeah, if we keep struggling on the CT sides, we'll have to talk about it.

It's still a very strong map for us, but it's the way we just play on CT sides. Well played to them, they played well and caught us on the B bomb site.

We're just going to focus on ourselves, trying to give our best, as usual

Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire on final vs. NAVI

You're in the final against NAVI, the number two team in the ranking, trying to get up to you guy. At the same time, the last two times you played them, you beat them in a grand final and you beat them in Rio in the quarters. Are you comfortable playing against them?

I'm never sure if I'm comfortable playing against NAVI. Of course the stats show it, but in my head, I'm never like that. It's another game, it's a new opportunity for us to win. And it's a grand final, a grand final is never like the other games. So for us it's going to be a tough one.

We're just going to focus on ourselves, trying to give our best, as usual. But we know, once again, and it's what I repeat a lot, we didn't show it in the whole game here, but if we play our A game as we did on Mirage, I think people have no chance. But will we bring our A game tomorrow? That's the question.

Is there anything you'll try to do with the boys to bring your A game?

Overall, today we lacked a bit of playing with balls in some rounds. We played a bit too passively, and didn't get enough information. In the tough moments we're usually a team that takes the advantage and goes for plays, but today we didn't, and for me that's going to be the thing tomorrow — to be a bit more pushy on what we do and try to get more information and try to get more duels. In a way, to not let them execute, because that was a mess today. But, of course, we won't play Overpass tomorrow.

We probably won't find a team after this one at the same level, so we need to use every opportunity to get a trophy

Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire

We're heading towards the Major, and Rivals is one of the smaller events, with eight teams, and you can see the vibe is a bit more chill. How do you keep the motivation and the energy up?

I'll be honest, I'm roasted right now. I don't know why. It was a tough day for me. It's what I repeat, we're lucky to have the best team. For me, without a doubt, now the best team of all time. We probably won't find a team after this one at the same level, so we need to use every opportunity to get a trophy.

When you look at the stat that a player like mezii or flameZ has more trophies than FalleN or NiKo, it's a crazy stat. Like, flameZ is 22... That's what I try to keep repeating to him, let's keep grinding, all of us, because sometimes we tend to take a step back. Like the first game against FUT, we were a bit too easy, we played good on Mirage...

I'm this annoying older man who just keeps repeating, 'Guys, let's fucking go again. I know you're fried, but let's fucking go.' We can create something so big if we keep going. Maybe in a year we won't win any matches, [but] if we keep doing a lot, this lineup is special, and I'm sure we can still win trophies for two or three years. I just want to win as much as possible, so I try to give my teammates the same context and tell them to give a lot.
