S0tF1k to work alongside hally in Spirit; both to miss start of IEM Cologne Major

Spirit has announced a restructure of its coaching staff, which will see Sergey "⁠hally⁠" Shavaev return as head coach and work alongside Dmitry "⁠S0tF1k⁠" Forostyanko, who stepped in for IEM Rio and PGL Astana after the former temporarily stepped down while "battling certain health issues."

Spirit's Brand Director elaborated on the new structure in a post on X, saying that hally would be at the "head of the project" and that the team would play "his brand of CS," whilst S0tF1k would be responsible for "micro stuff, strats, mechanics, nades."

Contacted by HLTV, he also confirmed that the long-term plan is for hally to remain behind the team during matches.

S0tF1k on future with Spirit's main team: "We will see after Astana"

Neither coach will be with the team for at least the start of their IEM Cologne Major campaign, which begins in Stage 2, as Spirit announced that S0tF1k was not granted a Schengen visa and hally was "urgently hospitalized."

"We expect Sergey [hally] to recover soon and reunite with the team during the later portions of the tournament," Spirit's statement reads. "Meanwhile, both coaches will help the team online."

The coaching duo was supposed to make its debut in Germany, where Spirit are among the favorites after an uptick in form during S0tF1k's coaching stand-in stint that saw them end a nine-month trophy drought at PGL Astana after finishing second to Vitality at IEM Rio.

S0tF1k's long-term future with the team's primary roster became a talking point in the wake of the two tournaments, with analysts praising the 31-year-old for his impact in improving Spirit's utility usage and some of the team's players crediting him for "fixing some very important mistakes" and improving their fundamentals.
