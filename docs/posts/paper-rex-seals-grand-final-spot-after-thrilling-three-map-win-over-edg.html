Paper Rex seals grand final spot after thrilling three-map win over EDG

It took until the third-to-last day of VCT Masters London for the competition's only pair of international trophy winners to meet, and the series was pure chaos. It took three maps and two overtimes for the series to reach its conclusion, with Paper Rex Paper Rex Asia-Pacific Rank #1 invy Adrian Jiggs Reyes Jinggg Wang Jing Jie f0rsakeN Jason Susanto d4v41 Khalish Rusyaidee something Ilya Petrov defeating EDward Gaming EDward Gaming China Rank #1 ZmjjKK Zheng Yongkang (郑永康) nobody Wang Senxu (王森旭) CHICHOO Wan Shunzhi (万顺治) Smoggy Zhang Zhao (张钊) Jieni7 Zhang Juntai (张君泰) 2-1. It was EDG and PRX's seventh-ever meeting in VCT play since 2022, with the series score now sitting at 5-2 in favor of PRX.

The series was played out on Paper Rex's pick of Breeze, EDward Gaming's pick of Fracture, and the decider map, Split. EDG won Breeze 15-13, PRX won Fracture 14-12, and PRX won Split 13-6

For all intents and purposes, Breeze was Paper Rex's map. A VLR stat watcher could have told you the same. PRX claimed both pistols on the map, had six more first bloods, five-on-five had more kills than EDG, and put themselves in what should have been an unlosable position, just to let the map slip from its grasp. Those who watched the match up to the point PRX claimed a 12-9 lead could have told you the same thing: this should have been Paper Rex's map.

After a relatively even start to play, PRX gave up a five-round run to EDG's attack, but had its own attacker side coming. PRX looked poised to close out the map. A loss in the would-be bonus seemed to be the only hiccup in a 6-1 run to claim an 11-6 lead, and match point was realized one round later. From there, it seemed like EDG could not lose. A 2v5 clutch from Jieni7 and Smoggy with their backs against the wall was the crown jewel of a map on which EDG had no fewer than seven round wins while facing a man disadvantage.

Smoggy and Jieni7 capitalize on PRX's over-extension on map point.

In overtime, a quick exchange of near-perfect defender-side rounds was followed by moments of mechanical excellence from Jieni7, with EDG claiming an attack round and then whittling down the PRX numbers to a 2v1, which it closed out to claim the map 15-13.

Fracture began in an eerily similar fashion to Breeze. Paper Rex once again claimed both pistols on the map, and after gaining some early momentum, let EDG back into the map. A run of six straight rounds gave EDG the lead at halftime, but PRX responded with six straight to start the second half. The strong attack side gave PRX yet another 12-9 advantage, but EDG again clawed back three straight to force overtime. Paper Rex claimed a round on defense, and was then tasked with a low-clock scenario to close the map out. In a brawl that had nine players in virtually the same quarter of the map, PRX rose from the dust to avoid a second-straight blunder to win 14-12.

Paper Rex avoids a second-straight heartbreak, closing out Fracture 14-12.

After back-to-back overtime, the age-old adage about Split rang true once again; all bangers end on Split. Once again, PRX started the map with considerable momentum. After winning its fifth pistol round of the series, PRX gave up three rounds, but went on a run of six straight to help build up an 8-4 halftime lead. EDG won its first pistol of the series to kick off the second half, but that was about as good as things got for the Chinese champions. In a matchup of mirror comps, PRX's just seemed to be the stronger of the two on this day, with elite team play and coordination leading the way for PRX.

Paper Rex closes it out 13-6.

Jinggg's series-leading 68 kills set the tone for Paper Rex, setting the stage for a potential MVP case should Paper Rex be trophy-lifters for the second time in the past year on Sunday. invy and something 's 58 kills should not go without mention, while Smoggy's 56 and CHICHOO 's 53 were not enough in the end for EDG.

With the win, Paper Rex secures not just its spot in Sunday's grand final but also locks in a double map ban for that match. It will be the third Masters grand final that Paper Rex participates in, with Masters Santiago and Masters Toronto taking place within the last year.
