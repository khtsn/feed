Wordle 687 3/6

⬜🟨🟨⬜⬜ ROUTE

Yeah, I know there are mathematically determined, scientifically approved “best” opening Wordle words, but I like to leave the choice to a higher power: my television. I play at night while watching TV. I’ll open my laptop mid-show, and the first five-letter word a character speaks that doesn’t end in “ed” or a plural is my opener. Lately I’ve been binge-watching “The West Wing,” a show that I never watched when it was on, but whose greatness, especially for broadcast television, only becomes more evident with the passing years. Someone else in my household clogged up the DVR by recording every episode when HLN did a “West Wing” marathon awhile back.

I was partway into Season 4, Episode 2, when I played this puzzle. The show, the second half of a two-parter called “20 Hours in America,” had one plotline that involved a few presidential staff members who were stranded in Indiana after having been accidentally left behind by the presidential motorcade and who were caught in a “Planes, Trains and Automobiles”-type nightmare trying to get back to Washington. They’re about to board a train when a skeptical Josh, a top presidential aide, asks, “The engineer knows the route?”

ROUTE got me two letters, O and U, out of place. So I moved them over one spot with CLOUD (a random guess) and was rewarded with two greens. The key, though, was the L. It seemed unlikely to be the first letter, so it had to be the last, and there was only one five-letter word I could think of that ended in “OUL.” Game over.

I like GHOUL, a Halloween season word if there ever was one, at this time of year because it’s a reminder that, at least as far as I can tell, Wordle pays the calendar no heed. Don’t bother guessing LABOR on Labor Day or GRAVY at Thanksgiving. So why did I give GHOUL only two stars? Because it’s annoyingly, unnecessarily complicated. It could just as easily, and more efficiently, be spelled “gool.” It’s the kind of word that gives English a bad name.

Today’s word is GHOUL, a noun. According to Webster’s New World College Dictionary, it’s “an evil spirit that robs graves and feeds on the flesh of the dead.”
