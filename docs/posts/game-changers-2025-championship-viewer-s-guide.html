Game Changers 2025 Championship viewer's guide

With another year of the Game Changers scene close to wrapping up, the international spotlight will shift to the Game Changers Championship, which heads to Asia for the first time in Seoul at LoL Park.

The event will run from November 20 to November 30, and features 10 teams from around the world. Each team earned its way to Seoul through regional qualifiers, circuit points, and main events. Defending GCC champions Shopify Rebellion Gold Shopify Rebellion Gold North America Rank #1 dodonut Karnthida Chaisrakeo meL Melanie Capone alexis Alexis Guarrasi Noia Nicole Tierce sarahcat Sarah Simpson and defending back-to-back champions G2 Gozen G2 Gozen Europe Rank #4 Akita Esllene Xu amy Amy Lai Vania Aleyna Keskin Petra Petra Stoker mimi Michaela Lintrup are both in the hunt to lift their second and third GCC trophies.

The 10-team, double-elimination bracket for GCC '25.

This year's ten-team, double-elimination bracket is the same one used last year. At Berlin in 2024, teams from the same region were placed on the same side of the bracket, but they have been placed on opposite sides of the bracket for this year's event to ensure a variety of international matchups. All series will be best-of-three, with the lower final and grand final being best-of-five.

Drops for this year's GCC.

Fans will have the opportunity to collect Game Changers Championship broadcast drops this year. To claim drops, viewers will need to connect their Valorant accounts to their YouTube and/or Twitch accounts, and then watch the event on drop-enabled channels during specific match times and on Finals Day.

Viewers from November 20-29 will receive a "GO OFF" title, while those who tune into the Grand Final on November 30 will receive a KAY/O PUNCH spray.

The tournament will be broadcast in multiple languages on Riot's official channels.
