chopper on the future of his career: "I'm contemplating what to do"

Three months after being moved to Spirit's bench, longtime in-game leader Leonid "⁠chopper⁠" Vishnyakov recently shared his thoughts on his time away from competitive Counter-Strike on Russian podcast Smol Talk.

While Ivan "⁠zweih⁠" Gogin, who was also benched following the StarLadder Budapest Major to make room for the additions of Myroslav "⁠zont1x⁠" Plakhotia and Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev, immediately returned to competition with PARIVISION, chopper opted for a different route, announcing on Telegram that he would be stepping away from professional play until the summer to "rest and reset."

chopper announces break from competition following Spirit benching

"I was getting ready to [step away from professional play], more or less," chopper shared on the podcast. "As time goes on, you begin to understand that with this entire schedule, this life of playing this much and putting this much effort into it... it isn't like it used to be.

"The feelings are not the same, the spark is sometimes not there. And you know that with this approach you know that it is going to happen soon, as simple as that."

chopper highlighted streaming and community casts as alternatives to returning to competition

"I understand that if I'm going back to the game, I'd need to give so much time to it. I need to find an org, I need to find a coach, and dedicate a lot of time and effort to it.

"Secondly, I have these thoughts come to me, and I also understand that there's this new interesting thing: streaming and community casts. And if everything works out, it is as if I could put my career to an end. I'm doing what I like: telling something on stream, sharing my experience, and so on. Sometimes there are thoughts that I want to go back, but realistically, you understand how much time and effort you need to do. So now I'm contemplating what to do, so to say."

chopper has not completely forgone the thought of returning, though he acknowledges the investment it would require to achieve his goals, namely building a team from scratch.

"I have some dreams that haven't come true, and one of them is actually trying myself as a captain who would build everything on his own with a partner-coach's help.

"But for this, you need to put in a lot of effort, have a lot of motivation for it, and everything. So it's one of those dreams that I haven't made come true, so maybe we will see that, who knows? But considering what we've talked about before, if some other ideas of mine come into reality, I would be satisfied with that, and I wouldn't need to go back or anything."
