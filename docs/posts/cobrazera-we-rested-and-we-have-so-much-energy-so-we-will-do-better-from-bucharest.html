cobrazera: "We rested and we have so much energy, so we will do better from Bucharest"

The MongolZ returned from a month-long break with some clear ring rust, going 3-10 down against magic in their PGL Astana opener and needing four overtimes (and fending off six map points for magic) to get the job done.

Speaking to HLTV after the match, Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar admitted the team needed some time to get back into the swing of playing in a tournament, but added that the extended break has gone a long way in reenergizing the team after they finished third at PGL Bucharest.

"I think we will do better because we rested and we talked to each other," he said. "We know that, we have a feeling. We rested and we have so much energy, so we will do better from before, from Bucharest."

Listen to the interview here.

Congrats, you got a win, but it was a bit difficult. Not the best start to your run, so tell me about that.

We're just kind of getting used to the tournament, I think. We were on break for almost a month, and we didn't play any tournaments for a month. So we're just breaking that and getting back into the tournaments.

What have you been doing during that month off?

We rested, and kinda personal things like hospital, health... yeah, just doing our own things. And we practiced maybe for 10 days before Astana.

Where did you practice?

In Mongolia with Asian teams.

How was the practice there?

(laughs) Yeah, we didn't play the Asian teams a lot, but it was kinda strange. The playstyle, everything is just different.

How are you feeling in the team now? It has been a while you've gotten used to things, so how are you feeling?

It's good. I'm just thinking of improving myself, and it's good.

Do you feel like it has been a big jump in competition now that you've played at tier one for a while?

Yeah. It has changed, and I think I'm getting used to the teams and knowing their playstyles and everything. It makes me improve I think. I'm just adapting to tier-one by playing them.

Outside the game, I think we kinda got closer than before; Inside, we talked playstyles, and I think we are changing

bLitz mentioned that the team has changed some things coming into this event, you have some people helping with out-of-game stuff, so can you talk about that a bit?

Outside the game, I think we kinda got closer than before, and... (laughs), yeah, that's it. Inside the game, we talked playstyles, a lot of playstyles, and I think we are changing. We are playing differently from before right now.

How so?

Less mistakes. We're trying to do less mistakes and improve our personal things.

You also didn't have maaRaa behind you for a while, a few events where he couldn't make it, but he's back now. How has it been having him back?

Yeah, when he is behind us, the whole thing feels full. When he's not there, it's not full. That's the feeling.

How big of a difference does it make when he is there?

Not that big, but there is a difference. Outside the game and inside the game both. There is a little difference.

You talked about getting back into the swing of things here after starting slow, but it is only going to get more difficult with the teams you face later. So how do you think you'll do once you start facing Falcons, Spirit, those kinds of teams?

It will not be easy, but we will try to win. They're not easy opponents. We'll try hard.

Also for The MongolZ, the results recently have been a bit up and down. You did decently at Bucharest, but overall, it's not quite how they were last year. What do you think you'll be able to do here to make a difference?

I think we will do better because we rested and we talked to each other. We know that, we have a feeling. We rested and we have so much energy, so we will do better from before, from Bucharest.
