GamerLegion dump Liquid out of IEM Atlanta

GamerLegion have eliminated Liquid from IEM Atlanta after getting the better of Jonathan "⁠EliGE⁠" Jablonowski and company in a two-map series (Mirage 13-9, Nuke 13-6).

They will now face off against Astralis in the final match of the group stage.

Liquid picked Mirage in the series and displayed a lacklustre T side during which they racked up just two rounds, but rallied on the defense to put together a comeback. GamerLegion never allowed the scoreline to get close, however, as two low-buy victories netted them a comfortable 13-9 victory.

Nuke was an equally straightforward victory for Janusz "⁠Snax⁠" Pogorzelski's troops, who produced a flawless CT side to breeze to a 13-6 victory and move one win away from the playoffs.

Liquid's struggles continue

"Where do I start," Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek replied when he was asked what went wrong. "We're starting the game slow every time, and overall we didn't perform like we should."

Liquid especially struggled on T sides in Atlanta, as they recorded a 39.5% win rate on the offense, putting them ahead of just BC.Game and Passion UA. "We have what it takes to play a T side, but we're faling so execute it properly. It's not easy when we haven't won in a long time," admitted siuhy.

After dropping from No. 13 to No. 44 in the VRS since the start of the year, Liquid now hope to bounce back at CS Asia Championships 2026, set to start on May 20.
