Maden announces free agency

The former fnatic player is no longer tied to any organization and is free to join another team.

Pavle "⁠Maden⁠" Bošković has announced that he is no longer a part of fnatic and has entered the pool of free agents.

Maden was brought on to replace Benjamin "⁠blameF⁠" Bremer on fnatic at the beginning of the year, but had a rough start posting a .92 rating amid role clashes.

Not long after saying that he was starting to find confidence in his positions and finding his footing on the team, he was moved to the bench when the Black and Orange signed a fourth Ukrainian player in Viktor "⁠Br4tkO⁠" Kondratets

Maden: "If everything clicks, we can go far"

"I’m now officially a free agent and open to new opportunities," Maden posted on X..

"Over the past years, I’ve had the privilege of competing at the highest level of Counter-Strike, gaining valuable experience across international teams, big tournaments, and high-pressure environments. I’m proud of what I’ve achieved, but I’m even more motivated for what comes next.

"I’m looking for a project with clear ambition, strong structure, and the drive to win. I bring experience, professionalism, and a deep commitment to improving every day and contributing to a team environment.

"If you’re building something serious and believe I can be a part of it, feel free to reach out."

After his most productive years in ENCE from January 2022 to December 2023, where he won IEM Dallas, Maden spent nearly a year in Falcons, from December 2023 to November 2024, without much success, before joining regional teammates in Zero Tenacity briefly from May to November in 2025.

Now, after his short stint in fnatic, the Montenegrin rifler is once again back on the market for a new team.
