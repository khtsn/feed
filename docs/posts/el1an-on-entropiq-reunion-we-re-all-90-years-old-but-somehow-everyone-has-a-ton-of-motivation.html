El1an on Entropiq reunion: "We're all 90 years old, but somehow everyone has a ton of motivation"

The core of the former Entropiq team that rose up in 2021 is back together, having reunited on WHITEBIRD in November before the roster was signed by SPARTA in March.

The team's lineup features four out of the five players that fell a single win shy of reaching the playoffs at PGL Major Stockholm, swapping Vladislav "⁠Krad⁠" Kravchenko for Aleksandr "⁠TRAVIS⁠" Timkiv, and took part at their first LAN under the new banner at MPKBK CIS LAN Season 4.

The team missed the playoffs after a narrow 0-2 loss to TDK in the Group D winners' match and were eliminated after losing a rematch to CYBERSHOKE Prospects, but have their eyes set on the future as they hope to capitalize on invites to online tournaments and qualify for a future Major.

"I won't sit here and claim we're gonna upset Vitality — nope, we won't," Aleksei "⁠El1an⁠" Gusev told HLTV in Moscow. "But there are some wild dreams that at our best, in good form, we could play around top 10. I could believe in that, but it's a long road. For starters, in the next six months to a year, let's just be consistently top 30, that would already be nice."

Editor's note:

This interview was conducted in Russian and translated by HLTV staff.

Tell us about your current team, SPARTA. How did it come together, and who's the leader of the roster?

Originally we all played together in Entropiq, before that in Winstrike as well. Essentially, this is our old roster, but without Krad. Instead of him, we were getting a different player, a common acquaintance of ours. But we realized he wasn't quite at the level yet, and at our age of like 90, training someone from scratch takes too long. So we brought in Travis. Initially it worked out that Nickel and I were just without an org. Lack1 got benched from GUN5, and Forester got benched on CYBERSHOKE.

So it was just a shared idea: we were having fun playing together, let's get together again. Then we looked for sponsors and an org, and found one: WHITEBIRD. It wasn't a proper organization in the traditional sense, just a crypto exchange that sponsored us. No contracts, nothing [formal]. We were looking for other options in parallel, played well, broke into the top 40, and then we got an offer from SPARTA. That's it.

As for the leader — well, we used to have hooch in Entropiq. Right now there's no coach, so it's collective. Well, Nickel is the IGL, but he's not really some kind of incredible leader. He's just a very good, warm, decent person, a friend, and so on.

A team without a coach sounds a bit like a mix nowadays. How serious is your approach and mindset toward the team and your prospects? Is the goal to get a coach and add a more classic, structured approach to it?

The thing is, if you look at the scene right now, there aren't that many available coaches. The ones who come to mind are Xoma and PASHANOJ, and both of them are already in orgs. So bad luck.

But we've known each other for eight to ten years, played together for at least five. So there's already an inherent understanding of who we are, what each person's strengths and weaknesses are. Plus everyone has experience, so we don't need someone just to make sure we're not banging our heads against the wall.

But an outside support, a different perspective would be useful. So yes, a coach is something we'd want eventually — just no candidates right now.

In 2021, there was a robbery: for some reason they decided to give stickers starting from the top eight, and we finished ninth

What are your overall goals and ambitions for this roster? Do you have more concrete plans for the future?

Well, globally — if we're not talking about the absolute top, where teams are in the top 10 or academy teams — everyone's goal is basically the same. The goal is simply to qualify for the Major, get a sticker, and then fight it out from there. As for our specific team, I won't sit here and claim we're gonna upset Vitality — nope, we won't.

But there are some wild dreams that at our best, in good form we could play around top 10. I could believe in that, but it's a long road. For starters, in the next six months to a year, let's just be consistently top 30, that would already be nice.

It's been quite a while since you yourself were close to the top 10. Now that you've set that as a goal, do you feel like you have essentially pulled off a comeback?

Yeah, I do feel it. My career is already probably 10 years in, same with Lack1. And we're two retards still with no personal stickers. [At PGL Major Stockholm] in 2021, there was a robbery: for some reason, they decided to give stickers starting from the top eight, and we finished ninth. Really disappointing. So yeah, those goals are there.

We had a period before, when we were top 70-80, we accepted invites to all of the events that were there, every tournament available. We had like three officials a day. I caught those vibes of 2020-2021, it was very pleasant and interesting. So now we'll have some invites, and we will just have the same simple task — grind, play, play, play. It happens that we're all 90 years old, but somehow everyone has a ton of motivation.

Grinding tier-3: is it 'pleasant and interesting,' or is there something else to it?

Depends on what kind of grind. Obviously, when you're playing playoffs of CCT, for example, that's nice. But if you're in some sketchy group stage with some... nameless teams, we'll leave it at that. Very interesting guys, not exactly pleasant.

It was just a temporary thing — play, maybe get lucky, get stickers

Since you left Entropiq, you played for JiJieHao, which had a great variety of European and Middle-Eastern players representing it. Tell me about your experience playing for them.

I was just sitting without an org. The choice was either to keep playing in some semi-random mixes where I could already tell nothing would come of it, or to just jump into... obviously not the strongest team, obviously with some language issues, but since the Asian region is weaker, there was potential to get stickers. We actually made it to the [RMR], played there, and lost to TYLOO and Grayhound, which is fine, honestly. So it was just a temporary thing — play, maybe get lucky, get stickers. Didn't get lucky. But there were no serious plans [for this team], obviously.

Can you tell me more about what impression you got of the org?

It's just Chinese businessmen with an insane amount of money. As I understand it, he owns a shopping mall in China — just a very wealthy person who for some reason wants their own stickers. So he was just giving money to cover for travel, a LAN center to play from Dubai, and that's it.
