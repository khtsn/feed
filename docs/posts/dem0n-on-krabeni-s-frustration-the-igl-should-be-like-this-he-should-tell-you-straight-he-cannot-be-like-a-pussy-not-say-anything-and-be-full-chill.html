dem0n on Krabeni's frustration: "The IGL should be like this. He should tell you straight, he cannot be like a pussy, not say anything, and be full chill"

FUT narrowly kept their IEM Cologne Major run alive after eliminating B8 in the 0-2 pool of Stage 3, a match that meant more for the European combine as a loss would likely have dropped them out of the top-12 invite bubble to tier-one tournaments.

"I'm just happy that we survived one more game," Dmytro "⁠dem0n⁠" Myroshnychenko said in his first-ever interview with HLTV. "It was really important for us because, for now, we secured all the invites. So, some pressure [taken] off, but still we are not playing the best Counter-Strike here. So, we need to wake up and for the next days because we still have a chance to make it to the playoffs and I believe that we can make it."

The 18-year-old also touched on how he is adjusting to regularly facing tier-one competition, and admitted he needed the kind of wake-up call that Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija gave him in a voice comm that was aired on the broadcast.

"I wasn't really waking up, I was in my own thoughts, and this, in some way, he helped me to bounce back and I kind of did a great job on T side," dem0n said. "But overall, I think the IGL should be like this. Sometimes he should tell you straight what is happening; he cannot be like a pussy and just not say anything and be you know full chill."

Listen to the interview here.

Congrats, you stay alive at the Major. A rough game at the end, but you got it over the line. What are you thinking after that?

I don't know, really I'm just happy that we survived one more game. It was really important for us because, for now, we secured all the invites. So, some pressure [taken] off, but still we are not playing the best Counter-Strike here. So, we need to wake up and for the next days because we still have a chance to make it to the playoffs and I believe that we can make it.

We [had] a bit more pressure because we are 0-2, and if we don't get one win, we'll lose all invites

Can you tell me why you might not be playing up to your level? You had a couple of rough matchups against Vitality and G2 to start, but just in general.

Overall, I wouldn't say that we are playing so terrible, so shit, because we are playing against Vitality, against G2, who are not bad teams. We just lack experience against these types of teams, but today we [had] a bit more pressure because we are 0-2, and if we don't get one win, we'll lose all invites and it will be hard. So yeah, maybe today it was just a bit more pressure overall.

How's it been playing with that pressure and trying to adjust to it? You've been playing a little bit more in the tier one circuit for the last couple of months, but to hang on to that kind of invite is really important, right? So how much stress were you guys under with that?

We just wanted to really make it, so at the end of Ancient, we were a little giving them a comeback and we really wanted to win, and that's why we kind of fucked up a bit.

I wasn't really waking up, I was in my own thoughts, and this, in some way, he helped me to bounce back

During the tournament, listening here, but even just some of the comms that have been put out on the broadcast. there have been times where Krabeni seems really frustrated. One of them was directly to you on Mirage saying something like, 'This isn't CCT.' Can you just tell me about some of that frustration and what you think is causing it?

I mean, for sure, at this moment I can tell about myself: I wasn't really waking up, I was in my own thoughts, and this, in some way, he helped me to bounce back and I kind of did a great job on T side. But overall, I think the IGL should be like this. Sometimes he should tell you straight what is happening; he cannot be like a pussy and just not say anything and be you know full chill.

I mean just look at apEX, he is all the time blaming Vitality when they are doing something wrong. I just remember when we were fucking them on Anubis, apEX was just full blaming. I mean, it's 50/50, sometimes it's necessary, sometimes it's not.

How do you handle that inside the team when he's bringing up those kinds of conflicts? Is it something that you support him with, or how is it actually working in that dynamic?

I mean, everyone should control their emotions, and it depends on each person, how they're taking this type of pressure. Everyone is raised differently, and for example, Krabeni, he doesn't care at all. He's just with a different mentality, and that's all.

I wanted to ask about your development as well. Coming into this team and playing in tier-one now. How have you felt adjusting to the circuit and to playing against some of these best teams individually?

It just took me some time to find myself here because it was really unusual because you have to travel to play these types of LANs, and not instantly grinding online tournaments and doing whatever you want. So it's more professional, in some ways, and I just think we are adapting. It's only our first half-year in tier one.

these types of teams are fully anti-stratting; everyone is preparing so hard for you, and sometimes you are just lost and don't know what to do

Have you found it difficult, individually, to adjust to that?

Yeah, in some way, it's true because these types of teams are fully anti-stratting; everyone is preparing so hard for you, and sometimes you are just lost and don't know what to do because you think everything might not work.

You're 1-2 now, still a pretty difficult path to the playoffs. How do you feel about your chances to actually make it, and how important would that be to you?

I think we really have a good chance; it depends on which team we play because first, we played Vitality, who is the number-one team, and then we played G2, who I really rate, a top-five team, in my opinion.

So I don't think we did something bad, and it was close games against Vitality and G2, so I won't say we are, I don't know, shitters and something or whatever. We still can make the playoffs, and I really believe in it.

Are you still riding some of the confidence from the last stage, where you went 3-0?

It's not a lack of confidence; we kind of [have] the same mentality, I would say. Just extra pressure, better teams, it's just overall everything better and extra.
