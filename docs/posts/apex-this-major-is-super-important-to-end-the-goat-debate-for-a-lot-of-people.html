apEX: "This Major is super important to end the [GOAT] debate for a lot of people"

apEX is one Major away from matching dupreeh's five

Vitality enter IEM Cologne off the back of a month-long break from tournaments, having last played at IEM Atlanta, where Natus Vincere stopped their run of success in the quarter-finals.

We spoke to Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire on the media day before Stage 3 to ask him about Vitality's trip to the US, which they largely used to take some time off between the BLAST Rivals and IEM Atlanta, and how it affected them in the lead-up to the Major.

Needless to say, the captain isn't worried. "Of course I hope that the teams could beat us if we don't play Counter-Strike," he said in jest.

"A week off is not enough sometimes to get better and rested, so we decided to do that. We didn't win and people felt we were a bit less good for sure, but it's okay, we still won five trophies in the first part of the season, so [blows a raspberry], I don't care."

When dupreeh joined Vitality in 2022, I had one Major and he had four. I never imagined being close to him at any time

Vitality have always set up goals around the season to ramp up to key events, and this one is no different. If anything, it is even more special for apEX. "The biggest goal of the year is the Major, Cologne as well, which I feel like makes it even bigger than the usual ones, so it would be nice to win it," he said.

"People still think we are a bit behind Astralis because of the Majors, even though I feel the Grand Slam counts for more than a Major," apEX went on. "It's been a long journey in that we're trying to still find new goals, still try to catch Astralis. But for us this Major is super important as well, just to end the [GOAT] debate for a lot of people."

apEX, welcome to Cologne. I wanted to go back to your time in America first, because it was clear that Vitality didn't maintain quite the same level you had throughout the year so far. That came against the backdrop of a lack of practice and some time off you took during the time, but it also showed to the other teams that there's blood in the water. Do you feel like it affected that indomitable aura that the team had?

Well, of course I hope that the teams could beat us if we don't play Counter-Strike because at some point it would be tough for the others. But, actually, we don't think about that at all. When we come to a block of tournaments, for example the first block was Bounty, Krakow and Cluj, and the goal was to win Krakow. Then the second block was to win the Grand Slam. So we did what we had to do and we are setting up goals for the team.

We are doing our best, but sometimes it's impossible to go for three months straight and give your best at every tournament. We've been working that way all the time, just the tournaments this year have been a bit different to last year. Last year, it was more two by two by two. Here we had two, three, four, and one. So for us we had to be sure we wouldn't be burnt out. A week off is not enough sometimes to get better and rested, so we decided to do that.

We're fine with that, we've always done that, and, yeah, we didn't win and people felt we were a bit less good for sure, but it's okay, we still won five trophies in the first part of the season, so [blows a raspberry], no matter what, I don't care.

So how did you ramp up to the Major, then? How did you guys spend the last month?

A week off again, but a real week off at home, it's always different. We had a nice week off in the US, but it was a bit raining as well. I won't lie, when we got out of Atlanta, I was pretty tired. But then a week off without CS, enjoying with families etc. because we were away for a long time, and then a full grind.

We like the 2+ weeks of preparation. We had like five-six days at home and five days of bootcamp of practice. I think it went well. We liked the way we were preparing. That's really important for us to feel like we are ready. It went okay, like the preparation we used to have. The biggest goal of the year is the Major, Cologne as well, which I feel like makes it even bigger than the usual ones, so it would be nice to win it.

In the meantime, we've seen Spirit rise up again after a rough start to the season, with donk showing some incredible form, we've seen Falcons get a bit closer to that goal of finally getting over the line with karrigan, and NAVI took a win off of you guys in Atlanta. How do you see the competition at this Major and how difficult it's gonna be compared to the last couple?

To be honest, it's not something that we really think about, like who is strong and who isn't strong. For us, every opponent can be strong and we treat them the same way. It's been our strength to not overthink as well.

donk is insane, yeah, indeed, he's insane, but it's not something we think about. We just try to think about our team and how we can be good, because, I repeat that for 18 months, if we play our A game, no matter who, they have no chance. That's something we've been trying to work on.

Yeah, some teams have risen up while we were not there, and that's good for the scene, but I think the truth is that we're still the favorite here no matter what, even though we lost one trophy. Don't forget about what happened before. That's in our hands to be sure that we will give our best here and try to bring our A game.

You said before that you're good at consistently setting these goals. You've set a lot of them and they've fallen one by one, to the point where you've already said you consider your team to be the greatest of all time after you grabbed your second Grand Slam. Is it difficult to maintain that same level of pace now that you're like, 'okay, we've passed Astralis'?

I actually think that people still think that we are a bit behind Astralis because of the Majors, even though I feel the Grand Slam counts for more than just a single Major because it's harder to win a Grand Slam than Majors, when they've been won less than Majors since they've been created.

For me, yeah, it's been a long journey in the way that we're trying to still find new goals, still try to catch Astralis, in a way. But for us this Major is super important as well, just to end the debate for a lot of people, to be the only team with Astralis to win back-to-back-to-back Majors and me [matching] dupreeh and not wasting our time in the way that we are once again lucky to have this team and to give the best energy possible to win, because you never know when the dropdown is gonna be.

Maybe it's gonna start now, maybe in six months, in a year, we have no clue. For us we're just giving our best all the time. We have some records to break, and maybe it's gonna be our own record to break after, and that's something to try to be the best at, to motivate my guys to be sure that we are the best, until I retire (smiles).

You were talking about dupreeh, obviously being one away from matching his five and being only the second player to that. How significant would that be for you personally?

You know, what's crazy is that when dupreeh joined Vitality in 2022, I had one Major and he had four. I never imagined being close to him at any time, and now I'm just one behind him. To equal him would be fantastic and it would show how hard work I've put into my career so far and how lucky I was as well to have such a great team with me.

I think it's a really tough thing, to get five Majors in a career, and it's a long journey, but it would be nice to do it here in Cologne. A lot of people will be here for an iconic tournament, also families will be here as well, my family, so... I really want to do it here because it would be one of the best wins I could get ever.

Speaking of your family, congratulations on the big news, a baby on the way. I saw you spent a lot of time with mezii back in New York, have you been getting some parenthood tips or anything?

Yeah, a bit, of course (laughs). We are the same in the way that we are traveling a lot and he doesn't see his daughter a lot, and I won't see my boy a lot as well, but that's part of the job. I won't last long, so it's okay, I can still enjoy a bit, that the kid will grow up and not remember much from his first years, I would say. It's gonna be tough, but he gave me some nice tips, and let's see how it goes (laughs).

You're saying he won't remember the first years — it feels like you don't really see it as a changing of a perspective on your life and career and how it will affect it?

It will, for sure, but you never know when it never happened for you, I guess. I will know the feeling and everything when the baby arrives, but for now it's way too early to speak about it.

I don't have the experience yet, but for sure it's gonna change a lot of things. Maybe it will remove my motivation to play Counter-Strike as well, and maybe I won't play as much as I expect and maybe I will feel sad to be far away from my family again when you want to share those moments with your kid, as well. I don't know. I have no idea how life will be with him, but I'm just excited. It's the path of life, I would say.
