LEVIATÁN starts London campaign with 2-1 win over Global

In the final match of VCT Masters London's opening Swiss Stage, LEVIATÁN LEVIATÁN North America Rank #4 spike Rodrigo Lombardi blowz Guilherme Oliviera Neon Bruno Rodríguez Sato Eduardo Kenzo Nagahama Sato kiNgg Francisco Aravena defeated Global Esports Global Esports Asia-Pacific Rank #5 PatMen Patrick Mendoza UdoTan Go Kyung-won (고경원) Kr1stal Savva Fedorov xavi8k Xavier Juan Autumn Kale Dunne 2-1 in a match to remember for several players.

Seven players in the server made their international debuts in this game, including all five players for LEV, but it was second-year players UdoTan and Sato who impressed the most for their respective teams. The series was played out on Global's Pearl, LEVIATÁN's Fracture, and the Decider map Split. Global won Pearl 13-4, LEV won Fracture 13-11, and then went on to win Split 13-9.

While several teams have moved away from using Neon following the Duelist's recent nerfs, both UdoTan and spike opted to play the agent on the opening map, with the latter playing her on all three maps. From the get-go, Global beat the wheels off of LEV on Pearl. An early 7-0 run saw the multi-national squad get out to an early lead and never quite lose grip of it. The Global players fought back from down in numbers several times, with UdoTan and Kr1stal combining for four clutches on the map.

Kr1stal caps off a 21-kill map with the bold stick to close out Pearl.

LEV's pick of Fracture was a map that Global has avoided for much of the year, playing it just once through Pacific Stage 1 and banning it seven times. While both teams went with Waylay-centric comps, LEV's front-footed double-Initiator comp with Tejo and Breach appeared to be far more disruptive to start. LEV overwhelmed GE 5-1 to start, but GE fought back to keep the score even at halftime. Once LEV was on attack, the map was back and forth. Neither team could really seem to pull away from the other until LEV finally found the edge.

LEV shakes off some heroics from UdoTan to close out the map.

On Split, both teams opted for different variations of the classic Omen-Viper comp, with LEV running Phoenix-Neon while Global had UdoTan's Raze as the solo Duelist and xavi8k on Sage. Global just appeared to have a better grasp of the map on attack, using Autumn 's late lurks to gain control over the map en route to an 8-4 halftime lead. The lead came in no shortage of thanks to UdoTan, who had 14 in the first half alone. Once LEV got onto attack though, the tide shifted.

Where Global was strong in breaking down the map, LEVIATÁN was elite at utilizing set plays on barrier drop and overwhelming Global's defenses. A run of six straight rounds gave LEV the lead, and GE could only claw back one round to stop the bleeding. Nerfs be damned, spike was strong on the Neon, logging 19 kills on Split to combine with Sato for 38 kills on the map in a turnaround 13-9 victory.

Another smooth attack round seals the series for LEV.

UdoTan's 56 kills were not enough for GE in the end, while Satos' 54 led the way for LEV. LEV advances in the Swiss stage, now one win away from qualifying for the playoffs, while Global will need a win to avoid elimination.

With the first round of the Swiss Stage complete, an on-stage draw show was held to determine the stage's next matchups. In drawing the 1-0 teams and 0-1 teams against each other, several regional matchups were drawn, promising some heated matchups between teams who have history against each other.
