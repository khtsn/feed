Five teams qualify for playoffs in week two of China Stage 1

Week two of VCT China 2026 Stage 1 has already confirmed some playoff teams, with EDG and XLG remaining undefeated after four games. TYLOO remains the best team in Group Alpha at 3-1, while Wolves and Nova are still looking for their first wins in Stage 1.

Here is how the matches went for week two of VCT China 2026 Stage 1:

With each team having already played four matches, the playoff picture is quickly forming in China. EDG and XLG have qualified for the Stage 1 playoffs, dominating Group Omega, and will face each other in the final group-stage match of Stage 1. Both Nova and Wolves continued their struggles, with Wolves losing two tight series against DRG and XLG while Nova has yet to win a map. Since Wolves and Nova cannot qualify for the Stage 1 playoffs, Trace and DRG also qualified for the playoffs, now changing the focus in Group Omega from playoff qualification to playoff seeding.

In Group Alpha, a 1-1 week for TYLOO kept its position at the top, thanks to Bilibili losing to TEC and defeating FPX afterward. Due to its map differential and record, TYLOO is the only team from Group Alpha to have secured a playoff slot in Stage 1.

As for standout players in week two, it is hard not to highlight a TYLOO player after the team qualified for the playoffs. In its win over All Gamers, duelist splash outdueled regional star f4ngeer , dropping 62 kills in three maps, with a 1.33 match rating and 15 first kills. If you include the loss against FPX, Erv was the best TYLOO player, ending the week with a 1.31 match rating and 81% KAST.

However, it is hard to deny one player of their statistical dominance in week two, and that is AAAAY from FPX. Similar to how dominant ZmjjKK was in week one, AAAAY top fragged in both matches this week and was magnificent in his match against TYLOO. He ended the week with a region-leading 1.39 match rating, 1.46 K:D, 82% KAST, 1.01 KPR, and an absurd 48% headshot percentage. He was also second in ACS with 286.2, and second in ADR at 184.8, only behind ZmjjKK. The fact that AAAAY did this on a non-duelist role easily puts him as the player of the week, but he might need to keep that form up to secure FPX a playoff slot in a competitive Group Alpha.

  • Trace Esports vs. Dragon Ranger Gaming
  • Titan Esports Club vs. FunPlus Phoenix
  • All Gamers vs. Bilibili Gaming
