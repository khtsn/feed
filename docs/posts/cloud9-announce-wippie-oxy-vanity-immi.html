Cloud9 announce wippie, OXY, vanity, Immi

Cloud9 Cloud9 North America Rank #4 Xeppaa Erick Bach jakee Jake Anderson vanity Anthony Malaspina OXY Francis Hoang wippie Maxim Shepelev have announced a new roster, consisting of former G2 Esports G2 Esports North America Rank #14 trent Trent Cairns valyn Jacob Batio JonahP Jonah Pulice neT Michael Bernet core of Maxim "wippie" Shepelev, Francis "OXY" Hoang, and head coach Ian "Immi" Harding. Additionally, the organization welcomed back Anthony "vanity" Malaspina while keeping Jake "jakee" Anderson and Erick "Xeppaa" Bach.

Wippie, vanity, and Immi have spent time together on Version1 Version1 Inactive during their VCT NA: Challengers 2021 campaign. After securing a slot for Masters: Reykjavik 2021, V1 placed outside the top four in the event, but wippie couldn't attend due to visa issues. vanity later left the team after Reykjavik for Cloud9, while wippie and Immi remained. Following the disbandment of V1 in 2022, the duo later joined G2 Esports alongside OXY ahead of the VCL NA: Challengers 2023. G2 came out strong, finishing second in the VCL NA Midseason Face-Off. However, the roster started to falter as they had a 0-5 second split, then ended their year with a top-six placement in the playoffs. The roster later split in July.

Cloud9 have had a long-standing tenure in Valorant since 2020, with 2021 being their best year given their top-eight placement at Champions. In 2022, the roster didn't carry the same momentum as they failed to qualify for international tournaments. Vanity stayed on for the Americas Partnership League project, but after losing to DRX in LOCK//IN, Cloud9 parted ways with the in-game leader before the official start of the split, with jakee being one of two replacements. vanity joined Shopify Rebellion Shopify Rebellion Inactive v1c Victor Truong mada Adam Pampuch during VCL NA Challengers Split 2, where they placed in the top eight for playoffs.
