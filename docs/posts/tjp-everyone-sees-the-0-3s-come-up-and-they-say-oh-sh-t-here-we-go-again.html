TjP: "Everyone sees the 0-3s come up and they say, 'Oh sh*t, here we go again'"

Stage 1 of Major always throws up a surprise or two in the best-of-one stage, and IEM Cologne is no different.

Favored 0-3 pick THUNDER dOWNUNDER got the better of MIBR with a dominant 13-6 win on Inferno, with the team's Major rookies Tyson "⁠asap⁠" Paterson (2.04 rating) and Tynan "⁠TjP⁠" Purtell (1.70) leading the way for their team.

"The three veterans of the team make it very easy. You just slot in, they play well around this, and they set us up for success," TjP told HLTV after the win.

"I think it'd be stupid to say that it was all done by us, that it was an individual effort. It was a great team effort all the way around."

Listen to the interview here.

First of all, congrats. As I said, something quite unexpected for everyone. How do you feel after getting the better of MIBR today?

Look, it's always good to get the monkey off the shoulder so to say, to get that first win. It's good, it's relieving, but also it's a show of our hard work, so it's good.

To ask about the hard work. You knew about the opponents quite a bit in advance, so was this win down to some anti-stratting as well?

It'd be stupid to say there wasn't. There's always that bit in the game, like everyone does it. I think we built a strong game plan, my credits to the coach and aliStair. It was a strong game plan, we executed well, and it's always a relief.

I spoke to dexter yesterday, he said that you guys need to play with your balls out. And you definitely did, as did asap, the two inexperienced players on the team. How did it feel to be the deciding factor, I guess you could say, in the win?

It's always good to play well. There was a lot of pressure, I'd say, there's no saying there wasn't. But the three veterans of the team make it very easy. You just slot in, they play well around this, and they set us up for success. I think it'd be stupid to say that it was all done by us, that it was an individual effort. It was a great team effort all the way around.

You were one of the 0-3 favourites for Pickems, as usual with Aussie teams, and you ruined it once again for everyone. Do you have anything to say to the people that picked you 0-3?

Oh, eep doing it, it gives me a bit of enjoyment. It gives everyone enjoyment. Everyone sees the 0-3s come up and they say, 'Oh shit, here we go again.' And then they see us do the exact same thing over and over again. Maybe they'll learn, who knows.

To just ask about the rest of the tournament. You've still got some best-of-ones to play and considering you are the underdogs, it should favour you. Are you just trying to get as much done as possible in the best-of-ones before you get to best-of-threes, which will test your map pool more?

The best-of-ones are always good for us. You know, our map pool isn't as strong as probably in Europe. They have a lot more chances to practice against good teams and stuff like that. But, you know, we're confident. With our preparations and stuff like that, as long as we execute well as a team, I think we can go deeper than a lot of people think.

You've surpassed expectations already of many people, but is Stage 2 a possibility for you guys, if we're talking about in-team expectations?

Yeah, look, we haven't really talked about it and I haven't even thought about it. But after today, I think it definitely opens a possibility. It's just about grinding through the gears, doing what we do, and if it comes, it's a blessing.
