Spirit forfeit projected $420,000 ACI share following IEM Atlanta skip

ESL has confirmed that Spirit have forfeited their share of the 2026 Annual Club Incentive (ACI) after exceeding the allowed number of tournament declines during the 2025-2026 period.

Spirit were tied with FURIA for first place in the ACI ranking after IEM Rio with a projected share of $420,550. Instead of going to Atlanta, the Russian team attended PGL Astana, earning $512,000 (split equally between team and club share) by finishing first.

It was Spirit's third decline of an EFG tournament invite during the 2025-2026 period, having previously skipped IEM Melbourne and IEM Dallas in 2025.

Under normal circumstances, teams may decline up to three invites in a two-year period, provided that they do not skip more than two tournaments in a single calendar year. However, if an EFG tournament has been promoted to a Major — which is the case of IEM Cologne 2026 —, the maximum number of declines during that period is only two.

Spirit will not be eligible to re-enter the ACI program until 2027. However, any tournament declines this year still count towards the 2027 ACI.

The Annual Club Incentive is designed to reward teams for their contribution to the ESL Pro Tour "in the form of tournament attendance, viewership, as well as promotional activities related to ESL events." Just like in 2025, it will reward the top 16 teams in the standings at the end of the year.
