JessieVash and Team Secret part ways after five years

One of the VCT's oldest stints has come to a close nearly five years later following Team Secret Team Secret Asia-Pacific Rank #137 kellyS Kelly Sedillo Zeus Jhian Vega Sylvan Go Young-sup (고영섭) BerserX Rizkie Adla Kusuma 's announcement of Jessie "JessieVash" Cuyco's departure.

JessieVash led various iterations of Team Secret's rosters over the span of 1737 days. (Photo: Colin Young-Wolff/Riot Games)

Initially onboarded back in late 2021 alongside the rest of the Bren Esports Bren Esports Inactive squad, JessieVash remained with Team Secret throughout their acceptance into the VCT partnership in 2023 and into the new era of the competitive circuit.

Unfortunately, the organization achieved lackluster results within the Pacific League as Vash's squad failed to qualify for a single international event – their closest run manifesting in a second-place finish at 2023's Pacific LCQ – and slowly began nosediving in standings over time.

Many roster changes over the years would bring some hope to fans, but as Team Secret continued to miss out on global events, nothing seemed to bring them remotely close to reaching a Masters or Champions event.

The 2026 season saw secret finish dead last in Pacific's Kickoff, a result that was narrowly avoided in Stage 1 after the team scraped up their first win of the year against Gen.G Gen.G Korea Rank #5 Lakia Kim Jong-min (김종민) ZynX Kim Dong-ha (김동하) Ash Ha Hyun-cheol (하현철) Karon Kim Won-tae (김원태) t3xture Kim Na-ra (김나라) five months into the season.

JessieVash's departure shuts the door on a journey that spanned over multiple years that not many outside of Jake "Boaster" Howlett and Erick "Xeppaa" Bach can boast, especially at the top level of the VCT.

“Nothing but gratitude. Grateful for the journey,” wrote JessieVash. “Looking forward to spending more time with my son. While keeping an open mind for what's next.”
