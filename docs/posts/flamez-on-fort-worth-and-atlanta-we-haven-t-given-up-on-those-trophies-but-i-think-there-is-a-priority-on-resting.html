flameZ on Fort Worth and Atlanta: "We haven't given up on those trophies, but I think there is a priority on resting"

The Israeli rifler hopes to win two more trophies before the Major as Vitality try to balance their ambitions with conserving energy for the season-ending event in Cologne.

Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan took some time after a fought-out three-map series against FUT, playing with their coach András "⁠coolio⁠" Fercsák in place of Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea, in which Vitality had to dig deep to push it over the line.

"There's frustration because we lost to ourselves," flameZ said of blundering Dust2, a map that Vitality hadn't lost all year, "we didn't lose to them."

The Israeli rifler talked about what went wrong, what went right, and the situation Vitality are in after winning the ESL Grand Slam during a busy period of travel going back-and-forth from the Americas while trying to conserve their energies ahead of the Major.

"We've kind of accomplished what we wanted in this block," flameZ says of getting two trophies at BLAST Rotterdam and the Grand Slam-winning IEM Rio during a four-tournament stretch that includes BLAST Rivals and IEM Atlanta, "but we still want to get as many trophies as we can."

Listen to the interview here.

You had a kind of crazy game. Started off on Mirage, so far so good, then you go to Dust2, a map that has defined Vitality during this winning period. You hadn't lost it all year, and then you drop it here against a team with a coach [playing]. What happened?

We took it too easy. At the beginning of the T side, we were like 6-1 up, or 6-2 or 6-3, and we just didn't convert rounds that were so easy. Nobody played situations that we usually play so good. We played them like shit this time, so they took those rounds.

I think there's the factor that it felt like we were rusty in this game because a lot of communication was missed, sometimes there was communication but not the correct response... we haven't done much out of ourselves, we haven't made plays and initiated that much, and I think that sometimes is going to happen and you have to take those losses as a good thing. You can't just be sad that you lost, you have to take it and wake up and try to do your best on the second map.

They also have good individuals. They played well, and they had good timings on the lurks and those kinds of things on their T side. We lost some entries, and then we decided to play back instead of doing our thing, and we just didn't have the right plan in many rounds.

The goal is to always make the playoffs, and once we reach the playoffs, it's a different case for us

Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan

It seemed like you were stressed. You had a little desk slam there at the end of the map. You let the anger out and went to have a talk [between maps]. What happened there? What happened in the huddle?

Obviously, there's frustration because we lost to ourselves; we didn't lose to them. They were not insane to beat us, we just played like shit, and we were in charge of that shit, so we went outside, and it's a new map. We gotta put it behind us, take the things we know from this map that we didn't do good, and try to improve on the third map.

But you know, it's Counter-Strike. When a team doesn't have pressure, and their individual skill is that high, and they shoot really well, it's going to be tough. I don't feel like we were the sharpest today, the French were playing super good, but the other three, we weren't individually really, really sharp.

It paid off for them to some extent, but also, I think that the communication we took from the second map to the third map, we had to step up the level of communication, meaning that we had to communicate about how to close the rounds, and not just let them. We took it really well after that, we reset very quickly, and had a good CT side.

You came back with your hands wrapped [between maps]. What was that about?

I don't know why, but the hottest countries are the coldest. The amount of AC felt like winter here; my hands felt so dry, it's so cold, like we were all freezing in the booth with five hand warmers. It's not BLAST's fault, right? Being the morning game is kind of like a social experiment. You play, and BLAST sees what needs to be adjusted and whatnot.

It's not anybody's fault because it's such a big place, and even if the AC is bad, it takes like one or two hours just to adjust it. My hands were so dry and so cold, so I had to warm them up. I took two hand warmers, put them in my hands, wrapped them with paper, and hoped that maybe it would have some effect.

Did it work?

For a bit. Now the temperature is really good, so it got better. It was working for the time I had the warmers on, but that's it.

I think we were just a bit rusty on a bit of utility and some understanding of certain rounds

So tell me about that Nuke. It looked like it was done after that first half, and then you had to go all the way to the last round. It seemed like there was a lot of frustration there, as well. What was happening there?

I'm not so sure because in many rounds we had an OK situation, and we had a good plan, but one small thing goes wrong, and that kills us. Or they played good, I feel like they played really good some rounds. We had opportunities, but in many rounds, I wouldn't say that we lost to ourselves.

There were many rounds that I thought we had a good plan, but I'm not sure if we failed utility, or missed a kill here and there, just sloppy things on that side of utility and individual skill. I'm not so sure yet on that T side, but we'll watch it, and if we play Nuke tomorrow, we'll come in with way more preparation on those things. I think we were just a bit rusty on a bit of utility and some understanding of certain rounds.

We've kind of accomplished what we wanted in this block, but we still want to get as many trophies as we can

Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan

Do you think winning the Grand Slam affects the mindset going into these tournaments during this time before the Major? Is the intensity maybe not there for you guys?

Maybe that, or we didn't attempt to pracc. We came for four days, five days home, and we just flew back. We didn't have time to prep. We went from one jetlag to Europe jetlag, and to the USA, so I wouldn't say there's no intensity. It's tough to bring a lot of energy when you lack energy outside of that, and then obviously we just want to get better with every map we play at this tournament.

We always do that, we don't care if the beginning of the tournament may be rough, the goal is to always make playoffs, and once we reach the playoffs, it's a different case for us. I would say that I think it would be easier to win the Grand Slam and then go back-to-back tournaments and you still have some CS form, than going West and then flying to America again, but it's just the game, it's just the events, we can't complain or do anything, so we don't really care. We just want to do our best, play good Counter-Strike — today it wasn't the case —, but for tomorrow we'll come with a very different approach.

There was also a tweet about how apEX and mezii are making plans [between events in America]. Is it like a point in the season where you're trying to conserve your energies a bit more before you go to the Major?

In these four tournaments, the main goal was to win the Grand Slam, and we won two out of the four already. We're not giving up yet on the last two trophies, but I think it's more about getting better in the officials and trying our best to, as you said, conserve energy, because the grind before the Major will be very intense and we have a lot of time to do that, so we'll have to come strong, and for now, we've kind of accomplished what we wanted in this block, but we still want to get as many trophies as we can.

We have such a great team that even when we play sloppy, we can win those games, so yeah, we haven't given up on those trophies, but I think there is a priority on resting because it has been a long while that we've grinded, grinded, grinded for the Grand Slam, and the Major now is the most important. Still, we're happy we accomplished the Grand Slam, but this is the bigger priority now.

Do you have any plans while you're here in America?

Me and ropz are going to go hike through some places in America, and we're just going to enjoy ourselves and have some time off, but we'll still be on the computer. ropz is not leaving it alone.
