Vitality take down PARIVISION to claim PGL Cluj-Napoca title

Vitality have laid claim to the trophy at PGL Cluj-Napoca 2026, taking down BLAST Bounty 2026 Season 1 champions PARIVISION in a 3-0 (Overpass 13-10, Dust2 13-4, Inferno 16-13) affair.

While superstar Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut had been topping the charts for Vitality throughout PGL Cluj-Napoca, the grand final saw all five members of the international side's ranks make important contributions, with Israeli opener Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan in particular stepping up with a whopping 1.48 rating across the series.

This win on captain Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's 33rd birthday caps off yet another excellent tournament run from Vitality, notching their 11th tournament victory since adding Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool to their ranks in January 2025. Vitality faced no lightweights throughout their Cluj-Napoca campaign, besting G2, The MongolZ, and MOUZ to secure a flawless 3-0 group stage record. Vitality continued their success in playoffs, marching past Aurora and The MongolZ before pushing past PARIVISION in the grand final.

The series began on Vitality's first pick of Overpass, a map they had won nine times in a row ahead of this matchup, with their last loss on it being back in early November at IEM Chengdu. PARIVISION hit the ground running with a 4-0 streak off the back of a triple kill from captain Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali.

With guns finally in their hands and entry kills to their name, Vitality started racking up rounds midway through the half, responding to PARIVISION's streak with a seven-round streak of their own, before PARIVISION clinched the final round of the half to head into their T-side with a 5-7 deficit.

Vitality further extended their lead by snagging the second pistol round and subsequent conversion, but PARIVISION retorted with the following two rounds to put the CTs in dire economic straits. It looked as if PARIVISION were on track to close the gap, but a force buy victory from Vitality capped off with a Deagle 1v2 from ropz immediately crushed the Russian side's momentum.

Vitality marched on to map point, but a fumbled anti-eco 5v2 kept PARIVISION's hopes for the map alive. The Russians followed this up with two more rounds, but Vitality put an end to their comeback efforts with a 13-10 win.

Things did not get any easier for PARIVISION on their first pick of Dust2, a map that Vitality likewise enjoyed profound success on, with an eleven-map win streak spanning from November heading into the series. Masterful anchor work from William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman on the B bombsite, paired with strong individual showings on the CT-side from his remaining teammates, made the first half a breeze for Vitality, who headed into the offense with a 9-3 lead.

A stuck defuse from Ivan "⁠zweih⁠" Gogin gave PARIVISION a lifeline to start their CT-side, but Vitality quickly responded with the force-buy victory to cruise to a 13-4 map victory on their opponent's pick.

While Dust2 was just about as straightforward as could be, Vitality's second pick of Inferno was far from it. Vitality bagged an early lead with a pistol round victory on the offense, but PARIVISION battled back with a successful B stack with limited weaponry and a ninja defuse from Jame as the Ts anticipated a trademark save from the AWPer. A beautifully played 1v2 from zweih further built up PARIVISION's momentum, allowing them to emerge from the scrappy first half with a 7-5 lead.

Andrey "⁠BELCHONOKK⁠" Yasinskiy was the star of the show for PARIVISION in the second half, securing his team the T-side pistol with a quad kill, before helping his team reach double-digit rounds with an ace.

Though Vitality were on the ropes, they did not give in. A quad kill from flameZ to hold down the A site kickstarted an astounding comeback from Vitality, expanded upon by a 1v2 with a jaw-dropping spray transfer from ZywOo and a post-plant 1v2 from apEX. A 1v2 from Vladislav "⁠xiELO⁠" Lysov gave PARIVISION a second chance by forcing overtime, but Vitality were by this point thoroughly in control, marching ahead to a 16-13 win to lock down the series 3-0.
