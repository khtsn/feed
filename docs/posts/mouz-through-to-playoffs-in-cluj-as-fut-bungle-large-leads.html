MOUZ through to playoffs in Cluj as FUT bungle large leads

MOUZ are through to the PGL Cluj-Napoca playoffs with a 3-1 record after defeating FUT in two maps. Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin and company showed the composure that is expected of the bigger, more experienced team in a series that FUT choked after letting 7-2 and 12-3 leads get away on both maps.

First came FUT's comfort pick of Mirage, where they have a 9-0 record since the beginning of the year and have beaten the likes of Astralis, FURIA, and Aurora.

The map started with FUT taking a 2-7 lead, but worrying signs came when Džiugas "⁠dziugss⁠" Steponavičius and Dmytro "⁠dem0n⁠" Myroshnychenko, the team's two most promising prospects, were silent while Nikita "⁠cmtry⁠" Samolotov did most of the heavy lifting.

MOUZ won the final three rounds on the defense to make it 5-7 and then took control on the attack, winning the pistol round and giving up only the fourth round before going on an 8-1 tear to close the map out 13-8 in dominating fashion while handing FUT their first Mirage loss this year.

Overpass had a turbulent start as MOUZ won the first two rounds before FUT claimed a forcebuy. Two rounds later, MOUZ won a forcebuy of their own to regain the lead, 3-2, but that would be it for Brollan and his men on the attack as they were incapable of securing a single gun round in the half.

It looked like FUT put Mirage behind them in a huge way as they went on to take a 9-3 lead on the defense with Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea posting a fantastic 2.12 rating while Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás struggled mightily with no kills in the whole half.

FUT continued on a clear path to victory after winning a big second pistol round, which propelled them to a 12-3 lead, and it looked like nothing could stop the match from going to the Dust2 decider, but that's when FUT's lack of experience started to show.

The Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija-led squad hit a wall time and time again, unable to find a way to break through the defenses, and with every round MOUZ's confidence grew while FUT's shrank.

The former MOUZ academy players showed the composure of a big team, years removed from their time in the developmental stage, while the youngsters recently departed from NAVI Junior struggled in the high-pressure situations.

After successfully defending nine map points, MOUZ went on to cleanly defend all three rounds in the first half of overtime to take a commanding 15-12 lead. FUT found their footing once again on the attack, winning two in a row, but were finally taken down 16-14 in what will be a big learning moment for the young team.

This isn't the end of the road for FUT in Romania, however, and lauNX will still have a chance to qualify for the playoffs in hopes of playing in front of his home crowd. Their 2-2 record puts them through to the final day of group play in the do-or-die deciders.
