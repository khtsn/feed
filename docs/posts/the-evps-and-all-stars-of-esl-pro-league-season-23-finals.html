The EVPs and All-Stars of ESL Pro League Season 23 Finals

ESL Pro League, in the absence of Vitality, was a chance for the pretenders to stake a claim on the throne.

And it was fifth seed Natus Vincere who took the spoils after a standout performance from Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri.

The young Kosovar, who placed third in the Rookie of the Year race last year, ended the LAN portion of the event with a 1.38 rating, 1.13 kills per round win, and with 100% of maps above a 1.00 rating.

While his 1.25 rating in the online group stage was not considered for the MVP race or for this EVPs list, it is also worth mentioning to complete the picture of makazze's fantastic showing all event long.

It was the type of firepower Andrey "⁠B1ad3⁠" Gorodenskiy has called for from his team, and now makazze's task is replicating it.

Radar Chart stat glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • Swing in RW Δ: round swing in round wins

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov

w0nderful was the player of the match in the grand final, after ending it with a 1.56 rating and 82 frags over four maps.

Like for Emil "⁠nota⁠" Moskvitin at Bounty and Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan at Cluj, a brilliant final was not enough on its own for the MVP medal but it did push w0nderful right to the top of the EVP list.

A 1.70 on Mirage against FUT also helped his case, and w0nderful's collection of six playoff peaks ensured it was a live conversation when it came to the MVP.

Ultimately, w0nderful's lower floor and quiet quarter-final cost him in the head-to-head with makazze, but the Ukrainian's grand final is one that will be remembered for years to come as he sets his sights on returning to the Top 20 Players list.

Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın

Wicadia was Aurora's leading MVP candidate before the grand final with a 1.36 rating up to that point.

And, even with a poor final, these lists are all about pushing your team forward and that is exactly what Wicadia did in Stockholm by playing a key role in all four of his side's map wins, where he had a 1.56 rating.

He was also their best in round wins (1.05 KPRW, 99.1 ADRW) and was a superb fragger overall (0.78 KPR). Therefore, he did enough for an EVP.

Love "⁠phzy⁠" Smidebrant

Our final EVP is phzy, whose demolition of Spirit may have been termed "lucky" by a half-joking Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev but was a scintillating performance on home soil for the Swede regardless.

He finished the match with 41 frags and a 1.76 rating, dumping Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets and company out of playoffs with nothing.

In a short eight-team event like this, such a strong performance against one of the favorites put phzy close to EVP range already, and he confirmed it with another fragging bonanza and a 1.68 rating against FUT to secure third place for Astralis.

Having had a strong start to 2026 in new colors, phzy's long and winding career path via China and the States is finally paying off — and he has his first-ever EVP to show for it.

Honorable mentions

As the online portion of events will not count towards EVP or MVP races from 2026 onwards, there was a high bar for an EVP at the eight-team Finals.

Valeriy "⁠b1t⁠" Vakhovskiy's consistency was strong, ending on a 1.19 rating, but his 1.18 rating in map wins and 0.85 kills per round win were both fourth inside his team and below the usual mark for a nod.

Özgür "⁠woxic⁠" Eker had a better final than Wicadia, but had fewer peaks (only two maps above 1.30) in the run to the final.

Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan had a brilliant semi-final, but ended the event with a negative round swing after more than his fair share of frags against weaker weaponry.

Victor "⁠Staehr⁠" Staehr and Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea showed good moments, especially the Dane in the third-place decider, but neither stood out enough in the playoff bracket proper for awards.

All-Stars

Our cap of three Natus Vincere players led us to picking b1t in addition to w0nderful and makazze, because the Ukrainian was so clear of other anchors.

That meant no room for Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen, helping Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli — who was great individually and called Aurora to a 55.6% win-rate on T side — into our side.

Then, Wicadia completes the lineup as the closer after winning five clutches in Sweden.
