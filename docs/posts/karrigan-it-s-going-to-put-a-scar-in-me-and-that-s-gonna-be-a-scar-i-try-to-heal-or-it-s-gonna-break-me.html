karrigan: "It's going to put a scar in me, and that's gonna be a scar I try to heal, or it's gonna break me"

FaZe have endured the most difficult season of the organization's history in Counter-Strike, posting a torrid series record and more importantly failing to qualify for the Major for the first time ever.

Following their failure to qualify for IEM Cologne Major FaZe travelled to PGL Bucharest and immediately exited the tournament, having forfeited two games whilst they attended HLC Belgrade PRO in a last-gasp attempt to make the Major, before suffering an 0-2 defeat at the hands of Inner Circle.

"The past two or three weeks have been the most stressful thing I have ever been through," Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen told HLTV in the wake of his team's loss to Inner Circle, and he also gave us a brutally honest assessment of the team's season. "I think we played shit all season. I don't think it's something that is very surprising that suddenly there are cracks shown, I think there's been cracks showing the whole season since the Major run."

FaZe difficulties have not only left them absent from IEM Cologne Major, they have also taken their toll on the players according to karrigan. "What happened now is going to leave scars, scars for every player in the team, I know it's going to be tough for me at least and I know it's going to be tough for the guys."

karrigan says he is open to whatever is needed to see FaZe return to the top. "With these performances it's not good enough. So if I'm stepping down, let's do that. I just want FaZe back at the top, with or without me."

With the Major out of reach and FaZe in the midst of the toughest period in their history, karrigan spoke at length with HLTV about what has gone wrong for his team, the emotional toll of their recent struggles, and the future of both FaZe and the man himself.

How difficult was it to come and play this tournament after what happened in Belgrade and what happened with your Major campaign?

I think if you're ever a competitor, you know that you can always reset after a match, but it's no joke that we have high expectations and playing the Major is the bare minimum. Not making the Major… I said to the guys today 'I understand, I have nothing to say that can relieve this pain, I feel the pain.'

Playing this game was literally impossible, I think we showed up as professionals but we did not show up as a team. There's a huge crack in the team, in everyone's confidence and the confidence in each other, with what we've been through. Probably the hardest match I have ever played in my career, I think.

Probably the hardest match I have ever played in my career, I think

How much of FaZe's struggles, especially in these last couple of games, come down to confidence versus things in the server, things in game?

I think we played shit all season. I don't think it's something that is very surprising that suddenly there are cracks shown, I think there's been cracks showing the whole season since the Major run. Even before the Major with this lineup we had problems. We managed to fix them at the Major, hit a good level, coming into the season we have been working our ass off trying to figure out solutions for different things.

I don't think it comes down to that much pressure now, because in one way we didn't have pressure in the beginning of the season, in the end we just didn't manage to play. Obviously I feel bad about the whole situation, starting 0-2 here, flying here just to get 0-3. What happened now is going to leave scars, scars for every player in the team, I know it's going to be tough for me at least and I know it's going to be tough for the guys. The past two or three weeks have been the most stressful thing I have ever been through.

The past two or three weeks have been the most stressful thing I have ever been through

Playing these VRS LANs, do you think a large portion of your struggles to perform against some of these teams we would expect to beat came down to stress?

No. I think if I've seen how good we can play in officials and then we fail in the RMR or what in the past, then yeah I would say so, but we have been playing bad all season. We haven't won against any top teams, we had like what, two LAN wins, until we played these smaller events, and that's not good enough. Obviously we were never in a position to beat a top five team, we were close sometimes to beating some teams, but in the end a loss is a loss.

We are sitting here missing out on the Major, going 0-3, and there's not much to say honestly, I think the results speak for themselves. I can not say anything that is going to tell more about the situation.

With these team performances individuals are going to have confidence cracks, it's spreading, it's a virus everywhere

Considering that run in Budapest, all the way to the final, and then the way you've played this season, what happened? What went wrong in between the Budapest final and then this season starting?

I mean, the matter of the fact is that we were almost going out of Stage 1 at the Budapest Major. Then we found a comfort, we found a confidence, and I think what we had was confidence in each other, believing what we're doing, the team was now reacting a certain way. We did that really good at the Major in Stage 2, Stage 3 and playoffs. None of this has been seen this whole season. Glimpses of it, but mostly we have seen what we performed in Stage 1 is basically around the same level I would say, the last few months, and that's nowhere near good enough.

With these team performances individuals are going to have confidence cracks, it's spreading, it's a virus everywhere. We are changing small details, maybe changing approach, we have not just sat back and waited for someone to do something, we have tried all season. I think that that's where I've been every week; every Monday after a tournament trying to rewatch everything and figure out a plan together with the team. I just think that the level we have shown as a team and as individuals, besides frozen, this year has been subpar and nowhere near where minimum expectations for the players are.

karrigan and his men agonizingly missed out on Major qualification at the death with a grand final loss at HLC Belgrade PRO

When you did have that run in Budapest and sat down afterwards once the dust had settled, did you think that you were on an upward trajectory, or that it was FaZe magic creating a one-off? What were your reflections when Budapest was over?

In the moment you are seeing the positives more than you are seeing the negatives of that tournament, right? One thing I remember after Budapest is that I wanted to play more. Finally we came to a final, it's been a long time since we've been in a final as a team. Then after vacation we started with losing to EYEBALLERS online, we are not ready when the season comes up.

When you're in the moment you think 'okay we've figured something out, we can see the players being more comfortable in the roles, they are striving under the pressure of the Major and playing good.' I could not anticipate this kind of dropoff or fallout. Of course there was some magic happening, or doing something that makes us play better during Budapest, but as a leader and I guess as an org as well, you don't know if that was the peak. Is there more potential, was that a one-off? We don't know, because in theory we only played three or four events with the lineup before Budapest, and that's what we saw. In the end we have seen this season where we are, and it's undeniably bad.

As a leader and I guess as an org as well, you don't know if that was the peak. Is there more potential, was that a one-off?

Where do FaZe go from here?

I think that's a question you need to have with the FaZe organization. I'm not gonna sit here knowing what's going to happen, I actually have no idea. Missing the Major is incredibly bad, NEO got benched a few weeks ago. Let's see what happens, we have two tournaments coming up that are really important to secure some points, because we're gonna fall very far down the ranking, even further than we are now, which is… yeah, unbelievable.

Speaking about NEO, who like you say was removed a few weeks ago, did the timing of that hurt you? Because it seemed like a bizarre time to make such a significant change.

I think that's very hard to answer. I think our results were not good this year, and they were not better after. Of course having a main coach that can say the right things and see some other details, but we tried everything within the team, with and without a coach. So I think it was a signal to the team that there are a few months left for people to step up, and we tried.

I think it was a signal to the team that there are a few months left for people to step up, and we tried

The most frustrating thing is how good we do in practice and how bad we do in officials. I have never been in a team like this, all my past teams have been bad in practice and good in officials, and here it's the opposite way, and that makes me really frustrated. Every tournament I come into, I come fairly confident, but then it just all falls apart.

There's obviously going to be a lot of speculation around FaZe, but also around you personally. Where you at in terms of thinking about your future?

I think it's the first time where I have to consider where I am, in myself, in my life. Playing this bad with these results is really rough, so I don't know what's gonna happen. Gonna play the season out, but it's one that's going to put a scar in me, and that's gonna be a scar I try to heal, or it's gonna break me. It's no joke that my mental of this season has been bad, even though I try to be a strong leader. These results are by far [below] what I accept as a leader of this team, as a player who's been in FaZe for a long time, so I understand.

If I'm stepping down, let's do that. I just want FaZe back at the top, with or without me

I have also told FaZe that if I need to step down, I'm ready to do that, they should not… there is something wrong right now. It can be the vision, it can be the players, it can be everything, because with these performances it's not good enough. So if I'm stepping down, let's do that. I just want FaZe back at the top, with or without me.

Lastly, I wanted to give you a chance: is there anything you want to say, anything you want to get off your chest?

I don't really think much can be said. Social media hasn't been fun lately, and I know a lot of hate is going to come our way again. I hope some of my players won't get it, also me included of course, but in the end we are humans as well, and you can feel the last few days that we are not being treated like humans. So yeah, rough times.
