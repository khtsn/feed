flameZ: "We want no debate, where people will clearly say that the Vitality era was the better one. [Today] is another opportunity for that"

Just one best-of-five victory in the grand final of IEM Rio against Spirit separates Vitality from clinching their second ESL Grand Slam, but the team is refusing to get ahead of itself even amidst a near-unparalleled level of dominance.

"When we play the game, we just focus map by map, and we don't get into those kind of things," Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan told HLTV after Vitality beat hometown favorites FURIA in the semi-finals. "It's kind of useless, maybe it stacks a bit of pressure, but we like that pressure, we handle it well. The main thing for us is that we're consistent, and that's the main goal."

The Israeli rifler touched on beating the Brazilian team on stage and how Vitality were prepared for FURIA to pick Ancient, revealing that his team has been practicing it regularly since the start of the year to get a leg up on Aurora and FURIA, specifically.

"We knew that we needed to have Ancient at the back of our minds because those two teams might be in the top four," flameZ said. "So we started practicing Ancient as much as we could and kind of developing our game on it."

The 22-year-old also spoke about his personal development and how it helped him recover from a 0-11 start on Overpass, as well as Vitality's mindset headed into the title decider on Sunday.

"[It] is a great opportunity for us, we'll come and give a lot of energy," he said. "It's just about ourselves, if we don't lose to ourselves, it's going to go well for us. [...] If Spirit play like they're the best team in the world, I think they'll still have a fight from us because we're going to be there."

Listen to the interview here.

Congratulations, you're in the final. Fun playing against the crowd?

Yeah, when we walked on stage we felt how much energy the crowd came with today, and we knew that they were going to... not like us that much (smiles). But I think we were all passionate to silence the crowd to some extent- not silence them, it was actually that we wanted to handle that crowd, and we did it amazingly well. We were a really good team today.

I didn't expect it to be that loud, even though it's Brazil and famous for being that loud, but I didn't expect the crowd to be that passionate and that loud. But I'm happy we played good Counter-Strike, and I think for them, it might have been tough with the pressure because if they don't win, the crowd is silent, and I think it's very tough... not to play like that, but it's a bit weird, I guess.

When that halftime kicked off, I was thinking about IEM Cologne, but I knew that the same wasn't going to happen here. I developed so much in my personality and how I handle these kinds of things

You started out the series 0-11 on the T side. I'm not sure if you know this Chinese meme, about you being the Chief Engineer of R&D at Intel-

Yeah, I know that one (laughs).

-but you finally developed the processor at the start of the CT side. Tell me about your rough start and getting back into the map.

To be honest, at the beginning of the T side, I didn't feel like there were many opportunities. I had maybe a kill here and there, but I played different and pretty good, I would say. So I wasn't... if I compare it to IEM Cologne [Editor's note: where the Intel R&D meme came from], I think I was more aware, I had more things in mind for that game, and I really didn't care that I'm not killing [anyone] and we kept on grinding those rounds.

We had some sick rounds, some important individual kills, and for me that's what matters. Once we had those few rounds, I knew that once I went to CT, I'll have a lot of opportunities and I just need to get that one kill and I'm back in business. But yeah, I'll be honest, when that halftime kicked off, I was thinking about IEM Cologne, but I knew that the same wasn't going to happen here. I knew that I developed so much in my personality and how I handle these kinds of things that it's all good. It's just another challenge that we succeeded in.

They picked Ancient, and apEX said that it wasn't something that you didn't expect. What can you tell me about the preparation now that it's over?

At the beginning of the year, we saw Aurora stepping up a lot with Anubis, and FURIA were really good in the second season of last year, so we knew that we needed to have Ancient at the back of our minds because those two teams might be in the top four. So we started practicing Ancient as much as we could and kind of developing our game on it.

I don't know if they prepped or they didn't prep, they would have to prep if they picked Ancient I would assume, but we were kinda ready. We had some games on it, we practiced, everybody knows utility — not like last time that we failed a lot of utility, it was sloppy. Obviously we're not going to play it against Spirit now and stuff like that, but we're very confident against these kind of teams because we know it's going good in practice.

We don't need to be insane on that map, we just need to have the basics under control, and that's basically it. So we did prep it from the beginning of the year, and especially after Krakow, because we just knew that those two teams are gaining a lot of form so we needed to handle that.

Even though we had a [24]-map winstreak, we didn't really feel like we were the best by far. We know that we're the best, but still, every map you [play], it's a new challenge

Last year was historic for you, this year has still been historic. You had another match winstreak, you had a map winstreak, they both got broken here. But you did set the longest streak of top-four finishes at Big Events, it's at 20 now. How did feel to do that yesterday?

I don't really care. After you get it, you're kinda happy with it because it's cool that you got to this point-

Just another record in the books, yeah (laughs).

(laughs) Yeah, obviously we are not a team that is like, 'Astralis era is [up] there,' we want to beat them, we want to keep it under the no debate question where people will clearly say that the Vitality era was the better one. Tomorrow is another opportunity for that, to get another argument into that, but those records are super cool, afterwards.

When we play the game, we just focus map by map, and we don't get into those kind of things. It's kind of useless, maybe it stacks a bit of pressure, but we like that pressure, we handle it well. The main thing for us is that we're consistent, and that's the main goal. That's what we're working for, and once you're trying to show up, every day you're showing up and showing up and doing the best as you can — because some days you're gonna play against G2 and almost lose three times, and Falcons when we lost to them — but trying to do your best, and then those trophies, those records, they come by themselves.

That's the main goal in Vitality, that we just want to do the best. We just want to come to every map the best we can, and then we'll see afterwards what we can improve on. Even though we had a [24]-map winstreak, we didn't really feel like we were the best by far, you know what I mean? Like we still had some trouble in some maps. We know that we're the best, but still, every map you [play], it's a new challenge.

Well speaking of that, and wanting to get the trophies, get the titles, you have a chance for two tomorrow. Not just the tournament, but also the Grand Slam. So how do you feel going into that?

To be honest, tomorrow is an insane opportunity. We'll have pressure for sure. It's the Grand Slam, I hope the crowd will be there cheering because we've seen sometimes, when there is no Brazilian team, there are not that many people in the crowd, so I would hope the crowd will be half full, full.

But tomorrow is a great opportunity for us, we'll come and give a lot of energy. It's just about ourselves, if we don't lose to ourselves, it's going to go well for us. We'll have a lot of opportunities at least; if Spirit come tomorrow and they play like they're the best team in the world, I think they'll still have a fight from us because we're going to be there.

How do you think Spirit are looking?

Individually, tN1R is doing good, maybe donk hasn't been the most dominant this tournament on all the maps, but they're all getting together, aligning more, and they're sorting themselves out. Obviously, tomorrow, they're facing a team with good individual skill as well. They did beat Falcons today, but again, they're not with their coach, maybe they don't have as much pressure, but it still doesn't matter. We don't care what team we'll play against, we just want that Grand Slam.
