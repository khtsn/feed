luchov: "Yesterday I decided to come back to my old mouse, and it gives me more power"

9z have carried on their good form in Cologne with a repeat of their PGL Astana victory over PARIVISION, led by a 2.00-rated series by Luciano "⁠luchov⁠" Herrera.

The 25-year-old had dominated PARIVISION in Astana and did so again here, having his career-best map (2.83 rating) on Overpass after returning to his old mouse.

"I was using my mouse, Superlight 2, a long time ago, and in Astana I also used that," he told HLTV after the game. "But then I decided to change to Superstrike, and it isn't very different, but I think it [makes] me less confident to shoot and take decisions, so yesterday I decided to come back to the old one. It's my mouse from a long time ago, and it gives me more power (laughs)."

luchov also admitted to feeling nervous and anxious amidst 9z's step up at bigger events, but says that all fades away when matches kick off.

"When the tournaments were getting close, I was nervous and anxious, and also when I was sitting at my peripherals," he said. "But when it starts, I think I just forgot all those emotions and focused on the game. Sometimes I can control it, like it is difficult, but [yeah]."

Listen to the interview here.

Congratulations, a win over PARIVISION to start off Stage 3. Your career-best map as well, how did that feel? STYKO mentioned you switched your mouse, so talk to me about that.

Yes (smiles). I was using my mouse, Superlight 2, a long time ago, and in Astana I also used that. But then I decided to change to Superstrike, and it isn't very different, but I think it [makes] me less confident to shoot and take decisions, so yesterday I decided to come back to the old one. It's my mouse from a long time ago, and it gives me more power (laughs).

Especially against PARIVISION, I guess?

(Laughs) Yes, I think so because in Astana I also played really good against them. I think it's because of the way they play, it fits more comfortably for me.

Talk to me about your form a bit. Even in Astana you were playing really well, here you're playing well, what have you been working on to get yourself to this level when playing against these kinds of teams?

I don't know exactly. I watch a lot of demos of all the teams, so that also helps me to see new things, and also when I'm playing against them to sometimes know what they are doing. That's it. I play a lot of deathmatch also, but that's all (laughs).

When you're not regularly competing at the best events, how much has watching those demos helped you? How much have you been able to study and put into your own game, and are there any players you watch to pull from?

I really like NiKo, and now I watch makazze on Ancient T side, he's really good. But I don't know, I don't especially have players I [watch], I watch the teams and see what they are doing, and all players have good ideas, so I steal from them. Also, I use things from my own.

Back in Astana, BIT was telling me that because you don't go to these big events all the time, it was a good chance to see where 9z is at and see what your level is. Now that you've played in Astana and here a bit, where do you think your level is at?

When we are in a good mood in terms of communication and energy, I think we can beat every team. I'm not including Spirit and Vitality right now; I think they are a bit more difficult and we need to play against them a lot of times to improve.

For you, playing this well at the biggest events, how have you adjusted so easily? Do you feel any nerves at all?

Yes, of course. When the tournaments were getting close, I was nervous and anxious, and also when I was sitting at my peripherals. But when it starts, I think I just forgot all those emotions and focused on the game. Sometimes I can control it, like it is difficult, but [yeah].

max listens a lot, and the way he treats us, he almost never gets tilted

max gave BIT a lot of credit for how much he's been working with him, so can you tell me how it's been having him as an in-game leader and how BIT is working in the system?

BIT is a really good coach, he works a lot, he wakes up very early and he does our game plan, and also studies the other teams. max is a really good captain because he [listens] a lot, he's open to hearing what we want to do in some rounds. Sometimes he says, 'No, I prefer doing some other thing,' and I think that the way he also treats us, he almost never gets tilted, so I think he's a good captain.

Making it into Stage 3 was already the best result for a Hispanic-American team at a Major. You're now 1-0 in this stage too, so how is 9z approaching the rest of the Major?

We know it's very long, and now we are happy because we won, but we know that maybe one team can win the first match and then go out 1-3. So we are going match by match, focusing on the next, and if we win tomorrow or lose it will be the same because we have to win three [games to advance]. That's the way we are taking it.

No real pressure then, in terms of that?

No, I think not.
