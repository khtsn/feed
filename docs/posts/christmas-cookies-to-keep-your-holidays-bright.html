Christmas Cookies to Keep Your Holidays Bright

A great cookie platter should excite and inspire, from first electric sight to last delicious bite. Every December, New York Times Cooking unveils showstopping cookies and videos for our annual Cookie Week. This year’s class is especially dazzling, with chewy gingerbread blondies, buttery lemon swoops and spicy hot chocolate crinkles, to name a few. Watch the videos below (or on YouTube), save the recipes on NYT Cooking, then make one — or make them all. VAUGHN VREELAND

Samantha Seneviratne achieves these easier-than-they-look delights without any piping. Instead, she pours icing the colors of a Santa Fe sunset over orange spice cookies, but you can use whatever hues you like: dreamy blues and greens, elegant grays and whites, or festive pastels. Each cookie will be uniquely itself — and uniquely you.

Chewy brownies meet gingerbread cookies in these festive bars from Melissa Clark. Brown butter deepens their caramelized flavor and enhances their notes of sugar and spice. Make a double batch for sharing: They travel well, which is maybe the best gift of all.

These delicate but sturdy piped shortbreads from Yewande Komolafe melt in your mouth thanks to a combination of butter, egg yolk and cornstarch. A little lemon juice and zest keep them balanced — sweet but not too sweet.

A chocolate, strawberry and vanilla pound cake was one of Sue Li’s favorite childhood treats. Here, it grows up. The three doughs come together simply in a food processor, then are cut and stacked into a checkerboard pattern for cookies that both the young and young at heart can love.

Gooey marshmallows give these cookies with a kick the most satisfying pull. Vaughn Vreeland smartly freezes the confections to keep them from melting into the dough, which bakes up wonderfully fudgy and pillowy soft.

Nostalgia-packed and ready to channel the ’90s, these one-bowl beauties from Sohla El-Waylly’s cookbook, “Start Here: Instructions for Becoming a Better Cook,” recall the kind of store-bought cookies you may have tucked into your lunchbox. But they’re even better because they’re homemade.

A pompadour of old-fashioned ermine frosting tops these chewy Grinch-green cookies from Eric Kim. But they’d look just as polished with a dusting of powdered sugar.
