huNter-: "I'm really happy I skipped Rio because I saw what my teammates are capable of"

G2 got off to a flying start at BLAST Rivals at the Dickies Arena in Fort Worth, beating Astralis 2-0 with strong showings on Dust2 and Overpass. The series marked Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač's return after sustaining a leg injury that kept him away from competition at IEM Rio, where G2 played with academy youngster Vilius "⁠tAk⁠" Keserauskas.

G2 outclass Astralis in BLAST Rivals

"I saw what my teammates are capable of," the captain said of their time in Brazil, "I could see what I could expect even more for the future when everybody stepped up without waiting for my calls."

huNter- also spoke about the mood following Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz's addition to the team, comparing it to another phase in G2's history. "It's kind of like the mood when NiKo came to G2," he says, " how I was feeling and how he was feeling when he joined, and I'm really happy to see that."

Listen to the interview here.

Big victory against Astralis. First of all, welcome back. You were out for an event. How are you doing? Recovered? Still limping around a bit?

Thank you, first of all. I'm not 100 percent recovered. I still have a problem with my leg, but I'm trying to rest and to put it up at every moment that I can, even here in timeouts or during half-times, but yeah, I still have this swelling that's going down, and it's not easy, but it will be good.

I just want to be with them, I want to play with them, because the mood is good, everything feels good

Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač on coming back after sitting out IEM Rio

Every time I see NertZ he seems so hyped and happy to be here, it seems like the vibes are really good on the team. After sitting out one event and coming back, what did you see? How is everybody? How's the mood?

To be honest, everything is good. I'm really happy that I skipped Rio because I saw what my teammates are capable of. I was looking closely, and I could see what I could expect even more for the future when everybody stepped up without waiting for my calls. That's the main thing I understood by being home and not being with them in Rio.

Coming back, I wanted to come back so bad, even though my leg is not fully recovered. I just want to be with them, I want to play with them, because the mood is good, everything feels good, and yeah, NertZ is happy coming from Liquid to G2, especially because he has one of his best friends, HeavyGod, sitting next to him, of course.

So yeah, it's kind of like the mood when NiKo came to G2, how I was feeling and how he was feeling when he joined, and I'm really happy to see that. I just hope we'll continue with the good mood.

Tell me a bit about this Astralis match. A really good first map, and then Overpass — I heard some of the analysts saying it could be an Astralis map —, but you guys had a really good start, and they did make a bit of a comeback in the second half, but you managed to close it out. Run me through the series a bit. What you expected, how it went, and especially getting that big win.

Yeah, I think Astralis is a good team, and they showed it lately with their results overall. As you mentioned, on Overpass [we had] a good start, and on Dust2 we had a good start with a 6-0, and on MR12, if you have a good start and if you're playing well at the beginning of the game, it's not easy to come back when you're on the opposing side.

So yeah, because we started good, that helped us with confidence and the mood and with having fun overall. For me, and for us, it will be important that if we start badly, especially tomorrow, that we just need to bounce back and stay focused and keep having fun, no matter what is happening, because we can't fix all of the mistakes during the game.

We know that mistakes are for after the game. So yeah, I'm really happy with the way we played against a really good team, as Astralis is right now, and I just hope for more games and that it will continue this way.

I think he's playing better, and I'm really happy for him and hope he will continue that way

Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač on SunPayus making improvements

You talk about how the team was reacting and communicating while you were away. This is something I spoke about with SunPayus in the past and he said maybe you can have more freedom and rely more on mid-rounds and players making calls and helping you out. Tell me about that evolution, how is that going?

Yeah, as I said, I was watching them practice with tAk before they left for Rio, and I was amazed by the way everybody stepped up, and [how] everybody was giving ideas, making calls, just from default they were winning a lot of rounds, as well.

I was watching the games with the voice comms after they came from Rio, I just wanted to learn and see what was good and what we can do better, but yeah, I was amazed with the way everybody was stepping up at every moment. They were never giving up and always giving ideas on all sides. Really important stuff.

I think that's how Vitality is playing, overall, with some set things but mostly defaults with people reacting, people giving ideas together with apEX, and that's why they're kind of the best. People have freedom, and if they know how to use that freedom, it will make my life easier, as well, so yeah, I just hope to see that more.

Some games it will be bad, I know it, and I just need to accept it, but we should just keep going that way, not change too much, and yeah, so far it's been good, and that's one of our goals for the future, to try and play that way.

We definitely have potential, we all know it, and when we see the way we are playing and shooting, [...] it looks good

Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač

Speaking of SunPayus, whom I brought up, he's been struggling a bit for a while and is trying to find his best form. He had a good game today, although everyone played well today, so it was one of those games, but do you think he's figuring himself out and working in the system, and have you seen improvement there?

Yeah, definitely. Since he joined the team, people are talking a lot of bad things about him, but I was always supporting him because he was always helping me a lot, giving a lot of ideas, and he's the guy who is working very hard so that the team is playing better.

He likes to be in this good mood, good zone, and if he wants to be in a good zone, he has to share his things, and I appreciate that he's sharing everything he wants. I think this is how they played in Rio, and how we're trying to play here. I think it's helping him a lot because if he can do things mid-round individually, and if he can give some ideas to me in the mid-round, not only in freeze time — especially on T side because on CT side I don't see any issues —, I think it will be better.

I think he's playing better, and I'm really happy for him and hope he will continue that way. I don't want to talk too many good things, but yeah, I'm really happy with the way he's playing and hope he will continue. I don't want to jinx it.

It seems like you're gelling and progressing, and watching G2 really feels like there's potential. Do you feel the same way? That there's plenty of potential to tap into? Where do you think that can lead?

We definitely have potential, and we had potential before, when malbs was on the team, but our biggest problem was consistency. The consistency problem before, of course, we didn't have too much experience playing together, and I didn't have that much experience being an IGL — I stil don't, I just need a bit more events, more playoff games against good teams, that will help me —, but with the way we're trying to play now, that's where we'll find consistency if we just keep working properly.

We definitely have potential, we all know it, and when we see the way we are playing and shooting, even in practice, and what we're doing individually, it looks good. It's just about us having that consistency, and that's our goal.

Where do you see G2? If we put Vitality as the team to beat, and then title contenders, and teams that sometimes make playoffs... Where do you see G2 in the top-tier ladder?

You mentioned it good, sometimes making playoffs, that's what we were the last couple of months, but our goal is to play playoffs at every event, to be honest. Until the end of the season, and the next season, as well, that should be our goal. We're playing for G2, one of the biggest orgs, and I've been here for almost seven years, so our goal must be to play the playoffs and fight for trophies.
