Legacy sweep NAVI to earn EPL playoffs berth

Legacy advanced to the playoffs of ESL Pro League Season 23 after beating Natus Vincere in a two-map series in the 2-1 pool.

Natus Vincere are down to their last life in Stage 2 after wins over Monte and B8 and losses to G2 and Legacy, and will take on either paiN or 3DMAX depending on the outcome of the final round four games.

The victory is a mark of success for the Brazilian lineup in its debut event with Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan, and a return to form after the team struggled to find its footing in 2026 after winning CS Asia Championships last year.

"We are feeling good because we have been practicing a lot, to be honest, and it has been a tough month for everybody," Eduardo "⁠dumau⁠" Wolkmer said in a post-match interview on broadcast. "Everybody has been really insulated, waking up earlier, having really hard talks about like, 'You don't have to do this.' [...] We just started talking to agree and be on the same page, and these talks just made us be more comfortable in the game and playing better in situations."

The Brazilian rifler also heaped praise on his new in-game leader, saying he took his time to understand each player and that he has instilled belief within the team. "arT understood what is strong in each individual in our team, and by this position, where he understands which player... even in our first practice, he didn't do any strats or things because understanding each other is the most important," dumau said.

"He just gave us space to be ourselves, and then we see what kind of person every individual is. Then he could understand better what positions and situations are better for each person, and I think he's doing a great job. He has a good group mentality, he united us to be one, and everybody just believes in what he is saying and what he does. He's really trying to win."

Legacy had little trouble carving through Natus Vincere's defenses when the series got underway, a pithy start by Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan, Valeriy "⁠b1t⁠" Vakhovskiy, and Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen seeing them muster just one kill apiece during an unanswered eight-round run by the Brazilian side. The streak was finally broken near the end of the half, but the three rounds NAVI salvaged by the break did little to ease the sting of a 4-13 defeat.

Legacy picked up where they left off to take a 5-1 lead on Mirage, with Natus Vincere's sole round coming by way of a iM Deagle quad-kill. Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri and iM started to find a few openers to help close that gap, but Legacy retained a slim lead at halftime and extended it to 9-5 after winning the second pistol and conversion.

arT's attempts at misdirection caused some chaos in the early gun rounds, but ultimately cost Legacy their lead as Natus Vincere didn't fall for his tricks. The comeback seemed to be on after Aleksib denied Guilherme "⁠saadzin⁠" Pacheco a 1v2 clutch, but Legacy converted a 4v5 with only Deagles in hand just two rounds later and didn't look back, sweeping the last three for a 13-10 victory.
