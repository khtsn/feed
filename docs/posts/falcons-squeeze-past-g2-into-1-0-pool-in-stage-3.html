Falcons squeeze past G2 into 1-0 pool in Stage 3

Falcons got their IEM Cologne Major campaign off to a winning start after a 2-1 victory against G2 in a highly entertaining series filled with highlight-worthy plays.

They had to come back from a map down after losing Inferno, where Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač's 2.27 rating — the second-highest of his career at Majors — was not enough to carry an uninspired Falcons team to victory.

Falcons fought back on Dust2, where they were ahead from the get-go, before completing the turnaround on Ancient after surviving a series point.

"I'm so hungry," Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov said after the match. "I'm so hungry to play every game here at the Major. From the first [day] when we arrived here, you can see in everyone's eyes that everyone wants to play this game."

Inferno quickly became a back-and-forth affair that had fans and casters invested, with the teams taking turns winning rounds. Falcons managed to carve out a narrow 7-5 lead after a late string of rounds, but it would have been a much different story if not for a carry performance from NiKo, who posted a +20.32% round swing after some impressive rounds, including a key 1v2 clutch.

Falcons' issues continued into the second half as they again won the pistol round, only to lose the conversion. NiKo came up huge on the B site with a heroic 1v4 clutch, injecting some life into his team, but it was overall a lackluster T showing from the team, whose A hits were simply fruitless as G2 calmly finished the game off.

Falcons shook off the loss and produced a much more efficient offensive showing — despite again losing the second-round conversion — as they strolled to a 10-2 lead, led by m0NESY, who had four multi-kills and a +14.18% round swing.

G2 fought back in the second half and even put Falcons against the ropes, but then René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen came to his team's rescue with some great multi-kills on the B site before m0NESY put the game to rest with a 1v1 clutch.

G2 jumped out to a 4-0 lead on Ancient, capitalizing on a nearly pistol ace by Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García, but the rest of the half was controlled by Falcons, who had in Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen and Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin their top performers as they combined for 20 frags, including seven opening duels.

The game became a scrappy affair in the second half as nerves set in. G2 were the first to hit 12 rounds but could not deal the finishing blow as Falcons took the game to overtime, where they took the game by the scruff of the neck.
