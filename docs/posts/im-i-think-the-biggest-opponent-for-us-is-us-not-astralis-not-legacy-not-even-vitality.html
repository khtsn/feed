iM: "I think the biggest opponent for us is us, not Astralis, not Legacy, not even Vitality"

Natus Vincere enter IEM Atlanta in the shadow of Vitality, with the latter side looking for their sixth tournament win in a row in their final outing before the start of the IEM Cologne Major.

Natus Vincere, who have failed to score a map win against Vitality across the eight maps they have played this year, have now set themselves up nicely in Atlanta, notching a straightforward win over Passion UA and a hard-fought victory over GamerLegion, while their rival Vitality fell to the lower bracket after a loss to BetBoom.

Following his team's 2-1 battle with GamerLegion, Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan caught up with HLTV regarding the close nature of the series, the team's troubles with Vitality, and whether or not he believes the team have a shot at the trophy in the Peach State.

Or you can listen to the interview here.

2-1 win over GamerLegion, hard-fought win. You're in the playoffs, how are you feeling?

It feels good to win, but it was a very close game, it could have gone both ways, to be honest. We got smacked on Ancient, and we kinda fucked them, not as hard as they did to us, on Inferno, and it was a very very close Mirage afterwards. It feels good, but it felt a bit weird in a way.

What do you think went wrong in that Ancient game? You played them on Ancient in Rivals, and you destroyed them there. What was the issue here?

The only difference was that they chose to start T-side, on our own pick. I don't know. From what I remember, we won the first buy round, we lost the pistol, and after we misplayed, overpeeking in some moments, everything fell apart. Then, in mid-round situations, it was not how we used to play. That's why the score showed this, because we are peeking one by one, and we are dying.

You just need to be on point; you cannot afford any small mistakes, any overpeeks, and so on, against any team nowadays

Do you think you were having those same troubles on Mirage? Like you said, that map was not an easy win.

Maybe. But that Mirage maybe was a bit stressful because of the score, because we got smashed, they are playing very fast-paced, hitting A, faking B, and then hitting A, or rushing Middle or whatever. Sometimes we got caught off-guard, but we won at the end of the day, so I'm happy for that (laughs).

You guys are the clear #2 this event behind Vitality, but they just got upset by BetBoom and haven't practiced much beforehand. Do you feel like you guys have a good opportunity to get the trophy from underneath them?

We are not thinking about this. I can also say again, who doesn't beat Vitality doesn't deserve the trophy. There's also a huge chance that they'll be on our bracket; if we win the next game, we might meet them in the semi-final, and still we have to play them, and we cannot avoid them. Even, like you said, BetBoom upset them, GamerLegion almost smashed us because of that Ancient.

We see more and more upsets, which is understandable, because of the antistrat you can do from Skybox and so on. You just need to be on point; you cannot afford any small mistakes, any overpeeks, and so on, against any team nowadays.

[Vitality] showed weakness, because they lost against BetBoom, and we for sure need to exploit that if we want to win

What do you think the big issue is for you guys in that Vitality matchup specifically?

I kinda want to say mental-wise, but it's not mental-wise. I don't know, it's something weird. I feel like it's kinda the same way, even though they are a better team than us today, it kinda feels us against G2 whenever we had that streak. We see teams taking some maps at least from them, but we cannot. We had Nuke 11-1, and still we cannot win a map, which is crazy, and I'm thinking, maybe it's like a G2/NAVI back in the days in '24.

But, I don't know. I think at one point we will win a map against them (laughs). Hopefully, if we play them this tournament, it's going to be this one. I think they showed weakness, because they lost against BetBoom, and we for sure need to exploit that if we want to win.

You're gonna play the winner of Astralis vs. Legacy for a spot in the semi-finals, any preference between the two?

No; I think, even you saw today, we almost lost against GamerLegion when we beat them comfortably last time. I think the biggest opponent for us is us, not Astralis, not Legacy, not even Vitality, I would say.
