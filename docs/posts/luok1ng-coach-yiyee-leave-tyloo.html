LuoK1ng, coach Yiyee leave TYLOO

TYLOO TYLOO China Rank #11 Flex1n Lo Rui (罗瑞) slowly Kelun Sun (孙克伦) Ninebody Ding Yi (丁毅) ICEKING Cheng Wanpeng (程万鹏) AAK Shi Yekai (施叶凯) hfmi0dzjc9z7 Zhang Juncheng (张峻程) Eren Yang Zhengwei (杨曾威) have parted ways with Zhang "LuoK1ng" Zhan Peng (张展鹏) and coach Zhang "Yiyee" Tianyuan (张天元). Substitute player Zhu "mikiya" Jianan (朱佳楠) has also left the team. The team remains with a full roster should they promote substitute Kelun "slowly" Sun (孙克伦), but have no additional coaching staff.

Yiyee joined the team as coach at the beginning of 2023, in preparation for China's broader integration into the international VCT circuit. The team failed to perform, however, and after a number of roster changes ultimately decided to undergo a rebuild.

LuoK1ng joined after TYLOO were unable to find success in the FGC Invitational. The new roster was able to win their inaugural event, qualifying to the China Preliminaries. There, they placed in joint-first, guaranteeing a spot in the VCT China Qualifier, which would determine the teams attending Champions 2023. LuoK1ng was one of the better performers in the Preliminaries, achieving a 1.18 rating as the controller player.

TYLOO just missed out on their first international appearance, with losses to EDward Gaming and FunPlus Phoenix eliminating them from the event. Following that result, the team trended downward. They only reached the top eight in the first two Acts of the China Evolution series, and failed to qualify to Act 3.

In 2024 they were invited to VCT China as one of ten partnered teams. The team made their debut in Kickoff, but ended in last place after a loss to Trace and surprise 2-0 from Nova Esports. TYLOO will next play in Stage 1 of VCT China against Bilibili Gaming.
