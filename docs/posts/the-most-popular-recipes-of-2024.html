Margaux Laskey

Dec. 9, 2024, 5:01 a.m. ET

Well, that was fast: It was January, we blinked and now it’s December. Quite a bit has happened in the last 300 or so days, and the team at New York Times Cooking created, tested and published about 1,000 delicious recipes to help you eat well through it all. Your favorites — the dishes you added to your rotation, the meals your kids actually (OK, mostly) ate, the dinners you were excited to cook — are below. Once again, the three Cs (comfort, convenience and chicken) were king. You can view the complete list of our 50 most popular recipes here. Find one you haven’t cooked yet, and try it tonight. Happy cooking and Happy New Year.

25. Roasted Cauliflower and Garlic Soup

“This soup was absolutely positively delicious. I’m really not sure how that’s possible with two basic ingredients plus olive oil, salt and pepper and water! Insane! So oh, oh, good!”

Ali Slagle’s practically magical three-ingredient soup combines roasted cauliflower, a whole head of garlic and olive oil for a surprisingly creamy, complex vegan soup.

View the recipe.

24. Kung Pao Tofu

“Vegetarian family favorite that pleased everyone. Mom tip: Baking the tofu (400 for 25 minutes) means you can be at the stove for seven minutes less and still have some bite in the tofu. And if you have teens: double the recipe. Fourteen ounces of tofu is enough for about two teens.”

Ham El-Waylly’s vegetarian take on kung pao chicken stars crispy tofu, dried chiles, tingly Sichuan peppercorns and crunchy peanuts.

View the recipe.

23. Parmesan-Crusted Chicken

“This was deliciously juicy and tender. We loved it!”

For perfectly crisp and savory cutlets, skip the dredging, and do as Lidey Heuck does: Slather boneless chicken cutlets in mayo, then coat with a panko-Parmesan mixture before frying.

View the recipe.

22. Orzo Salad

“If my Greek boyfriend loved it, I’m doing something right.”

Wonderfully adaptable and totally portable, this orzo salad from Lidey Heuck is perfect for potlucks or for nibbling on all week long.

View the recipe.

21. Miso Leeks With White Beans

“Totally divine. Wonderful and easy!”

In this riff on the classic French dish leeks vinaigrette from Hetty Lui McKinnon, braised leeks are tossed with a vibrant miso vinaigrette and white beans, then served with a runny egg.

View the recipe.

20. Dumpling and Smashed Cucumber Salad With Peanut Sauce

”What a find! Dead easy, delicious and easy on the budget. And it tastes much more sophisticated than its simple preparation implies. MORE LIKE THIS PLEASE!”

Hetty Lui McKinnon, inventor of the dumpling salad, does it again with this smashed cucumber and peanut sauce variation. To reduce mess, place the cucumbers in a resealable plastic bag before smashing to your heart’s content.

View the recipe.

19. Cucumber Salad

“So simple and delicious! We devoured this. There was enough dressing left at the end so I chopped more cucumbers and made a second bowl!”

Five ingredients — cucumbers, red onion, honey, vinegar and dill — are all you need for this cool salad from Ali Slagle.

View the recipe.

18. Easy Burritos

“Made these up in batch for my NYC stagehand husband’s lunch. There is now a box of burritos in the fridge. He is one happy camper.”

Ali Slagle’s freezer-friendly quick burritos are packed with ground beef, black beans and cheese seasoned with cumin, smoked paprika and oregano.

View the recipe.

17. Lentil Tomato Soup

“This is one of my favorite recipes I’ve made from NYT, and I have made hundreds. It is so simple, and so comforting.”

You’ve had tomato soup, and you’ve had lentil soup, but have you had lentil tomato soup? Carolina Gelen’s gorgeous recipe starts with simmering butter and cream until they thicken and develop brown bits around the pot, resulting in a richer, more nuanced soup.

View the recipe.

16. One-Pot Tortellini With Prosciutto and Peas

“Please. Make this one just as written. You can’t improve it with anything. It’s easy, fast and delicious, and you will get not a single complaint.”

Ali Slagle’s in the house with yet another one-pot pasta dish that will make you feel like you’re winning at life. Inspired by pasta alla papalina, this dish uses tortellini and cooks them right in the broth and heavy cream, so the flavor permeates.

View the recipe.

15. Chocolate Doughnut Muffins

“Tasty and definitely doughnut-like in flavor. I was carrying a baby and entertaining a toddler so didn’t mix everything in the right order. They still turned out light and fluffy.”

Yossy Arefi can do no wrong as far as we’re concerned, and these foolproof baked “doughnuts” prove it. Sour cream and little oil keep them moist so you can pack them for lunches all week long.

View the recipe.

14. Paprika Chicken and Potatoes

“This was the first NYT recipe I cooked, and it did not disappoint! As a college student, I’m looking for quick, easy, cheap and reheatable dishes that I can meal prep, and I think I found my new favorite one!”

Inspired by patatas bravas, this sheet-pan recipe from Yasmin Fahr calls for coating boneless chicken thighs with mayonnaise and smoked paprika, which cook on a layer of thinly sliced potatoes. Everything is served with a tangy, lime-paprika mayo.

View the recipe.

13. Roasted Chicken Thighs With Hot Honey and Lime

“I just came to say that’s one of the best recipes I’ve made in a long time!”

In this lively dish from Vallery Lomas, bone-in chicken thighs are basted in hot sauce butter while they roast, then brushed with a mixture of the remaining sauce, hot honey and lime juice.

View the recipe.

12. One-Pot Chicken Meatballs With Greens

“This was really excellent, healthy and, with everything so expensive at the store right now, super affordable.”

What a clever weeknight trick from Yasmin Fahr: She sears chicken meatballs in a pan, then tops them with lemon slices and fresh greens, which wilt as the meatballs gently finish cooking.

View the recipe.

11. Cheesy Green Chile Bean Bake

“This was so good assume you will need to double the recipe.”

Another lovely dish from Ali Slagle, this one-pot wonder features charred green chiles, pinto beans, Monterey Jack cheese and salsa verde.

View the recipe.

10. Lemon-Garlic Linguine

“This pasta was epic. This is restaurant quality flavor yet so easy and so delicious.”

This simply perfect pasta from Ali Slagle is excellent on its own or served beneath a fillet of salmon or a pile of shrimp.

View the recipe.

9. Chicken au Poivre

“This dish blew us away.”

For spicy-saucy fans, Kay Chun delivers with these boneless chicken thighs that are seared, then simmered until cooked through in a peppery sauce of cream, butter, shallots and cracked black peppercorns.

View the recipe.

8. Creamy, Spicy Tomato Beans and Greens

“This is simple and amazing! My husband thinks it should be renamed ‘Marry Me Beans.’”

Alexa Weibel has a brilliant way to make a can of beans feel fancy on a weeknight: Simmer cannellinis with onion, garlic, red pepper, tomato paste and heavy cream, as well as sun-dried tomatoes and Pecorino until they’re creamy and dreamy. Serve with chunks of good bread.

View the recipe.

7. Pesto Beans

“Served with garlic bread, and it was divine! I could eat this every week.”

White beans meet Parmesan, toasted pine nuts and basil in this cozy one-pot meal from Christian Reynoso.

View the recipe.

6. Sticky Miso Salmon Bowl

“I think this recipe actually cured my depression.”

In this smart recipe from Andy Baraghani, grapefruit zest and juice are combined with miso, honey and ginger, then slathered on salmon before it’s slid under the broiler for a sticky-sweet finish.

View the recipe.

5. Everything Salmon With Creamy Caper Sauce

“Delicious! A lovely elevated version of classic lox bagel toppings.”

Carolina Gelen coats salmon fillets with everything seasoning, then sears them in a pan before topping with a silky cream cheese-caper sauce.

View the recipe.

4. Taverna Salad

“Brought a big batch of this to a potluck to rave review. Crisp and fresh! Bonus for us: Leftovers hold up quite nicely.”

Adapted from “Cooking in Real Life” by Lidey Heuck, this cheerful jumble of a salad is packed with chickpeas, tomatoes, bell pepper, cucumbers, halloumi, olives, capers, shallots and pita chips in a tangy Italian vinaigrette.

View the recipe.

3. Peanut Butter Noodles

“Wow. Just made this tonight. Uh-May-Zing.”

Eric Kim did it again with a genius combination of peanut butter and Parmesan in this 20-minute recipe.

View the recipe.

2. One-Pot Chicken and Rice With Caramelized Lemon

“Delicious — filled the house with a familiar smell. Tasted like childhood but somehow grown up? A Peter Pan meal.”

Tangy, tender lemon slices that have softened and caramelized in chicken fat top Dan Pelosi’s cozy, satisfying chicken and rice.

View the recipe.

1. Microwave Chocolate Pudding Cake

“This recipe must have been written by a scientist — it’s so incredibly perfect.”

When the 2 a.m. craving strikes, Samantha Seneviratne’s one-bowl puddinglike cake will save you. Pro tip: Take the ice cream out of the freezer while you make the cake so it’s perfectly scoopable.

View the recipe.
