Jame: "At this tournament I've run into a problem where I'm not sure what to call, because I know there will be errors"

PARIVISION came into PGL Bucharest as the highest-ranked team in attendance and clear favorite to take the trophy. Instead of delivering on that status they struggled in the group stage, scraping through with a 3-2 record.

"We're not in the best shape right now," Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali admitted to HLTV after his team secured their playoff berth with a win over Wildcard. "We've raised the bar both for ourselves internally and for our fans because of our previous wins at tier one. We're still unstable and are pursuing consistency."

Jame puts his team's inconsistency down to the fact they are still "a young team," and their inexperience affects their ceiling. "Our team doesn't allow us to consistently beat everyone thanks to our [level of] CS. We can have problems at any moment on any map"

With a playoff campaign on the horizon, Jame spoke to HLTV about his team's difficult group stage and his own difficult calling considering the tools at his disposal.

This interview was conducted in Russian and translated by a HLTV staff member.

Give me your thoughts on that series. A very long first map.

We're not in the best shape right now, but on top of that Wildcard are a good team, they played good CS and surprised us in some moments. So playing against them wasn't easy.

Overall, how do you rate your team's performance in this group stage?

As I said from the beginning, without inflated expectations, our goal for this tournament was playoffs. Thankfully we achieved that.

The only definitely positive thing I can say about our game today is that we gave our best. We mentally pulled ourselves together and wanted to win a match, where we could just give up because it doesn't really matter much. But that's partly because we've raised the bar both for ourselves internally and for our fans because of our previous wins at tier one. We're still unstable and are pursuing consistency. This is a young team.

It really just comes down to our current ceiling as a team, and right now it's not particularly high.

Do you think your position as the highest-ranked team, and probably the favorites to win the tournament, affected your performances at all?

I think it can cut both ways, it's hard to assess factors like that. But I think the real issue is the mistakes we're making prevent us from being a stable tier-one team. Fixing these mistakes takes a lot of effort, work, and time, because players can't just understand things first time around, it only comes with years of play. So it really just comes down to our current ceiling as a team, and right now it's not particularly high.

I've seen a lot of talk that your team plays to the level of your opponents. So when you play a good team, you play much better, when you play a team low in the rankings, you play much worse. Do you agree with that, and why do you think that might be?

A lot of situations are simply new to us. Even situations we've been in before that we've reviewed two, three, four, five times, we still make the same mistakes because we're still green, not tier-one players as a collective. So maybe in tier one there's fewer variables in terms of the game we're playing and what we expect from opponents. The range of things that can go wrong is actually narrower than when we face lower-ranked teams, who can do more things we're not prepared for and outplay us.

It's a team game and every player has to contribute equally, so sometimes calls are secondary and the overall skill plays the main role

Overall it just comes down to the fact that our team doesn't allow us to consistently beat everyone thanks to our [level of] CS. We can have problems at any moment on any map. It all comes down to focus and preparation.

I saw a tweet that Thorin made that said that he thought your style of calling worked better against better teams. Do you think that's also true?

I call what I think will win me the round in the moment, based on what I believe we can execute. At this tournament I've run into a problem where I'm not even sure what to call, because I know there'll be errors in some basic elements, and that comes from the mistakes we're making. So it's hard for me to judge [whether my calling] is better or worse. In the end, it's a team game and every player has to contribute equally, so sometimes calls are secondary and the overall skill plays the main role.
