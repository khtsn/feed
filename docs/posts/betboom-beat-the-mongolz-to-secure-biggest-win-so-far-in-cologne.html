BetBoom beat The MongolZ to secure biggest win so far in Cologne

FL4MUS has kept up superb form in Cologne

BetBoom have kicked off their IEM Cologne Major Stage 3 campaign with a 2-1 upset over The MongolZ after a hard-earned Ancient decider win.

The Russian team had already impressed in Cologne, having only suffered one loss across the first two stages, to a super-dominant Spirit side that ran over the opposition in the second.

But this is BetBoom's biggest win yet at the Major, after they previously beat the likes of GamerLegion and Liquid on the way to Stage 3.

They took down The MongolZ after losing an 11-13 Dust2 opener with a dominant response on Mirage, where they lost both pistols and still got away with a 13-6 win, and a 13-11 Ancient decider by winning a 2v4 and two eco rounds, punishing the Mongolian team's characteristic over-exuberance.

"It was really hard," Kirill "⁠Boombl4⁠" Mikhailov said about the Ancient win on the broadcast. "We lost some crucial rounds and they got economy, but we won some force rounds with two guns. It was insane."

Timur "⁠FL4MUS⁠" Marev ended the series on top of the scoreboard in a continuation of his superb form in Cologne so far. He's averaging a 1.25 rating across 13 maps while having to slot in for the sidelined Pavel "⁠S1ren⁠" Ogloblin this time around, after replacing Daniil "⁠d1Ledez⁠" Kustov in the lead-up to the Major.
