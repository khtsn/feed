Vitality have kicked 2026 off in almost identical fashion to their unthinkable run in the first season of 2025. A loss in BLAST Bounty has been put in the rearview with three consecutive trophies, the latest coming at BLAST Open Rotterdam, and they did so in dominant fashion to extend their map winstreak to 22 in a row —the second-best at Big Events of all-time.

Heading into the playoffs, it looked to be a two-way race for the MVP between Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut and Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan, with the latter dominating Aurora in the semi-finals to put himself in pole position to be the first teammate to snatch the medal out of ZywOo's hands.

But a transcendent performance from Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool in the title decider, as well as in the playoffs overall, saw the Estonian walk away with the accolade instead, his first in a Vitality jersey and sixth in his career.

Congratulations on the victory and the MVP medal as well. Your sixth MVP, the first one with Vitality. How does it feel to be the first teammate to take one medal from ZywOo? Is it a little bit of vindication for Budapest?

I mean, I'll start off by saying this is completely unexpected. I'm speechless right now. We were sure that coming into the playoffs, we had flameZ and ZywOo. So obviously I had a great final, and I didn't even consider after playing that final that I would be the MVP. But that's cool, that's really cool.

It's an achievement in a way, definitely. Not to take anything away from ZywOo or flameZ, they were also definitely a part of this tournament and they deserve it as much as me. And yeah, I'm just speechless right now.

Was there anything different about today's match? Because, as you said, heading into the final, it was pretty much between ZywOo and flameZ but you came out of left field and pulled off an amazing performance.

I don't know, as a joke I said, 'This is the first day I got breakfast,' because we had an early match today, but other than that... not much. I've probably had a slow start to this year, and-

Give me your thoughts on the series as a whole. Were you surprised in any way that they picked Anubis?

Yeah, we didn't really expect Anubis. Other than that, I don't know. I'm not sure if they also expected Inferno as the first map, they were probably considering Dust2 as the first pick from us as it's a very good map for us. And yeah, it was good to play Anubis, honestly, I think it's a map we play not so often, and we found some solutions on the map.

We had a good T side on almost all the maps, and the T side today, apEX was calling really well, for example, and it really carried us. And me finding some extra form in the end helped us win the match as well.

We didn't know that we were going to start a map streak, we just know that we want to win tournaments, you know? But if this is the fashion in which we do it, I'm happy.

You won the tournament without dropping a single map, and it's now 22 map wins in a row. How does it feel to win the tournament in such a dominant fashion?

It feels unbelievable. Again, it's just something like... you wish for that kind of thing, you know? To win every game, every match, and to actually do it is an incredible feeling. We had something going last year with the match streak, again, like totally unexpected. We didn't know that we were going to start a map streak, we just know that we want to win tournaments, you know? But if this is the fashion in which we do it, I'm happy.

You've played three finals this year already, each time against a different opponent. Which of the three finals would you say was the hardest?

Okay, there was Krakow with FURIA, PARIVISION, and then NAVI today. I believe they were all 3-0? Ah, FURIA was 3-1, okay. I don't know, it's hard to say specifically.

I think today was maybe a bit easier, just because we had a good T side. Sometimes you have to make a comeback or something like that. At the end on Dust2, it got a bit scary. I think we had a lot of good reads with the flank on flameZ, but unfortunately, he missed, so stuff like that. We just didn't hit the shots at the end and it got a bit scary on Dust2, but other than that, today was a bit easier I would say, again thanks to the T sides. So probably one of the matches from before from PARIVISION or FURIA.

After this, it's Rio. A big target for you because you want to win the Grand Slam as soon as possible. What is the plan for that one, are you going straight back to work or are you going to enjoy a little break?

Actually, I don't know what the schedule is for next week. The fun fact is, we have a full media day tomorrow in Paris. That's something we... we plan things ahead a lot, and we planned that we go from a full media day in Paris, which is going to be tomorrow, then we fly home on Tuesday and stuff like that.

So our schedule is, in a way, always very detailed and very planned out. Other than that,we just try to manage it so that we can peak again for Rio because that's the goal, to win a Grand Slam obviously before the Major. We have two chances in Rio and Atlanta, but that's the big goal for now. Further than that, it's not much else.
