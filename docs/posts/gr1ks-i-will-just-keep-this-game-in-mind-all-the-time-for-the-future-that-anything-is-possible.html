gr1ks: "I will just keep this game in mind all the time for the future, that anything is possible"

Gleb "⁠gr1ks⁠" Gazin wasn't feeling the pressure at his first Major. That is, until NRG were putting BIG to the sword.

"I was shitting my pants," gr1ks told HLTV when asked about how he felt at half-time. Some time later, after BIG came back from 0-12 to win 16-12, the only feeling was elation.

Jon "⁠JDC⁠" de Castro believed — or made it look like he did — right from the start. At 1-12, he was already heard telling his team to take it 'round by round.' After each round, the cheers got louder and louder. The belief was spreading.

"And we would not have made it if not for faveN, really, this fucking motherfucker," gr1ks says, interrupting the interview to give BIG's anchor a deserved hug.

As experiences go during a Major debut, this is a hell of a start for gr1ks. "I felt kind of terrible with the pressure," he admits. "On the third map, I just shit in my pants, really, and I felt terrible. I don't know. This game, for sure, I will just keep in mind all the time for all the future games, that anything is possible."

Listen to the interview here.

gr1ks, just walk me through the emotions after that.

I fucking don't believe it, really. I don't have much to say. That's really unbelievable. And my head right now, like, is blowing, really. I have a full headache after these emotions. It's crazy.

It seemed like JDC started believing quite early, but when did you kind of think that this actually could happen?

I will say the truth that on a 12-0 score, and as well when in previous rounds, I made mistakes. For example, I missed a smoke, I didn't kill the guy somewhere, under this pressure. I felt terrible. And this three-minute break half just reset me, kind of, I don't know.

And as well, [coach] Basti [xenn], everyone here in the team, we just raised our mood. We won the pistol, we won the second round, and we won another round. And this motivation, this belief, was increasing, increasing, increasing. It's unbelievable at the moment, really.

From the start of second half, I really fully believed that we would fucking do it. And we would not have made it if not for faveN, really, this fucking motherfucker. I don't know, this guy is crazy. Just these two kills in underground and clutch, 1vs2. Fucking insane.

It was a pleasure. I was standing in front of JDC for 15 rounds. I've got 15 videos, and he didn't stop cheering the whole time. How are you even able to believe in coming back from that situation?

I don't know, really. He's crazy. I have a little bit of a different mindset. I believe, but I cannot cheer on a team like he did. It's amazing, and he's just unique, really. He's a fucking champion.

JDC believed right from the start

This is such an important tournament for BIG, with the home country [factor] and everything. How much does this mean for the team to carry on into Stage 2?

For everyone, this tournament is insanely important. As well, to prove ourselves, to prove that we worked hard before, and we can do anything, we already showed it right now. That's fucking unbelievable. But I think, yes, it has insane meaning as well for tabseN; he is in his hometown. He wants to play in an arena, not only here on stage, and we want [it] more for him. After this, I feel the pressure and the importance that we want to win every game. We want to be in the next stage.

It must be a crazy experience for you as well, to be at your first Major and already kind of make history in this way. How are you dealing with the emotions of this being your first Major?

In other games, I didn't have emotions, and I wasn't pressured at all. Here, maybe I woke up on the wrong leg and so on, I don't know, but I felt kind of terrible with the pressure. And I'm saying the truth. On the third map, I just shit in my pants, really, and I felt terrible. I don't know. This game, for sure, I will just keep in mind all the time for all the future games, that anything is possible. In every moment, we can do anything; it's amazing, really.
