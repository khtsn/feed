The MongolZ earn first-ever series win over MOUZ

Techno posted his best-ever LAN map with a 2.30 rating on Inferno

The MongolZ have bagged their first series win in five attempts over MOUZ in their opening bout at BLAST Open Rotterdam, taking the European side down 2-0 with victories on Nuke (13-11) and Inferno (13-3).

Much of the talk before the game was focused on how MOUZ would react to their disappointing quarter-finals exit in their previous outing, at ESL Pro League, and the reaction was a poor one. They could be forgiven for losing The MongolZ's map pick in close fashion, but the collapse on the second map was a foreboding sign for the team.

Crisp cobrazera multi-kill denies MOUZ

The MongolZ snuck a win on their pick of Nuke in what was a streaky affair. MOUZ got the better of the first half by punishing The MongolZ's loose approach on T side, before the Mongolian squad answered back late in the second half with a CT surge powered by Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar.

Inferno was an entirely one-sided affair as MOUZ completely capitulated on their own map selection, Sodbayar "⁠Techno⁠" Munkhbold running rings around the European side as only a single MOUZ player managed to hit double-digit frags.

The MongolZ finally defeat MOUZ with Techno's best-ever LAN map

The MongolZ had previously not been able to beat MOUZ in series play, despite playing each other in five BO3s since late 2024. In fact, The MongolZ hadn't won a single map across all of those series.

The Mongolian squad broke that streak in emphatic fashion today, staying resolute on Nuke before ruthlessly exposing a poor MOUZ performance on Inferno, largely down to the best-rated LAN map showing of Techno's career. All of this was done without coach Erdenedalai "⁠maaRaa⁠" Bayanbat behind them, making the feat all the more impressive.
