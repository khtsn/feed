PGL to host March 2027 event in Lodz

The Polish city of Lodz and its 15,000-seater Atlas Arena will host PGL's third event of 2027, taking place from March 18-28.

It will mark the return of the Romanian tournament organizer to Poland, nearly a decade since it last hosted an event there with PGL Major Krakow 2017.

That comes after PGL's previous attempt to return to the Slavic country was foiled when its booking of Krakow's TAURON Arena for the first 2027 event was cancelled by the venue over a scheduling clash.

Along with the location announcement, PGL also revealed the rest of the additional information required by Valve's Tournament Operating Guidelines, including the distribution of financial incentives, information about qualifiers, and potential rules for player or team exclusion from the tournament.

A key inclusion to its "Integrity and Conduct" rules is the following line, which serves as punishment for withdrawals from the tournament after accepting an invitation: "Any Roster that withdraws from a PGL event, regardless of the timing of the withdrawal or the surrounding circumstances, is subject to a disqualification from receiving a Direct VRS Invite to the subsequent PGL event."

From the 16 participating teams, 14 will be invited directly from the December 7 Global VRS, and two teams will come from a European qualifier. That is a distinct change from this year's format, which featured qualifiers in Europe, North America, South America and Asia.

The European qualifier will be split into an open (January 4-7) and closed portion (January 9-12), which will feature an additional six invites from the European VRS.

The distribution of the $1 million prizepool looks as follows:

1. $180,000 prize + $100,000 club share

An additional $300,000 will be distributed to the top-five highest-ranked teams who accept an invitation to the tournament, up to any team ranked within the top 14 of the Global VRS at the time of invitation.

As part of PGL's expansion of the circuit for 2027-2028 and $22 million pledge over the two years, PGL Lodz event will also count towards a $2.8 million annual viewership incentive, a scheme that rewards teams for their participation and the viewership it brings to the event.
