The first half of Spirit's Dust2 pick was incredibly one-sided, as Spirit won all but two rounds. One of MOUZ's wins came with a half-buy, while the other came courtesy of Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás and Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman both pushing smokes to find favorable timings.

A second-half pistol could have given MOUZ a lifeline, but their comeback was stopped as soon as Spirit got their guns out.

The first half of Mirage followed a similar storyline. A rampant Spirit cruised to a 9-3 half in part thanks to Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets consistently getting the better of xertioN in the entry department.

MOUZ put together a better attempt at a comeback on their pick, but it proved far too little. While the deficit was reduced to just one round at one point, Spirit took over quickly to secure a dominant 13-8 win after donk single-handedly demolished the A-site defense with a triple entry in the final round of the game.

Has tN1R finally found his place on Spirit?

The Belarusian had an underwhelming first few months on Spirit after joining as HEROIC's star man, but IEM Rio is his best event with the team so far thanks to some recent role changes, such as returning to B Short on Mirage.

He has averaged a 1.24 rating so far, the second-best on his team after donk (1.30), and has had some standout maps that have coincided with Spirit looking as good as they have in a few months.

While the sample size is small and could prove a flash in the pan, an upturn in his form might return Spirit to title-contender status once again.
