saadzin: "arT is very good, not only in the server but also out of game"

We caught up with Guilherme "⁠saadzin⁠" Pacheco ahead of the ESL Pro League Season 23 Finals, set to take place in Stockholm, Sweden, and where Legacy are set to take on Aurora in the quarter-finals.

Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan will have his LAN debut with Legacy after leading the team as they navigated through the first two online Swiss stages. In the second of the two, the Brazilians already beat the rivals they are about to face on LAN.

"The game is more about you than about them," saadzin says about the rematch against the Turks. "I think they are a great team, but for us, we have to do our things."

How are you feeling after your efforts in the previous stages?

Feeling great. I think I handled the pressure in the second stage very well. We played against tier-one teams, like NAVI, and I think it was… not easy, but it was good to do the plan of your IGL, arT. He put a lot of confidence in my plays, so I think that's why I played very well in the match against them.

In general the team has had a good tournament, beating some good teams, and doing so very quickly after adding arT. Are you surprised with how quickly you stepped up to a good level?

Yeah, of course (laughs). We had the expectation to start well, but not like this, very, very well. I think it was because of the climate in the team, arT is very good not only in the server but also out of game, he's very friendly. When we end the scrims, we always go to eat something together, play some tabletop games together, and I think this is important because your team gets more confident in each other. I think this is key for us.

Is this something you were looking for when you brought arT into the team, were you looking for that vibe?

Yeah, we were thinking about getting someone who can realise the potential of the team, you know? Like I said, we got very surprised because we were not expecting to get so good so fast. It was very nice, we are just happy with him and just want to do more and be even better in the playoffs.

Has there been much of a change to your style since bringing in arT?

We didn't change too much. I still like to be a good support for the team, but at the same time, I think with arT I can do more of my game. When I do my entry strategies against teams, I show them to him, and he says, 'This is very good, do it when you feel it's good, I will believe in you.' I think this is key for me, as I am a very aggressive AWPer.

If you have the confidence of your IGL, you can go, and if it doesn't work, it's okay. You don't have too much pressure when your team believes in your play, and I think this is key for me. In the last games, I got much better.

The other thing that is helping me a lot is, in the latest bootcamp, I played a lot. I think this is the bootcamp where I played the most Counter-Strike. I am playing a lot of the game, like 24 hours per day (laughs). I'm very happy that I'm growing with the team.

We were expecting to start well, but not this well

In general, your team has a reputation for being quite aggressive. Is that why arT fits so well? Because obviously, he is known for being a very aggressive player himself.

Yeah. I think Counter-Strike doesn't have only one style, and like you said, we love to play aggressive. arT likes this, also...

It's like a match made in heaven.

Yeah, and it's working. Sometimes it's arT who says, 'Guys, stop being so aggressive.' He is the aggressive guy, but sometimes we are way more aggressive than him, so he has to say to me, 'Do your plays, but go a little bit slower in this round.' Sometimes he has to rein me in a little bit.

Looking forward to the playoffs, how confident are you guys feeling coming into this LAN?

To be honest, we are feeling confident and happy, because the last time we were playing in an arena was in China at CAC, a long time ago. We love to see the crowd, it's always amazing, and yeah it will be nice. We just want to have a good time in the arena.

Aurora is an interesting matchup. You have played a few times recently. You played last year in the Bucharest finals and have already played them in the ESL Pro League, so how are you feeling about that matchup?

Like any other match, we just try to focus more on us than the other guys. The game is more about you than about them. I think they are a great team, but for us, we have to do our things. If we do our things well, it will be a good match, so we are focused on this.

Looking forward more generally for Legacy, what is your long-term goal for the team? Do you have an idea about being top-five HLTV, for example?

As an individual, I really want to become a better AWPer for my teammates. I am close to two years with Legacy, especially dumau and latto, and they have really taught me a lot in the game. I'm another player today because of them. I just want to be great and show them that the confidence they have in me for this long is justified.

As a team, like I said to you, we were expecting to start well, but not this well. When you start very well, you always want more. At the end of last year, we finished in the top seven on the Valve ranking, so the goal is getting back to the best rank we had as a core, and with more pracs we find the top five, top three, top one.

The sky is the limit?

Yeah, your goal will always be to, step by step, reach top one, reach the level that Vitality is today.
