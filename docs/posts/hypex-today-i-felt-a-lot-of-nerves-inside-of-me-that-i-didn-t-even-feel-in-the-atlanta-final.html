hypex: "Today I felt a lot of nerves inside of me that I didn't even feel in the Atlanta final"

GamerLegion were one of the favorites to progress through Stage 2, and the European combine did so after a win over BIG in the 2-1 pool.

"I think I can say I feel some relief inside of my body," Milan "⁠hypex⁠" Polowiec told HLTV after the nail-biting clash, while admitting that the Major still means something special to him.

He also spoke in more detail about the game against BIG, his personal uptick in form, and GamerLegion's expectations for the rest of the Major.

Listen to the interview here.

You made it to the next stage after the win against BIG. How do you feel after getting the first part of the job done pretty early?

I think I can say I feel some relief inside of my body. I'm happy that we proved that we can go to the next stage. Hopefully we're not done here, we want to go as far as possible. This was a hard game, personally for me as well, and I'm just happy we did it.

How did you stay in the game? Because especially on Overpass, you lost that ninja defuse, for example, and they won 8 in a row as well, but in the end, you rallied and won both maps. How proud are you of the team for their resilience?

I'm very proud. But usually in these hard moments, I feel like we deal with it pretty well. We can reset real quick and focus on the next round, and that's what we did. We lost that ninja defuse, then we lost the 2vs1, and It was obviously hard, because we had to win two hard rounds on the T side on Overpass just to make it to overtime.

My head was so not in the game at that point that when we won the round for 12-12, I thought it was already overtime at that point, I thought we just won the first Overtime round. It is what it is, but I think we're pretty good at these situations.

To ask more about the team. Recently you had some really good results, a top four, and a final in Atlanta as well, and it feels like it kind of happened out of nowhere. Is there anything that changed in the team, or is it just about the system clicking at the right time?

We had like two or three months going to these lower-tier events, grinding our way back in the ranking before we had the tier-one events. We were training a lot from home as well, we didn't even have a boot camp, but we just trained so much from home, went to these lower-tier events, and then we wanted to show what we can do.

In both of these events we only lost against Vitality and NAVI three times. It was a shock to us as well

We didn't expect two times playoffs instantly, semifinals and finals. In both of these events we only lost against Vitality and NAVI three times. It was a shock to us as well, but we were very happy, but that's not where we stop. We don't think we're a top-three team or anything like this. We know we still have to do a lot of work to maintain being consistent, and that's what we're trying to do.

During this run, you personally showed much improved form in the last few months. What is that down to? Is it just you feeling more comfortable in the team, getting some more space from your teammates, or what's the situation there?

I think it's kind of everything. I got to know the guys for a longer time now, so obviously I'm more comfortable. But also inside the game, we keep playing, we keep training, we see what's the best for me, we see what's the best for the guys, and we all find that solid consistency we want to have.

I've been working on myself a lot as well, so I think it's working for me. I'm still getting used to these new events, you know, the Major is still pretty new to me. Today, for example, I felt a lot of nerves inside of me that I didn't even feel inside the Atlanta final. It was kind of new, but I'm glad we did it.

Do you feel this is being made easier by the recent [Animgraph] update? We saw a lot of AWPers struggling in CS2 and it feels like some of you found some form. Do you think that's connected?

I don't know. After the Animgraph update I felt much worse. Even in practice, I couldn't play. Then some little update came out, and since then I've been back again. I don't know what it is, it might be fully placebo, I don't know. Genuinely, I have no idea. I don't like it because of the new aim punch, so as soon as they hit you with the bullet, your crosshair goes up a bit. I'm not a fan of that.

You're in Stage 2, and there are some pretty good teams entering the stage. Given your recent results, I guess you could be expected to pass at least to Stage 3. Do you share the same opinion, and how far do you think you guys can take it here?

We're not really trying to look at it like this. As I said, we only had two tier-one events since we were back. Obviously, we reached the playoffs, semifinals, finals, but we know that's not our level yet. We're still working to make it further and further, so there's no expectations to reach Stage 3 or playoffs. We're just going to take it game after game and whatever happens, happens.
