w0nderful claims first career MVP at IEM Atlanta

Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov was the 11th best player of 2024 and has won six notable trophies in his career, including a Major, but for the first time, he has earned an HLTV x 1XBET Most Valuable Player (MVP) award for his performance at IEM Atlanta.

For anyone ready to call w0nderful a tier 2 farmer due to the field at IEM Atlanta, the Ukrainian sniper had a 1.45 rating in the 2-1 victory over the world's best team, Vitality, powering Natus Vincere to victory with 1.95 and 1.87 ratings in the two maps they won to secure the series.

The second most notable series was, of course, the grand final, where w0nderful led Natus Vincere on the scoreboard with a 1.44 rating in the 3-0 victory against GamerLegion.

Even in defeat, w0nderful showed up, with a server-leading 1.41 rating in the 1-2 loss to Legacy in the group stage final that put Natus Vincere on a collision course with Vitality.

Other than being the second-highest rated player at the event behind Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto's 1.32, w0nderful led in K-D difference (+107), total kills (289); was second in KPR (0.86), DPR (0.54), total AWP kills (105), total opening kills (47), and round swing (+3.04%); third in damage difference per round (+17.0) and success in opening rounds (69.1%); and fifth in clutches (6).
