A video posted to YouTube earlier by Global Esports CEO and Co-Founder Rushindra Sinha verified that although the paperwork is not yet official, the change in coaching staff has been submitted to Riot and is awaiting approval before any official announcement can be posted.

“We ideally would have wanted to make as few or no changes,” said Sinha. “Unfortunately, [we] had to make these changes. The timing could have definitely been better. I will also say that this was absolutely my decision…Only time will tell whether this was the right call or it wasn't. We'll know in a few weeks.”

FrosT – who was signed just last November – took Global Esports to its highest level of success yet.
