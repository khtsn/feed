The roster will consist of members of the former MU squad, headlined by head coach Matthew "mCe" Elmore, and former MU players Adam "Ange" Milian, Caide "chloric" Heaton, Tanner "geeza" McGhee, Austin "renz" Dickman, and Yahya "Shondex" Amani.

NRG shared that the partnership looks to address the balance between fully committing to competitive play and pursuing their education.

"That's why we're proud to partner with Maryville University, one of the most established collegiate esports programs in the country," NRG said. "Together, we're creating a structure that connects competition, training, and education - one where eligible players can pursue a degree while developing in a high-performance environment on a professional track. Through this partnership, players who join NRG Academy will also have the option to attend Maryville under scholarship (subject to eligibility and admissions), compete in the collegiate ecosystem, and earn a degree while continuing their development."
