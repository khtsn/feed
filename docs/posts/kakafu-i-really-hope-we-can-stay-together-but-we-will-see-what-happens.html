kakafu: "I really hope we can stay together, but we will see what happens"

kakafu was left distraught after Monte could not close out a Dust2 decider from 11-10 to get eliminated from the Major

HLTV spoke to Monte's coach, Alexander "⁠kakafu⁠" Szymanczyk, in the aftermath of the team's elimination from the IEM Cologne Major.

The team had just suffered two narrow losses to Falcons and The MongolZ to go out 1-3, two losses that could have gone the other way, but the Austrian chose to focus on the positives.

"Obviously, sad in the beginning, but very proud of what we showed here," kakafu said. "The whole journey is just amazing and shows what a good, structured team can do with pieces that believe."

That, along with the team's progression through Stage 2, was a stark contrast to Monte's first tier-one showing at PGL Astana, where they were slammed in matches against the elite-level opposition.

In our space we just need to appreciate what we have in the moment. What happens tomorrow happens tomorrow

"Very objectively, this is 'we can compete.' At the beginning, we were probably 0-3 in all the stages on the Pick'Ems, but we knew from practices and our games before that we can shoot back, tactically and also skill-wise," the coach went on.

Heading into the off-season, kakafu hopes he can keep the team together, but understands that may be a tall task for an up-and-coming squad whose individuals showed plenty of promise.

"At any given moment, somebody can get bought out or leave, find another opportunity," he said. "I think in our space we just need to appreciate what we have in the moment. What happens tomorrow happens tomorrow. We don't know. I obviously wish this group can stay together."

Commiserations, kakafu, for the elimination from the Major. Tell me your first impressions after such close losses in the last couple of days could have gone the other way.

Yeah, for sure. Obviously, sad in the beginning, but very proud of what we showed here. I think the whole journey is just amazing and shows what a good, structured team can do with pieces that believe.

Yesterday, a heartbreaker XvX against Falcons, today kind of the same thing and we had it, but we couldn't pull it through. I think they also played good. Inferno, we kinda lost the T side, it was pretty dead, the mood was going down and I tried it to keep the motivation, but we just didn't play good on the T side. Dust2, we had it, it's a good map for us, and couldn't close it.

Does it feel like progress, anyway? In Astana, you showed competitiveness against the teams around your level, but against the top teams it looked pretty rough. Here, you were much more competitive against Falcons, The MongolZ. Do you still see that as a positive sign moving forward?

Yeah, for sure. Astana was our first tier-one tournament. The boys are super inexperienced and I also wasn't there for the first two days or three days. But we learned a lot and that's the most important thing, that we learn a lot when we attend these types of things, we talk a lot and we improve a lot.

It's 100% positive. Very objectively, this is 'we can compete.' At the beginning, we were probably 0-3 in all the stages on the Pick'Ems, but we knew from practices and our games before that we can shoot back, tactically and also skill-wise. Also super happy about AZUWU. He was a big question mark coming into this tournament, and he showed up. When he shows up, we have a very solid chances.

There was also a lot of positive feedback towards Gizmy. I saw several analysts give him props for a lot of the calling that he showed at this tournament, the creative calls he was coming up with mid-round. What did you think about that?

I think Gizmy is a fantastic IGL. I really enjoy sparring with him. All the T sides are basically on his shoulders, a lot of protocols that he implemented are helping us to win games, so I'm very happy. I think he has a bright future. What he does, he does it with passion, and I think that's super important as an IGL.

His calling is obviously enabled by a lot of different pieces around him. People cannot know it, but afro is a very big piece in that too, they meditate each other, if somebody is wrong, or right, and afro is basically the second voice that helps a lot, also in the mid-round calling.

We put a lot of emphasis in communication overall. We put a lot of emphasis in, okay, what do we want to do in the XvX, in the 5v4s and the 4v4s, what's the structure, who is lurking where, what is our ending calling, and Gizmy is doing a very good job of understanding this and also calling it. And if he has a clear vision of it, it's very nice and it can look like this.

Being part of an up-and-coming team that's showing off some of its pieces, it's always dangerous to come into the off-season with the uncertainty of whether some bigger team is going to come calling for your players. Do you have any indication whether this is team will be able to continue forward with the same roster?

It's hard to say. I don't know what's happening with the players. At any given moment, somebody can get bought out or leave, find another opportunity. I think in our space we just need to appreciate what we have in the moment. What happens tomorrow happens tomorrow. We don't know.

I obviously wish this group can stay together. As I sometimes tell them, we're like the boy band, and when we have these trips, for me it's like the boys' trips. They're way younger than me. Just being around them, it's really enjoyable. I really hope we can stay together, but what happens, we will see.

What would be the goals for the next season if you were able to stick together with this?

I hope that we can just continue. It's all about consistency. You can have a good tournament here and there, but it's all about if we can show this at the next tournament and the next tournament. I always set my sights on being a team that can always make playoffs.

This is my goal right now as a coach, because I think the other goals are very unrealistic. These types of tournaments give me hope that my team and me can reach these types of stages. I just hope that we can improve, everybody is still hungry, and I think if the players are ready to keep listening to me (laughs) and keep working and they believe in what we're doing, then I think the sky is the limit.
