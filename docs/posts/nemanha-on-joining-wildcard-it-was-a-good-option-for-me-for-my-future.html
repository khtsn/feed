nEMANHA on joining Wildcard: "It was a good option for me, for my future"

nEMANHA sees a bright future with his new team

Wildcard find themselves in the 2-2 pool at PGL Bucharest after picking up wins against FaZe (by forfeit) and FOKUS and suffering losses to B8 and The MongolZ.

This is Nemanja "⁠nEMANHA⁠" Đukić's third LAN event with Wildcard after he took over as IGL following Anthony "⁠CLASIA⁠" Kearney's departure. The North American team previously placed 5th-8th at Urban Riba Open Season 3 and 9th-12th at Roman Imperium Cup VII under the Serbian IGL.

It was a bit of an unexpected move from nEMANHA, who had raised eyebrows while leading the free agent roster illwill, which was ranked 23rd in the global VRS at one point.

"They contacted me while I was in illwill, before the Parken Challenger Championship, we were in contact already, I think," he told HLTV.

"In illwill we didn't have an organization, no salary, and it was a hard time. After that tournament, I had some opportunities with other teams, but I decided to play in Wildcard, because I saw it was a good option for me, for my future, playing a tournament like this, one of the biggest tournaments in CS."

nEMANHA was pleased that his team "showed our best CS" against the strongest team it has faced so far. The first map of the series, Dust2, went the full distance, while Nuke ended in a respectable 13-8 scoreline.

"I think we're getting better form now, and we will have better matches, because we didn't have too much practice before this," he said. "From match to match, we will be better, and I'm happy because we showed good rounds and a good game against a good opponent. I see a bright future with this team.

"I am very surprised [with how well the team is playing this quickly], and actually it's my first tier-one tournament.

"It's a new experience for me, and I'm very happy because we gave good damage against a good opponent. The MongolZ is, I think, a top five team in the world. I'm very happy."

Wildcard secured a last-minute invite to PGL Bucharest following The Huns' withdrawal. It's still early days for the team, but there is no doubt that there are also some very encouraging signs.

Questioned about what he hopes to achieve with his new team, he said, "To play more tournaments like this, S-tier tournaments. Secondly, to make the next Major."
