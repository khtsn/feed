TeSeS on hard side of bracket: "It's made for the perfect storyline for whoever goes on to win"

Falcons are among the teams given the harder side of the IEM Cologne Major playoff bracket, with Vitality first up in the quarter-finals and the winner of Spirit vs. G2 next.

"Oh, we are a grand final team, so we'll get the second place," René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen joked to HLTV on the media day when asked about what Falcons have ahead of them. "No, it's a good bracket, and I like the storyline that I can build in my head of first taking down Vitality, then Spirit or G2, whoever comes against us. It's only good teams from here on out, and for me, that's very motivating."

With Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov missing the media day due to illness, we asked TeSeS a question originally intended for 'The Flash,' who has faced community criticism for costly overpeeks.

"When we're leading, we're feeling good, the economy is well, and we have the space to take risks, he should go for it because he can secure the round from 60% to 100% by getting that extra kill," TeSeS said after stating the team does review and talk about these mistake. "So it's about not limiting anyone, but also knowing when enough is enough."

Vitality in your first match, a tough one. You haven't played them with karrigan yet, so how's the approach changing going into that? How do you think that's going to go?

Yeah, it'll be fun to see what we can do with karrigan on the team against them. Of course, with kyxsan, we had a kind of good record against them overall, or at least in the last part. But yeah, it's always fun games against them, and I feel like it's always entertaining, at least for us, to play the game. And just knowing that we'll be meeting the best team in the world is always fun, like it's always nice to have the possibility of playing them.

Their form's looking a little shaky at times during this event, and your form is also, though you're still trying to work on integrating karrigan. How do you think that's going to go with the way they're playing and the way you have been playing so far?

It's too tough to say that their form is going down, like it's a period of two weeks where they had all the time before that where they have absolutely dominated everything. So I think it's unfair to say they are going down; they're still on their A-game, they're still winning everything in my head at least, so yeah, it will be fun.

And of course, we have also been struggling a little bit, not the cleanest games we've had, and also the loss against a well-playing BetBoom. We're trying to adjust from game to game still, and learning from every game, so it shows that we are still quite early in our progression as a team for sure.

Did the BetBoom loss come as a bit of a confidence blow at all? How did you guys approach afterward?

I don't think it came as a confidence blow. I think we actually learned a lot from that game, we knew what we had to work on in the next games, and we were able to show it immediately the day after. Even though it was a close game against Monte and especially a close game against NAVI in the end, I'm still happy that we overcame all of that and also kind of improved along the way.

We were talking in Astana about the way that you had changed up a couple of positions, how you're adjusting to being in some of these anchor spots like on A on Mirage, B on Dust2. How do you think that's gone now that you've had a bit more time?

Yeah, some positions are still better than others for sure (laughs). At first, I struggled a lot with Dust2 B; now, Dust2 B is on full lockdown. Now Mirage A is a bit of a problem, at least against NAVI (smiles), so yeah. It's kind of changing up from game to game right now.

Some games I feel really comfortable, sometimes not because the opponents have a good game plan or are abusing the way I have been playing in the previous games. So there's definitely a lot of stuff that I still need to learn on certain positions, and this is what I'm working on.

We were originally going to talk to m0NESY here, and I want to ask him this: there's been some criticism about him overpeeking or repeeking a lot. What's the conversation been like in the team when you go back and review stuff during practice about some of those situations?

Yeah, of course we talk about it, and we also point out that the most crucial mistakes sometimes is overpeeking from all of us. It can happen to all of us. But we need to point it out and need to be able to talk about it, and I think everyone is open to it and open to the criticism.

But it's also important that you don't limit these star players, especially Ilya [m0NESY], because he's winning way more rounds because he's taking that extra kill than losing us that round; it's just more when he does it. If our economy is really shit and we actually have the advantage, maybe more times than not, then maybe don't repeek.

But when we're leading, we're feeling good, the economy is well, and we have the space to take risks, he should go for it because he can secure the round from 60% to 100% by getting that extra kill. So it's about not limiting anyone, but also knowing when enough is enough.

How has he reacted to that feedback when you have had those conversations?

Everyone is reacting good; there are no bad feelings involved at all. It's just about the game of CS, the game of percentages of winning, so it's very objective and not feeling-based.
