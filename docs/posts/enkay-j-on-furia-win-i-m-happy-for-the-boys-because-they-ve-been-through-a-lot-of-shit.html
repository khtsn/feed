enkay J on FURIA win: "I'm happy for the boys because they've been through a lot of shit"

FaZe qualified for the BLAST Rivals playoffs with a big victory over FURIA, ending the series on the Nuke decider in classic FaZe bullshit fashion with a 2v4 retake by David "⁠frozen⁠" Čerňanský and Helvijs "⁠broky⁠" Saukants after an errant frag after calling a save turned it into a 2v3.

We caught up with FaZe's new coach, Niclas "⁠enkay J⁠" Krumhorn, after the series, to hear his thoughts on how the team is doing since he joined, his relationship with Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken and management, and how Ryan "⁠Neityu⁠" Aubry fits with the team as a stand-in.

"At the moment, we're focusing on what is happening right now, and in the background, upper management and I will have conversations on how we see things, how we're clicking, does it make sense, how is Russ feeling personally, is he living up to the task? Is he comfortable in it?" enkay J said on the team's roadmap regarding in-game leadership. "So far I'm very, very pleased with what I'm seeing."

Listen to the interview here.

What does it mean to get that win against FURIA, especially for the team morale?

Obviously, for the team morale, it's a big win. Hopefully, it lifts everybody up. We've been in a pretty decent mood overall in the last week, but this might hopefully get the ball rolling.

At the end of the day, we're just happy that we have the win. I'm happy for the boys because they've been through a lot of shit over the last couple of weeks, months maybe even, so I'm just very content with the situation right now.

I missed the last round while filming a video, and right after it, I got a message telling me that I had to check it out, so I did. Can we say that the FaZe bullshit is still alive?

[Laughs] I guess you can.

I, personally, I'm German, you know, I would have much rather seen the save there because we would have had a good situation next round coming in with two important weapons saved, but they're obviously way more experienced than I am, right?

I'm happy with the W. I'm very certain that we would have closed it out eventually, in the upcoming rounds, but yeah. Maybe it's back? I don't know.

I don't really feel that kind of darkness that could be around the team. I don't really feel it. Everybody is really positive, and I'm happy about it

How has it been for you coming into the team at such a critical moment in the middle of so many changes, especially with a club legend leaving and a weird atmosphere? How has the integration been?

It has actually been very smooth. The players were on a break when I signed my contract, they went on vacation, so I took individual calls with them to get to know them, see what their feelings are, and maybe how I can help them individually.

Then, overall, they've been very welcoming. Not only the players, but also the staff. We've been working very closely together, the staff, and also Russ [Twistzz] and I, with how we want to approach things, and it has been a very pleasant experience even though the times may be somewhat dire.

But I don't really feel that kind of darkness that could be around the team. I don't really feel it. Everybody is really positive, and I'm happy about it.

You speak about how you get along with Russ, and are talking to him. With him taking over the captainship, is that something you've talked about regarding the mid and long term, or is it something you're just doing now in the short term? How will that work out?

That's a good question that I personally don't know the answer to yet. At the moment, we're focusing on what is happening right now, and in the background, upper management and I will have conversations on how we see things, how we're clicking, does it make sense, how is Russ feeling personally, is he living up to the task? Is he comfortable in it? It's just going to be a lot of talks going on, but so far I'm very, very pleased with what I'm seeing.

I spoke to Twistzz during the media day, and he was talking about how Eddie [Han] is taking more of a GM role, so I guess you'll be talking to him, as well. How is that relationship developing?

Very positive. With the GM, I have to be in very close contact on a daily basis, just to give him updates on how practices are going, and he's also a lot of times part of practices, to see how we're doing.

There's also, on top of that, a lot of conversation on how we see things can be going, but overall, very positive. Right now, with us being here in the US, it's very easy to communicate because the time zones are a bit different, but overall, we're moving in the right direction with the pieces that we have.

The mood was very low, so we needed a lot of positivity, and Ryan is that kind of guy

Tell me a bit about the FURIA match. It looks like they're also going through something. You had an incredible T side on Nuke, but tell me about the series, your expectations, and what you found, and what worked so well on that final map.

We were going into that match with no real expectations. We knew that they were going to be trying to disrupt our defaults on Dust2, but it didn't come as much as we were expecting. On Mirage, we made, unfortunately, too many crucial mistakes in important rounds, which led us to not really having a good economy on the CT side. We didn't really click on the T side, either, unfortunately.

There was a lot of frustration building up, which was, in my opinion, a bit unnecessary. We addressed it afterwards, outside. We went very freely into Nuke. No real expectations there either, in all honesty.

We were just playing our game, and I told the guys, 'Boys, we need to just play what we're comfortable playing, if you have a good spawn, a feeling, do it, just have fun,' because we're doing the best when we have fun, and all of a sudden, we come into a flow state.

Twistzz called perfectly on the T side. After the second round, he was in the zone, he was tunnel-visioning, he was in a flow state, and everybody trusted him and executed everything that he called, and it was just a flawless T side in general.

Bringing on Neityu, he's maybe your boy because you coached him before and you know him. What made you think of him to bring him in?

We were assessing the market, who's available at this short notice, and who can we bring in that's a fit role-wise, most importantly. Not just on the server, but also off the server.

When I was looking at the team, what was happening around them in the last couple of weeks, it looked a bit dire, [there was] frustration, the mood was very low, so we needed a lot of positivity, and Ryan is that kind of guy. I knew him very closely from my time in ENCE, and I knew that what he was going to bring to the table was going to help the team elevate the atmosphere, the vibes.

Role-wise, he's also slotting in pretty well, we [just] had to make some slight adjustments. There were other candidates out there, and it was not only me deciding this. I was not even necessarily the first guy bringing him into the conversation, but also people from FaZe. Eddie was dropping his name, so were frozen and Twistzz because they had him on their radar for a bit longer, as well. It was like a joint decision, more or less.
