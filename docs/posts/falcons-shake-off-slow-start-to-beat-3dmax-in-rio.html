Falcons shake off slow start to beat 3DMAX in Rio

Falcons got their IEM Rio campaign off to a winning start with a 2-0 victory against 3DMAX, who showed much promise during the first half of Inferno but flamed out dramatically.

The French side travelled to Rio off the back of a fourth-place finish at PGL Bucharest and raced off to a 9-1 lead on Inferno, led by an inspired Lucas "⁠Lucky⁠" Chastang. But the 28-year-old failed to post a single frag in the second half as Falcons took the game by the scruff of the neck.

The victory comes just one day after it was reported that Falcons are set to install Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen as their new in-game leader. The Danish veteran is expected to take over from Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski after Rio.

Falcons grabbed an early lead on Inferno with a flawless pistol round, but things quickly took a turn for the worse. 3DMAX hit back on a force-buy and went on an absolute tear, winning nine rounds in a row without breaking much of a sweat before Falcons secured the final two rounds of the half.

The landscape of the game changed completely once Falcons switched to the T side. They picked 3DMAX's defense apart, losing only one round — in which Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov almost pulled off a 1v4 clutch — in the half before putting the map to rest.

After a quiet showing on Inferno, Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač went 15-4 to power Falcons to an 11-1 lead, with kyxsan then pulling off a pistol clutch to thwart any comeback hopes 3DMAX still had.
