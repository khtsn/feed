soulfly: "I really want to beat Vitality; I want to prove we're different from others"

Aurora are through to the semi-finals of BLAST Open Rotterdam with a 2-0 victory against The MongolZ — snapping a five-match losing streak against the Mongolians.

Speaking to HLTV after the match, Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici said his team "had a really good plan" for the match, and agreed with the notion that Erdenedalai "⁠maaRaa⁠" Bayanbat's absence disrupted The MongolZ.

When you have a coach, he knows when you need to take a timeout, and he also sees the game from the outside," he said. "He can see the five screens. Sometimes, as a player, you cannot see what's going on around you, but the coaching is giving so much help to the in-game leader, to the other players as well.

"What I saw was that the MongolZ were missing the timeouts so much. We had been watching their games live, and I had never seen them taking timeouts, which is really bad. Sometimes, you even need to take a timeout to take their [the opponents'] momentum, you know? I think that was the biggest issue."

soulfly said that the win against The MongolZ is a timely confidence boost ahead of Saturday's semi-final against world No.1 Vitality. Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's troops have had the better of their two encounters this year, but soulfly is not counting his team out.

"I'm also really excited for tomorrow because we're going to face Vitality and I really want to beat them," he said. "We lost to them, and many teams have been losing to them. I want to prove we're different from others. I'm happy."

soulfly, a 2-0 victory and you move on to the semi-finals here in Rotterdam. What are your reactions to that series? It felt like you were in control most of the time.

Honestly, it feels really good because it was also important for Stage 3 invites. I think we had a really good plan as a team. I was really excited today. I'm also really excited for tomorrow because we're going to face Vitality and I really want to beat them. We lost to them, and many teams have been losing to them. I want to prove we're different from others. I'm happy.

The first half of Mirage ended 6-6, but then you just took over the game on the CT side. How confident were you that you'd run away with the game in the second half?

I was pretty much confident because we had a game plan, you know? We knew what we were going to do in the first and second gun rounds. With the pistol win, it felt really good. If they force, you basically win three rounds at the start of the second half.

Day by day it is becoming normal. But still, there is a lot of emotion, obviously. Maybe I didn't show it on the cam, but inside I was really happy

In both maps, it felt like Mongolz were just out of options. Do you think the absence of their coach made a big difference today? Because they couldn't call a pause, talk things over and come up with a new plan. Do you think that played a role in the way things went down?

Yeah, 100%. Because when you have a coach, he knows when you need to take a timeout, and he also sees the game from the outside. He can see the five screens. Sometimes, as a player, you cannot see what's going on around you, but the coaching is giving so much help to the in-game leader, to the other players as well.

What I saw was that the MongolZ were missing the timeouts so much. We had been watching their games live, and I had never seen them taking timeouts, which is really bad. Sometimes, you even need to take a timeout to take their [the opponents'] momentum, you know? I think that was the biggest issue.

Back at EPL, you told us that a lot of emotions were going through your head because it was your first stage win. Now that you have a bit more experience, has it started to feel more normal?

Yeah, day by day it is becoming normal. And today, as I said, was a big win for us because we had been losing to them. But I hope it will become much more normal for me in the next events. But still, there is a lot of emotion, obviously. Maybe I didn't show it on the cam, but inside I was really happy.
