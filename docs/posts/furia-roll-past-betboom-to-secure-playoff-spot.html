FURIA roll past BetBoom to secure playoff spot

FURIA have punched their ticket to the playoff stage of the IEM Cologne Major with a quick 2-0 victory against BetBoom, capping off a near-flawless Stage 3 run that saw them drop only one map.

It was a largely one-sided progression contest, with FURIA never looking likely to cede control. Apart from brief periods of individual brilliance, BetBoom were second best in every department, their issues exposed time and time again.

"It would be amazing if I could win it again ten years later," Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo said after the match about returning to the LANXESS arena, where he won the 2016 and 2017 Cologne tournaments. "I think I would be the first one to win it three times. It would be quite cool.

"I had great moments there in 2016. 2017 was also special because my family came all the way from Brazil. Everyone was watching. It would be a dream come true.

"I still have some things to chase in CS but not many, like Grand Slams and all those things. Whatever comes next, I'll be happy. I'll take anything."

FURIA were off to a flying start on their pick of Overpass as they established a 4-0 lead on the T side. BetBoom then enjoyed a spell of success on the back of a series of multi-kills by Kirill "⁠Magnojez⁠" Rodnov before the Brazilians drew level with a 1v2 clutch by FalleN in the final round.

BetBoom's struggles continued in the second half, when they failed to put up a fight on the T side as FURIA comfortably went on a flawless 8-0 run.

FURIA showed no signs of slowing down as Dust2 kicked off as they jumped out to a 5-0 lead on the CT side. BetBoom fought back in the rest of the half and made the scoreline competitive, but FURIA never lost their grip on the game and quickly put the game to rest.
