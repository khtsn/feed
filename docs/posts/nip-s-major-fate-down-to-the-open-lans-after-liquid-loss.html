NIP's Major fate down to the open LANs after Liquid loss

malbsMd has got his first win in Liquid

Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa has his first win as a Liquid player after the team recovered from 1-0 down to win Mirage in double overtime and run away with the series on Ancient against Ninjas in Pyjamas.

The result has damning implications for Ninjas in Pyjamas, who were hoping to make the most out of B8 and FaZe's Rotterdam elimination by securing some crucial VRS points.

Instead, the trio will go into the tier two open LANs with plenty of work to do to secure a spot in Cologne. After both went 0-2, NIP sit in 17th in the projections, hanging on by the skin of their teeth.

NIP cannot say they did not have their opportunities to win, too, after dominating Nuke and reaching double overtime on Mirage. At this stage in the Major race, a loss to the world number 37 is something they might regret.

r1nkle ace keeps NIP in it

For Liquid, the relief from being out of the European Major race was evident on the server, as new boy malbsMd stepped up with a 1.28 rating on the Mirage marathon and under fire in-game leader Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek top-fragged on Ancient.

Their lack of practice — and game time, given malbsMd last played Nuke in June 2025 — was obvious as Marco "⁠Snappi⁠" Pfeiffer called his favourite Upper execute time and time again on NIP's map pick.

But Liquid wrote it off calmly, won Mirage despite losing both pistols, and were by far the better side on the decider of Ancient.

"He's a very selfless player," siuhy said about malbsMd post-game to the desk. "That's what we've needed, it's what we've been lacking in the last few seasons. We're yet to implement him fully, but so far a lot of positive signs from him and for us. Very happy about it."

He continued: "What we lacked was a back up to the aggressive style. It didn't look like a team. One guy was being aggressive one guy was on a different island and thinking about something else. We're hoping that Mario fills in that gap, not having the separation between an aggressive style and more passive style."

Snappi bemoans decisions that "lack tier one experience"

NIP fumble 5v2

Snappi was honest about the team's issues, cutting a frustrated figure talking to Mike "⁠DarfMike⁠" Winnick. "We have some serious issues closing out games, and it's starting to get really frustrating. We make crucial mistakes at crucial points, really stupid mistakes to be quite frank.

"I don't know how to solve it, a lot of it is just individual decision-making that lacks tier one experience. We are doing some really bad stuff at times, I don't know how many times we should win Mirage but we do overpeeks when we shouldn't, hiding when we shouldn't, it's starting to get on my nerves."

There is still a chance for the Major if NIP perform well at Stake Ranked, but the road has just got much tougher.
