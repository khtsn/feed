Spirit breeze past Falcons for first grand final appearance since August

The support cast was on top of the board in Spirit's convincing wins on Dust2 and Mirage

Spirit have defeated Falcons in a swift 2-0 series to secure the first grand final spot at IEM Rio.

It was not the usual tale of Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets leading the way, as the phenom's impact was not required on either Dust2 or Mirage, where Spirit secured two dominant defensive halves.

Instead, Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev led the way on the former map and Myroslav "⁠zont1x⁠" Plakhotia on the latter, with no one on the other side putting up much of a fight outside of Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov's strong Dust2 presence.

The AWPer was the only one keeping Falcons in the fight thanks to his unpredictable active movement on the CT side, where he was picking off donk in crucial rounds to recover a 0-4 start.

But then the team gave out with just one round on the T side, a halfbuy that broke through the B defense, while Spirit went on a big run to close out map one smoothly.

"It feels a little bit scared to play," Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen said in a mid-series interview on the broadcast. "Spirit is trying to be in our heads and we have to dictate the game. We need to bring the fight to them, we have been giving them too much respect."

If that's what the Danish coach told his team in the talk between maps, it was not seen on the server on Mirage, where Spirit dominated Falcons some more in a solid team-wide performance.

For Spirit, it is a crucial milestone to make it to the grand final already, as they have not played a title decider since they won IEM Cologne and BLAST Bounty back-to-back last August.

Now, they're hoping to go one further and return to the top spot, facing the winner of FURIA vs. Vitality in the best-of-five on championship Sunday.
