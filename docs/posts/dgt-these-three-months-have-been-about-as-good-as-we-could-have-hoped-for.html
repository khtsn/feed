dgt: "These three months have been about as good as we could have hoped for"

9z head into BLAST Open Rotterdam with confidence running high after winning Ace SA Masters 2026 Season 1 — the regional qualifier for the BLAST event — and two LAN events, BetBoom RUSH B Summit Season 2 and BetBoom Circuit X Pantanal Cuiabá.

That sequence of results has catapulted 9z to third in the Americas VRS and all but assured the team of an IEM Cologne berth — the first Major appearance for the Argentinian organization since 2022.

"We've achieved goals we thought were impossible," Franco "⁠dgt⁠" Garcia told HLTV on BLAST Rotterdam's media day. "We haven't played at a Major in four years, since 2022, and so quickly, out of nowhere, the impossible has happened. We’re very happy, but we want to keep growing and improving."

The Uruguayan, who moved back to 9z in January, also gave his thoughts on paiN's inability to build on the semi-final run at the Austin Major, which culminated in the team moving on from him and David "⁠dav1deuS⁠" Tapia Maldonado at the end of the year and returning to an all-Brazilian lineup.

Looking ahead to Thursday's match with Vitality, the Uruguayan issued a warning to the world No.1. "If we see that they’re not at their best, we’ll beat them," he said.

It's been three months since you returned to 9z. How would you sum up this period?

I think we’ve achieved a lot of really good goals. I didn’t actually expect everything to happen so quickly, but the team has improved a lot. I think these three months have been about as good as we could have hoped for But obviously we want to keep growing and improving so we can come here and play more tournaments on a regular basis and play against the best teams in the world, like FURIA and paiN do, or like Legacy is doing right now, to keep growing and see just how high we can reach.

When you returned to 9z, you were almost outside the top 100 in the VRS. Now, you're ranked 19th, and you're a lock for the Major. Are you surprised with the team's meteoric rise?

Yes. I would be lying if I told you otherwise. I think we’re, as I said, improving a lot, very quickly. I think we have a lot of good things going, and that has allowed us to improve a lot. And we're playing without pressure, which for me is very important. And because of that, we've achieved goals we thought were impossible. We haven't played at a Major in four years, since 2022, and so quickly, out of nowhere, the impossible has happened. We’re very happy, but we want to keep growing and improving.

You've just won three tournaments, two of them on LAN. However, this will be your first big test of the year. What expectations do you have?

To be perfectly honest, I don’t think we have any expectations. Our team is still very new. We’ve got three players who haven’t made it to a Major yet, and three who haven’t played tier-1 CS for a while.

We don’t have many expectations, but obviously, we want to come to compete and see what level we’re at. I think the level in South America has improved a lot. We’re all playing very well. Obviously, the level here is what we're aiming for.

It was something we’d been aiming for for a long time. It will improve us a lot and will help 9z a lot, too

One of the biggest changes to the team recently was meyern's switch to the AWP. How do you think he's settled into that role?

He switched roles not too long ago, and he's still learning. He has improved massively and he’s a really laid-back guy; he doesn’t take up much space on the team and he plays really well, so I think it’s been great. He’s still finding his feet in the role, but he’s improving really quickly. I think he will keep getting better. With time, everyone will keep improving, and we will build a much stronger team.

 As you said, you haven’t played in a Major since 2022, and now 9z's spot in Cologne is practically guaranteed. What would it mean for 9z — as a team and as an organisation — to return to a Major, especially given that we’ll have a Major in Buenos Aires next year?

For me, playing a Major in Buenos Aires would be… I’ve played in a Major semi-final before, so qualifying for this Major is really important to me. It’s like when FalleN qualified for Rio. He’s obviously the greatest. I’m talking only about my own feelings, you know? About playing on home soil. I’m from Uruguay, but I feel a bit like Argentina is my home. And, obviously, it’s very important to me, but we must not put pressure on ourselves.

And obviously, qualifying for the Cologne Major was very important for us. It was something we’d been aiming for for a long time. It will improve us a lot and will help 9z a lot, too.

Speaking about you individually, you've had a very strong start to the year, with an average 1.22 rating. It hasn't been the best of opposition, but those are still some strong numbers. Do you feel you're returning to your best form?

 Yes, I feel that... I think, above all, rather than focusing on my individual performance, I feel I help the team a lot. And that’s what matters most to me. Numbers will follow naturally. Obviously, we've not been playing against top teams, but we’ve been playing against the best in Brazil all the time because we've been reaching every final. And having good numbers is good, too, because it shows I’m on the right track.

I have to keep going down this path because sometimes I make mistakes and go down the wrong path, and sometimes it’s hard for me to realize and say, ‘Fran, what are you doing? You’ve got to go this way.’

Expectations were really high when you and dav1deuS joined paiN, but the team couldn't get results consistently. At the end of the year, paiN decided went back to a full Brazilian lineup, which suggested that there had been communication or cultural issues. In your opinion, what went wrong with that project?

I didn’t have any communication problems. Obviously, it was a bit different at the start, but I don’t think it was down to communication, nor was it a cultural issue. In fact, for me, in terms of friendship, they are really great guys, and I got on with them really well.

Inside the game, there were a few different roles, and there wasn’t that much space. And I felt like we had a few players with similar roles. We didn’t manage to get much practice in Europe with the squad either. We lacked time. I felt the players lacked a bit of confidence and freedom.

And when we started losing, we wanted to improve, but I felt that, 'Well, we've made it to the semi-finals of the Major, great, but we didn't do well in the next tournament. What can we do to improve?' And instead of gaining more freedom and continuing to grow together, I felt as though we lost a bit of trust, lost a bit of freedom, and everything went downhill. And we were never able to recover.
