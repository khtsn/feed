Meet the IEM Cologne Major 2026 rookies

Five rookie cores debuting in Germany add a significant influx of new Major players.

There are 31 rookies ready to make their Major debut at IEM Cologne, a number inflated by the fact that there are five teams with three or more first-time Major players. 9z comes in with a core of three rookies, two of those teams, FUT and Monte come into the Major with four new rookies, and two more, Sharks and SINNERS, come in with full fives.

This influx of teams with a majority of new players marks a renewed influx of rookies. When the Valve-backed events expanded to 32 teams in Austin, the number of first-time Major players skyrocketed to 42, but then dipped down to 19 in Hungary.

The majority of this Major's rookies are outside of the scope of our prospect ranking, but there will be prospect representation across all three stages, with FUT contributing nearly half of the nine top youngsters making their Major debut in Cologne.

Stage 1 will see the brunt of newcomers make their debuts, as is expected, with 17 new players in the opening round. The second round will have fewer but similar numbers, with 12, and for the first time since the VRS was put into effect and the Major was expanded to 32 teams, we will have multiple rookies in Stage 3.

Stage 3

Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici

The third phase of the Major is reserved for the best of the best, and few are the rookies who get to begin their Major journeys on a top 8 team. At the last Major, in Budapest, it was only the much hyped Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin who made his debut in this stage after a highly anticipated move to Falcons.

This time around, soulfly and xelex get the honors of starting in the final of three group stages, just three victories away from the playoffs, but the way they got here was not the same.

soulfly joined Aurora ahead of the 2026 season and has been a supportive element in making sure that the Turkish team remains in the top 10. The 21-year-old has posted a 1.01 rating during the season leading up to IEM Cologne as an Anchor-Lurker and has been a steady presence in Aurora's continuing to be usual playoff contenders, even if they haven't reached the heights they did in 2024 and 2025.

xelex sprung to fourth in our prospect ranking ahead of his Major debut

xelex on the other hand was MOUZ's academy golden boy, the star player with the best roles and a 1.18 rating to show for it. He was brought onto the team late in the season during a big shake-up that saw Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius join on loan as Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin and Jimi "⁠Jimpphat⁠" Salo were moved to the bench in hopes of staving off stagnation.

Coming into the Major from the substitute position, he will not have his own sticker just yet, but make no mistake. The young Hungarian, only 17 years old and #4 on the Top 50 Prospects ranking after a jump from #17 since graduating from the academy to the main team, has already gotten praise from teammates and shown that he can handle himself on big stages just as well as in studio environments, and his 1.01 rating in MOUZ's third-place run at PGL Astana was followed by a 1.18 in the third-place finish at CAC.

Stage 2

Džiugas "⁠dziugss⁠" Steponavičius (#1)

Matias "⁠HUASOPEEK⁠" Ibañez Hernandez

The Stage 2 rookies are, for the most part, the cores of three teams: FUT, 9z, and Monte. The only solo player here is HLTV Prospects graduate ryu, who aged out as a young up-and-coming player last season and has been a part of Astralis' resurgence as a top 10 team since he joined at the beginning of the year.

FUT is, without a doubt, one of the most exciting teams not just at the Major, but in all of Counter-Strike. The international European squad is made up of five youngsters, four of whom had no experience at the top level before 2026 and have blossomed into a fringe top 10 team with a notable title to their name.

Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea, whose previous experience excluded him from being a top 50 prospect, is the only player on the team who is not a rookie going into IEM Cologne, and the only one not among the world's top prospects. dziugss (#1), dem0n (#3), Krabeni (#6), and cmtry (#13) are all going to graduate the prospect list after the Major, with dziugss having become the top prospect and cmtry having secured an MVP award at PGL Bucharest.

FUT's four Major rookies and HLTV prospects at BLAST Rivals

9z, whose Maximiliano "⁠max⁠" Gonzalez and Franco "⁠dgt⁠" Garcia have already played Majors, will procure a core of three rookies in meyern, luchov, and HUASOPEEK. meyern, once a subject of our One for the future series, is reshaping his career as an AWPer for La Violeta, where he has a 1.04 rating since joining.

Of the three players, luchov, averaging a 1.19 rating since joining the team in November, has been the biggest revelation, especially after showing fantastic form with a 1.29-rated effort in 9z's 5-8th place finish at PGL Astana.

HUASOPEEK, who joined 9z in 2023, right after the team's outburst in the international scene with two Major appearances in 2022, will finally get his flowers in the form of a beautiful IEM Cologne sticker. A well-deserved reward for the Major rookie, who has posted a 1.10 rating since joining the Latin American team.

Monte also bring a core of four Major rookies to Cologne

The last core in Stage 3 is that of Monte, with four players making their Major debut: afro, Gizmy, AZUWU, and Rainwaker, as well as Aurimas "⁠Bymas⁠" Pipiras making his return to Counter-Strike's biggest event since playing his first and only Major with MOUZ in 2021 at PGL Stockholm.

This Monte team made up of two UK players and three players who have been under big name organizations in the past but got discarded along the way — Bymas in MOUZ, afro in fnatic, and Rainwaker in Liquid —, has been toiling in the lower end of the VRS ranking since mid-2025 and has grinded its way to a Stage 2 spot at the Major, making it a favorite for those keen on both underdog and comeback stories.

Stage 1, with a mix of cores and solo players, is where the brunt of rookies will begin their Major journeys. From full international teams in Europe and the Americas to a duo from APAC and a player from China, all VRS regions will be represented by newcomers in this stage. Notably missing is the North American subregion, with no rookies at all.

SINNERS' SHOCK and beastik were two rounds away from qualifying for the PGL Antwerp Major with a 14-11 lead in the Inferno decider of their final match of the RMR, but Bad News Eagles came back and snatched that spot in a historic moment for the Kosovar and Albanian CS scene. Now, the Czech duo will be immortalized in the game alongside three more rookies in teammates MoDo, stressarN, and kisserek.

The other whole team of rookies is Sharks, whose koala is one of the most exciting young players in Brazil and holds the #18 spot in our latest prospect ranking update. The 1.14-rated prospect isn't the highest on the team, however, as 23-year-old doc sports a 1.17 since joining the team in 2023. rdnzao and gafolo, also from Brazil, and Argentine AWPer maxxkor complete the neophyte team.

The other rookie prospects in this group are TYLOO's Zero (#42), China's only rookie at IEM Cologne, BIG's 20-year-old AWPer, gr1ks (#26), and our youngest rookie at IEM Cologne, 16-year-old s1zzi, who just made the prospect ranking for the first time in 32nd place after 1.10 and 1.12-rated performances at IEM Rio and IEM Atlanta before dipping to 0.90 at CAC in his last three events.

Rounding out the list are TjP and asap from THUNDER dOWNUNDER, who are on the team with the old FlyQuest core and Major veterans Christopher "⁠dexter⁠" Nong, Alistair "⁠aliStair⁠" Johnston and Jay "⁠Liazz⁠" Tregillgas, as the two APAC rookies at the event. asap, in particular, has been a menace in the Asia and Oceania region, averaging a 1.23 rating since joining the team in October 2025.
