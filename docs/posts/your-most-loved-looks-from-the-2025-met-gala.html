Your Most Loved Looks From the 2025 Met Gala

One night, $31 million, hundreds of celebrities and your six favorite looks.

On Monday, stars including Madonna and Rihanna walked down the blue carpet to celebrate the Metropolitan Museum of Art’s Costume Institute show “Superfine: Tailoring Black Style.”

Every year we round up photos from the Met Gala. But this year we gave readers the ability to choose their favorite looks from the carpet. And last night, 30,000 readers like you hit the save button 100,000 times. For over five hours, we tracked which looks were the most loved.

Our Coverage of the 2025 Met Gala

  • Dressing for the Met: The gala’s blue carpet style showcased Diljit Dosanjh’s nod to a maharaja from the Punjab region of India, Janelle Monáe as a “time-traveling dandy,” Ryan Coogler’s lifelong fashion dream of a cumberbund and Anna Sawai’s changed-up look.

  • Sculpted Curls, Dramatic Trains and No Pants: The themes of the night included hair styles seemingly inspired by the singer Josephine Baker, appendage-like trains and lots of leg.

  • Kamala Harris’s Surprise Appearance: The former vice president skipped the carpet, but slipped into the gala in a black silk gown in one of her first major public appearances since the 2024 election.

  • Rihanna at the Gala: Fashionably late, the pop star seemed to confirm rumors she was pregnant when she met shouts of congratulations with: “Thank you!”

  • Diddy's Shadow: Sean Combs was lauded by Anna Wintour, was a regular guest at the gala, and his influence on the current exhibition is undeniable despite awaiting trial on charges of racketeering and sex trafficking.

  • Black Dandy Fashion: It’s easy to forget that the Met Gala isn’t just an expensive-looking celebrity parade: It’s also the opening celebration for a museum show. This year, the party will inaugurate “Superfine: Tailoring Black." Here are 7 of its highlights.

  • Black Male Style and the War on D.E.I.: The Met’s show, which focuses on the way Black men have used fashion as a tool of self-actualization, revolution and subversion, has taken on an entirely different relevance now that the federal government is openly hostile to programming related to race.
