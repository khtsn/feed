xertioN: "I was a bit worried I would be this IGL that is just a burden, but so far, I'm feeling pretty comfortable"

MOUZ's new captain is happy with his fragging while adjusting to his role and changes within the team.

Despite taking over captaincy duties starting with PGL Astana, Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman hasn't lost his individual luster. The Israeli rifler finished atop the board in both of his team's wins over Gentle Mates (1.52 rating) and G2 (1.29 rating).

"It's only been two weeks since I took over the role, but I think it has been a blessing so far," xertioN told HLTV after posting a 20-4 K-D on the CT side of the Inferno decider against G2. "In the past, I was very confident about my ability to understand the vision of Counter-Strike that teams are playing, but now I think it's at an even deeper level. We're really benefiting from that as a team."

Adjusting to the new role comes with further challenges on top of a stretch of "11-hour workdays" that xertioN endured over the first week, with the 21-year-old admitting he has to work on the emotional side of his game.

"I was always a little bit on the emotional side as a player, I was also tilting a bit, but I'm now trying to make sure that it's in a good balance so that I'm not shouting at the guys every round that it happens," he said. "I think that I need to learn how to manage my emotions better."

He also touched on what Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius and Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze bring to the team, stating that the former's presence on stage was much-needed and the change was a necessary one for MOUZ to actively take a step towards challenging the likes of Vitality, and how he is taking better care of his personal health as he moves into a new chapter in his career.

Listen to the interview here.

Congratulations, a fragging IGL performance on Inferno to close out over G2. You seem like you're pretty comfortable in that position after this change, so just tell me how you're feeling with that.

Yeah, definitely. Obviously I didn't set out to be that guy who has these performances, but it's a great bonus for the team. I would say that even though I'm performing well, I'm still very focused on how the team should play on both sides.

I'm calling both sides now, which is very demanding, so I need to really focus on what we're seeing, what they are doing, what we're doing, and stuff like that. It's a great feeling to also not be... like, I was a bit worried I would now be this IGL that is just a burden for the team, but so far, two games, I'm feeling pretty comfortable.

This step was necessary for us to take a step forward as a team

Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman

It's a big change for you, and yeah, you were calling a bit on CT side before, but when you're doing it all it's not the same. How has the transition been for you and how does the role feel?

First of all, it's only been two weeks since I took over the role, but I think it has been a blessing so far. For me personally, I think I'm understanding the game at an even higher level. In the past, I was very confident about my ability to understand the vision of Counter-Strike that teams are playing, but now I think it's at an even deeper level. We're really benefiting from that as a team. Me and Denis [sycrone] work super well together, and it's just a joy to have a coach like him as my first coach as an IGL. I feel really ready for that, and this step was necessary for us to take a step forward as a team.

How is the stress management going?

I'll be honest, it's a bit tough (laughs). The first week and a half of prac, we were working our asses off. Especially me and sycrone, making sure the system that we want to play because for me, it's really important that we have his vision in our game because I think he understands the philosophy of CS at the highest level. We were talking a lot after practices, I had 11-hour workdays basically every day for the first week, so it was super tough but we managed.

We are as prepared as we can be coming into this tournament, and with the stress, yeah, when we are losing stupid rounds, I just want to smash the table to be honest (smiles). I was always a little bit on the emotional side as a player, I was also tilting a bit, but I'm now trying to make sure that it's in a good balance so that I'm not shouting at the guys every round that it happens. But I'll be honest, today, even if we're winning 11-1 and we lost this stupid anti-eco on B, I was a bit upset, so yeah, I think that I need to learn how to manage my emotions better.

jL really brings something we were lacking in terms of this presence that he has

Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman

How do you work through that? Trying to figure out how to manage those emotions while doing this transition and still regularly competing at big events.

The most key part that people underestimate is your health as a player, and I think that I have not been the most healthy in my life. I am now taking active steps to have more of a healthy lifestyle, so that I'm feeling better, the mind feels better, I have more energy, and I'm feeling more confident about myself. That is a really important thing for an IGL. I'm actively taking steps with my sports psychologist on how we see fit, and that is going well so far.

While you've gone through the change, the team has also had a couple of changes. jL is in for two events, but you've also brought in xelex as this academy prospect. Without getting into the individual side, what do you make of the changes overall?

Without getting into individual players, I think that our ceiling is much higher now. We have a superstar in jL, I would say, because I think he really brings something we were lacking in terms of this presence that he has. People call it 'aura' on LAN (smiles), but it is more like his confidence, and I think that he is a bit arrogant in a good way. Like playing against opponents, not necessarily in the team, but more when we play against people, and that is something really positive to have in the team.

So he was a great addition, and just in general, the fresh blood of having xelex in the team as well just helps the team feel more fresh now. We are much more fired up. Unfortunately, maybe we didn't end the best with the last roster, but this change was definitely needed for us to take the active step in actually beating Vitality or trying to do something against these guys.

Tell me more about xelex, obviously an academy prospect who isn't very experienced, but touted as one of those who has potential and has come through the MOUZ NXT pipeline that has shown it can bring people up. What are you seeing from him?

Before he joined, the management and Denis had a lot of praise for him. Obviously I had never seen him play except for [when he stood in at] Katowice, but I think that he has incredible aim, that goes without saying. You can see it easily when you are watching him play the game.

I think most players around his age at the moment have that aim, so the bonuses he has are that he is a very nice person to be around. He is vocal, but not too confident yet. He needs to get more comfortable in the team, but I definitely think he has great ideas, a great mentality to improve, and he knows he's here to learn from other players, which I think is really beneficial for him.

Now that I'm more of an experienced player, I'm trying to help him as much as possible to be comfortable in the game, but of course he needs a little bit of time, just like every young talent — except donk basically — to get to the scene and actually show his true potential. But so far, I'm super impressed by his output.

With me coming in as IGL, we have more direction in the game. I am much more vocal than any other IGL I've ever had in the past

Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman

You said that a change was necessary to get over this hurdle of beating Vitality, to get these important results in playoff games, so why do you think this is the change that will be able to do that?

Hmm, why... I mean, first of all, we are now kind of hoping; we don't know yet of course. We need to play on stage, we need to play these playoffs games, and maybe the first ones will not be easy, or maybe they will be, we don't know.

With me coming in as IGL, we have more direction in the game. I am much more vocal than any other IGL I've ever had in the past. I'm really trying to make the game comfortable for my teammates, and sometimes at the sake of my voice, maybe. But I think that is just the way we do it, and now me and Denis together have the best chemistry that we can have to lead the guys.

Overall, as I highlighted about Justinas [jL], he has that stage presence, which we definitely need. We're 100% still far away from Vitality, it's not even in our mindset to beat these guys in a consistent manner because they are just the best. So for us it's more about just building our way up, and maybe also winning some VRS points on the way because we are pretty down the list right now. That's it; taking it a step at a time.
