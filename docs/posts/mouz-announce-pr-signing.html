MOUZ announce PR signing

The Czech star returns to MOUZ after previously representing the German organization at an academy level.

MOUZ have announced that they have completed the signing of Oldřich "⁠PR⁠" Nový from GamerLegion, finalizing a roster overhaul that began in April with the double-benching of Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin and Jimi "⁠Jimpphat⁠" Salo.

After fielding Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius as a stand-in at PGL Astana and the CS Asia Championships, MOUZ ended the season with a 12th-14th place finish at the IEM Cologne Major, where Brollan made his final appearance with the team.

During the off-season, the German organization bid farewell to Brollan and Jimpphat, who moved to HEROIC and Aurora, respectively

PR is no stranger to MOUZ, having played for the organization's academy team, MOUZ NXT, between July 2023 and January 2025, when he was transferred to GamerLegion. He has garnered considerable international experience while playing under GamerLegion, attending a host of tier-1 LAN events, including the Budapest and Cologne Majors.

The Czech player will be hoping to rediscover the form that saw him heavily linked with a transfer away from GamerLegion at the end of last year. He has struggled for consistency this year, averaging a 1.05 rating on LAN (down from 1.13 in 2025).

"I am very excited about PR's return," Jan Dominicus, MOUZ's Chief of Business Development, told HLTV. "Not only does he fit the position very well, but he has also known our team for a long time – and our team knows him just as well.

"We initially came close to promoting him from our NXT team, but ultimately decided to bring in additional experience for the roster. Now, 18 months later, PR has proven he can compete at the highest level, and we are looking to take the next step together.

"It also makes us proud that we are able to integrate another player from our NXT project, bringing us back to four of our five players – plus our coach – having come through our own ranks. A great testament to our continued commitment to player development."

PR will make his MOUZ debut next week as MOUZ will compete in the online stage of BLAST Bounty Season 2. Next month, the team will travel to Paris to play at the Esports World Cup.
