FaZe dumped out of BLAST Open by TYLOO, leaving Major berth in danger

TYLOO did just enough to consign FaZe to an embarrassing early elimination in BLAST Open Rotterdam.

TYLOO have sent FaZe crashing out of BLAST Open Rotterdam by virtue of a 2-1 series victory, winning on Mirage (13-11) and Overpass (13-11) either side off a loss on Ancient (8-13).

FaZe will be left ruing the fact some of their stars let them down today. Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen and even Helvijs "⁠broky⁠" Saukants put up some high-impact moments, but Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken had a woefully poor series and David "⁠frozen⁠" Čerňanský struggled to find impact on the final map.

TYLOO kicked off the opener of Mirage in dominant fashion, racing out to an 8-0 T-side lead off the back of Yi "⁠JamYoung⁠" Yang and Su "⁠Zero⁠" Jingshen's powerful performances. FaZe cobbled together some rounds to give themselves hope at half-time, and broky pulled off a nasty clutch early in the second half to continue the comeback, but it was ultimately not enough.

broky hits outrageous prefire one-tap in 1v1

FaZe started Ancient in far better form, building a 5-0 lead off the back of a T-side pistol win, before Jingxiang "⁠Mercury⁠" Wang and Qianhao "⁠Moseyuh⁠" Chen combined to get TYLOO on the board and guide them to a 6-6 half.

A pistol win greeted TYLOO at the start of their T side, but once guns came up FaZe were in full control and strolled to victory, with karrigan of all people doing the bulk of the heavy lifting.

karrigan clutches 1v2 with AWP

TYLOO came out of the gates swinging on the T side of Overpass, their up-tempo approach leaving FaZe reeling early in the half. karrigan was once again FaZe's saviour, his multi-kill B holds getting his team back into the game to at least give them a chance heading into the second half.

broky and karrigan stepped up for FaZe at the start of the second half, giving their team the lead with high-impact frags in multiple rounds, but it was all for nought. A loss to a half-buy heralded FaZe's downfall, and Moseyuh and Mercury picked up a string of multi-kill rounds to guide TYLOO to a narrow victory.

Snappy Mercury 4K puts TYLOO on series point

FaZe's Major spot in doubt

Already FaZe's season had been dreadful thus far, and now the team sport a 3-11 series record across 2026. Their poor form has left the team staring at the prospect of not making the IEM Cologne Major at all.

We now have everything in our own hands, and if we don't qualify for the Major, we didn't deserve to go

Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen

The team have signed up for DraculaN Season 6, which is currently the only tournament they have lined up before the cut off for Major invites. The team desperately need a deep run in that event to secure their attendance at the season's marquee event.

karrigan spoke to the analyst desk after the match, and he was frank in his assessment. "It is in our own hands, simple as that. We were close to maybe not going to that event [DraculaN], and I said it was best to go. We now have everything in our own hands, and if we don't qualify for the Major, we didn't deserve to go."
