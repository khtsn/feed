ISSAA hangs up the mouse: "My impact can now be greater from a coaching role"

Former HellRaisers and OG rifler Issa "⁠ISSAA⁠" Murad has retired from competitive play, and has moved his focus onto a coaching career as an assistant, analyst, or head coach.

"It was honestly one of the hardest decisions I’ve had to make, because Counter-Strike has been my life for many years," the Jordanian tells HLTV.

ISSAA: "Counter-Strike is in my blood"

"Over the past months, I took the time to be very honest with myself about where I can bring the most value today. I reflected deeply on my experiences — the pressure moments, playing multiple roles, leading teams, and especially what I learned during periods where I was an IGL.

"Through that process, I realized that my impact can now be greater from a coaching role, where building, guiding, and developing players motivates me more than competing myself.

"I’ve been that young player once. I know what players feel in crucial moments, how confidence is built and broken, and when the right feedback can make the biggest difference. That understanding only comes from having lived it. Right now, I feel that I can give more to the game as a coach than I can as a player."

ISSAA at the 2024 Copenhagen RMR with JiJieHao

ISSAA played at three Majors and won an MVP at DreamHack Open Tours in 2018, but his playing career stalled during and after the pandemic.

His star rose under Tomáš "⁠oskar⁠" Šťastný and Kyrylo "⁠ANGE1⁠" Karasiow in HellRaisers, where they placed second at the aforementioned DH Tours, and he was one of the first pieces in OG's entry to Counter-Strike in 2019.

ISSAA was benched in 2021 after struggling to play online from his home in Jordan and moved to VALORANT in 2022 while he finished university, before returning to CS with JiJieHao in 2023.

He continued: "One of my long-term goals as a coach is to finish what I couldn’t achieve as a player — to compete for and win the biggest titles, including a Major, but in a different role this time."
