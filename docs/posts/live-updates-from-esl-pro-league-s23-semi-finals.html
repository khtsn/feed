Live updates from ESL Pro League S23 semi-finals

Astralis's map pick of Nuke is set to kick off the second semi-final matchup of ESL Pro League Season 23, followed by Aurora's choice of Dust2. Should a decider be necessary, the two sides will duke it out on Inferno to end the series.

On quarter-final against Legacy: "It's always hard to play against Legacy, when we play against them, it's always going to third maps and overtime and that sort of stuff. We managed to win, even though on Overpass we choked a bit."

On soulfly: "I think he's a cool guy. He doesn't shout a lot, he doesn't scream a lot, and he dosen't get nervous a lot. He's just doing his job in the server."

On Astralis matchup: "I think I can say they're playing good individually, phzy, ryu, and jabbi, their performances increased a lot. I think we should be careful about them.

FUT captain Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija spoke with HLTV shortly after his team's loss to Natus Vincere to share his feelings following their semi-final elimination.

On feeling after loss: "I think... shit... nothing kind of good. We had it in our hands on Dust2 T-side. If we could win after the Scout round, then we would have had a chance."

On why FUT lost; "On Mirage they had a good plan on how to punish us in Middle. We also did some bad mistakes, I would say. On Dust2 6-2 round we had a 2v1 clutch that we played one by one, late rounds we also fucked up a bit. Nothing we could do."

On feelings about overall result: "Maybe the team feels proud, but for me this is not that an insane achievement to go to the semis. For me, I know we can do more."

Enough said.

Nothing really went the way of FUT as Natus Vincere kept meeting them head-on in their mid-aggression and getting away with the advantage every time.

The only rounds FUT contested for outside of their two round wins were a couple of straight A executes in low buys.

"Let's enjoy and don't get fucking frustrated," a listen-in from before the match started showed up just as Natus Vincere clinched a 7-0 lead.

Safe to say there's nothing to be frustrated about for now.

"NAVI's weak point is that they are mentally not a stable team, I can say," András "⁠coolio⁠" Fercsák told Tres "⁠stunna⁠" Saranthus ahead of the match. "If we start the game good, they will get in their head and then I think we can beat them."

"Their strong part is the game plan. B1ad3 is a smart coach, he was probably preparing heavily, trying to find our weak point, but it's not easy," he went on with a smirk.

NAVI Junior broke off from the organization to join FUT seven months ago to make sure they could compete on the tier-one circuit, and it didn't take long for them to do just that.

Seven months later, the students have a chance to surpass the masters in their first-ever meeting.
