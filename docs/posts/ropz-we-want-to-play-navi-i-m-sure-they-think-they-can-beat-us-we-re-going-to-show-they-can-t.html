ropz: "We want to play NAVI. I'm sure they think they can beat us; we're going to show they can't"

On the hunt for their third title of 2026, Vitality marched through the group stage of BLAST Open Rotterdam without dropping a map, taking down the likes of PARIVISION and The MongolZ along the way.

Even still, Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool says that the team has more to improve. "With the game with PARIVISION, it was kind of issues with communication, just small things missing here and there," he shared regarding the team's bout with PARIVISION in the Group B upper-bracket final.

"We won that game on kind of [an] individual [basis]. You know, ZywOo 1v5 and that sort of stuff."

Now, after trouncing Aurora with back-to-back 13-5 map victories in the Dutch event's semi-final, ropz feels much better about Vitality's showing. "I think today, the result speaks for itself. I think today we did much better."

Now, ropz has his sights set on the grand final, where Vitality will face off against either Natus Vincere or PARIVISION in a best-of-five clash.

"We want to play NAVI," ropz said about his preferred grand final matchup. "They are feeling good. They won EPL.

"They have a bit of confidence, and I'm sure they think they can beat us. But, you know, we're here. We're going to be there, and we're going to show them that they can't."

A quick 2-0 victory and you're heading to the grand final here in Rotterdam. Give me your reactions to the series.

My reaction would be just feeling good in a way that it was not a tough game, which we kind of expected. The last time we met Aurora, it was with Ancient, and it was a tough battle, and today, it was a bit easier. Again, we have a really good flameZ in form, just dominating, and I think it helps us so much because he's an aggressive rifler and he's getting so many openings and so many moves that are working for him. He's creating so much space for everyone. I mean, if you have that kind of form, it just feels amazing to play.

Aurora picked Inferno after winning six games in a row on this map, but you were in control from the beginning. How confident were you, heading into that game?

I think we totally expected the veto, we saw they had a six-game win streak, so I think our prediction was that '99%, they will go for Inferno'. So I think we had good prep going into it. We just looked at what had worked for them because they had been winning Infernos, and they had some things that were working, so we had to just see if we could counter them and find some moves that worked for us.

Going in, I think we made a good prep. We knew what the veto was going to be, and that's what helped us.

When I spoke with apEX after the match against PARIVISION, he said that it had not been a great game from the team. What did you do during the break to prepare for this one? Do you think today was a better showing and something much closer to your A-game?

I think with the game with PARIVISION, it was kind of issues with communication, just small things missing here and there. We weren't connecting as well as a team, but I think with the PARIVISION game, we said that we won that game on kind of [an] individual [basis]. You know, ZywOo 1v5 and that sort of stuff, so I think that's what we wanted to improve on coming into this series, that we didn't really show our best team game in the PARIVISION one. And I think today, the result speaks for itself. I think today we did much better. It was good.

I think flameZ is probably above donk at this point, he's playing so well

Once again, a fantastic performance from flameZ. It feels kind of unfair when you have someone like him and ZywOo playing like this at the same time.

Exactly. I mean, I don't even know if I've been in a situation like this in my career, and I've been playing for, like, ten years. And you have, probably for a year, like, two of the best players right now. I think flameZ is probably above donk at this point. He's playing so well. But, you know, we'll see how the year goes. I'm not doing any predictions now, but I think it is just so easy to play. My job is a lurker. I barely see people nowadays, like in this year. I don't get many chances. You have ZywOo popping off, flameZ popping off, both showing incredible numbers. It's just, yeah, it's incredible to play with those guys.

And now, NAVI or PARIVISION. You haven't faced NAVI since Budapest, where you beat them in two close maps. You've played PARIVISION two times this year, but some of those games were very close. Any preferences?

I guess we want to play NAVI. They are feeling good. They won EPL. They have a bit of confidence, and I'm sure they think they can beat us. But, you know, we're here. We're going to be there, and we're going to show that they can't, but the final will decide that. They still have a match to go, but I think we're hoping to play NAVI.
