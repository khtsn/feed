kyousuke: "People are saying I have more motivation when I'm playing with karrigan, I think maybe it's true"

Falcons arrive in Cologne after two consecutive runners-up finishes at their first events with Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen. Limited practice time before the events meant that the Danish captain had to call on the fly during games, and though finishing second to Spirit in Astana was a solid result, losing to Legacy in the final of the Chinese event came as a blow.

"Even if we [had] won in China, [we] wouldn't be 100% satisified because it's not a full 100% tier-one tournament," Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin told HLTV on the media day ahead of Stage 3. "If we [had] won Astana, everyone would feel less pressure in the team and each player would feel less pressure. (Laughs) But we met Legacy in China and we lost; it was kinda frustrating."

The 18-year-old rifler has shown an uptick in his playoff form since karrigan's arrival, something which he attributes to the atmosphere the Dane has brought to the team.

"I am the guy who is like, I think more than everyone, depending on the mood and the atmosphere in the team," kyousuke said. "If the atmosphere is bad, I will play bad- not bad, but I could play better if it's a good atmosphere. I think Finn helps a lot when it comes to energy and atmosphere, he helps everyone, he makes a lot of jokes, and he brings a lot of good mood during the games."

Welcome to the Major. You told me you had a bootcamp coming into this, so what was the preparation like ahead of your appearance?

Just adding some stuff because we didn't have a lot of stuff during Astana and China. We had a couple of rounds, and Finn [karrigan] was freestyling about these rounds. We added some stuff, and it went good.

Yeah, let's talk about CAC and Astana. It was your start with karrigan, and as you said, it was working on the fly a little bit. What was that experience like for you?

It was kind of crazy because Finn had a lot of stuff on each map, but he was just freestyling about this stuff, he was faking, faking, like karrigan style, you know? (laughs) It was the first time I've played with this kind of guy, who can freestyle these rounds, and we had a lot of freedom, I would say, during these rounds with what we can do. It went good for us in Astana; in China I thought we were going to win, and everyone thought we were going to win, so it was a bit frustrating after China, but it's still a good result.

If we [had] won Astana, everyone would feel less pressure in the team and each player would feel less pressure

Tell me about that frustration a bit. Obviously NiKo had that whiff and he was really upset with himself about that, but for you, it's been four finals in Falcons and never a trophy. What has the frustration been like trying to hunt that down?

I think even if we [had] won in China, [we] wouldn't be 100% satisified because it's not a full 100% tier-one tournament. If we [had] won Astana, everyone would feel less pressure in the team and each player would feel less pressure. (Laughs) But we met Legacy in China and we lost; it was kinda frustrating.

How important is it for you to get that win? Falcons haven't really had that trophy, m0NESY especially, always second-place [in Falcons], so how important is it to break through that at some point?

I think it's very important for us to get a trophy, like a real tier-one trophy, and win [against] at least a couple of tier-one teams in playoffs because everyone will feel less pressure. And if you win one trophy, you can just get like Vitality era, you know? They lost one tournament in the beginning, and after they just win everything, it was kinda snowball effect. You win one trophy and you just feel more comfortable and more confident in your games. I think it would help us a lot.

Let's talk about you and how your development in the team has gone. Initially, you had a couple of good tournaments, then struggled to find your place a bit [in playoffs]. But with karrigan, you're really finding your form. How has that been, what has changed for you with him coming into the team?

Overall, I think since karrigan joined us, I don't know what happened for me. But I think he's just a good guy and he brings a lot of energy and atmosphere. I think I'm the guy who is depending on team atmosphere and team energy, and I think he brings a lot of jokes and some stuff when it comes to energy. He just helps me a lot during the game; for example, if I start the game not the best, he will help me, he will go and say, 'I will jump for you,' and it just helps me. He's a good guy and I appreciate him for this.

I am the guy who is like, I think more than everyone, depending on the mood and the atmosphere in the team [...] I think Finn helps a lot

I've noticed your personality seems a lot more bubbly with karrigan. How much has he changed the environment for you, in terms of the mood?

Yeah, I am the guy who is like, I think more than everyone, depending on the mood and the atmosphere in the team. If the atmosphere is bad, I will play bad- not bad, but I could play better if it's a good atmosphere. I think Finn helps a lot when it comes to energy and atmosphere, he helps everyone, he makes a lot of jokes, and he brings a lot of good mood during the games. He can scream, he can bring a lot of energy.

He told me in Astana that he's seeing you work really hard, and some of the impressions i got from some of the other players is that you seem more motivated with karrigan coming in. Why is that?

I don't know (laughs). When people are saying I have more motivation when I'm playing with karrigan, I think maybe it's true, actually. I don't know why (smiles).

Do you feel like you're trying to prove yourself to him, in a way?

Of course I want to prove myself, in every tournament. I'm trying to get better every tournament, just step by step.

Coming into this Major, it's a big conversation for Falcons with trying to hunt down a trophy, but also with the Major stakes at hand too. Vitality obviously have been really good this season, so what is your approach coming into this Major? karrigan said long-term the team is looking more towards Singapore, but how about this Major, what is the focus on?

I think the focus point at this Major is to go to playoffs, first of all, and play good in playoffs. Play against good opponents and play good against good opponents in playoffs.

You're playing against G2 in your first game, so what has the preparation been like for that game so far?

Against G2? We didn't prep anything [yet], we will start prepping now.

As you look toward the rest of the Major, Spirit is also looking good, beat you in Astana. What is the approach for Falcons for the Major overall, other than trying to make playoffs? How are you approaching the rest of the teams here and how you'll play against them?

We don't focus on other teams at all. We just focus on ourselves and our games, just adding stuff and trying to make everyone in the team comfortable, and just focus on ourselves. We don't look at other teams; we just want to play our A-game and we're going to win [against] everyone.
