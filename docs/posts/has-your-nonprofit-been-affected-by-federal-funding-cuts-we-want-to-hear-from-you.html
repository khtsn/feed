Has Your Nonprofit Been Affected by Federal Funding Cuts? We Want to Hear From You.

The Trump administration is slashing federal grants. The New York Times wants to talk to nonprofits that have lost funding — or fear they might.

By David A. Fahrenthold

David Fahrenthold is an investigative reporter who has covered nonprofits for The Times since 2022.

Jan. 31, 2025

This is a time of fear and worry in the nonprofit world.

The Trump administration has taken extraordinary steps to reduce federal grant funding to nonprofit groups. It has largely shut off foreign aid. It briefly froze all outgoing grants. Now, even though that broad freeze has been lifted, the White House says it is reviewing thousands of grants to see if they conflict with President Trump’s executive orders banning “woke” ideologies.

Tens of thousands of U.S. nonprofits — aid groups, universities, hospitals and many more — rely on government grants for a significant part of their funding.

If your nonprofit is among them, I would like to hear about it. I’ve been covering nonprofits at The Times since 2022, but I’ve never seen a moment when people in the nonprofit world have been this confused and concerned. With your help, we’re trying to piece together what’s happened — and what will happen next.

Please tell us what you’ve experienced, using the form below. I may reach out to you to ask more. The Times will not publish the details of your submission unless you give us permission to do so.
