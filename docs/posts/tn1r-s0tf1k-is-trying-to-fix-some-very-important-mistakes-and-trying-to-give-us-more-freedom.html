tN1R: "S0tF1k is trying to fix some very important mistakes, and trying to give us more freedom"

Spirit have looked vulnerable in Rio, where they are without head coach Sergey "⁠hally⁠" Shavaev, but recovered from a blowout loss to Falcons in the upper bracket and a 0-1 start against RED Canids by defeating the Brazilian team and G2 en route to the playoffs.

It was Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich who led the charge in the qualifying series, looking more like the talented rifler who caught Spirit's eye while on HEROIC than the inconsistent rifler he has been in the last few months.

"it's a very hard period for me because [after] I joined Spirit, I'm inconsistent, and I'm trying to find my way," he told HLTV following his team's victory over G2. "I dunno, sometimes it's good, sometimes not, but when you have some good players around you, sometimes maybe you should be so consistent. But yeah, I want to play better because sometimes I've felt I've played very bad. But I dunno, I dunno why, but I'm trying to fix this."

The help of Dmitry "⁠S0tF1k⁠" Forostyanko, Spirit's academy coach, is also having an impact despite the 31-year-old facing limitations on what he can adjust.

"S0tF1k right now doesn't have a lot of permissions to change a lot of things in our roster because hally will be back," tN1R said. "But right now he's trying to fix some mistakes, very important mistakes, and trying to give us more freedom and space for everyone just to check how it will go."

Congratulations, you're through the playoffs. Got close in the end on Dust2, so walk me through that series and what you're thinking after it.

Hello, thank you! I don't know, I'm just happy because sh1ro said to me, 'It's a very good arena because the fans are very insane,' and I'm very happy we are qualified to the playoffs because I want to feel how it is to play in this arena.

We really have very highly skilled individual players, but sometimes, we didn't play as one mechanism

You missed playoffs in Rotterdam, and at EPL you lost to Astralis, so what does getting through to the arena here mean for the way the team is progressing?

We're just trying to find our way because for real, we really have very highly skilled individual players, but sometimes, we didn't play as one mechanism. We're trying to fix that, trying to find our way, and trying to find comfort for everyone because sometimes there's really not a short way to fix this. But we're just trying to do our work [as well as] possible.

How has it been playing with magixx as an in-game leader? That's a big change for you and the team.

I don't know, everyone is happy because Boris [magixx] really has a lot of ideas, and for us it's very good. Sometimes, we have very a lot of ideas, but in general, it's better than nothing. Everyone is happy, we support each other, we support Boris because it's really a very hard role for him. It's a lot of change for him because he's never been captain, and I think... yeah, all is fine.

How about for you individually? Your form has been up and down for a while, you had some good maps in this series obviously, but overall, how are you feeling about your form and what is missing for you to find the consistency you once had?

For real, it's a very hard period for me because when I joined Spirit, I'm inconsistent, and I'm trying to find my way. I dunno, sometimes it's good, sometimes not, but when you have some good players around you, sometimes maybe you should be so consistent. But yeah, I want to play better because sometimes I've felt I've played very bad. But I dunno, I dunno why, but I'm trying to fix this.

Maybe pressure, or confidence, either of those?

I'm not sure about pressure because I really don't care about that. It's more about my comfort, maybe.

We are listening to TeamSpeak [with S0tF1k] and just trying to fix our communication, [...] and it helps us because right now we are more stable with our communication

Here and in Astana, you have S0tF1k instead of hally. What have you noticed as the difference here with him instead of hally?

For real, S0tF1k right now doesn't have a lot of permissions to change a lot of things in our roster because hally will be back. But right now he's trying to fix some mistakes, very important mistakes, and trying to give us more freedom and space for everyone just to check how it will go. He also helps us prepare before matches. I like him.

He told me that he is trying to help work on your communication and the way that you play after trading. How has he been helping with that?

I dunno, he's just trying to explain what options we have. Also, we are listening to TeamSpeak [with him] and just trying to fix our communication, what might be better to say in this moment or another moment. And it helps us because right now we are more stable with our communication.

At this event you got blown out by Falcons, and then you lost a map to RED Canids earlier today. How does that set you up in terms of your form going into the arena?

I think we will just fight, and that's it. And try to enjoy! [NertZ jokingly from the background to tN1R: 'You piece of shit!'] (tN1R laughs)

And as you said, you're looking forward to playing in there?

Yeah because it's very good emotions, I very much like it.
