G2 up against the ropes in Romania after loss to PARIVISION

Having drawn two tough teams and lost both matches, the European squad is in a complicated situation going into day three.

PARIVISION lost their five-game winning streak on Mirage but still prevailed 2-1

PARIVISION beat G2 in a series that started out with the Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač-led squad looking like the better team out of the gates, but ended up with the Russian squad taking it in three.

G2 got off to a brilliant start on Mirage, with a dominating 9-3 CT half. From a beautiful triple by Matúš "⁠MATYS⁠" Šimko to kick things off in the pistol round, to strong B holds by Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko, and Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García at his best with the AWP, G2 looked like the team to beat.

PARIVISION couldn't turn it around after the switch and took their first defeat on Mirage in 2026 as G2 secured it 13-8, making it the end of a five-game winning streak.

G2 had a golden chance to close it out as the series moved to their pick, Inferno, but a disastrous 0-9 start on the attack with a dominating Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali strong on the defense showed that it wouldn't be so easy.

HeavyGod, after losing a 1v1 to Jame, got one back in another head-to-head against PARIVISION's captain to put G2 on the board, but it was too little too late as even a 6-0 start on the CT side was not enough to prevent map three and Jame put the final touch with a 1v3 clutch to end it 13-8.

G2 were no match for a sharp PARIVISION side in the decider, and even after winning the pistol round, they fell to an eco which included two Glock kills by Andrey "⁠BELCHONOKK⁠" Yasinskiy in a 2v2 situation.

Four Desert Eagle kills by Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa put G2 back in the lead the following round, but it didn't take long for PARIVISION to hit their stride as they even turned around three 3v5 situations during a seven-round streak to close the half 8-4.

It was smooth sailing after the switch, as a smoke defuse by BELCHONOKK in the second pistol round kickstarted a four-round streak to win the map out 13-4.

G2 fell to Vitality in the opening day and PARIVISION on day two, the two teams to have won trophies so far this year, and are now in a complicated situation as they need to win every match they play in the group stage if they want to keep their playoff hopes alive.

Meanwhile, PARIVISION get their head above water with this victory and soften the blow from their opening day loss to MOUZ as they head into day three of PGL Cluj-Napoca in the 1-1 pool.
