Christina Applegate Gets Standing Ovation at Emmy Awards

The “Dead to Me” actress makes a rare public appearance to present the first Emmy, for supporting actress in a comedy series.

  • Published Jan. 15, 2024Updated Jan. 16, 2024, 9:45 a.m. ET

Christina Applegate made a rare public appearance to present the first Emmy, for supporting actress in a comedy series. Glamorous in a plunging gown and visibly overcome by the long standing ovation she received, she still made self-deprecating jokes about Ozempic and disability. “You’re totally shaming me with disability by standing up,” she told the crowd. “It’s fine.”

Applegate was nominated for best actress in a comedy for “Dead to Me.” (Quinta Brunson won, “Abbott Elementary.”) The Netflix series was a sleek, savage container for Applegate’s gifts, both her forceful PTA Mom prettiness and the darker currents of anger and ambition that swirled just underneath, giving her comedy a whetted edge. She played Jen to Linda Cardellini’s Judy. Jen was a woman unmoored by the death of her husband who found port in a ride-or-die friendship and an escalating series of crimes.

Applegate hasn’t announced any new projects since receiving a diagnosis of multiple sclerosis in 2021. (“It’s not like I came on the other side of it, like, ‘Woohoo, I’m totally fine,’” she told The Times in 2022, speaking of that diagnosis. “Acceptance? No. I’m never going to accept this. I’m pissed.”)

Applegate is the rare actress to have translated teen stardom (she was Kelly Bundy, long of leg and scant of prefrontal cortex, on “Married With Children”) into a robust career as an adult that includes the sitcoms “Samantha Who” and “Up All Night,” as well as the “Anchorman” and “Bad Moms” film franchises.

Highlights From the Emmy Awards

Delayed from September by Hollywood’s actors and writers strikes, the ceremony took place on Jan. 15 at the Peacock Theater in Los Angeles.

  • Christina Applegate: ​​The actress, who hasn’t announced any new projects since receiving a diagnosis of multiple sclerosis in 2021, made a rare public appearance to present the first Emmy of the night.

  • The Last Triumph of ‘Succession’: HBO’s chronicle of a feuding media dynasty won best drama for its final season, the third time the show has claimed television’s most prestigious prize.

  • ‘The Bear’ Wins Big: The first season of FX’s restaurant comedy series took home six awards, including best comedy, best actor in a comedy for Jeremy Allen White and best supporting comedy actress for Ayo Edebiri.

  • A Strong Performance for ‘Beef’: The riveting dark comedy, which received 13 nominations, won for best limited series. The show’s leads, Ali Wong and Steven Yeun, also won awards for best actress and best actor in a limited series.

  • Making History: Quinta Brunson won best actress in a comedy for her performance in “Abbott Elementary,” becoming the first Black woman to win the category since Isabel Sanford took the prize in 1981 for “The Jeffersons.”

  • A New EGOT: Elton John won an Emmy for outstanding variety special for his livestreamed farewell concert, making him the 19th person to win all four major entertainment awards.
