HooXi: "We should be happy about the [team's] progression so far"

Astralis are through to the playoffs after a victory over FUT in the BLAST Rivals Group A lower bracket final, giving Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen and his men the chance to gain some valuable experience when they take to the Dickies Arena stage on Friday to play a quarter-final bout in front of the Texan crowd.

"There's still a long way to go, and I think there's a lot of potential," HooXi said of Astralis' journey so far this year as far as the team's growth goes. "It's a lot about learning by doing."

Now, Astralis' in-game leader hopes to capitalize on the lead-up to the Major. "We're the top of tier two, and this is a good time for us to try and make a good result because the top teams are building up to the Major," he said. "So maybe we can catch them sleeping a bit."

Listen to the episode here.

You just made the playoffs. Eight teams, six go to the playoffs, so it's a bit of a forgiving schedule, maybe. You opened against G2 and lost, now you beat FUT. What are your first impressions after the groups here in Dallas?

We threw away way too many rounds against G2 that you cannot throw away to win against a tier-one team. That actually sums it up pretty well. Today, it's a completely different scenario because we're the favorites and they're playing with a coach, and it's a bit tough for them.

What we learned from the last tournament is that we play to win and that we're not scared to play how we're usually playing just because we're the favorites, so I'm happy about that.

This is a good time for us to try and make a good result because the top teams are building up to the Major

Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen

You haven't maybe shown it against the elite teams, but you've shown that you're pretty good against teams around your ranking, and you're in the top 10 now. Where do you see this team right now?

There's still a long way to go, and I think there's a lot of potential. It's a lot about learning by doing. It's hard to prepare yourself to play a Major without having played a Major before. You can talk about it, you can do all of the right things, but in the end, you still have to cope with the pressure and do the right thing.

As I said, it's just learning by doing; you can't really do more than that. It's also what shows, like when we played the final in Bucharest, we talked a lot about the issues we would have and how to overcome them, but in the end, when you sit in the scenario, it's completely different than talking about it. But I think it's going pretty quickly, people are working hard, and we should be happy about the progression so far.

You bring up the Major. It's coming up, we're in that ramping-up phase toward it, so how is having that on the horizon?

In one way we have to just focus on ourselves and try to progress as a team and fix as many mistakes as possible before the Major, yada yada, but as you say, we're the top of tier two, and this is a good time for us to try and make a good result because the top teams are building up to the Major.

For example, I saw Vitality saying that this is like, 'Of course we're focused, but we're still trying to rest for the Major,' you know? So maybe we can catch them sleeping a bit. It's nothing we really talk about or focus on, but that's the situation.

You talk about how you need to learn by doing. You will get a bit of time in the arena. How important is that, especially for a guy like ryu, maybe, to get some experience there?

Oh, I think it's super important. As I said, we can talk about it from now until Christmas, and it's still different when you're actually in the situation, so... But it's not only him, it's also for phzy, for Staehr — to be in an arena and have the positions he has, where he has to communicate and take a lot of initiative — it's also learning by doing for him. We take any experience we can get.

We also just got a second-place in Bucharest, and it didn't feel like a confidence builder at all

Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen

Looking forward to being in that playoff, where you're starting in the quarter-final, how do you see the team's possibilities moving forward?

They're fine. As I said, all of the top teams are probably chilling a little bit right now. Maybe testing some stuff, or not wanting to show all of their new gimmicks, so it's a good chance, but of course, we'll have to play to our best to progress.

If you can capitalize on that, could it be a good confidence builder as you work towards the end of the season?

In a way, yes, but we also just got a second-place in Bucharest, and it didn't feel like a confidence builder at all. We'll see what happens. I think a lot is not about the result, but how we get to the results.

If we feel like we played badly and didn't play to our top level, we will not gain confidence, but if we feel like we did and lost to a better team, then it's fine, and we will learn from it, and I think we'll be happy.

You say Bucharest didn't feel like a confidence-booster even though you made the final, which was pretty big.

It didn't.

Why do you view it through a negative prism?

We had one focus, and that was not to lose to ourselves, and we failed on that. Then we went back to practice for two weeks, and the practice didn't go well, either. Then, of course, we don't feel a lot of confidence going into this tournament, but we can turn it around in one or two days, so it's fine.

It was with a coach playing, so it wasn't like beating FUT with full force, but did it at least feel a bit good to get one back at them?

Oh yeah, it always feels good to beat FUT, [laughs] fuck these guys.
