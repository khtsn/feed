Liquid eliminate HEROIC from Major in Stage 1

Liquid have kept their Stage 1 run at IEM Cologne Major alive at the expense of HEROIC after a two-map series victory in the 1-2 pool (13-5 Nuke, 13-11 Inferno).

A match against FlyQuest in the 2-2 pool now stands between Liquid and a spot in Stage 2.

HEROIC's campaign in Cologne ends with a whimper with only a win over Gaimin Gladiators after best-of-one losses to Lynn Vision and Sharks on day one.

The team had been on the uptick in the middle of the season despite playing with Simon "⁠yxngstxr⁠" Boije as a stand-in for Alimzhan "⁠Alkaren⁠" Bitimbay, winning DraculaN Season 5 and placing second at CCT Global Finals, but dipped when it mattered most just before the Major with five straight series losses between PGL Astana and Stake Ranked Episode 2.

"This last part of the season hasn't been ideal at all, and far from acceptable, so we're going to find ourselves in a different level of opposition, and rightfully so," Christoffer "⁠Chr1zN⁠" Storgaard said to Tres "⁠stunna⁠" Saranthus after the match. "It's tough, but we'll have to go from there.

"It's a very stressful position to be in, so errors will of course occur in our game. But we're not playing up to the level we can, we just crumble under the pressure. We just don't really get to follow through on the plans, we have good scenarios in some rounds but we misplayed it, and congrats to them."

It was utter domination to start out as Liquid gave HEROIC no breathing room on Nuke, making up for a lost pistol with a six-round run where Keith "⁠NAF⁠" Markovic, Jonathan "⁠EliGE⁠" Jablonowski, and Roland "⁠ultimate⁠" Tomkowiak all had their time to shine. Chr1zN offered his team a small lifeline with two crucial multi-kills, but it did little to halt the onslaught of a Liquid side that was firing on all cylinders with great teamplay, crossfires, and individual moments that let them march to a 13-5 win.

HEROIC's fortunes took a turn for the worse on Liquid's Inferno pick with Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa being nigh-unstoppable in a dominant 6-1 start. A back-and-forth battle at the end of the half saw HEROIC again muster four rounds by the break, and this time they won the second pistol and converted against Liquid's force to close the gap to one round.

But again, malbsMd had no mercy and tallied a triple and quad-kill back-to-back to set Liquid on double digits, and they seized upon that to reach 12 rounds. It wouldn't be a Liquid match without some level of choke, though, and the team lost a 5v3 and 2v1 back-to-back before finally sealing the win in round 24.
