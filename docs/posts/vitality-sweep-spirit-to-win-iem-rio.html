Vitality sweep Spirit to win IEM Rio

Vitality won IEM Rio 2026 on Sunday with a 3-0 victory against Spirit, picking up their fourth title of the season and making history as the first team to win two Grand Slams.

"I don't know the secret," Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire said after the match. "The secret is to work hard, to be always one step ahead and to have these amazing guys.

"Even though we won 3-0, I was mad because we were losing so many bad rounds. I want us to be perfect all the time. But I'm proud of them, they played insanely well."

Spirit had good chances to win at least Mirage, where they fell off the pace after taking an 11-8 lead. It was the kind of game that laid bare just how punishing Vitality can be the moment an opponent's concentration slips.

Another brief slip-up in the second half of Nuke then potentially killed any hopes Spirit had of taking the map after a respectable CT side. Dust2, the last map, was simply a one-way street.

As the series came to an end and Vitality's players hoisted the trophy, many were left to wonder how different things might have been if Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets had played to the best of his abilities. The Russian prodigy began to show some signs of life only on the third map, but by then it was already too late to make a difference. He finished the series with a 1.00 rating, still the highest on his team.

Spirit came out guns blazing as they raced to a 5-1 lead on the T side of Mirage with a string of successful B hits. Vitality then stabilized their defense and managed to restore parity before the side switch, but they had to be kicking themselves after winning all but two opening kills during the half.

Now playing on the T side, Vitality took the lead for the first time after a near-flawless pistol, but the landscape of the game changed once Spirit had money in the bank. The Russian team bolted to an 11-8 lead before Vitality hit back, forcing overtime.

And that was when Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool went into grand final mode, pulling off multi-kills in four rounds in a row to power Vitality to victory after Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich had fired Spirit into the lead in the first round of overtime.

Spirit shook off the loss and put up a resolute defensive performance on Nuke for an 8-4 lead, with Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev and Myroslav "⁠zont1x⁠" Plakhotia sharing the top of the scoreboard with a combined 22 frags.

But any momentum was shattered when they lost to a handful of pistols three rounds into the half. Vitality took the game by the scruff of the neck and calmly closed the map out.

Vitality picked up where they had left off and jumped out to a 5-0 lead on the CT side of Dust2. Spirit kept themselves in the game with a three-round streak, but they would only win one more round in the rest of the half, courtesy of a last-second donk clutch.

Vitality were unwilling to let that rare blunder alter the course of the game and secured the ensuing pistol round with a perfect A fake, riding that momentum to a quick finish.
