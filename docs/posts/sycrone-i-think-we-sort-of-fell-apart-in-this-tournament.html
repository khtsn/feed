sycrone: "I think we sort of fell apart in this tournament"

MOUZ were sent packing in last place at BLAST Open Rotterdam following defeats to The MongolZ and 9z.

It's the toughest moment in more than a year for MOUZ, who had made the playoffs of every LAN event since IEM Katowice 2025, which they attended with academy player Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze as a stand-in following the benching of Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek.

"It's a unique situation for us to be in," Dennis "⁠sycrone⁠" Nielsen reflected after the match. "It's tough. I don't think I've seen the team play as bad a brand of Counter-Strike as we did this tournament. Not that we don't know it, but especially the mid-rounds; just how we put it together, the overview as the rounds go on."

MOUZ will now have three weeks to lick their wounds before the trip to Brazil for IEM Rio. sycrone admitted "there is cause for concern" after the last two events, but he noted that the team still has plenty of time to find its groove before the Major.

"We were not supposed to peak," he said. "I don't think any of the top teams are really focused on, 'This is when we have to be at our best.'

"Maybe, if anything, it gives us a bit of a wake-up call. It gives us more time now to focus on the level we want to bring."

A tough loss and you are out of the tournament after just two matches. What happened? Because even on Dust2, the map that you won, it didn't feel like you were a dominant team; you were just really good at punishing their mistakes.

Yeah, you're right. I think we sort of fell apart this tournament. A lot of the fundamentals are not there. It's not that we don't know how to play. If you're in a man advantage situation, play for info, set up a crossfire… We were just not thinking clearly. And we have to go back and look at why that was the case.

The type of Counter-Strike that we play demands a very high cerebral level and a high level of communication. And it wasn't there now. And I would have wished for us to take a step forward this tournament and in the previous one, and we didn't. We've taken a step back; maybe even two. There are things to turn around.

After the first match, you told Dust2 Brasil that you felt the team lacked energy. Was that the case today as well? What did you feel, standing behind the players?

We had very flat energy against The MongolZ. It had also been the case against FUT when we played them. One of the takeaways just in that game was that FUT wanted it way more than us, and it can never be that someone has more willpower than you. This is one area where we're really outclassed. And we talked, going into the tournament, that we had to up it quite a lot.

We need to analyze not only the tactical aspects but also the mental and the energy aspects. And I think those two are probably the bigger topics right now

Was it the case today as well, that you felt the energy was flat? Especially on Nuke, where it felt like you were just mentally checked out.

Yeah, definitely the focus has gone a bit down. It's tough for me to maintain it all the time, and I couldn't always find the right way to do it. But it's not just energy; it's also the energy in thinking clearly, right? Having that cerebral Counter-Strike that wasn't there. It's in more departments, and it shows also in energy and also in communication.

This was your first event on LAN since EPL 20 where you couldn't make it out of the groups. There was also IEM Katowice 2025, but you didn't have a full roster. What kind of impact do you think this will have on the team?

It's a unique situation for us to be in. It's tough. I don't think I've seen the team play as bad a brand of Counter-Strike as we did this tournament. Not that we don't know it, but especially the mid-rounds; just how we put it together, the overview we have as the rounds go on.

I think the impact, just in the short term, is that there's more time before IEM Rio. So we will have more time to look into things. It's not usual that we get such a long amount of time to break down what is going on, to have a mental reset, to make sure we come in with some momentum. That's definitely missing for us right now.

You'll have approximately three weeks to prepare for Rio. What is the plan? Have you thought about the strategy for Rio?

Originally, the plan was that we would take some time off because we've been going non-stop since the start of the season. Usually at this time, I sit down with the coaching team. We have a look at how we're doing. What are some of the topics that're going on? We try and make a plan for how we progress further.

We need to analyze not only the tactical aspects but also the mental and the energy aspects. And I think those two are probably the bigger topics right now.

After the Budapest Major, you said something along the lines of, 'We'll see what happens with regards to the roster.' Are you still confident that this is the roster that will bring titles to MOUZ?´

That comment, back then, was also about my own situation. Had to figure that out.
