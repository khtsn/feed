9z take down MOUZ to reach Astana playoffs

9z have become the first team to secure a spot in the quarterfinal stage of PGL Astana following a 2-1 success against MOUZ, who came back from a map down with a statement victory on Inferno but then flamed out on Ancient after a bright start.

The victory against this new-look MOUZ side caps off a dominant Swiss stage run that also included wins over PARIVISION and Falcons. Luciano "⁠luchov⁠" Herrera was once again the team's top performer, with Maximiliano "⁠max⁠" Gonzalez also playing a vital role with many key plays.

9z bounced back from an early deficit on Nuke and took the lead for the first time in the game thanks to two clutches from max. The Uruguayan and Matias "⁠HUASOPEEK⁠" Ibañez Hernandez were the heroes for the South American team on the T side as the first half ended in a 6-6 stalemate, while Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman and Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze did most of the heavy lifting on the defense, helping to make up for an off game from Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás.

The back-and-forth trend continued in the second half as neither team managed to stamp their authority on proceedings during the early stages. A messy 1v3 clutch from xertioN gave MOUZ a timely boost, but his efforts would be for naught as 9z retook control of the game and went on a 4-0 run to close out the map.

MOUZ came out on Inferno looking sharp, cruising to a 9-3 lead on the T side as 9z struggled to find their footing on the map. One round was all that the South American team could muster in the second half as MOUZ quickly finished the game off, with xelex posting multi-frags in three of the team's four round wins.

MOUZ carried that momentum into Ancient as they strolled to a 5-0 lead on the CT side. 9z then finally found their confidence and their rhythm and hit back with five rounds of their own before xertioN inspired his team to a narrow 7-5 advantage with three kills on a shoddy buy.

9z took the game by the scruff of the neck once they switched to the CT side. On two occasions, MOUZ were able to answer back after a timeout, but it was simply not enough to break 9z's flow on the defense.
