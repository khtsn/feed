Wordle 656 3/6

⬜🟩🟩⬜⬜ HEART

I almost always start with the word HEART. It’s got good vowels, the R and the T are helpful in solving, and the H lets you know if you might have a consonant pair, like PH, CH or SH. Plus, it’s romantic and underscores our very humanity: Amo, ergo sum!

Right away, the EA lit up green — a great sign. My actual, anatomical heart may have even fluttered. If more than one letter is green in the first round, I feel I have a better chance of solving the puzzle in three tries, and as we all know, three is the magic number. I aim for three!

Knocking out the R and the T, which are common letters, meant I needed to mull. Usually I would choose an S in the next round, but SEA doesn’t lead to much. SEALS? SEAMS? While I was thinking about SEAMS, the letter M got lodged in my brain, so I decided to try MEALY. Not sure if that was a logical choice or a shot in the dark, but I guessed it anyway. The L turned yellow, and the Y lit up green, and my breath caught — real progress. So close.

Knowing that the L was the first letter, I had LEAFY and LEAKY in mind. I really wanted to guess LEAFY. It’s a pretty, positive, evocative word, green trees shimmering in the sunshine. LEAKY has a rusty drip-drip of sadness about it. The K makes a harsh sound in your throat; LEAFY is a whisper in a spring breeze. I went with LEAFY — and solved. WORDLE IN THREE! Give me my commemorative jacket, please. (Yes, I sometimes make mock-ups of varsity jackets to award myself if I get Wordle in 3.)

Today’s word is LEAFY. According to Webster’s New World College Dictionary, it’s an adjective meaning, “Having broad leaves or consisting mainly of such leaves.”
