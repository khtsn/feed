Spirit march past G2 into Astana semis

Despite a valiant comeback attempt on Ancient, G2 were easily second-best in this series.

Spirit have claimed a semi-final berth at PGL Astana to continue their recent resurgence, earning their place among the top four with a 2-0 win over G2. The result continues Spirit's immense upward swing since it was announced that Sergey "⁠hally⁠" Shavaev was taking a break ahead of the IEM Cologne Major, and some of the trademarks of said upswing were fully apparent today.

Spirit played with a degree of freedom that enabled their individuals to shine, particularly Dmitry "⁠sh1ro⁠" Sokolov, and Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev's massively improved mid-round calling was on display at various points throughout the series, particularly on CT sides.

Sick MATYS 1v3 denies donk the ace

The series kicked off on Dust2 and it was an interesting battle in the early stages, the teams trading force-buys before Spirit eventually took control of the economy, and with it the game.

sh1ro's aggressive AWPing combined with Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets and Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich's opening prowess secured a winning offensive half, and although G2 fought back with a pistol win when the sides switched, Spirit's defense was in the right place far too often once guns came up, granting them an easy win.

NertZ pistol 4K grants G2 a lifeline

Ancient began in much the same fashion, G2 looking far short of Spirit's level even on their own map pick. Another force-buy loss set G2 on the back foot early on their offense and they never really recovered, finding themselves running into a brick wall both on B and in Middle, and out-called in the mid-round if they managed to get there.

The second half was far more competitive, Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz hitting hard in the pistol round to give his team a platform for the comeback before Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García rose tall to provide a firepower boost. G2's calling was much improved as mid-round aggression helped them stifle Spirit, but it was too little, too late as Spirit did just enough to find the rounds needed to get over the line.

G2 will be disappointed with the fact they struggled to get going until three halves of CS were already played, and even then it was only really SunPayus who kept them in contention on Ancient. As of yet, it seems the addition of NertZ has yet to move the needle for G2, whose ceiling seems to be that of a gatekeeper to the elite. They have time ahead of IEM Cologne Major to change that fact, but the clock is ticking.
