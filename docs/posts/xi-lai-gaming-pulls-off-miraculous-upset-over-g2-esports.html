After meeting twice before at Masters Toronto and Masters Santiago, XLG finally got not only its first map win against G2 in this series, but its first playoff win of any kind on the international stage after Swiss Stage and Group Stage eliminations at each of the last three international events.

The series played out on Xi Lai's pick of Lotus, G2's Ascent, and the decider map of Split. G2 won Lotus 13-7, XLG won Ascent 13-4, and then went on to win Split 13-9.

Xi Lai unsurprisingly went with its trademark triple-Sentinel comp on Lotus, featuring Duelist player Rarga on Sage along with a Deadlock and a Cypher. XLG started the map on attack and began strong, winning the pistol, anti-eco, and converting the bonus to claim an early 3-0 lead. A thrifty, converted in large part thanks to BABYBAY 's Vyse setup on C, seemed to be a small hiccup for XLG en route to a 5-1 lead, but G2 quickly got a read on XLG's attack. From the braintrust of valyn and JoshRT , G2 navigated the map and its midrounds to near perfection, winning six straight rounds to enter halftime up 7-5.

A second pistol round win on the map helped XLG start the second half in the right direction, but G2 reversed that momentum in no time, converting an eco round with five pistols. While Xi Lai clawed back a round after re-stabilizing its economy, G2 flexed its muscle. Four straight rounds went G2's way, making for an easy 13-7 win on XLG's map pick.

Since Ascent's return to the map pool, G2 has opted for a double-Duelist look with Yoru-Phoenix, Vyse, Sova, and Astra. In this match, G2 switched out Yoru and Astra for Neon and Omen, while XLG went with Jett, Phoenix, and Cypher to complement Omen and Sova. The map began in a similar tempo to Lotus, with XLG beginning dominantly with wins through the bonus, only to lose to a low buy of several Stingers. Unlike Lotus though, that momentum was sustained by XLG, as the Chinese side stormed into halftime leading 8-4.

While defense is regarded as the stronger side of the map, XLG impressed on attack as well, effortlessly breaking down G2's defenses to shut them out of the half en route to a clean 13-4 win.

G2 entered halftime down 7-5, but moved onto defense with what could be argued as the more defense-sided comp of the two teams.
