Vitality beat GamerLegion to set up Rotterdam final rematch vs NAVI

Vitality moved past GamerLegion 2-0 en route to the grand final of BLAST Rivals Fort Worth, dominating Janusz "⁠Snax⁠" Pogorzelski's men on Mirage (13-1) before needing overtime to put a comeback effort on Overpass to bed (16-14).

A best-of-five rematch of the BLAST Open Rotterdam grand final against Natus Vincere awaits Vitality on championship Sunday, and though the team is looking vulnerable amidst a lack of practice after closing out the Grand Slam in Rio, a 3-0 sweep in that last series still leaves Natus Vincere with a lot of ground to make up.

Despite Milan "⁠hypex⁠" Polowiec jokingly telling Dust2.us after beating FURIA that GamerLegion were here to stop Vitality, he learned just what it meant to play what an overwhelming majority of people now consider the best team of all time on stage.

While Vitality have been setting records left and right over the last year and a half, GamerLegion set one of their own in this series — although, admittedly, not one they'll be proud of. They were obliterated 13-1 on their pick of Mirage, garnering just 17 frags in the first half, and 21 total.

That set a record for the fewest kills in a first half and map in a CS2 arena match, and had the series poised for a quick finish with Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut terrorizing the server with a 2.12 rating and Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan close behind (1.96).

GamerLegion showed tenacity on Overpass, preventing a blowout in the first half thanks to a few multi-kills and a pair of clutches from hypex before climbing back from a 5-10 deficit in the second half. Snax and Oldřich "⁠PR⁠" Nový powered their team's effort, but excellent teamplay and trading were also key in a recovery all the way back to a 12-11 lead.

But playing Vitality on stage is a different beast, and GamerLegion fumbled their sole opportunity to force a Nuke decider. Two OT rounds on the T side weren't enough to overcome a resplendent showing from the world's best, who carried momentum forward from flameZ's 1v2 clutch after the swap to seal victory.
