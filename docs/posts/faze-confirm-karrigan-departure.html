FaZe confirm karrigan departure

FaZe have confirmed the departure of in-game leader Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen after a five-year spell that brought the North American organization its first Major title.

The news comes amid reports that karrigan is on his way to Falcons, where he will reunite with his former FaZe teammate Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač and his ex-Astralis coach Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen.

karrigan rejoined FaZe in February 2021 after a two-year stint with MOUZ, replacing Olof "⁠olofmeister⁠" Kajbjer in the starting lineup. It marked the Dane's return to the North American organization following a first stint between 2016 and 2019.

karrigan won eight notable LAN events during his second FaZe stint, including the Antwerp Major in 2022, as well as the Intel Grand Slam Season 4 in 2023. He also guided the team to the finals of the Majors in Copenhagen, Shanghai and Budapest.

IEM Chengdu 2024 was the last tournament that karrigan won with FaZe

"I came back to FaZe for a reason, to create history, and that we did, especially in 2022," karrigan wrote on X. "It’s always been a privilege to wear this jersey, with it comes the fantastic organization and its players.

"For all the teammates I had on my journey, thank you for giving me the trust to lead you guys."

The grand final run in Budapest came at the tail-end of a very challenging year following the departure of Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool to Vitality on a free transfer. The team was extremely close to being eliminated from the Major in Stage 1, then picked up form as the tournament went on before losing to Vitality 3-1 in the title decider.

FaZe decided against making changes in the off-season, perhaps expecting that the team would capitalize on that run, but the same issues came back to haunt them in the new season. A dreadful run of form saw them fail to qualify for the Major for the first time since the North American organization entered the game in 2016.

In an interview with HLTV at PGL Bucharest, karrigan opened the door to a move away from FaZe.

"With these performances, it's not good enough," he said. "So if I'm stepping down, let's do that. I just want FaZe back at the top, with or without me."

karrigan's departure comes just days after FaZe named Niclas "⁠enkay J⁠" Krumhorn as the team's new head coach — signaling the start of what could be a long and arduous rebuilding process as they look to return to the top.

FaZe have not yet announced who will fill karrigan's shoes in the future. According to recent reports, the team is expected to play with ENCE benched player Ryan "⁠Neityu⁠" Aubry in the next weeks, presumably at both BLAST Rivals Season 1 (April 29-May 3) and IEM Atlanta (May 11-17).
