The second match of the day saw the highly anticipated debut of the full Leviatán roster. Luckily for Leviatán, Neon's debut came at just the right time, as with their full roster now on stage, the team looks immediately improved. Neon came in and immediately put up a star performance in his first map of VCT, putting up a 1.81 rating. Leviatán won Bind in a 13-4, completely dismantling KRÜ, who unfortunately were still without their main roster.

It didn't take long for Neon to warm up to the VCT stage.

Split was a bit more competitive, with Leviatán's inexperience showing as they made several ill-advised plays that gave KRÜ chances in the map. However, the overall skill level of the team was enough to bring them a 13-10 win on the map, and finally get Leviatán rolling in the Kickoff bracket.

EG and Leviatán will move on in the bracket, but Santiago is a long ways away. Five more matches stand between them and qualification, with no room for error.

Americas Kickoff will continue tomorrow with NRG vs. 100 Theives and G2 Esports vs. Cloud9.
