Indigo's first coaching job in Valorant came with T1 Academy T1 Academy Inactive in 2022. Since then, he has coached Nongshim RedForce Nongshim RedForce Korea Rank #1 Xross Jeonghwan (정환) Rb Goo Sang-min (구상민) Francis Kim Mu-bin (김무빈) Dambi Lee Hyuk-kyu (이혁규) Ivy Park Sung-hyeon (박성현) , and in 2025 was an assistant coach for the Masters Bangkok-winning T1 team. Indigo departed T1 at the end of the season following the expiration of his contract.

The move sees Indigo reunite with Autumn , whom he worked with at T1. The pair's relationship extends far past Valorant. Autumn was the former head coach of Korean PUBG team OGN Entus Ace, the team that Indigo played for from 2018-2020. Autumn coached the team from 2018 to 2019, and departed shortly after for Valorant, while Indigo continued in PUBG until 2022.

After winning Champions Seoul in 2024, expectations for EDG were high in the 2025 season. The season started out with a win at China Kickoff, which was par for the course for the Chinese giants, and was followed by a third-place finish at Masters Bangkok. Early struggles in Stage 1 prompted roster changes for the squad, which only got the team as far as a fourth-place finish at the end of the playoffs. The finish shut EDG out of the second Masters Event of the year. EDG went 3-2 in the group stage of Stage 2, but again fell short of the expectations set by its trophy-laden past in regional play, finishing third. The team qualified for Champions Paris as the region's third seed through Championship points, but was eliminated in the group stage.

Since the end of the season, EDG's roster moves have been largely off-stage. Head coach Muggle , analyst JuiceFlip , and manager Aqua departed, replaced by Autumn, Indigo, and 24K as coaches, while former manager Hibari returned.
