FaZe eliminate Monte to stay alive in ESL Pro League Season 23

All 24 rounds were played on both maps played in the series.

FaZe have beaten Monte 2-0 on Dust2 and Overpass in an ESL Pro League Season 23 Stage 2 elimination match to stay alive in the online tournament with hopes of reaching the LAN finals.

FaZe had a tough start to the event, going 0-2 after losses to G2 and paiN in the Swiss group stage, and are still two wins away from reaching the next stage.

The series against Monte started on Dust2, and a strong CT side from FaZe put Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen and his men in a favorable position as they took 8-4 at the half, giving up just one round after the initial pistol and anti-ecos.

It wasn't an easy victory for FaZe, however, as a won pistol round put Monte right back in it as they once again won the first three rounds of the half to get back to within one. Six rounds later, the Jack "⁠Gizmy⁠" von Spreckelsen-led squad even had an 11-10 lead, but FaZe squeezed by winning two out of the last three to steal the map.

Play moved Overpass, where FaZe got six valuable rounds starting on the T side with David "⁠frozen⁠" Čerňanský leading the way. A fast start on the CT side then put them ahead, 11-7, before a lengthy tech pause halted the game.

Once again, the second map went the distance after play resumed with Monte gunning for a comeback, winning five of six rounds, but karrigan pulled a 1v2 clutch off in the final round of regulation to push his team over the finish line.
