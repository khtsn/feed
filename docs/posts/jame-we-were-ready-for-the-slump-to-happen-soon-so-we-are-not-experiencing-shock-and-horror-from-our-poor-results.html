Jame: "We were ready for the slump to happen soon, so we are not experiencing shock and horror from our poor results"

PARIVISION suffered an unceremonious group stage exit at PGL Astana after a loss to G2, causing an already frustrated Dastan "⁠dastan⁠" Akbayev to miss out on an appearance in front of his home crowd.

It's been a rollercoaster season for PARIVISION, with incredible highs but also some disappointing lows. The Russian team will make one final stop, at the CS Asia Championships, before their Cologne Major campaign kicks off in Stage 3.

After PARIVISION's elimination, Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali sat down with HLTV and discussed the results in Astana, the team's consistency issues, dastan's bluntness, and the preparation for the next two events.

Editor's Note: Jame answered in Russian, and his answers were translated by HLTV staff.

Commiserations on the loss, hard way to go out in Astana. Just tell me what you're thinking after that and how you're feeling.

I'm thinking about the way we should spend time moving forward and how to set up the work process to get the most out of what we have now. I don't feel the sadness of playing in the playoffs yet, but I believe with the time, I'll get there.

You've had a really up and down year. You won at the start but it's been inconsistent. You've had a month off coming into this. What did you do during this month, and why do you think it didn't show here?

One can always come up with a lot of excuses, and one of those scenarios is that it's been a while since we played, so the first game turned out to be messy and we stressed a bit. 9z is a good opponent. The second game we won, the third game against Aurora was an early one, and I think we didn't really adapt to such schedule changes. We didn't show up on map one, and the second one was ours to take but we still lost it. Against G2, we gave a fight, which is overall a good development within this event.

This was one of the scenarios of why we played the way we did. In reality, we gave ourselves time to relax, most didn't sit in front of their computers for five or six days, some made health checkups and fixed some related issues. And for 15 days we were playing and breaking down maps, so I think it was a good, fruitful bootcamp. There we tried to make sure we get to this event in our best form, but as usual, we might have worked for the distance before, so it's unclear why we played the way we did without showing what we're capable of.

This is obviously a home event for dastan, and he's been critical of the team recently — he said players need to step up or they're going to be cut after the Major. What do you have to say about the team's performance?

It's only the first season for our team. In all honesty, there should be nothing expected from us. All the pressure there is the one we create within ourselves because of the past results. But in reality, there's space to grow for us, we have a lot of work to do.

It isn't an easy path where you come in and it's a tier one team from the get go. To achieve that, you need years in order to find yourself in this game and with this team, to understand why you play, if you enjoy it or not — it's all a novelty for us. And as it usually happens, we were ready for the slump to happen soon. That slump happens to every team except one now, so we're not experiencing shock and horror from our poor results.

You've worked with dastan for a long time and you're used to his style of coaching. Do you think it's sometimes too harsh for players as young as this?

We try to get most out of the current situation and try to make it work. Whenever we find something, it might stop working: we might solve an issue and another pops up, or not solve old issues and the new ones will still appear.

We exist in this tempo all the time, so it's hard to say how hard it is for the guys. It's generally tough for them because it's their first season and their first failures. Before that, they were climbing up fast, but now you have to put in more effort to get a foothold [at the top].

You have two events left this season — CAC and then the Major. What are you going to do between now and then to try and turn things around?

Actually, we've had a productive bootcamp and fixed all the things we should have, the ones we missed at past events. But at this event, new problems appeared that we need to fix. Not really problems even, more like tasks and places to improve. So I think we'll need to catch our wave at which we'll get confidence in ourselves and the team, and that will help us go deep. Currently, we're in a search of ourselves as a team.
