T.c talks try's signing, moving on from Grim and hallzerk, and Senzu's "little bit rough English"

Passion UA were no strangers to left-field roster moves, but their recent double addition of Santino "⁠try⁠" Rigal and Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold surprised nearly everyone and signaled a clear intent to join the big leagues.

In the midst of their chaotic start to the year, the team attended Roman Imperium Cup V with Michael "⁠Grim⁠" Wince in place of Senzu, who could not travel in time for the event, and suffered a quarter-final exit against Gentle Mates.

After the game, Tiaan "⁠T.c⁠" Coertzen spoke to HLTV about the team's new direction following a turbulent start to 2026, their expectations for the season, and Senzu's adaptation to English communication.

Tc, a 2-0 defeat and you're out of the tournament in the quarter-finals. You knew it was always going to be rough, given the circumstances. What are your main takeaways from the tournament?

Overall, we had mostly close scorelines here. But we've had two days of practice, and that wasn't even with Grim. It was with Senzu. We obviously knew, coming here, that it wasn't going to be easy to get wins, but I think overall, we actually played pretty well. We made some small mistakes that we went over and stuff that we fixed. But it's tough, considering the circumstances, to come here and expect to win the tournament, right?

What was the reason Senzu wasn't here? Was it visa issues?

It's personal circumstances. He could only travel on the 21st or something like that.

 The last couple of weeks have been quite eventful, I would say, with two new players coming in. Walk me through the start of the season and the decision to make two changes just weeks into the season.

 It's obviously been a very chaotic start to the season for us. Like everyone else, we were exploring the market during the off-season. That's what you kind of have to do, at the end of the day, to stay ahead. But we came into the season, obviously, with still full belief in the squad. In Krakow, it didn't go so well for us. We had Bounty as well, where we got knocked out in the first round. Then, we went to NA, played BLAST… Actually, none of the results affected why we wanted to make the changes so soon. We did explore the market and so on.

That's pretty much what sparked all of it, I can say, is the interest from NRG in Grim, and we just had to act fast

When Mike [Grim] got the offer from NRG, or when NRG came to buy Mike, that obviously changed things and sped up everything that we had to do and made us act a lot faster on getting new transfers and new players. So that's pretty much what sparked all of it, I can say, is the interest from NRG in Grim, and we just had to act fast.

 It was important for us to maintain the Americas region, but we also wanted to get upgrades for the team. As a core, the squad has been together for a very long time, and we were just looking to make some changes, get some fresh blood in, get some fresh ideas. I think we have a really strong squad now, and I think we can play really well as long as we sort out all the communication issues that we might have.

 What was the mood like in the camp, with a player who is on his way out of the team? Given that it was his decision to leave, it was kind of normal, right?

One hundred percent. The same as usual for us. We've played together for three, four years now. There's no bad blood or anything like this. It's personal relationships, also, at the end of the day. And for Mike, the move also makes sense because he wants to be on an American team so he can be home more. He has family and his girlfriend back on America. So, honestly, the best case scenario for him is that he can go to an American team, where he can be at home more and he can actually practice in NA. Whereas with us, we are in Europe constantly, we spend so much time away from home. Sometimes, it's like three months at a time. That's obviously very difficult when you still have things back home.

I wanted to get your thoughts on try. What do you think he brings to the table compared to hallzerk?

Obviously, still very, very early, early days for us. So for me to give, like, an objective opinion, a proper assessment… Like I said, it's been, like, two days of practice, you know? But so far, I have only good things to say about him. He's got plenty of ideas. Every round, he's talking, he has ideas, good reads on the teams as well. We were looking for a strong second voice on the team for a long time.

Unfortunately, with the old team, we had it at times, but no one stepped up into that role fully. And a lot of the times, especially in matches where we were struggling, we really needed that. So with try, especially with his experience from being the IGL of Imperial for a little bit, I think that will help us a lot, hopefully, just to have that stronger second voice, have more ideas in the mid-round and keep things more calm.

But, honestly, I have nothing bad to say about hallzerk. He is really, really a great guy. He contributed a lot to the team. A lot of our success was due to him playing well at those times. Often, when hallzerk was playing well, the whole team was playing well as well. Honestly, I've got nothing bad to say about hallzerk.
