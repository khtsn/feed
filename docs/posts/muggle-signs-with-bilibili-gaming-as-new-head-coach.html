Muggle joins after spending three seasons with EDG, with half of that stint being as a coach to head coach AfteR and the second half as the head coach. The peak of Muggle's time with EDG came in 2024, when he led the Chinese giants to success on the international stage with a trophy-lifting campaign at Champions Seoul. Muggle and EDG's 2025 season was, in comparison, disappointing. After stamping its regional dominance with a win at China Kickoff, EDG finished third at Masters Bangkok, but came up flat in Stage 1, finishing fourth. The result was EDG's first time not winning a regional event in the partnership era, and it was also the first time it missed out on an international event in the partnership era. EDG finished third in Stage 2, qualifying for Champions Paris through Championship points, where they were eliminated in the group stage.

The 2025 season was arguably the best in BiliBili's history. With second-year head coach bail, the org achieved a second-place finish in Stage 1 to qualify for its first-ever Masters event in Masters Toronto. While the run in Canada lasted just three games, BiliBili kept its foot on the gas, following up a 4-1 record in the group stage with a trophy-lifting campaign in the playoffs, handing the organization its first VCT trophy and qualifying for its third consecutive Champions.

Since the end of the season, signing Muggle to replace bail has been the only roster move for BiliBili.
