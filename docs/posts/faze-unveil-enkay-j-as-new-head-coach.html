FaZe unveil enkay J as new head coach

FaZe have announced that Niclas "⁠enkay J⁠" Krumhorn has been named the new head coach of their Counter-Strike team. The 36-year-old will fill the vacancy created by Filip "⁠NEO⁠" Kubski's dismissal last month.

enkay J had been on the lookout for a new challenge since March, when ENCE benched their entire roster and announced plans to build an all-Finish team. He had been in charge of ENCE's team since September 2024, notably guiding it to the title at Elisa Masters Espoo 2024.

Previously, he worked for MOUZ, Envy and G2 as an analyst, and coached ALTERNATE aTTaX, Sprout, BIG and Entropiq.

enkay J inherits a FaZe squad in disarray after a difficult start to the year that culminated in the team's failure to qualify for the Cologne Major — snapping a perfect run of 16 Major appearances since the North American organization entered CS in 2016.

The future of FaZe's lineup is the subject of much conjecture following reports that Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen is on his way to Falcons after IEM Rio. Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken has since effectively confirmed his teammate's departure by saying that the Danish captain's move "was sudden" and that he "wasn't kicked."

With no Major on the horizon, FaZe will be hoping to pick up some much-needed LAN wins during their upcoming trip to the United States for BLAST Rivals (April 29-May 3) and IEM Atlanta (May 11-17). The question now is whether the team will pull the trigger on a second player swap as they look to start a new cycle with a clean slate.
