3DMAX's Major campaign goes up in smoke after brutal loss to B8

The Frenchmen couldn't get anything going on Ancient and Dust2 in the do-or-die matchup.

3DMAX are out of the running for a spot at the IEM Cologne Major after losing to B8 in the consolidation final of Parken Challenger Championship.

The Ukrainian team sat below the Frenchmen and just below the invite threshold in our predicted ranking before the series, making it a must-win game for both teams to salvage their campaigns.

B8 started the match on rocky footing after losing 8-13 on 3DMAX's Inferno pick, but came roaring back with dominant 13-3 wins on Ancient and Dust2 — adding insult to injury by closing the latter with a 2v5 — to reverse-sweep the series.

Frustration was clear for 3DMAX as the series wore on and they saw their Cologne hopes slip away, with the loss punctuating what has been a dreary period of results. The Frenchmen were ranked inside the top 15 on the VRS at the start of the year, but slipped down the standings after dismal results at a handful of LANs.

The swap of Alexandre "⁠bodyy⁠" Pianaro for Kévin "⁠misutaaa⁠" Rabier didn't turn that slide around, with the team put in the danger zone after a Stage 2 exit in ESL Pro League Season 23. Their Major campaign took a turn for the worse at Urban Riga Open Season 3, where they were by far the highest-ranked team but suffered losses to FOKUS and Phantom to leave their hopes resting on a run at Parken Challenger.

A loss to BESTIA in the group stage, and then again in the upper bracket final of the playoffs, left 3DMAX on the brink of an invite to Cologne, but they couldn't repeat their win over B8 from the upper quarters and are left with no chance to reclaim points.
