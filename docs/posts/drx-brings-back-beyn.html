DRX brings back BeYN

DRX DRX Korea Rank #4 Flashback Cho Min-hyuk (조민혁) MaKo Kim Myeong-gwan (김명관) free1ng No Ha-jun (노하준) HYUNMIN Song Hyun-min (송현민) BeYN Kang Ha-bin (강하빈) has announced the return of Kang "BeYN" Ha-bin (강하빈) to their starting roster. He will take the place of Na "Athan" Ha-jun (나하준), who has decided to step down from the starting roster and compete with DRX Prospects DRX Prospects Korea Unranked SacrificE Athan Na Ha-jun (나하준) in 2025.

After a number of years with DRX Prospects, BeYN finally got his chance on the main roster in 2024, coming in to replace Flashback after Kickoff. BeYN's addition helped DRX retain their place in the upper echelons of VCT Pacific despite moving on from their longstanding starting five. DRX placed second in Stage 2, and finished the season with a top six finish at Champions. Following the season's conclusion, BeYN was let go from the team as DRX planned to rebuild for 2025.

Athan was the final piece of DRX's refreshed 2025 roster. Although his time with the team was short, he participated in multiple off season events. Athan debuted with DRX at the TEN Asia Invitational, where they found a win against the eventual tournament winners of Gen.G Gen.G Korea Rank #1 Foxy9 Jung Jae-sung (정재성) t3xture Kim Na-ra (김나라) Munchkin Byeon Sang-beom (변상범) yoman Chae Young-moon (채영문) Karon Kim Won-tae (김원태) , but ended up finishing in third. DRX later played in the Radiant Asia Invitational and SOOP Valorant League tournaments, placing in the top four of both. Finally, Athan played among the DRX Changers DRX Changers Japan Rank #63 lucky Dia Healing Won Yoo-jin (원유진) YOME Ache roster at the Pacific Spotlight Series, placing third.
