Aleksib's silver lining

Live

When questioned about the difficult Vitality matchup, Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen went with the usual mantra most seem to tell themselves when going up against the team up for the title of greatest of all time.

"We need to understand that we have nothing to lose now," the Finn said. "We want to win very hard; we're always doing twice the amount of work when we're going to face them next, to understand how we can beat them."

"We just can't be nervous tomorrow. We need to play with the same comfort level that we're playing these other teams, and to understand that with three tournaments remaining for us, Vitality is going to be in each one, and it seems like we're playing against them and they're the final boss, so let's just enjoy it."

The history between Vitality and NAVI isn't exactly encouraging

Live

An 18-1 record in maps dating back to Natus Vincere's international move and Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan's arrival to Vitality in mid-2023 paints a pretty bleak picture of what we can expect today.

However, Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire says Vitality shouldn't count their chickens before they hatch.

"I'm never sure if I'm comfortable playing against NAVI. Of course the stats show it, but in my head, I'm never like that. It's another game, it's a new opportunity for us to win. And it's a grand final, a grand final is never like the other games. So for us it's going to be a tough one."

"If we play our A game as we did on Mirage, I think people have no chance. But will we bring our A game tomorrow? That's the question."
