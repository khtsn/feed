nifee handed four-year ban by ESIC for match manipulation and betting-related corruption

The Esports Integrity Commission (ESIC) has announced it has issued a four-year ban to benched Inner Circle rifler Dmytro "⁠nifee⁠" Tediashvili after conducting "a detailed investigation into match manipulation and betting-related corruption."

nifee was "temporarily" moved to Inner Circle's bench on October 22, with the organization citing "personal matters" and expressing hope for his return.

In a press release, the esports watchdog explained that it found a pattern of unusual incidents in Inner Circle's matches at ESL Pro League Season 22 related to repeated deaths to molotovs and incendiary grenades, which coincided with "unusual spikes in betting volume on the relevant in-game outcome market."

Update 12:12 : Inner Circle have released a statement, saying that they never even acquired nifee from TNL in 2025 due to "doubts regarding the integrity of the player."

As for their October benching, they said: "After receiving information about suspicious activity from ESIC, we decided to place the player on the bench."

ESIC stated that those bets came from newly created, dormant, and high-value accounts, and were outside of the expected baseline for that market.

"In parallel, gameplay review and expert analysis identified repeated decision-making patterns that were inconsistent with normal competitive behaviour, including instances where the player was assessed as unnecessarily exposing themselves to damage without meaningful competitive engagement," it added.

Proposition bets allow users to bet on specific in-game incidents, such as winning a pistol round or a team dealing a certain amount of utility damage, rather than on a match result itself. ESIC affirmed its commitment to taking enforcement action on prop markets, outlining its "acute integrity risk due to their susceptibility to manipulation through isolated incidents."

The watchdog added that nifee initially denied breaching ESIC Codes in his first interview, he "ultimately admitted to the conduct and provided assistance relevant to the broader investigation."

For his cooperation, nifee was sanctioned for a period of four years instead of five, meaning he will be prohibited from participating in all ESIC member events from October 21, 2025, to October 20, 2029.
