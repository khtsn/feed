FL4MUS: "I'm just happy to play good CS. I was on the bench, and this is the Major playoffs, insane"

BetBoom completed a Cinderella run from Stage 1 of the IEM Cologne Major all the way to the playoffs, doing so despite playing the last several months with two stand-ins and using yet another roster composition at the Major after Pavel "⁠S1ren⁠" Ogloblin was ruled out due to visa issues.

"The only thing that can help us that we can take from those three stages is the way we've been leveling up our game; we have lots of trust in each other," Timur "⁠FL4MUS⁠" Marev told HLTV on the media day ahead of the playoffs.

"Since we played a lot in this tournament, we started to learn [about] each other and understand each other more. But it's only in this tournament, because before the tournament there was no preparation (laughs)."

The 21-year-old rifler was loaned out to OG at the start of the year, but returned to Virtus.pro's bench in March. His future after the Major remains uncertain, but he says he is just happy for the chance to show himself and play in the playoffs.

"I am just happy to play CS," he said. "I was on the bench for a long time, and right now I'm able to play Counter-Strike, play good Counter-Strike, play at a good Counter-Strike event (smiles). This is the Major playoffs, this is insane. But my future, I don't know. We will see. I will just see what life is going to bring to me."

Here on media day ahead of the playoffs. Bit of an unexpected run to the playoffs for you, but I'm sure you're happy with it. Just talk to me about your feelings about making this run and heading into the LANXESS Arena.

Tired. Obviously everybody is just happy to be here, to play. To show that level of CS that we are actually able to show, and we are showing, lots of the work we put in. Sorry, I'm tired (laughs). I'm just happy, there is nothing to add.

Probably just because I am not a really, really bad player. I can do something, probably that's why

Tell me how you've been able to show this level of CS so quickly without really having a lot of practice with this lineup, with you even as a stand-in.

We had only one day of practice with this lineup and it was already at IEM Cologne, before the Major, I mean. During the Major we've been practicing a lot. This is the understanding of the game, you prepare a little bit of stuff for yourself individually, and you see what is happening on the map. It's all coming from your level of understanding, I would say.

Even though, before the Major, I didn't know that I was coming — I wasn't even practicing — and two days before the Major, I knew that I would come to play and I played FACEIT for two days. I mean, I don't know how to answer that question (laughs). Probably just because I am not a really, really bad player. I can do something, probably that's why.

What has that uncertainty been like for you? You were on the bench for a bit, you've been coming in as a stand-in for a couple of events for them. How has it been for you playing with that uncertainty about your future and what's going to happen?

I am just happy to play CS. I was on the bench for a long time, and right now I'm able to play Counter-Strike, play good Counter-Strike, play at a good Counter-Strike event (smiles). This is the Major playoffs, this is insane. But my future, I don't know. We will see. I will just see what life is going to bring to me.

Earlier at this event, in early stages, Magnojez was having a really good event, Boombl4 was having a really good event. This stage it's been more of a team-wide performance, and everyone stepped up at different times. How have you felt the team elevate? How have things changed from Stage 1 to now?

Teams got better, opponents got better. That is probably the reason. Against the teams in Stage 1 and 2, people are letting you destroy them. They're just letting you play good. When you're playing against the teams we played — MongolZ, FURIA, Vitality, Falcons, and FUT — this is insane.

If you look at the last [stages], there is no such team as those teams. Against these teams, everybody should be on point and they will not let you win easily, so that's probably why our team became more balanced statistic-wise.

We've been leveling up our game; we have lots of trust in each other. Since we played a lot in this tournament, we started to understand each other more

As you said, this run has been crazy. The teams that you've had to play in this stage have been really difficult. How has that prepared you for the playoffs? Because it's just going to get more difficult even though on your side of the bracket, it's the easier teams in a sense.

I don't think that there is... I mean, from the side of the viewer, for sure there is an easy side and a hard side, but for me, it doesn't matter. When you are in the playoffs of the Major, it doesn't matter who you're playing against. You are going to play against the second or third-best team in the world anyway, you will somehow play against them.

The only thing that can help us that we can take from those three stages is the way we've been leveling up our game; we have lots of trust in each other. Since we played a lot in this tournament, we started to learn [about] each other and understand each other more. But it's only in this tournament, because before the tournament there was no preparation (laughs). I don't like to make predictions, whether it will help us or not, I don't know. I don't like predictions.

With S1ren out, the role balance has been a little bit strange, a lot more aggressive players at this event for you. How has it been playing with d1Ledez and trying to balance the roles, since you have some different ones here?

Hard, for sure. There are some maps that have been harder for me, some maps where it was easier for me, but overall it's nice. If you know how to play at least at some level — I'm not saying that I'm a great player, I'm just saying that I can play — and if you can play, you can probably play not only one position on the map, you can play everywhere.

Lastly, going into your first quarter-final match, it's going to be against Aurora. What are your thoughts on that game?

We have the chance to beat them, they have a chance to beat us. Nothing really interesting. We're going to work, prepare, go onto the stage, try our best, and bring our energy. And then we don't know what life is going to bring us, how we're going to feel, or how we're going to play. Everything is going to depend on us and we will see on the 18th.
