GamerLegion move to Rivals semis over Astralis

GamerLegion have locked down a spot in the semi-finals of BLAST Rivals 2026 Season 1, taking down Astralis in their first notable LAN playoff match of the year in 2-0 fashion, with a solid win on Overpass (13-8) followed up by an impressive comeback on their pick of Inferno (16-14).

GamerLegion, who secured their playoff spot in the first place after a 2-1 victory over FURIA, now have a daunting task in front of them as they are slated to face Vitality in their semi-final matchup. The two sides clashed earlier in the year on LAN at BLAST Bounty 2026 Season 1 Finals, where GamerLegion faltered 0-2 to Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's troops while keeping the map scores close.

The series kicked off on Astralis' map pick of Overpass, where Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen's men immediately fell on the back foot after fumbling a 1v3 to Fredrik "⁠REZ⁠" Sterner. Astralis had a brief rebound following the first gun round, but a loss to pistols fueled by a double Deagle kill from Janusz "⁠Snax⁠" Pogorzelski allowed GamerLegion to find their own footing, leading to a tied half as Astralis headed into their CT-side.

Despite a difficult first half for GamerLegion, Snax's men took control on the T-side, letting just two rounds slip away as they pushed ahead to a 13-8 scoreline. REZ and Oldřich "⁠PR⁠" Nový led the charge for GamerLegion on the offense, with the Swede in particular racking up a whopping 2.22 rating on the offense with +11.57% Swing, 109.2 ADR, and three multikills.

Astralis looked determined to drag the series to a third map, stifling GamerLegion's offense to rack up a 9-3 half to start the latter side's map pick of Inferno. Though they missed out on the second pistol round, Astralis immediately retorted with a force-buy victory that allowed them to reach match point. Facing a whopping 4-12 deficit, GamerLegion began a slow crawl back into the map, stringing together eight rounds in a row to eventually force overtime.

Astralis finally bucked their losing streak in the first round of overtime, but GamerLegion remained undeterred, cruising to series point in extra rounds. Jakob "⁠jabbi⁠" Nygaard managed to give his team a lifeline with a quadkill hold on Short, but it provided little solace as GamerLegion bagged the next round to lock down the map 16-14 and the series 2-0.
