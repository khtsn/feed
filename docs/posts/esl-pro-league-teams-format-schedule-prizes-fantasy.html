ESL Pro League teams, format, schedule, prizes, fantasy

All of the information about ESL Pro League at a glance.

ESL Pro League is back, but this time starting with two online phases, Stage 1 on March 1-5 and Stage 2 on March 6-10, before it finally moves to LAN in Stockholm for the Finals on March 13-15.

Teams and format

Stage 1

The first online stage starts with a Swiss group stage featuring 16 teams, including the likes of PARIVISION, G2, Astralis, and FUT. 3DMAX, who just changed Alexandre "⁠bodyy⁠" Pianaro and brought in Kévin "⁠misutaaa⁠" Rabier will also be in this stage, as will Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold making his Passion UA debut. Another notable debut will be Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan's with Legacy.

Passion UA

Stage 2

Eight teams are waiting directly in Stage 2, including the likes of FURIA, MOUZ, Spirit, and Natus Vincere. They will be joined by the top eight performers of the first stage in another online Swiss best-of-three stage.

FURIA

Natus Vincere

Finals

The Finals are where it gets interesting, as the game finally moves to LAN for the playoffs. Eight teams will populate the best-of-three single-elimination bracket starting in the quarter-finals, which will also include a third-place decider and will conclude with a best-of-five grand final.

Schedule and opening match-ups

The opening round of the first stage is set, with dual streams running the first match-ups on the opening day. A new round of the Swiss group will be played every day until all eight teams headed for Stage 2 are known.
