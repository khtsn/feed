Passion UA CEO on Senzu: "There were rumors about what NAVI were willing to spend, and we agreed with MongolZ on a slightly higher sum"*

Artemijs Rjabovs says the team has an option to make Senzu's loan permanent after IEM Cologne, having won the bidding war for his signature.

Update, 23:29 : The headline of the article has been updated to better reflect Rjabovs' full comment.

Update, 23:14 : Natus Vincere's Head of Esports, Amiran "⁠aMi⁠" Rehviashvili, has taken to X to refute Rjabovs' comments about Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold: "That’s a lie, we didn’t offer anything."

Artemijs Rjabovs, CEO of Passion UA, has claimed that the organization has an option to purchase Senzu at the conclusion of the IEM Cologne Major if all parties are satisfied, in an interview with Konstantin "⁠leniniw⁠" Sivko.

"The negotiations were tough because, if I’m correct, this is the first time The MongolZ sell a player," he said, in comments translated by HLTV staff.

Later, Rjabovs alleged that Passion UA even outbid regional giants Natus Vincere for Senzu: "There were rumors about NAVI and the money they were willing to spend, and we agreed with The MongolZ on a little bit higher sum than what NAVI were offering."

Andrey "⁠B1ad3⁠" Gorodenskiy confirmed to HLTV before this news broke that NAVI spoke to possible targets towards the end of 2025, but insisted that this was a "strategy for the organization for a long time" and that "it doesn't mean we will take them."

The Latvian executive also revealed that Passion owner Oleksandr "⁠Zinchenko⁠" Zinchenko called Santino "⁠try⁠" Rigal several times to convince him to join the project, and succeeded despite the Argentine AWPer being the subject of interest from three top-ten teams.

"It was very important that [try] would join us," Rjabovs said, "because we see what potential he has, how he solo carried Imperial at the Major, and we really lacked the consistency that a sniper needs to provide."

Passion UA sign try and Senzu

Passion UA made their first Major with a homegrown Ukrainian lineup around Rodion "⁠fEAR⁠" Smyk and Dmytro "⁠jambo⁠" Semera in Shanghai, but only made their Big Event debut after their purchase of the Complexity core towards the end of 2025.

This double signing, that sees them move past that core after just five months, represents a statement of ambition from Passion UA as they look to move past their top 20 ranking and establish themselves as contenders for titles.

And Rjabovs certainly sees it in those terms: "Two years ago: who were we?," he said. "We were playing United21, and now we will have Senzu, HLTV No. 13. When I was speaking with JT, I told him he doesn’t speak English well but it will be fine, and he said 'the most important thing to me is that he shoots well.'"

Senzu will take English lessons to follow the example of Vladyslav "⁠Kvem⁠" Korol, who "didn't speak good English" when they bought Complexity but has improved into "one of the most vocal players in the team."
