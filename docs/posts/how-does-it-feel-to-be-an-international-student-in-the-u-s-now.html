How Does It Feel to Be an International Student in the U.S. Now?

We want to hear about how the Trump administration’s immigration policies have affected your travel plans and experience on campus.

March 31, 2025

The New York Times is looking to hear from international students in the United States about how the Trump administration’s immigration policies have affected them.

We want to hear your thoughts about the policies, any challenges you have encountered and how you have adapted to the changes.

We will read every submission and reach out if we’re interested in learning more and possibly publishing part of your story. We won’t publish any submission without contacting you and hearing back. We won’t use your contact information for any purpose other than to get in touch with you, and will not share it outside our newsroom.

Our Coverage of U.S. Immigration

  • Conservative Voices Raise Alarm: As the administration has rushed to carry out deportations as quickly as possible, the unified front in favor of President Trump’s immigration purge is beginning to crack.

  • Nobel Laureate’s Visa Revoked: Óscar Arias Sánchez, twice president of Costa Rica, has been a vocal critic of Trump. Sánchez said that the United States had revoked his visa to enter the country, with no reason given.

  •  Legal Funds for Migrant Children: A federal judge ordered the restoration of legal funds for migrant children who enter the United States alone, temporarily reversing a Trump administration decision that had left children at risk of deportation.

  • Costs of Building a Home: Take a line-by-line look at the estimated costs of building a four-bedroom home in Phoenix, Ariz., under Trump’s agenda.

  •  Former Taliban Ambassador to Spain: Mohammad Rahim Wahidi, a lawful permanent resident, was detained by United States immigration officials for more than 30 hours upon returning to the country and remained in custody after a federal judge declined to order his release.
