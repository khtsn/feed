By Striker

Spinx: "PARIVISION can be really annoying to play against"

vs.

On NAVI win: "They are a really good team and they always keep you on the edge. We had a really bad T side on Ancient, but outside of that, all the other halves were pretty good."

On showing a bit more attitude against the crowd: "We are not one of the teams who interact with the crowd that much, it's true. I want to do it, but I think sometimes in games it's hard, you're really focused on the game. But I think when I see other people do it, it's cool, it's nice for the show. torzsi's doing it sometimes, so trying to enjoy the moment and adapt into it, but it's not something we talked about or anything."

On PARIVISION rematch tomorrow: "They have a really unique style. It can be really annoying to play against, especially when you lose. I think we are good at understanding what to do and how to play against them, we showed it in the group stage. But still, they are a strong team, they just beat Falcons and they are playing great CS. We were confident against NAVI and we lost T Ancient like this, so you can't underestimate anyone."

On why PARIVISION can be annoying: "Sometimes they can be really passive, sometimes they can be really aggressive, and then you have Jame, who is suddenly lurking with the AWP when you don't expect it. They can play on the last five seconds of the round. It's not as common. They're unique, yeah."

By king_dempz

zonic: "We just keep f***ing up these rounds we are not supposed to"

vs.

Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen stopped for a quick chat with HLTV following Falcons' elimination from PGL Cluj-Napoca at the hands of PARIVISION, emphasising his team's tendency to throw away favourable rounds.

On what went wrong: I think obviously on Dust2... we knew it was a good map for them but, in my opinion, I felt that we had it in our hands, and we just keep fucking up these rounds we are not supposed to.

It's getting a bit tiring because we work so much on it. I know that it's probably not what all people are thinking at home, but I can tell you that this is one of the main topics every time we are practicing, going through these rounds.

We do extremely well some part of the tournament, even some part of this game, I felt on Mirage T side especially we were never overextending and really trying to play as a team. It's costly, at this level you will lose games just because of that.

On if he was talking about 1v3 on Dust2: Also I think the two on one. I think there were a couple of episodes where we had to play better together, sometimes we even have to not take a fight, and it's frustrating right now.

On losing to Glocks on Ancient: We know we have an extremely good CT side on Ancient, but if you lose that second round it's going to be tough. Not just because you lose that round and get reset, but also because of the mental part. It's super frustrating and yeah, back to the drawing board.

By NER0cs

NiKo: "We are just bad. We are not winning the rounds we are supposed to win"

vs.

A frank and damning on-stage interview with Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač courtesy of James "⁠BanKs⁠" Banks:

"I have no idea what happened. Huge disappointment losing in such a fashion. We knew theyre a tough team to play against, no matter how well we prepare. Even if we knew what they were doing for some reason they are a very tough opponent.

"We are just bad. We are not winning the rounds we are supposed to win. If we give them the rounds this often this is the result, it's hurting us results wise and also confidence wise. It's really frustrating, no excuses, very very frustrated about how we played.

"We know we have aggressive players, who like to peek, but we have to minimise those things if you want to win. We have the team to afford mistakes, if it happens once a game, but when it's so often it's really embarrassing.

"It's noticeable that we take wrong steps, we sometimes try and finish the round too fast, sometimes we take a duel too soon, and then sometimes we don't take a duel when we shouldn't. It's a hard situation to tackle right now. Obviously we don't have the right answer."
