S0tF1k on future with Spirit's main team: "We will see after Astana"

Spirit lost just five rounds over two maps to secure a landslide victory over MOUZ in the semi-finals of PGL Astana, securing their second straight finals appearance after reaching the title decider at IEM Rio in April.

The uptick in form for the Russian team comes amidst a stand-in period for academy coach Dmitry "⁠S0tF1k⁠" Forostyanko, who previously coached Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets and Myroslav "⁠zont1x⁠" Plakhotia during their formative years and has been reunited with them for two events after head coach Sergey "⁠hally⁠" Shavaev was sidelined due to health issues.

hally to miss IEM Rio and PGL Astana

The players' responses to James "⁠BanKs⁠" Banks regarding S0tF1k's impact on the team have ranged from changing nothing to changing everything, and when asked about those comments by HLTV after the match, the 31-year-old clarified he is changing a lot of small things, but not impacting hally's overall system.

Asked about whether anything has changed regarding his long-term plans with the team amidst their run of results, S0tF1k says we'll have to wait until after Astana to see.

Listen to the interview here.

Congratulations, you're in the grand final. Back-to-back for you as well; how are you doing it? You came into this team and you're not really missing a beat.

I'm not doing a lot for the team, I just let them play how we want, and just construct maybe a game plan. Consult magixx for something, like pistols or force-buys maybe, and the players just do their work on the server.

hally is doing a lot, and he's doing it by himself. We're now working together with the players

When I talked to you last, it was early in your time [standing in] for the team. But now, the players are all saying different things: sh1ro is saying you've helped a lot with utility, zont1x is saying everything has changed, and then donk is saying you've changed nothing (S0tF1k smiles). So which is it?

I think I'm changing a lot, but a bit, you know? Not like a big difference from hally's approach; I mean, hally is doing a lot, and he's doing it by himself. We're now working together with the players, and we're just coming to me, coming to Boris [magixx], and just saying 'can we do this, can we do that.' We just construct how to do it in a better way.

As I said, it has been a short amount of time in the team for you, but people are seeing these results happen and they're wondering about your future and what your long-term plans are. Originally it was said that hally is coming back in after this event, so is there anything you're able to share about that?

We will see after Astana.

You faced Falcons in Rio, they'd beaten Spirit a bunch times before that (S0tF1k smiles), and you won there in the arena. It's looking likely you'll rematch them in the final here, but it will be different with karrigan. How are you preparing for that, anything different? Anything that might change in that matchup?

I think we're going to play our game, as always, and try to be not predictable for them. That's how we're going to prepare.

Lastly, I wanted to ask about how some of the players have been performing because it seems tN1R is doing a lot better recently, magixx even talked about it. What do you think of their performances recently and how things are looking up now?

We're trying to make space for players, for different players. Sometimes it's tN1R, sometimes it's zont1x. That's how the game should work, I think.
