Paper Rex punches Shanghai ticket with three-map win over Team Vitality

The win also qualifies Paper Rex for Champions Shanghai, as the top-three promise guarantees enough Championship Points to automatically qualify for the year-end event.

With the win, Paper Rex advances to the upper final and secures a top-three finish. Additionally, it extends Paper Rex's strong run of form on the international stage, as it will be the organization's sixth trip to a finals weekend since the beginning of VCT's partnership era.

The series was played out on Vitality's Breeze and Paper Rex's Split, and the decider map of Fracture. PRX won Breeze 13-3, VIT won Split 13-5, and PRX won Vitality 13-6 in a blowout-heavy series.

Paper Rex began Breeze on an absolute roll, with the only early hiccup being a failed conversion on its bonus round. With a double-Duelist comp up against Vitality's double-Initiator look, PRX used a mix of aggression and well-spaced setups to claim an early 7-1 lead, but showed signs of being human after dropping a round being over-aggressive against an eco. Despite the minor slip-ups, PRX entered halftime up 9-3 following its defender side.

After winning the pistol round to start its attack, it was clear that PRX was running circles around Vitality on its own pick. Moments of technical prowess stood out, but the reason for the score disparity was clear: Paper Rex was simply the better team.

f0rsakeN and d4v41 complete the duo ace to reach map point.

On Split, the teams fielded mirror comps, with the Omen-Viper pairing assisted by a Jett-Raze duo and Skye as the lone Initiator. Vitality, beginning on attack, dropped its third pistol round of the match, but put together a rather impressive half. While it wasn't the flashiest attack side, Vitality used a slower, more methodical approach to break down PRX's defenses. PRX responded to the impressive Vitality attack half by clawing back one round, but it was still 8-4 at half.

Derke with a sharp 4K to get Vitality started on defense.

What seemed like a must-win pistol for Paper Rex was dropped, and despite a heroic ace from Jinggg threatening to kindle the flame for a PRX comeback, Vitality stormed forward to level the series score with a 13-5 win.

After a chaotic, back-and-forth run through two maps, it was only fitting that the final map of the series would be Fracture. Vitality stayed with its usual Fracture comp, going with a Phoenix-Raze double-Duelist look with Cypher, Fade, and Brimstone. On the other hand, Paper Rex retooled its comp. The Pacific side moved away from its usual double-Duelist look that had f0rsaken on Neon and Jinggg on Raze, instead keeping Jinggg's Raze as the lone Duelist while switching to a double-Initiator look with f0rsaken on Skye and invy on Breach.

After enjoying success with a slower, patient approach on Split, Vitality looked to go back to that well but struggled to find that same level of success. Paper Rex played proactively, but not necessarily aggressively, by controlling crucial space across Fracture's four lanes to make the map a treacherous one to navigate.

That approach gave PRX the upper hand in the map, and despite strong play from Vitality Controller PROFEK , the EMEA squad only managed four rounds on attack. Paper Rex won the pistol to open its attack half, and while Vitality flipped an eco round to threaten a comeback, Paper Rex responded with a lower-eco round of its own to inch towards match point. With backs against the wall, Vitality put its economy on crutches, using force-buys to try and find some good fortune in the map but fighting against the tide. Eventually, six was all Vitality got on Fracture, sealing three blowouts in a back-and-forth three-map series.

Paper Rex brushes Vitality aside.

Jinggg and d4v41 had 49 and 47 kills over the course of the entire series, but Initiator player invy had the highest rating of anybody in the server with 1.22. In the losing effort, Derke had 46 kills, but lost some significant pace on Fracture, his lowest-fragging map by a wide margin.
