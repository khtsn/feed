Short news: Week 24

Coalesce have been removed from United21 Season 51 and all future events by the tournament organzier after a manual admin review.

The TO cited "questionable decisions and gameplay patterns that appeared inconsistent with a genuine attempt to achieve the best possible result."

The team's co-founder, Sam "Omni" Nevin, responded in strong terms, saying: "To drop a public statement based on a random "admin review" without providing a single piece of evidence to our director team or players is an absolute joke. You’ve publicly dragged our reputation through the mud without even having the professionalism to consult the organization first.

"We have always thrived on providing the highest standards of competitive integrity. I will be actively pursuing legal action against United 21 for this unless tangible, conclusive evidence is provided to us immediately."

A new women's league has been announced, the Fragster Challenger EU Female Masters, with $5,000 in prizes. The league will have two divisions with promotions and relegations, and is open to cisgender and transgender women based in Europe.

The league is expected to begin in summer 2026, following open qualifiers in July, with plans already in place for two seasons in 2027.

IEM Krakow, which will feature an eight-team playoff and four arena days, will have a 360 stage setup, Director Esports for Counter-Strike at EFG, Marc Winther, posted on X. Tickets for the event in Poland will go live on July 2.
