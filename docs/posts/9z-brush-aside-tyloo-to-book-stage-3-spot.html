9z brush aside TYLOO to book Stage 3 spot

9z have punched their ticket to Stage 3 of the IEM Cologne Major following a convincing 2-0 win against TYLOO, who will have another chance to reach the next stage on Tuesday in the 2-2 pool.

It was a well-earned victory from 9z, who were ahead the entire series (save for a brief moment on Inferno). TYLOO were undone by their slow starts on Overpass, while they were never really in the game on Inferno.

With this win, 9z make history as the first team from Hispanic America to reach the top-16 stage of a Major.

"I'm speechless," Ignacio "⁠meyern⁠" Meyer said after the match. "I'm very happy for all the guys. They deserve this. We all deserve it. We haven't seen our families and friends. We have been completely away. I think we deserve it. We want to play more, we want to get better."

9z came out guns blazing on Overpass and stormed to a comfortable 7-0 lead on the T side. It was only then that TYLOO managed to get into their rhythm, with Qianhao "⁠Moseyuh⁠" Chen leading the charge as the Chinese team ended the half chasing only a two-round deficit.

The second half followed the same pattern as the first, with TYLOO attempting to rally from a slow start. They managed to close the gap to one round, but then a key trio of AWP frags from meyern put 9z on map point before the South American team finished the game off with a well-executed 4v4 A retake.

A clean pistol round at the start of Inferno earned TYLOO their first lead of the series, but it would not last. 9z hit back immediately and were in control the rest of the half, with Matias "⁠HUASOPEEK⁠" Ibañez Hernandez and Luciano "⁠luchov⁠" Herrera combining for 27 frags and a perfect 7-0 opening duel record.

More misery was to come for TYLOO, who saw their deficit grow after a poor pistol round. Their comeback attempt was short-circuited when Franco "⁠dgt⁠" Garcia won a 1v2 clutch on the A site, with 9z calmly putting the game to rest in the following round.
