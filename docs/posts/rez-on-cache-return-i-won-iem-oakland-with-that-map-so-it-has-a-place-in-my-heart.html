REZ on Cache return: "I won IEM Oakland with that map, so it has a place in my heart"

We sat down with GamerLegion's Fredrik "⁠REZ⁠" Sterner after he and his teammates kicked off their BLAST RIvals run with a big victory over FURIA.

"This win at least will give us some confidence, and now we get to play in an arena, as well," the Swede told HLTV after the match, "and I think this is just the perfect opportunity for us to develop as a team and be ready for the Major."

REZ also spoke about his thoughts on Cache, a map he is happy to see back in the game and hopes will be put in the Active Duty pool in place of Dust2, and about the Animgraph update.

REZ also talked about what he calls GamerLegion's first big test, the group stage upper bracket final match against Natus Vincere. "I don't care if we win or lose," he said, "I just want us to play as we do in practice and go through with everything we worked on and be happy with our performance."

First big upset of this BLAST Rivals. Big victory over FURIA. You've been toiling a lot in VRS tournaments, fighting in the mud, and now you get to come here and beat a big team. What are your first emotions?

First of all, it was a little surprise that we got this invite. I don't think we really expected it, since there are only eight teams here, and mostly the tier-one teams got invited, and we weren't that high in the ranking when we got the invite. So it was really nice that we got the opportunity to come here.

Insanely good game. We started off really slowly and pretty badly. It was a bit concerning in the beginning of the first map. Nothing was clicking, we made a ton of mistakes, and everything we did in practice, we basically didn't do here in the first game.

It was a lot of nerves; these first game nerves kind of happened. It's been a while since we played, so we were kind of sloppy in the beginning. But after that first map, we felt like we got more and more into it, going into the second and third maps.

That's when everything started clicking, and we did everything we were working on because we had a lot of time after Stake to practice for this event. These last two maps, we definitely showed our capabilities and everything that we worked on.

This is the event for us to really show our fans and everyone that we're here to play and we can beat these teams

You made the playoffs. hypex hasn't played in an arena yet, and that'll be kind of cool for him. You've been around, and done that plenty, but how is it to share this moment with him?

It's great to let hypex get this experience this fast and early into the season because the more arena and stage games we play, the better it's going to be for him when he gets more comfortable, and I think that this event is a great start.

I wouldn't say this event is like IEM Cologne or one of these big, big tournaments, but it's a small step up, at least, and of course, it's good for him and it's a great start for him to gain some experience. I'm really glad to be on this guy's journey, and with PR and Tauson and these guys. They're still very young players.

We have reached some stages before, and I'm glad we can keep doing it and keep climbing the rankings because the beginning of this season was pretty rough, we had to play these tier-2/tier-3 LANs, Parken and the like, so we had to work our way up again, and it's really nice to see that we're showing that we're a tier-1 team. I think that this is the event for us to really show our fans and everyone that we're here to play and we can beat these teams.

A pretty big win for the confidence, too, going into the end of the season. You spoke about Cologne, and we're building up to that, so what is it like, getting a big victory like that in this setting?

This win is super important for the Major. It's going to be crucial for us to gain confidence up to that event because going to the Major with a bunch of losses and bad placings is really rough, and this win at least will give us some confidence, and now we get to play in an arena, as well, and I think this is just the perfect opportunity for us to develop as a team and be ready for the Major. I'm super excited, and I think the guys are super excited, as well. We're really happy to be here and show what we're capable of.

A big report came out about GamerLegion the other day-

-I can't comment on that.

I assumed as much, but I wanted to ask if it has been hard to deal with the outside noise. Have you been able to block it out and just focus on the game?

Of course. I feel like this happens sometimes, right? There's going to be noise sometimes, whether it's from the org, or from fans, or we get hate and comments, and stuff like that. I've been through it all, I feel like — in my past teams, as well —, so it's not that hard to block it out.

We're a unit in this team, we're all together, we're like a family, and we focus on our thing. Anything that happens in the background happens in the background, and we're just here to play CS. That's our job, and that's what we want to focus on.

Janusz [Snax] was, like, 'Brother, these kids...'

Cache just came out. You're like the only guy on your team who remembers it.

And Snax. [Laughs]

Of course, you and Snax. Have you joked with the younger guys about it at all?

Yeah, we had a little moment in the pracc room. The guys were, like, 'How do you play this map? What's the default like, is it 2-2-1, or 1-2-2?' Janusz was, like, 'Brother, these kids...' [Laughs]

But yeah, I'm super happy. I hope it replaces Dust2, that would be really nice for me because I don't really like that map. Overall, the map looks nice. I played one premier game on it, and I think it looks nice. I'm super happy to have it back, and I'm excited to pracc it, as well.

Back in the day, on NIP, it was one of our strongest maps, so I'm super comfortable on it, and I won IEM Oakland with that map, so it has a place in my heart, right? [Laughs]

Some people have said some positive things about the Animgraph update; others have said more negative things. Where do you sit on that spectrum?

The first pracc day we had with the new Animgraph thing was horrible. I was so tilted on how it worked, how it looked, how people are swinging and stuff. The legs were throwing me off; it was not nice at all.

I don't know if they did an extra hotfix update after that, because it started to feel better in the next days. I think it's just one of the things that you get used to in the end. It's strange in the beginning, and it's new and weird, and you're not used to it, but the more you play, the more you get used to it, and it's fine.

Right now, this game, for example, I didn't think about it that much. The only thing that throws me off right now is probably the spray. I felt like, numerous times in the game, when you spray, it's totally different. I don't know if they lowered the spray pattern or something, but it feels like when you're spraying, the bullets are not going the same as before at all.

A few situations in the game, when FURIA were spraying me, they weren't hitting any bullets. It's like everyone thinks the spray is different. It was like before, but there's something different about where the bullets are going.

It's something you have to get used to quickly because you have a ton of muscle memory with the spray, like how you've been doing it for years, and now suddenly it's completely different, and that's going to take some time to get used to, for sure. But yeah, that's the most important thing I want to nail down right now.

I'm super excited for this game, and I think it's important for everyone to get a chance at being in a semi

You guys are playing NAVI for seeding, the number two team in the HLTV ranking, right above FURIA. How do you feel about that match-up?

I think NAVI is the first real test for us. A very solid and structured team, I would say. A really good test for us to see where we're at. Last BLAST we played VItality, and back then we felt like this was going to be the game where we could really show the world what we'd been working on and how good we were, and we almost beat them.

We played a really close game against Vitality, so I feel like it's going to be kind of the same going into NAVI here, that we can really show everyone that we can beat these teams. So yeah, I'm super excited for this game, and I think it's important for everyone to get a chance at being in a semi.
