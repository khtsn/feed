The U.S. Ended Some Covid Vaccine Recommendations

Briefing|The U.S. Ended Some Covid Vaccine Recommendations

https://www.nytimes.com/2025/05/27/briefing/covid-vaccines-federal-contracts-harvard.html

The Covid vaccine will no longer be recommended for healthy children or healthy pregnant women, Health Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. said today. The decision was a significant departure from the government’s previous approach of recommending annual shots for everyone 6 months and older.

The announcement by Kennedy, a longtime vaccine skeptic, upended the standard process for vaccine recommendations, which are typically made by the C.D.C. and its advisers. He said there was no clinical data to support additional shots for healthy children. Federal health officials did not offer an explanation for the change regarding pregnant women.

Some public health experts criticized the decision, pointing out that the risk of severe disease and hospitalization among infants was comparable to that among adults 65 and older. Others condemned the decision to stop recommending the shots for pregnant women, who are at high risk of severe illness and complications from Covid. Our health reporter Dani Blum explained the data.

Chaos overran an aid site in Gaza

Disorder broke out at an aid distribution center in Gaza on the first full day that humanitarian assistance was being distributed through a contentious effort conceived by Israel. In one video, a crowd ran away as several bursts of gunfire could be heard in the distance.

The Gaza Humanitarian Foundation, the organization behind the effort, said that the site was so overwhelmed that the foundation’s team retreated “to allow a small number of Gazans to take aid safely and dissipate.”

In related news, mediators are seeking to urgently broker a cease-fire deal. But Israel, the U.S. and Hamas have sent conflicting messages about progress.

The unforgettable music of Nat King Cole

Nat King Cole was an astonishingly talented musician, but his 1956 variety television show was abandoned after just over a year because it couldn’t secure a single national sponsor. That story is at the core of “Lights Out,” a theatrical production written by Colman Domingo and Patricia McGregor, who also directed it.

We talked to Domingo and McGregor about five of the production’s most important songs and how they are central to Cole’s groundbreaking career.

Stephen King is out with a tale of stalkers and serial killers

In “Never Flinch,” which hit store shelves today, Stephen King follows the brilliant but troubled private investigator Holly Gibney, who appears in six of his previous books. The novel comes with a heavy dose of social critique: It calls attention to the dangers “of standing for nothing but your own image, satisfied by only appearing to have done the right thing,” our reviewer wrote.

She might be the reason you’re lifting weights

Jan Todd is, as Arnold Schwarzenegger put it, “a legend in the world of strength.” She lifted her Ford Fiesta for fun and became the first woman to lift Scotland’s Dinnie Stones. Todd was, in the late 1970s, considered the world’s strongest woman. But her greatest feats were yet to come.

In the decades since, Todd, now 73, earned a Ph.D., started an academic journal and opened a museum, all focused on strength training. Throughout, she helped to transform weight lifting from a fringe activity into a cornerstone of healthy living, particularly among women.
