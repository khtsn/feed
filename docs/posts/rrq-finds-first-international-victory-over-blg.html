Both teams were looking to bounce back from their loss, and recover their tournament run.

Starting on RRQ's map pick of Sunset, the teams were evenly matched in the first half. RRQ started on the defense side, one where they've been struggling recently, so keeping it even with BLG throughout gave them confidence for the attack half. However, on the attack, Levius became a highlight reel for BLG. With three 3ks and a 4k, he and BLG only allowed one more round as they quickly won the map 13-7.

Head coach Ewok gave some insight as to how the team was able to recover from the battering sustained in map one. “Everyone is used to being in this situation,” he said, “we just needed to clear our heads and realize also who we are as a team and that we are a strong opponent.”

Heading to BLG's pick of Lotus, RRQ was in control for almost the whole time. They took a steady lead in the first half, and while BLG gained some momentum in the second half, even taking the lead at one point, once RRQ stabilized, they quickly strung together six rounds in a row to win the map 13-8.

Monyet closes out map two with an ace.

The final map came down to Abyss, where RRQ delivered an even bigger stomp than map two. Jemkin had a field day with BLG on the map, with a 2.08 rating and more than 400 ACS. BLG could only muster together two buy rounds before losing the map 13-4, exiting Champions with a whimper.

RRQ on the other hand have found their first win on the international stage. After a breakout domestic performance in Pacific this year, many new faces from RRQ hit the global stage for the first time at Masters Toronto. At Toronto, however, they were unable to live up to expectations as they were eliminated in just two matches. Now that they have broken past the first barrier, the next step in their path to the playoffs is MIBR.

In the post-match press conference, Jemkin was asked how he felt about going up against MIBR and aspas . “Very confident,” he said, “we watched them a lot… and it's nothing new.”
