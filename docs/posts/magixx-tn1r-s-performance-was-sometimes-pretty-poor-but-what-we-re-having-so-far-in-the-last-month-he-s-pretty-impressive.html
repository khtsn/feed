Spirit secured the second playoff berth at PGL Astana after a hard-fought 2-1 victory over FURIA in the final match of day three. The match was made more difficult as communication fell apart at the start of Overpass, Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev old HLTV after the win, and that issue is something the team will hope to address during the extra off-days they've earned.

"At some point, in the middle of the game [t's like], guys, I can only hear from you something like... just info," magixx said. "Just info without anything else, no ideas, no setups, no calls, no asking for flashes. Just raw info. And it was an extremely bad, like an off-game, when we played on CT side. It was super bad. But on T side, we pulled off a great comeback, we played super good. I don't know how it works, but it is what it is."

Spirit's in-game leader also spoke about the need to level up as competition ramps up throughout the event, and heaped praise upon Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich as the team heads to the Barys Arena hoping to defend it's crown in Astana.

"I never knew who [tN1R] was when he was signed for Spirit sometime in September, I'd never talked to him or played with him, and their results were shaky and his performance was not optimal," magixx said. "It was sometimes pretty poor, and from what we had from him in Rio and what we're having so far in the last month, I could say, he's pretty impressive. I like the way that the dude is playing, and I also like what he brings to the team."

Congrats, you're through to the playoffs. A tough game against FURIA in the end but you got it done, so just give your thoughts on that match.

It was an extremely difficult one, being fair, because we started on Dust2, and we played insanely good as a team. I was extremely proud of how we were playing because we dominated the T side, we lost a couple of pretty random rounds on CT side, but overall it was solid. It was solid as fuck. But once we started playing on Overpass, I heard, I felt that something was not right; that the comms were bad as fuck, and that was something that we missed a lot.

At some point, in the middle of the game [it's like], guys, I can only hear from you something like... just info. Just info without anything else, no ideas, no setups, no calls, no asking for flashes. Just raw info. And it was an extremely bad, like an off-game, when we played on CT side. It was super bad. But on T side, we pulled off a great comeback, we played super good. I don't know how it works, but it is what it is.

We played a half on Overpass like bums, and a half like geniuses, and on Mirage it went completely the same way

And when we went into Mirage, YEKINDAR just pushed us, he didn't let us do a single idea in the very beginning, and it was hard for me as well in some ways. But we managed to get some full rounds, and it is how we made a comeback. We played a half on Overpass like bums, and a half like geniuses, and on Mirage it went completely the same way. Like we played pretty bad on T side, and going into CT side we played super good.

Back in Rio, you also talked about this communication and making it something that you're focusing on and trying to resolve, because you got frustrated by it sometimes as well. How did you work on that between maps, like what was the convesation like between Overpass and Mirage, for example?

I felt like there were more things said by me in between Dust2 and Overpass because, as I said, we won it pretty confidently. But there were still a little bit of shaky rounds that we lost, and I told the guys what is going to be different on the next map. But it happened extremely differently from what I expected, and then between Overpass and Mirage, we understood that we just played poorly in the beginning and we pulled off a great T side.

It's something that is hard for the enemy, because they could have closed it earlier so many times, but it was a good side, so you have to be focused on the good side you had on the map; not being focused on your shit communication during CT side, you know? So that's it, that was the point.

You beat The Huns, MongolZ, and now FURIA to make the playoffs. Not specifically talking about this match, but what do you make of your run overall, how do you feel about how you played?

I feel like every team is decent, and some of them are really good. So in The Huns, you have controlez, who has been an owner of donk at some point, as we remember in Chengdu, he was owning him. And after that, we faced The MongolZ. Last time when we played against them, we lost, and it was an important one for us. The game which we played against The MongolZ was extremely close, but it was close because of our faults, or our mistakes and our bad decisions. The FURIA game was pretty bad because of what the enemy was offering to us.

if we compare it to Rio, we're in pretty good form, and the level of the team is good; almost as good as in Rio. But the comms is the biggest difference

With each match, the opposition you're facing is getting much better, to the point where we almost lost today. And if we compare it to Rio, we're in pretty good form, and the level of the team is good; almost as good as in Rio. But the comms is the biggest difference I feel like because, you know... there were plenty of situations between the last two best-of-threes where we were not playing properly. When something was happening, when we were losing a couple of rounds against The Huns, I can understand that. It was the first game of the tournament, but you know, with each game, you have to become better because as I said, the opposition is getting better as well. So you have to step up and lock in.

Going into the playoffs, the teams are going to keep getting better too. You're going to have the added environment of the stage too with the audience, so do you think these couple of days off are going to help you reset from what you're talking about? What are you going to do to turn things around in terms of how the communication is going?

I'm not sure how many days we have, but we have to take a look because we are spending a lot of time on preparations, like me, personally, with S0tF1k. There was not so much time to watch what happened with The MongolZ, so we have to spend these couple of days practicing and the most important is reviewing what was going on in The MongolZ and FURIA games.

tN1R's performance [in the previous roster] was not optimal. It was sometimes pretty poor, and from what we had from him in Rio and what we're having so far in the last month, I could say, he's pretty impressive

You're also the reigning champions of Astana, so how do you feel going into that?

I'm feeling good because it was actually the last trophy I won in the last year, and I feel that people around me are getting better from what we had in the beginning of the year. I can measure the development of myself from what was happening at the beginning of the year and now, and I also feel and see the way that people are playing.

tN1R, for example, is pretty impressive for me. I never knew who he was when he was signed for Spirit sometime in September, I'd never talked to him or played with him, and their results were shaky and his performance was not optimal. It was sometimes pretty poor, but from what we had from him in Rio and what we're having so far in the last month, I could say, he's pretty impressive. I like the way that the dude is playing, and I also like what he brings to the team because he is having ideas. And if I am capable of implementing those ideas, he is also capable of shining, and the team is also functioning, everyone is tryharding.
