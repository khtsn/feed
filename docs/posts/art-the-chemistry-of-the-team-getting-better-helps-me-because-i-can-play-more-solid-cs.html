arT: "The chemistry of the team getting better helps me, because I can play more solid CS"

Legacy's in-game leader has racked up an impressive 1.24 rating through the group stage of IEM Atlanta.

Legacy had a difficult run in their last notable LAN outing, bowing out of IEM Rio in last place after a tough loss to MOUZ followed by a disappointing defeat at the hands of HOTU.

Despite the lackluster showing on home soil, Legacy have turned a new leaf in IEM Atlanta, racking up wins over M80 and Astralis before upsetting Natus Vincere to clinch a place directly in the semi-finals of the American event.

Legacy in-game leader Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan spoke with HLTV regarding his team's hard-fought win over Natus Vincere, the turnaround in the team's form after IEM Rio, and his own stellar individual form this event.

Or you can listen to the interview here.

You guys got a hard-fought win over NAVI, and you're gonna be starting the IEM Atlanta playoffs in the semi-finals. This is your deepest result at a notable LAN with the team so far; how are you feeling?

It feels great to reach the playoffs. It's a very important goal for us, especially coming to the Major, and just washing over the bad results we had in those past tournaments.

What do you think was going your way in the NAVI game?

I do think the individuals were very strong. I think we had some very very strong rounds. Mainly thinking about Dust2, it changed the course of the game, just at the first gun round dumau had a crazy round, a 2v5 or something like that. After that, we got another 5v3. I think it was very good, strong indiviudals. Of course, we prepared, and after that we got some team rounds, but this changed the course of the game.

I think the mentality was not on point, and that was one of the things we fixed after the Rio game

Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan

The individuals have been pretty fantastic for you guys this tournament; you're the second-highest rated player on the team with a 1.24 average rating. Have you been setting yourself up to make big plays, or just a lucky good tournament for you?

(laughs) I think it's more like maybe the positions I play sometimes. I used to be the first contact, the guy going in front, and CT-side as well, doing the first contact on the entry sites. But some other maps I play, like Inferno, I like playing crossfire with n1ssim, so I'm getting great positions sometimes. I don't know, just overall, I think the chemistry of the team getting better helps me a lot, because I can play more solid CS, and I trust them to do whatever is needed on the map.

Your last big event before this, IEM Rio, did not go very well. What do you think the issue was there, and what have you guys done since to make things better?

On the Rio games, we lost a lot of confidence, and the team had not glued together as much as we wanted to, so we broke down a bit during the games and did not have the energy to come back after tough rounds. I think the mentality was not on point, and that was one of the things we fixed after the Rio games.

We talked a lot, and we sat together, and we just managed to get the trust of everybody with the team. We just believed that we could do a whole lot better, and trust each other more, and just play the rounds. After that, we managed to get the ball going, and just believe in every round, everyone believes in each other, so I think the mentality was way better after that.

It doesn't matter who it can be, I think we have a chance there if we are confident enough, and we are really really wanting to win

Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan on the possibility of winning the event

Do you think the local LAN win not that long ago helped out with the confidence issues?

Yeah, definitely. Rio was just one of the last bad results, but we had, prior to Rio, bad games, bad losses, bad matches, against teams that we're supposed to beat, we lost some maps we're not supposed to lose. It was shaky before that, so Rio was just one other one of those.

You guys are in a pretty good spot with Vitality and NAVI on the other side of the bracket. How do you feel about your chances to go for the trophy this event?

To be honest, it's going to be, either way, a tough semi-final. Of course, the opponent is not Vitality, and that's a good thing, I guess. I think I'm just giving all my attention to the semi-finals; if we manage to get a good semi-final, and we get to the final, it doesn't matter who it can be, I think we have a chance there if we are confident enough, and we are really really wanting to win. I guess it's more about the semi-finals; if we can do good there and manage to get to the grand final, we have a chance.

Do you have any preference between GamerLegion or paiN for your semi-final?

Not really. I think they're both good teams, and they can be very hard to beat on some maps. They have great peaks. I think, again, it's a matter of good we can play.
