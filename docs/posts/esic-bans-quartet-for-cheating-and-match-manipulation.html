ESIC bans quartet for cheating and match manipulation

The Esports Integrity Commission (ESIC) has announced that it has handed down bans ranging from five years to life to Egor "⁠zLy⁠" Polyakov, Dmitriy "⁠propleh⁠" Senigov, Aleksandr "⁠Ruler⁠" Maximov and Petr "⁠timeagento⁠" Markheev for the use of cheating software and/or match-manipulation activities across several teams and competitions.

Of the Russian quartet, only zLy has been handed a lifetime ban because of "the severity of the conduct and the player’s formal admission of guilt, alongside a request for a permanent ban." The 26-year-old was last seen playing for SEAW in the Asian Open Qualifier for ESL Challenger Melbourne 2024.

The remaining players were all banned for five years.

propleh, who notably had stints with Monolith, 1win and NASR, was part of the Victory Zigzag team that qualified for BLAST Premier Fall Showdown 2023 but couldn't play in the tournament after Joel "⁠joel⁠" Holmlund was suspended by ESIC and coach Ivan "⁠Hardwell⁠" Alekseev was banned by FACEIT for cheating.

All players had already been issued sanctions by ESIC in October 2025 as part of the case involving Erkhan "⁠gokushima⁠" Bagynanov, who provided assistance to the watchdog, "including information and corroboration that supported subsequent enforcement actions against other participants."

Because of his cooperation, gokushima's match-fixing ban was reduced from two years to 18 months and 13 days, ending October 10, 2025.

This is the third outcome that ESIC has issued this month. On April 1, it slapped former Inner Circle member Dmytro "⁠nifee⁠" Tediashvili with a four-year ban for manipulation and betting-related corruption. And on Tuesday, it handed a lifetime ban to Maurizio "MAUschine" Weber following an on-stage assault at the CAGGTUS LEIPZIG LAN PARTY.
