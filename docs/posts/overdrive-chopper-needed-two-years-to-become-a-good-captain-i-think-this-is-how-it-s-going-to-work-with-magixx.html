OverDrive: "chopper needed two years to become a good captain, I think this is [how] it's going to work with magixx"

Community concern has started to set in for Spirit after the team followed up their quarter-final loss to Astralis at ESL Pro League Season 23 Finals by missing the playoffs at BLAST Open Rotterdam.

Spirit beat Liquid and 9z on either side of a loss to PARIVISION in the upper bracket, but were stopped short of making the arena by The MongolZ after a superlative showing from Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold.

But it is the way in which they lost games that truly stood out, with even more reliance than ever upon Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets to pop off amidst a loathsome period of form for Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich, and Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev quite evidently still adjusting to leadership duties.

The Russian phenom ended the event averaging a 1.61 rating, well beyond his next closest teammate, Dmitry "⁠sh1ro⁠" Sokolov, at 1.15, with an equally severe drop off leaving magixx (0.92 rating) ahead of tN1R (0.87) and Myroslav "⁠zont1x⁠" Plakhotia (0.80).

It is no surprise, then, that discussion about where the team is headed and what this means for their future has been rife following their elimination, and amidst that conversation on HLTV Confirmed, Spirit's talent scout and CIS insider, Aleksei "⁠OverDrive⁠" Birukov, phoned in for a brief appearance.

The 40-year-old opened by sharing his thoughts on the criticism that has been leveled at magixx after he transitioned to in-game leading, stating he'll likely need much more time to adjust akin to how it was for Leonid "⁠chopper⁠" Vishnyakov.

"As we remember, chopper was a rifler," OverDrive said. "Then, when he came to Spirit, he became the captain, and he needed about two years to become a good captain. So I think this is [how] it's going to work with magixx."

OverDrive admitted that was not the main issue, though, and highlighted several things the team is struggling with.

"No, there are a lot of problems," he said. "For example, if we're talking about tN1R: Before Spirit, he was the best player in all the teams he played in. [...] And he comes to a team where he is [number] five. He needs to do what donk requires, what sh1ro needs, and it is hard start for him. He is adapting for this, and we see he has good results and he is working on it."

OverDrive was then pushed further by Chad "⁠SPUNJ⁠" Burchill regarding what community expectations should be for Spirit's results, considering the form donk is in and the idea that magixx may need two more years to adjust to in-game leading.

"It's not working like that, like if you have donk, you need to win," he said. "You need to build a system around donk, and everyone needs to know their roles. If you remember the Shanghai Major, how magixx played there, how chopper played there, donk couldn't win the tournament solo. It's teamwork.

"And now, Spirit has a difficult time, and I think the managers and coaches will talk, understand what the best decisions are, what are the best things we can do to the team to fix the problems which Spirit has now."
