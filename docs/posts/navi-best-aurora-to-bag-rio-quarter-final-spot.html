NAVI best Aurora to bag Rio quarter-final spot

NAVI have laid claim to their fourth playoff appearance in a row.

Natus Vincere have locked in their spot in the playoffs of IEM Rio 2026, finalizing the event's six-team playoff after taking down Aurora in a 2-1 affair. NAVI made quick work of Aurora on the Turkish side's pick of Inferno (13-4), suffering a narrow defeat on their own pick of Mirage (11-13), before closing out the series with a resurgent performance on Dust2 (13-4).

Natus Vincere now have a difficult task ahead of them, as they are slated for a rematch of the BLAST Open Rotterdam grand final with a quarter-final matchup against Vitality, after Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's men faltered to Falcons in the Group A seeding match.

Natus Vincere cruised to an early lead on their opponent's map pick of Inferno, shutting out Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli's men on their T-side to net a strong 9-3 scoreline. Natus Vincere look to end things quickly with a pistol round win, but a quad kill from Ismailcan "⁠XANTARES⁠" Dörtkardeş in the second-round force gave Aurora a lifeline. Natus Vincere retorted with a force buy victory of their own, however, and quickly wrapped up the map 13-4 not long after.

Aurora fared far better on NAVI's pick of Mirage, spearheaded by a stellar showing from Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın on the defense. The Turkish rifler racked up five multikills and a 17-8 K/D, culminating in a 2.19 CT-side rating and a solid 8-4 scoreline for his team.

Aurora snagged the second pistol round, looking to quickly send the series to the Dust2 decider, but Natus Vincere won out the second-round force, allowing the international squad to get themselves back into the game. After Natus Vincere tied things up 9-9, the two sides traded rounds back and forth, with Aurora ultimately coming out with the upper hand, locking down the map 13-11.

Natus Vincere shrugged off their disappointing Mirage showing with ease on the Dust2 decider, rolling ahead to a 10-2 half. Aurora did not make matters easy for themselves, fumbling an anti-eco and a 5v2, which allowed NAVI to run away with the game on the offense. Aurora responded to a pistol-round quad kill from Valeriy "⁠b1t⁠" Vakhovskiy with a buy round win, keeping them in the game, but a near-full eco victory for NAVI allowed the international side to secure the map 13-4 and the series 2-1.
