Short news: Week 29

Luca "⁠Luken⁠" Nadotti has announced he is no longer under contract with Gaimin Gladiators and is open to new opportunities. The organization benched the entirety of its roster after the Cologne Major following a "strategic review" of its Counter-Strike program, and now has only Henrique "⁠HEN1⁠" Teles left on its books.

"Unfortunately, following the changes made by Valve regarding the stickers, the organization understood that the project was no longer profitable and the team could not be sustained," Luken said on X, before adding that the organization treated the team well.

100 Thieves have lost their first match with Jack "⁠Gizmy⁠" von Spreckelsen at the helm in the StarLadder StarSeries Fall Europe Closed Qualifier, getting sent down to the Group D elimination match after a narrow loss to Ninjas in Pyjamas in their opener.

The team will have to collect itself quickly as they are set to face Cologne Major champions Falcons in BLAST Bounty S2 later today.

The latest edition of Last Week on HLTV has been sent out, summing up our concerning reports from the IEM Cologne Major sticker sales as well as the month-long rostermania in one big swoop.

Make sure to check your spam just in case the email landed where it wasn't supposed to, or sign up to get the latest edition yourself if you haven't.

Valve has shipped a new update to Counter-Strike 2 that sees the new bomb shockwave blow away smoke grenades and snuff out molotovs/incendiary grenades.

The update also adjusted the bomb damage health preview to only show when the bomb becomes audible to players.

A few community maps were updated and a workshop maps section was added to the Steam China release, with the complete patch notes available here.
