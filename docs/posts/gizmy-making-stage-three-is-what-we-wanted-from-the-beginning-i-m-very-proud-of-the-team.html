Gizmy: "Making stage three is what we wanted from the beginning, I'm very proud of the team"

Monte have been one of the success stories of 2026 thus far, with Jack "⁠Gizmy⁠" von Spreckelsen and Alexander "⁠kakafu⁠" Szymanczyk creating a system helping the team punch above their weight.

After some tough moments in Astana, where they still managed to place 9-11th, the Monte that won CCT Finals and surged to their Major invite has turned up in Cologne.

After taking G2 close, the European combine impressively took out both Legacy and paiN, securing their continent an extra Major spot for Singapore in the process.

Listen to the interview here.

Okay, congrats, stage three, how does that feel?

Thank you, it feels good. It's one of those ones where the game was a bit too messy for my liking. So, as an IGL, I'm a bit like 'hmmm', but otherwise it was really good. Like obviously, the win is what matters in the end, and making stage three is what we wanted from the beginning. So I'm very proud of the team and how we have been shooting this tournament, how we've been communicating with each other, all of this stuff.

I think you've lost your last four Dust2s, which was such a good map for you early in the year. Was there anything pre-Major that you looked at to try and change things around?

Three of them were from Astana, and mainly it was us playing tier one teams and them really being able to pick apart our defaults and find the gaps. Maybe in the tier two scene, we took too many liberties with that.

Since then, we've added more variations of defaults. Also, I've put a lot of emphasis on players having more of a voice in the early rounds and not going quiet in important games. So, we can replicate what happens in practice, where people do different moves and make their starts unpredictable. That thus makes us a better team.

All of that coming together, I was still very confident to pick the map today. We ground our way through five rounds on the T side, I think we should have got a lot more, a lot of moments, details missed again, but we will look back at them and improve.

I've got to ask about AZUWU as well, because this is a crazy uptick in form, at the perfect moment. What do you think it is? Is it just shooting harder? What's been behind this form?

Our coach has been working a lot with him. We all want AZUWU to do well. He's in the star roles, he's our entry, and he's a super important person for the team. Our coach has been working a lot with him, and I've been looking at how I can activate him in strats. But at the end of the day, a lot of it comes down to him.

Especially in Astana, but then in some past games, he was probably giving away entries too easily, not making things hard enough for the opponents. We spoke a lot about that, and he's really stepped up a lot on this. Of course, he's shooting hard as well. That always helps.

As for him personally, I don't know. He's actually ill right now, so maybe that's the solution, just being ill, and then he's just wrecking everyone.

You guys have a bit of a distinctive style, in terms of how aggressive you can be. Do you think, especially against these South American teams, that don't practice you and don't play you as much, do you think it's kind of catching people off still?

It can do, it can do. As you said, we can play aggressively. We can also cut the pace down a lot. I think it can be hard to predict the pace we'll play in games, there's a good balance of fast-paced rounds and whatnot, and that can also vary between games. They might have seen from prep that we've been playing slow in some maps recently, especially on Nuke, and then we come with some faster rounds or whatever. It might surprise them.

But actually, our mini-talk going into it is that we shouldn't run too many fast rounds versus them regardless, because they're a Brazilian team. They can get very loud because they have a lot of passion, and that means they can play off momentum very well, so the last thing you want to do is just feed them fast rounds. I think if we've also seen that with some of the teams that they played in this tournament.

Our coach made a good point to be very confident, just to slow the rounds down, especially after entries, and make sure we got every micro detail correct. The macro call will be made, and then we need to focus on getting the micro one.

On Kakafu as well, in terms of how he's to help develop your game, can you kind of give me a few words on that?

The main thing he does, which I find the most beneficial for me, is the way he challenges me, in some ways. Like, he presents new ideas on rounds, presents new styles on T sides, and stuff that he's seen be successful. We often talk about different rounds and how we can integrate things into our system, or whatever. This is always good, it keeps you humble, like it always makes you remember that one system isn't perfect, and you always have to keep on refreshing things and refining details and stuff like this. So it's very useful.

A lot of the useful things he does regard the CT side. I think that was always a problem before, that I didn't have enough help with the CT. And now, he's came in and he's taking over like the CT systems a lot. He's helped a lot of our players; obviously all of our players, including me, are fairly inexperienced, at least compared to the tier one scene. Him building the meta systems on CT side has been very helpful for a lot of our players.

It's so important that on CT sides that, when your AWP is doing a move, the whole map is doing something specific.

Is that part of that down to afro? I remember device praising Afro's early round openings three years ago. Is that one of those things, trying to activate him with these openers that we know he likes to use?

Yeah. That is there. It's so important that on CT sides that, when your AWP is doing a move, the whole map is doing something specific. It's not just like your AWP goes here and that's it. The whole map gets affected by what the AWP does, so that corresponds perfectly. For where the AWP starts, we have a lot of rounds where people know what they're doing on the other side of the map.

They know if they can save utility or not, they know if they need to block if afro gets a kill or whether they can continue greeding their smokes or whatever it is. Our coach has a lot to say with that, we've had a lot of discussions, and we've really come together as a team and have a good idea of how we want to play CT sides. And afro's entrying prowess is one tool that we have.

I know you don't want to get back to your uni exams. So how much longer can this carry on? How far can you go?

Well, I'd love to obviously make the playoffs now. So it's always step by step, now we've made stage three. We'll see what happens, really.

Obviously we'll be playing all of the tier one teams. I think I'm at this point very excited to play them. I really want to see how the games go. I want to prove something, and this is the perfect chance to do it, of any. It's the major, the pressure's on for these tier one teams. We'll see what happens.

As for the exams I don't actually have any more from now. The last one I had was before coming here, and all my other ones got deferred. That's a whole different situation, I've got exams in a few months, I don't know what's gonna happen there, because there's a chance of a tournament on top of those exams. So we'll see but yeah, that's the situation.
