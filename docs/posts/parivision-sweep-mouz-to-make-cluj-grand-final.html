PARIVISION sweep MOUZ to make Cluj grand final

PARIVISION's young side are in the grand final of their first tier-one arena LAN.

PARIVISION have booked a spot in the grand final of PGL Cluj-Napoca, clinching two close maps over MOUZ (Dust2 13-11, Inferno 13-10) to sweep the series 2-0 and make their second final of the year.

MOUZ kicked off their PGL Cluj-Napoca campaign with a relatively comfortable 2-0 victory over PARIVISION, and so may have felt optimistic heading into this semi-final bout. The European side had also played up to their role as villains in the quarter-finals, so it felt perhaps they could roll with the fact they were once again booed throughout this semi-final matchup.

Instead, PARIVISION showed grit and determination to clinch two close maps. It was an impressive feat for a PARIVISION side who have very little experience in high-pressure games in front of a crowd, particularly a crowd of such size.

Jame toys with MOUZ to find insane triple kill

Dust2 was the Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali show, the AWPer and IGL hitting the flow state both in terms of fragging and in terms of calling. MOUZ started brightly in both halves, running with the momentum of the pistol win in the first and a force-buy win in the second, but Jame had their number beyond that.

Jame went 6-3 in opening duels, made numerous high-impact plays and ended the map with +8.64% round swing as he dominated the gun rounds to ensure PARIVISION started the series with a 1-0 lead.

Sick xiELO 3k rips open the A site and breaks MOUZ's economy

PARIVISION ran with their map victory into the first half of Inferno, dominating to the tune of 7-0 to kick off their CT side; their crossfires were true, Jame called multiple correct stacks and MOUZ struggled to get going. Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman woke up after a slow start to find some entries towards B, which was enough to give MOUZ something to work with in the second half.

MOUZ sought to mount a comeback when the sides switched, Jimi "⁠Jimpphat⁠" Salo and Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin standing tall to rack up seven rounds in a row for their team. PARIVISION were not to be denied, however, offering some pace changes to keep MOUZ guessing whilst trading onto bombsites effectively, in no small part thanks to Ivan "⁠zweih⁠" Gogin, to clean up the series 2-0 and book their grand final spot.
