NiKo: "We're just not good enough in playoffs right now"

NiKo leaves Rio de Janeiro with a bronze medal

Falcons' IEM Rio run ended on Sunday with a hard-fought 2-0 win over FURIA in the third-place decider. The victory acted as a consolation prize for the Saudi organization's team after the semi-final beating at the hands of Spirit, a team they had soundly beaten in the event's group stage.

Questioned about Falcons' drop-off in the playoffs, Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač lamented that the team once again struggled to perform in the latter stages of an event.

"It doesn't happen in the group stage, it doesn't happen in practices, so it's really annoying," the Bosnian told HLTV after the match.

"We haven't managed to find a solution yet, so it was just... I don't want to say it was a bad day because it has happened multiple times this year. We're just not good enough in playoffs right now, and we just have to see and keep trying to find the final solution in these games and try to improve."

With IEM Rio behind them, Falcons will now turn their focus to PGL Astana — a tournament that looks set to usher in a new era for the team as Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen is reportedly on the brink of joining the team from FaZe.

"I think it's very hard to say what we should expect," he noted. "We are going to try to get as much practice as we can before Astana, and uh... (laughs) it's very hard to speak on this, but it should be... different. Yeah (laughs)."

Listen to the interview here.

NiKo, third place in Rio. You just spent, what, 40 minutes signing for the fans? Talk to me about that experience.

Yeah, it was a small little signing session (smiles). No, I think the fans deserved to see all the players and get as many autographs as they can since we don't have the opportunity that often to come to Brazil. We come every second year, and I haven't been in an arena for six or seven years here in Brazil, so I wanted to try to go out there and just make people happy if it makes them happy.

Talking about the match a bit, two 13-11 maps. Close in the end but you got it over the line. I know third-place matches are kind of a consolation prize in the end, but just talk me through that a bit and getting the medal in the end.

Yeah, I think if it was any other team than FURIA, this match would have been whatever. Playing against FURIA in the arena was, even though it was a third-place decider, still very special, so I'm really happy that we got to play them. It sucks that it was a third-place decider, I wanted to play them in the final, but it is what it is.

I mean, for us, it's a tough game to play, you know? You want to win, you're going to do your best to win, but you can't really take too much away from the win or from the loss. And so far, we have won all three times when we have played in the third-place decider, so I dunno, they should introduce some kind of trophy for that (laughs). No, I'm joking. I'm happy that we won and that we finished on a good note as well, and it was a great experience in the end.

Spirit really changed up the way they played completely, at least against us, so props to them for that

Taking you back to yesterday, zonic mentioned that you all just weren't playing with confidence. How did you reflect on that after the game?

We lack a lot of small microdecisions; we are just making very small mistakes that can cost the whole round. We are not making the right rotations when we are supposed to, sometimes we overextend when we don't have to, sometimes we're not taking the space when we have to, sometimes we're not taking the steps back when we have to. It's a lot of small details that cost us the game, and in the end, Spirit really played amazing yesterday as well. They really changed up the way they played completely, at least against us, so props to them for that.

But I don't know, we obviously seem to struggle in these kinds of games, and it's very annoying because, somehow, it's just hard to fix it. It doesn't happen in the group stage, it doesn't happen in practices, so it's really annoying. We haven't managed to find a solution yet, so it was just... I don't want to say it was a bad day because it has happened multiple times this year. We're just not good enough in playoffs right now, and we just have to see and keep trying to find the final solution in these games and try to improve.

You mentioned it in the post-game interview on the broadcast yesterday, that everyone knows there are changes coming. This is probably the last time we see this iteration of Falcons. You have a little break until Astana when we see you next, so what should we expect from Falcons there?

(smiles) I think it's very hard to say what we should expect. We are going to try to get as much practice as we can before Astana, and uh... (laughs) it's very hard to speak on this, but it should be... different. Yeah (laughs).

The timing of making this kind of change is always a topic of conversation with the Major approaching, so how do you look at that with such a big event soon and only two events until then?

Can you ask me this in Astana and I answer to you honestly? (laughs)

(laughs) Yeah, you got it.
