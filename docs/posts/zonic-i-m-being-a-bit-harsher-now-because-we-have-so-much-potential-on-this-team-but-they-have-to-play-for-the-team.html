"We have to keep our heads up high and try to see the positives," Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen told HLTV during media day. "The best result we've had this season was with NucleonZ, reaching the finals. You never know what can happen.

"I think it's a lot about the mindset. The players and the staff, including myself, need to go in with a positive mindset. And then see how long we have to use NucleonZ for. But when we got the news, it was obviously frustrating."

The Danish coach also revealed that he imposed a FACEIT ban on players in the lead-up to this event and opened up about the team's tendency to make critical mistakes, especially in high-pressure games.

"Yes, on paper, it looks like Falcons should win every game," he said. "But we have other issues. We have issues getting the team to play for each other, having to sacrifice, playing time, not taking the next duel. Sometimes they're in a position where it's not them who should take the duel because they're in a good position. They have utility that they can use.

"And this is something that, in my talk from NiKo's VLOG, was just a bit of frustration because we don't see the issues in practice. I mean, we do, but not on this scale."

It's been a month now since you last played at an event because you skipped EPL. What has the team been working on?

Every time we come home from a tournament, we take situations, episodes during a game. It's been a lot of the same things, I would say, for the past six months that we have had to talk about. I think we tried a little bit of a different approach. Tried to have a deeper talk with the team. We also set some limitations for the team, which people are looking a little bit at, on the FACEIT stats, in terms of how much we are playing FACEIT. We put some boundaries, or I put some boundaries on the players.

We try not to take a specific episode from the previous game, against PARIVISION, for example. We try to work with the team on a deeper level, try to understand, because we have a lot of good players, to continue to put the team first when it matters. And this is something we have spent the majority of the last three weeks trying to emphasize.

This season so far has been a mixed bag. You had the final at Bounty, then top-8 at the following two tournaments. It seems the consistency you had going for you last year is not there anymore. What do you make of the issues that the team is going through at the moment?

It's a lot about what I said before. In order for the individuals to shine, the team and the foundations need to be in place. I think that in Cluj-Napoca, we actually had a really good group stage. But then in the playoffs, when nerves start hitting, we don't communicate properly. Also, the guys who are dead have a big responsibility of directing the team. Something gets off, and we make mistakes that we don't see in practice. Mistakes that we shouldn't make at this level.

It's hard to explain. Because, whether it's a 2v2, a 3v3 or a 2v1 situation, some of the mistakes that we have done, not just in the previous tournament, but historically, are something I don't see in practice that often, or that we have been focusing on and improving. But this doesn't continue when we hit the playoffs, and this is something we have addressed hard this time around. Because we have to be able to make the fundamentals.

That is my attack. It's also been, from my side, a rough start to the year. But I wouldn't put it as an excuse. I think we did well in Malta with NucleonZ. Krakow was the big disappointment, of course. And I think we did well in Cluj, apart from that quarter-final.

I wanted to ask about the team interaction that you had after the match against PARIVISION, which came out on NiKo's VLOG, where you said, 'We won't win unless you put your ego second and the team first.' Were you talking about anyone in particular? You said that you've talked internally to fix those issues. How have you addressed that?

I was talking to the entire team. I like ego. I think ego can be a very good thing to have. I want some of the players to have individual ambitions as well. Back in Vitality, I had a lot of talks with ZywOo because I wanted him to kind of use that head-to-head with s1mple as motivation to become even better.

I initiated this FACEIT ban before this tournament [...] I think that you can create a lot of bad habits by playing too much FACEIT

And the same goes here. I think a lot of good things can come out of having ego on the team. But when you have three players who potentially can be star players on any team, it's also a tough job. Yes, on paper, it looks like Falcons should win every game. But we have other issues. We have issues getting the team to play for each other, having to sacrifice, playing time, not taking the next duel. Sometimes they're in a position where it's not them who should take the duel because they're in a good position. They have utility that they can use.

And this is something that, in my talk from NiKo's VLOG, was just a bit of frustration because we don't see the issues in practice. I mean, we do, but not on this scale. We should know better. I won't mention names. It's between the team and me.

When you have a team like yours, with a lot of raw power but that is not clicking as a group, people normally tend to look at either the IGL or the coach as the problem. Are you still confident that you and kyxsan have the right tools to turn things around and make this a proper team as a unit?

The only thing I can say is that I'm trying my best every day, every night, to make this team function, trying to be harsher now. I initiated this FACEIT ban before this tournament, coming in, because I think we can... It's trying to see what we can optimize. I think that you can create a lot of bad habits by playing too much FACEIT.

And I'm trying everything I can. Whether I'm fit to do the job, I think that's not a question for me. I think that's a question for someone else. But all I can say is that I'm not lazy. I'm trying every day. And I'm also having talks with the players and being a bit harsher now, because we have so much potential on this team. But they have to play for the team. And this is all I can say.

The issue that the team tends to have when it comes to playoff games points to something mental. And I wanted to ask you about the change that happened with Lars [Robl]. He joined Falcons at the same time as you did. You two had worked together for many years. And now he's taking more of a backseat, right? He's more of an advisor. What kind of impact has that had on the team, the fact that Lars is no longer actively with the group? And are you looking to bring someone to help with that performance and mental side of the game?

Yeah, I think the plan is for Falcons to bring in someone. For Lars, I think that's also a question for him. So I don't really want to go too much into detail. Lars has bigger responsibilities than just working with the Falcons CS teams. He is the Head of Performance, and Falcons has a lot of teams. In that regard, it's beyond Counter-Strike, I would say.

This was a setback, but I want us to go in with our heads held high, and be positive when we go in tomorrow

All I can say is that, for me, having worked with a sports psychologist for many years, I think it's also a crucial part of CS nowadays, that you have someone, also for me, who can maybe spot something, who can come with his input. Sometimes I might have issues with the players, and then they can go to the sports psychologist to talk about that. So it's good to have someone working more on the mental part.

It has been announced that you'll be without kyousuke at least for the first match. It's not the first time this has happened this year. How does that affect your preparation and the game plan? Because you're practising with your main roster. All of a sudden, you get into a tournament, and you cannot play with one player. It's just another setback for the team.

It's not fun, especially for the mood on the team. Nothing against NucleonZ. I actually think he's doing a wonderful job, looking at how little experience he had in his first tournament. He ended up going to a grand final. But it sucks. We just spent, as I said before, almost every day [working] since we came home from Cluj.

We knew that this tournament was going to be important. It's the same as always when we start a season. We talk about where we want to peak, or at least which tournaments we treat as important, where we spend extra time as a team. And this tournament was at the top of the list because it's before the cutoff. We all know that Stage 3 at the Major is better than Stage 2. So it's a big blow for us. But we have to keep our heads up high and try to see the positives. The best result we've had this season was with NucleonZ, reaching the finals. You never know what can happen.

I think it's a lot about the mindset. The players and the staff, including myself, need to go in with a positive mindset. And then see how long we have to use NucleonZ for. But when we got the news, it was obviously frustrating. Because we've put in a lot of work and now we have to adjust. And it's also a vital piece of the way we see the game and the way we utilize the players. And especially Maxim has an important role now. He's a big part of the way we play, in terms of taking space and creating openings. Now we're putting that responsibility on a very inexperienced guy.

With that in mind, and with all the changes you want to implement, where you want the team to play in a more structured way, what do you want to see from this tournament? Not just in terms of results, but in terms of progress, I guess.

I think that obviously the progress is... I still want to see us make fewer mistakes in those crucial moments. Whether it's an after-plant situation, or a B-take, or an XvsX situation in general. Because this has been a high focus point, and it doesn't matter who plays. The four players have been working on this. But I, think for me, I want to see enjoyment, first and foremost. I want to see us not being frustrated about mistakes, trying to enjoy the game a bit.

At the end of the day, your mindset is extremely important. This was a setback, but I want us to go in with our heads held high, and be positive when we go in tomorrow. Then we'll see how far it takes us. But of course, on some of the things we've been working on, I want to see better communication than I've seen. And also, as I said before, better discipline in the right moments.
