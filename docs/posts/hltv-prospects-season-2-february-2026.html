HLTV Prospects Season 2: February 2026

FUT stars Dmytro "⁠dem0n⁠" Myroshnychenko and Džiugas "⁠dziugss⁠" Steponavičius break into the top five in our latest release of the HLTV Prospects report, the latter of whom will receive a spotlight as February's Prospect of the Month.

The latest list uses data primarily from the six months preceding February 9, 2026, and we will use the same algorithm each month to easily see which prospect stocks are rising and falling.

Emil "⁠nota⁠" Moskvitin breaks into the top ten after his fantastic grand final at BLAST Bounty (though his rise was halted after struggling to match those heights in Krakow), while the biggest movers are Klimentii "⁠kl1m⁠" Krivosheev and Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija.

MIBR sniper kl1m has been dominating the Brazilian LAN scene since he moved across the pond, and has a 1.26 rating in the six-month period this report uses. He's fired his team to four LAN wins, and now just needs to prove he can carry that form into European elite tournaments.

Krabeni, meanwhile, did just that with a 1.11 rating to start 2026 and an impressive 1.14 at IEM Krakow (including a 1.66 rating against FURIA). FUT's in-game leader does not receive a boost from his role in this ranking, but still did enough as a fragger to make it to 8th with a 1.24 rating against top 10 teams in the last six months (8 maps; 1.12 vs top 20 in 26).

Ilya "⁠flouzer⁠" Selifonov is the other player to jump more than ten places, jumping to 18th after a great start to 2026 with Nuclear TigeRES. But the biggest jump came for Spirit Academy graduate and once-Cloud9 tryout Daniel "⁠syph0⁠" Ageyev from outside the list to 13th, who has been fantastic for Nemiga thus far this year.

Several other new players also made the list: former Aurora AWPer Vladislav "⁠lattykk⁠" Vydrin is still eligible and started 2026 well enough to climb to 23rd, while Daniil "⁠Sdaim⁠" Tupov, Vladislav "⁠Alv⁠" Vorokhobov, and Jacob "⁠jocab⁠" Nerheden also made it into the top 34 Europeans.

Outside of Counter-Strike's heartland, Ozzy "⁠blood⁠" Rose made it in for Oceania, while João "⁠bacc⁠" Pedro pipped Henrique "⁠Machado⁠" Amaral and Pishoy "⁠den1ed⁠" M. took the place of now-banned Dylan "⁠slump⁠" Naylor in the Americas.

Those new players mean a host of Europeans have fallen out of the list: Nikita "⁠cmtry⁠" Samolotov has struggled despite FUT's success, and missed out on the top 50 by one spot. Alisher "⁠tasman⁠" Marat, Evgeny "⁠Djon8⁠" Mudrenov, Lukas "⁠Beccie⁠" Bauder Balcells, and Alexander "⁠AquaRS⁠" Kovalev also fell out of the ranking.

HLTV Prospects Season 2: January 2026

After featuring two openers in our first prospect report of 2026, the new #1, Kacper "⁠xKacpersky⁠" Gabara, and Mason "⁠Lake⁠" Sanderson, who graduated the list after last season, we're now shifting our focus to one of the up-and-coming anchors in the game, dziugss, who went from being 36th in our first prospect report last year all the way to the top five in this latest iteration of ranking.

dziugss has become one of the focus points of the fast-rising FUT lineup that now sits within the top 20 of the VRS global ranking. Two of his teammates, Krabeni and dem0n, also made big leaps, and the latter was already the focus of one of our spotlights late last year.

dziugss is a modern Anchor/Lurker who excels at clutching and is plenty consistent — at least below the most elite level —, while also having proficient firepower, trading, and utility usage stats. The only thing stopping him from being a proper all-arounder is his lack of opening, as is generally expected of a player in his role, despite going headfirst when necessary to let dem0n trade behind him.

dziugss started playing Counter-Strike after watching his brother play 1.6, just for fun at the time, and it wasn't until he saw another player from his country become a professional that he thought he could do it, too. That player was Aurimas "⁠Bymas⁠" Pipiras, who was in FaZe at the time. "I saw how fun it is to play in a professional Counter-Strike team," dziugss says, "so I wanted to do the same."

The FACEIT grind started, as well as playing on a local mix team, which, after a strong showing in a qualifier, led to Natus Vincere knocking on the door to sign him up to their farm system. First, through NAVI Youth in 2023, and then NAVI Junior in 2024.

dziugss at a local LAN in Lithuania in 2024

That NAVI Junior is where the current FUT core, along with Natus Vincere's future academy graduate, Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri, started to enter most Counter-Strike fans' consciousness. Another Lithuanian was having a breakout year in the same organization, but on the main team, Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius, who ended up as the fifth-best player of 2024. He named his junior compatriot as his bold prediction that year.

"He's a 16-year-old Lithuanian prodigy, and I don’t think we Lithuanians had a prodigy of this caliber yet," jL told HLTV. "We had a few talents and a few hard workers, but never a hardworking talent that had a passion for the game and was so eager to get better.

"His stats on HLTV don’t do him justice, the impact he brings to a team is immeasurable by stats. But most likely, even to an untrained eye, if you watch his game, you’d understand he’s about that life." dziugss posted a 1.05 rating in 2024, not far from the 1.07 average he had during his stint in NAVI Junior through mid-2025.

"When I saw it, like, it was just… I don't know, it gave me goosebumps," dziugss says of being jL's bold prediction. "It was fun to read. And yeah, I'm just working from then on."

NAVI Junior outgrew their status as a farm team and, after makazze's graduation to the main team, the core was sold to FUT. Little by little, and with the addition of Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea, the former juniors have started to carve out a name for themselves to the point that they have broken through the top 20 in the global VRS and the HLTV world ranking with incursions into the top 15.

The team's rapid growth can also be seen in dziugss, who has come into his own, pushing his average up to 1.11, even whilst playing against better opposition more consistently. Of course, there is a long way to go, as can be seen by his 1.01 rating in Krakow, where consistency was harder to find against more elite competition.

dziugss in Krakow, where FUT made it through the play-in and beat FURIA before losing to G2 and Astralis

Still, making it through the play-in and into the group stage, and then beating FURIA in the opening match, was special. "It feels great," dziugss says. "My expectation was to go to the second stage, and we did it. I'm really happy with how we played the first stage. Not the first game, but the next two games. We picked ourselves up, and we showed good Counter-Strike."

FUT finally bowed out of IEM Krakow in 9-12th place with a loss to Astralis, where dziugss' countryman and former top 50 prospect Gytis "⁠ryu⁠" Glušauskas plays, but before that, FUT beat FURIA in the opening series, and dziugss also got to play against G2 and the player he is most alike in the second round.

That player, dziugss's doppelganger, is Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko, a jack of all trades who settled for the hard Anchor roles on the defense, and is a Lurker who also entries on occasion when needed during executes to let the star player take the trades on the T side.

After the two locked horns at IEM Krakow in a series that G2 won 2-1, HeavyGod made a fun comment after the match during an ESL roundtable, speaking about when the FUT youngster was trying to psych him out at IEM Krakow. "I know them all from FACEIT, I play against them every day, almost," HeavyGod said of FUT's players. "They're kids, they have a lot of hunger, they want to succeed, and they put out all of their emotions. Especially the guy who stood up and screamed at me. He was pointing at me, and I liked it. For me, it's good. It was dziugss."

HeavyGod may be the player dziugss is most alike, but the Lithuanian anchor says he doesn't have any particular player he models his game after, although he admits that he likes to watch Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool's demos. "I think he's just really smart," dziugss says, "he's playing really well, he's hard-working, and he's like a role model." And that's where dziugss wants to be; lurking, anchoring, holding sites, and clutching.
