Falcons end Vitality's match win streak to secure IEM Rio semi-final berth

kyxsan's Mirage heroics helped Falcons strike first in the series

After 18 wins in a row, Vitality's undefeated streak has come to an end following their 1-2 loss to Falcons in the Group A upper bracket final at IEM Rio.

Their valiant attempt on the Nuke decider, where they nearly overturned a 3-9 start on the Terrorist side with a stalwart defense, came undone as Falcons clinched the match in overtime.

As a result, Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov and company will get to move straight into the semi-finals, leaving Vitality in with a tougher road to the ESL Grand Slam than they had anticipated.

Cracks had already begun to appear in the world's best team when they barely survived a playoffs-deciding match against a G2 playing with a stand-in, with Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire admitting the pressure of the Grand Slam has made its mark.

Now, following the loss to Falcons, Vitality will have to fight through what is shaping up to be a difficult playoff bracket.

In the quarter-finals, they will go up against the winner of the other group's lower final between Natus Vincere and Aurora, and then need to beat two of FURIA, MOUZ, Spirit and Falcons, who have already guaranteed themselves a spot in the Farmasi Arena.
