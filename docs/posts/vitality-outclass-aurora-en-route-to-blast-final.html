Vitality outclass Aurora en route to BLAST final

flameZ overtakes ZywOo in the MVP race

Vitality become the first grand finalist of BLAST Open Rotterdam after walking over Aurora in the Ahoy Arena semi-finals.

It was a foregone conclusion from the beginning of Inferno, where Aurora took the series first after failing their previous Overpass and Ancient picks in the matchup in Cluj-Napoca and Krakow.

The world's best team controlled the map from start to finish without even needing Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut, with only Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın putting up a fight on the Turkish side.

Nuke was more of the same with Vitality winning a couple of crucial clutches on the T side, courtesy of Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool on 6 HP and Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan in a halfbuy round, securing nine on the offense and rallying to a quick closing in the second half with ZywOo taking over.

With a flawless series, Vitality have now tied the Natus Vincere team of 2021 for the second-longest map win streak at Big Events at 19 in a row:

flameZ overtakes ZywOo in the MVP race

Everyone turned up on the Vitality side, but flameZ stood out from the pack in a continuation of his fantastic form at BLAST Open.

After the 1.83-rated semi-final, the Israeli has undoubtedly overtaken ZywOo in the race to the Most Valuable Player award, now sitting as the highest-rated player of the tournament at 1.62.

That sets up a mouth-watering narrative leading up to the best-of-five grand final, after which the 22-year-old could become the first teammate to take the accolade from the four-time No. 1 player.

There, Vitality will face off against a Natus Vincere frothing at the bit to take their stab at the team many now call the greatest to ever do it, or a PARIVISION for whom Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire and company have been a thorn in the side, like for so many.
