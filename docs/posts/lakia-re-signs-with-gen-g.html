Lakia re-signs with Gen.G

Gen.G Gen.G Korea Unranked Lakia Kim Jong-min (김종민) ZynX Kim Dong-ha (김동하) stand-in Ash Ha Hyun-cheol (하현철) Karon Kim Won-tae (김원태) t3xture Kim Na-ra (김나라) has announced the re-signing of Kim "Lakia" Jong-min (김종민).

Lakia on-stage at Masters Madrid.

After a brief departure from the organization, following contract expirations for four players and three coaches, Lakia returns to Gen.G for the second time. He joined midway through the team's Pacific Stage 2 campaign, but an early elimination and failure to qualify for Champions Paris meant that he did not see the stage before his short-term deal with the team expired.

Lakia rejoins a Gen.G team that looks different from the one that he left in 2024. The only members remaining on the squad from that year are Duelist player t3xture and Controller Karon , who is now entering his third year as a professional player.

With former in-game leader Munchkin leaving for T1 T1 Korea Unranked stax Kim Gu-taek (김구택) Meteor Kim Tae-oh (김태오) BuZz Yu Byeong-cheol (유병철) iZu Ham Woo-ju (함우주) Munchkin Byeon Sang-beom (변상범) , Lakia is expected to take on the team's IGL role this upcoming season. As an Initiator player, his reintroduction to Gen.G's system is also expected to cause a shift in roles within the starting five. Former Gen.G Global Academy Gen.G Global Academy Korea Unranked standout Ash , who has played the Initiator role since his call-up to the first team earlier this year, may move back to the Duelist role, which he played in his days with the academy squad. Gen.G's 2025 agent pools did not allow for double-Duelist team comps, but Lakia's arrival may allow Ash to do so, while also flexing into the Initiator role.

With Foxy9 announcing his restricted free agency and Gen.G opening player recruitment tryouts, Gen.G is still in need of a fifth man to round out its roster, with the secondary Controller and Sentinel roles empty.
