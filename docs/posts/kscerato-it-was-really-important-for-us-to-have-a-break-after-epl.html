KSCERATO: "It was really important for us to have a break after EPL"

FURIA got their BLAST Open Rotterdam campaign off to a good start with a 2-0 win against TYLOO. The Brazilian team cruised on Mirage but then left it late to secure Overpass, which went the distance as TYLOO came back from an 8-2 deficit.

It was FURIA's first appearance since a Stage 2 elimination from ESL Pro League. The loss to Astralis in the 2-2 pool snapped a streak of ten tournaments in which the Brazilian team reached the playoffs.

Heading into Pro League, FURIA knew that head coach Sid "⁠sidde⁠" Macedo would have to step in for Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo in the playoffs if they made it that far in the tournament. However, Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato refused to use that as an excuse for the team's performances in Stage 2.

"FalleN is the team leader and the backbone of the team, so his [potentially] not playing has a big impact, but I believe we had a few issues: we were trying out some things, and it didn't go well, plus communication problems, and so on," KSCERATO told HLTV after the match.

"It's normal stuff; we're working on it. Every team has that problem, for sure. But I think Pro League was a tournament for us to test and see what is actually working and what isn't. Because in scrims, to be honest, it's impossible to practice anything.

"T side is a bit tricky. In practice, a lot of teams are doing all sorts of things. You see some teams pushing, some timings, etc., but that's not really the reality of a tournament match.

"Even when we've got things sorted out, sometimes it doesn't turn out the way we want it to, because we lack practice, so I think we're trying to improve on that; we're also practicing how to counter those sorts of pushes."

After spending some days in Brazil because of a personal commitment, FalleN linked up with the rest of the squad on Tuesday. Though the captain's absence affected the team's preparations for Rotterdam, KSCERATO sees a silver lining in having had some time to decompress after Pro League.

"It was really important to have a break after Pro League, even though it wasn't something planned," he said. "It was really important for FalleN to be in Brazil.

"We managed to get our heads straight and be a bit more humble. We have to work, we have to sort these things out.

"Sometimes preparation isn't about playing a lot of maps or talking things through — sometimes it's about putting yourself in the right headspace and building confidence within yourself to make things work. So I think it went well, I think we're ready for this BLAST. Let's go for it."

With TYLOO out of the way, FURIA will take on Aurora in Group A's upper semi-finals, with a playoff spot on the line. The last time the two teams faced each other was at IEM Kraków, where the Brazilians ran out 2-0 winners in a one-sided affair.
