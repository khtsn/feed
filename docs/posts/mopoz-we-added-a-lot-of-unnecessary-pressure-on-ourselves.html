mopoz: "We added a lot of unnecessary pressure on ourselves"

Gentle Mates were in a good position to make the Major in March, after winning IstanbuLAN and coming in second at Roman Imperium Cup V, but after a month of no officials and poor showings at BC Game Masters and Stake Ranked Episode 1, the Spaniards are out.

Gentle Mates out of Major contention after Stake Ranked elimination

"If we had played more official matches, we could have gotten more game time," Alejandro "⁠mopoz⁠" Fernández-Quejo Cano says about the team's lack of officials, "but the truth is that you can't sacrifice ranking points at tournaments that aren't worth it."

Up next for Gentle Mates is IEM Rio and PGL Astana, two Big events that mopoz and company hope they can play well at to build up points and not have to suffer as much next season.

"Those two tournaments are important in that we want them to be the ones that catapult us in the ranking so that we don't have to be in this situation next Major cycle coming into these tier-two tournaments, but playing more tier-one events consistently," the Spaniard says.

This is as tough a loss as any. What are your first thoughts after your two matches here in Barcelona?

We haven't been the team we usually are — not regarding our attitude, but inside the server. We haven't been able to win the important parts of the map that we needed, we didn't rotate the way we needed to, and we didn't have the impact we usually do.

It's just disappointing because the Major was on the line, and it seems that because of that, we added a lot of unnecessary pressure on ourselves. Things don't work out the way they usually do then, and it's disappointing not to qualify for the Major, but now we have IEM Rio in 10 days, so that's what we have to focus on now.

You had a good beginning of the season, but you didn't play for a month because you were in a good spot on the VRS ranking. Do you think not having enough game time coming into this last stretch hurt you?

If we had played more official matches, we could have gotten more game time, as you say, but the truth is that you can't sacrifice ranking points at tournaments that aren't worth it. We decided to go to BC Game and Stake, two tournaments with a high prize pool that could have helped us get more points, and I think we made the right decision to not play official matches during that time and come to these tournaments.

If we came here without enough game time, or lost because of it, that's something we have to learn from because it's not something we need to have; it's something we need to learn to live without to continue playing the official matches we do have properly.

You have IEM Rio up next. How do you approach that?

I'll approach it with a lot of energy, and I think my teammates will, as well. We're all looking forward to going to Rio and playing at a top-tier event again. We want to show our best there, and the same with PGL Astana a week after we're back from Rio.

I consider that those two tournaments are important in that we want them to be the ones that catapult us in the ranking so that we don't have to be in this situation next Major cycle coming into these tier-two tournaments, but playing more tier-one events consistently.

Looking back, would you change anything before these tournaments?

I don't think so. We were practicing well before BC Game and Stake. The team has evolved a lot. We weren't able to show it in the official matches, but the work we've been doing is good, and it's what we have to trust in the long term.

There's a lot on the line for a lot of teams in these last VRS tournaments, which creates a lot of pressure for the teams involved. Do you think that pressure is to blame for your inability to show that good work in official matches?

Looking back, that was probably the reason. I can't find another explanation. If we were practicing well, we should have played the official matches well. If we weren't able to do that, it has to do more with us than with the rest of the teams, and it's something we need to keep working on because I believe that if we can fix it, we'll show a much better level than what we've shown at these last tournaments.
