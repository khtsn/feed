cadiaN: "CS2 has been pretty tough for me in many senses, but I'm just trying, day by day, to get better"

OG are through to the semi-finals at the Roman Imperium Cup VI after brushing aside JiJieHao in a quick 2-0 series.

It's only the first LAN of the year for OG, who are currently ranked 52nd in the Europe VRS. The team has been consigned to online CS since DreamHack Knockout Stockholm at the end of November.

"We are trying to trust OG's recommendation on how to maximize our chances of going to the Major," Casper "⁠cadiaN⁠" Møller told HLTV. "But it was a bit weird, not playing any LANs for the first three months of this year.

"Because the only LAN we played, DreamHack Stockholm, we actually performed quite well. We only lost to Monte, and they ended up winning the LAN and have been playing very well since. Now again, another top four. So, yeah, we need to go to more LANs [laughs]."

Questioned about his first months on OG, the Danish veteran admitted that it has been challenging to face lesser-known teams who are "willing to take every risk possible" and said he is working hard to recover his CS:GO form.

He also shed some light on the recent benching of Timur "⁠FL4MUS⁠" Marev and the addition of Alexandre "⁠bodyy⁠" Pianaro from 3DMAX.

"We had some struggles with overall team play," he explained. "And we struggled in some qualifier games. Honestly, he was one out of five parts of that problem. There could have been other changes. He has a lot of skill and can contribute a lot of good things to a team.

"Honestly, I think he was just a bit unlucky with the circumstances and how everything panned out."

You've been with OG for a few months now. How is everything? What has been the main surprise so far?

 How crazy the landscape can be when you're playing against lesser-known teams. They all have a pretty wild style and they're willing to take every risk possible, and it can be hard to try to play protocol-based CS, or really good CS. Because they will try to find all the gaps and abuse them more than tier-1 teams will. But so far it's been nice in OG. I like the organization, I like the staff around the team, and I'm getting along with my teammates very well. So it's been a good start on that note.

I think, results-wise, we have some way to go, but we are trying to trust OG's recommendation on how to maximize our chances of going to the Major. But it was a bit weird, not playing any LANs for the first three months of this year. Because the only LAN we played, DreamHack Stockholm, we actually performed quite well. We only lost to Monte, and they ended up winning the LAN and have been playing very well since. Now again, another top four. So, yeah, we need to go to more LANs. [laughs]

I think that adamb's skill ceiling is up there with the best anchors in the world

Does seeing PARIVISION's success give you hope for the AWP/IGL role?

 Jame has always been really good, both individually and as a captain. And his systems have always worked. I'm still trying to find myself. I have moments when I believe I'm back. And then I also have moments when I feel like I've never been further away from my normal shape. CS2 has been pretty tough for me in many senses, but I'm just trying, day by day, to get better.

What can you say about the benching of FL4MUS?

We had some struggles with overall team play. And we struggled in some qualifier games. Honestly, he was one out of five parts of that problem. There could have been other changes. He has a lot of skill and can contribute a lot of good things to a team.

Honestly, I think he was just a bit unlucky with the circumstances and how everything panned out, because I think he's a good guy and I like him. I have nothing bad to say about him. I think it's a shame, and I'm sad that it didn't work out better. I think you'll also see him play really well on another team.

Does bodyy, an ex-IGL, help solve the issue of the map control pack?

I think bodyy can still speak more than he does, because when he speaks, it's really good words. I also think, if you're looking at some of the Eastern European teams, when they're communicating, it's in a very high tone, they're really pushing out their words fast and stuff. I also have two Swedes on the team, and they're the opposite, really slow and calm. Probably the best mix is somewhere in between those two styles.

I think that's maybe part of why it didn't work out with FL4MUS, whereas I think bodyy is a bit calmer. And in that sense, closer to the style of the two Swedes and also of arrozdoce. He is a very chill Portuguese guy. But I think bodyy has some good experience. Honestly, it's a pleasure to play with him. We were trying out different players, and I think all of them had good things to offer. I'm happy to share the server with him.

adamb is developing into a true up-and-coming anchor/lurker. How do you look at his performances?

I think he has really good mechanics and, when he's trusting his own ability, you can see how dangerous he is. And I still think that there's room for growth. I think he should listen more to his captain, because that will get him pretty far. Because I think he has the talent and the potential to go very, very far. I think he's also a fun guy. I have a lot of fun with him outside the server. He's young, but we share many of the same interests. I think that his skill ceiling is up there with the best anchors in the world. It's nice to see.

You've got two more LANs this month. What are you hoping to see from the team by the end of this month?

Overall progression and the feeling that we are developing from game to game and that we are comfortable playing on LAN. That communication is calm and we understand how to make a good game plan. Making the Major or not, it's about developing as a team and having the best outcome to start the next season high in the ranking.
