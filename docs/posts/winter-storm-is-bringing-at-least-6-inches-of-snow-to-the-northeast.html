Published Jan. 18, 2025Updated Jan. 19, 2025, 9:12 a.m. ET

A fast-moving winter storm is expected to drop what could be the most significant snow of the season across the Northeast on Sunday, with at least half a foot expected from Maryland to Maine.

The heaviest band of snow is likely to fall north and west of Interstate 95, with up to eight inches possible, according to the National Weather Service, which placed large portions of the Northeast under winter storm warnings or winter weather advisories.

The New York City metropolitan area, which could receive up to five inches, is expected to start seeing snow around 1 p.m. on Sunday. The farther inland parts of New York will be under a winter storm warning during that time and could get seven or eight inches of snow.

Mayor Eric Adams said in a post on social media that salt spreaders and snow plows were ready for the “moderate snow” but asked people to take public transit when possible.

It has been years since New York City has seen as much snowfall as forecasts are predicting for this weekend. A couple of inches of snow fell on the city this time last year, breaking a streak of nearly two years without significant accumulation. The city saw less than eight inches of snow all of last year, and less than three inches of snow between 2022 and 2023.

Some areas could see snowfall rates of one inch per hour at times.

How Much Snow To Expect

Heavy lake-effect snow is also expected on the eastern shores of the Great Lakes. Portions of Central and Western Michigan could see as much as six inches of snow.

Forecasters said the snowstorm would start in the Appalachian Mountains — snow began falling early Sunday across West Virginia, quickly causing slick highway conditions — and then move northeast across the region through the day and clear out by Monday morning. From Virginia through Maine, three to six inches of snow is expected on the coasts and inland parts of Massachusetts and Pennsylvania.

Behind the storm is a mass of arctic air that is expected to bring several days of frigid weather to much of the country, starting on Monday.

The forecast prompted organizers of President-elect Donald J. Trump’s inauguration on Monday to move the event indoors. Washington will be under a winter weather advisory beginning on Sunday, the Weather Service said, and a part of Northern Virginia will be under a winter storm warning.

States along the southern coast from eastern Texas, up through North Carolina, are also expecting snow and freezing rain starting on Monday and going into Tuesday.

Portions of Louisiana will see two to four inches of snow, eastern Texas could get as much as three inches of snow, and Northern Alabama is expecting temperatures to hit lows of 8 to 12 degrees, the National Weather Service said.

While the Northeast is accustomed to snow, officials in the South warned their constituents about the snow and cold and took measures to improve their preparedness.

Gov. Jeff Landry of Louisiana declared a state of emergency from Monday until Thursday to help the area prepare for the rare winter weather.

Six people died as a result of ice and snow that Kentucky got earlier this month, said Andy Beshear, the state’s governor. Kentucky, which could get as much as five inches of snow, will have warming centers available, said Mr. Beshear, who also declared a state of emergency.
