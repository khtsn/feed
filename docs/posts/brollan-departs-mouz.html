Brollan departs MOUZ

MOUZ have announced Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin's departure from the organization after two and a half years.

The Swede's future with the team had been under question ever since he was benched in a surprise move to bring in Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius on loan and promote Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze just a month and a half before the end of the season.

He still played at the Major after stepping out for two tournaments and hoped to "have a future" at MOUZ despite his contract running out at the end of the season, but the organization appears to have opted to go another way.

Brollan first joined MOUZ in early 2024 on a loan from Ninjas in Pyjamas before he became a permanent member in the middle of the year.

In early 2025, he transitioned to the role of the in-game leader when MOUZ made a surprise move to remove Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek just weeks into the new season, and went on to help them become one of the most consistent teams in the world as they made it to the playoffs at 17 events in a row.

But as 2026 went on, that consistency waned with their first group stage exit at BLAST Open Rotterdam, which led to Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman taking over in-game leadership in the squad and the eventual removal of Brollan, who had briefly stepped back into his previous roles.
