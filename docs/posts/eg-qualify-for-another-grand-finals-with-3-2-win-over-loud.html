EG qualify for another grand finals with 3-2 win over LOUD

1 hour ago

The lower finals between Evil Geniuses Evil Geniuses North America Rank #1 Boostio Kelden Pupello ScrewFace Kyle Jensen Demon1 Max Mazanov Ethan Ethan Arnold BcJ Brendan Jensen icy Jacob Lange C0M Corbin Lee Apoth Vincent Le Reformed Jeffrey Lu jawgemo Alexander Mor and LOUD LOUD Brazil Rank #1 aspas Erick Santos saadhak Matias Delipetro Less Felipe de Loyola tuyz Arthur Andrade cauanzin Cauan Pereira was as good as advertised as EG qualified for their second straight grand finals with a win over LOUD. Three of the best players in the world, Demon1 , aspas and Less , all put up eye-popping numbers over the course of the five maps. EG advances to the grand finals of Valorant Champions 2023

On Ascent, the map was back and forth the entire time, with Demon1 and Less dropping 26 kills apiece. The map went into overtime, and a 3k by jawgemo in the first round of overtime led EG to a 14-12 victory in the opening map.

Lotus, however, was a different story. In a dominating performance from EG, they used 8 straight round wins to close the opening half and win 13-4.

Down 2-0, the defending champions showed their resolve by jumping out to a 9-3 lead at half. Despite letting EG close the gap to 10-8, LOUD would respond with a 13-8 win on Pearl.

On Split, LOUD showed their dominance once more, getting out to a huge lead before settling in and winning 13-7 behind a massive 30 kills from Aspas.

Bind, the fifth and deciding map, saw EG jump out to a 5-0 lead. A few round wins from LOUD led to a 7-5 first half lead for EG, despite 17 first half kills from Aspas. After losing pistol, EG won the eco to catapult them to a 13-9 victory in a five map thriller.

EG advances to face Paper Rex Paper Rex Asia-Pacific Rank #1 mindfreak Aaron Leonhart Jinggg Wang Jing Jie f0rsakeN Jason Susanto d4v41 Khalish Rusyaidee something Ilya Petrov in a rematch in tomorrow's grand finals, and LOUD come up just a bit short in their bid to defend their Champions title.
