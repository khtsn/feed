Team Vitality handily sweeps DRG at London

Both the second seed and third seed from China were swept on day one of Masters London, but this match was pure domination from Vitality.

The series began on Lotus, with DRG opting for double-Sentinel with Chamber and Cypher as Vitality ran double-Controller with Vyse. Vitality looked confident on defense, earning the first pistol and converting the bonus flawlessly. DRG nearly instantly bounced back with an eco, but Sayonara shot his way to the first ace of the event.

Sayonara aces DRG on Lotus.

Both Derke and Sayonara started the series on fire, though DRG fought through a rough start to enter halftime only down 8-4. Vitality easily won the second pistol to extend that lead, and a Jamppi ace clutch on the bonus effectively claimed the map a couple of rounds sooner than usual. Lotus ended 13-5, with Vitality ahead heading into Breeze.

Speaking of Breeze, map two began with DRG keeping Life on Chamber alongside double-Controller, while Vitality ran a double-Controller and double-Initiator comp with Harbor. Despite earning an advantage, DRG was unable to convert the pistol and once again started a map behind. Vitality earned another bonus round conversion, leaving DRG in another hole to climb out of. After using both pauses, DRG managed to avoid the 13-0 by winning the final three rounds to end the half down 9-3.

Chronicle won a 1v2 clutch on a chaotic pistol round to reach double-digit rounds, putting DRG in a do-or-die scenario. The comeback never truly came to fruition, as Derke got an ace as Vitality won Breeze 13-4 to complete the sweep.

With this win, Team Vitality move to the next game of the Swiss stage with a chance of qualifying for the playoff stage. DRG move on in Swiss with their one loss gone, now needing to win all remaining games to make it to playoffs.
