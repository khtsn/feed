Community reacts: Valve overhaul reloading

Valve shipped a surprise update to Counter-Strike 2 on Wednesday, overhauling the mechanics behind reloading to drop the used magazine and remove its remaining ammo when reloading.

"We think the decision to reload should have higher stakes," the game developer said in its update notes.

As with any change to mechanics, but particularly for ones that affect core gameplay and come unexpectedly, initial reactions from some community members were negative.

But there were more voices of reason than usual, with many pointing out how the change could add further tactical depth and force players to put more thought into smoke spams and positioning.

Alongside the change to reloading came adjustments to the reserve ammo pool for various weapons, which saw the CZ75-A and M4A4 gain a magazine and the AWP lose four, among a bevy of other changes.

But perhaps the biggest winners of all from the update are the props and pots scattered across maps, with Mirage's planter near CT spawn likely to enjoy life for a little longer than it has been used to.
