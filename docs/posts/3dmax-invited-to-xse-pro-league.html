3DMAX invited to XSE Pro League

The French squad will return to China after a last-place exit at CAC.

3DMAX will travel to China again in just a month

3DMAX have accepted an invitation to play at XSE Pro League 2026, the event's organizers announced on Thursday. The French outfit is the ninth team confirmed for the $1 million Chinese LAN, which will also feature the likes of FaZe, BetBoom and CS Asia Championship winners Legacy.

XSE Pro League will take place in Guangzhou between July 1-12, during the summer break. The tournament will begin with a 16-team Swiss stage, followed by a single-elimination playoff bracket with the top eight teams.

3DMAX will be hoping to use this event as a springboard for the rest of the year after failing to qualify for the Cologne Major by 22 Valve points. After a fourth-place finish at PGL Bucharest, the French outfit won just one match in five across their following two events, IEM Rio and CAC.

3DMAX are currently fighting for their tournament lives against Alliance at Stake Ranked Episode 2 following a 0-2 loss to magic in the opening round of the $100,000 LAN.
