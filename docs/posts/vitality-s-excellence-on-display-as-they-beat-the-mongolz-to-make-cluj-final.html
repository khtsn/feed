Vitality's excellence on display as they beat The MongolZ to make Cluj final

Vitality are through to the grand final of PGL Cluj-Napoca after sweeping The MongolZ 2-0 in the semis, stealing the Mongolian team's pick of Mirage (16-13) before overpowering them on Dust2 (13-3).

It was another series where Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's men showed their class, and one where The MongolZ put up a good fight on Mirage but couldn't put a stop to Vitality on their home turf of Dust2.

2025's Team of the Year now sits at a 39-4 record on the map since bringing in Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool, and the Estonian was the one to lead this victory as he constantly found impact from his B lurks to put Vitality through to the final.

A best-of-five match against PARIVISION now stands in the way of Vitality and their second Big Event trophy of the year, while The MongolZ will play MOUZ in the third-place decider.

We were treated to some excellent Counter-Strike on Mirage, with The MongolZ going blow-for-blow and keeping rounds tight despite Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut repeatedly prevailing in opening duels.

Early gun round wins let The MongolZ recover from losing the pistol and conversion, multi-kills from Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan and apEX gave Vitality the lead back, and Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar, Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold, and Sodbayar "⁠Techno⁠" Munkhbold helped recover back-to-back 4v5s to keep the teams deadlocked at halftime.

A triple kill from Techno in the pistol and a 1v2 clutch by mzinho in the first gun round after the swap swung the pendulum back The MongolZ's way. Vitality looked shaky at times, with ZywOo needing to knife Techno for a clutch after a teamkill by flameZ nearly spoiled the chance to win an eco, and The MongolZ kept the game within reach.

ZywOo and ropz were the heros for Vitality as the gap closed and they regained the lead, but The MongolZ pushed the map the distance with mzinho and Techno going big in two rounds, including a 2v4 recovery with SMGs at 12-11 to force overtime. ropz went from hero to zero as he clutched a 1v2 in the first round of overtime but whiffed on cobrazera the next round to give The MongolZ a chance to recover, but multis from ZywOo and flameZ ensured Vitality didn't let their lead slip as they closed out.

The analyst desk emphasized the importance of The MongolZ winning Mirage if this was to be a competitive series, and the reason why was on full display on Dust2. Vitality brought their record on the map to an incredible 39-4 with a masterclass across the board, led by a ropz who topped the board with a 2.37 rating, the second-best of his career, after being near-impossible to shut down on his B lurks.
