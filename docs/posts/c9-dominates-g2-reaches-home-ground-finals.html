C9 dominates G2, reaches Home Ground finals

Cloud9 Cloud9 North America Rank #8 Xeppaa Erick Bach Rossy Daniel Abedrabbo v1c Victor Truong moose Kaleb Jayne OXY Francis Hoang swept G2 Esports G2 Esports North America Rank #2 jawgemo Alexander Mor trent Trent Cairns valyn Jacob Batio JonahP Jonah Pulice leaf Nathan Orf 2-0 at the Red Bull Home Ground #5 semifinals, quickly stomping over a regional rival to reach the finals of the off-season tournament.

The maps for this series were Sunset from G2 and Pearl from C9, leaving Bind as the decider. This series was defined by the unstoppable attack side from C9, as well as some G2 pistol and bonus round woes.

The series began on Sunset, with C9 winning both the pistol and the bonus round to build an early lead. G2 had to end the half strong to keep the map within reach, and they did just that to enter the half down only 7-5.

When the second half began and C9 won the second pistol round, they steamrolled over G2 with good aim from OXY on Neon and a key triple kill from Xeppaa on Breach. The second half was dominant from C9, winning the map 13-5.

G2 had to win on Pearl to keep their tournament hopes alive. C9 kept their pistol rounds perfect with another round victory and bonus conversion to start Pearl, catching G2 off guard with constant two-man stacks and pop-flash plays. However, like map one, G2 ended that run, this time off of a clutch mid hold from JonahP .

JonahP holds strong with three kills against Cloud9.

G2 had a tough time preventing C9 from taking site and winning post-plants, especially with the hoard of utility from the Astra-led composition. Like Sunset, G2 adapted late into the half to keep the map winnable, down 8-4.

However, C9 won their fourth straight pistol off of a great two-man push from mid, and they converted their fourth straight bonus to win the map 13-4 and the series 2-0.

For this series, the MVP was OXY as he outplayed his counterpart jawgemo with 43 kills across two maps, as well as 13 first kills with only four first deaths.
