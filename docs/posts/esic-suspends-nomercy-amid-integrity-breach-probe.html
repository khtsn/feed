ESIC suspends NOMERCY amid integrity breach probe

The Esports Integrity Commission (ESIC) has announced that it has provisionally suspended NOMERCY and the team's players as part of an ongoing investigation into suspected breaches of its integrity program.

The esports watchdog said it is investigating various matches played by the team, including in the Radio Popular Roman Imperium Cup VII, a tier-2 LAN held in late March in Vila Nova de Gaia, Portugal.

NOMERCY finished at the bottom of their group after a 13-0 thrashing, a 13-3 defeat, and two 13-1 losses.

On X, a screenshot circulated of a $57,000 multi-bet placed on NOMERCY's matches with a -9.5 handicap on their opponents, yielding a $4.5 million payout.

The bet in question was voided by the bookmaker in the end, the tournament organizer told RTP Arena. It's unclear whether other bookmakers did the same.

ESIC said:

"The investigation relates to concerns including, but not limited to:

  • suspicious and abnormal betting patterns, including high-value wagers linked to match outcomes and round margins;

  • gameplay behaviour inconsistent with expected competitive standards, including repeated low-percentage decisions and failures to convert advantageous positions; and

  • contextual integrity concerns relating to team formation and participant history."

Oliver "⁠kzy⁠" Heck is the most noteworthy name on NOMERCY. The 35-year-old notably played for organizations such as MOUZ, ALTERNATE aTTaX and Unicorns of Love in CS:GO. Last year, he made a brief comeback on Permitta.
