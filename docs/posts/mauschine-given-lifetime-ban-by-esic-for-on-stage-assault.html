MAUschine given lifetime ban by ESIC for on-stage assault

The German player is now permanently banned from participating in any ESIC Member event in any capacity.

ESIC has announced that Maurizio “MAUschine” Weber has been given a lifetime ban from all ESIC events following a serious incident at the CAGGTUS LEIPZIG LAN PARTY.

The suspension relates to an unacceptable breach of the ESIC Code of Conduct, as the watchdog's investigation found that MAUschine committed a physical assault against another participant from an opposing team on stage following a match.

MAUschine has already been banned for 10 years from DACH CS Masters and Fragster prior to ESIC's statement immediately after the violent incident following the conclusion of the grand final at CAGGTUS Leipzig, and is now additionally banned from participating in any ESIC Member event in any capacity on a permanent basis.

"While ESIC does not adjudicate matters based on public reaction, the visibility of the conduct only reinforces the importance of decisive action," ESIC's press release states.

"ESIC will not allow behaviour of this nature to be normalised, minimised, or excused under any circumstances."
