Christopher "⁠dexter⁠" Nong put together the THUNDER dOWNUNDER project in October 2025, featuring two stalwarts of the Oceanic scene in Jay "⁠Liazz⁠" Tregillgas and Alistair "⁠aliStair⁠" Johnston alongside two ex-Rooster members Tynan "⁠TjP⁠" Purtell and Tyson "⁠asap⁠" Paterson.

The team battled valiantly and secured enough all-important VRS points despite not venturing outside of Asia at any point in the season, and now even overtook FlyQuest in the VRS standings ahead of the Major.

Despite this, dexter knows his team is up against it in Germany. "The small goal is to definitely fuck people's Pickems," he told HLTV at the Cologne media day. "I think we're doing pretty consistently over the last two or three Majors."

Listen to the interview here.

How is the team feeling after making it to the Major?

After making it here, pretty good. Definitely a lot of tough competition no matter what, but I think in general the mood is always a lot better.

You guys had a pretty, like, weird road to the Major, because you were kind of region locked for the entirety. You dominated domestically, you had a few Asian LANs, but outside of that we didn't really see you guys try to play. How would you rate the season so far?

I would rate the season as pretty funny. Because yeah, we were region locked and we played the system as best as we could, and we delivered on that result. So I think, pending everything, we did the best we could. Making it to the Major is pretty insane for a team that came together six months ago.

How is the Asian VRS from your point of view? Because with the ping issues it's bound to be unbalanced in some ways, but how do you feel about it?

I think most of it's good. I think when we have to kind of versus Asian teams and we have 90-100 ping, it's a unique world where you have to perform on very different scales, so that part is unique, but overall it's fine.

Is there a way that you can see that this could be improved for you guys and for the entire Oceania region?

I think the only thing that can be improved is if we had a LAN center or something in Perth, so WA, the other side of Australia. Because then we'll all have 50 ping, so being over there would be a lot better and more competitive for how we kind of play these games. I think that's the best route, apart from having more Asian LANs.

You are now the top VRS ranked team from Oceania after you focused very much on just maximizing the VRS ranking from your side. How did you find that? Was it an enjoyable experience in terms of thinking about where you can gain those points every time?

Yeah, I think we had so many calculations. We had the normal calculations that we did and then we did the AI calculations to make sure that we were right and everything like that, so it was very fun kind of doing all the math to see how far we would go, and just playing for the fun of it. That's really it.

You're playing with two ex-Rooster hall of famers here, I guess you could say. Especially asap had some really good ratings in domestic competitions. Do you feel like he has what it takes to keep up maybe not the same level, but a similar level here against EU teams as well?

I definitely think so. It's more for these guys about the confidence that they need to instill in themselves. You're playing on LAN and you're playing versus people that just have a lot more investment and time to play the game, so sometimes you have two choices. Either play with your balls out or not, and that's what I hope that they can do. And that the rest of us try to encourage them.

You're obviously coming into the tournament as a massive underdog. For the Aussie region usually, the 'Fuck your Pickems' was a big theme around the teams usually. Is that what you're going for this time as well, or do you have some bigger expectations?

Obviously making it to Stage 2 would be the best outcome for us. Not going to be easy at all, but I think that's the goal. And definitely the small goal is to definitely fuck people's Pickems. I think we're doing pretty consistently over the last two or three Majors. So yeah, here we're going to keep the fingers crossed and see how it goes.
