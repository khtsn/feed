m0NESY clinches IEM Cologne Major 2026 MVP

Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov has taken the HLTV x 1XBET Most Valuable Player (MVP) award for the IEM Cologne Major 2026.

The 21-year-old was playing his first-ever Major final in Germany, but was the driving force on map one and two against FURIA to put his team on the precipice of hoisting the iconic chalice.

A poor map three opened the door for teammates Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač and Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin to enter the MVP race, with all three players performing very similarly in map wins across the whole event. The trio even had the same number of map awards (7).

In the end, though, m0NESY's lead in overall rating (1.25) and KPRW (1.10) was enough to break the tie and keep the medal in his hands.

It's his seventh career medal, all of which came at Big Events, to put him ahead of Marcelo "⁠coldzera⁠" David there and level with Janusz "⁠Snax⁠" Pogorzelski (=11th) when you include lower-tier medals.

The Russian sniper was on an eight-game losing streak in grand finals going into the Major, which added to an already huge mountain of pressure on the Falcons.

But in the iconic LANXESS arena, Falcons overcame that hurdle. Playing against the world number one, two, three, and five on the way, it's a worthy way to end their drought — and for m0NESY to pick up his first winning MVP in nearly 18 months.
