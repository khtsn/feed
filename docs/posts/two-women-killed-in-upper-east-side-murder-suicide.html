Two Women Killed in Upper East Side Murder-Suicide

New York|Two Women Killed in Upper East Side Murder-Suicide

https://www.nytimes.com/2024/07/26/nyregion/upper-east-side-murder-suicide.html

The shooting took place just a half block from Gracie Mansion, the official residence of the New York City mayor, and was related to a child-custody dispute, according to the police.

July 26, 2024Updated 4:00 p.m. ET

Two women were shot to death in a murder-suicide on the Upper East Side of Manhattan on Friday morning, according to the police.

Officers responded to a 911 call around 8:55 a.m. about two women who had been shot near a white Honda Civic outside an apartment building at 519 East 88th Street, just a half block from Gracie Mansion, the official residence of the New York City mayor.

One of the women, identified by the police as Kathleen Leigh, 65, approached the second woman, identified as Marisa Galloway, 45, shooting her in the head and lower back. Ms. Leigh then shot herself in the head, the police said.

Ms. Leigh was pronounced dead at the scene, the police said. Ms. Galloway was taken to Weill Cornell Medical Center, where she later died.

Joseph Kenny, the chief of detectives for the Police Department, said the shooting was related to an ongoing child-custody dispute.

In a news conference on Friday, the police said they had a video showing Ms. Galloway putting her 1-year-old daughter into a car seat in the back of the Honda just before the shooting. Then, as Ms. Galloway walked to the rear of the car to put a stroller in the trunk, Ms. Leigh approached and shot her in the head, the police said. Ms. Leigh can then be seen shooting Ms. Galloway in the lower back.

The 1-year-old girl was unharmed, according to the police.

The authorities said Ms. Leigh was the grandmother of Ms. Galloway’s other child, a 4-year-old girl. Ms. Leigh was a retired Cook County probation officer from Chicago, according to the police, and had been living with relatives in New York City for the past three years.

The police recovered a loaded, 9-millimeter gun from the sidewalk and another firearm from a tote bag Ms. Leigh was carrying. The guns had no history of being stolen in the city, according to officials. The police said they were trying to confirm whether Ms. Leigh had owned the weapons during her time as a probation officer.

No arrests have been made in the shooting and the police said they were still investigating.

The 911 caller on Friday morning reported hearing three gunshots and looking out a window to see two women lying on the ground surrounded by a lot of blood.

Jaime Peralta, 60, the superintendent of a nearby building, said he was on the sidewalk when he heard the shots a block away. “Like: bam! bam! bam!” he said. “I said, ‘Maybe it’s balloons?’”

Mr. Peralta said he had heard no screams or anything to indicate a dispute.

The area where the shooting took place is a particularly quiet corner of the Upper East Side. There is little foot traffic and people can often walk at night without fear of crime.

Recent data from the 19th Precinct, which includes the block where the shooting occurred, shows that the area did not report any murders through July 21 of this year, though there were two shootings. There were three murders and one shooting during the same period in 2023, the data shows.

Bud Heckman, 55, a consultant who lives on York Avenue nearby, said he had walked past the scene of the shooting minutes before it happened. When he left his apartment again because of the commotion, he assumed something had happened at Gracie Mansion.

“There’s a high amount of protests,” he said. “I thought, There must be a protest.”

The police used metal fencing to cordon off East 88th Street between York and East End Avenues. The middle of the block was sewn up with yellow crime-scene tape. Investigators could be seen loading what appeared to be a bagged body into a van, which quickly drove away.

People stopped at the ends of the block to strain for a glimpse of the crime scene and take photos. In the early afternoon, three activists from the Harlem nonprofit group Street Corner Resources arrived to hand out literature about stopping street violence.

One of the activists, Naqqash Mustafa, 28, said he had been shocked to hear the news of the shooting earlier on Friday. He said he had been to the scenes of street shootings all over the city, but that this was his first visit to the Upper East Side.

When asked if he had ever expected to hand out violence-reduction literature on the same block as Gracie Mansion, he replied, “No, never. This is crazy.”
