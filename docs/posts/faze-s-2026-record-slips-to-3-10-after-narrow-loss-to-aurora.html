FaZe's 2026 record slips to 3-10 after narrow loss to Aurora

Aurora have opened the group stage of BLAST Open Rotterdam with a hard-fought 2-1 victory over FaZe (11-13 Nuke, 13-8 Inferno, 13-10 Dust2). The Turks arrived in Denmark after a run to the final of ESL Pro League Season 23, and despite a few shaky moments, outlasted FaZe down the stretch thanks in part to winning every second round of the series.

FaZe have slid down the VRS after a torrid start to 2026, a year in which they have won just three best-of-threes — against BC.Game, HEROIC, and BC.Game — and lost ten.

A win over Aurora would have gone a long way in turning that around as the team nears the brink of losing an invite to the IEM Cologne Major, and a bounce back in the lower bracket against the loser of FURIA vs. TYLOO is imperative to avoid the danger zone ahead of the April 6 cut-off.

FaZe were left fighting from behind for much of the first half of Nuke, not in rounds, but from man-deficits. They won three opening duels against Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli, but lost nine combined against Özgür "⁠woxic⁠" Eker, Ismailcan "⁠XANTARES⁠" Dörtkardeş, and Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici in what should have been a miserable offense.

Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken struggled immensely, but two clutch wins from Helvijs "⁠broky⁠" Saukants and Jakub "⁠jcobbb⁠" Pietruszewski, as well as a pair of multi-kills from Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen, helped FaZe recover from a 0-2 start to keep the scoreline even at the break.

FaZe's dreary record in openers fell further, to 4-12, as Aurora won the second pistol and extended their lead to 10-7. But it was jcobbb who stepped up with back-to-back triple and double kills to get FaZe back on the horse, and combined with David "⁠frozen⁠" Čerňanský, led a recovery to narrowly seal victory on their team's pick.

Force-buy wars unfolded early on Inferno after FaZe won the pistol, but it was Aurora who won the battle to race ahead on the offense. The Turks repeatedly found entries on B and did well to influence FaZe's rotations en route to a 7-4 lead, with FaZe winning three clutches and heavily relying on another stellar jcobbb performance, including an incredible quad-kill to end the half, to remain competitive before collapsing after squandering their pistol win in the second half.

Aurora maintained control on the Dust2 decider as jcobbb began to struggle, going 1-8 to start as he was repeatedly tagged crossing Middle or swarmed by several Aurora members. It was instead Twistzz who came alive for FaZe, tallying multi-kills in all six of the CT rounds his team won to recover from 0-5 down.

The Canadian's heroic efforts seemingly went to waste as Aurora used another pistol and conversion win to extend their lead to 10-6, but jcobbb again made the difference. Despite a miserable individual scoreline, he found double kills in three rounds as FaZe looked to mount a comeback, but it was cut short at the eleventh hour by back-to-back doubles from Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın that sealed Aurora's victory.
