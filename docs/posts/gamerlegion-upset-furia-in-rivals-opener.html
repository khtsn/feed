GamerLegion upset FURIA in Rivals opener

Snax's men came back from 5-9 down on the Mirage decider to lock in a playoff berth.

GamerLegion have secured a spot in the playoffs of BLAST Rivals Fort Worth after scoring a surprise reverse-sweep victory over FURIA in Group B (5-13 Inferno, 13-5 Nuke, 13-10 Mirage).

GamerLegion have been absent from the top LAN circuit since their Stage 1 exit at IEM Krakow, but came roaring back to life in Texas despite falling apart on their pick to start the series. Fredrik "⁠REZ⁠" Sterner and Oldřich "⁠PR⁠" Nový were the difference makers on Nuke and Mirage, but credit must also go to Milan "⁠hypex⁠" Polowiec for recovering from a dismal first half on the decider by finding several key openers and impact frags, leaving FURIA destitute in the last nine rounds.

Frustration was evident for FURIA, who came into Fort Worth hoping to bounce back after they failed to convert upon their flawless group stage run in Rio by losing their stage matches in Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo's last appearance in front of his home crowd.

GamerLegion had a clear game plan to focus on B early doors on their Inferno pick, and with FURIA often playing retake after early brawls, it nearly paid off. But a plan is only as good as its execution, and in the late round, GamerLegion came up short.

Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato, in particular, was key in helping his side claw back from a few disadvantages, but eventually GamerLegion's unrelenting efforts in Banana bore fruit. Still, they needed a 1v4 ace clutch from Sebastian "⁠Tauson⁠" Tauson Lindelof to recover from losing two opening kills to a USP and scavenge five rounds by the break, and a takeover by Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko after the swap ensured FURIA never let the map spiral out of control.

It was a different story on Nuke, where FURIA floundered on their offense with sloppy executions and defaults. KSCERATO went 0-5 in openers, FalleN was top of the board with six kills at the break, and GamerLegion ran away with a 10-2 lead thanks to excellence from REZ and hypex — the latter putting a bow on the map with a 1v2 clutch to force a Mirage decider.

GamerLegion picked up where they left off with a 3-0 start thanks to a pistol win, but soon found themselves repeatedly running into the brick wall that was KSCERATO. The Brazilian was a menace, tallying multi-kills from every area on the map, and ended FURIA's 7-5 half with a 20-6 K-D.

FURIA won the pistol and conversion to extend their lead to 9-5, only for GamerLegion to start picking up steam on the CT side. Nearly every round began with FURIA losing the opening duel, and between some fantastic rifle work by PR and a few openers from hypex, GamerLegion seized control and gave up just one round en route to a 13-10 win.
