cmtry: "It's becoming a bit tough to play because people are starting to read us and our style"

FUT have enjoyed a meteoric rise in 2026, aided by last-minute invites to PGL Cluj-Napoca and ESL Pro League Season 23, and converted upon the latter for their best result to date by making the playoffs.

Their individuals have earned plaudits from several opponents, but with more eyes and frequent appearances at big events, teams are starting to catch on.

"We had some other hard opponents, teams who were preparing for us," Nikita "⁠cmtry⁠" Samolotov tells HLTV about his team's campaign through Stage 2 of Pro League. "It's becoming a bit tough to play because people are starting to read us, starting to see our game and our style."

With that kind of attention comes the need to adapt, a new experience for the young squad but something they're trying to take in stride as they look to surprise against MOUZ in a rematch of the teams' battle at Cluj-Napoca.

"We're not trying to overthink it," cmtry says about how FUT are changing their game with more attention on them. "Obviously we are trying to change a bit, but we are still thinking that we have insane firepower and it's a key of the game. We are trying to change some small details and keep just being in a pack and shooting them down."

How are FUT feeling after the earlier stages?

After we went 3-0 in Stage 1 we felt really good, we felt that we have good chances to go into the LAN after Stage 2. After that it was a bit fucked in Stage 2, but we still had good chances in my opinion, we were still saying that we could do it, and we showed our best and bounced back into the next stage.

Like you say, you were really comfortable in Stage 1, so why do you think you came a little bit slow into the next stage?

To be honest I think it's about the teams. In Stage 1 it was kinda… we were the best team there, so it was easier to play for me and our team, it was easier to read the opponent. Then in Stage 2 we faced Astralis, it was a hard game, and then we had some other hard opponents, teams who were preparing for us. It's becoming a bit tough to play because people are starting to read us, starting to see our game and our style, so that's why.

Sometimes we have a hard time when we are losing some ecos [...] After we get some kills on the map we start to scream, saying plans and trying to move. We have a lack of experience so we are making these small mistakes

Now that you are playing in these top tournaments more often, teams will probably be watching your demos and figuring you out a bit more. How are you dealing with that as a team?

We're not trying to overthink it. Obviously we are trying to change a bit, but we are still thinking that we have insane firepower and it's a key of the game. We are trying to change some small details and keep just being in a pack and shooting them down.

You're the team everyone is watching at the moment. From the days as part of NAVI to now you have risen quite a lot, and grown as a team. How does it feel to know that you're one of those teams that everyone is keeping an eye on?

To be honest it doesn't really affect us, we are just playing the game. You don't think about other stuff in game, you are just trying to be better and better and get into the tier-one LANs, and play well there.

As a team, what do you think are the most important things for you to improve on, so you can keep taking steps forward?

I think sometimes we have a hard time when we are losing some ecos. I think we are losing a lot of anti-force, anti-ecos, and are choking some clutches, like 4v2. After we get some kills on the map we start to scream, saying plans and trying to move. We have a lack of experience so we are making these small mistakes, but now we are trying to work on it and fix it, so in the future it will be better.

So it gets a bit crazy in the comms when these rounds happen?

It is (laughs), it is a bit crazy.

Like you said, you guys are lacking a bit of experience, you are a very young team. How do you deal with that? Because a lot of teams with young players will have an older head on the team who can help out, but how do you guys deal with it?

We are just trying to work on our mistakes and that's it, to be honest.

Do you have a goal for these playoffs, or do you just take it one game at a time?

We're not trying to set some goals, we are just trying to win every game. Of course if we would win this tournament, it would be really good, but still we are not always thinking that we should win it, beat MOUZ, beat Spirit and NAVI. We are just trying to be good in every game and trying to win every game.

Are you confident, though?

Yeah, very.

Any team you face, you will be confident?

Very.

If we do really badly at the Major and in upcoming tournaments, some people can change their view about us and they can start to leave, but I think now and in the future we will stay together

As I said, FUT are a team a lot of people are watching at the moment and are very impressed with. There are rumours that other teams have wanted some of your players, but you seem keen to stick together. What makes you want to stick together as a team?

I think we have insane chemistry together, we have an insane vibe in the team. Everyone is really good friends with each other, and I think it's a key to why we want to stay together. We did the long journey from NAVI Junior to FUT, and everyone is really enjoying this.

How long do you think that will last? It's easy to want to stay together when you keep climbing, but is there a point at which you might say that as a team you've gone as far as you can go?

Of course. I think if we do really badly at the Major and in upcoming tournaments, some people can change their view about us and they can start to leave, but I think now and in the future we will stay together. We improved a lot already, and it will take some time of course to improve more, but I think everyone on the team trusts each other and will keep doing the same job and improvement.
