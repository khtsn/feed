Aurora secure EPL grand final spot over Astralis

Aurora are headed to their first grand final since PGL Masters Bucharest in October.

Aurora have battled past Astralis in the semi-finals of ESL Pro League Season 23, recovering from a narrow loss on the international side's pick of Nuke (11-13) with a statement victory on Dust2 (13-2) and a comeback win on Inferno (13-10).

Aurora, who had not even reached a semi-final in 2026 prior to this event, will now be preparing for tomorrow's grand final, where the Turkish side will be facing off against Natus Vincere in a best-of-five. Astralis, meanwhile, will be looking ahead to their third-place decider match against FUT.

The second semi-final got underway on Astralis's map pick of Nuke, where, despite a Dualies quad kill from Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın to kick off the map, the international side emerged from the first half victorious after a back-and-forth T side.

Aurora rallied on their own T-side thanks to another pistol victory, moving from a 5-7 deficit to a 9-7 lead, but Astralis kept them honest with four rounds of their own. Aurora managed to crush Astralis's economy at 11-11, looking poised to set themselves up for map point, but Astralis stole away the round with upgraded pistols, giving them the fuel they needed to take their pick 13-11.

Despite the sour ending to Nuke, Aurora were undeterred. Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli's men made quick work of Astralis on Dust2, rolling past them with a 10-2 T side before mopping up the map 13-2 off their fourth pistol round victory.

Aurora had a messy start to Inferno, winning out the pistol round before fumbling to Astralis's force buy. Despite this setback, the Turks retorted with a force-buy victory of their own that fully placed the momentum back into their hands. Excellent rifling from captain MAJ3R and poorly placed stacks from Astralis combined to help Aurora secure a 7-5 lead heading into the defense.

Astralis found new life on offense, securing their first pistol round victory of the series to take the lead, while a Jakob "⁠jabbi⁠" Nygaard 1v2 helped to keep Aurora at bay. Though they lost the first five rounds of the half, Aurora managed to stabilize in large part thanks to the impact of Wicadia and Özgür "⁠woxic⁠" Eker, allowing them to climb from a 7-10 deficit to a 13-10 victory to secure the 2-1 series win.
