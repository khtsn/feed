alex: "This was the first 2-1 win since February, and we needed it to break some mental barriers"

Gentle Mates are in the midst of a difficult time, a string of underwhelming results such as early exits from IEM Rio and Stake Ranked Episode 1 compounded by the fact they missed out on IEM Cologne Major qualification. The Spanish side's current campaign at PGL Astana began in tough fashion in a loss to MOUZ, but they have bounced back with a victory over K27.

"We are in a tough period right now," Alejandro "⁠alex⁠" Masanet admitted to HLTV in an interview conducted after Gentle Mates picked up their first win in Kazakhstan. "We need to set some… I don't know, goals, also certain new expectations, because of course when we didn't qualify for the Major it was so hard for us."

With a win under his belt, alex spoke to HLTV to talk about his team's recent struggles, their mentality after missing out on Major qualification, and how they will rectify their form moving forwards.

Congratulations, you got the win over K27 but it was a hard-fought one, and after yesterday as well, that was a rough loss. How's the confidence looking after this kind of game?

I mean confidence is great. We haven't won a 2-1 result since February, this was the first one and I think we needed it to break some mental barriers. I think we did good. Regarding yesterday I think we threw the Nuke away, but overall we play really good, so yeah.

Talking about your overall results recently, you missed the Major, then went into Rio and went out last there, and then at LOGAR Ranking you didn't beat SINNERS in the final, so it's been a rough stretch. How's the team feeling after these runs?

It's hard to say. I think we are in a tough period right now, we need to set some… I don't know, goals, also certain new expectations, because of course when we didn't qualify for the Major it was so hard for us. So it's been really tough times, but we have been working really hard for these kind of events and I hope that the results will come.

Part of the issues that mopoz pointed to in our last interviews were the pressure around making the Major that really played a part for you guys, as well as skipping a couple events and letting the VRS run down. With the pressure of the Major off now, how's the team trying to set up expectations for the next little while?

It's nothing more than trying to play our game, trying to go game-by-game. Before, I think we thought that we would beat every single team in tier-two, not tier-one, but yeah. As I said, when we were playing against these kind of teams, especially before the cut-off, we were really confident, but in the moment we lost, we started struggling the most. We are going to go game-by-game.

CS is full mental, so I think we need to keep fighting, even if it's tough as it was yesterday

Speaking of your position right now, Astana is the only event you have lined up at the moment and your VRS has dropped, I think you're in the 30s now. How's the team looking towards turning that around?

Winning I guess. We are going to try to do everything we can to win, to be back at least in the top 20, top 15. We are going to try to fight for those spots. As I said before, at this tournament and the one before in Barcelona where we lost to SINNERS in the final, it was really important for the confidence. I think nowadays CS is full mental, so I think we need to keep fighting, even if it's tough as it was yesterday, and that's it I would say.

How important is a good result at this event then, with all of that in mind?

To be honest we didn't think about the results at this event, but it's of course really important to get back up the rankings, and be able to be in the next tier-one events. So I think that's the main goal, not this tournament, but trying to win the most we can.
