After connecting on Instagram, Bianca Brunette and Ethan White grew closer when they happened to be visiting their families in Panama at the same time.

Jenny Block

March 22, 2024

You might say the algorithm made him do it. Norris Ethan White contacted Bianca Alexis Brunette in July 2014 after she appeared on his Instagram feed out of the blue.

“It was super random,” said Mr. White, who goes by Ethan. “This pretty girl popped up on my Instagram. We had zero mutual friends, but there were several little elements that made her seem like a good catch. So, I shot my shot.”

He sent her a direct message that read, “I’m sorry for the classless approach. But I think you’re beautiful.”

Ms. Brunette said that she was “very leery” when she first saw his message. She even teased him about it later on. “I said, ‘Ethan, I hope you’re not catfishing me,’ because I literally don’t know who this man is. He says he is a soccer player, but I also knew nothing about soccer.”

The two exchanged numbers and texted sporadically throughout August as Ms. Brunette was on a 21-day vacation traveling through Europe. When she returned, they met in person in September at Blockheads, a now-closed bar on the Upper East Side of Manhattan. Mr. White was living in Philadelphia at the time but happened to be in New York visiting a friend.

Ms. Brunette said there was “definitely some chemistry,” between them during their first meeting, but neither considered it a date. Their real first date came the following month. Mr. White took the two-hour train ride from Philadelphia to New York City to meet her for dinner at a Peruvian restaurant called Pio Pio 8 in Hell’s Kitchen.

It did not go well.

“I thought we were never going to see each other again,” Mr. White said. Shortly after the date began, Ms. Brunette dropped her phone, smashing the screen.

“Her energy changed,” Mr. White said. “We were just getting to know each other, and it was a very responsive interaction. It turned very dry because of her mood change.”

It was not the first time she had shattered her phone, “So, I started thinking, ‘I’m going to have to pay another $250 to fix this dang phone,’” said Ms. Brunette, who admitted that she is prone to “wear all of my emotions on my face.”

Despite the awkward night, they did see each other again. And, by November, they were talking almost every day. In early December, they discovered they would both be in Panama with family at the end of the month.

“My dad and his side of the family live in Panama, so, I had been going since I was a kid,” Ms. Brunette said. “Ethan’s brother was living there for a few months with his girlfriend.”

Once they were in Panama, Ms. Brunette had to fib to her father in order to stay with Mr. White and his family for a few days in Panama City, about 45 minutes from her father’s house. “I had to tell him that I knew this guy super well and that I knew his brother and girlfriend,” Ms. Brunette said. Neither of which was true, of course.

“We went hiking, explored the city, partied, we had the best time and finally got to spend a significant amount of time together,” Ms. Brunette said. “This was the true turning point for us.”

They even had their first kiss.

“We had gone on a hike that day to Cerro Ancon and had such a great day,” Ms. Brunette said. “We returned to the hotel and were getting ready for a night out and Ethan said he just had to get it out of the way. So, he just kissed me in the hotel.”

After returning to the States, Ms. Brunette visited Mr. White in Philadelphia in January, and on Jan. 11, 2015, the two officially became a couple. They dated long distance for a year.

It “was actually great because it felt like we were in our own little bubble,” Ms. Brunette said. “No pressure to meet each other’s friends or family so quickly. Because our time together was limited, we were really intentional about it being spent together.”

Then, the universe did the couple a solid. Mr. White, who was a defender for the Major League Soccer team Philadelphia Union, got traded to the New York City Football Club. In mid-January 2016, he moved into Ms. Brunette’s apartment on the Upper East Side, where they continue to live today.

When it came to the proposal, Ms. Brunette did not make it easy on Mr. White. (“You were a pain that day,” said Mr. White to Ms. Brunette during the interview. “I know, I remember,” Ms. Brunette said.)

In March 2021, the two traveled to Miami to attend an event at the Soho House, where Mr. White’s photo was included in a Black History Month exhibit.

Mr. White had planned to propose during a picnic on the beach, which they did often whenever they traveled to Miami, so on March 2, he suggested they do just that.

But Ms. Brunette had other plans. She was “dying to go to this restaurant, and I had already planned this picnic with all our friends and family,” Mr. White said.

He finally convinced her. But she was not thrilled. “I was kind of annoyed. I really wanted to try to this restaurant,” Ms. Brunette said. Then she got more annoyed, wondering why Mr. White wanted to walk so far to such a specific spot on the beach.

Then Ms. Brunette got a phone call, a prep call for a big job interview she had coming up for a supervising producing role at a new show on Comedy Central. She couldn’t miss it. He couldn’t wait to propose.

“The sun is setting, we’re losing light,” Mr. White said. “My friend was hiding in the bush to take pictures. I’m trying to get her off the phone.” Finally, she hung up. “I started talking, and she’s like, ‘Why do you sound like that? You sound weird.’”

Ms. Brunette went from confused to stunned. “All I remember is that he said something and then the ring was out,” she said. “And then I said, ‘Of course I’ll marry you.’ He puts the ring on, and then all of a sudden I hear yelling behind me.” It was her family and their friends running toward them, all of whom had come in from New York.

The couple was engaged for three years before getting married. “We knew we wanted to be together forever so there was no rush. We spent money on travel instead of wedding things,” Mr. White said.

Mr. White, 33, is a photographer and a founder and creative director of STADE, a creative agency based in New York. He is also a retired Major League Soccer player, having played for D.C. United, Philadelphia Union, and New York City FC. Mr. White, who grew up in Silver Spring, Md., attended the University of Maryland.

Ms. Brunette, also 33, is a supervising television producer. She formerly worked with ABC and Comedy Central and most recently with the SpringHill Company, LeBron James’s production company. She grew up in the Bronx and graduated from Buffalo State University with a bachelor’s degree in media production. In 2019, she won an Emmy for the “Rachael Ray” show.

The two were wed on March 11 at the Office of the City Clerk by Madeline Plasencia, a city clerk. Ms. Brunette’s mother and stepfather attended, as did Mr. White’s mother. “We all cried,” Ms. Brunette said. “And we didn’t think such a bleak room in a courthouse could do that.”

A celebratory wedding ceremony, led by a friend, Antonia Santiago, followed on March 16 in Panama at Panamá Viejo, an event space and historical ruins site in Panama City. Ninety-two people attended the weekend-long celebration. “It felt dreamy, magical,” Ms. Brunette said.

The weekend brought out the best in everyone, the couple said, including Mr. White’s mother. On Saturday night, “My mom, usually a quiet introvert, was partying and dancing with everyone until the last song,” Mr. White said. “I loved seeing all of my family and friends, some meeting one another for the first time, come together the way they did from all over the world to celebrate Bianca and I. It was an incredible thing.”

On This Day

When March 16

Where Panamá Viejo, Panama City, Panama

Panama or Bust “We decided on Panama because it had the most significance in our relationship — it was a turning point in our relationship,” Ms. Brunette said. “I’ve spent a lot of time in Panama. I would go every summer to visit family.” Ever since they began dating, Mr. White would go with her as well.

Honoring Tradition The couple had pollera dancers at their ceremony and Congo dancers at the reception. “Polleras dancers wear beautiful white dresses and dance Tamborito, which is the traditional folklore dance of Panama,” Ms. Brunette said. “Congo is a more unique and colorful manifestation of folklore in the province of Colon, which is where my family is from. The dance has its roots in Africa and came to Panama by way of escaped former slaves.”

Let’s Get crazy The couple also included “La Hora Loca,” which is a “tradition in many Latino weddings that translates to crazy hour,” Ms. Brunette said. Just when guests feel like the party is dying down, the lights are lowered and glow-in-the-dark favors are passed around. Then came a big surprise for guests. “Everyone was shocked when the Diablo Rojo bus came in blasting music and lit up,” Ms. Brunette said. “Then the dancers came out.”
