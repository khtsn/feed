Israeli Military Says It Is Investigating Explosion in Tel Aviv

An explosion in central Tel Aviv injured at least seven people, the Israel authorities said early Friday morning.

A preliminary investigation indicated that a falling aerial target caused the blast, the Israeli military said in a statement, adding that no sirens were activated.

A body was found in a nearby apartment building, according to a statement from the spokesman for the Tel Aviv police. Zaki Heller, a spokesman for Israel’s national emergency service, said on Kan TV that the body, a 50-year-old man, was discovered while emergency workers were combing through the area around the explosion and that it had sustained “penetrating wounds, as a result of shrapnel or glass.”
