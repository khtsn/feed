"We wanted to make the roster change so fast before the Major cut-off, that it would put us into an awkward situation where we need to accept many tournaments," coach Eetu "⁠sAw⁠" Saha told HLTV, "and we won't have the practice that we would like to have."

G2 will continue to make progress and tweak their game with NertZ at Radio Popular Roman Imperium Cup VII in Portugal and Stake Ranked Episode 1 in Spain, two lower-tier LANs, in hopes of getting the team in shape for a stretch of three big events ahead of the Major beginning with IEM Rio on April 13.

Your debut tournament with NertZ ended in the quarter-finals. How do you rate the performance, given the lack of practice?

It was also known to us, when we wanted to make the roster change so fast before the Major cut-off, that it would put us into an awkward situation where we need to accept many tournaments, and we won't have the practice that we would like to have. And then with some of the position changes and everything, there's not so much routine in our game yet, even though it's one player change, but it still has an effect when there are multiple position changes on different maps.

Overall, I think this tournament is still practice for us. We will be learning basically from all these small situations and building that routine for our team, which is the most important thing. In the end, the most important thing for us in these tournaments here in BC.Game, and then the Roman Imperium Cup and Stake, is going to be just to improve ourselves for the bigger tournaments. Then, at the same time, making sure that we are in the best position for the Major cut-off, which is happening after these tournaments. That's basically the take we have right now.

The move also brings a lot of changes in the team for the existing members. Is that a lot harder to do in the middle of the season?

Yeah, I think always, if you make a player change in the middle of the season, that's the hardest time to do it. You can't go, [like] after a player break and have a bootcamp and do things the way you would maybe like. But at the same time, looking at the calendar for CS, you can see there aren't so many gaps throughout the whole year. Even the start of the seasons will be rough in a way that you can maybe have a bootcamp, but then the tournament season starts, where it's event after event.

So, a couple of weeks here and there in practice is what you get at the start of the season, or mid-season if you have a gap here. In that way, there's never going to be a perfect timing for it. We just have to play these tournaments and learn as we go, the best way to learn is from officials in the end. I think we will get a lot of valuable lessons here.

Do you see this change as a risk, maybe?

There are risks, of course, and at the same time, doing it mid-season. The VRS is what it is, and there are many teams competing. We are right now somewhere around 10th, 11th, 12th, I'm not sure, but somewhere around those rankings, and the points are super close. It's a risky move in that way, and it can affect the future, but we will see.

You mentioned the Major cut-off earlier. G2 isn't in any real danger, but how important will it be to avoid Stage 1 for you?

It would be important in terms of... We have these three tournaments now, then we have Rio, Rivals, and Astana coming up. If we end up playing Stage 1 at the Major, it means our practice time is limited again between Astana and Stage 1. So obviously, we would like to maximize our practice and have a proper bootcamp before the Major, but then at the same time, we need to consider our energy levels with all this travel, all these other tournaments, and how we are going to do all of that.

So I would obviously hope and want us to go to Stage 2 if possible, and then we'll have some more time after these tournaments. Overall, the rest of the season and doing the change now is going to be a lot of learning, trying to implement NertZ as much as we can while we still have the remaining old structure, but then still doing changes in our position. So it's for the longer term, not only for the short term.

You have two more open LANs, as you already mentioned, in the next few days. Is the plan to play as many games as possible and try to get up to the speed with NertZ?

Yeah, basically. I mean, obviously, we wanted to go deeper in this tournament here. Tomorrow we most likely won't have much other than reviewing the game, watching what went wrong, what was good from us, and then learning from that and taking that into the next tournament, which I think we are traveling the day after tomorrow already. It's going to come fast.
