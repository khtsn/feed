The EVPs and All-Stars of PGL Astana 2026

Spirit are entering peak form just in time for Cologne, and star man Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets made sure they left Astana with the trophy.

In 14 maps, he was the player of the map eight times, averaged 0.98 KPR, and had a median map rating of 1.68.

He's posted a 1.61 event rating before in 2026, but that was in a group stage exit from BLAST Rotterdam. Now, with his team firing on all cylinders, we can see what Spirit should be able to do with donk in form.

The talk around Dmitry "⁠S0tF1k⁠" Forostyanko and flash assists is well-founded, but it also helps when your opener is fragging like this. Roll on Cologne.

Radar Chart Stat Glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • Swing in RW Δ: round swing in round wins

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov

m0NESY was Falcons' leading MVP contender before the final, but he finished nowhere near donk after a quiet final. He was poor on Dust2 overall, considering his 8-0 start, and absent on maps two and three.

But that does not stop what came before pushing him to the top EVP spot: Falcons' sniper averaged a 1.48 rating in map wins, +3.45% Swing, and 0.83 KPR.

He was the key man on the barn burner of a Nuke against FURIA, posting a 1.62 rating over the 42-round classic. Putting magic to the sword with a 2.26-rated Dust2 also strengthened his case, as did maps like his 29-14 K-D in an overtime decider against The MongolZ.

Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman

xertioN's first event as in-game leader of MOUZ was marked by his individual brilliance as much as his calling.

He had a 1.61 CT rating over the full event, with a 1.52 rating in map wins to push his team into the semi-finals. His 1.33 rating is 0.30 above Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás, who was second-best.

xertioN was player of the map seven times, only once fewer than donk, and that includes back-to-back arena demolitions of Aurora in quarters.

Unfortunately he could not maintain this sky-high form in the semi-final against Spirit, where a 0.69 semi-final rating kept him below m0NESY in this list, but to average 120 ADR in round wins over this large a sample puts him firmly in the elite category.

Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin

kyousuke hasn't earned an EVP since his debut at IEM Cologne, but in Astana he finally showed the potential we have been waiting for.

He's had good series in arenas before, like in Chengdu and Rotterdam, but this was the 18-year-old's first playoff showing that was strong from start to finish.

By rating, he was Falcons' best in playoffs (1.22) and in the grand final (1.18), which goes to show his improvement under the lights. He was also great into top 10 opposition overall (1.31 rating), with a strong series against The MongolZ in groups.

So while he was less important in map wins than m0NESY (1.25, vs 1.48) to get Falcons into the final, kyousuke was impressive enough to end on the same tier as his teammate and xertioN anyway.

Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko

molodoy averaged a 1.73 rating in the six map wins that got FURIA into playoffs, and he then kicked off playoffs with a 1.79-rated Mirage against Falcons to delight his home arena.

A disappointing drop on the marathon maps of Nuke (0.90) and Dust2 (1.01) prevented FURIA from progressing any further, but it does not undo the brilliant work beforehand.

He posted five maps above 1.65, and only one below 1.00, and also recorded 1.16 KPRW and +4.03% round swing overall. The team result will have been below what was hoped, but FURIA could not have asked for much more from their AWPer.

Dmitry "⁠sh1ro⁠" Sokolov

sh1ro had lower peaks than molodoy but also left the quarter-finals sure of his EVP. His highlights were three different Dust2 games, against G2 in the arena and FURIA and The MongolZ in groups.

But outside of Spirit's trusty map pick he was also solid, and 81.4% KAST en route to the trophy is further proof of his consistency round-to-round. His kill differential per round of 0.28 is bettered only by donk and molodoy, too.

It's only sh1ro's second EVP of what has been a fairly quiet year for the five-time top 10 player of the year, but, like his team, he is ramping up at the perfect time.

Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich

Next up is teammate tN1R, who now has two EVPs in a row after breaking his duck at IEM Rio.

The 25-year-old left it late in Astana, but had great impact in all five of Spirit's final maps (1.36 rating) to confirm his award.

It was his USP 4K that shifted momentum on Dust2 in the final, as he went from 3-10 to 21-19 by the end of the map. He was then the POTM on Mirage, and had a 1.48 rating in the first half of Ancient to finish as the second-best player of the final.

It's a Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool-style path to an EVP, and Spirit will be hoping tN1R can stay in this extra gear for the Major just around the corner.

Luciano "⁠luchov⁠" Herrera

luchov's Big Event debut at Rotterdam went largely unnoticed, but he made sure PGL Astana was different right off the bat when he delivered 27 kills, 2 aces, and +20.5% Swing in the first half of the tournament opener against PARIVISION.

He ended the map with a 2.68 rating, and was crucial as 9z went on to upset Falcons (1.28 rating) and MOUZ (1.20) to storm into playoffs with a 3-0 record.

Add in a 1.79-rated arena map against magic to figures like 1.23 KPRW, 88.6 ADR, and a 25% multi-kill rate, and an EVP was assured.

"I wanted luchov a few years ago," Maximiliano "⁠max⁠" Gonzalez told HLTV, and now we all know why.

Franco "⁠dgt⁠" Garcia

Even with luchov in form like that, a different 9z player was actually their strongest in map wins: dgt (1.56).

With a still impressive 1.02 KPRW, and very tough opposition in groups for their 3-0 run, it's hard not to find room for a player with three maps above a 1.76 rating and a 1.23 rating overall in the EVP list.

So dgt does just squeeze in for his career-first nod, buoyed by 1.88 and 1.76 ratings against event finalists Falcons and his own 2+ rated demolition of PARIVISION one map after luchov's.

Honorable mentions

Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato was the closest player to extending our already long list of EVPs, but couldn't get too much credit for groups given the gap to molodoy and 0.96 KPRW only being the third best in his team. Even with plenty of boost for the quarter-final, he fell short.

Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın led Aurora to quarters with 1.12 KPRW, but had spots of inconsistency and only a 1.08 rating against top 50 teams.

Svyatoslav "⁠MaSvAl⁠" Masko was magic's best in map wins, but it was a PARIVISION-style team effort that took the team to the top four, and nobody truly stood out.

magic made it to semi-finals as a team, without an EVP contender

Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko and Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García were G2's strongest contenders but it's a similar story to magic in terms of credit share. To prove that, Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač was actually their best in map wins (1.54) but worst overall (0.97).

In groups, Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold and Usukhbayar "⁠910⁠" Banzragch were unlucky not to make it to the arena, while FACEIT demon Daniil "⁠qw1nk1⁠" Kabilov impressed despite K27's early exit.

All-Star

S0tF1k has garnered plenty of the praise for Spirit's reversal in fortune over the last two events, but Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev deserves his fair share too.

One of S0tF1k's changes has been empowering magixx to take part in more of the preparation, and Spirit's flash assists have been improving ever since he took over as captain before exploding in Astana. So he rightly takes his spot as our IGL.

donk is the clear winner in a very strong field of openers, while tN1R's playoffs take him past dgt to be our closer.

AWPer is another tough fight, with top EVP m0NESY taking it over molodoy and sh1ro.

Finally, there's a close battle for the opposite reason for anchor. In a quiet event for our supportive riflers, HeavyGod stood out most and retains his spot from BLAST Rivals.
