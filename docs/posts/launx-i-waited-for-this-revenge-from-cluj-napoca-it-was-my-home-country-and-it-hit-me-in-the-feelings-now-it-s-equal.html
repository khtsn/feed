FUT secure statement win over MOUZ to reach EPL semi-final

Not only did FUT earn a statement victory but also a slice of revenge, as MOUZ took FUT down at PGL Cluj-Napoca and were partially responsible for Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea missing out on a playoff appearance in his home country.

"What I can say I am proud of is that us as a five managed to do what we couldn't do one month ago," lauNX said of his team's efforts. "That actually shows me that we evolve with each tournament we play. It's insane, it's a very good feeling."

HLTV caught up with lauNX after FUT's memorable victory to go in-depth on how they managed to make it happen, what it means for the team, and discuss his thoughts on their upcoming match with Natus Vincere.

First question, how are you feeling?

Insane to be honest. I waited for this revenge from Cluj-Napoca, it was my home country and it actually kind of hit me in the feelings. So now taking them out here, it's equal.

Does it feel like a little bit of redemption, to get that win over such a good team like MOUZ?

To be honest, yes, but at the same time even last time when we lost, we watched the game and realized that we actually could have won it. Even if we lost 2-0, we had a lot of rounds in our advantage which we just lost due to inexperience, but that's what we're working on right now.

You had a bit of a slow start on map one, it seemed like the team were a little bit hesitant and not as aggressive as you normally are. What allowed you to turn it around on that map?

First, of course it was coolio's tactical pause. He kinda shook us up with his words, so that actually helped us a lot. Then, as I said on stage, everyone realized how sharp we are, how good we can be, and it's actually a dream that we are living right now, right? We made it to an arena, now we won our first game, so let's see what we can do.

Everyone realized how sharp we are, how good we can be, and it's actually a dream that we are living right now

Next up was Overpass, which was another close map but one that didn't go your way. What was the mood like in the team after that second map, how were the team feeling having lost?

I think we were just fine. You don't have much to do in these situations, you kinda need to reset even before you go outside, you just need to snap out of it and get back to reality. It was 1-1, we played against one of the best teams in the world, we actually put up a good fight on the map we lost, so yeah, we had a very good and sharp mentality.

Mirage started out as a little bit of a stomp, you were crushing them, but they started to come back. Were there any nerves in the team as MOUZ started to put those rounds together?

I think on our side for sure we had nerves and pressure. Even myself, I kinda crumbled down a bit with some rounds we lost, and I'm not proud of that. What I can say I am proud of is that us as a five managed to do what we couldn't do one month ago, and that actually shows me that we evolve with each tournament we play. It's insane, it's a very good feeling.

We like to strive in chaos... and I think that comes from our nature, how we started playing CS from a young age. We are just a bit crazy in the head

In general across that series MOUZ posed a lot of problems for you, but you seemed to come up with answers more often than not. You're such a young team, so how do you do that, how do you dig deep and find those answers in the close games?

I think us, on individual skill if we take each player one by one, we are like your usual young CS player. We like to strive in chaos, especially when it's chaos we like to do a lot of crazy stuff, and I think that comes from our nature, how we started playing CS from a young age. We are just a bit crazy in the head.

You've got NAVI coming up now, how are you feeling about that game?

I am feeling great about that game, I waited for this game for a long time.

For the rest of the team there's a bit of history with NAVI as an organization. Do you know how they're feeling?

I don't think it's against NAVI itself, it's more about makazze than NAVI. I don't think they care that much that they play against the organization, but more about playing makazze, so yeah it will be a fun game. It's me and iM, it's them and makazze, it will be a very fun game.

Seeing so many people cheering for us and even for me sometimes, it was very nice and a good feeling. It reminds me what I grind for, what I live for

You've beaten MOUZ, who a lot of people would have said were the favorites for the trophy. Does this give you confidence you can go and win the trophy now?

I don't think it's good to get that much confidence because then, for example if we start choking up tomorrow, we will crumble again. So we just take it one by one, we play, we enjoy the game, and like I am saying, it's a dream that we made it to the playoffs. We finally broke the ice and that was the big next step for us. We wanted to do it before the Major, to make an arena playoff, and we did it. From now, I am happy with each game we play, even if somehow we lose tomorrow, it's insane what we did.

Do you have anything you want to say to the fans? You had a lot of fans in the arena today cheering you on.

It was insane, I really loved this experience, especially because I didn't play in a long time on a stage. For me, seeing so many people cheering for us and even for me sometimes, it was very nice and a good feeling. It reminds me what I grind for, what I live for.
