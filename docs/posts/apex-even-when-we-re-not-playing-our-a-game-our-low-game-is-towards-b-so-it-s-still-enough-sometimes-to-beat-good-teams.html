apEX: "Even when we’re not playing our A-game, our low game is towards B, so it’s still enough sometimes to beat good teams"

After already securing two titles in 2026, Vitality have now reached their 19th consecutive semi-final after defeating 9z, The MongolZ, and PARIVISION at BLAST Open Rotterdam without losing a single map.

Still, Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire is not entirely pleased with the standards his team has shown in the group stage. " I wish we could play perfect CS 24/7," he told HLTV after the game against Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali's troops.

"We still play good CS, no matter what, but that was a bit less good than usual."

The French IGL also admitted that the team remains focused on the Grand Slam, for which their next chance will come at IEM Rio, talked about Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut's 1vs5 clutch, and Vitality's recent break from competition.

apEX, a 2-0 victory against PARIVISION. Both maps were very close. Give me your thoughts on the series. Was it closer than you expected? Because it feels like, especially on Inferno, that you lost a few rounds that you normally don't lose, right? Some 4v3s, some 3v2s. Was it harder than you expected?

Yeah, of course. I mean, it's CS, and when you lose a round you're supposed to win, it's always tough. And I think on both maps that was the case. They played some great CS. Props to them. We didn't manage to do well the things we usually do well. I think today was probably one of our worst matches of the year in general, in the way that we were understanding each other, I would say. That was a bit of a mess, but that's normal.

And we still need to bring a really good game. And that's what is good with Vitality: that even when we are not playing our A-game, our low game is towards B, so sometimes it's still enough to beat good teams. We will have to step up a bit for the playoffs.

Do you think that's also a bit of a reflection of the break that you had coming into this one?

We had a break… It's funny because people forget that last year when we won seven trophies, we had a break after Katowice, we had a break after Lisbon, we had a break after Rivals. Always take a break… We did that and always won a trophy after. So it’s not about that. You could see the previous two games we played were pretty good.

Just a bad day.

Yeah, it can happen to anyone, and also to us. It's not about a break or whatever. We had ten days of practice, which is enough. Sometimes we came to tournaments with a bit less. We always prefer to have a good rest over going to tournaments back-to-back. Because when you look at, for example, PARIVISION, they did a lot of tournaments in a row, and it was pretty up and down for them. Having really good tournaments, really bad, really good. And that’s something we want to avoid.

We had ten days of practice, which is enough. Sometimes we came to tournaments with a bit less

You beat them 3-0 in Cluj, now 2-0, but some of those maps were very, very close. Is there anything special about them? Would you say that they're maybe the toughest, or the one that is the most special, to face?

Of course. I mean, they're stacking. If they have one low HP on CT, they're stacking, and that's gambling, A or B. Last time in Cluj, I didn't go into their stacks too much. Today I went a bit more, and that's tough.

Even though you try to fake or whatever, they're not moving. So yeah, we tried to find gaps, we couldn’t, and that's the way it is. I think if we had a bit more chance today in that regard, I think we would have won a bit easier on the T side. The problem is we had a rough CT side on both Inferno and Mirage, losing rounds we shouldn't have lost, in a way. We were good on bombsites and expected what they were doing, but we just lost rounds.

That's CS, you know? I wish we could play perfect CS 24/7. We still play good CS, no matter what, but that was a bit less good than usual.

It looked like it could go to a third map, but then ZywOo pulled off that amazing 1v5 in overtime. How did the team react? Did you feel that it broke their spirits?

I don't know if it would have gone to a third map, to be honest. I'm not sure, because it was only 12-12, so it would have been 13-12. And I know we can bounce back from that; it's not like a huge lead. So yeah, of course, when you lose a 1v5, it's always really tough.

And we could see that from the way they played, they were really scared of him. And yeah, that's what helps us; that X-factor. We know that when he's on, he can create that. And he played just the round perfectly, he won the 1v5. And then the next few rounds were a bit easier, for sure.

And now you're through to the semi-finals. What are your expectations? Is there anyone in particular that you’d like to face?

It's good we kept the streak alive for the semis. It's the 19th; we are like Astralis now. It's funny, because we had a few records. We are back in the wins in a row, we are back in the maps in a row, and now we are in the semifinals in a row.

I just expect us to give our best and win, even though this is not the key tournament for us. We want to win the Grand Slam. We want to finish the Grand Slam ASAP. That's the goal. Doing it in Rio would be the perfect way. For us, it's training for Rio, seeing what's wrong. But yeah, with Vitality, even though we try to see where we are right now, we can still win. And the goal is always to win with the lineup we have and with the history we have. We want to create something that has never been done before. We want to be better than Astralis. We’ve said it a couple of times, so here we go.

What about NAVI? Are you curious about them? Because you haven't faced them since Budapest, and now they're looking good. They just won a tournament.

Yeah, they look good. It feels like they really want to play us. They keep repeating that. But yeah, it's normal for them. They have the joy that they didn't have for a long time. I think the last trophy they had won was Rio 2024. So obviously, they are back to a good level. It took time for them. But yeah, I think it would be nice to play against each other, no matter what. But the sure thing is, I know if we play our A-game, we're going to beat them. But that's always...

I know if we play our A-game, we're going to beat them

The case for any team, is that it?

Yeah. But let’s see if we can bring it. But we know that the players we have and the team play we have, I think it's above theirs. And I think that's just like that. But we're never sure. But first, we focus on the semis. And then maybe NAVI, maybe whoever. But the goal is to win the trophy. So here we go.
