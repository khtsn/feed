The young, high-flying, energetic South American team obliterated Team Vitality across two maps, dropping just eight rounds in a 2-0 domination.

The series was played out on Team Vitality's Lotus and LEVIATÁN's Split. LEV won both maps 13-4, matching the biggest blowout of Masters London since Vitality's 2-0 win over Dragon Ranger Gaming Dragon Ranger Gaming China Rank #4 Flex1n Lo Rui (罗瑞) Life Qu Donghao (瞿东豪) vo0kashu Ilya Ushakov Akeman Zhang Haoran (张浩然) Nicc Shao Yi-qun (邵逸群) SpiritZ1 Lin Dingyu (林定宇) in the Swiss Stage.

On Lotus, Vitality began with a great energy, winning three straight rounds on defense to threaten an early snowball. A high-IQ play from veteran Duelist Derke saw two Raze Satchels prevent a plant on the B site to secure a bonus, giving VIT the perfect pedestal to build up a strong defender side. From that point on, though, it was LEV that had all the energy within the server. The South American squad dropped just one more round for the rest of the half, entering halftime up 8-4.

blowz gets the job done with the 4K to shut down a Vitality force-buy.

On defense, it was more of the same. LEV won the pistol and cleaned house easily, closing out the map 13-4. Neon and blowz were most impressive, notching 19 and 18 kills while the former had a sharpshooting performance with a 50% headshot rate.

On Split, LEV did not miss a beat. They gave no breathing room up to Vitality, this time claiming the opening pistol round and snowballing on defense to an early 8-0 lead. Vitality's more vintage Jett-Raze Duelist pairing seemed to struggle against LEV's more modern Phoenix-Raze, as the South American squad was much more clinical on its retakes and teamfights. Vitality managed to dodge an even bigger blowout by claiming three rounds before halftime, but very little could be done to avoid the inevitable.

spike styling on Vitality for the 4K.

A third pistol across two maps was claimed by LEV, and Vitality claimed the would-be bonus round after opting not to force in its anti-eco this time around. After that consolation, LEVIATÁN dealt the final blows, always seeming a step ahead and a head above over the veteran EMEA squad to win 13-4 on both maps.

Three LEV players finished north of the 30-kill mark, while no player on Vitality's side of the stage had more than 25. blowz had the game-high mark with a 1.42 rating, while the world continues to run out of superlatives for Sato , who had a match-high 34 kills.

With the loss, Vitality matches its best-ever finish at an international event, heading home in fourth place. Meanwhile, LEV advances to its first international best-of-five since aspas played for the team in 2024.

Masters London will wrap up this weekend with the following matches:
