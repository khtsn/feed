Official: Esports World Cup moved to Paris*

Update 18:15 : EWC format to remain the same

"All announced titles, tournaments, and competitive formats remain unchanged. A slight shift in the tournament schedule is possible," EWC replied to a query about whether the Counter-Strike's tournament format would change.

"Adjustments that flow specifically from the change of host city, such as broadcast scheduling adapted to European time zones, or operational details related to the new venues, will be communicated through our channels in the coming days."

Original story:

The Esports World Cup Foundation has confirmed the move of the $75 million festival from Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, to Paris, France, as first reported by GamesBeat.

Although the move was mainly hailed for amounting to "the first international chapter in EWC history," it comes as a response to the ongoing conflict in Iran.

EWC Foundation CEO Ralf Reichert had publicly expressed hope that the event would be able to remain in Riyadh as late as at the end of April, but the uncertain geopolitical climate in the Middle East ultimately forced the event to be moved.

The Counter-Strike portion of the festival was due to be held in the final two weeks of the festival, from August 12-23, with a whopping $2 million prizepool and 32 teams, double the number of the 2025 edition, as well as a preceding LAN open qualifier for up to 128 teams.

It is currently unclear whether the format will change in any way following the move, but HLTV can confirm the event has been granted a Valve exception to relocate, which it required, as it came nearly seven months after the additional information deadline had passed.

HLTV has reached out to Esports World Cup for clarification.
