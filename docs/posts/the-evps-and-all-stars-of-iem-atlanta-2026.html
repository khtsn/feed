The EVPs and All-Stars of IEM Atlanta 2026

IEM Atlanta may firmly be in the rear mirror now, but we still need to award players for their performances in the Peach State.

That begins with MVP Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov, who picked up his long awaited career-first medal after a 1.36-rated playoff campaign that included two 1.85+ rating eviscerations of Vitality.

"There were a lot of chances for me to get [an MVP]," he told HLTV, "but every time I was playing badly in finals or we lost. This is my first time, and I am super happy."

The 21-year-old just missed out on the top 20 in 2025 after placing 11th in 2024, and is on a charge back into the ranking after posting two EVPs alongside this MVP five months into the season.

Radar Chart Stat Glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • Swing in RW Δ: round swing in round wins

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Valeriy "⁠b1t⁠" Vakhovskiy

b1t has had to wait for his first EVP of 2026 after being an honorable mention four times, but this one was far beyond the threshold.

He was just behind w0nderful in playoffs, with a 1.32 rating and no maps below 1.21 in the arena.

He also dealt, on average, 20 damage more to opponents than he received per round all event long, the highest of anyone. That's backed up by his 102.2 ADR in round wins.

And while it was more about an incredible floor than peaks, he was still able to be player of the map twice in playoffs, including a 2.10 rating in the grand final on Mirage.

Fredrik "⁠REZ⁠" Sterner

REZ hasn't had a nod since the end of 2021, but ensured he made this list with a team-high 1.18 rating en route to a second place fnish.

His 1.20 rating in playoffs was 0.15 clear of any teammate, and overall he had eight maps at or above a 1.44 rating.

Those peaks came at crucial times as well, with 2.07 and 1.92 ratings in the quarter and semi final deciders, respectively, and a 1.98 rating in a 13-3 loss to Natus Vincere in the final.

GamerLegion's run to the final was a team effort, especially in map wins, but there's no doubt who ended top of the pile after the playoffs.

Milan "⁠hypex⁠" Polowiec

hypex could have become simply the latest AWPer in a string of GamerLegion snipers to flounder in tier one, but he is starting to come alive under the tutelage of Janusz "⁠Snax⁠" Pogorzelski; from a 0.93 rating in GL in 2025, he's up to a 1.08 in 2026.

He was GamerLegion's highest-rated player before the final in Atlanta at 1.19, which contained enough impact for him to earn his first-ever EVP.

A 2.42 rating against Natus Vincere in groups, even though it came in a series loss, was the highlight but he did plenty of work to push GamerLegion forward in maps against Legacy (1.62), paiN (1.39, 1.30), and Astralis (1.43, 1.42) as well to confirm his award.

Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto

latto, even before CAC, had entered a rich vein of form under new coach Alan "⁠adrrr⁠" Riveros and ended IEM Atlanta with an event-high 1.32 rating.

That started in groups, where latto had a 1.42 rating and 1.11 KPRW, but you can point to strong peaks all event long with seven maps above the 1.37 mark for Legacy's star man.

He didn't have the best playoffs, with two bad maps to go next to his 1.71-rated Mirage against GamerLegion, but went on to dominate the third-place decider with a 2.11 rating over the 2-0 win.

All together, he had a 1.36 rating in 5 maps against top 10 teams, 1.07 KPRW, and a tournament-high 81% KAST rate.

Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut

ZywOo, for once, was a borderline EVP decision but his event rating of 1.27 still speaks to the consistency the reigning player of the year brings to each and every tournament.

He ended Vitality's lower-bracket run in groups with a 1.38 rating and 1.15 KPRW, with a 1.45 in map wins and topping the scoreboard on four occasions.

Playoffs was therefore about not losing his EVP, and it would be too harsh to deny him an award based on one 0.64-rated half in the decider of the quarter-final. Before then, he was above the 1.00 mark on every map.

A 0.95 overall in playoffs (and accounting for weaker opposition in groups) still harms his case, and keeps him fairly low in the list, but the Frenchman did enough to pick up his sixth award of 2026.

Sebastian "⁠Tauson⁠" Tauson Lindelof

Tauson's simply had to get a nod after posting a team-high 1.35 in map wins (next best being 1.26) to take GamerLegion to the final.

He had seven standout maps in Atlanta, three coming in playoffs against Legacy and paiN, and was a near ever-present in map wins across every series before the final.

But he was not too dominant in round wins (0.93 KPR was third in GL), and eight maps below 1.00 also need to be taken into account which means he has to remain at the bottom of the EVP list.

Honorable mentions

Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan came close but was held back from a late dash to an EVP by a 0.89 KPRW in playoffs after a slow 1.05-rated start in groups.

Oldřich "⁠PR⁠" Nový was an important part of GamerLegion's run, and was great against top 10 teams, but did not do quite enough in playoffs to get over the line for a nod.

Eduardo "⁠dumau⁠" Wolkmer was better than latto in the semi-final but more part of the pack beforehand (1.22 in map wins, vs 1.34), leaving him just short of the EVP mark as credit was shared between he, Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan, and Guilherme "⁠saadzin⁠" Pacheco.

Kirill "⁠Magnojez⁠" Rodnov had three stellar peaks, one in each series, to get BetBoom to semi-finals including a 1.79 against Vitality. But he needed more for a nod: His fifth-best map at the event was a 1.04 rating against paiN, and his median map rating was just 1.01.

Guilherme "⁠piriajr⁠" Barbosa's 33-frag destruction (2.59 rating) of GamerLegion in quarters was one of the highlights of playoffs, but like Magnojez his floor was too low (1.00 median map rating) to enter EVP territory.

Victor "⁠Staehr⁠" Staehr was Astralis' strongest piece en route to a 7-8th finish, but was below the very high bar for a group stage EVP. For the other team in that placing, B8, teenage sniper Danylo "⁠s1zzi⁠" Vinnyk led the way with a 1.12 rating.

Further down the list, David "⁠frozen⁠" Čerňanský, Dmytro "⁠dem0n⁠" Myroshnychenko, Džiugas "⁠dziugss⁠" Steponavičius, and Vladyslav "⁠Kvem⁠" Korol all did well to post high ratings despite a lack of team success.

All-Star

Natus Vincere trio Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen, w0nderful, and b1t are easy picks as the core of our all-star best five, leaving just two spots for their competitors.

REZ was the best opener of the event, especially after the final, and is also an easy pick.

Closer is a bit trickier; PR, Tauson, and iM all qualify, but latto's volume of peaks and status as team leader see him take the spot.
