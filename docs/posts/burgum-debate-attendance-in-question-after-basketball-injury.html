Burgum Debate Attendance in Question After Basketball Injury

Gov. Doug Burgum of North Dakota injured himself playing basketball with his staff on Tuesday and was taken to an emergency room ahead of his planned appearance at Wednesday’s first Republican presidential primary debate.

Mr. Burgum, 67, injured his leg, according to two campaign aides, and it was unclear on Wednesday morning whether he would be able to stand at the debate. The injury was earlier reported by CNN.

One of the campaign aides said that Mr. Burgum planned to do a walk-through at the debate site, Fiserv Forum in downtown Milwaukee, and the team would assess the situation after that.

On X, formerly known as Twitter, Mr. Burgum posted a throwback photograph of himself playing basketball and said his pre-debate game of pickup wasn’t the first to send him to the emergency room.“Appreciate all the well-wishes!” he wrote.
