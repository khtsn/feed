MOUZ win on PR debut as BLAST Bounty S2 kicks off

The world's top teams are back in action as BLAST Bounty gets underway.

Elite Counter-Strike is back with BLAST Bounty Season 2, bringing the world's top teams back to action after the summer player break.

BLAST Bounty's unique format, where the lower-ranked teams choose their opponents, leads to a few close matchups sprinkled amongst many David vs. Goliath games, and the favorites largely prevailed on the opening day of the event.

Astralis and MOUZ delivered on their status as comfortable favorites, Ninjas in Pyjamas triumphed in a 50/50 matchup with M80, whilst Wildcard grabbed what was perhaps the only upset of the day as they brushed aside Alliance.

Martin "⁠stavn⁠" Lund was the best performer on the server as Ninjas in Pyjamas swept M80 2-0, a strong T side getting the job done on Inferno before some stellar play by Kacper "⁠xKacpersky⁠" Gabara sealed the deal on the CT side of Ancient.

The only upset of the day, at least by VRS rank, saw Wildcard crush Alliance in comfortable fashion across two one-sided maps.

Alliance recently picked up a top-four finish at XSE Pro League Guangzhou and many would have backed them to build upon that result in BLAST Bounty, but instead they found themselves completely outmatched against Wildcard.

bobeksde is just meant to fake the A bombsite, but grabs an ace instead

Wildcard kicked things off by dominating Alliance on Inferno, despite the Swedes sporting a 13-strong winstreak on the map, and then cruised to a win on Dust2, even though Alliance won a string of clutches in the first half and benefitted from a pistol ace by Jonatan "⁠bobeksde⁠" Persson in the second.
