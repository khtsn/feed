Aurora ease past BB into the Major semi-final

Aurora have progressed to the semi-final of a Major for the first time ever after they beat BetBoom 2-0 in Cologne.

The talk before the game was whether Aurora could deal with two kinds of pressure. One was the usual one, the pressure of the arena and the game itself in the first true test of the post-sports psychologist Aurora.

The second was how the team would deal with the rumors around their move to an international roster after this event, with Ashley "⁠ash⁠" Battye set to join as the head coach

In the LANXESS arena, Aurora passed both tests with flying colors. They were a step ahead tactically on Nuke, with only the clutches of Kirill "⁠Boombl4⁠" Mikhailov keeping the first half competitive.

Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın, long dubbed a future star of the nation, has matured into the team's top player in 2026 and it was his Deagle shots that swung the first map in his team's favor.

That gave a platform for Ismailcan "⁠XANTARES⁠" Dörtkardeş to erupt on T side, storming to his career-best arena rating on Nuke with a 1.78.

And the 30-year-old star carried that form over to Anubis, where Aurora took full advantage of a BetBoom side that had avoided the map all Major long.

Three consecutive retakes threatened to bring back Aurora's old demons, but they held on to take the map 13-9 and the series 2-0.

XANTARES shows up in style

XANTARES's struggles in playoffs are well documented. In CS2, he drops from a 1.17 to a 1.06 in playoffs. In 2026, it's been even worse, a 0.91 over 20 maps.

But when XANTARES hit an unbelievable flick one tap, shocking himself to the extent that he couldn't help smiling, something unlocked inside the Great Turk.

With the game evenly poised at 7-5, XANTARES was beyond destructive in a 168 ADR T side. On Anubis, fully woken up, he looked like he was in the flow state at times, and a 46-30 K-D was illustrative of his form.

Whether he can carry this form into the semi-final against tougher opposition remains to be seen, but it can only be a good sign for the Turkish combine.

BetBoom find their limit

Boombl4 has had impact throughout BetBoom's run to playoffs, but even he had his limits and that was fully exposed when he sat with a 0-15 K-D on Anubis.

The team's run has exceeded expectations at every step, but this was a step too far even for the Stockholm Major's winning in-game leader.

Had Boombl4 not won back-to-back clutches on Nuke, the scoreline might have been even more convincing. But you can take nothing away from the team after they came here from Stage 1, with no practice and two stand-ins.
