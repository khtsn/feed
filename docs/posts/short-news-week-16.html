    Phantom is down to four players on its active lineup after the organization announced the benching of Wiktor "⁠mynio⁠" Kruk on Saturday.

    The Polish organization also announced "a new strategic direction," which will see it pivot to an international lineup for the first time since entering Counter-Strike in 2025.

    "Recognizing that assembling a stable, top-tier roster consisting of five Polish players is currently an immense challenge, the organization will now expand its search to include talent from the broader European region," Phantom said in a press release.

    "Despite this international expansion, Phantom Esports remains committed to its roots; the organization aims to maintain its Polish identity and keep the national flag proudly displayed next to the team name."

    Karim "⁠relaxxie⁠" Miftakhov has departed K27, the org have announced.

    "Such decisions are difficult, especially regarding a person who has been with us from the very beginning of our journey," CEO Ivan Kondratov said. "But we have made the decision that from today we are forced to move forward in a different form."

    Stake have revealed the invites for their upcoming Ranked Episode 2, set to be played from May 27-30 and feature a $100,000 prize pool.

    3DMAX are the highest-ranked team in attendance, with the new-look FaZe and Ninjas in Pyjamas squads also in attendance.

    The six directly invited teams will be joined by two closed qualifier winners.
