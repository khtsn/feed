Esports Nations Cup qualifiers registration open

More than 15,000 players worldwide will be able to compete in the qualifiers for the event in Riyadh.

The Esports Foundation (EF) has launched registration for the open qualifiers for the Counter-Strike 2 tournament at the Esports Nations Cup. The national team competition will take place between November 10-15 in Riyadh, with $1,320,000 in prize money.

EF is running the "largest open qualifier in Counter-Strike history," with 96 qualifiers worldwide. The national champions will then be grouped into regional qualifiers, which will determine the 24 teams that will go to Saudi Arabia.

The list of 96 countries featuring qualifiers was created through the ENC ranking, which was based on the June VRS. Each country's score was calculated by combining the points of the top five highest-ranked players, with a maximum of three players from the same VRS team considered.

For the countries without enough VRS-ranked players, a separate ranking was established "based on the sum of the FACEIT ELO of the top 40 active players" during May.

The national qualifiers will take place between July 6-16, while the regional qualifiers will be held between July 17-19.

Below you can find the list of countries featuring national qualifiers, how they have been grouped up into regions, and the number of slots each region has been allocated:

  • Western Europe (4 ENC Slots): Denmark, France, Sweden, Great Britain, Germany, Spain, Finland, Portugal, Belgium, Norway, the Netherlands, Ireland, Switzerland, Luxembourg, Iceland, and Italy

  • Eastern Europe (4 ENC Slots): Ukraine, Israel, Poland, Lithuania, Romania, Kosovo, Czech Republic, Bulgaria, Hungary, Latvia, Estonia, Slovakia, North Macedonia, Bosnia & Herzegovina, Serbia, Croatia, and Slovenia

  • North America (3 ENC Slots): United States, Canada, Mexico, Guatemala, Panama, Dominican Republic, Costa Rica, and Puerto Rico

  • South America (3 ENC Slots): Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay, Chile, Peru, Colombia, Venezuela, and Paraguay

  • Middle East & Central Asia (3 ENC Slots): Türkiye, Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Lebanon, Azerbaijan, Georgia, Iraq, Palestine, United Arab Emirates, Turkmenistan, Kyrgyzstan, Islamic Republic of Iran, Tajikistan, Saudi Arabia, Jordan, and Armenia

  • North Africa (1 ENC Slot): Algeria, Tunisia, Morocco, Egypt

  • Sub Saharan Africa (1 ENC Slot): South Africa, Mauritius, Senegal, Nigeria, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Kenya, Ghana, and Angola

  • South & East Asia (2 ENC Slots): Mongolia, People's Republic of China, Pakistan, India, Republic of Korea, Hong Kong China, Chinese Taipei, and Japan

  • Southeast Asia (2 ENC Slots): Thailand, Singapore, Indonesia, Vietnam, Malaysia, the - Philippines, Cambodia, and Brunei Darussalam

  • Oceania (1 ENC Slot): Australia, New Zealand, Guam, and Fiji

Per the Nations Cup's rules, a national team cannot feature more than three players from the same VRS-ranked team. Organizations that release a core of three players for the Nations Cup will be able to earn VRS points as the main event will be classified as a tier-2 LAN.

Seeding for the qualifiers will be based on the June VRS ranking. For countries not featured in the VRS, the cumulative FACEIT ELO points of the players registered will be used as a seeding tool.

According to the rulebook, at the deadline of registration, only "the best 32 rosters registered will be allowed to play into the qualifier." In certain countries, that number will be increased to the best 64 rosters registered.

Teams can sign up for the ENC qualifiers by heading over to FACEIT's website and filtering the game by CS2.
