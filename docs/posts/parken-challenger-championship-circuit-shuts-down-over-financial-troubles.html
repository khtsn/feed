Parken Challenger Championship circuit shuts down over financial troubles

New Breed Agency ApS, the company behind the Parken Challenger Championship, has announced that it has initiated bankruptcy proceedings. As a result, the nine remaining events planned for 2026 have been cancelled.

The decision comes as a huge surprise as the tournament organizer had announced a circuit expansion less than two months ago. Starting from Parken Challenger Championship 9 at the end of June, every tournament was set to feature 24 teams, up from 18, and the prize pool was to be increased from €10,000 to $25,000.

"Over the past several months, management, shareholders, and partners have worked extensively to secure additional funding and explore alternative solutions that could allow the company and its projects to continue operating," a statement from PCC read.

"Unfortunately, despite these efforts, a viable solution could not be reached.

"As a result, the company is no longer able to continue its operations or fulfill its future commitments, leaving no responsible alternative but to commence bankruptcy proceedings."

In a joint statement on Saturday, Esplay and Barstool Production had announced the "suspension of operational involvement" in the PCC circuit without providing more details.

GamerLegion, 100 Thieves, B8 and BC.Game were among the teams that competed in the PCC at the Crowne Plaza Copenhagen Towers during the first half of 2026. STATE won the last Championship event, which was held on May 23-24.

The circuit was due to culminate in a stadium LAN finals event at the end of the year, also in Copenhagen.

HLTV knows that some teams that played in the circuit are still waiting to receive their prizes.

In its statement, New Breed Agency ApS acknowledged that there are "outstanding obligations connected to completed events and ongoing operations."

"Following the initiation of bankruptcy proceedings, all creditor matters and outstanding claims will be handled in accordance with applicable bankruptcy legislation and under the supervision of the appointed bankruptcy estate," the company added.

"We understand that this news will be disappointing and frustrating for many. We share that disappointment.

"Additional information will be communicated directly to affected stakeholders as soon as it becomes available."
