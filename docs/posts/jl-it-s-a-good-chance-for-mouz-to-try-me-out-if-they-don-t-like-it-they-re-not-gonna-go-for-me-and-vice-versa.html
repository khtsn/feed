jL: "It's a good chance for MOUZ to try me out; if they don't like it, they're not gonna go for me, and vice versa"

The Lithuanian rifler is looking forward to making the most of his two-event stint with MOUZ.

Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius is finally set to make his return to the server at PGL Astana after nearly 10 months away from tier-one competition, playing his first of two events on loan to MOUZ. The Lithuanian spoke to HLTV ahead of his first match in his new jersey, revealing he didn't expect to return so soon after previously indicating he would find a team after the summer break.

"I wasn't even thinking about returning now, to be honest," he said. "I was keeping my form, keeping my shape, but I didn't expect to be back before the Major because it is unusual to make such changes before. But I'm ready regardless; it is a good chance to feel myself out, and it's just that... I feel like now, I get a chance to shake off my rustiness, and if I do play well, that's good. I'm good for the summer, for either MOUZ or another team, and if I play badly, I know what to work on."

jL also spoke about his integration into the team, his impressions of Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman as an in-game leader and of young prospect Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze, as well as his hope to bring new life to MOUZ on stage and turn the community's sentiment about them around.

"I feel like whenever I play arena games, I'm confident, and you kind of see it from me," he said. "The way I stand, the way I play the game, the way I talk even, and I feel like confidence is an infectious trait. You cannot confuse it with arrogance, but I feel like if you are confident, that also gives confidence to your teammates, and they'll try to be the same.

"[...] So I want to bring that, and also bring the vibes to arena games because I feel like some teams, when they are playing an arena game, they miss the enjoying part. They're just playing under pressure and trying to perform as good as they can, but if you enjoy the process of playing in an arena, enjoy the process of being under pressure, you are going to perform better because then you can play your game and you feel like you want to be here instead of finish the game as fast as you can and leaving the arena."

Welcome back. Different colors for you now, so how does it feel to be back, first of all? Outside of the small Liquid stand-in stint.

It feels good to be back. I don't count the Liquid stand-in as being back, it was a lower-tier tournament and also we didn't do practice, we didn't do prep. I was basically a stand-in, and now I feel like I am a part of the team because we practiced a lot and we prepped a lot.

Yeah, it feels good to be back. You see familiar faces and then you're back in the competition, because when you do content or streaming, there is no competition. I mean, there is competition of course, with other streamers, but it is not as serious as with other players and other teams. We came here to show a great game.

Almost 10 months away from competition. What has it been like, actually getting back into the server and into the routine?

Getting back into the server was never a problem. I kept my form, and of course there were downswings of form where I didn't play for 10 days, but most of the time I used to have at least 40 hours in the past two weeks, sometimes even 60-80. I've just been enjoying life, but enjoying life also means playing CS because I also enjoy playing CS. So I'm feeling good.

If they really don't believe they're going to win the tournaments that are coming up, why not make the change now?

You come into MOUZ at a bit of a strange time in terms of them making this change where you're not actually going to play the Major, but you're in for this event and CAC. Talk to me about that, the timing and getting this chance to come in.

The timing is... I wouldn't call it weird, but it is interesting, in a way. Teams don't usually do that, but... I didn't really speak to them about it because it's not my business, but if they really don't believe they're going to win the tournaments that are coming up, why not make the change now? If you think you're going to do poorly at the Major, why would you not change before the Major? Now, if I get to stay with them, if I will play after Major, it is a good head start for all five of us. I feel like it is a change for the long run, and yeah, I do understand the decision from MOUZ.

Talking about your actual return a bit — when we talked to you when you stood in for Liquid, you said you expected to be back after the summer break. Did this MOUZ move expedite that return, or was this something that you already thought was in the cards for the summer instead?

Hmm (smiles)... I won't answer that one, sorry.

Okay! For you then, in terms of making this move before the summer, do you think that you're ready now? Did you want to return now as well?

I wasn't even thinking about returning now, to be honest. I was keeping my form, keeping my shape, but I didn't expect to be back before the Major because it is unusual to make such changes before. But I'm ready regardless; it is a good chance to feel myself out, and it's just that... I feel like now, I get a chance to shake off my rustiness, and if I do play well, that's good. I'm good for the summer, for either MOUZ or another team, and if I play badly, I know what to work on. So I feel like it is a good change for me, to be honest.

I wasn't even thinking about returning now, to be honest [...] I didn't expect to be back before the Major

What is your impression of the team so far? You obviously haven't had a ton of time to practice and no officials quite yet, but what are your initial impressions after this little bit?

I'm surprised at how well they communicate with each other — I mean now, us, as a team. And I've always held MOUZ as a strong CT-sided team, and now I understand why. There's a lot of communication going, there are a lot of plans in the mid-round, there is always activity going on in the map; it's not just you holding the angle. I really value that because I always thought that is what a good CT side team is supposed to be, and now I see it in MOUZ.

I feel like Dori [xertioN] as an in-game leader, I didn't have any expectations for him, but he is doing really well. The way he comes up with rounds and reactions, how to play the rounds, super fast, it is astonishing to me. I feel like he has a lot of potential as an in-game leader. And the rest, xelex is a great shooter, Spinx, I like him, I like the way he plays. He plays similarly to me, but a little bit on the more passive side, if we compare each other.

torzsi is a great guy, good energy. I love practicing with him because sometimes it might get a bit dull, you're too focused on the rounds, and he will pop a scream out of nowhere. I'm glad they're not bullying me out of the team yet (laughs), but let's see what happens in the future.

I want to get your thoughts on the players a bit more, but you mentioned this role with Spinx and how he is playing. There is a bit of an overlap between you and him, especially on T side, and on CT side you have no anchors as well. How are you working through this, and what is your role in how this is going to fit in?

In terms of the T side, I am the more aggressive lurk, which I have always played in my life, so there is no role clash there. On CT side, I pretty much kept most of my roles. Some positions I switched, but it's not that I don't like them. For example, I did have to make a sacrifice here and there, but besides that, I moved into positions that I'm comfortable with, that I love playing and enjoying playing.

Some positions I have to learn, but I feel like right now it's a good balance of who is supposed to be where. If you look at us and how we evaluate ourselves as players, I feel like now it's a really good balance. Maybe we'll do some changes in the future, but for now, I feel like we're in the best positions if you look at it team-wise.

In terms of that T overlap I mentioned, does that mean then that Spinx is going to go more towards Jimi's roles and you're getting more of his aggressive lurk spots then?

I don't know what Jimi played; I know that he was the passive main lurker, so... yeah, right now I'm the more aggressive one. So Spinx is going back to his old roles, and I'm going into the more aggressive ones which I've played before.

Right now it is a good chance for MOUZ to try me out, and if they don't like it, they're not going to go for me in the future, and vice versa

You mentioned where this might go in the future. How much into that are you looking? Right now it's a loan deal from NAVI, so what does this mean for your future and where that can go?

I feel like this is good for both of us, for me and MOUZ, because I need to like it, but also they have to like me and playing with me. Right now it is a good chance for them to try me out, and if they don't like it, they're not going to go for me in the future, and vice versa. So I feel like it is a good move for both [parties], and if MOUZ don't go for me, at least there is some evidence, some data, of me playing actively, for the other teams to look into. It's a good chance to show what I'm capable of after such a long break.

You come into MOUZ at a time where it's a little rocky for them. They made a lot of deep runs in playoffs, repeatedly, but couldn't get the trophies in the end a lot of the time, and recently the form has dipped down. What have you seen coming in to maybe change some of that? torzsi, when I was in Rio, said they had been going into a bit of a bad spiral and overanalyzing things, and that was something they were trying to fix, so what have you noticed coming in, and how is the atmosphere in the team now?

The atmosphere is great. I feel like, with their past lineup, they didn't have as many people responsible for the vibe, and right now they added me. I kind of love getting a good joke in or vibing with the team, so that is an upgrade in that term for sure. And then xelex is always interested; he's always learning, everything is new to him, so it is fun to watch from the side. Yeah, I feel like right now, the problem is not going to be the atmosphere; the problem is going to be the gameplay for sure because we didn't have a lot of time to practice. Of course we'll try our best, but it is hard to be on top of your game with such little practice.

Talking about the gameplay a bit, you mentioned xertioN turning in an in-game leader. No officials in the books yet, but how has it been playing under him so far?

It feels good. I feel like whatever call he makes is so fast and precise. He understands where the gaps are in the map and how to exploit them, and then he is also good at pointing out what you should do. He's not big in micromanaging, but sometimes he sees something, and instead of just saying, 'We need to go B,' he says, 'jL, we need to go B, you need to go through Cave and you need to molly Pillar,' because he knows someone is there and he just tells me what to do instead of explaining the whole situation. So I like that, because he has a bigger overview of how things are going as the in-game leader. Yeah, just a super fast and very precise speaker. I love it.

How would you compare it to how Aleksi was calling back when you were on NAVI?

Aleksi was giving more freedom to the players on how to do stuff. So for example, Aleksi would say, 'We're going to go A,' and he would expect iM, for example, to say, 'I'm going to smoke CT,' and b1t, 'I'm going to smoke Ruins,' whatever, if it's not a set call. With Dori, it's easy, because he's going to say, 'Okay, you smoke this, you smoke that,' and we kind of have a general understanding of how things are supposed to be.

Like if I go entry, or Lotan [Spinx] has to entry, or Adrian [xelex] has to entry, we understand that, and it is kind of easy to understand who is supposed to do what. For example, if torzsi has an AWP on Inferno, he is not going to be going in first. I really like the way we are playing right now; I feel like it has a lot of potential. Of course we need to work on the details, but it does have a lot of potential.

Obviously with Brollan going out, it is a structural change in this team. But at the same time, it was considered sycrone's team in a way, his overall structure and his overall setup. What has coming into that structure been like? What are your impressions of sycrone and how the MOUZ system is designed and built?

The MOUZ system is for sure different to NAVI's, but... I don't know, I didn't spot the negatives yet. For me, the real challenge comes when the officials come. Right now, we didn't even play our first official, so there is not much that I can say because practice is one thing, and officials are another. They have a little bit of a different system preparing for the game, so I'm really keen on trying that out. Maybe in the future I can tell you a little bit more, but right now I can't give a conclusive answer.

Anything you can tell me about that system in preparing for games that is different?

Hmm (smles)... no, I'll keep this one to myself.

Okay. You talked about xelex very briefly, but what have you made of him? He's big on our prospects list, touted as one of the next big players to come up, so what are your early impressions of him without playing any officials?

He's a very sharp shooter for sure, and he's very keen on learning. He kind of has his game style, but he is open to changing it in a way that fits us as a team, and I feel like it is a really good trait to have as a prospect because, when you're a little bit younger, it's easier for you to learn stuff and acknowledge it. And if you understand that this is where you lack, and you have to focus on this, he is going to be an even better player in the future because he is going to understand what the team needs, what tier-one is for other people, and... yeah.

It was so funny, we were playing practice against a tier-two team, and he said, 'Okay, this is the round they're going to rush A now,' and I asked, 'Why do you think that?' [He said], 'Because I've played in this level, I know how they are thinking,' and they actually did rush A. So yeah, these little things he'll have to change for tier-one, but besides that, we'll see how he performs in officials because this is his first tier-one tournament. So far he's been good in practices and I hope he keeps up the form.

I'm not sure how many tier-one teams you've played in practice, but have you noticed a big difference in how he adapts in those kind of games?

No, not so much. I feel like we've focused more on ourselves when we've practiced. I mean, you can focus on the other team when you play officials, or if you treat the practice like an official, but we were focused on ourselves so I didn't see the difference.

Another big topic around MOUZ that I mentioned earlier has been chasing those trophies for a long time, but struggling when it gets to those games where it really matters in the arena. You, the jLegend himself [jL laughs], known as a bit of an arena player obviously, so what are you hoping to bring to MOUZ to turn around those performances?

Just confidence. I feel like whenever I play arena games, I'm confident, and you kind of see it from me. The way I stand, the way I play the game, the way I talk even, and I feel like confidence is an infectious trait. You cannot confuse it with arrogance, but I feel like if you are confident, that also gives confidence to your teammates, and they'll try to be the same. Even though sometimes it might be fake confidence, it's better to be fake confident than not confident at all.

So I want to bring that, and also bring the vibes to arena games because I feel like some teams, when they are playing an arena game, they miss the enjoying part. They're just playing under pressure and trying to perform as good as they can, but if you enjoy the process of playing in an arena, enjoy the process of being under pressure, you are going to perform better because then you can play your game and you feel like you want to be here instead of finish the game as fast as you can and leaving the arena. Just enjoy the moment because you play for these moments, and that's [what] your whole life revolves around: being in arenas, being in semi-finals, grand finals, winning the trophies. You cannot win a trophy without enjoying the road, in my opinion.

You've always been a player who is a bit of a fan favorite when you get into an arena, whereas MOUZ have been struggling in that aspect recently. They don't really have the support of a lot of crowds, so with potentially going into an arena, what do you expect from a crowd and how do you hope to change how MOUZ has been treated by a lot of fans recently?

I feel like I don't have many expectations. I really have to feel it because right now it's a different lineup, now we have different players. They've got me, and I'm kind of good with the Russian-speaking community, so... I don't know, we'll see how it happens. I feel like outside the hotel, whenever you meet fans, they're always super nice and super chill. At least, they tell you that they are supporting you, but they probably say it to every person that they meet (smiles). But it feels good for now, we'll see how it is in the arena if we make it there. But I don't know, I just want to see it, I have no expectations to be honest.

The community has not been as nice to MOUZ as they have been to me, for example. And I feel like it is a spiral that people kind of join in on and the hate is so forced

You mentioned in a video you released when you joined the team that people say MOUZ don't have aura, and maybe [you agree in some way]. You played into it a little bit, but how are you hoping to change that conversation in the community overall? You said you wanted to do some content and get their faces out there, so talk to me about that.

The first priority is getting our CS down, but if we do have a day off, or if we get time to spend time with each other, I really want to showcase them as human beings because I feel like they are just seen as pros who play the game and they don't show personality. Of course it's their choice to not show it, but I feel like the community has not been as nice to them as they have been to me, for example. And I feel like it is a spiral that people kind of join in on and the hate is so forced; there are no reasons for them to hate MOUZ, and still people do.

I understand if they were toxic, or if they were doing some things that are not accepted, but people just hate MOUZ because 'they have no aura.' That is no reason for a person to hate a team, and if people have ever valued CS, they would look at MOUZ and be like, 'Okay, they are playing good CS and I'm going to support them.' I mean, you don't have to support them, but hating is unnecessary.

I've always admired MOUZ for their CT sides and the way they play the game. It's always teamwork; it's never one player carrying the game, and that is something that I, as a CS fan, enjoyed. But I guess the fans look at different things, like 'aura,' star player capability. Yeah... I feel like MOUZ have struggled to show personality, but you don't need it. I mean, you do need to show it in a way, but to get hated for not showing personality is crazy, like beyond understandable for me.

You mentioned it twice, that MOUZ are known for their CT sides, and it has been a big component of how consistent they've been. But the T sides have lacked at times, so what kind of changes are you hoping to bring in terms of that?

I feel like the T sides are mostly blamed on the in-game leader, which I think is not true. I mean yes, to a certain extent maybe the in-game leader could take 40% of the blame, but the rest is also players. If you have four passive players and you're not going for the plays, you're not going for map control, you don't understand the intricacies of positions when they've left, where you have space, where you have to fight 100% and take the duel. I feel like MOUZ, previously, have not been the best team at taking the timings, the gaps, and understanding where the enemy team is pivoting as CT players.

I feel like we've upgraded on the firepower for sure, and the knowledge of where the enemies are and what their plan is. Of course, it also comes down to prep, but also the feeling of the game, and sometimes you can feel something but not go for it because you're just not that type of player, and I feel like now we have a good balance of going for it and not going for it. But we'll see how it plays out in the officials.

You really only have two events; this and CAC. You said you wanted to use it as time to prove yourself, whether that results in something long-term with MOUZ or if it gives you an opportunity afterward. But it is not a lot of time to prove yourself, so what do you make of this short period you have to do that and get a result with MOUZ?

I feel like two events are enough to prove yourself. You can just feel if a player is good or not, like sometimes you can have a bad event because you've been unfortunate, and what I'm looking for from myself is making good decisions, being communicative, bringing ideas and executing them well. I'm not looking to post a 1.40 rating; I'm looking for a team win and I want to do everything in my power to make the team win. Sometimes that means you take your knife out and you jump for the team, and for me, that's going to be resulting in a death on the statistics. But I know it's a worthy sacrifice because we won the round, so if that grants me a death but gives us the round, then I won.

Two tournaments is enough because if I can show a good game now, I don't feel like much is going to change. Yes, I'm not in peak form right now because I still haven't played officials, I still need to work on some things, but the foundation is there and I just have to show it.

Every door is open if Vitality is not behind the door (laughs). But I wouldn't even want to face them now [...] we'd be waiting for the inevitable to happen

Just talking about Astana: you're not the only team coming in with changes. Falcons have karrigan, and Spirit are looking much better after Rio. How do you feel about the overall field here and what do you hope to be able to do with MOUZ here?

It is winnable, for sure. Of course, we're not aiming for the trophy, but it is winnable. The chances are slim, but they're never zero. Falcons made a change, so we'll see if it's for the better or for the worse, but I hope we face them. I feel like it is a team that would be a very good challenge for us; a team that has a lot of firepower, but we'll see. And Spirit, for sure they're looking good, we practiced against them a couple of times because they were mostly the team that was available from the tier-one scene because the others were in America.

Yeah, I feel like it is winnable of course, the chances are slim, but I feel like FURIA are beatable, I think every single team is beatable and I just want to face them. Of course, first we have Gentle Mates to take care of, so we'll see how that plays out. We'll aim for quality games and playoffs.

Obviously no Vitality here either. They've been winning everything while you've been gone, so to not have them here as a test, and not at CAC either, do you feel like there is a bit of an open door in that sense?

Open door? I mean, yeah (smiles). Every door is open if Vitality is not behind the door (laughs). But I wouldn't even want to face them now, it would just be... not a shame, but we'd be waiting for the inevitable to happen. I feel like we're in no shape or form even close to beating Vitality, and no one has been. I wouldn't even want to face them to be honest, so I'm glad they're not here, they're not in CAC. It's better for us, for sure.
