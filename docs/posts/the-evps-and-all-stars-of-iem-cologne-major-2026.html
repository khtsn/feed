The EVPs and All-Stars of IEM Cologne Major 2026

Falcons got over the mightiest of hurdles when they won IEM Cologne, and it took great performances from each and every member to do so.

There was little to pick from between the fragging trio of Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov, Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač, and Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin, while René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen had stable impact from tough roles and Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen's leadership deserves huge credit.

But there's only one MVP medal, and m0NESY's consistency, round wins, and lightning-quick start to the grand final ensured it went home with him.

Post-game, he was quick to credit karrigan for the jump in form, after a change of style helped take m0NESY to his career highlight moment.

"The things we are doing," he explained, "all the rotations, the chaos we are building on the server, this is me, this is who I am.

"This is where I can help all the time because you are always putting a lot of responsibility on yourself, but you're winning rounds because of these chaotic moments. So... Finn is amazing, as I said. Finn is amazing."

Radar Chart Stat Glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • Swing in RW Δ: round swing in round wins

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin

kyousuke has averaged a 1.27 rating in playoff matches under karrigan, and he was even better than that in Cologne with highlights in every arena series.

A 1.30 arena rating was the best of anyone present, with 1.15 KPRW in the playoffs and six standout maps against top-5 opposition when you include Natus Vincere.

Some quiet moments in groups meant he ended on a 1.15 rating, but against top opposition he was right up there with m0NESY (1.26 vs top10, m0NESY 1.25) and just ahead of NiKo (1.21).

It's his third EVP in three events, and all three have been very strong; of everyone, it's kyousuke who has benefited most from the change of captaincy, and the difference it has made to Falcons is clear.

Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač

Joining his teammates on a truly standout podium, NiKo was a vital part of Falcons' run to the Major title he has yearned for since becoming a professional more than a decade ago.

He posted a 1.29 rating in map wins, the same as MVP m0NESY, and ended the grand final as the top player on a 1.43 rating. As vanquishing ghosts go, top-fragging on Inferno alongside karrigan is a brilliant way to consign Boston's final to the dustbin of history.

Fairly weak round win stats (0.95 KPRW overall, 0.89 in playoffs) kept him third within his team and the event, but all three of Falcons' triumvirate will receive huge amounts of credit from Cologne for the Top 20 come January.

Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets

When donk opened proceedings by obliterating Stage 2 and then dropping a 2.48 rating on world number two Natus Vincere's heads, you'd be forgiven for thinking it was a re-run of Shanghai.

He ended Stage 3 with a ridiculous 1.53 rating, and five maps above the 1.60 mark out of seven.

In the playoffs, it was not quite as effortless as Spirit started to miss having a coach behind them and donk came down to Earth.

But we should be careful in holding him to a higher standard than other players. The quarter-final was still great, with a 1.32 rating during one of the most intense (and longest) matches of the year.

A 0.97 rating against Falcons in the semi-finals is harder to forgive — he admitted on Telegram that he "choked" — but does not undo the vast body of work he had put in up to that point, with a 1.61 rating in map wins and a very strong EVP secured.

Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut

Roles were reversed between the game's top two players in Cologne, with ZywOo outdoing donk in rating (1.42-1.34) and KPRW (1.19-1.12) but having to settle with a lower placement both in the event and the EVP list.

But even with a quarter-final exit, ZywOo did more than enough for a top-four EVP with an absurd 1.90 rating in Vitality's six map wins that got them to playoffs.

He was top of the event in ADR (92.6), KPR (0.89), and Swing (+3.80%) as he tried with all his might to breathe life into a Vitality that — for once — was struggling to click into gear.

Unfortunately for the Frenchman, he could not be Superman on every map: He was quiet on the first and third maps of the quarter-final defeat to Falcons, which was at least part of the reason for the team's elimination, and his placing below donk on this list.

Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko

molodoy's consistency in Cologne made his EVP inevitable, with 87% of maps above a 1.00 rating and the third-best kill differential per round at the event (+0.26).

His short career has been defined by arena highlights but also lowlights, not least at the Majors in Austin and Budapest. So it was encouraging to see him stand tall against Falcons with a 1.26 rating in the grand final (FURIA's second best had a 0.90).

And it was that performance on Sunday that ensured he finished top of his team on the EVP ranking, buoyed by his high floor up to that point.

Mareks "⁠YEKINDAR⁠" Gaļinskis

YEKINDAR led FURIA in map wins (1.42 rating) and in doing so, made himself the frontrunner for the MVP award before the final.

A 0-3 defeat and a 0.89 rating against Falcons put that timeline to bed, but it is important context for what is still a solid EVP — YEKINDAR's first of 2026.

He was 0-5 in Major quarter-finals before the match against 9z, but refused to let it become 0-6 after a 1.87-rated Mirage to level the series in a sign of his character under pressure.

Overall, he had eight proper single-map peaks, the most of any finalist, with all of 9z, MOUZ, Aurora, BetBoom, and B8 feeling his wrath.

Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko

HeavyGod narrowly missed out on EVPs at PGL Cluj-Napoca and BLAST Rivals, but he's off to the races in 2026 with one at the Cologne Major.

It's a convincing one, too, with nine standout maps and a 1.26 event rating when none of his G2 teammates exceeded 1.00.

His quarter-final against Spirit was hampered by his bizarre community ban halfway through the Mirage decider, a dent to his momentum he did not recover from even as the game went into several overtimes.

But his level beforehand was more than enough to be seen as exceptionally valuable, and we're sure Natus Vincere would agree after his 1.54-rated series dumped the world number two out of the tournament in groups.

Dmitry "⁠sh1ro⁠" Sokolov

In all 14 of Spirit's maps in Cologne, sh1ro had a positive K-D and a rating at or above 1.00. He's the only player at the event to have a 100% record there, a level of sheer consistency deep into the tournament that marks him as exceptionally valuable on its own.

sh1ro was player of the map three times, so there were peaks to go with his consistency, too. He was even Spirit's best player in their semi-final elimination at the hands of Falcons, whilst he kept up with donk in round wins (1.11 KPRW, vs 1.12).

It is his 26th Big Event EVP, enough to be fourth in the all-time list as further evidence (if you needed it) of his brilliance as a second star.

Yuri "⁠yuurih⁠" Santos

We're up to the tenth best player of the event now, but yuurih's playoff performance en route to the final meant we had to include him as an EVP.

Only m0NESY and kyousuke can outdo his tally of four EVP+ maps in the arena, which was enough to convert his 1.20-rated group stage into a nod. He was in the top-three on the scoreboard in 60% of his maps, an almost ever-present part of FURIA's run to their first-ever final.

A quiet Falcons game held him back from going higher, as did his weak fragging in round wins, but he had already done enough for an EVP before a single round was played on Championship Sunday.

Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın

Finally, we reach Aurora and Wicadia, who continues to come of age as his team's best player as Ismailcan "⁠XANTARES⁠" Dörtkardeş' star begins to wane.

Our tenth EVP, Wicadia still could not be left out having led Aurora in map wins (1.39 rating) and round wins (1.06 KPRW) in their run to a top-four placing.

Their run was not the toughest a Major semi-finalist could have had, which is part of why Wicadia sits at the bottom of the lengthy EVP list, but plenty of teams had tough times against BetBoom, 9z, and G2; just not Wicadia and Aurora.

Honorable mentions

Even with a full showmatch lineup of EVPs, there are plenty of other players who did enough to garner credit for their work in Cologne.

Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato had a 1.30 rating in FURIA's map wins, but only a 0.98 rating against top 10 teams while nearly half of his maps ended below the 1.00 rating mark.

Next is Luciano "⁠luchov⁠" Herrera, who has left his first two MVP events as a frontrunner for Rookie of the Year after an EVP in Astana and coming just a whisker shy of the same feat in Cologne after a 1.04-rated and 39-45 K-D quarter-final. His 2.00-rated three-map series against PARIVISION will live long in the memory.

Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool was quiet by his standards, but still strong compared to the average player with a 1.27 rating in map wins and a 1.13 in playoffs despite Vitality's defeat.

Kirill "⁠Magnojez⁠" Rodnov led BetBoom's surprise run to playoffs with 1.07 KPRW and a 1.36 rating in map wins, but could not confirm his EVP against Aurora.

In groups, Valeriy "⁠b1t⁠" Vakhovskiy was the standout on a 1.17 rating, though he will be kicking himself for his 0.65 rating against Falcons in a map that would have sent Natus Vincere to playoffs.

Džiugas "⁠dziugss⁠" Steponavičius and Aurélien "⁠afro⁠" Drapier also deserve credit for decent performances, even as their teams failed to crack the top half.

All-Star

It's one of the times where more than three of the winning team would canter into our all-star lineup, if we didn't have a rule capping it at three per team.

That meant a tough decision, as we had to leave out one of karrigan or the three top EVPs. The IGL is the easiest player to swap usually, but karrigan could not be left out having transformed Falcons from chokers to overtime specialists and Major winners.

In the end, it was kyousuke who we left out because the gap to the next-best player in his role was the smallest of the quartet. Therefore, we combine m0NESY and NiKo with donk to create an even more formidable star trio than Falcons.

As our linchpin, it's the ever-impressive HeavyGod who takes his spot as Anchor of the Event for the third time in 2026 already, just pipping yuurih to it.
