Spirit beat Falcons in Astana to end nine-month trophy drought

Spirit took home the PGL Astana trophy after besting the new-look Falcons in the best-of-five grand final (Dust2 16-12, Mirage 13-7, Ancient 13-10).

The victory marks Spirit's second trophy in Astana after the Russian squad secured the title on the event's debut in 2025.

The beginning of the match was headlined by Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets missing his usual impact, as the young phenom began the match 0-6 and struggled for the entirety of regulation. Still, Spirit did enough to take a 12-9 lead and while they couldn't close out either of the three map points, they gave their superstar enough time to wake up.

donk then went 7-2 in overtime as Falcons' individuals went missing, allowing Spirit to take the lead in the series.

Mirage was a much different affair. Falcons offered nothing on their T side outside of a pistol-round victory and a Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin 1vs3, and while a solid start to the CT side made the scoreline somewhat close, Spirit were never in danger.

The Russian squad continued demolishing the Falcons on Ancient, where they quickly found themselves 8-1 up by limiting Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov to zero frags in the first nine rounds before closing out a 13-10 victory after converting one of their six tournament points.

Spirit go back-to-back in Astana

The win against Falcons netted Spirit their second trophy in Kazakhstan's capital after they won last year's edition of PGL's event.

The Russian squad will hope that this is a similarly good omen for the future, considering they followed up their last Astana trophy with consecutive titles at IEM Cologne and BLAST Bounty.

donk and company are ramping up just as Vitality is showing some, albeit explainable, weakness — so we might get an exciting race for the Major title after all.

karrigan not enough to break grand final curse (yet)

Much has been said about Falcons' lack of titles since signing m0NESY, with the star-studded roster now racking up six second-place finishes with the Russian AWPer.

Despite the mitigating circumstances mentioned by Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen in a pre-match interview, where he admitted that the team hasn't had enough time to get the map pool up to standard for a best-of-five series, the lack of fight shown by Falcons in a worrying sign.

While the Danish captain emphasized that he joined the team as part of a long-term project and will no doubt have the chance to properly stamp his authority on the team over the coming months, the recurrence of many of the same issues that plagued the squad under previous leadership is unlikely to quiet the criticism surrounding Falcons.
