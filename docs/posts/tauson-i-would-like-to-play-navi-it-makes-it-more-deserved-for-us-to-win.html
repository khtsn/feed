Tauson: "I would like to play NAVI; it [makes it] more deserved for us to win"

Tauson is bound for his first notable grand final after a year and a half with GamerLegion.

After notching a third-place finish at Stake Ranked Episode 1 and a semi-final placing at BLAST Rivals 2026 Season 1, GamerLegion have managed to keep up their impressive run of form at IEM Atlanta.

The international side overcame their opening opponents of SINNERS, before narrowly falling to the lower bracket at the hands of Natus Vincere. GamerLegion rebounded to qualify for playoffs with wins over Astralis and Liquid, and have stayed strong on stage, besting Brazilian sides paiN and Legacy to clinch the organization's first grand final berth since the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023.

Just after his team's hard-fought semi-final victory over Legacy, GamerLegion rifler Sebastian "⁠Tauson⁠" Tauson Lindelof spoke with HLTV regarding the match, the changes to the team's structure following the addition of Janusz "⁠Snax⁠" Pogorzelski at the beginning of the year, and his hopes for their upcoming best-of-five grand final.

Or you can listen to the interview here.

Huge win over Legacy, and you're headed to the grand final of IEM Atlanta, your first big final with GamerLegion. How are you feeling?

Feeling amazing. I've been on this team for two years now, almost, and it's the first big grand final in a tier-one event, so I'm looking so much forward to it.

Tell me about that Mirage game, because that got out of hand really fast. What went wrong?

I don't even know how to explain it, but we were not on the same page. Some people were playing too aggressive when we are not supposed to, and when we were supposed to play aggressive, we didn't do it. We were just not on the same page on that Mirage. We came back on Inferno, and I'm happy we managed to reset after that Mirage, because it was not good enough.

What was the talk like after Mirage?

I mean, the talk was just that it was not good enough. We need to step up all individually, we need to be more on the same page, talk to each other in the mid-round. I think we managed to do that pretty good on Inferno.

It feels like you guys are finally hitting your stride. Third place at Stake, semis at Rivals, now a grand final here. Do you think you guys are starting to hit your peak?

I hope we didn't hit our peak out. I think we're going in the right direction, and I think this team can be very, very good, even better than the one we had last year.

What has it been like working with Snax? What is he doing differently from what ztr was doing before?

I think when we had ztr, we were playing very loose, and our game style was kinda dependent on the players, and not the IGL. Right now, Snax is doing a very good job at controlling our mid-rounds and really setting the idea of the round, painting the picture for us. I think the other side is also good, if the players are good enough, but I think at the end of the old roster, I think we were just not on the same page with how we wanted to do things.

He helps you get the support of the crowd here a bit, as well.

Yeah, he does, he does.

You'll have a NAVI rematch or face BetBoom in the finals. Thoughts on those matchups?

I would like to play NAVI, to be honest. You can say it's more deserved for us to win, if we win. I would like to play NAVI and get revenge against them, because I think we could have won the last game we played against them.
