malbsMd: "I want to be the malbs that I once was, and I'm hoping to find myself again"

The Guatemalan star discussed the circumstances of his move to Liquid and his expectations for this new challenge.

After nearly two years with G2, Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa is ready to embark on a new challenge, having joined Liquid in a deal that saw Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz move in the opposite direction.

The transfer gives malbsMd a fresh start after a rough period with G2 following Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač's departure to Falcons. At one point, the Guatemalan admitted he was struggling to enjoy the game and needed to step away from social media.

"Of course, I'm excited to play with the boys, but I'm looking forward to being a new person, a new player as well," malbsMd told HLTV on BLAST Rotterdam's media day.

"That's what I'm kind of looking forward to. I want to be the malbs that I once was, and I'm hoping to find myself again."

With malbsMd back on the roster, Liquid will need to play five matches before they can enter the Americas VRS. After BLAST, they will be attending DraculaN and WINAMAX, hoping to get some LAN wins under their belt.

"We just need to be really good at fundamentals and all of this," he said. "If we get to work on this really well and be precise with it, I think we can be good for those."

You’re fresh off joining Liquid, a new team after two years with G2. How does it feel to be part of a new project?

It is exciting, for sure. I'm not gonna lie, though: I didn't see myself leaving G2 this quickly. But yeah, other than that, I'm really excited for, like, a new era, kind of, to put it that way. And yeah, I'm looking forward to playing with the boys.

I wanted to ask how this move came about. When did you learn that this could happen? Was it something that was kind of imposed on you, or did you have a say in it?

It was imposed. Like, they came with the offer and then I received a call from G2 that they had received the offer and they were willing to do the transfer. And yeah, that's how it happened.

It's no secret that things were going rough for G2 as a team and you personally, especially after NiKo left. There was a lot of pressure on you to become the new NiKo and take on that very active voice on the team. Do you think that this new challenge could help you find yourself again in the game?

For sure, yeah. I think I just needed, like, a new environment to play in, and that's what is exciting me the most. Of course, I'm excited to play with the boys, but I'm looking forward to being a new person, a new player as well. That's what I'm kind of looking forward to. I want to be the malbs that I once was, and I'm hoping to find myself again.

Talking a bit about G2 and the way things went down, the team had that honeymoon period in London, but you could never build on that. Looking back, what do you think that went wrong with the team?

I don't know. I think we could never find the consistency that we wanted to find. It's hard to say. I cannot really think about something. I would just say it was because of me not performing in the roles I was playing back then, because I also switched to anchor positions at the end. I don't know. It's tough to say, honestly. I have no idea. I cannot really give you a proper answer.

What are your first impressions of this new team, and what will be asked of you, compared to what you were doing with G2?

I think it's good, because we have a really big voice in EliGE, and from what I've played with him just now, I think he has a really good voice, and he's trying to help siuhy with the comms and everything. But I think I'm going to have a more chill time, trying to think more on myself as a player a little bit, because I was trying to focus on forcing myself to be this kind of communicative player, and I couldn't focus really on myself. I think it's going to be good. I think we have a good mix of good voices, not many voices, and it's a good balance, you know?

The team just came back from Portugal, where they played in the Roman Imperium Cup. Have you had any practice at all with the team?

A little bit. Not too much, because of course they were playing with jL, and we didn't really have much time to spend on the server. But we have talked a little bit, they have gone through some stuff, basic stuff from the team with me. I think it will be enough.

And looking at G2 getting NertZ, how do you think that things will work out for them with a new player, a different player, compared to you?

I hope everything works out for them. Of course, I'm really good friends with huNter-, so I'm always rooting for him, and I hope everything goes right for the team. But I think overall, I think it's a good trade. I think it benefits both parties. They got a more balanced player in NertZ, and Liquid got a more aggressive player in me. So I think it's a good balance.

After this event, you’ll have DraculaN, and then WINAMAX. Those are two very important tournaments when it comes to the Major qualification. If things go well, you can gain a lot of points. But if things go south, it can also cost you a lot of points. How do you think that the team will deal with and handle that pressure, considering what's on the line, and the fact that you will have very minimal practice before those events?

I think we just need to focus on practicing, working on those small details, the basics and all of this stuff so we can be strong on fundamentals. That is the most important thing for when we play these kinds of events, like DraculaN and all that stuff, that we play smaller teams or a little bit worse teams. We just need to be really good at fundamentals and all of this. But yeah, if we get to work on this really well and be precise with it, I think we can be good for those.
