PARIVISION exit Cologne winless

PARIVISION are the second team to depart Stage 3 of the IEM Cologne Major, exiting with a 0-3 record alongside B8. Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali's men came out on the losing side of three close best-of-threes throughout their run, faltering to 9z and Monte before Legacy sent them home after two overtime maps.

FUT stay alive in Cologne at B8's expense

PARIVISION started their Major campaign in Stage 3 of the event, owing to their solid results during the first few months of 2026. The Russian squad slowed down leading to the Major, exiting PGL Astana in 12-14th place and the CS Asia Championships in 7-8th place. PARIVISION have matched the result of their first Major outing, a 0-3 exit from Stage 3 of the StarLadder Budapest Major.

"We're still a young team with young players, and we just have less experience on the map," Jame shared after the game. "That's all, it's nothing crazy. They're just better, honestly."

"I think it always hurts when you lose. But, I don't know. It's a bad game, but nothing special. We just played poor, the same mistakes. But we're young, we need to learn from this Major, it's the second Major for us as a team. Nothing bad."

Legacy hit the ground running on their pick of Inferno, picking up an 8-4 lead, with their offensive success only briefly interrupted by a three-round streak from PARIVISION's defense. Legacy struggled to maintain their momentum on the CT side, missing out on the second pistol round and succumbing to nonstop B hits from their opponents, leading to overtime. A well-timed flank from Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan gave Legacy the foothold they needed in overtime, as they quickly put together four rounds to lock down the map 16-14.

PARIVISION were undeterred by their narrow Inferno loss, wresting control on the T side of Dust2 off the back of a 1v2 from Andrey "⁠BELCHONOKK⁠" Yasinskiy and a 1v3 from Jame. Legacy managed to soften the blow towards the end of the half, but a CT pistol victory for PARIVISION made it seem like a third map was in the cards.

Despite the dismal scoreline, Legacy managed to piece things together on the offense, with Eduardo "⁠dumau⁠" Wolkmer, Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto, and Guilherme "⁠saadzin⁠" Pacheco providing crucial impact throughout the second half to claim the lead. The Brazilians were unable to close out the map in regulation, but multikills from saadzin and latto in overtime allowed them to claim the map 16-13 and the series 2-0.
