JW makes first MVP-event playoffs in five years as EYEBALLERS take down FOKUS

EYEBALLERS have made it to the playoffs of PGL Bucharest after taking down FOKUS 2-1.

"Holy fuck I've missed tier one," Jesper "⁠JW⁠" Wecksell tweeted on media day, and the veteran Swede is making the most of his return five games later.

Now the all-time map count leader by some way on 3045, JW has played on EYEBALLERS, the organization he founded, since the summer of 2022.

A default win over FaZe, plus victories against NRG and FOKUS, have now taken him to his first playoffs at an event of this level since cs_summit 7 in January 2021 — a wait of five years, two months, and eight days.

"It feels very good [to make playoffs]," JW tells HLTV after the match. "I think no one expected it, other than ourselves. I watch the young guys at practice every day, I know what they are capable of. I'm proud they get to show that in front of the world."

He added: "Everything from here is a bonus, but I'm a bit delusional; every tournament I enter, I obviously go for a win."

Everything from here is a bonus, but I'm a bit delusional as well, the person I am. If you don't go for a win or think you can win, then why are you even competing?

The series began on Inferno, where EYEBALLERS recovered from 12-7 down to force overtime and a 16-13 win. Kalle "⁠Ro1f⁠" Johansson was in particularly good form, with 96 ADR and a 1.44 rating.

FOKUS shrugged off that psychological blow to win EYEBALLERS' pick of Nuke 13-5, and the decider was finely poised after a 6-6 first half.

Once on their T side of Dust2, however, EYEBALLERS hit the gas pedal. They won the first six rounds of the second half, leading 12-6, and, unlike FOKUS on Inferno, they made their advantage count to win 13-9 and book a spot in playoffs.

EYEBALLERS missed out on a Cologne spot but have enjoyed a decent 2026, that started with a win over FaZe at Bounty and also included a second-place finish at ROG Spring.

This result, though, takes it to a new level, with a minimum prize payout of $62,500, and JW back in a top-tier event playoffs.
