s1n: "We want to give German people a team to cheer for, this is the ultimate goal"

As one of two "hometown" teams alongside BIG, M80 began their Cologne Major campaign on the right foot by securing two dominant wins against Lynn Vision and Sharks.

That means M80 are just one victory away from securing a Stage 2 spot, but Elias "⁠s1n⁠" Stein has set his sights on appearing in front of his home crowd.

"We want to play in front of a crowd. We want to give German people a team to cheer for, this is the ultimate goal," he told HLTV after the win against Sharks.

"That could elevate us also, once we are really there, but we got to take it step by step."

Listen to the interview here.

Congrats, you're 2-0 after the first day, which I guess is a relief after the best-of-one stage. How do you feel after getting the job done today?

Yeah, I feel very good. It's now my fourth Major, and as everybody knows, I think the first day is the most important day. It's the best-of-ones. It can give a direction of how the tournament is going to go, or the stage is going to go. So yeah, just really happy that we managed to 2-0 on the first day.

The game against Sharks looked pretty straightforward, I guess you could say, which isn't always the case with the best-of-ones. Is it just about the team really feeling it today?

The team is feeling it. I think the prep was amazing from the coach, I think we have a simple but a strong idea that we follow as a team. Everybody feels comfortable, individuals chime in. Individuals say what they see in the analysis and see what the other team is doing, so everybody's giving their input, and I think just the whole vibe in the team right now is amazing. People play confidently, and I love to see that as IGL.

People play confidently, and I love to see that as IGL

You said it's your fourth Major, but this one is a bit special for you, I'm sure. How does it feel to play here in your home country?

It feels amazing. I only had the chance to play in Cologne one time, and it was just a Play-in, but even then I could feel the aura of this whole stage, of the venue, of the people around. And even though it was just as a viewer, I could feel the LANXESS Arena. No doubt we want to play in front of German fans. We want to give people someone to cheer for, and it's going to be M80.

I wanted to ask about the whole preparation, because I saw you flew back to the US, which is preparation-wise probably not the best, but at least you got the job done in terms of results. But did that hamper the preparation in a bit? Because results-wise it doesn't seem so, but I guess, long-term it could prove to be like that.

You've got to be honest, so I think it first took a little bit of a toll on us regarding jet lag. So we had to travel from the US to China, then we went from China to the US, and back from the US to Germany now. So really long travel dates in a short period of time. But in the end, we're just blessed that we got the opportunity to play at the Major. And like you said, I think we're going to see with now the best-of-threes coming up.

It's going to test our map pool a bit and see how deep we really are. But yeah, we just can't take anything for granted. Obviously, I don't want to get too ahead of myself, but if we make it to the next stage, we're going to have some more days off, and we can use these days off for preparation, building the map pool, and doing some finishing touches on other maps. But yeah, so far I'm really happy with what we showed, and we just got to keep it up.

To ask about the results that you mentioned while flying around the world. You took MOUZ really close in China and Falcons as well, but you were kind of missing that final touch that you mentioned before, and it feels like this team went through that before, like in Budapest, where you almost made it to Stage 3. Do you think that maybe now with integrating JBa as well, this is what you need to kind of get to the next level?

Yeah, I think ever since we started playing with JBa, it's like a transition period where we got to make him comfortable. Make him understand what we want to see from him, and also like how we play with each other on the server. So it took a bit for him to get used to it. I think the China tournament was a key moment since before that we had some lackluster performances, to be brutally honest.

Playing top teams in MOUZ and Falcons, it just gave us some new belief in ourselves, I would say, even though we couldn't take a win

Playing top teams in MOUZ and Falcons, it just gave us some new belief in ourselves, I would say, even though we couldn't take a win. We also got dominated, for example, on Mirage by MOUZ, but it's just a learning process. Now we're here, and we're playing the biggest tournament of our careers, so we're going to give it all.

About this tournament, right? You guys are not going to play these top names yet, but with Stage 3 also having fans this time around, is that the goal? to play in front of your and slaxz's home crowd?

Yeah, 100 percent. We want to play in front of a crowd. We want to give German people a team to cheer for, this is the ultimate goal. That could elevate us also, once we are really there, but yeah, we got to take it step by step. In the end, it's a Major, and it's just the cherry on top that we're going to play in Germany. It's just unbelievable, and we're just going to have so much motivation to lock in and get it done.
