Wordle 646 5/6

🟨🟨⬜⬜⬜

I decided to do Wordle with my 15-year-old because they love the game, and it was a chance for us to bond a little. Immediately, we argue over our starting word. I always go with SLATE because that’s what the Wordle analysis suggests. They insist on ONSET.

“The thing is, even if you don’t get any letters right, you eliminate five really common letters, including S and T, which are also in your beloved SLATE,” they say.

We play rock-paper-scissors. I throw rock, and they call out “tornado” and swirl their fingers, which apparently beats all. I’ve never heard of that, but I don’t argue because, as the parent of a teenager, I’ve learned to pick my battles.

With ONSET, we get two of the correct letters in the wrong spots and eliminate three really common letters. It’s a good start. We follow up with WRONG, which seems like a strong move.

They are confident we can get this in three.

“I usually get it in three and sometimes four,” they tell me. “But there was a really bad day when I got it in six, and I almost cried.”

Then we sit. And sit. We decide to do a Hail Mary and try a word that doesn’t include any of the letters already on the board. It’s a desperate strategy. We use CHALK.

“That actually did a lot for us,” they say with renewed confidence. We now know the G has to be first if the O is last.

Then we sit. And sit. We are in agony, which we decide to use as another long-shot guess. Then it hits me, clear as day: GUANO. Bat poop!

“I didn’t even know that was a word,’ they say, heading back to their room.

So props to Wordle. It nearly brought us to our knees, but my kid learned a new word.

Today’s word is GUANO. Webster’s New World College Dictionary describes it as the accumulated waste of bats and birds living near the sea, which can be used as fertilizer.
