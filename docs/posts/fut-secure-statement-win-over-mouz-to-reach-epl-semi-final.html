FUT secure statement win over MOUZ to reach EPL semi-final

FUT have secured an upset win over MOUZ in the ESL Pro League S23 quarter-final (Dust2 13-11, Overpass 10-13, Mirage 13-11).

The young squad will now meet Natus Vincere in the semi-final, an organization they used to represent under the NAVI Junior tag alongside Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri.

The result continues a meteoric rise for FUT, who began the year ranked No. 50 in the VRS but have slowly but surely proven they belong in tier-one CS, crowning their rise with a win in an, albeit small, arena in Stockholm.

FUT went for a Dust2 pick in the series, and got off to a solid start thanks to an innovative call that hinged on Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija's Scout wallbang to open up the B site.

MOUZ then started to gather some momentum, as Jimi "⁠Jimpphat⁠" Salo's 1vs2 clutch started a five-round streak that got them an 11-5 lead. FUT didn't let that faze them, though, and won a 3vs5 that started their comeback attempt from 5-11 down.

Nikita "⁠cmtry⁠" Samolotov started to find impact on the AWP after racking up just two kills in the first half, with his rifling support all hitting their shots to allow FUT to win eight in a row and take the map 13-11.

Next up was Overpass, where MOUZ put up a respectable five rounds on their T side thanks to a second-round force-buy victory. Their start to the CT side was then aided by Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman coming to the rescue with a crucial 1vs2 against Deagles, which meant they secured a 13-10 victory and the Mirage decider.

FUT began Mirage by winning the early force-buy wars and never looked back, running away to a 9-3 half before strolling to an 11-3 lead.

Their inexperience reared its ugly head after that, however, but Džiugas "⁠dziugss⁠" Steponavičiuss provided a Heimlich maneuver to stop the choke with a crucial double from palace to deliver the map for his team.
