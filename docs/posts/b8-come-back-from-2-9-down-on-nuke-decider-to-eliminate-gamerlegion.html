B8 come back from 2-9 down on Nuke decider to eliminate GamerLegion

B8 have kept their IEM Cologne Major dreams alive after outlasting GamerLegion in a three-map series, grabbing narrow 13-11 wins on Inferno and Nuke on either side of an 8-13 defeat on Mirage.

It took a comeback on the Nuke decider to get the job done for B8, who were out of sorts after a meager defense and several lost late-round situations that had them staring down a 2-9 deficit. But it seemed like the pressure was off when they swapped to the attack, and instead of crumbling, they looked like a team that had little concern for their impending elimination and salvaged their campaign at the expense of GamerLegion.

"We can talk actually what happened in the whole tournament," Janusz "⁠Snax⁠" Pogorzelski told Tres "⁠stunna⁠" Saranthus after the match when asked about what happened at the end of the series. "From playing in Stage 1 and Stage 2, we didn't show ourselves and how we usually play. You could see it clearly. I don't know, maybe pressure, maybe stress, and we didn't just play like we used to.

"We were memeing a bit in the team that it was kinda like a 'bucket on the head' when we are talking about the information we are giving, we even know what is happening and we are not registering the information that we got. It was a tough tournament, but it was an experience still.

"Even though this Major sucks from us, the journey that we had starting from December to being here — even some people put us as favorites at some point in Stage 1 and Stage 2 to go through and it didn't work. There is still a lot of work to do, but overall... about the Major, I'm not happy, but about the way we played for the entire season, it was not bad."

GamerLegion took an early lead on Inferno after converting a second-round force-buy, and were up 5-2 before B8 finally broke through with three in a row to keep the score close at halftime. A force-buy of their own after the swap off of a triple kill from Artem "⁠kensizor⁠" Kapran turned the tide and allowed B8 to take the lead, but a Oldřich "⁠PR⁠" Nový triple kill turned a 2v4 and had GamerLegion closing in on victory, 11-9.

Hopes of a win came crashing down, though, when B8 won a save round with only a few Deagles in hand, punishing GamerLegion for giving away fights in Apartments. kensizor and Andrii "⁠npl⁠" Kukharskyi made full use of that opportunity with further multi-kills to lock GamerLegion out of any comeback opportunity, clinching victory 13-11 on their map pick.

But the tables turned on Mirage, where PR was near-unstoppable. An early anti-eco ace set the tone in what quickly became a dominant half for GamerLegion, who gave up just two rounds out of the first 10. B8 finally salvaged two before the break and converted the pistol after the swap to bridge the gap, but once GamerLegion had guns in the hand, PR again stepped up to force a Nuke decider.

That dominance carried into Nuke as B8 struggled to find purchase on the defense, barely scrounging round wins between two multis from npl and Alexey "⁠alex666⁠" Yarmoshchuk in a dreary 2-9 start. But the Ukrainian side showed tenacity despite staring elimination in the face, and played like a team that wasn't mere rounds from elimination as they dominated the second half.

They recovered several disadvantages and clutch scenarios during that spree, including back-to-back 1v2s in a must-win pistol round and the ensuing anti-force, and even losing to GamerLegion's half-buy at 11-10 didn't sour the mood as B8 answered right back to clinch match point and swept the round after to close out.
