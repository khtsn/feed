Wordle 690 3/6

🟨⬜🟨🟩🟨 CUTIE

Though my first guess served me well, this was such a hard Wordle! I solved it seated at my desk in the office, and it took me about half an hour of head-scratching, nail-biting anxiety. I got out paper and a pen, and drew arrows around the page until it looked like the meme of the guy mapping out a conspiracy theory on his wall, with papers and photographs fanning out in all directions.

CUTIE gave me all but one letter in the winning word. I tried out some of my options after my first guess, like _ECIT and _ETIC, and went around the board filling in consonants I thought might work until my mind was swimming with unusable guesses. The only actual word I could fathom from this strategy was RECIT.

RECIT told me what I already knew and gave me a bit more information about where letters couldn’t be. So I wrote out five blanks as if I were playing a game of Hangman, and under each one, I wrote the excluded letters. In the first spot, I wrote, “Can’t be C.” In the second-to-last spot, I wrote, “Has to be I.” In the last spot, I wrote, “Can’t be E or T.” My options narrowed.

This Wordle stumped me, and I faced a mental block when it came to considering guesses that started with E, but realizing I could employ the TH letter combination changed everything.

When you solve with pen and paper, what do you find is the most helpful way to visualize your game?

Today’s Wordle gets one star for making me believe I would never find the answer.

Today’s word is ETHIC, a noun. According to Webster’s New World College Dictionary, it refers to “a system of moral standards or values.”
