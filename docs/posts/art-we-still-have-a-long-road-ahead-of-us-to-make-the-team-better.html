arT: "We still have a long road ahead of us to make the team better"

Legacy were stopped just short of reaching the playoffs at PGL Bucharest, and almost bounced back upon arriving in Rio as they were a map up on MOUZ and held a 10-6 lead on Inferno, only to lose seven rounds straight and collapse on the Mirage decider.

It is only the team's fourth event since Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan joined and took over as in-game leader, and the Brazilian captain admitted that, while the progress has been positive, there is still a lot of work ahead for the team to reach its potential.

"We're playing kinda scrambled sometimes and not understanding each other, so this kind of chemistry is not on point," he told HLTV after the match. "It's something which, if we improve fast, we can solve a lot of problems within the team."

Listen to the interview here.

Commiserations on the loss, not the start you would have wanted. What went wrong?

The situations we played on Inferno, it was supposed to be the other way. One retake B and one lucky round by time, those two were winning for the game. We did not manage to close those ones, and they played better in some situations. That's about it.

It's a home event for you, so is there any added pressure with that?

Maybe not, I don't think so. It's more about ourselves, the way we play. We need to be more confident and play better.

After you came into the team, the results have been getting better. You made playoffs at EPL, 2nd at FERJEE, and then almost made playoffs in Bucharest. What has the integration into the team been like for you?

It's been very good. The whole team believes in the process; they believe we can have a very consistent and solid team. Of course it's hard losing, we really wanted to get playoffs in the last tournament and win FERJEE, but that's how CS works. It's also a matter of understanding how you can lose without losing your confidence, your playstyle, and we need to just keep working to grind it out and be back to a strong team.

We need to change a lot of ways of thinking, playing, teamwork, chemistry of the team has to step up.

What has it been like becoming the captain of this team?

Also very good. The guys all trust me and trust the process. Of course it's changed a lot of things in the team, we need to change a lot of ways of thinking, playing, teamwork, chemistry of the team has to step up. So there is a lot of things happening and we are still improving on a lot of those, but it has been a good process.

How have you been evolving as a player and leader since coming in?

I'm still finding my groove in the team, just because I'm playing a lot of positions I did not use to play. We're still trying to find out how we can play on the map because we also have dumau, who's playing very aggressively, so I need to have some chemistry with him. So some maps he does his things, other maps I am doing those things. We still have a long road ahead of us to make the team better.

Do you find it hard to rein yourself in? You were always a really aggressive player for the longest time.

(laughs) Yeah, it has been a little bit hard because I'm used to making the first point of contact, taking more space, and now I need to give the boys more space and also try and support them sometimes. Just playing different situations than I was used to, so that is some part of the process for me as a player as well, and it's all together. I need to improve as an IGL, as a player, and it's something we're working on together.

We cannot lose confidence because of this loss and just feel the pressure; we cannot get in that hole

This is one of a few tournaments you have before the Major, so how do you look at that in terms of how you'd like the team to progress? What do you need to get into better form for the Major?

That's a hard question to answer with quick words, but we have many key points we need to work out. Our decision-making overall, as a team and unit, has to be better. We're playing kinda scrambled sometimes and not understanding each other, so this kind of chemistry is not on point. It's something which, if we improve fast, we solve a lot of problems within the team. So just the communication and chemistry within the team would help us a lot if we improve fast.

Heading into your next match, you have your backs against the wall. It's going to be against the loser of Aurora and HOTU, so how are you going to approach that game?

The approach has to be the same as always. We need to work, talk about the game, and just be ready. We cannot lose confidence because of this loss and just feel the pressure; we cannot get in that hole. We need to think it's just another game that we need to give it all to win. Of course it's an elimination match, but we need to play every game as an elimination match.
