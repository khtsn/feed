Foxy9, who competed for the fifth spot in DRX's on-stage roster against veteran Zest , was benched two games into DRX's Masters Tokyo campaign, and did not play at Champions Los Angeles. He stayed with DRX in 2024, playing a flex role for the squad that year, but was best known for his lockdown ability on the Sentinel role.

At the end of the 2024 season, Foxy9 joined Gen.G as part of a large-scale rebuild that saw the team scramble to replace Masters Shanghai winners Meteor and Lakia . As Gen.G navigated an up-and-down 2025 season, Foxy9 was a mainstay in the squad, helping the team achieve a 5th-6th finish at Masters Toronto. Once the season ended, Gen.G moved Foxy9 to the bench, signing former DRX Academy DRX Academy Korea Rank #20 Kyra Park Jae-young (박재영) apeX Lee Chan-min (이찬민) XoNs Cloy Kim Min-gyu (김민규) Athan Na Ha-jun (나하준) player ZynX to play the secondary Duelist and secondary Controller positions. With Lakia, an Initiator player, assuming IGL duties, a shift of roles saw Karon move into the Sentinel role and Ash into the Controller role at Kickoff, seemingly burying Foxy9 in the depth chart.

Gen.G, after going 1-3 at Kickoff, reintroduced Foxy9 into the starting lineup as a Controller in its first game of Stage 1, but quickly brought back Ash, parting ways with Foxy9 just under three weeks later.
