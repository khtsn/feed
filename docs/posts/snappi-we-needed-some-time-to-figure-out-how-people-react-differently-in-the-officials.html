Snappi: "We needed some time to figure out how people react differently in the officials"

"We went to Roman with only one goal, and it was essentially to win it," Marco "⁠Snappi⁠" Pfeiffer tells HLTV as Ninjas in Pyjamas aim to wrestle their season back under control after their win in Portugal.

The January signing of Artem "⁠cairne⁠" Mushynskyi in place of Michel "⁠ewjerkz⁠" Pinto was intended to increase the team's firepower, but it resulted in numerous role changes that scuppered any chance of a honeymoon.

After early exits at all three tier one events NIP have attended, Snappi is hoping the team have turned a corner after their trophy lift put them back into the Major race.

A good result in Rotterdam, and NIP can also escape the cycle of having to attend so many open LANs, the true goal of the team: "Most players are lying if they say they don't prefer being at the tier-one LANs," Snappi explains.

"Your schedule is just more set and you can prepare better, you can plan better. And for me, I have two kids, so it's nice to know when I'm away and when I'm not."

Snappi, you're here, straight off the Roman Imperial Cup. Things were looking very rough for the team in terms of the Major qualification, but now with a win it's a little bit better. How does it feel to come into this one after winning a tournament?

It feels good. We went to Roman with only one goal, and it was essentially to win it. That is the goal if you attend these tier-two LANs for us. We got one bad VRS loss, but we also kind of have to look at it in a positive way, that we did get away with the trophy and got a lot of important VRS points. We're obviously satisfied with the result we got there.

What has it been like for you to play these tier-two LANs again? I've spoken to some players and most of them have said that they're quite excited to go back to this kind of environment. There's a big upside in playing these tournaments, getting the LAN wins and everything for the Major qualification, but at the same time, if you lose one of those games like you did against 100 Thieves, that can be quite painful.

Yeah, I mean the last Roman where we didn't do well was painful, because we also lost out on some bigger LAN tournaments that aren't the Major. We were down on the next VRS ranking, and that obviously hurt us for the rest of the season. In terms of these LANs, I think it's both exciting but also I think most players are lying if they say they don't prefer being at the tier-one LANs. Your schedule is just more set and you can prepare better, you can plan better. And for me, I have two kids, so it's nice to know when I'm away and when I'm not.

Winning tier-two tournaments these days isn't as easy as people think

And when you're not in the tier-one circuit, it is a little bit more up in the air and things can change quickly in a couple of days about where you are in the world. So I would say that I would obviously prefer that we stabilize sooner rather than later. That is also the goal for me, because the goal is to be a tier-one team. And I mean, we got a new player, we've been with him for two months and there's been some mixed results. There's been some positives and also some negatives, but I think that we are figuring out more and more stuff to make the team work in the manner that we want it to work.

Back in [EPL], you said that you were different teams in practice and officials. Have you been able to identify those issues and try to work on them?

I think it was way better at Roman Cup. But yeah, some of the issues are obviously that in practice, I think people were playing maybe without the pressure. Whereas when, of course, NIP is still a big organization and there's obviously pressure from fans and pressure that the players put on themselves. They really want to show good form, and the pressure to win and get into tier-one is there. I think that kind of halted the performances sometimes because that pressure led to maybe people not playing always to win, but rather playing not to lose. And that is kind of one of the things we've been working on.

Just in general, we needed some time to figure out stuff in terms of how people react differently in the officials, and I think we are getting there more and more. We are figuring out more and more stuff so that we are more and more prepared going into officials, because that's also one of the things with this VRS system. Normally, when you create a new team in the past, you would play a lot of officials to learn.

But nowadays, you can't really do that because it's too scary to expose yourself to the VRS system. Therefore, we kind of just have to do it this way. And as I said, it's been both positive and negative, but I think at Roman Cup, we played a bit better. I know it's a tier-two tournament, but winning tier-two tournaments these days isn't as easy as people think. So yeah, I'm happy [what] we got out of there.

NIP have had mixed results since adding cairne over the winter

Looking at the start of the season and looking only at tier-one tournaments, you went out of BLAST in the first round and then out of EPL and Krakow in Stage 1. How do you look at these results? Has it been harder than you expected to find this consistency and to take that next step?

As I said, one of the things we've also been working with is the pressure. Because if you look at some of the results, it's like we had match points against Liquid at BLAST, and we lost in overtime. We had five match points, I think, against Legacy at EPL, and we lost. And also against G2, I think we were actually the better team in that match, but we lost four pistols and the consecutive rounds, and it was a close match, right?

I think a lot of these matches come down to small details and us being better at handling the pressure. It's some of the things we're working with, and I think it's a process, it doesn't happen overnight. Now, we actually started to win some close games, we started to win some overtimes, and I think these successful experiences are going to rack up eventually and we can push through.

The reality was that there were too many things that we had to kind of fix for the team to just click instantly

You're now going up against PARIVISION, a very tough tactical team. What is your opinion about the matchup? Also considering that you just came straight from a tournament and they've not played in two weeks, they've had more time to prepare for this one.

They're coming into the match with the advantage of being more prepared, but then again, we also come in with the advantage of coming from a successful experience for us, whereas they lost the last time they played. I don't know what is better. PARIVISION is a strong team, we played them a couple of times, and I think at the Major, with some of the pressure, we definitely choked the game against them in the Major Stage 2, when we would qualify for Stage 3.

I think we played them at Stake where we won, so we have experience, but I also think they became a lot better since we played them last time. They won a tournament, and they are a different team now. It's going to be a tough matchup for us, also because their map pool is a little bit annoying for us, so we'll have to do our best. But I see our chances as pretty strong.

Considering where you are in the ranking and that you have this tournament and Stake after this one, what goals have you set for this tournament?

We haven't set any specific goals, but obviously there's the goal to qualify for the Major and that requires a level of results that is going to be at least average or above average, so I guess that is the goal. There are no goals for specific placements in the tournament, the main goal is that we put ourselves in a good situation so that we can get to Cologne.

Do you think you're closer now to finding that consistency, to being able to stabilize around the top 20? Do you think that's more possible now than it was at the start of the season when you got cairne into the team?

Yeah, definitely. The thing was, we were obviously hoping for a honeymoon with cairne, as you always do when you get a new player, but the reality was that there were too many things that we had to kind of fix for the team to just click instantly. There were too many role changes and just things we had to iron out, and that obviously has now put us in a bad situation because we didn't really get any of the good tournament invites. But we can still qualify for the Major, and I think we are in a good position to do so.
