dastan on ZywOo's 1v5: "I hope this will teach my guys to play 100% every moment"

PARIVISION came into BLAST Open Rotterdam in mixed form, having taking second at PGL Cluj-Napoca before bombing out of ESL Pro League in the first stage in their previous two outings. Their campaign thus far has been a success, a statement win over Spirit helping them secure a quarter-final berth.

"I'm happy that we beat Spirit because it's an elite team with donk," Dastan "⁠dastan⁠" Akbayev said of his team's group stage performance. "I'm also not that frustrated after losing to Vitality."

The loss to Vitality did not smart, according to dastan, because the "skill difference" is too much right now. "I said that in Cluj-Napoca it was a best-of-five and I think we had a 20% chance. I hoped this time it would be 40%, just play better than before, that's all."

With a quarter-final matchup with Falcons on the horizon, dastan went in-depth with HLTV on his team's tournament thus far, their struggles in ESL Pro League, and their upcoming playoff campaign.

Watch the full interview here.

You made it to the quarter-finals after beating NIP and Spirit, before losing to Vitality in the group final. Give me your thoughts on those matches.

We came to this tournament after losing a lot of points in EPL. Our goal was to try to go to Stage 3. It is not super needed, but in terms of our situation, we don't want to be the last team who goes to Stage 2.

I'm happy that we beat Spirit because it's an elite team with donk, and I'm also not that frustrated after losing to Vitality because even in that 1v5, I know that my guys were aware that it's ZywOo. Generally, before the game, I said that in Cluj-Napoca it was a best-of-five and I think we had a 20% chance. I hoped this time it would be 40%, just play better than before, that's all.

You've now played two matches against Vitality, three maps in Cluj and now two here. You lost every map but some of those games were very close. What lessons did you learn from those two series?

I've faced ZywOo and apEX for a lot of years, since AVANGAR, and maybe me and Jame when we face them it's a bit easier for us. For young players it's still not enough, it's too much skill difference right now, in terms of experience and skill-wise. Step by step I hope we will play better against them. Also, if we think about the case of MOUZ, they played a lot against Vitality and still didn't beat them. Maybe a few times. I believe one day we will beat them… (laughs) I hope.

I believe one day we will beat them… (laughs) I hope

I wanted to ask about the 1v5 by ZywOo. I spoke to apEX after the match and he said that he felt your guys were a bit scared of facing ZywOo. Was that the case? And after you got together outside to talk about the match, what was said about that round?

When we play against elite players, it was always like s1mple, donk or ZywOo, it's not afraid, it's awareness. I think in all my career in practice I said to my guys, when we lose or are winning some situations but don't play 100%, in this case if it was ZywOo he will clutch. At least it was not an elimination game, and I hope this moment will teach my guys to play 100% every moment. If it was more overpeeks, I think I would be more frustrated, but I know that my guys, even though we lost that round, they wanted to win and were aware that it's ZywOo, that's all.

You made it look very easy against Spirit, a quick 2-0 series. There's a lot of talk about why Spirit isn't working, even though the lineup is very talented. Do you think that they're an easy team to read, and what do you think is their biggest issue right now?

I can't talk about Spirit much because I know them, and they're from the same country as my guys. Previous times, when I was in Virtus.pro, they beat us every time on LAN. I think new guys should help donk much more, and they need to perform better.

About magixx as an in-game leader, he needs time. Being an in-game leader takes a lot of years, even if you watch other leaders of other top-10 teams, a lot of these guys have been playing around 10 years. magixx may have been playing, but in-game leading needs time.

I think donk wants to win titles, that's a big difference in the vision right now. I can't say I think they know better than me, I don't want to say bad things about them, I think they're still a good team. I don't know, donk is performing very well, super high-class player, and the other guys should just play at least I think at a 1.00 rating.

Now that you mention Virtus.pro, I wanted to ask about their new vision for the team, with mir as the IGL and four guys coming straight out of the academy. What is your opinion on that team and mir as an IGL?

mir is a good player, but for me it is also interesting how he changed his vision and how he will develop as a captain. I also can't say anything bad about Virtus.pro, I think it's at least trying something new and I hope they will get the level which they had before.

I don't know the new players. They are too young maybe, but in time they will play better. It's all about which players they have: maybe step by step they will return to tier-one.

This is your first LAN since Cluj, but between those two events you also had EPL. A lot of people were surprised to see you struggle there: was it the move back online, do you think the players were tired from all the travelling?

I think it was tiredness and some… I don't know, they didn't play to win, it was also a new experience for them and I think they didn't understand that we are losing a lot of points after that period. I also sensed that some players needed a break, some players go home for five days and return, but even that didn't help.

Next time maybe I will be more strict and control, add more preparation for these kind of matches because it's online. I feel like in online games people are more hungry and more safe to beat us, it's easier because there's no pressure, easier to prepare between maps, everything. It might cost us more if we don't get to Stage 3, it was a huge reason that we lose those three matches. On Twitter I read an article that if it was on LAN, we would lose less points, but we lost almost 100 points and it was painful.

I feel like in online games people are more hungry and more safe to beat us, it's easier because there's no pressure

You are now going up against Falcons in the quarter-finals. You beat them in the Bounty finals when they had a stand-in and also in Cluj. What are your expectations for this series?

Right now there is an atmosphere that… I think all teams here are now safe in Stage 3. Aurora are playing DraculaN… the atmosphere is pretty peaceful. One week ago I thought this playoffs would be super important because of Stage 3 invites, but now there is no pressure. I think Falcons are playing better, if I see the player cams it was pretty comfortable, not raging, everyone feeling a good atmosphere.

I think this break helped us more than them because they were in good form, they came back against FURIA. I don't know, at least we beat them two times, I hope it will help us in confidence. Map pool is pretty similar I think, so I don't know. Falcons are a good team, they are always favourites… if we lose, nothing bad will happen because it's Falcons, so we will try to use it when we play. No pressure, that's what separates us.

When we play on LAN, some maps we don't play a lot, and when we compete with elite teams like Vitality, they have huge natural experience on maps

I saw your recent blog, that you split the season into three different cycles. After this you have Bucharest and then Astana: what goals have you set for this cycle?

I think we need to finish this tournament and we will think about that after Bucharest, because there is a small amount of time between this tournament and Bucharest. I think we need to be prepared for the Major, we need to go to the playoffs, play better and improve maps. Because when we play on LAN, some maps we don't play a lot, and when we compete with elite teams like Vitality, they have huge natural experience on maps. We need to improve maps.

The goal is to be in the invite zone, and try to be top 10 in general over the year. If we will be top five, it would be nice, but there is no goal to win tournaments, just hoping my players will be better tournament after tournament. They are very young, I don't know the last time when you see new players come to Counter-Strike and they are in the top 10. I hope they will develop more. This schedule of professional players is new for them, travelling and everything is new, and I hope they will be elite players. We need some superstar, I hope some of them like xielo or nota will play better, because we need some superstar player to close games easier.
