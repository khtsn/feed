ESIC issues disciplinary sanctions in SENZA case

The Esports Integrity Commission (ESIC) has announced that it has finalized its investigation into the former SENZA/-72c squad and "associated entities" connected to it in relation to matches played in CCT Season 3 Europe Series 3 in June 2025.

The team's rostered players, coaches, and staff were provisionally suspended in October 2025 while ESIC investigated suspected breaches of its code of conduct, "including possible use of outside information, betting misconduct, and account irregularities."

ESIC issues interim suspension against SENZA

Kirsan "⁠byek⁠" Ivanov has been slapped with a two-year ban after ESIC's investigation revealed that the player engaged in account-sharing and identity-related cheating misconduct.

"ESIC found that the account used under the identity "byek" was not consistently controlled by a single individual, with evidence including unexplained performance and connectivity irregularities indicative of possible mid-series account hand-offs," ESIC wrote.

"This indicates that another individual may have played using the account during matches, misrepresenting the identity of the competing player. This finding was corroborated by an express admission provided in writing by the team ownership."

In addition, the esports watchdog identified "integrity concerns" relating to -72c's matches against HEROIC Academy and CPH Wolves.

ESIC issued formal requests for information, but "the majority of the participants failed to respond to these requests or provide the required materials, other than limited information relating to the participant byek."

As a result of their failure to cooperate, the following players have been suspended for one year:

  • Petr "⁠timeagento⁠" Markheev

  • Spiridon "⁠spira⁠" Popov

  • Vladislav "⁠melanta⁠" Lazarev

  • Stepan "⁠borosto⁠" Antonov

  • Aleksandr "⁠1nhuman⁠" Reshetnikov

The suspension is a cumulative punishment for timeagento, who was last week banned for five years in a separate case about the use of cheating software and/or match-manipulation activities. His combined sanction period began on March 20, 2025, and runs until March 2031.

The SENZA organization has also been fined $20,000 for its failure to cooperate with the investigation. According to ESIC, the "organisation did not provide proper responses to formal interrogatories, failed to produce requested materials, and did not preserve or supply relevant evidence, including information relating to roster identity, devices, communications, and its own stated internal investigation – which it failed to produce at any time."

The money must be paid in full via a donation to a charity nominated by SENZA and approved by ESIC. Until the proof of payment is provided and accepted, the organization will not be able to compete in ESIC member events.
