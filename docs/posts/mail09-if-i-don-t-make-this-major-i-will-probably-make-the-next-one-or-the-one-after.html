MaiL09: "If I don't make this Major I will probably make the next one, or the one after"

MaiL09 and upE talking after the match

Liam "⁠MaiL09⁠" Tügel, the 16-year-old rifler picked six times as Bold Prediction at the start of this year, #33 on our latest Prospect ranking, and highest-ranked FACEIT player in the world last season, spoke to HLTV at the ROG JOURNEY Spring LAN in Stockholm shortly after his side Alliance beat GamerLegion to keep their dream of reaching the Major alive.

The youngster spoke about the move from Metizport, his daily routine, looking up to Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač and Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov, and the goals for this year.

Congrats on the win, you just beat GamerLegion at ROG Journey. Let's start with that, how does it feel?

It feels amazing. We had a rough start at the start of the tournament. We managed to win, barely, the first two BO3s and then we lost to EYEBALLERS. It's a good feeling to bounce back strong.

For the last month or so, Alliance is in the race for the Major, there is a chance you make it. The EYEBALLERS loss was maybe not the best thing for that, but this win puts you back in that direction. Tell me about how it has been? The chance to go to the Major, how has it influenced you and the team?

Playing a Major has always been my main goal when it comes to professional Counter-Strike, but I don't stress about it because I know if I am going to be a professional player, I have at least 5 or 10 more years in my career. If I don't make this Major I will probably make the next one, or the one after. So we are just playing every match and we'll see what happens.

It's good to know that big organizations and players are looking at me playing

You joined Alliance recently, you seem to have about 6 months in each team, first Johnny Speeds, then Metizport, now you are Alliance. How did it come about that you made the change of the teams?

Johnny Speeds was my first professional team, they took really good care of me. It wasn't like I didn't want to play in Johnny Speeds, but a better offer came. Metizport just played a Major, they were a really strong team so I joined them. But then things didn't click, they wanted to make player changes, and I found this amazing opportunity to go on a loan to Alliance.

And it has been going well so far. Starting this year, the Bold Predictions, I have to ask about that. You ended up being picked six times, by some of the best players in the world. How does that feel, what was that experience like?

It feels good, I don't really think about it too much because I still need to perform on the server. But it is of course nice to know that good players know about you and keep track of you, then hopefully the team does as well. So it's good to know that big organizations and players are looking at me playing.

I like how m0NESY acts, outside the game, inside the game, his mindset. I'm trying to be as professional as him

What about you, are there any players you look up to, players that you like to watch demos of, model your game from?

Honestly I like to watch NiKo and m0NESY, of course. I like NiKo's playstyle and I always have, since CS:GO, and I like how m0NESY acts, outside the game, inside the game, his mindset. I'm trying to be as professional as him.

Moving from that and to you as a player, what is your routine like these days? Obviously still developing, playing a lot of FACEIT, been #1 for last season if I remember correctly. What does your daily routine look like?

I dropped out of school so I don't have that anymore. Maybe I wake up around 9, we start to practice at 10, then I practice the whole day till like 18. Then I eat something and just play FACEIT. That's it.

Talking about Alliance and the team, how have the teammates been? Whats different compared to previous lineups you've been on?

The teamwork in this team is amazing. We don't win our games with strategy, we win by teamwork and everyone working together. We have really experienced players, we have some that are not so experienced, so I think it is a good mix and everyone can learn something from someone.

You said you are taking it game by game, but do you have any goals or anything you want to achieve in this season or this year?

Of course, we have the goal to go to the Major, but we are not be disappointed if we don't make it. But the biggest goal is to go to Esports World Cup, I don't remember when it is, but that is the biggest goal.
