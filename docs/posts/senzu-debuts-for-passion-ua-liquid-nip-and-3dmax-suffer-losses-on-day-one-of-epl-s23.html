Liquid in trouble as Passion UA begin their global offensive

The loss to Passion UA, who made their debut with Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold in the lineup, adds to a particularly concerning run of results for Liquid. Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek's men were ranked #22 on the VRS coming into the event, but have now dropped two spots and currently sit well outside the list of invites to the IEM Cologne Major on our prediction page. MischiefCS had them at a 29% chance of making the Major before the start of EPL.

Liquid began the year by qualifying for the BLAST Bounty Finals, but were eliminated in the first round by Falcons with Matej "⁠NucleonZ⁠" Trajkoski as a stand-in. The Dutch organization then suffered a setback at IEM Krakow after Keith "⁠NAF⁠" Markovic missed the event due to "urgent family matters," and were eliminated in Stage 1 after a win over Ninjas in Pyjamas and losses to FUT and G2.

Passion UA will be happy with their overall showing in their opener, closing out Dust2 comfortably (13-8) before needing double overtime to convert Nuke after dropping an 8-4 lead. Insult to injury was added in the final round with Santino "⁠try⁠" Rigal and Vladyslav "⁠Kvem⁠" Korol recovering a 3v5 (into a 2v4) to clinch victory, sending Liquid to the 0-1 pool.

"The teamplay is better than I expected it to be," Janko "⁠YNk⁠" Paunović remarked of Passion UA during the second map, impressed by the way the team has already come together with five different nationalities, lack of much practice, and Senzu's limited English.

Kvem did the heavy lifting at the start of the series before Senzu took over on Nuke, mustering a 34-21 K-D, 10 multi-kills, a 1.43 rating, and looking more comfortable than expected in his international debut. try also didn't miss a beat, finding impact across both maps of the series to finish with the highest rating.

NIP's woes continue

Ninjas in Pyjamas are in a similar boat to Liquid and went from #27 to #31 after losing to a Legacy playing with Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan as their new in-game leader for the first time. Marco "⁠Snappi⁠" Pfeiffer's side were eliminated from Roman Imperium Cup V following losses to Monte and 100 Thieves, beating the latter in a best-of-one but faltering in the rematch in a tournament result that ultimately cost them five spots on the VRS.

Ninjas in Pyjamas pushed Legacy the distance in the series, trading narrow wins on Ancient and Nuke, but a complete collapse on the offense of Overpass (1-11) proved too much to overcome in the second half. They, too, sit well outside the predicted invite list for Cologne, although they began EPL with a much higher chance of making the Major than Liquid according to Mischief's prediction (39.8%).

3DMAX's debut with misutaaa spoiled

3DMAX came into ESL Pro League after overhauling its coaching staff and swapping Alexandre "⁠bodyy⁠" Pianaro for Kévin "⁠misutaaa⁠" Rabier on the heels of a rough start to the year, which included a last-place exit from PGL Cluj-Napoca where the French side went 0-6 in maps.
