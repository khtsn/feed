NiKo on seeking revenge against PARIVISION: "We made them look better than they are"

We caught up with Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač before the BLAST Open Rotterdam playoffs, where he and his Falcons are slated to take on PARIVISION in the first round of the six-team bracket.

The Bosnian rifler covered a range of topics, from Falcons' group stage showing to the team's mental resilience, Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin's evolution since joining the team, and his own individual performances since the beginning of the year.

"It's hard for me to produce the level that I did before with Maksim [kyousuke] now on the team, which I'm also fine with," NiKo tells HLTV when asked about his 1.14 rating on LAN since the beginning of the year, "but if I continue to play like this, I'll definitely be satisfied with my level."

You made it to the quarter-finals, beating NRG, TYLOO, and FURIA, and you lost to NAVI. Give me your overall impression of how the team played in the group stage.

It's been a bumpy group stage for us. Especially having NucleonZ for the first game and then Maksim [kyousuke] coming right before the game against NAVI, and then we won the next two games. Losing a map to TYLOO and then a very close match against FURIA.

It's kind of hard to describe how it all went because under the circumstances we were in, I'm kind of happy that we managed to get to the playoffs, but I'd say the win against FURIA is probably the most important one of the group stage, and I think we could see the progress of the things we have been discussing and the things we wanted to improve after Cluj in that game. Overall, a positive group stage for us, I would say.

When I spoke with TeSeS during the group stage, he praised the team's mental resilience, and I guess that was on display in the series against FURIA. There was a tech pause that kind of broke your flow, and then you were down 9-3 and managed to come back. Do you agree that the team is mentally stronger now?

Definitely, we're trying to put emphasis on it, and we want to get better in every aspect that we can, and the mental one was definitely something we struggled with in the previous season, so we want to focus on it right now.

But, as you said, we showed some very good resilience against FURIA. Not only on Dust2 but also coming back on Mirage, where we were down 11-7 and won it 11-13. So yeah, the mental resilience was definitely on point for this event, and hopefully we can keep it up for the playoffs now, as well.

We haven't been losing rounds we shouldn't, or maybe not that many, in the group stage, so I would say that the progress is there and that's the most important

TeSeS also told me that with the exception of Inferno against NAVI, the team was playing more the way zonic wants you to. More as a unit, more compact. Do you see that as well, that the team is playing more as a unit?

As I said, I've definitely seen improvements at this event, but again, the first two days were very uncomfortable for us, having NucleonZ in the first game and then Maksim coming right after arriving and landing for the game against NAVI. I would say the team was not in the right place, even though I'd say we played well against NAVI.

NAVI is probably a stronger team than us right now, they're in very good shape, and their confidence is also very high from what I can see in the interviews, so we definitely knew it was going to be a hard game and I think that now, if you want to beat NAVI, you have to be on your A game and I feel like it was hard for us to produce that with the things that have been going on.

I do believe we play better as a team, and the structure is becoming better. We haven't been losing rounds we shouldn't, or maybe not that many, in the group stage, so I would say that the progress is there and that's the most important.

I wanted to get your thoughts on kyousuke because it still feels like it's a bit inconsistent when it comes to those high-pressure games like the one you played against PARIVISION in the Cluj playoffs. Is this something normal for a player who is this young, and how do you look at the way he's been playing?

It's definitely normal. What people probably think is that what donk has done, now they expect Maksim to do, and that's kind of unrealistic, even though everyone would have loved for Maksim to play the way donk does, but sometimes it can't be that easy.

We're also not the same team as Spirit is and Maksim probably doesn't have as much space as donk has, so maybe it's harder for him to perform, but I also think you can see a bit of inexperience on his side. He's punishing himself a little bit, instead of being happy with one or two kills, he's going to go for the next one, and he's going to die, so that definitely plays on a confidence level. These are the things we're focusing on right now for him.

I don't feel like [kyousuke] is struggling under the pressure; it's just that I would say his confidence right now is not where it should be

He needs to grow as a player, we want him to develop as fast as possible and I think now we're seeing a bit more of him rather than at the start of the season because when he came in we wanted to give him space, we wanted to see what we had, and now we're seeing a bit more of these mistakes and we're focusing on it a bit more.

It's hard to be very harsh on such a rookie player when he just joined the team. You need to give him the space he needs to feel a bit more comfortable, and then you slowly develop him. This is how it went the first six months, and now we're trying to develop him a bit more, trying to teach him some things and stuff like this, and he's very open to it.

Maybe right now it's taking a toll on his level, but I think it will probably benefit him in the future once he gets comfortable in these moments. There's really no worry about Maksim. I don't feel like he's struggling under the pressure; it's just that I would say his confidence right now is not where it should be.

Personally, I don't even see him struggling individually. I think it's just about him, maybe playing stronger opponents, and people figuring out how he likes to play and stuff like this. I think it was like donk when he had a period when people figured out how he likes to play, then it was a bit easier to punish him, so I think he's going to overcome it very fast, and we're here to help him.

You were obviously very disappointed with your place in the Top 20 Players of 2025. This year, you're averaging a 1.14 rating on LAN and already have one MVP. Are you happy with the way you've been playing this year?

Yeah, if I could keep this constantly throughout the year, I would be kind of okay with it, with the roles that I have right now. I know that it's hard for me to produce the level that I did before with Maksim now on the team, which I'm also fine with.

The only thing that I'm struggling with is that it's just kind of hard to get into the game for some reason, and this is one of the things I'm trying to focus on. But, as I said, if I continue playing the way I did — the start of this event has maybe been a bit worse —, but if I continue to play like this, I'll definitely be satisfied with my level.

You're now going up against PARIVISION in the quarter-finals. You've played them twice this year and lost both series, even though the first one was with NucleonZ. What are your expectations for this one?

I expect us to win versus them. I don't know how we're losing to them. I think we made them look better than they are in these games, and that's very frustrating, so I'm confident going up against them. They're kind of an established team right now. They've been very consistent since the start of the year, but we want to take revenge.
