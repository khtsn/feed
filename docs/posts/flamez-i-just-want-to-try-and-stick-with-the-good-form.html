flameZ: "I just want to try and stick with the good form"

Vitality topped their BLAST Open Rotterdam group without skipping a beat, defeating 9z, The MongolZ and PARIVISION by 2-0 scorelines.

In the lead-up to the playoffs, much of the conversation about Vitality has been centered on Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan, who has been in the form of his life this season with a 1.29 rating, second only to Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut's 1.45. After back-to-back HLTV Top-20 appearances at No.7, the Israeli appears to be on course for his highest-ever placement in the ranking.

"Obviously it could be if I keep going, it could be a really good year, but the goal is to make seven again and go full jackpot, 777," flameZ told HLTV on media day before the playoffs.

"If I get something lower than that, then sure, but I don't want to go above, I just want to try and stick with the good form. The top 20 is nice and all, but the trophies speak for themselves, so for me, as long as we keep winning I don't care if I go down in level and other players step up. I don't mind."

flameZ also gave his thoughts on the mentally taxing series against PARIVISION, ZywOo's jaw-dropping 1v5 clutch, and his expectations for the semi-final clash against the winner of The MongolZ vs Aurora.

First of all, I have to ask, what are they feeding you? Because you had a 1.56 rating in the group stage, it's unreal.

I don't know… obviously it's been really good. I feel good, my sleep and everything is just going pretty well, but also the team is in good form. Everybody is doing good. It helps a lot when you know there are so many opportunities because a teammate takes a 1v5, a teammate takes a 1v2 spray transfer, this kind of shit happening. Then you get more opportunities, more buy rounds, this kind of stuff. It's been going good and I don't want to jinx it (laughs).

Your impact on the team this year has been fantastic, you are averaging a 1.29 rating and already have two EVPs. After two years at No. 7 in our top 20, do you think that this can be your best year individually?

Yeah, obviously it could be if I keep going, it could be a really good year, but the goal is to make seven again and go full jackpot, 777. If I get something lower than that, then sure, but I don't want to go above, I just want to try and stick with the good form. The top 20 is nice and all, but the trophies speak for themselves, so for me, as long as we keep winning I don't care if I go down in level and other players step up. I don't mind.

I spoke to apEX after the match against PARIVISION and he was in a bit of a bad mood, with a bit of a headache after that series. Was it mentally draining for you as well?

Yeah, this game, man… They play so slow; it's so fucking boring to play the game like that. They are playing good Counter-Strike, and I guess it's beautiful to watch if you love the game and watch for the game, but my ADHD, man, I cannot sustain that kind of slow speed.

I think that this specific game was a bit tough with how the schedule went, we were not ready for the delays that happened to some extent. That was a bit tough on us because we came into the game and it was a bit… we didn't expect that to go that bad for us. It wasn't like shit, but we just came not as ready.

On the other hand, how they played, if you are not really energized and on top of your communication... I think it's the case that sometimes on that Mirage or on that Inferno, there wasn't a bit of information that was needed to get, there were not decisive rotations. Those are due to the energy and communication outside of the specifics of the game, and how awake the individuals are and shooting. On the other hand, that's how they play and we need to deal with that, and I hope next time we don't need the ZywOo crazy clutch to have a good score against them.

I think they need to find themselves sort of again, and I think they will be back to the top quickly

Looking at the current landscape, do you think that maybe PARIVISION are the toughest opponent out there at the moment?

I think they are pretty good. Spirit, we haven't played them with the new roster, I believe. I expected FURIA to be a bit more competitive since Krakow, they haven't been in that many playoffs but are still a team that I think is good. I think they need to find themselves sort of again, and I think they will be back to the top quickly.

The one team we haven't played since Hong Kong is Falcons, or we played in Bounty but with NucleonZ, and I'm curious to play against them. I feel like they are pretty good, but they also don't perform as well against some of the teams that are not reaching playoffs, and then you don't face them in a lot of tournaments. I think so far this year they give us tough games, and they are a big challenge.

Walk me through that fantastic 1v5 from ZywOo, what did you think as he was getting kill after kill? After the match apEX told me they were maybe a bit scared of facing ZywOo in that situation, did you feel the same?

I think they would play similar against most other players, I think they have really big discipline. I felt like it was a 1v3 to some extent because Jame died as a trade, ZywOo killed him pretty quick, and then zweih maybe could have played it slightly better, if he goes back in Pit and doesn't peek him. On the other hand, they did their job on A, and okay he dies. I think in the 1v3 they maybe tried a bit fast… they didn't try to play it a bit fast, but a lot of people criticised them because you can always throw the smoke, this kind of stuff.

It's not easy to play a close game like that, obviously there are some mistakes that are going to happen, that's just the game. I don't know if it's necessarily that they were scared of ZywOo, but I do think that some clutches that he plays, there is a bit of fear to peek him. That also makes clutches way harder for him sometimes, because in the past people gave him those peeks, and he won 1v3s and 1v4s every week, but now people are trying to be really good specifically, they say 'This is ZywOo, careful, guys.' Sometimes that helps him. In this clutch, the way he moves in the peeks, he isolates the duels so well, and you've got to give it to him that he also played it really well.

You're now going up against Aurora or The MongolZ. You've already faced The MongolZ three times this year, beating them every time including in this tournament, and Aurora twice. Any preferences for this match?

I don't care. I think with Aurora we have the map pool going on there with us, and them with the Ancient, it's always going to be there in the map pool. I think with The MongolZ, man, I feel like last year and this year we played against them non-stop, and that's just life. If we keep playing them I wouldn't mind, I wouldn't care, and I think they are still good. It's really impressive to see this roster, that no matter who they get, they are still capable of playing good, and I really like the cobra guy. I don't mind playing against them, but also… if you told me it's The MongolZ or a team we hadn't played in a while, I would take the other team, but we've just played both of them, so I don't really care.
