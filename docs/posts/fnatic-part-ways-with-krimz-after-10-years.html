fnatic part ways with KRIMZ after 10 years

fnatic have announced the benching of Freddy "⁠KRIMZ⁠" Johansson from the team, signifying the end of the Swede's decade-long tenure with the organization.

The move follows the team's significant downfall in 2026, which has seen it drop from a borderline top-30 side at the beginning of the year to 80th on the Valve Regional Standings as of June 5, and resulted in their failure to qualify for the ongoing IEM Cologne Major.

That happened all the while the organization slowly transitioned towards a Ukrainian roster, first bringing in Nikita "⁠jackasmo⁠" Skyba at the end of 2025 to join his former Passion UA teammates Rodion "⁠fEAR⁠" Smyk and Dmytro "⁠jambo⁠" Semera, and Viktor "⁠Br4tkO⁠" Kondratets in March.

While the organization did not comment on the reasons behind KRIMZ's removal or his replacement, it is not difficult to imagine that it was made to complete that transition.

Though the Swede is currently an inactive player still contracted to fnatic, his removal from the team signifies the end of his time with the organization, which was the longest-running active tenure of a player under a single team and the second of all time at 3,511 days (just 28 behind Håvard "⁠rain⁠" Nygaard's with FaZe).

KRIMZ won 22 trophies with fnatic, including back-to-back Majors in 2015

Combined with his previous stint with the organization from 2014-2016, which was only briefly interrupted by his move to GODSENT and back and two months later, it amounts to the longest tenure ever at 4,288 days in total.

During that time, KRIMZ won 22 notable events with the organization from 2014-2020, including back-to-back Majors at ESL One Katowice and Cologne in 2015, and became the Most Valuable Player at five of them.

fnatic couldn't replicate the world-beating form in more recent years, transitioning away from a Swedish roster in 2021 to make KRIMZ the only remaining member of the Major-winning squad, fluctuating between the top 10 to top 30 through several rebuilds.

"A massive thank you to Fnatic for believing in me, supporting my growth, and making my childhood dream a reality," KRIMZ said in a statement on X.

"Going from watching my idols as a kid to actually sharing the jersey with them is more than I ever could have imagined. For the past 12 years, you’ve been my home, my family, and my life. It has been an absolute honor and privilege to represent the black and orange for such a long time."
