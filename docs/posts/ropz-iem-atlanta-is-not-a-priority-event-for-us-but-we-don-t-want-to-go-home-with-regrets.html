ropz: "IEM Atlanta is not a priority event for us, but we don't want to go home with regrets"

On the hunt for their sixth trophy in a row, Vitality entered IEM Atlanta as the heavy favorites, even despite the fact that the team spent a week off after their last victory at BLAST Rivals 2026 Season 1.

Vitality took some time to find their footing in Atlanta, besting BC.Game before getting stunned by BetBoom in their only loss to a team other than Falcons this year. Vitality looked much sharper in the lower bracket, eliminating FaZe and B8 to lay claim to the final playoff spot up for grabs in Group A.

After his team clinched their place in the quarter-finals, Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool stepped aside with HLTV to discuss Vitality's rustiness in the first few days of IEM Atlanta, whether complacency could be an issue for the team, and their plans as they prepare for the IEM Cologne Major.

Or you can listen to the interview here.

2-0 win over B8, very comfortable, and now you're headed to the IEM Atlanta playoffs. How are you feeling?

I feel relieved. There was a bit of a mess, coming into the lower bracket, a disappointing loss against BetBoom. Not much else. Obviously, we didn't practice, and everyone knows that by now (laughs). But still, we want to make the playoffs, and we want to go deep, no matter what, even if we don't prepare.

That's what the idea has been; once we're here at the tournament, we take every moment, every second we can to get better and better, because we're maybe a bit rusty, but we can use this tournament, the deeper we go, the better we get. In that sense, it's good for us now that we're in the playoffs, because I think we can find our form again.

As far as those first two games went, would you chalk that up strictly to because you guys didn't practice? Were the other factors?

Not practicing is always gonna be an issue against any team, because I think you're kinda just getting a little bit out of shape, here and there, whether it's with your aim, whether it's positioning, the ideas, people not taking initiative, or just purely communication. We don't say a key word here and there, and that might cause us to lose a round. That kind of communication might have lost us the game as well, because I don't think we were that good in that part.

The other part is also the energy; obviously it's the third week now in the U.S. for us, and we're gonna be on the lower side of energy. We just try to find the last bit of that, what we can, and I think other than that, we're feeling comfortable.

Aside from maybe the first map against FaZe, today you guys looked like the Vitality we've seen all year. What was the big difference today?

Today was tough in the beginning, with Nuke against FaZe. Other than that, apEX finding his calling and calling well on a lot of T-rounds, on Nuke T-side as well everyone knows that we've been struggling, but on other maps we're feeling really good. Just the team coming together again, and finding the form, because when you don't play for a week almost, you get rusty in many many aspects, and it's just about finding those things again. People playing well again, flameZ had a good game here against B8, I believe ZywOo had a good game against FaZe. People popping off here and there is also going to help.

We want to make the most out of the situation that we're in, that we are the best team in the world, we might be the best team in history

Are you worried that you guys might be getting a little complacent? Nonstop winning, taking some time off, getting into your head a bit?

Complacency is definitely not an issue for us. One of the top things that we prioritize is that we don't to waste our time. We want to make the most out of the situation that we're in, that we are the best team in the world, we might be the best team in history, but that's up for debate as always. In any case, we're in this position and we can make the most out of it, and we're still in it. As long as we're a team, I think we're gonna keep going.

I don't think we'll get complacent, because we have a great system in the team, with the staff supporting us, we talk about these things constantly, and we pay attention to things which might cause complacency or something else. Again, here we're in IEM Atlanta, which is not a priority event for us, but still we don't want to go home with regrets. We do everything we can to be satisfied with our game.

You're up against the loser of NAVI vs. Legacy for your quarter-final match. Do you have a preference?

(laughs) No, not really. I think either one of those is fine. At this point, NAVI, they want revenge. I think Legacy would be cool. We lost the best-of-one against them in the Austin Major, so that would be cool, but I'm down to play both.

You have a great deal of time between the end of this event and the Major. How do you guys plan on spending it? I assume not another Yosemite trip?

Not another Yosemite trip, no. But we'll have a few days off, or even multiple, up to a week, after this tournament, because we're in the last stage of the Major and that means we have some extra time compared to some other teams. We'll rest a bit after this tournament, we'll have a bootcamp, somewhere, I'm not sure of yet, but we'll have a bootcamp. The usual, what teams do, just find a day to start going at it 100%, and make sure we're fully ready for the Major. That's the number one goal for now. That's what the preparation looks like.
