Leviatán coach Itopata concedes to issues against Trace in press conference

Note: This article is a text summary of the Leviatan vs. Trace Esports post-match press conference and will be updated shortly with a full summary. The full video press conference can be found here.

While Split and Bind were quite competitive, Ascent ended up being pretty one-sided. What would you say went wrong or caught your team off guard in the final map?

C0M: "I don't even know. I would have to look back at the VOD, honestly. It feels like a lot of things went wrong. Overall, today we didn't play fully as a team, and they played pretty well as a team. You can tell they're pretty comfortable on the map. I just have to look back at the VOD and see what happened."

On the third map, Ascent, you guys have been running with Deadlock and had been undefeated until today. Did you notice anything wrong apart from not executing the game plan? Do you feel like they anti-stratted you pretty well on defense, or was something wrong with the team's execution?"

Itopata: "I think we didn't execute the composition properly, but they also did some good anti-stratting on us. They were keeping control of certain areas of the map that we usually never get contested on, and I think that threw us off a little bit."

For the next match, it'll be against either Talon once more or Vitality. Which one would you rather face, something new or a team you've already been against?

Mazino: "It doesn't matter who I have to face. The important thing is to win, and I personally don't care who we have to play."
