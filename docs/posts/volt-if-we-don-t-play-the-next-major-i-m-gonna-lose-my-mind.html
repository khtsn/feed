volt: "If we don't play the next Major, I'm gonna lose my mind"

FOKUS' squad may have only been brought together less than two months ago, but already they have put themselves on the map, displaying excellent form in tier-two events, winning Urban Riga Open S3 and flying up the VRS.

The international conglomerate continued their rise by taking down 3DMAX in the opening match of their PGL Bucharest campaign. "We were pretty confident going into the game," Sebastian "⁠volt⁠" Maloș told HLTV after his team's win. "Our prep was pretty good, and also I don't think they are in the best form right now."

Whilst FOKUS came onto the scene too late to realistically secure a spot at IEM Cologne Major, volt is confident the team will earn their berth for the second Major of the year. "For me at least, I expect us to play in the next Major... with how we've started, I think we're almost top 30 now, if we don't play the next Major I'm gonna lose my mind."

In his chat with HLTV volt also touched on his thoughts on the VRS system, the potential of his team's best players being poached, and how the FOKUS project was brought together.

What are you first reactions after that match?

We were pretty confident going into the game, we were already 1-0 up against them from Riga. Our prep was pretty good, and also I don't think they are in the best form right now with what happened with their Major run. Overall I'm just happy, things went as planned.

I wanted to talk about your team in general, because you came together not that long ago but have already enjoyed quite a lot of success. What's the journey been like so far?

It feels great, it's my first big opportunity again since GamerLegion times, and to be honest I feel like this whole team has the GamerLegion vibe to it as well. I see the same kind of players and vibes as the team that made the Shanghai run, and I really feel like it's happening again to be honest. The personalities that we have and the overall game style I think is really good and I feel super motivated, way more than ever. It's just really good for me to show myself again.

How did this project come together? Because it's quite a lot of different pieces from different countries all over the place.

It started with kuben, he wanted to make a new fully international roster and he was looking to pick up different pieces. I think he wanted to make a mix of inexperienced and more experienced players, I guess I fall in the boomer section now being a bit older. We talked a bit, he reached out and after some interviews I guess he saw something good in me. I don't know about the rest of the guys, but it was pretty simple for me.

To be honest I feel like this whole team has the GamerLegion vibe to it as well. I see the same kind of players and vibes as the team that made the Shanghai run

What are the goals for this lineup long term?

I'll say the next Major for sure. For me at least, I expect us to play in the next Major. There was some crazy alternative timeline where we played this Cologne one, but we needed too many wins for that. I think we put a bit too much pressure, especially during the Parken LAN. Now, with how we've started, I think we're almost top 30 now, if we don't play the next Major I'm gonna lose my mind.

Speaking of qualifying for the Major, like you saidm it was probably too much of a stretch to get to this one. With this whole VRS madness, how do you feel about that in general? It's a bit crazy for the teams just outside the tier-one circuit, with a lot going on.

I don't like it, first of all. I think it's ridiculous that we're seeing 20 different LANs pop up in the last two weeks, that weren't even announced, and it's just so hard to follow. You're seeing FaZe for example going from LAN to LAN, they're experiencing the tier-two environment, and I don't like how pay to win it is. I feel like for teams with lower budgets, how do you even get to the Major at that point? You can't get enough points online, and if you want to play on LAN, it's too expensive.

I would say a better solution would be to invite the top eight or whatever, and do some RMR-style event again for the lower teams, but right now it's way too pay-to-win. Who likes to watch from home 20 different LANs and have these people on Twitter doing all these spreadsheets? It's hard to follow and it's horrible for teams to manage to even calculate.

I would say a better solution would be to invite the top eight or whatever, and do some RMR-style event again for the lower teams

So you're not looking forward to being involved in it next time around?

I mean, I would like to be one of the teams that has like a 99% chance, not like 50%. I think SINNERS are probably still watching games now and hoping teams are losing. I don't want to be a team that's praying for other teams to lose just to make the Major. I think it's really ridiculous. It's a long time until the next Major, we haven't even played the Cologne one yet, so we will just see how it goes.

You're at a tier-one event now, and have been playing some of these tier-two LANs like Riga. What's the difference like between those two types of events?

First of all the PCs are a bit on the rough side, monitors and PCs could be better for sure, but you can't really expect really high budget environments. I think this is what the tier-one teams are struggling adapting to, it's just a bit different. You don't have separate booths 20 meters away, everyone is almost face-to-face sometimes, you can hear like three teams shouting at once, and it feels like BYOC almost.

Playing this one, how it was back in the days, feels nice. Everything feels more smooth, more controlled. If this was Riga now we would probably be playing two more BO3s, but now we can go back and prep for the next game. It's just…

Now you're living in luxury?

Yeah, no chaos and I can breathe.

Like I say, you have put yourselves on the map very quickly. For a team like yours, when does it get concerning that you're doing well and individuals are doing well and attracting attention? When do you get worried that bigger teams might start sniffing around?

Uhh… contract jail (laughs)? I don't know, I'm worried about it as well but it is what it is. We are just one of those teams that unfortunately cannot lock players, hopefully everybody will just believe in the project enough to stay. If we keep doing well, nobody has to leave, everything is nice and everyone is happy.
