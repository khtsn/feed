Grim: "I think the future is going to be brighter when we can get more time"

NRG are through to Group A's second lower bracket round following a 2-0 win against B8 in a one-sided series in which they were ahead from the initial exchanges.

It was NRG's first victory since Michael "⁠Grim⁠" Wince replaced Jeorge "⁠Jeorge⁠" Endicott in the starting lineup. The new rifler is still adjusting to life in his new team, but his initial impressions are very positive.

"I'm very familiar with most of the stuff they have here," he told HLTV after the match. "After playing on Liquid and Complexity for like five years, nitr0’s calling style is very easy for me to fit in. And I don't really need to learn too much new stuff at all. It's just about learning how they play and playing off of them."

Grim also confirmed that it was his decision to leave Passion UA, citing the need for a "change of scenery" because of the team's grueling schedule.

The victory against B8 will pit NRG against the loser of Aurora vs FURIA in the next round. It will be a good test for the North American team, who are ranked fourth in the Americas, as they look to get more reps in with Grim.

"Right now, just get as many wins as we can and get all the experience we can," he said. "The more matches, the better. Because they'll see how I want to play as well."

Congrats on the win, your first win with NRG. Are you happy with the performance? Because it felt like you were in control for most of the series.

I'd say that as time goes on, it's just going to get even better. Obviously we've had very limited practice, even now going into this event with me. But it's very easy to play with these guys, they make it all really easy for me. And I feel like I'm slotting in pretty well. I’m just excited to play more.

This is your second tournament with the team after EPL. How are you settling in? Because it feels like you have more room to shine on this team than in some of your previous lineups.

It’s been good. Like I said, we only had four days of practice in Europe before the last event coming into this. And we had like a week in NA, so we haven't had too much time to really iron stuff out. That's why I think the future is going to be brighter for us when we can get more time.

Overall, I'm very familiar with most of the stuff they have here. After playing on Liquid and Complexity for like five years, nitr0’s calling style is very easy for me to fit in. And I don't really need to learn too much new stuff at all. It's just about learning how they play and playing off of them.

You went out of EPL without a single win, but especially the last match, against NIP, was very close. What were your main takeaways from that event?

I'd say the biggest thing is our communication. When the rounds break down, it was getting very hectic sometimes. We had a very bad round against NIP because our comms weren't the best. So we were trying to harp on the comms, get some stuff named so it's easier for people to realize what's going on. I'd say the comms are a big thing, and also it's going to be expected bringing me in because they don't know how I like to communicate and that type of stuff. So more time will fix that.

What has it been like to play with these guys, especially oSee and nitr0, two other former Liquid players, even though you didn't get to play with them back then?

I wish I had got to play with them earlier. I really like nitr0. Honestly, he's a great captain, great a guy.And he's very similar, calling-wise, to JT. So that's why what I said of how I fit in very easily is because most of the stuff he's calling is what I was already used to on Complexity. And osEe is obviously a very good AWPer. His comms are very good for an AWPer, and he is very good at directing in the mid-round. And I just really like playing with him, too. I had a chance a while ago. I almost got to play with him on Dignitas like ten, nine years ago now. But I'm glad we got to it now.

This event, just get as many wins as possible. And it's good practice against these teams to see where we're at in a sense

I want to ask about your departure from Passion UA. You had been with that team for a while now. Just walk me through your time there. Some highs, some lows as well. What was it like for you to leave that team after such a long period?

Yeah, it was pretty good for the most part. We had some success. We won Urban Riga LAN, and that was really good. And then we made Stage 2 in a Major, which was also good. I didn't get benched or anything. It was my decision to leave. I wanted a change of scenery, and NRG reached out. I thought it would be good for me because I've been getting a little burnt out a bit with the travel schedule. And NRG has an easier travel schedule than Passion did. It’s nothing against those guys. I'm still great friends with them. I wish them the best.

The expectation is to get as many wins as possible. Obviously, VRS is a cutthroat system. We kind of needed to refresh our wins on NRG. Since I joined, we hadn't won anything until now. So they were kind of dropping down a bit. So this win was nice for that. This event, just get as many wins as possible. And it's good practice against these teams to see where we're at in a sense.

After this, we will have a ten-day boot camp, which will be really nice. Because it's our first one. And then after that, we'll have a lot of stuff to iron out. But yeah, right now, just get as many wins as we can and get all the experience we can. The more matches, the better. Because they'll see how I want to play as well.
