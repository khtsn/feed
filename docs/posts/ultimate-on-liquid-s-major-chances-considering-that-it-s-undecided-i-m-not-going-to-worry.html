ultimate on Liquid's Major chances: "Considering that it's undecided, I'm not going to worry"

Liquid's Major hopes remain in the balance after a 7-8th place exit at DraculaN 6, as they anxiously await the results of other Americas teams.

Losses to regional rival M80 and Sashi in Romania were proof that adding Mario "⁠malbsMd⁠" Samayoa would not be as instant a fix as might have been hoped — as well as evidence of the challenges at lower-tier LANs compared to one-match-a-day Big Events.

"We're just not used to playing for such a long time," Roland "⁠ultimate⁠" Tomkowiak said. "I feel like I was personally gassed out at the end of the day; I couldn't play my best, especially in the last game."

Commiserations, a hard loss to end your run at DraculaN. Obviously even with limited practice, this result and Rotterdam haven't been the start to life for the roster you would have hoped for. What went wrong here?

We're just not used to playing for such a long time. I feel like I was personally gassed out at the end of the day; I couldn't play my best, especially in the last game. It's a lack of everything, to some degree. I feel like we're still building what we want to build, so yeah, we'll see what the future holds.

You haven't had much time with malbsMd, but how has it been adjusting with him in the team? He seems like the kind of more aggressive fit you needed, so what are the biggest differences you've noticed?

I'm not sure about the differences, I feel like malbs is an amazing player. He understands the game on a very high level, he knows how to adjust to the situations, to players that he's playing around, and yeah. He's amazing.

You've now played a few of these lower-tier LANs, which have become crucial turning points in Major races. How does your approach change for these, and what do you think of how the circuit has evolved with more and more LANs popping up?

I personally like the LANs. It's the best way for worse teams to get better, that's how I see it. Back in the day, I was playing in a lot of Polish teams, like tier-three teams and stuff like this, so it would be an amazing opportunity to play events like this. I like it, I think it's nice.

ultimate calls malbs "an amazing player"

There was some outcry about how, with a Europe Major invite looking unlikely, adding malbsMd swapped Liquid back to the Americas. Some were cynical, saying it was unfair to take it a spot away from other Americas teams. What is your response to that?

I don't know, I'm just playing here. I have no idea (smiles).

This is a blow to your Major hopes, and control is out of your hands with results depending on other teams. What is going through your mind right now around that?

Considering that it is undecided, I'm not going to worry about it. Whatever is going to happen is going to happen anyways, so might as well just be happy and move on. We have plenty of events to play still, so let's hope for the best.
