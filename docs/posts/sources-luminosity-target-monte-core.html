Sources: Luminosity target Monte core

Luminosity are in discussions with the Monte core of Aurimas "⁠Bymas⁠" Pipiras, Oscar "⁠AZUWU⁠" Bell and Aurélien "⁠afro⁠" Drapier, whose contracts with the Ukrainian organization are about to expire, multiple sources told HLTV.

Aleks "⁠Rainwaker⁠" Petrov, the latest addition to the team, could also move to the North American organization in a transfer deal, while IGL Jack "⁠Gizmy⁠" von Spreckelsen is in talks to join a different project, sources said.

The team is expected to be captained by Lucas "⁠lux⁠" Meneghini, who, as Dust2 Brasil reported in April, has been locked in talks with Luminosity. lux was moved to Legacy's bench in February before the Brazilian organization signed Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan.

HLTV knows that Monte's management attempted to negotiate contract extensions with the trio before the Major. After the team was eliminated by The MongolZ, the players informed the organization that they intended to move on.

In Cologne, Monte beat teams like Legacy, paiN and PARIVISION. and pushed G2, The MongolZ and eventual champions Falcons to the wire before bowing out in 12th-14th place.

Luminosity returned to activity in January 2026, when founder Steve Maida reacquired the brand from Vertiqal Studios for CAD $300,000 (approximately $212,000) — seven years after he sold the company to Aquilini GameCo Inc. in a deal worth CAD 21.5 million (over $16 million at the time).

Luminosity's list of achievements includes the 2016 MLG Columbus Major trophy, which the team, an all-Brazilian roster featuring players like Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo and Marcelo "⁠coldzera⁠" David, won by beating Natus Vincere in the final.

The roster was transferred to SK shortly after the Columbus triumph, winning another Major under the German organization. Luminosity continued to field a Brazilian lineup until leaving the game in 2019.
