apEX: "Sometimes I have to focus on being the IGL and not only trashtalking because doing both is tough if you struggle"

Vitality came into IEM Rio after already securing three titles in 2026 and were therefore once again lop-sided favorites for the title, but their games in Rio haven't satisfied the French captain.

"I think it's the worst game we played in the last three months, to be honest," he told HLTV after they barely overcame a G2 side missing their IGL Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač.

"They don't have much weapons right now without the IGL and without anything, but their players stepped up and they played better than us."

The Frenchman also spoke about the team's goal of securing the Intel Grand Slam "as soon as possible," the upcoming clash against Falcons, and joked about Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut being "robbed" by Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool in the BLAST Rotterdam MVP race.

Congratulations, you're through to the playoffs. You got it done the hard way with a rough start to the series, but brought it back after a close game on Inferno. Talk me through that game, what made it so close this time around?

As I just tweeted, I think it's the worst game we played in the last three months, to be honest. Like on Dust2, for example, we had so many moments where we were crazy good, and we just did a small thing that makes it go really bad. So yeah, that's how it is.

I think G2 played without pressure with a stand-in, and sometimes it's annoying to play against that because people, I promise, play differently when there is a little bit more pressure and they have goals. And today, they just didn't care- I mean, they care, but they just played ballsy, and that doesn't help. While they played good and we played bad, it made it a close game.

Yeah, I heard you say after the game that they 'played with balls that they don't usually do.' You also struggled against Falcons with NucleonZ, so how is it playing against a team with a stand-in like that for you? How different is it?

In a way, I think we were not ready to go against that. It's something that we keep repeating to ourselves, but this time we maybe did it less and they just played better, and just played with their weapons. They don't have much weapons right now without the IGL and without anything, but their players stepped up and they played better than us.

They don't have much weapons right now without the IGL and without anything, but their players stepped up and they played better than us

We have to be sure that we step up in that way. It was the same when m0NESY joined Falcons last year and we played them in Melbourne. They played with less pressure and that's normal, and we just have to play better in those kind of moments.

Losing Mirage at the start of the series meant you also lost this 24-map winstreak that you had. Any feelings about that?

No, I think we set a record that is so tough to get with MR12 and everything in CS nowadays. I was like, 'When is it going to be the time we lose the map and how will it feel?' because we didn't lose a single map for the last two months. And yeah, I mean we got stomped, but it's fine, we still have the series at least. We have 18 series [in a row] now. We're right behind NIP, us, and Liquid, so it's already a really good one.

But we're not here to get a few crazy records, we're just here to win as much as we can. As we said here, there's a lot of pressure in this tournament for us because we want to close the Grand Slam, but in a way, we don't care if it's ugly, we don't care if we win games ugly, we just want to win. That's it.

We're not here to get a few crazy records, we're just here to win as much as we can

There was competition on the server with how close it was, and there was also some competition between you and NertZ with the banter happening back and forth. How fun was that for you?

Yeah, he likes to do it (smiles). Today, it was a bit harder for me to do it because we were struggling a bit, so sometimes I have to focus on being the IGL and not only trashtalking because doing both is tough if you struggle. I think he screamed a bit more than me, like trashtalking, because I had to really focus on myself.

Sometimes I have to focus on being the IGL and not only trashtalking because doing both is tough if you struggle

A big part of your story in Rio is that you can close your second Grand Slam here. What's the approach and conversation been like for the team with all the pressure that adds?

We've been dealing with pressure well overall, but... yeah, that's the way it is. We just want to do it here, and it's the same we did in Melbourne last year. We said, 'We want to finish it as soon as possible,' then we can get our heads into preparing for the Major. That's the big goal.

The pressure will be high and we know that, but we have to play better than today. The first map, individually and as a team, we were a bit lost, but when you think about the other two, it was more about the team play and not doing mistakes that we usually don't do.

Heading into your next match, you're through to the playoffs, but you'll be playing the winner of Spirit vs. Falcons. A potential match against Falcons [at full strength] which hasn't happened in a while, so how do you feel going into that, or into the Spirit game?

It's going to be a tough one, no matter what, because Falcons have less pressure right now. We all know what's going to happen after this event, and for the Major and the next few events, so I think the guys will take it in an easy way, I would say. And Spirit, I don't know what to expect from them. They've been struggling this year, so let's see how it goes in this game, and I think it will be a tough game for us anyway.

Last tournament, ZywOo missed out on an MVP to a teammate for the first time. Can you tell me what happened after that, were there any funny conversations within the team?

Poor ZywOo got robbed by ropz, that's what I can say (apEX smiles). No, I think this competition is good, maybe it will... ZywOo never had competition in a way for the MVP, I mean ropz has been close a few times, like Melbourne last year was close and also the Budapest Major.

It's good for Mathieu [ZywOo] to have someone who fights him. We know that with s1mple, it happened once with electroNic at the ICE Challenge or something like that. This time, ZywOo lost it, but it's fine. We have a great team and I don't think we care who wins the MVP that much. The most important thing is to win the trophy because that is what we will all remember. Individual [awards] are good if you win, because if you don't win, who cares about the individual trophies?
