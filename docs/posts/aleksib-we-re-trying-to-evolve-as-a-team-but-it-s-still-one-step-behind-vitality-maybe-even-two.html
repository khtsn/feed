Aleksib: "We're trying to evolve as a team, but it's still one step behind Vitality. Maybe even two"

Natus Vincere's hopes of winning their second trophy in a row and putting an end to Vitality's dominance were shattered on Sunday as they were swept 3-0 in the grand final of BLAST Open Rotterdam.

Speaking to HLTV after the match, a downhearted Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen said Vitality were the "better team" of the series, explaining that a lot of small mistakes, or "microdetails" as he put it, proved costly.

"There were a lot of after-plants where when we were proactive, they were punishing us, and when we were too passive, they were punishing us," the Finnish captain said.

"And we were just losing some unlosable after-plants, so to speak, and if you're losing so many of them against a team like this, the score will be 0-3, right?"

Aleksib will try to take the positives from the tournament as NAVI prepare for their next tournament — a trip to Brazil for IEM Rio, where 16 teams will be vying for $1 million.

"[laughs] You know, why not another grand final?" he said. "I think that we're playing well as a team. We're trusting each other. We are deep in the system, and we understand how we can play Counter-Strike.

"But still, winning zero maps against Vitality in this grand final isn't the best thing in the world. But we need to try and look at the positives and try to do better next time."

Aleksi, a 3-0 defeat in the final. Commiserations on the loss. Give me your reactions to the match.

They just showed that they were the better team. I mean, it was expected that they would come into this game with almost no weaknesses. We showed some strength on the T side, and I feel like, on all maps, like the anti-force on Inferno, Anubis 3on2 after-plants... There were a lot of after-plants where when we were proactive, they were punishing us, and when we were too passive, they were punishing us.

And we were just losing some unlosable after-plants, so to speak, and if you're losing so many of them against a team like this, the score will be 0-3, right? So, I think we fought, we gave our best, but the better team won.

We're trying to go home from here and take our lessons and try to learn from them

There were moments on Inferno and Anubis where you were getting a grip on the game, but then either you made a small mistake or Vitality pulled off a clutch, and then the momentum was gone. You almost have to play a perfect game in order to beat them. That sounds very frustrating.

Yeah, exactly. Like, they won some, as you said, force-buy rounds, and even a round where, I believe, on Inferno that ZywOo had a hero AK dropped to him, makazze and I didn't play time on the B exec and we gave them a 2-on-2 after-plant and they just managed to outplay us. It's a lot of these mistakes that cost us the game.

Going up against players like ZywOo, ropz and flameZ when they are all in top form is hard. Do you think you missed that X-factor a little bit?

I think that we kind of found the X-factor in ourselves, in makazze especially. That's something that we needed: the explosiveness and the ability to even win incredible clutches, just not fearing to do anything, like playing super fearless Counter-Strike. And that's something that you can see when you look at Vitality and how they play.

The thing is that we're trying to evolve as a team, but it's still one step behind Vitality. Maybe even two, but we're trying to go home from here and take our lessons and try to learn from them.

What were the main positives from this match? Would it have been a bit different if you had had more time to prepare?

Yeah, it's unfortunate that we didn't have time, but I don't want to rely on excuses. I think it's just that we had a lot of rounds in scenarios that we want to be in. Having those man advantages and after-plants, we just need to nail the microdetails. Because, you know, as I said previously, I think that when we were trying to win clutch situations against them, you could feel like they always held it down, like held the bombsite or kept winning like one or two rounds, like on Dust2, ZywOo coming through the B smoke and winning the round.

It's just that these micro details didn't go our way, and they are showing the individual brilliance in those moments. And yeah, we can do a lot better as individuals and as a team as well.

You'll have Rio in a couple of weeks. What is the plan? Are you going straight back to work? Are you going to have a bit of a break?

We are going to fly back tomorrow and probably going to have a few days off. And after that, it's just work for Rio.

After two finals in a row, what are the goals for Rio?

[laughs] You know, why not another grand final? I think that we're playing well as a team. We're trusting each other. We are deep in the system, and we understand how we can play Counter-Strike.

But still, winning zero maps against Vitality in this grand final isn't the best thing in the world. But we need to try and look at the positives and try to do better next time.
