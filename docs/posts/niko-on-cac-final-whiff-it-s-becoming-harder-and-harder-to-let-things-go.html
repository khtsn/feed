NiKo on CAC final whiff: "It's becoming harder and harder to let things go"

Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač joined Chinese streamer "小盟哥FPS英语" (Xiao Meng Ge's English FPS) in the aftermath of CS Asia Championships 2026 to discuss how the final turned against Falcons.

It was the seventh successive grand final defeat for Falcons, a run in which NiKo averages a 0.92 rating, and he made it clear to the Chinese streamer that he expects more from the team despite feeling "exhaustion" and a lack of time with Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen.

It was very frustrating for me because it's happened before that I've missed like this in a final

"We expected ourselves to win this event and overcome the difficulties we have had in finals," NiKo said on stream. "We were tired, we were exhausted, our game was not as clean as in Astana [...] but it's still not acceptable that we lost another final. This one will probably affect us a little bit more than some others, but we will try to overcome it and find the positives out of the final as well."

A key turning point in the final was when NiKo flanked B cave on Ancient, with Falcons leading 1-0 and set to make it 10-10 on map two. But disaster struck when the 29-year-old missed his initial shot and Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto turned on a dime to kill the flanker and post a 4k of his own.

"I tried to play a bit too fast," NiKo explains. "I tried to go for a headshot and start running."

It's a moment that NiKo admits affected him: "This round, when I missed the guy in the back, definitely affected the half. It would have given us a higher chance of getting to overtime or winning.

"Tough moment, very frustrating for myself because it's happened before that I've missed like this in a final. It's a battle in my head [...]. It's becoming harder and harder to let things go. It definitely had a small impact, at least on me, I don't think it did on the team because some of them were alive."

The whiff was all the more unfortunate as NiKo had helped Falcons even get to that point, recovering from a 0-6 K-D start and 3-9 half to win two 1v1s and have a solid CT side up to that point.
