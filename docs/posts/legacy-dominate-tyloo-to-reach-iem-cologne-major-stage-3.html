Legacy dominate TYLOO to reach IEM Cologne Major Stage 3

Legacy did what their paiN and MIBR countrymen couldn't, and secured a spot in the third stage of the IEM Cologne Major. To get there, they beat TYLOO in the deciding round of Stage 2.

Legacy and TYLOO played each other at the CS Asia Championship less than one month before this deciding bout at the Major, and the result was the same, albeit the Brazilians needed both maps to go to overtime in Shanghai. Now, they cruised to 13-7 on Mirage and 13-4 on Inferno.

TYLOO were the ones who hit first on Mirage, winning the first pistol round and taking a 3-0 lead, but Legacy responded swiftly and showed why it was their pick as the Chinese squad failed to keep up on the defense, tallying just one more round before the half.

Down 4-8, TYLOO won the second pistol round, but even then could only score three more rounds before the guns came out and Legacy chained a clean five rounds on the road to a 13-7 victory. Eduardo "⁠dumau⁠" Wolkmer, with +8.49% swing and a 1.89 rating, led the way.

The series flipped over to Inferno, and this time Legacy managed to get on the board by winning the crucial CT-side pistol round. Not only did Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan and his teammates dominate the map as they had on Mirage, but they did even better on the way to a ten-round half.

Up against the wall, TYLOO's Dongkai "⁠Jee⁠" Ji and Su "⁠Zero⁠" Jingshen got all five frags in the second pistol round, but it was too little too late, as Legacy took back control two rounds later to win it out 13-4.

Guilherme "⁠saadzin⁠" Pacheco, who kicked off the map with a 1v2 clutch and closed the first half with a triple, went on to be the highest-rated player on the map with a +12.86% swing and 2.05 rating.

It wasn't either of dumau or saadzin who topped the board across both maps, however, as Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto's quiet consistency earned him a server-topping 1.57 rating in the decider match.
