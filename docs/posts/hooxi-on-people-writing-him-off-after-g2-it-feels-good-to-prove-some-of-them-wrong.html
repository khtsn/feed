HooXi on people writing him off after G2: "It feels good to prove some of them wrong"

Astralis have secured their best placement since going international at ESL Pro League Season 23, grabbing a bronze medal in Stockholm thanks to a hard-earned 2-1 victory over FUT in the third-place decider.

Astralis shut down FUT in OT to take bronze at ESL Pro League

"It's showing that we are doing something right," Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen told HLTV after his team secured bronze. "We still have some way to go, especially when it comes to consistency, but I think right now we should be happy about what we achieved so far."

Not only are Astralis trending in the right direction, but HooXi is also emphasising his personal credentials as an IGL, having been doubted at points in his career. "I know how much work I put in, that I'm doing my best and am capable, so all the noise outside the server will always be that."

HLTV spoke with HooXi after he claimed a bronze medal to get his thoughts on the match with FUT, Astralis' trajectory, and his own contributions to their current upward trend.

Listen to the full interview here.

What are your initial thoughts after that series?

I mean, a lot of ups and downs (laughs), a real rollercoaster of emotions. You could definitely feel that the game from yesterday was still hitting us a bit on the first map, but I think we snapped out of it on the second map. It was a mix of nerves and just a close game, being very intense, in the third map, that made us make some mistakes. In the end we pulled through, and I'm super proud of that.

How did you pull through? You were down in both halves on Overpass, but managed to put together streaks and get in to overtime.

It's a mix of jabbi playing super well on CT for example, to make the comeback happen, and individuals stepping up in clutches, the 1v2 round in OT by Staehr for example. Just everyone not being scared of taking initiative, and I think that's when we play our best.

The team thinks it's very important to have a good mood, but at the same time we are sometimes bad at putting energy into keeping the mood up

A common theme throughout the tournament when we hear snippets of Astralis' comms has been a lot of 'don't tilt, don't tilt.' Can you give me a bit of insight from inside the team about that?

The rest of the team thinks it's very important to have a good mood, but at the same time we are sometimes bad at putting energy into keeping the mood up. I think it's very tough as a captain, because I also have to fix things and tell people when they're doing something wrong, but at the same time keep the mood up. I can't really be… I can't wear all the hats!

Good cop and bad cop?

Exactly, at the same time, which is tough. Mood is important and I think we play the best when everyone is enjoying the game.

How does it feel to get this kind of success so soon into the international lineup, the first time Astralis have gone in that direction?

It of course feels good, and it's showing that we are doing something right. I think that we still have some way to go, especially when it comes to consistency, but I think right now we should be happy about what we achieved so far. We will keep working hard.

The new guys in the team should not suffer from what has happened in the past. I'm just super happy that people are supporting us.

I wanted to talk about your personal story, because I think towards the end of G2 a lot of people had written you off, and it took a little while for you to get a team. Now you have come to Astralis and, with two different lineups, made your team competitive. How does it feel to have that personal vindication, knowing what you can do and proving it on the server?

Obviously it feels good but I know how much work I put in, that I'm doing my best and am capable, so all the noise outside the server will always be that, no matter how good you do it. You can see even apEX on Twitter is like 'guys, we won five tournaments in a row and lose one final and you're complaining,' you know? There will always be someone complaining and it's fine, but it feels good to prove some of them wrong at least.

I'm hearing a lot of talk now, they were saying it on the desk, that Astralis is likeable again. How do you feel about saving the Astralis brand?

(laughs) I mean it feels good of course. I don't know, they got some kind of villain status at some point. I think I'm not the only one, the whole rebrand and the new guys in the team should not suffer from what has happened in the past. I'm just super happy that people are supporting us.
