allu returns to ENCE as head coach

allu takes over as ENCE's coach

ENCE have announced that they have named Aleksi "⁠allu⁠" Jalli as the new head coach of their CS team following the recent departure of George "⁠Whitey⁠" White.

The news marks allu's return to ENCE after playing for the Finnish organization for over five years across three spells between 2013 and 2022. His third ENCE stint, after international experiences with FaZe and OpTic, saw him win two notable trophies and reach the grand final of a Major before the team imploded.

allu played his last competitive match in April, when his team, CSDIILIT, lost to ENCE in the grand final of Elisa Open Finland Season 12.

"Returning to ENCE feels right," allu said in a statement. "This is where some of my best memories in Counter-Strike were made, and being back in this environment means a lot to me.

"I won't pretend I have all the answers as a coach. This is a new challenge for me, and I'm stepping into it knowing there's a lot to learn. But I'm genuinely excited about that.

"I have spent years at the highest level of this game. I want to bring that experience and knowledge to this team and the next generation of ENCE players."

ENCE added that the current roster, which was promoted from the academy in April, "remains under evaluation," with allu now integrated into the process. The team is currently ranked 143rd in the world.

ENCE are now:

Aleksi "⁠millert⁠" Lehtopuu

Aleksi "⁠allu⁠" Jalli (coach)

Paavo "⁠podi⁠" Heiskanen (benched)
