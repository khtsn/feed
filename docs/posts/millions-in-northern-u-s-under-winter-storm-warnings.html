Millions in Northern U.S. Under Winter Storm Warnings

Forecasters warned of the possibility of wind gusts as strong as a hurricane as another round of heavy lake-effect snow and blustery cold moves through the Northeast.

Judson Jones and Hilary Howard

Judson Jones is a meteorologist and reporter for The Times. Hilary Howard reported from New York.

Dec. 5, 2024Updated 1:13 p.m. ET

Millions of people across the Midwest, Northeastern United States and southern Ontario, Canada, were under winter weather warnings as snow squalls moved through Thursday morning ahead Arctic temperatures blasting through the region with blustery and potentially damaging wind gusts.

After a week of bitterly cold temperatures and days of lake-effect snow, another shot of cold air was sweeping through the Northeast after sending temperatures in parts of the Midwest plunging into the teens, 10 to 20 degrees below normal. Wind gusts up to 80 miles per hour are expected Thursday afternoon into the evening, bringing blizzard conditions to areas with lake-effect snow and making travel treacherous.

Forecasters with the National Weather Service in New York City said that Thursday night into Friday would be the coldest night and early morning of the season, especially after accounting for wind chill.

In New York, which experienced one of its warmest Halloweens ever this fall, some welcomed the return of more-seasonable temperatures. At an outdoor Christmas tree lot in Midtown Manhattan on Wednesday, Rosie Roop, 25, a hospitality and business student, was buying a wreath. “I’m happy it’s getting colder,” she said. “I just want it to snow.”

Ms. Roop said she had worn her puffy coat for the first time this week. “I definitely like the four seasons,” she said. “I have not been a fan of the murky in-between.”

The chilly weather inspired Danny Offermann, 28, an assistant principal at a charter school in the South Bronx, to visit a Christmas tree lot and buy a two-foot tree ($60) for his apartment.

“I was a little disappointed about the warm fall,” he said. “I’m usually, like, a sweater-weather, fall kind of guy, and I didn’t get that at all this year.”

Mr. Offermann said he welcomed the cold weather because he lives in an old apartment with a furnace that constantly produces heat. “It’s finally balanced,” he said. “Classic New York living.”

Down the street at a Target store, Lisa Spaeth, a former ballet dancer, wore a fox fur hat, a fox fur vest over a green coat, and faux fur leg warmers under her boots. She said the cold didn’t bother her too much, as long as she was dressed for it.

“I want to enjoy being outside,” she said. “As long as I have my fur, I’m fine.”

Snow and wind could be hazardous.

An initial shot of snow fell across commuters Thursday morning as the storm system moved across eastern Canada and the cold air dipped across the Northeast. For those along the coast, burst of snow was mixing in with rain, and conditions slightly farther inland were an icy, slushy mess.

Snow squalls moved through the region in the morning, and forecasters warned that they could rapidly reduce visibility, making travel dangerous.

The strong winds also could knock down tree branches and cause power outages. These widespread winds are forecast to at times gusts above hurricane-force (74 m.p.h. or higher) and could whip through New England and the mid-Atlantic and down through the Central Appalachians.

On Thursday morning, more than 12,000 households in New Jersey were without power, along with more than 17,000 in Pennsylvania and more than 14,000 in Virginia, according to poweroutage.us, which tracks data from utility companies.

For areas east of the Great Lakes that were hit hard during the lake-effect snow event this week, another foot of snow could fall Thursday and Friday, especially downwind of Lake Erie and Lake Ontario.

Mark Poloncarz, the executive of Erie County, N.Y., urged residents to take precautions when shoveling snow after two people died in the county on Wednesday of heart attacks while shoveling or snow blowing.

A blizzard warning was also issued for Erie County in Pennsylvania, where winds could reduce visibility to less than a quarter mile on Thursday into Friday. Blizzard warnings were also issued for portions of the Allegheny Mountains, where a couple more inches of snow is possible.

Moderate amounts of snow are possible over interior New England on Thursday. After the initial band of snow, light snow showers will be possible later in the day across the Northeast, including New York City.

Blustery conditions will remain in New York City on Friday. By Sunday, temperatures will moderate, and temperatures in the Northeast may return to above normal early next week.
