Virtus.pro debut with MPKBK LAN victory

b1st topped the rating chart for his team

Virtus.pro have debuted their new roster with a LAN trophy by claiming victory at WINLINE MPKBK CIS LAN Season 5, taking home the lion's share of the $30,000 prizepool as well as an over 300 point bump to their VRS standing.

The result represents a successful first step for a Virtus.pro squad that was completely revamped following a torrid start to the year, when well-known names like Evgenii "⁠FL1T⁠" Lebedev, Petr "⁠fame⁠" Bolyshev and Ilya "⁠Perfecto⁠" Zalutskiy were benched as Virtus.pro opted to rebuild around Nikolay "⁠mir⁠" Bityukov and their academy squad.

The team had not played an official since late January, only finalizing their roster in March before embarking on an extended bootcamp in Serbia until late April. It seems the bootcamp paid off for the young squad, who sport four teenagers alongisde veteran mir.

Whilst victory at the LAN in St. Petersburg is a promising result in itself, the manner in which Virtus.pro went through the tournament is also cause for optimism as the Russian squad did not drop a map until the semi-final, and were only really pressured across a whole series by ARCRED in the final.

The carry duties were spread across the squad throughout the tournament, with Dias "⁠F0R3VER⁠" Kutubay, Vladimir "⁠b1st⁠" Krasikov and mir all topping the scoreboards in different series. b1st came out on top of the rating chart for his team, posting a 1.17 over 12 maps, but not a single Virtus.pro member was among the top 10 rating in the event, illustrating how much of a team effort their campaign was.

With their successful tournament campaign Virtus.pro have now jumped to 38th in the live global VRS, and they will have the opportunity to build upon that platform further when they begin their journey in European Pro League Series 7 on June 4.
