Passion UA sign try

try will try his hand outside of a South American squad for the first time in his career.

Passion UA have announced the addition of sniper Santino "⁠try⁠" Rigal from Imperial to take over the AWPing duties on the team. Whilst Passion UA have not stated who will step back from the starting lineup, it will presumably be previous AWPer Håkon "⁠hallzerk⁠" Fjærli.

The news comes amidst a largely disappointing start to the year for Passion UA who failed to win a series in either of their two international LAN outings thus far, BLAST Bounty Season 1 and IEM Katowice Stage 1.

try arrives having long been touted as one of the most talented AWPers hailing from the South American region, serving as one of the best-rated players on each team he has been a part of during his career.

The 21-year-old spent the last 18 months turning out for Brazilian side Imperial, with whom he has attended three Majors and produced multiple top placings in regional competitions, like Circuito FERJEE de Esports 2025 and FiReLEAGUE Buenos Aires 2025. Throughout his tenure try has been by far Imperial's top-rated player, posting a 1.19 average rating.

The team largely struggled to make headway in international competition, however, barring a surprise run to Stage 3 of the last Major, StarLadder Budapest Major. try was a pillar of his team's success in Hungary, and the only player to acquit himself well individually against the stiffer competition in Stage 3.

It is expected try will replace hallzerk, who had served as a member of the core of Complexity and Passion UA across three-and-a-half years. The Norwegian was regularly one of the better-rated players during his tenure in Complexity and Passion UA, but regularly saw his level dip significantly when playing against teams higher up the world ranking.
