Media: Neityu to stand in for karrigan as FaZe prepare for US events

Twistzz will reportedly take up in-game leadership duties for the team.

FaZe have turned to ENCE's benched player Ryan "⁠Neityu⁠" Aubry as a stand-in for Falcons-bound Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen, according to Croissant Strike's Guillaume 'neL' Rathier.

The French player is expected to play for FaZe "in the next weeks," which suggests he could attend both BLAST Rivals Season 1 (April 29-May 3) and IEM Atlanta (May 11-17).

If the move is confirmed, it will reunite Neityu with Niclas "⁠enkay J⁠" Krumhorn, who was named head coach of FaZe's team on Thursday.

A former member of MOUZ NXT, Neityu made the jump to tier-1 CS in October 2024 when he joined ENCE and played an important role in the team's title-winning run at Elisa Masters Espoo.

The 20-year-old and the rest of ENCE's squad were benched on March 11, when the Finnish organization announced it was pivoting to a local roster in a bid to develop homegrown talent.

According to neL, Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken is expected to take over in-game leading for the team, though it's unclear whether the Canadian will hold onto that role beyond these two tournaments.

Falcons' run at IEM Rio ended on Sunday with a 2-0 victory over FURIA in the third-place decider. The Saudi organization is expected to unveil karrigan as a replacement for Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski shortly.
