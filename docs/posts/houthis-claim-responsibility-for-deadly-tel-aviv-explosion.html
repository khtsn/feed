Houthis Claim Responsibility for Deadly Tel Aviv Explosion

The militia said it had used a new kind of drone. The city’s mayor said Tel Aviv was on heightened alert after what he called a drone attack.

Gabby Sobelman, Rawan Sheikh Ahmad and Erin Mendell

Gabby Sobelman reported from Rehovovot, Israel, and Rawan Sheikh Ahmad reported from Haifa, Israel.

Published July 18, 2024Updated July 19, 2024, 1:30 a.m. ET

The Iran-backed Houthi militia claimed responsibility for an explosion in central Tel Aviv that killed at least one person early Friday morning. The Israeli authorities have not commented on responsibility for the explosion.

The Israeli military said in a statement that a preliminary investigation indicated that a falling aerial target caused the blast, and that no sirens had been activated. Tel Aviv’s mayor, Ron Huldai, said the episode had involved a drone and that the city was on heightened alert.

“The war is still here, and it is hard and painful,” he said on social media, referring to Israel’s war with Hamas in the Gaza Strip.

A spokesman for the Houthi militia, Yahya Sarea, said in a televised statement that the group had targeted Tel Aviv and that it would continue to target the city. The group used a new drone that can get past Israel’s defense systems and is not detected by radar, he added.

Since November, the Houthis, who are based in Yemen, have been attacking ships in a vital route in the Red Sea in a campaign in support of Palestinians.

The body of a 50-year-old man was found in a nearby apartment building as emergency workers were combing through the area around the explosion, according to a statement from Zaki Heller, a spokesman for Israel’s national emergency service. The man was found in his apartment and had shrapnel injuries, the Tel Aviv police said in a statement.

The police said 10 people had been wounded and described them as “lightly injured.” Four of them suffered penetrating and blunt force trauma, said Roee Klein, a paramedic for the national emergency service, in a video.

Witnesses described a “very loud explosion” that could be heard miles away. Surveillance video widely circulated on social media and verified by Storyful shows a bright flash of light, followed by a boom and the sound of an alarm.

The police said that a large number of forces was responding to the explosion. The military said that security forces were at the scene and that the air force had increased patrols of Israeli air space.

Aaron Boxerman and Ronen Bergman contributed reporting.
