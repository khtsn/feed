Houthis Claim Responsibility for Deadly Tel Aviv Explosion

The cause of the blast was not immediately clear, but the Israeli military said in a statement that it was looking into the reports that there had been an aerial target.

Gabby Sobelman and Erin Mendell

Gabby Sobelman reported from Rehovot, Israel.

An explosion in central Tel Aviv injured at least seven people, the Israel authorities said early Friday morning.

A preliminary investigation indicated that a falling aerial target caused the blast, the Israeli military said in a statement, adding that no sirens were activated.

A body was found in a nearby apartment building, according to a statement from the spokesman for the Tel Aviv police. Zaki Heller, a spokesman for Israel’s national emergency service, said on Kan TV that the body, a 50-year-old man, was discovered while emergency workers were combing through the area around the explosion and that it had sustained “penetrating wounds, as a result of shrapnel or glass.”

The police described the other wounded people as “lightly injured.”

The police said that a large number of forces was responding. The military said that security forces were at the scene and that the air force had increased patrols of Israeli air space.

Witnesses described a “very loud explosion” that could be heard miles away.

This is a developing story.

Aaron Boxerman and Ronen Bergman contributed reporting.
