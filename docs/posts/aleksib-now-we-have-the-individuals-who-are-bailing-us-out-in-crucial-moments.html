Aleksib: "Now we have the individuals who are bailing us out in crucial moments"

With the Major approaching, Natus Vincere have found the best time to ramp up with a run to second place at BLAST Rivals and a win at IEM Atlanta, where they finally got one over their perennial nemesis, Vitality, on the way to the trophy.

A crucial part of that has been the improved form of Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri and Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov, who got their career-first MVPs in NAVI's two wins this year from Pro League and Atlanta, and they hope to build on that success to end the season.

"I hope w0nderful plays the same way here in the Major, and it's gonna be good for our team," Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen told HLTV on the media day. "When we analyze some of our games, we're winning some of the clutch moments that, previously when you watched some of our results and we didn't do as well, we were losing those moments."

"Now we have the individuals who are backing it up, bailing us out in crucial moments, and that's something you see from elite-level teams time and time again."

You can listen to, watch or read the interview in full below:

Aleksi, welcome to Cologne, Stage 3 coming up. It's been a month since you came back from the Atlanta victory. Can you tell me what that meant for the team, beating Vitality on the way and finishing that to get your second title this year?

I think the time we had in the US was good. We lost in Rivals playoffs against Vitality and then we ended up beating them in Atlanta and winning the tournament, so I think overall for us it was a great couple of weeks there. We grinded a lot.

We didn't have too much time off since then, but we know it's a Major, a lot of teams are looking sharp. We also want to contend for the trophy, and I feel like our team and the shape and everything is there for us to make a deep run, so just excited for that. Overall, it's been a lot of practice now since Atlanta, so it's just always nice to get on the server and play some officials again.

We saw w0nderful grab his first MVP and he's been looking great in the last couple of tournaments. B1ad3 spoke about it in the past with w0nderful that he tended to be up and down because of his mental. Have you seen a shift in his confidence lately?

Yeah, I do think so. For him the mental side is the toughest, and I feel like that goes for a lot of players. If the individual feels confident in a team, you even see some players like NertZ, for example, playing tremendously well in G2 right now, and I just think it comes down to the confidence and the trust that everyone puts in you.

I think for w0nderful, he maybe lacked it at some point and now I feel like he's back. I hope he plays the same way here in the Major and it's gonna be good for our team.

In the past NAVI have spoken about not having any superstars or big stars in the team and having to play up to that and balance the team around that. We've seen makazze get his form up a lot and w0nderful has joined him as well. Has that made your approach to how you craft your playstyle change, or is it all the same NAVI, just with players in better form?

I think it's that, the same NAVI with players in better form. I do think that when we analyze some of our games, we're winning some of the moments or some of the clutch moments that, previously when you watched some of our results when we didn't do as well, we were losing those moments.

Now we have the individuals who are backing it up, bailing us out in crucial moments, and that's something you see from elite-level teams time and time again with ZywOo and donk and the players that you are used to always pulling those moments off.

It's always nice to see that your teammates are playing better and getting in better shape because I feel like it's important for them as individuals, and it's just nice to always see the progress that you're making as a team. In the elite level, you need that to happen if you want to lift a trophy, and that's a good thing for us this season.

You've said you would have a bootcamp leading up to the Major. How did it go?

We had a bootcamp, I think overall we played for two weeks, but we didn't spend the whole time in Barcelona this time. We came here probably four days ago and we had three days of practice here, now a media day and the officials are tomorrow.

It was kinda nice to play a couple of days here since we have 3 ping, slightly better PCs, so it's good to shift from the bootcamp mentality to get yourself in the tournament, play with the practice PCs and practice rooms. It's just nice to play here a couple of days early and get some practice in.

We played a lot of practice this season and, as you mention, we just won the previous event and we still played a two-week bootcamp, so I think everyone is excited to play, but we all know that the Stage 3 teams are... a lot of them are playing well and I don't think it's gonna be easy at all. It's gonna be BO3s now, so something different... Overall I just think we're ready as a team, ready as individuals, and we're excited to get the season wrapped up.

You mention best-of-threes, which is a big change coming into this Major. Does favor you as one of the favorites, or is it all the same to you?

I think historically we've been playing pretty decent in the best-of-one games overall, if you take into consideration all of the tournaments, but I think we've found ourselves in the 1-1 bracket a lot of times as well.

I think it's a nice change. I do think that if a team goes 3-0 you give it a bit more credit, since they have to play six maps at minimum, and you'll probably see which one of the elite teams are performing way better than the others and who struggles more. So I think it's just a good change. You'll get all the playoffs teams that really deserve to be there, and I hope that we're gonna be ready since day one.

Leading up to this tournament, we've had a few teams have an uptick in form. You won the last tournament, but Falcons also got closer with karrigan, and we've seen Spirit with donk in pretty amazing form already here at this tournament. How do you see the competition here and how difficult is it going to be at the top?

Exactly the teams you mentioned, and I also think FUT, Legacy is there as a dark horse and historically they've beaten us two times in a row now. There are a lot of teams that can be scary, and some of them have some experience playing here now, but I don't think it's really that big of a thing. It's hard to keep up the new strats and everything when you already showed how you played in the previous stage, so they all have to come up with something new perhaps for this stage.

Overall, there are a lot of teams now that are in great shape. Everyone is waiting to see how Vitality's gonna play since Atlanta, since they ended their streak, so to say, so are they gonna look even better than what Spirit showed in Stage 2, or are they gonna struggle?

Day one and two will tell a lot of how this Stage 3 is gonna go and who the favorites going into the playoffs are already. If a team goes 2-0 and there's no competition, you can kinda call them in as one of the favorites to maybe even win the event. It's gonna be interesting to see how this Stage 3 pans out because of the best-of-threes. You will see a lot of maps and CS from all of the teams. I just feel like I can't wait to play (smiles).
