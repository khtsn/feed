FalleN: "I think everyone in the team is trying to find new ways to play well"

FURIA's stop-start 2026 has continued at BLAST Open Rotterdam, as Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo's troops suffered a group-stage elimination at the hands of Falcons.

Despite the result, however, the Brazilian captain said that the team is still adjusting to their new playstyle and therefore has no regrets regarding the tournament's outcome.

"So I don’t think there’s anything to feel sorry about regarding this tournament. Obviously, being so close to qualifying, we’re very sad," he told HLTV after FURIA's elimination.

FalleN also spoke about Mareks "⁠YEKINDAR⁠" Gaļinskis's mouse issue during the match, FURIA's expectations for IEM Rio, and the work Yuri "⁠yuurih⁠" Santos is putting in to return to his best.

FalleN, a 2-1 loss and FURIA are out of the event. You let an 11-7 lead on Mirage slip and you couldn't close it out on Nuke after a 9-3 first half. What do you think went wrong?

I think it was a great match and we could have won those maps, just as they could have won Dust2. So I think it was a match where both teams had great chances. And we had two big leads — on Nuke it was even bigger — which we failed to convert. But credit to Falcons, too. I think they managed to sort out their defense, and we couldn’t close out the games.

I wanted to ask about the controversial pause on Dust2. BLAST has revealed that there was an emergency situation with KSCERATO. Is everything okay with him? And what did you make of the pause?

Yes, everything is fine. He was feeling a bit unwell and, when he noticed the issue with the mouse, he went to sort it out. I think YEKINDAR got frustrated and slammed his mouse on the desk. Something like that, I suppose. But luckily, he managed to sort it out. He used fer's technique — he banged the mouse again, and it worked again.

Weren't you worried that he was tilting and that it could have a negative impact on the team?

No, I think every player has their own way of releasing some of the frustration from what happens. He wasn’t frustrated with anything specific about the team or anyone in particular; it was really just because a particular play didn’t work out for him.

The circumstances coming into the event weren't the best, with you joining the team the day before it started. What are the main positives?

I think internally we've made progress in certain areas within the team, in some issues we’ve been working on. Certain actions, certain things we’ve been trying to improve. The tournament itself wasn’t bad; we lost to Aurora, who've had a great run recently. They reached the EPL final and are doing well here, too. We’ve now lost to the Falcons, a team against whom the matches are always close.

I think internally we've made progress in certain areas within the team, in some issues we’ve been working on

So I don’t think there’s anything to feel sorry about regarding this tournament. Obviously, being so close to qualifying, we’re very sad. And we'll have to keep our heads up and train even harder for the next tournament.

Something that has been pointed out recently is that yuurih has been far from the level he showed at the end of last season. How is he trying to overcome this?

I think yuurih has been working really hard and taking all the feedback on board and really trying out different things to get back to the form he was in last year. I’m 100% sure he’ll manage to do that. In fact, I think he played really well today. I think he managed to play much closer to the level he’d been showing.

And I think everyone in the team is trying to find new ways to play well. Because there’s no point in us looking back and thinking, 'I wish I could do what I did last year'. We have to find new ways to complement each other, to play well. And I think that’s what we're after. So I think it's just a matter of time. I believe that with a few more tournaments, we’ll be a bit more in form, in terms of finding consistency and confidence.

We have to find new ways to complement each other, to play well

After this, you'll have IEM Rio, an event that is very important for you. How are you going to prepare for that one?

We're going back to bootcamp to prepare, just like we always do, and prepare hard. Our sole focus is on ironing out the areas we think we can improve so we can perform well on home soil. It's going to be very cool to play in Rio.

And, I assume, this is one of your big targets of the year, right?

Absolutely, absolutely. For me, it would be really good to do well in Rio, especially to win it. It's a CS tournament in the new versions that I haven't won yet. And for the team as a whole, it would be a dream to win it.
