Graviti: "It feels good to play good CS, it's not about the playoffs"

3DMAX suffered a significant blow prior to their appearance at PGL Bucharest, failing to qualify for the Major after a string of underwhelming results in lower-tier VRS LANs. Things seemed to be continuing downhill in Romania as 3DMAX opened their account with a loss, before struggling past a Voca side who were playing with a stand-in.

3DMAX's Major campaign goes up in smoke after brutal loss to B8

The French side made an admirable resurgence in their final group games, taking down B8 before pulling off a confident 2-0 upset of PARIVISION, securing a playoff berth with a game to spare.

With that victory over PARIVISION under his belt and a playoff campaign on the horizon, Filip "⁠Graviti⁠" Brankovic gave HLTV a quick interview to discuss his team's resurgence at PGL Bucharest and what it means to the team.

You can listen to the full interview here.

How does it feel to be in the playoffs?

It feels good to play good CS actually, it's not about the playoffs for us, it's more about playing better CS. We finally play our game and we see why and understand why we are good, and why we are bad the last few weeks.

Why do you think you were able to play your best game against the best team that you have played?

We just work a lot and the work pays. We are finally on the same page as a team, and that's it to be honest.

Do you think it was just about having enough time since the changes to find your best form again?

No, because we didn't have time to be honest. We are playing a lot of tournaments since we made the change, so no it's not about time, it's just about understanding of the game from everyone in the team. We lost ourselves, everyone. So that's it.

We lost ourselves, everyone

You personally were the IGL before and are now not on that role. Do you feel more comfortable now?

No, I don't feel more comfortable or not more comfortable, I'm just normal with that. I am okay that Maka is the IGL, I trust him a lot, so that's okay for me.

Why do you think that decision was made, from your perspective?

I don't know. We were playing too many tournaments. I didn't decide that, but I was okay that Maka took the IGL role.

Because you've been playing so many tournaments, do you think it was hard to adapt to the IGL role? You didn't have a lot of time to practice and settle in that way.

For sure it was a problem, but for everyone it's the same. I guess it was not the right time for me, maybe it will be in the future. I hope it will be in the future, that I will be an IGL again, but right now with Maka it's very nice. I hope we will do good with Maka as an IGL.

We want to win this tournament, to prove we are a good team

Looking forward at the playoffs, do you have any expectations, or are you just happy to be playing in the playoffs?

Actually we want to win this tournament, to prove we are a good team and that all the bad teams, let's say, in the past that won against us… that we can finally prove that we are still the same, we didn't change that much, it was just in our minds that we fucked up. Everyone, as I said before, everyone fucked up in the team, it's not just about one person.

Are you proud of the team being able to do so well here, especially considering you missed out on the Major? I think a lot of teams would have struggled.

I think the coaching staff are helping us a lot, especially wasiNk. On the mental side he is really helping us to be strong, and it's so fucking important in CS to have a strong mental. We have it actually.

Since we made the change, even if it was a really rough period, we are still fighting and still working, still trusting everyone, not blaming… trying not to blame everyone. Of course sometimes it is the case, but we know we have to focus on ourselves. If we are better individually, the team will be better. The coaching staff are working, the IGL is working, so we just have to work and trust the process.
