Cologne Major race update: B8 surge after PCC sign-up, Legacy all but confirm spot after EPL run

Less than four weeks remain before the April 6 cut-off date for invites to the IEM Cologne Major, and the race at the bottom end of the list is as tight as ever.

Cologne Major race update: BIG on the rise, Liquid and NIP in danger

The conclusion of Stage 2 of ESL Pro League Season 23 and Parken Challenger Championship Season 2 had a limited impact on the standings in isolation, but the fallout from some teams' results — like Liquid and FaZe's early exits — has marked a noticeable shift as several notable names have signed up for regional LANs ahead of the cut-off.

B8 surge back into contention with PCC S3 sign-up

B8 currently sit outside of the invite range on our predicted list, and with only BLAST Open Rotterdam on their calendar, had just a 16.7% chance of making the Major according to Finn "Mischief" Farrer's prediction on March 8.

Mischief had included the caveat that B8's chances, and those of teams around them, would change dramatically if the Ukrainian organization signed up for any of the numerous LANs scheduled ahead of the cut-off.

That has become a reality with B8 signing up for Parken Challenger Championship Season 3 this week — deemed by Mischief as "their optimal choice" — to boost their chance of making the Major to 61.7%.

FaZe, 3DMAX, Liquid, and BIG all in the fight

As predicted, B8's sign-up has had an immense knock-on effect among the bottom end of the invite list, which now has FaZe, 3DMAX, Liquid, and BIG in a tight battle for the final two spots.

FaZe are in a similar position to where B8 was last week as they have yet to confirm their attendance at a LAN, but will see their stock rise once they do. They were among the teams registered for BC.Game Masters Championship, but ended up declining the spot.

Liquid will endure a gauntlet as they look to climb back up the ranking ahead of the cut-off, with their current schedule seeing them compete almost back-to-back at four LANs in a row. The first of those is Roman Imperium Cup VI, where the team will field Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius as a stand-in for Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz, followed by BLAST Open Rotterdam, DraculaN Season 6, and WINAMAX Trophy.

BIG suffered a slight blow after missing out on qualifying for Stake Ranked Episode 1, but didn't see their odds affected too much as they benefitted from teams losing in Stage 2 of EPL and are still slated to attend BC.Game Masters Championship and Roman Imperium Cup VII.

Legacy all but confirm Major spot with EPL run

Legacy's run to the LAN finals of ESL Pro League Season 23, in their debut with Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan no less, has almost guaranteed their Major appearance. The Brazilian organization already had favorable odds at 82.2%, but rose to 97.6% after securing their berth to the Annexet.

9z, too, have all but cemented their place after back-to-back trophy lifts at BetBoom Rush B Summit Season 2 and BetBoom Circuit X Pantanal Cuiabá, and the latter event was key in influencing positions for a few other teams.

Sharks' third-place finish has put them firmly in invite territory, while Marsborne's surprise run to the grand final saw their chances go from 2% to 27.1% in Mischief's latest update.

M80 and NRG are also mostly safe, and instead it is Passion UA who might have some worry creeping in after dipping out of the predicted invite list. However, the newly global-offensive team has three LANs on the docket ahead of the cut-off and plenty of chances to accrue points before invites are sent out.

Important events this week

The month-long race for the Cologne Major will kick off in earnest this week with the first of several influential LANs. The LAN finals of ESL Pro League Season 23 will begin on Friday, but will mostly serve to influence the opponent network among competitors as all eight teams are almost guaranteed Major invites.

Roman Imperium Cup VI will be the biggest one to keep an eye on with a wealth of notable names in attendance, including Liquid, BC.Game, Ninjas in Pyjamas, and M80. Both Marco "⁠Snappi⁠" Pfeiffer and Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek's troops have a lot riding on the next few weeks, with the latter sitting on a 21.5% chance of qualifying for the Major, according to Mischief.

LanDaLan organizers MPKBK are also hosting a $20,000 LAN in Moscow, Russia from March 9-12, an event which could provide a crucial boon to K27's 2.4% chance of qualifying.
