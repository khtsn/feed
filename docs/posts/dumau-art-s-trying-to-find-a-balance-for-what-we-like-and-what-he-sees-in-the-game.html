Following his team's win over M80, Legacy rifler Eduardo "⁠dumau⁠" Wolkmer chatted with HLTV regarding arT's stylistic adjustments to the team, their upcoming series against Astralis, and their plans building up to the IEM Cologne Major.

Here with dumau after a very straightforward win over M80; how are you feeling?

I'm feeling good with this win.

You guys had a really tough outing at IEM Rio, about a month ago. What was the difficulty there, and why do you think things are looking better here to start?

I think it's a good start as a team. After we lost in Rio, we could doubt more, not only being on the same page, but outside of the game, I think we are more for each other. Now, we have more accountability, and we have confidence in each other, more confidence, so whatever we do in the game, or whatever we do outside, we just buy the idea from each other.

About a month into arT's time on the team, saadzin said in an interview that he felt that arT hadn't changed that much of the team's structure. We're a bit later into arT's tenure, so do you agree with that statement, or do you feel the system has changed quite a bit?

To be honest, now in CS, things have to change at some point. If you don't change, you're gonna be a tier-two team, and you're going to be stuck. I think changes are coming, but he's doing it in ways that are like, he can see how you like more of this, then okay, we can do this. So he's trying to find a balance, for what we like and what he sees in the game and his vision, and he tries to show his vision, and we can complement each other. But this requires more conversations and talks.

You and him are both very aggressive players. How have you balanced that? Have there been any difficulties for you?

No, he says, "I was a dog but now I'm a support." But I think now, because of maps and positions, sometimes I support him more. We played for a long time before, when I was 12 years old, so we have more chemistry. So I can see him, follow him, and complement him, because I know what he likes, the nades and stuff, we talk before the game, so I know what he needs. When he looks at me, I know he knows what you complement from me. For us, this part is easy. We just have chemistry, and we can play around.

You guys play Astralis next; you played them at EPL and it wasn't too big of an issue for you. How do you feel about that matchup?

Astralis are a good team, and I think this is going to be a good match. It's always a good fight against them. We look to win as we would look [to win] against any team.

You're headed to China for CAC after this, and then have a bit of a break before the Major. What does the pre-Major plan look like?

We have a bootcamp for a week, but I think for now, game by game, we have to do what's in our minds, have confidence in each other, and just focus game by game. Try to win every day, as much as possible. I think the rest is going to be a consequence of what we are doing today. We have to have too much energy, and be ready for every game, every day. That's it; nothing much to think about, just things to do now and focus on.
