JamYoung powers TYLOO to victory over Sharks in elimination match

JamYoung was a force to be reckoned with in the deciding map

TYLOO beat Sharks in the IEM Cologne Major 1-2 pool in the decider map after each team won their map comfortably. The Chinese squad move on to the final 2-2 pool, then, while the Brazilians' bid has come to an end.

Yi "⁠JamYoung⁠" Yang was the player of the series, with a 1.87 rating and 7.99% round swing, but he was particularly valuable to his team with a monster performance on Anubis, the decider, where his heroics earned him a 2.37 rating in the do-or-die map.

The series kicked off on Nuke, Sharks' pick, where the Brazilians got off to a quick start on the attacking side as #18 prospect João "⁠koala⁠" Pfeffer downed four with his AK on the fourth round before TYLOO put their first on the board.

From there, the Victor "⁠gafolo⁠" Andrade-led squad kept marching on to post a great eight rounds on the T side, more than enough leeway after the switch as they gave up just two rounds on the way to a decisive 13-6.

koala dualies ace

Just as Sharks dominated their Nuke pick, TYLOO did the same on Inferno. Despite losing the first pistol round to an ace by koala and going down 0-2 on the T side, they rallied the second they had weapons in hand to an 8-4 lead at the half.

After the switch, the Chinese side once again dropped the pistol round, but after securing the forcebuy went on to take five in a row to a 13-5 victory.

JamYoung triple in 2v3

It all boiled down to Anubis, where we finally got to see a competitive half as TYLOO rallied from a 1-5 deficit to a 6-6 half with JamYoung running rampant on the T side with 15 kills and a 111.4 ADR.

After the switch, Máximo "⁠maxxkor⁠" Cortina had a beautiful sequence with the P250 to break the stalemate, and Sharks took a three-round lead before TYLOO called a timeout.

JamYoung didn't slow down after the switch and once again led the charge for TYLOO, securing a 2.37 rating and +11.29% round swing on the map as he and his teammates stood strong to take the 13-10 victory.
