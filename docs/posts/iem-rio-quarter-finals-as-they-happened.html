IEM Rio quarter-finals as they happened

On his reaction to the loss: They're showing why they're probably the best team we've ever seen in the history of CS. It's not easy, you go into your map pick, and ZywOo has 30 kills and five deaths. It felt like they were playing with us and it was not very serious.

We started both games slowly. We won the CT pistol on Dust2. Maybe if we had won the first gun round, we would have made the map competitive, but it didn't feel like that today. The maps didn't matter. They showed up as better individuals and better as team. We played a worse game than in Rotterdam, and even that was 3-0.

On the positives from the event: Whenever we play any other team, it seems we can always fight and we fully believe we can win. But you're not going to be happy if you're coming in second. I told everyone, 'We're not happy even if we make it to the finals and lose to Vitality.' This time, we met them in the quarter-finals and we lost to them. If we're not able to beat them, we+re not deserving that trophy up there. I'm happy that we managed to play against them, but after getting whooped like that, it's hard to put it into words yet.
