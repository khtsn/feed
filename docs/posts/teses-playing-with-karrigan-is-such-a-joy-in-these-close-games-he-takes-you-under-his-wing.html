TeSeS: "Playing with karrigan is such a joy in these close games; he takes you under his wing"

Falcons booked their spot in the semi-final stage of PGL Astana 2026 following a 2-1 win against FURIA in a hectic series with five overtimes across Nuke and Dust2.

Speaking to HLTV after the match, René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen, who posted a 1.25 series rating, weighed in on the intensity that Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen brings when games enter crunch time.

"He brings this team atmosphere completely on another level, and I'm just really happy to have him here," TeSeS said.

"Playing with him is such a joy in these close games because he kinda just takes you under his wing, and every time someone does something crazy, he's hyping everyone up. It's amazing to play with him."

With FURIA out of the way, Falcons will now turn their attention to Saturday's semifinal clash with magic, the tournament's surprise package. Despite being heavy favourites, TeSeS insisted they would not be taking magic lightly.

"I have no big expectations, but you can never disrespect someone or anything like this," he said. "The most important for us right now is to get home, get the best rest we can, and prepare as we always do."

Congratulations. That was a marathon of a series, so just tell me what's on your mind after that because there were just too many rounds to go through one by one.

This was such an amazing game, and well played to FURIA. These are the games that we always play against them, it's always freakin' close, and I'm just happy that we came out on top once again. Crazy crowd, it was fun to play in there, and it was one of the most fun games in a while with this team. I really, really enjoyed that.

Nuke was crazy, a lot of clutches for both you and them; m0NESY was going nuts. Talk me through that map a bit.

Yeah, Nuke was (laughs) extremely close as well, and suddenly m0NESY started turning on some kind of entry button on T side where he just started going for every peek possible and he kind of succeeded with it, and that opened up the whole T side for us. CT, I'm very proud of myself for closing some rounds and doing some good multi-kills. I've been in a bit of a down period where I struggled to perform really well, but at least very happy on that map.

You also started Dust2 really strongly. How do you feel like you're fitting into this roster now? You've had a couple of role changes, so it has been a bit of a hard period for you, right?

Yeah, a very hard period with a lot of transitions on different maps. Nuke is one of those where I haven't changed, and I usually feel pretty comfortable. On Dust2, I'm playing B for the first time ever, and I'm really clueless sometimes, getting caught like I never should be as a normal player. But yeah, there are some maps I definitely have to work on-

Mirage you're on A now as well.

Yeah, Mirage also, for example. So yeah, this is something that I have to really improve on as fast as possible, and it's something I'm working on.

As you mentioned, it's always really close games against FURIA. You had beaten them three times in a row, this was the fourth, but something was different this time around as you had karrigan. What was the difference on the server, did you feel some karrigan magic perhaps?

(Laughs) Just the way he brings energy is absolutely insane, and it's so enjoyable to play with him. He brings this team atmosphere completely on another level, and I'm just really happy to have him here. Playing with him is such a joy in these close games because he kinda just takes you under his wing, and every time someone does something crazy, he's hyping everyone up. It's amazing to play with him.

He mentioned that you're sometimes a little too selfless (TeSeS laughs), and he wants you to be more selfish. How much has he been pushing towards that?

I wouldn't say he has pushed me so much to it (laughs). We kind of set roles from the beginning of the team and how karrigan sees the different roles on the team, and the roles are that NiKo kind of needs to be the main closer of the team. And when there are any mid-round situations where it is a bit hard and I'm in the pack, and we have to go into a site, then I'm running first as you probably could see in this tournament. Today, it succeeded a little bit better than in previous games, but yeah, this is what we're working with. At the same time, some rounds I have to be really selfish, and some rounds I have to be completely not selfish, so it's a hard balance and it's something that will get better over time.
