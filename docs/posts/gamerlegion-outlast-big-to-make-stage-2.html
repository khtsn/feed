GamerLegion outlast BIG to make Stage 2

Avoiding a near-crumble on Overpass and a crucial force-buy on Ancient turned the tide in a tight two-map series.

GamerLegion have secured an IEM Cologne Major Stage 2 berth after a hard-fought 2-0 victory over BIG in the 2-1 pool (16-14 Overpass, 13-11 Ancient).

BIG had their chances in the series, and will lament losing their Overpass pick after squandering several late-round situations early in the half. GamerLegion clawed back retake after retake to jump out to a 6-0 lead, and though BIG won four back to get their foot in the door, they were quickly left facing a mountain to climb with GamerLegion winning the last two and converting an unorthodox Short stack in the second pistol to lead 10-4.

Multi-kills from Gleb "⁠gr1ks⁠" Gazin and Benjamin "⁠blameF⁠" Bremer started to turn the tide in the second half, and BIG pulled off a miraculous 2v4 smoke defuse to further twist the screws in an eight-round spree that saw them hit map point first. But GamerLegion came alive at the eleventh hour with two final rounds to force overtime, and recovered another 3v5 there to help clinch a 16-14 victory.

That momentum carried into Ancient, where GamerLegion turned another 3v5 and Janusz "⁠Snax⁠" Pogorzelski won a 1v3 en route to a 4-0 start. Josef "⁠faveN⁠" Baumann, who struggled immensely on Overpass (9-23 K-D, 0.49 rating), came alive on Ancient with several high-impact multi-kills to usher in another recovery for BIG to take a slim 7-5 lead at the break.

A Dualies quad-kill from Sebastian "⁠Tauson⁠" Tauson Lindelof and the conversion let GamerLegion tie the game after the swap, but BIG soon regained control and started closing in on forcing a decider, even making up for giving away a 1v2 to Snax after he went unchecked in Cave.

But the turning point came when GamerLegion won a crucial force-buy with mostly pistols in hand at 8-10, hitting a few stellar headshots against overeager peeks that ultimately gave them the runway needed to clinch a 13-11 win and earn a Stage 2 berth.
