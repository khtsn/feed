NAVI take down FaZe to reach Rivals grand final

Natus Vincere are headed to the grand finals of BLAST Rivals 2026 Season 1, besting FaZe in their semi-final matchup after a one-sided victory on Dust2 (13-5) before eking out an Ancient victory (13-11) after stifling an impressive comeback effort by FaZe.

"They were just together, trading us, and we ended up completely failing the clutch situations," Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen said regarding his team's near choke on Ancient. "I think they just got control, and we were a bit annoyed because we don't usually lose this way; we have decent protocols. They caught us off guard at first, but I'm glad we came back."

Natus Vincere are now once again in the grand final of a top event, having earned grand final berths at both ESL Pro League Season 23 and BLAST Open Rotterdam, before being consigned to just the quarter-finals of IEM Rio. Natus Vincere are likely to face off against Vitality in the best-of-five final, contingent on Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's troops not succumbing to an upset against GamerLegion in their own semi-final match.

"I believe for sure [we can win]," Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri shared regarding the prospect of a grand-final series against Vitality. "Last time we played, they fucked me hard. I didn't even have a chance to kill them, so tomorrow if we play them, I will make sure that I will do the same stuff that they did to me, I will do it to them."

FaZe opted for a different approach to veto this series as compared to their group-stage meeting with NAVI, selecting Dust2 as their map of choice as opposed to Anubis as in their previous matchup. This pick worked out well for FaZe in the beginning, as Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken's men stifled a 1v3 attempt from Valeriy "⁠b1t⁠" Vakhovskiy to take the pistol round and an early 4-1 lead along with it.

Despite the early setback, Natus Vincere remained undeterred; Aleksib's troops rallied in the latter half of their CT side once their economy stabilized, claiming the lead with a 7-5 scoreline thanks to the efforts of Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov and Aleksib on the A site. Some crisp shots from w0nderful helped NAVI clinch the pistol round, which gave them all the momentum they needed to wrap up their opponents pick 13-5, going flawless with a six-round run on their T side.

Ancient looked like it would be a quick endeavor; Natus Vincere stormed ahead to an early 5-0 start on the offense, fueled by back-to-back-to-back multikills from makazze. FaZe eventually managed to break up the streak with two rounds of their own, but NAVI still ultimately came out of the half of the victors, capping off their T side with a stunning 1v4 from w0nderful.

Starting down a 3-9 deficit, FaZe manage to compose themselves, with Helvijs "⁠broky⁠" Saukants giving the team a lifeline off the back of a 4k 1v3 in the second pistol round. FaZe managed to convert off this pistol, and although NAVI looked primed to shut down FaZe's streak with a triple kill from makazze in the first gun round, Twistzz answered back with a critical 1v3 to keep his team's momentum alive.

Although FaZe briefly managed to eclipse Natus Vincere to take a 10-9 lead, the latter side found their footing once their economy stabilized. Natus Vincere saved themselves from an overtime fiasco by winning out four of the final five rounds, clinching their map pick 13-11 and the series 2-0.
