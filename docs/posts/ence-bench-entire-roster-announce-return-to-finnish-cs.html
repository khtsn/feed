ENCE bench entire roster; announce return to Finnish CS

ENCE have announced the benching of their entire CS2 roster, with all players and coaching staff available for transfer as ENCE refocuses on Finnish CS.

"From the beginning, ENCE has aimed to build a competitive and sustainable esports organization. Over the past years, we have invested heavily in competing internationally and building rosters capable of reaching the highest levels of Counter-Strike," ENCE's statement reads.

"Unfortunately, in the current esports ecosystem, this has become increasingly difficult for organizations like ours."

ENCE made history for their country by making the grand final at IEM Katowice 2019 with a Finnish roster led by Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen, but the team couldn't repeat that success after a period of instability following the removal of the Finnish IGL, and now aims to return to their roots.

"Our focus moving forward is clear: to rebuild a Counter-Strike team, where Finnish talent will be at the core, and to develop the next generation of Finnish stars."

In CS2, however, ENCE have only made one Major, namely PGL Copenhagen, and haven't appeared in the top 20 of the world rankings since the first half of 2024.

The organization also shared that Paavo "⁠podi⁠" Heiskanen is benched due to expressing his wish to pursue tier-one Counter-Strike instead of playing in a team of Finnish up-and-comers.
