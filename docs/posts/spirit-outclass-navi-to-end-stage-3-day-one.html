Spirit outclass NAVI to end Stage 3 day one

Spirit have moved into the 1-0 pool of IEM Cologne's Stage 3 with a statement victory against Natus Vincere in a one-sided series.

The Russians extended their Dust2 winning streak to seven games with a dominant 13-1 rout, then showed real progress on Anubis — a map they had only played twice in the past three months, both times with disastrous results — with a solid 13-8 victory.

NAVI were hoping to use this series to capitalize on the momentum gained after lifting the IEM Atlanta title three weeks ago. Instead, they have been brought back down to earth with an almighty bang after a humbling defeat that will ensure an element of doubt in the players' minds for the rest of the event.

Six maps into their IEM Cologne run, Spirit still have not let an opponent reach double digits.

Spirit had Dust2 by the throat from the initial exchanges as NAVI's players looked apathetic on the CT side, losing a 2v5 and a 3v5 as the first half ended with a lopsided 11-1 scoreline.

Any hope Natus Vincere still had of launching an improbable comeback quickly evaporated after the side switch as Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets picked up back-to-back triple frags to kill the game off.

More misery was to come on Anubis for NAVI, who saw their 2-0 lead disappear as Spirit pulled off 2v4 and 3v5 clutches. They showed some signs of life on the T side as they began to chip away at a 3-9 deficit, but Spirit held firm and closed out the map before things got troublesome.
