New York State Parks Worker Dies Fighting Wildfire in Orange County, N.Y.

New York|New York State Parks Worker Dies While Fighting Wildfire

https://www.nytimes.com/2024/11/10/nyregion/orange-county-fire-sterling-forest.html

The blaze in Orange County, N.Y., was the latest in a series of wildfires to plague the region.

Nov. 10, 2024Updated 3:10 p.m. ET

Officials said an 18-year-old employee of the New York State parks system died on Saturday while battling a wildfire in Orange County, one of several blazes in the New York metro area to break out recently amid drought conditions.

The employee, Dariel Vasquez, was killed while fighting a fire in Sterling Forest in Greenwood Lake, N.Y., the New York State Police said in a statement late Saturday.

Steve Neuhaus, the Orange County executive, said that Mr. Vasquez was helping to clear trees and shrubs, which are fuel for a fire, from a wooded area when a tree fell and hit him.

Mr. Vasquez, who was from Rockland County, had started working for the parks system in May, Mr. Neuhaus said. Mr. Vasquez’s job was not firefighting, but he had been brought in to help with the response.

Mr. Vasquez planned to begin college in January and had been the captain of the baseball team at Ramapo High School, according to an online fund-raiser that his family organized.

In a statement, Mr. Vasquez’s family said they would remember him as a “true hero,” and noted that he had died while protecting his community.

“His bravery and selflessness in those last moments were a reflection of his pure heart and unwavering commitment to others,” the statement said, which added that Mr. Vasquez had been born into a family known for community service and he “embraced this legacy wholeheartedly.”

“He was not only an admired figure in his community but a guiding light for his family,” the statement said.

Mr. Vasquez was responding to the Jennings Creek Fire, a blaze in Sterling Forest State Park, which is about 50 miles northwest of New York City.

The State Police statement said Mr. Vasquez was a parks and recreation aide for the State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation.

The parks office said in a statement on Sunday that an 18-year-old employee who worked in the Palisades region had died around 3:30 p.m. on Saturday while working as a wildland fire crew member, though it did not name Mr. Vasquez.

The statement said that a tree fell, injuring the teenager, and efforts to save him were unsuccessful.

“I commend his dedication to serving and protecting his fellow New Yorkers, and his bravery on the front lines,” Gov. Kathy Hochul said in a statement.

“New York is battling multiple wildfires due to the dry conditions we are currently facing,” Ms. Hochul said. “Our state employees are working around the clock to protect our communities and we are keeping them close in our thoughts as they put their lives on the line to stop the spread of these wildfires.”

The Jennings Creek fire began in Passaic County, N.J., and was 2,500 acres and zero percent contained as of Sunday morning, according to the New Jersey Forest Fire Service.

Rain is forecast for Sunday night and officials hoped it would help slow the growth of the fires.

“We’ve been going pretty much nonstop in the last 24 hours trying to put out different fires around the county,” Mr. Neuhaus said.

In New York, wildfires in New Jersey prompted a statewide smoke advisory on Friday, and a fire in Prospect Park in Brooklyn blanketed New York City in smoke on Saturday.
