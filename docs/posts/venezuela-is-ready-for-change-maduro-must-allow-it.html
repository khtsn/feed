Venezuela Is Ready for Change. Maduro Must Allow It.

Opinion|Venezuela Is Ready for Change. Maduro Must Allow It.

https://www.nytimes.com/2024/07/26/opinion/venezuela-elections-gonzalez-maduro.html

Guest Essay

July 26, 2024, 1:00 a.m. ET

By María Corina Machado

Ms. Machado, a former member of the National Assembly of Venezuela, is the leader of the country’s political opposition. She wrote from Caracas.

Venezuelans are putting everything on the line in Sunday’s election. Faced with President Nicolás Maduro’s intention to entrench himself in power for six more years, the democratic movement has steadily built a pathway for profound change around Edmundo González, our presidential candidate.

My compatriots are fed up with all the hatred, coercion, corruption and misery brought on by 25 years of tyranny and destructive economic policies under Mr. Maduro and his predecessor and mentor, Hugo Chávez. These are the reasons that roughly a quarter of our population has emigrated across the world, including hundreds of thousands to the United States.

An overwhelming majority of Venezuelans are ready for change. This sentiment is palpable throughout the country. Despite electricity and fuel shortages and relentless harassment from the Maduro regime, huge numbers of people are participating in our campaign events. Just a year ago, Venezuelans felt hopeless, migration through the Darién Gap began surging, and the opposition was leaderless.

Credible pollsters confirm our imminent victory. Even the most conservative estimates suggest that the opposition’s support is twice the incumbent’s. At least one poll indicates a margin of 47 percentage points.

The regime has made this election neither free nor fair nor truly competitive. Mr. Maduro has no viable way to win it honestly. He relies on sham elections that can be accepted as faits accomplis in and, in some cases, beyond Venezuela. While he may try to manipulate reality even past the election, his days in power are numbered.

We have organized the biggest citizen electoral observation network known so far in the country. Mr. Maduro will be defeated, and he must accept the Venezuelan people’s will.

We have publicly proposed negotiations with him and his circle to allow a peaceful transition of power. This is the best opportunity for the regime to reach a deal with us. Political guarantees are necessary to heal wounds and steer our nation back to democracy. In order to deal with the past, we need to take care of the future.

Because people are our priority, we will bring urgent reforms to tackle the social crisis and pave the way to prosperity.

Our first step will be to restore freedom, democracy and the rule of law. This starts with freedom of speech, a free media, independence of the branches of government and equal access to justice for all citizens.

Over time, we envision a smaller, more efficient government. We will re-establish international relations with all nations, particularly democratic countries and especially within the American hemisphere. We will aim for a professional, modern military to safeguard our sovereignty.

It is also crucial to address the humanitarian crisis that has left most Venezuelans living below the poverty line and an economy in deep stagnation. Our economic plan, which we call expansionary stabilization, will restore order and restart growth. The fiscal and monetary causes of inflation and currency devaluation will be tackled. The new government will enforce the protection of property rights and will develop a market economy.

We will also encourage the expansion of the economy with public and private investment in infrastructure and public services. This investment plan will create jobs and enhance productivity, helping to overcome poverty and expand the middle class.

Venezuela will emerge as the energy hub of the Americas. We will need to revamp oil production to finance the reconstruction of the country. At the same time, we will restore and develop our vast, clean hydroelectric energy sources to achieve the goal of zero net emissions and advance our transition to green energy.

The new government will reintegrate Venezuela into international markets; negotiate a financial and technical program with the International Monetary Fund, the Inter-American Development Bank and the World Bank; and restructure the country’s substantial public debt, worth almost 160 percent of our gross domestic product as of 2022.

This is the country we dream of. This is the nation we will build. It will not be easy and could take many years. But we believe it can be done, starting with the crucial civic act of voting and continuing with the indispensable political act of accepting the results.

Nobody can prevent our people from making possible a new beginning. This struggle is a journey of redemption. Our common aspiration is to bring our children back home, reunite our families and proudly live in our own country, Venezuela. This is the truth that will prevail.

María Corina Machado, a former member of Venezuela’s National Assembly, is the leader of the country’s political opposition.
