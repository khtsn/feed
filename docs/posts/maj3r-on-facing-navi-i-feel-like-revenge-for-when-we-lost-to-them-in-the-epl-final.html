MAJ3R on facing NAVI: "I feel like revenge for when we lost to them in the EPL final"

Aurora are on the hunt for their first trophy of the year, following up their playoff finishes at IEM Krakow and PGL Bucharest with a run all the way to the grand final of ESL Pro League, taking down Astralis to get there.

Not only do Aurora have the chance to bag a trophy, but also the chance to take revenge upon their grand final opponents Natus Vincere, who defeated the core of this Aurora squad in the grand final of ESL Pro League S20 back in 2024.

"I like playing against NAVI," Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli told HLTV in the wake of his team's win over Astralis. "I think it will be a great matchup and I'm very excited to play tomorrow."

With a battle for the ESL Pro League trophy on the horizon, MAJ3R spoke with HLTV to discuss his team's semi-final win, what it means to make a final with Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici, and his desire to take revenge upon Natus Vincere.

Listen to the interview, here.

What are your reactions after that series?

Very tired because I don't like playing late hours to be honest, but it is what it is. Tired and very happy to be in the final here, and also playing against NAVI. I feel like revenge from the last time in ESL Pro League when we lost to them in the final.

Nuke was a close one. It felt like you were in control of that map and probably could have won it, so how was it resetting after that one?

I think on Nuke we lost so many advantageous situations on CT side and on T side. Even in the last round on T side, they were eco and I called to go Ramp, and we just choked this round.

A lot of good situations we threw on Nuke, and what I said to my boys was 'guys, I feel that everyone is shooting very well, me included, so we just lost because they played more intelligently than us on Nuke.'

This is Nuke's strongest thing, if you play really well and rotate well, so on Dust2 and Inferno which are more shooting maps I really believed that we could beat them. After Dust2, where we beat them with a very large score, they felt it also, and I'm very happy that we closed it on Inferno, but it was a very close match.

On Inferno you seemed again like you were in control before Astralis started to come back into it. You must feel good that you showed resilience to get it over the line in the end?

Yeah. I really feel, from outside maybe you say the same, inside the server I had control the whole game. I feel like I had control on T side, we lost some rounds but we were playing way better I think.

On CT side after losing the pistol and the force, and doing some bad gambles in mid-rounds, it was a little difficult, but I am very happy that we found the solutions after that.

We had to force some fights B, in the pause we said to my coach we had to fight B, he said 'okay let's fight B.' I'm glad that it finished well, but tomorrow we have the final and we have to focus on that.

You made a change in the off-season and brought in soulfly. It must feel good that you've gotten to a final so soon after he joined the team?

Yeah because we never know when we do a change if it will be good or not, you know? But soulfly is a very intelligent player, he's learning fast, listening really well, and doing his best for the team.

He's sacrificing a lot. It's new for him, playing a lot on stage and playing a lot of important matches. On Nuke it was a little complicated today, but I believe that he will work more and I believe in him that he will be way better.

You have NAVI coming up, which like you said is a rematch of the Pro League final in 2024. How do you feel about that matchup?

I feel great, I like playing against NAVI. I think it will be a great matchup, and I'm very excited to play tomorrow.
