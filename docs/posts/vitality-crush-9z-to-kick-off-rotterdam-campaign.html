Vitality crush 9z to kick off Rotterdam campaign

"Just chilling," Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut said with a smile when asked about what he did during his break following Vitality's domineering 2-0 victory over 9z in their first match of BLAST Open Rotterdam.

It has been nearly a month since Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's team last played an official after lifting the trophy at PGL Cluj-Napoca, opting to skip ESL Pro League Season 23 due to its online stages, but there was no evidence of ring rust at all.

Vitality were dominant in an emphatic return to the server, beginning with a statement, all-headshot Five-SeveN triple by ZywOo to recover from a lost pistol. William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman added a quick 1v3 to deny 9z a force-buy in reply, Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool was a thorn in 9z's side all half long with well-timed aggression to go alongside his usual variety in spots, and ZywOo was his usual self (16-5 K-D, 134.9 ADR) as Vitality romped to a 10-2 lead.

apEX received a teasing call-out on broadcast for his 3-8 K-D after 9z responded to Vitality's pistol win with a force-buy, and the French captain seemingly took that personally as he clutched a 1v3 to complete a 4k to set his team on map point before they closed out.

apEX's impact continued on Nuke, another Tec-9 quad-kill spoiling 9z's early hopes while Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan repeatedly ran over the defenses. ZywOo was barely needed in what amounted to an 8-4 lead at the break, but he had his moment anyway as he ripped another second-round conversion away from 9z with a fantastic quad-kill in a 2v5.

That sealed the South American team's fate as flameZ continued to rack up multi-kills, ending the map with seven to his name and a 24-10 K-D as Vitality ensured a swift victory.
