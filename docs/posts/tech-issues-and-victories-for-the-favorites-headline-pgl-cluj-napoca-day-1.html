Tech issues and victories for the favorites headline PGL Cluj-Napoca day 1

The first day of play of PGL Cluj-Napoca 2025 has drawn to a close, seeing the end of the first set of matches in the Swiss group stage.

The Romanian tournament organizer's first event of the year did not get off without a hitch, however, as lengthy tech pauses and a messy crash from Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač at the end of G2's series against Vitality put a damper on the event's first round.

Tech issues abound

MOUZ's game against PARIVISION was the site of the first major tech issue of the event, as the gap between the third and fourth rounds of the first map of Dust2 was nearly an hour long. Though the lengthy pause may have been a detriment to PARIVISION's momentum in the first half, they found their footing in the second half to lock down their pick 13-7 before MOUZ clinched the remaining two maps of the series.

Similarly, FaZe's matchup against HEROIC also featured a lengthy tech pause, shortly after the start of the second map of Ancient. FaZe, who won the first map of the series in convincing 13-5 fashion, came out the victors on Ancient despite the technical issues, securing their map pick 13-9 and the series 2-0.

Later in the day, after nearly the entire series between Vitality and G2 had been played out, huNter- suffered a crash as Vitality were on series point. A review from the admins followed, as huNter-'s crash came nearly at the exact same time as the first damage in the round had been dealt by Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan; if huNter- had crashed after the damage, G2 would be unable to replay the round.

huNter- shows apEX the crash from his POV

Ultimately, the admins concluded that huNter- crashed after damage had been dealt, securing the map and series victory for Vitality.

B8's 16-year-old AWPer shows promise

Daniil "⁠s1zzi⁠" Vinnyk, B8's new 16-year-old sniper, who had just two games on HLTV to his name before today, showed some solid form in his debut with his new team against FURIA.

Despite the daunting matchup, s1zzi racked up a solid 1.19 rating on his team's pick of Mirage, topping the charts for his team before FURIA stole away the map with a hard-fought 13-11 scoreline.

Unfortunately for s1zzi, he was unable to keep his individual form up on FURIA's pick of Inferno, mustering occasional impact before FURIA marched ahead to a 13-10 map win and 2-0 series victory.

Favorites largely avoid upsets

Barring FUT's impressive victory over Aurora to kick off the event, the remaining matches of the day saw the favorites overcome their opposition in the first round of play.

FUT open PGL Cluj-Napoca with statement win over Aurora

While FUT clashed with Aurora, the other game that kickstarted day one was Natus Vincere's match against the new-look Astralis. Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen's troops came out on top in large part thanks to the efforts of Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan and Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov, who posted impressive 1.63 and 1.53 series ratings, respectively, to lead Natus Vincere to a 2-0 win.

Falcons' win over 3DMAX was similarly direct, as the international side bested their French opponents thanks to individual impact from Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač, who posted a stunning 2.10 rating on the first map of Nuke, and Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov, who followed that up with a 2.09 rating on Dust2, to clinch the 2-0 victory for their team.

The MongolZ's path to victory was not quite as straightforward, as Garidmagnai "⁠bLitz⁠" Byambasuren's troops suffered a 7-13 loss on their opponent's map choice of Nuke. The MongolZ were unfazed by this unfortunate start to the series, as young guns Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar and Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold stepped up to bat for the team in the remaining two maps.

mzinho led the charge on The MongolZ's pick of Mirage, accruing a whopping 2.43 rating as a result of his 16-3 K/D and +14.53% Swing, while cobrazera took the mantle on Dust2, one-upping his teammate with a 2.61 rating, a 25-3 K/D, 142.2 ADR, and +13.82% Swing. All told, The MongolZ dropped only three rounds in the final two maps of the series.
