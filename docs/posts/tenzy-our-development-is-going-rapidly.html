tenzy: "Our development is going rapidly"

magic and Nikita "⁠tenzy⁠" Kochenyuk were staring down the barrel of a 0-3 exit at PGL Astana just two days ago, but fought back to complete a surprise run to the Berys Arena.

Having started in the open qualifier for Astana and only signed Abdurakhim "⁠mo0N⁠" Issa just two months ago, the team now sits at number 24 on the VRS and has secured a payout of at least $80,000.

"Our development is going rapidly," tenzy tells HLTV. "For us it's a really big jump in VRS, and it's gonna give us a lot, in terms of both opportunities and experience."

The 21-year-old, the #23 prospect, has been a key part of their rise with a 1.20 rating since the team's formation. He even dropped 50 kills on K27 in the 0-2 elimination game.

Editor's Note: tenzy answered in Russian, and his answers were translated by HLTV staff.

Congratulations on qualifying for the playoffs! You beat Gentle Mates in your last match, what are your thoughts on the game?

This game was very hard in terms of morale preparation because it was a kind of challenge for our team. But as the game has shown, most likely we managed it.

You started the event 0-2, but beat HEROIC, K27, and now Gentle Mates to recover. What are your thoughts on your run overall?

Our performance shows that a new team can come back from 0-2 at an event and show really decent results. It's decent.

It's the first tier-one event for us, we're going to be playing, giving our all to the game

The team hasn’t been together for long, but has been slowly rising up the ranking with some decent results. What do you think about your development so far?

Our development is going rapidly, because this iteration of the team with a new sniper is two months old at best, I think. For us it's a really big jump in VRS, and it's gonna give us a lot, in terms of both opportunities and experience.

You are one of the players on our Prospects list, which highlights upcoming players. How do you feel about being on the list? Has it helped you in any way?

To be honest, I didn't really think about making the Prospects list, I just saw my nickname and was like "that's cool", and that's it. Wasn't really much of a reaction besides that.

You’re going to play in the arena, a big moment for you and the team. How do you feel about that?

It's the first tier-one event for us, we're going to be playing giving our all to the game, hopefully not feeling the pressure. Since it's our first playoffs and experience of that kind, so we just gotta enjoy it.

When you have a team like this that is so new, how does it feel to grind tier-two circuit and come up the way you up.

It's actually a really hard work that only pays off if you work all the time, constantly change something, face more problems that you solve... And that path is hard, but we've made a big jump in terms of our VRS, and we'll see how it's gonna go. It's going really good so far.

How has this opportunity been for you, playing at this kind of event? What it's been like?

It's a really great opportunity since that we've made it all the way from the open qualifier, and it's a great feeling.
