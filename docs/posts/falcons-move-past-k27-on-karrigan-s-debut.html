Falcons move past K27 on karrigan's debut

Falcons are through to the 1-0 Swiss pool at PGL Astana following a 2-0 victory against K27 in Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen's first match with the team.

Even with limited practice heading into the event, Falcons emerged victorious in just two maps, though it was not all smooth sailing. After a comfortable victory on Dust2, Falcons left it awkwardly late to secure Ancient, closing out the game only in the final round of regulation just when a third map seemed inevitable.

Falcons dictated the pace in the first half of Dust2 with a tight defense that was rarely breached. Daniil "⁠qw1nk1⁠" Kabilov was the only player on the Russian outfit that looked like he could make something happen, racking up 13 frags while the rest of his team combined for just 16.

Down 3-9, K27 hit back after the side switch thanks to a Dual Berettas quad-kill from Aleksey "⁠xeedo⁠" Mikulich, but the respite proved brief as Falcons went on a 4-0 run once they had money in the bank.

Falcons carried that momentum into Ancient, where they strolled to a 6-0 lead. Once again, qw1nk1 was a one-man show, putting up 16 frags on the CT side as K27 brought the deficit down to two rounds after capitalizing on some late mistakes from their opponents.

A quad-kill from Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin kick-started a dominant period for Falcons, but then the game shockingly got away from them. K27 restored parity on 11 rounds following a five-round streak before Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač came to his team's rescue in a 2v3 situation, with Falcons then comfortably winning the final round.
