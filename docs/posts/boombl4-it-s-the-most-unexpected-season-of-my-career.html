Boombl4: "It's the most unexpected season of my career"

BetBoom's surprise run at the IEM Cologne Major continues with their biggest scalp yet in Falcons, who fell by the wayside as the Russian squad came back from 4-11 behind on Nuke and closed it on Ancient.

For Kirill "⁠Boombl4⁠" Mikhailov, it has been just as big of a surprise as to anyone. With Timur "⁠FL4MUS⁠" Marev being slotted in and out of the roster as others struggled with visas from the beginning of their qualification campaign all the way to Stage 3, it's been the "most unexpected season" of his career.

"Fierce is trying to build a structure, but then you're playing with stand-ins and only four players can understand what he's telling," Boombl4 said. "But at LANs we are trying to make a plan for every match and we're just following the plan."

Up 2-0, BetBoom could be forgiven for thinking playoffs are all but a lock, but Boombl4 doesn't want to think that way.

"You can feel confident when you're beating great teams, but I don't wanna think about 'we should do it now 100%' because I don't wanna feel any pressure. Still, we're playing with a stand-in. If it goes 2-3, it's okay for us."

Congratulations, a crazy victory, especially on the first map of Nuke. From down 4-11 to 13-11, tell me about the secret to that.

It's no secret, bro. We were just following the plan. We got the first buyround and we had the energy like we want to try the comeback, that's all.

You've made a lot of surprises here already to be 2-0 in Stage 3 after going all the way from Stage 1, and the only team you've lost to were Spirit, who have destroyed everybody so far. You've come in in such a difficult scenario, how has this happened?

It's the most unexpected season in my career because we're playing with stand-ins, we go from the LAN tournaments to the Major with stand-ins. And two days before, we're playing with stand-ins at the Major again.

I don't know, we're just trying to practice every day. Fierce became our new coach and he's trying to build a structure, but then you're playing with stand-ins, only four players can understand what he's telling. But at LANs we are trying to make a plan for every match, we're just following the plan, and this is all.

What's the key to making that work? FL4MUS had to play one role, then d1Ledez comes back in and FL4MUS has to play another role. How do you make it all work in such a short notice?

It's the coach's opportunity. He's telling how he's thinking it will be working in the best way. I'm not doing anything about this.

Can you tell me about FL4MUS and how he's stepped up to this challenge, having to slot in and out and not even knowing he would play the Major?

Yeah, it's sometimes hard for him to take the new spots, but still, he's a really energetic guy with good aim. In CS2, like I said in some interviews, you should shoot well to be a professional tier-one player.

That's what he's doing.

That's what he's doing, that's all.

You have three chances to qualify for the playoffs. I'm sure this wasn't the situation you anticipated for yourself when you entered the Major, but after this run, you have to feel very confident that this is possible, right?

Yeah, of course. You can feel confident when you're beating great teams, but I don't wanna think about 'we should do it now 100%' because I don't wanna feel any pressure. Still, we're playing with a stand-in. If it goes 2-3, it's okay for us.
