B8 through to Stage 2 after outlasting M80

B8 have advanced to Stage 2 at IEM Cologne Major after a narrow 2-0 victory over M80 (13-11 Ancient, 22-20 Inferno).

Alexey "⁠alex666⁠" Yarmoshchuk's troops converted on their status as one of the 3-0 favorites in Stage 1, although their road was not without difficulty as they were pushed the distance by THUNDER dOWNUNDER in the 1-0 pool and again in both maps by M80.

"We were playing for fun in the end," alex666 said to Tres "⁠stunna⁠" Saranthus with a smile on his face after the game. "We had no stress, I was saying for maybe 15 rounds on the T side, 'let's do this round,' but we were still giving entries and other stuff and still didn't end how we wanted. But yeah, it was like... clash of trash (laughs)."

A hot start on their Ancient pick, which included an all-headshot AK-47 ace by Artem "⁠kensizor⁠" Kapran, saw B8 leading 5-1 on the offense, only for their lead to collapse as Fritz "⁠slaxz-⁠" Dietrich outdueled Danylo "⁠s1zzi⁠" Vinnyk round after round. That, combined with multi-kills by Michael "⁠Swisher⁠" Schmid and two clutches, let M80 pull ahead 7-5 at halftime, but B8 kicked into a higher gear after the swap.

A pistol win and punishment of M80 dropping a 5v3 saw the Ukrainian team romp to a 12-8 lead, and though a Josh "⁠JBa⁠" Barutt triple helped M80 covert a 3v5 of their own, their comeback was put to bed at the eleventh hour as B8 edged out a 13-11 win.

Inferno unfolded much the same, with a hot start by Swisher letting M80 go up early before B8 recovered to post five by the break. Dmytro "⁠esenthial⁠" Tsvir clutched a 1v3 to deny M80's force buy win after the swap and looked to be closing in on victory, but an overeager push through smoke in a 4v3 by alex666 enabled a JBa clutch that became the catalyst in M80's recovery.

The North Americans won five straight to sit upon map point and were moments from forcing a Dust2 decider, but two heroic triple kill holds on B by alex666 allowed his team to force overtime, where chaos ensued.

Mistakes aplenty from both teams saw them trade leads, and clutches again proved to be the difference-maker as, despite opening duels going overwhelmingly in favor of M80 (25-17), B8 won six of the eight 1vX situations. The key one came from s1zzi as he went aggressive without a bomb plant to win a 1v3 and deny M80 map point, and the 16-year-old was the one to get his team over the line in the end with another 1v2 in the final round.
