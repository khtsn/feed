The series began with Lotus and ZETA quickly made a statement in the first half. With hero performances out of Laz , crow , and TENNN , ZETA were unstoppable. Laz brought out the Chamber pick for the first time in the LCQ and the decision led to great success for his team. The half ended with a dominant 9-3 scoreline from ZETA.

A necessary pistol win came through for Secret but it wasn't enough to stop ZETA's impressive form. The comeback from Secret was quelled by a solid hold from Dep to secure the map 13-9 for ZETA. Laz's Chamber demolished Secret on Lotus. With 26 kills and 205 ADR, Laz was the MVP in the server in every regard.

Laz's Chamber is back

Fracture saw another surprise come through in the agent selection but this time from Team Secret. JessieVash locked in Killjoy for the first time in his entire Valorant career. While Laz possesses a storied history on Chamber, JessieVash has zero competitive experience on Killjoy.

ZETA looked convincing in the gun rounds after losing the pistol; Secret managed to stay in the game with a pairing of successful thrifty round wins late in the half. The score was tied at 6-6 coming into halftime.

A pistol round win for ZETA was followed by a bonus round gifted to the Japanese squad via an excellent double kill from Laz. The momentum was building for a ZETA win with Secret facing an 11-6 deficit before picking up their first round. ZETA's lead was too much for Secret as the map ended with a swift 13-7 ZETA victory.

ZETA brushed aside the Secret players to put themselves one best of five away from a spot at Champions. ZETA will play in the grand finals on Sunday, July 23.

Secret drops to the lower bracket finals and will face off against the victor of RRQ and Gen.G tomorrow.
