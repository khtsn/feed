Gen.G make playoffs after narrow series against FPX

Gen.G Gen.G Korea Unranked Munchkin Byeon Sang-beom (변상범) t3xture Kim Na-ra (김나라) Meteor Kim Tae-O (김태오) Lakia Kim Jong-min (김종민) Karon Kim Won-tae (김원태) are the first team to make the playoffs in Shanghai after narrowly edging out FunPlus Phoenix FunPlus Phoenix China Unranked Autumn Kale Dunne Life Qu Donghao (瞿东豪) AAAAY Zhang Yang (张杨) BerLIN Zhang Bolin (張柏霖) Lysoar Liang Youhao (梁优好) in a three-map affair.

The map veto resulted in Gen.G picking the first map, Lotus, followed by FPX picking Sunset, with Ascent as the decider.

FPX denied an impressive comeback from Gen.G on Lotus after leading 10-2 in the first half. Following a 1v2 clutch from Autumn in the third round, Life 's attacking Operator and slow defaults from FPX worked brilliantly to overcome Gen.G's setups.

Despite losing the second pistol, Gen.G managed to bring some momentum back into their favor by winning a force in the second round and taking the next eight rounds straight. A strong overall performance from Karon and a reinvigorated t3xture led to Gen.G forcing overtime. FPX started to pick up some defensive rounds once extra time came into play and closed out the map 16-14. Life dropped 30 and 281 ACS to continue a streak of good form from the Chinese domestic scene.

An excellent performance from Karon kept the map close.

Gen.G recovered the series with a convincing defense side on Sunset after an even 6-6 first half. Both pistols and conversions went the way of Gen.G to pave an easy route to Ascent. Life's numbers were not enough for FPX this time around as the individuals on Gen.G started to take the upper hand in duels. The opening kills also went heavily in the favor of the Koreans with every player recording a positive opening duel success rate.

Life continued to excel in the map loss.

Gen.G were fully in the driver's seat in the first half of Ascent with FPX's lack of a strong entry duelist once again hindering their attacking ability. FPX struggled even in the round wins and accrued just four rounds before halftime. A pistol win and an absurd round from BerLIN brought some energy back to FPX as they kept the score close at 10-8 and forced a Gen.G timeout. Back and forth rounds ensued and Gen.G's existing lead allowed them to narrowly take the map 13-11 and the series 2-1.

Meteor went off on Ascent.

Gen.G are the first team to make it out of the swiss stage in Shanghai, while FPX will have another chance in the 1-1 pool on Monday, May 27.
