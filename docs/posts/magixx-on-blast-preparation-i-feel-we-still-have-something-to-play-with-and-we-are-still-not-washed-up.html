magixx on BLAST preparation: "I feel we still have something to play with and we are still not washed up"

Spirit enter BLAST Open Rotterdam after a disappointing quarter-final exit at ESL Pro League S23, where they fell to Astralis in a one-sided series and wasted a really good chance for their first trophy under Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev's leadership.

"They fluked the win," the Russian IGL told HLTV about the game. "And phzy life-gamed, just to wake up the next day to play against Aurora and post one frag per eight rounds."

The result is no reason to wallow ahead of their next outing, where Spirit have been placed in group B and will face Liquid in their opener.

"The next tournament is right here, and if you get your shit together, you're playing the playoffs in Rotterdam," magixx says.

You're straight out of EPL into a new tournament. First off, I've got to ask you: what happened during that match against Astralis? Because you won your three games online — not the best opponents, but still convincing wins — and then you got into the match against Astralis, and it was a mess. It was like you were never in the game at all.

I'll start with the late game sucks. They fluked the win. And phzy life-gamed, just to wake up the next day to play against Aurora and post one frag per eight rounds. So just lucky for him.

They fluked the win. And phzy life-gamed, just to wake up the next day to play against Aurora and post one frag per eight rounds

I wanted to ask about the interview that tn1R gave us in Krakow, in which he said that, 'sometimes, the hardest opponent is ourselves.' Does it feel that way? Because sometimes, it feels like you get in your head too much and you're not playing your game at all.

I think it has a piece of truth because, you know, like, we have individuals, we have structure, we have strats, we have everything. And sometimes it might be about, like, mental.

Looking at that tournament as a whole and the bracket, it felt like it was a must-win for you. There was no Falcons, no Vitality. FURIA didn't even make it to the playoffs. When you look at the fact that you couldn't go beyond the quarter-finals, what kind of impact does it have on a team like yours?

It's definitely an unlucky turn of events, but you should not be thinking a lot of it because it has actually happened and you are not capable of doing anything with it, and you just need to get your shit together. The next tournament is right here, starting in Copenhagen. And if you get your shit together, you're playing the playoffs in Rotterdam. Just lock in and step up, everyone.

It's definitely an unlucky turn of events, but you should not be thinking a lot of it because it has actually happened and you are not capable of doing anything

When you look at the season so far, before EPL you were top-8 in Bounty and third in Krakow. How do you analyse this start of the season, considering the changes that happened, with you coming in as the IGL, zont1x coming back into the team, and tN1R having slightly different roles?

In terms of roles or whatever, we are doing well. We know what to do. At, EPL, we were 3-0 online and the first game… Going to LAN after the group stage, the first game is the most important, probably. Just to get into the stage, be ready instantly and play great.

If you look at results, the same thing happened [in Bounty and EPL]. A 3-0 online and a loss on LAN in the first game. But if you dive deeper into what happened, those are kind of different losses.

You're now three months into your stint as an IGL. What would you say have been the biggest challenges so far in the role?

Waking up early.

And when it comes to managing the team and getting the structure in place, and working with hally? Has that been more difficult than you expected?

There are plenty of difficult things, plenty of things I need to learn still. There have also been a few things I felt would be harder. Some things are easier than I expected. Some are harder. It's fine, I guess.

One of the biggest changes before EPL was that you bootcamp before the event, which is something you hadn't done in a while. Was that different in a way? Will you be doing that more often?

I think that this year, we might go for bootcamp multiple times, when we have enough time. Because it's actually a great and profitable activity. Going into this, we had some ideas, some goals set going into the bootcamp, which we should be working on, and we did a lot in there. So I feel that this is a great thing. We couldn't rate it because the last two years or so, we had never done any bootcamps. It was also a part of personal comfort for some people. At this time, I feel like it's easier for us to get to the bootcamp and, like, do whatever.

I think that this year, we might go for bootcamp multiple times, when we have enough time

You had almost no time to prepare for this tournament because you came straight from from EPL. With that in mind, what do you want to see from this tournament? Not just in terms of results, but, I guess, progress within the team?

Everything we have been preparing for was actually like for two tournaments, right? Because we knew that we were going to two events back-to back. We were just trying to play better. I feel we still have something to play with and we are still not washed up. We just have to play there. We have a lot of stuff ready. We have a couple of matches to review. There are a few games that we haven't watched yet, officials.

I feel we still have something to play with and we are still not washed up

With this material coming up, we will have some time in here before the game. We can prepare for the opponent and once we have played our first opponent, we will have an extra day. And this tournament is good because you have a one-day break between each match if you win. So you will have enough time to review the last game. You will have time to prepare.
