Falcons head to Cologne Major final over Spirit

Falcons have laid claim to their place in the grand final of the IEM Cologne Major, battling past Spirit in a hard-fought three-map series that saw overtimes on two separate maps.

With this win, Falcons have secured their first Major final appearance in the organization's history. The team already eclipsed their quarterfinal finish at the StarLadder Budapest Major with their win over Vitality in the quarterfinals. This will serve as the first Major grand final for Russian duo Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov and Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin, and the second, third, and sixth for René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen, Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač, and Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen, respectively.

"We just have to follow through what we're doing," Falcons captain karrigan shared on broadcast after the match. "This team is so good; i said when I joined, this team is special.

"I think we're showing how many times it looks bad, but we manage to come back. When we're getting the highest pressure you can get, yesterday from Vitality, today from Spirit, we can withstand that pressure. It just feels natural to be in these games."

"It's hard to believe that two months ago I wasn't supposed to be at the Cologne Major, and now I'm here in front of this amazing crowd and making entertaining games, and tomorrow there's a grand final."

This result also puts an end to Spirit's impressive Major run. The Russian squad marched through Stages 2 and 3 with flawless 3-0 records, despite the fact they had to employ Jab "⁠Jab Jabich⁠" Jabich in lieu of coach Sergey "⁠hally⁠" Shavaev.

"The last couple of months we have shown some glances of form that we might play well," Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev said after the game. "We're just getting better from what we had at the beginning of the year.

"To complete this season, I would say we definitely improved from what we had, and there's only a way up. See you in Singapore."

The Major's second semi-final kicked off on Falcons' pick of Anubis, where neither side was able to firmly establish themselves in the first half. Falcons grabbed an early lead thanks to a m0NESY triple kill in the pistol round, but the two sides ultimately traded streaks of rounds to end up with a 6-6 scoreline at the half.

Another individual highlight, this time a triple kill from kyousuke, netted Falcons the second pistol round, but Spirit wrested control on the CT side once their economy stabilized. A force-buy victory for Falcons made the game competitive, with a Dmitry "⁠sh1ro⁠" Sokolov clutch allowing Spirit to reach match point before Falcons forced extra rounds. Falcons roared ahead in overtime, stringing together the first three rounds, and although Spirit retorted with multikills from Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets and sh1ro, Falcons managed to close out their pick 16-14.

Falcons hit the ground running on Mirage, with a hot 8-1 start from karrigan helping the team quickly accrue a 4-0 lead on the defense. After a brief timeout, Spirit composed themselves for the remainder of the half, clambering to a 6-6 scoreline to wrap up their offense. Falcons bagged their fourth pistol round of the series to briefly reclaim the lead, but Spirit firmly took charge on their map pick once their economy stabilized, stringing together seven rounds in a row to lock it down 13-8.

Spirit finally managed to secure their first pistol round of the series to begin their CT side on the Dust2 decider, but Falcons were undeterred by this early deficit, responding with a trio of rounds. The two sides went back and forth in the first half, but Spirit ultimately came out on top 7-5 after a jaw-dropping 2v4 post-plant fumble from Falcons.

Despite the dismal ending to the first half, Falcons managed to bounce back, claiming their fifth pistol round of the series. A 1v2 from TeSeS allowed Falcons to take the reins in the map, but an anti-eco loss gave Spirit a lifeline. The Russians were able to force overtime, but Falcons did not relent, claiming four rounds in a row to lock down the map 16-12 and the series 2-1.
