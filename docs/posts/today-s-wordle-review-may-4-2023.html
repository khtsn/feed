Wordle 684 4/6

⬜⬜⬜⬜⬜ FORTH

I’ve never seen Star Wars and I have no interest in changing that (I know, I’m sorry). Pretty much the only thing I know about the franchise is that, on this day every year, my social media feeds are inundated with people saying “MAY the FOURTH be with you.” Unfortunately, FOURTH is a six-letter word, so my first guess for Wordle today had to be a misspelling: FORTH.

I thought maybe we would have a return to the controversial themed answers, like when the answer was FEAST on Thanksgiving Day — I’m in the camp that it was a cute addition to the game. Unfortunately for me, it seems that Wordle has decided to go in the complete opposite direction, as I see five gray squares.

It’s time to focus and leave my hopes of outsmarting the game behind. My second guess is CLAMP. I’ve guessed ten letters so far, and only two of them have been vowels. Certain people (I’m looking at you, ADIEU starters) will scoff at this strategy, but I am of the belief that consonants should be prioritized, especially when playing in hard mode. In my experience, if I know the consonants, I can figure out the rest of the word. But if I only know the vowel placement, there are seemingly countless consonant pairings that could complete the puzzle.

At this point, I’m pretty convinced that the word will end with a Y. I’m starting to be excited that this could be a fun word, which is my hope for every Wordle. My initial thought is PUPPY, which, while it would be incredibly fun, is a bit too risky for me because of the tripled letter. I settle on PESKY and am stumped when I see only a yellow P and a green Y. How could it not be PUPPY? I consider BUMPY and JUMPY before realizing that the M has already been eliminated.

Then it hits me. I give this Wordle five stars: What word could be more fun than GUPPY?

Today’s word is GUPPY. According to Webster’s New World College Dictionary, it’s a noun that refers to a species of brightly colored fish native to Barbados, Trinidad and Venezuela.
