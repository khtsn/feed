S0tF1k on academy reunion: "It felt like coming home to your own guys - like a grandma when your kids come back for summer"

Spirit are without their usual coach, Sergey "⁠hally⁠" Shavaev, for IEM Rio and PGL Astana due to health issues, with Dmitry "⁠S0tF1k⁠" Forostyanko taking his place.

hally to miss IEM Rio and PGL Astana

The 31-year-old is more than familiar with several of the team's players, having mentored and guided them throughout their formative years on Spirit Academy, and is happy to be reunited with them once more.

"It felt like coming home to your own guys — like a grandma when your kids come back for summer," he told HLTV after Spirit beat Liquid in their opener. "That kind of feeling."

S0tF1k's arrival, albeit as a stand-in coach, comes amidst a rut in form for the Russian team, and he is using the time to help Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev's in-game leader transition and identify issues the team has been struggling to correct.

"I believe there was a lot of problems when I came in. Or not a lot, there were rather some specific problems when I joined," he said. "I needed to focus on one or two, and I tried to do that, because fixing everything all at once is quite difficult. So we worked on some specific things, and we see some results coming out of it."

Editor's note:

Questions for this interview were translated into Russian with the help of Spirit's staff, and answers were translated by HLTV staff.

Congratulations on starting Rio with the win. You're coming in as stand-in coach while hally is not here. What was the experience like coming into this event?

Overall it's been good. I knew the guys, so it was comfortable to start working again and they therefore accepted me as one of their own. It's an interesting experience. Tier one team — it's cool. Something like that.

They've become much, much stronger [individually], much stronger mentally. They understand me much better than when I was coaching them

Speaking of the Spirit system, obviously you've worked with a lot of these players through the academy. How has being reunited with them been?

It felt like coming home to your own guys — like a grandma when your kids come back for summer. That kind of feeling.

Have you noticed much change from the players from when you were coaching them to now?

Yes, definitely. A lot of changes. They've become much, much stronger [individually], much stronger mentally. They understand me much better than when I was coaching them. A lot has changed. But CS has changed too.

A big part of this period is also magixx taking over IGLing. Have you done anything to help him with that?

Yeah, I've helped him a bit. I have experience as a captain myself, so I share that experience with him — giving advice on how to better lead the guys, where to guide them more. We've done specific work together focused on communication and on how to work with people inside the team and within the system.

There were some specific problems when I joined. I needed to focus on one or two, and I tried to do that, because fixing everything all at once is quite difficult

It's been a rough period of results for the team. Can you talk to me about that, and how you're trying to help with that, even though it's relatively short-term right now?

I believe there was a lot of problems when I came in. Or not a lot, there were rather some specific problems when I joined. I needed to focus on one or two, and I tried to do that, because fixing everything all at once is quite difficult. So we worked on some specific things, and we see some results coming out of it.

Is there anything you can share about what some of those things might be?

Communication, primarily. Communication and our game after trades, those are the most important.

A while ago you did a long interview where you said that donk and zont1x have learned to play "the S0tf1k way". Do you feel that's still the case?

Yes, I still think so.

You're here for Astana and Rio. What are you hoping to accomplish with the team in this short period?

We just play. Play until we win.

Going into tomorrow, anything changing based on what you saw from today's match?

Yes, we need to change our approach. Liquid have a lot of problems right now and it shows that there's a lot they need to work on in their game. The next opponent will be obviously stronger, so we'll see.
