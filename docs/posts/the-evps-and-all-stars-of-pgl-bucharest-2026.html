The EVPs and All-Stars of PGL Bucharest 2026

The close MVP races keep coming thick and fast in 2026. This time, Nikita "⁠cmtry⁠" Samolotov outdid Džiugas "⁠dziugss⁠" Steponavičius on the basis of his performance in map wins overall and in the grand final.

The medal represents a stunning turnaround for an AWPer who started the year with a 0.94 rating in January. Since then, his form has picked up with a 1.12 at Cluj-Napoca and now a 1.24 en route to the MVP in Bucharest.

In a team where the riflers are the stars — cmtry had the second-best flash assists of anyone in Bucharest —, cmtry is happy to focus on his late-round role and deliver for the team.

But he also delivered when required, with three crucial peaks during the playoffs in the overtime against B8 and the first two maps of the final.

As always with these razor-tight MVPs, he and the top EVP will receive the same credit for the top 20, but there is only one medal to hand out and cmtry took it home.

Radar Chart stat glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • Swing in RW Δ: round swing in round wins

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Džiugas "⁠dziugss⁠" Steponavičius

dziugss was FUT's highest-rated player in Romania, delivering massive output from his anchor-lurker roles.

His highlight was the map three defeat to Astralis in the final, where he was badly let down by his teammates as FUT fumbled a 10-4 lead with dziugss on a 1.54 rating.

He was also great in the quarter-final against B8, with a 1.59 rating POTM on Overpass, which helped him to a 1.28 playoff rating overall.

Already HLTV's #2 prospect, we expect we'll be hearing a lot more about dziugss in the years to come.

Jakob "⁠jabbi⁠" Nygaard

jabbi was the MVP leader before the final, with a 1.31 rating going into the game against FUT.

Every opponent Astralis played in Bucharest made the playoffs, which makes his 1.41 rating in map wins en route to second place even more impressive.

The medal slipped out of his grasp after three bad maps in the final itself, but the tournament is a reminder of jabbi's quality only a few months after being restored to rotator roles on CT side, where he had a 1.44 event rating.

Dmytro "⁠dem0n⁠" Myroshnychenko

Despite a quiet group stage compared to cmtry and dziugss, you can argue that dem0n was right up there in the MVP race after a 1.30 in FUT's eight maps against top 10 teams.

Highlight maps included a 1.62 against PARIVISION, 1.52 against The MongolZ, and a career-best 2.57 against Astralis.

But it was a case of blowing hot and cold: dem0n was top of the scoreboard in 27% of maps, but below a 1.00 rating in 47%, which kept him below the three main MVP candidates.

Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea

lauNX's great record on home soil continued this week, as he won his fifth LAN title in his native country.

He was vital along the way, too, with big peaks against PARIVISION, The MongolZ and Astralis underlining his impact.

After map two of the final, where he hit a 1.84 rating, lauNX was well in the MVP race, which shows just how close it was between the top performers in FUT's team effort.

Having been brilliant in Cluj-Napoca's group stage too, lauNX is finally coming good nearly four years after his Major debut with Sprout at the Rio Major.

Victor "⁠Staehr⁠" Staehr

Staehr slides in just below lauNX, having averaged a 1.29 rating for Astralis' run to the semi-finals.

That spell included three huge peaks, all on Ancient, against B8, The MongolZ, and EYEBALLERS.

He went quiet afterwards, which kept him low on this list, but he did turn up with 31 frags against FUT on Nuke to complete Astralis' comeback in the grand final.

Staehr also topped his team in round win output (1.06 KPRW, 1.02.8 ADRW), which helped him cement a third career EVP.

Love "⁠phzy⁠" Smidebrant

phzy's poor final tanked his overall event rating, but as always with EVP lists a bad finish does not remove all of the credit he earned for pushing his team forward beforehand.

phzy was impactful in seven of Astralis' map wins en route to the final, and was on a 1.17 rating going into that match against FUT.

The best moment was the 1.47-rated semi-final against 3DMAX, but phzy also showed up against EYEBALLERS (POTM on map three) and was brilliant for the first four map wins against MIBR and B8.

Given all of Astralis' opponents made the playoffs, there was enough in phzy's run before the final to make him an EVP.

Ivan "⁠zweih⁠" Gogin

zweih's second EVP as a PARIVISION player comes despite the team's quarter-final exit, as he stepped up even while the rest of the team's riflers floundered.

His 1.21 event rating was backed up by 1.01 KPRW, and he was rated in the top three on the scoreboard in 50% of his games.

After a strong group stage, a 2.15-rated peak (and good first half on the decider) against The MongolZ in playoffs then confirmed his EVP.

Dmytro "⁠esenthial⁠" Tsvir

esenthial recorded his best-ever LAN event in Bucharest, crossing the 1.20 rating mark for the first time.

To mark his improvement in 2026, he's now on a 1.13 LAN rating for the year despite continuing in supportive rifler roles.

His round win output was the second best of anyone in the playoffs, with 1.07 KPRW, and he averaged a 1.39 rating in B8's map wins (1.37 including the quarter-final elimination).

esenthial was also rated above the 1.00 mark in all but two maps, was top of the scoreboard in 36% of his 14 games, and did enough to claim the eighth and final EVP for the event.

Honorable mentions

Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija's fragging was impressive for FUT, with 0.94 KPRW from the in-game leader position, but he averaged a 1.04 rating against top 20 opponents, 0.10 below lauNX who was fourth in his team.

Third-place side The MongolZ leave without an EVP, though Usukhbayar "⁠910⁠" Banzragch came close despite some inconsistency and Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar was just behind him.

The same goes for 3DMAX; Lucas "⁠Lucky⁠" Chastang had a strong event, but his peaks came against weaker opposition, while Kévin "⁠misutaaa⁠" Rabier had a decent floor throughout the run without crossing the exceptionally valuable mark.

Klimentii "⁠kl1m⁠" Krivosheev had his best international event to date, but he and Felipe "⁠insani⁠" Yuji did not perform well enough in playoffs to match esenthial and zweih's quarter-final EVPs.

In the group stage, Matias "⁠Banjo⁠" Kivistö, Jadan "⁠HexT⁠" Postma, and Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto stood out, with Denis "⁠electroNic⁠" Sharipov also having some good moments.

And it would be remiss of us not to mention Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen's 1.34-rated 2-map farewell to FaZe.

All-Star

FUT's all-round team effort means we need to enforce the cap of three per team once again, to see who will join MVP candidates cmtry, dziugss, and jabbi in the best five.

Krabeni makes sense as our captain, given that he called FUT to a 63.2% T side win rate compared to Astralis' 42.1%.

That then leaves room for Staehr over lauNX as the team's opener for a 3-2 split between the finalists.

Multi-award winners

A medium event like PGL Bucharest won't massively move the needle for the top 20, but phzy and zweih join the list of multi-award winners.
