Eternal Fire and Vitality move forward in the upper bracket and both stand one win away from Masters London.

The first match of the day pitted the Group Alpha third seed in Heretics versus the Group Omega second seed in Eternal Fire. Coming into this match, Heretics were on the rise with three consecutive wins after a poor start to the stage, while Eternal Fire had a convincing 4-1 group stage with their only loss being a close one to the undefeated FNATIC FNATIC Europe Rank #2 crashies Austin Roberts Boaster Jake Howlett kaajak Kajetan Haremski CyvOph Clement Millard Veqaj Sylvain Pattyn Alfajer Emir Ali Beder .

The match began on Heretics' pick of Fracture, which they were 2-0 on in the group stage. They continued to look confident on the map versus Eternal Fire, pulling away after a 3-3 start to an 8-4 defensive half with proactive plays towards the south side of the map.

Eternal Fire had an opportunity to get back into the game with a bonus round, but an audaz Bandit ace was denied by a benjyfishy 1v3 to keep the game in Heretics' control as they converted it to a 13-7 win. Wo0t and Boo were the top fraggers in the map, finishing with 22 kills and 21 kills respectively.

benjyfishy cleans up a 1v3 situation and denies the enemy ace.

Next up was Eternal Fire's pick of Pearl. It was a mirror matchup with both sides opting for Neon, Phoenix, and Cypher. Heretics won the first pistol but quickly lost their grip on the half after echo and audaz won a 2v5 in round four; from there, Eternal Fire overwhelmed Heretics with their aggressive, proactive playstyle and individual skill, running away to an 8-4 lead.

echo and audaz flip a 2v5 out of nowhere.

A second pistol cemented Eternal Fire's lead as they got up to 10-4—Heretics managed a few more rounds, but a Thrifty win for the Turkish squad in the final round concluded the map at 13-8. echo went on a tear with a 21/14/3 KDA and 268 ACS.

Lotus was the decider. Much like Pearl, Eternal Fire continued to suppress Heretics, winning the majority of individual duels on their attack side and forcing brawls on every side of the map to go up 8-4 at halftime. A double pistol win ensured the most dominant score of the entire match as Eternal Fire cleanly closed out the map 13-5.

Eternal Fire will return to the stage tomorrow, May 8 in an all-Turkish showdown against FUT Esports for a spot at Masters. Heretics, down to the lower bracket, will face BBL Esports on Saturday.

Liquid versus Vitality was a match between Group Alpha's second seed and Group Omega's third. Liquid had a fairly comfortable 4-1 group stage, losing only to FUT. On the other side, Vitality just scraped into playoffs after Eternal Fire defeated GIANTX at the end of week five to force a three-way tiebreaker.

Vitality chose to open the series on Pearl, where they fielded their more unorthodox KAY/O and Chamber look against Liquid's more meta Neon-Phoenix. The composition proved to work well for Vitality; they consistently got Chronicle on Chamber on a deep line on one side of the map, allowing the rest of the team to get in position for site holds and retakes on the other side. Vitality won twelve consecutive rounds after losing the first pistol for a 10-2 half and a 13-3 victory. Jamppi 's KAY/O flashes were on point as he finished with a 17/7/15 KDA, while Sayonara had an unreal performance with a 19/8/6 KDA and 222 ADR.

Sayonara closes out the 1v2.

For the second map, Liquid brought out the first Ascent after its return to the VCT map pool for playoffs. Both teams opted for nearly identical Neon-Phoenix compositions, with the only difference being Chronicle on Vyse versus nAts on Cypher.

Vitality started out in the driver's seat, winning the pistol and finding repeated success on the A site to go up 6-2. Liquid gradually began to grind the score back, bringing the half to 7-5.

Despite dropping the round 14 Thrifty after winning the pistol, Liquid's bounceback progressed into the second half. Vitality found themselves unable to put a stop to Liquid's consistent site hits through Mid and even an A hit through the Defender Side Spawn as Liquid marched back to a 10-10 draw and then an 12-10 lead. Vitality mustered a round to bring it to 12-11, but Liquid managed to close out Ascent in regulation 13-11.

Lotus was again the series decider. Vitality had another strong start, converting the first bonus round after punishing a deep push from the opponent for a 4-0 lead. The rest of the half got scrappy, but Vitality held fast to their lead thanks to multiple man disadvantage round wins including a 2v5 in round six, a 2v3 in round nine, and a 1v2 from Sayonara in round 12 to end up 8-4.

Sayonara was a beast all throughout Lotus.

After Liquid won the pistol, Vitality took four consecutive rounds to go up 12-6. Liquid fought to the end, but the deficit was too much to overcome; Vitality sealed the map 13-10 after a timeout. Sayonara outdid his Pearl performance with a wild 31/16/4 KDA and 228 ADR, going 61/41/19 with a 1.47 rating over the course of the series.

With this win, Vitality books themselves for a week two rematch versus FNATIC tomorrow for a place in London. Liquid will fight for survival against Gentle Mates on Saturday.
