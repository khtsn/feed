Falcons see off Vitality in Cologne to set up Spirit clash

Vitality's bid for a Major three-peat ended on Friday at the hands of Falcons, who picked up a hard-fought 2-1 victory in the quarter-finals of IEM Cologne.

The victory extends Falcons' winning run to four over Vitality, who haven't been successful against the Saudi organization's team since ESL Pro League Season 22. The previous time the two teams had faced each other was in the group stage of IEM Rio, Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski's final event with the team before making way for Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen.

Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin and Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov were the stars of the show in a series where Falcons were the better team for long spells. Vitality lacked their usual cutting edge and couldn't turn to Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut for inspiration as the French star turned in lackluster performances on Anubis and Dust2.

"We fought like crazy the whole time," Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač said after the match. "I'm super proud of the boys. We kept our composure throughout the whole time, even though we lost some rounds towards the end. We stayed calm, and we had some good reads towards the end. Huge relief right now."

The result means Falcons have bettered their previous best Major showing, a quarter-final run at the Budapest Major. All that stands between them and the grand final is Spirit, who were many's pre-tournament pick for the trophy,

Falcons got off to a blazing 5-0 start on the CT side of Anubis by capitalizing on a crisp quad-kill from kyousuke in the pistol round. Rémy "⁠XTQZZZ⁠" Quoniam called a timeout to try to slow the surge, and it worked to perfection as Vitality immediately found their footing on the T side.

The rest of the half was controlled by Vitality as Falcons struggled to get back into the game, even after calling two timeouts. With Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan and Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool leading the charge, they went on a 6-0 run before Falcons tied up the score.

Vitality carried that momentum into the second half and quickly hit double digits, winning the pistol round and the first full buy without breaking a sweat. Falcons then took the game by the scruff of the neck, stringing six rounds together, and held off a late comeback attempt by Vitality as kyousuke put the game to rest with a crisp 3K in the final round of regulation.

Falcons romped to a 2-0 lead on Inferno on the back of a team ace in the pistol round before Vitality began to take control. The reigning Major champions went on a dominant 6-1 run, with Falcons finding a late streak of three rounds thanks to multi-kills from kyousuke and René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen.

The back-and-forth trend continued in the second half as neither team seemed capable of gaining a stranglehold on proceedings. With hope fading after a period of Falcons dominance, ropz went nuclear with the MP9, and then ZywOo helped get his team across the finish line with multi-kills in the final stages.
