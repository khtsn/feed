IEM Cologne Major quarters as they happened

On his thoughts about the series: It was a huge fight. We knew that going up against Vitality, we couldn't expect an easy game. It's been a very long day. We fought like crazy the whole time. I'm super proud of the boys. We kept our composure throughout the whole time, even though we lost some rounds towards the end. We stayed calm, and we had some good reads towards the end. Huge relief right now.

On staying calm towards the end: Towards the end, we didn't have the best buy when we won the last round. I think two or three months ago, we would have probably lost more guns in these rounds and maybe even lost this game towards the end.

On winning four in a row vs Vitality: I don't think we're doing anything specific against them. I think maybe it's now into their heads that we're the only team that is making it very competitive against them and winning most of the time. I think maybe they respect us a lot and they tend to play a bit differently against us. But nothing crazy. Even in G2 times, with Ilya and me, the games were always very enjoyable and exciting. I'm just happy we won in the end.

With this win, Falcons set up a mouth-watering contest with Spirit in the semi-finals of the Major.

On his reaction to the series: "A tough match, a long one. In the end, you can say that the better team won. We had our chances, but we couldn't do it in the end. That's the part that is hard about it. To end the season in that way, losing a close match like this. It was a good fight by the guys. We have to be proud of that. There were many rounds… it requires resilience from the team, a lot of these close rounds. I'm happy about how they performed but sad with the result in the end. It is what it is."

On his reaction to the series: "This game was insane. It felt like we were playing CS:GO. Solo rounds, insane clutches, insane situations. It was really an amazing game."

On the tech pause: "I want to say thank you to the crowd. This was a technical pause. We just breathed and chilled. The tech pause was really good for us, it was a good moment to reset our mind."

On overcoming quarter-final curse during a broadcast interview: "It was kind of a mental block for the team. Today, it started very rough as well, but the most important thing is that everyone got themselves back into the game."

On improving the team's Overpass: "The meta is very CT-sided, so it's very hard to play against a team that knows how to play CT. Whoever wins pistol and controls the economy, can run away with the half."

On FalleN's impending retirement: "We are here for him, and he is here for us. He wanted to retire last year, but he sees the talent in the team. No one in the team has pressure because of that."
