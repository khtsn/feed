r3salt on not earning enough VRS points at XPL: "We'll keep slogging through the shit"

We caught up with Evgeny "⁠r3salt⁠" Frolov after Nemesis were eliminated in the first round of the playoffs to Alliance at XSE Pro League following a 3-1 run in the group stage with victories over Luminosity, B8, and EYEBALLERS.

In the interview, r3salt spoke at length about the slog that is online play and his wish to get to play at more LAN events. "At this point, if they tell me to play a tournament, a match, at whatever time, I don't even look at the name, don't look at the bracket, nothing," he told HLTV. "Just a match. As long as I get to play, as long as I win, that's it."

The 21-year-old also spoke about his team's run in China, how they have gotten better since he joined, and the hunger to make it to the Major in Singapore, where Nemesis is based.

You lost the quarterfinal against Alliance. What went wrong?

Honestly, I set my sights higher for this tournament, and so did all the guys. We were aiming higher. But even on that first map, it wasn't our game, even though we won that.

We actually went outside afterwards, and everyone was yelling at each other. Even I was yelling at someone on the first map, which isn't like me at all, but it happened. And it ended in a loss. After the first map, it was obvious to some of us how it would go, while others played to the end. That's it.

You say you set higher goals but, in terms of ranking, your team was last at this tournament. Where did that confidence to compete come from?

Well, everyone asked me, "Why did we go to China if we wouldn't make the playoffs?" That was the first objective, and we completed it. Then the objective became, "If we don't reach the final, why the fuck are we even here?"

We just got eliminated in the quarter-final, and we didn't get any points, the points we needed to lock ourselves into this tier one or one and a half to play at all these tournaments. That's it. We got eliminated. No points earned. So now, apparently, we'll keep slogging through the shit.

If you set aside the disappointment of the loss a bit, what can you say about this result? Making the playoffs is something.

In the group stage, we genuinely came together and played well, showed excellent CS against BetBoom. There was even a moment where we could have won the game.

Overall, if we break down this loss against Alliance and how we played the group stage, which we've already partly done, we should be locking ourselves in at this level consistently.

I wanted to ask a bit about your career development. After 9 Pandas, you spent some time on loan at Sangal. Can you tell me about that team and that period?

I wouldn't even call it career development. I think I've used the word "stagnation" in an interview before, and it's still stagnation. We just got knocked out from a tier one event; it was cool to feel this vibe again, but I still don't see it as development. If we lock in and keep playing for at least another half a year, that would be cool.

As for everything that happened in Pandas and Sangal... Sangal just let me breathe. Otherwise, I'd have been sitting idle. They gave me a real chance for a month to not keep sitting on the bench at Pandas, because I could easily have ended up benched again. So I got lucky twice. And now I'm playing, luckily.

Tell me how your move to Nemesis happened. How did you get the offer and what were your thoughts on it?

It all went through Pandas. They told me that initially they'd been thinking of taking me on loan because a huge amount of money was being asked for me. I was begging Nemesis to just buy me out, because if they can do it, I'm not against it, so I can play full-time.

Accordingly, all the questions I asked were, "What happens if you leave CS? What do I do then?" I obviously can't just take every word on faith, but what else is left for me besides trusting?

Evgeny "⁠r3salt⁠" Frolov

Now it's clear things are working out for you. But when you joined the team, did you see it as just a chance to keep playing, or did you actually look at the roster and believe in the project?

Honestly, I didn't expect this kind of... I wouldn't say takeoff, more like a runway. I hoped we'd at least play consistently around top 30. And well, we do. But my addition pushed us a bit higher — relative to my expectations, of course.

You had great online results, even two back-to-back CCT wins. Consistency online is a good sign. Did you expect things to go this well?

Well, yeah, we are CCT farmers, kind of, for real. But I don't want to just play CCT, EPL, some tournaments out there. At this point, if they tell me to play a tournament, a match, at whatever time, I don't even look at the name, don't look at the bracket, nothing. Just a match. As long as I get to play, as long as I win, that's it. That's how it is.

So for you, the online grind is just something you want to get through as quickly as possible and forget like a bad dream?

I'm already tired, tired of that endless online play. That constant thing where nobody even knows what'll actually happen there. Here it's completely different. You want to be here, not there.

How did you manage to reach this uptick in results? You mentioned your addition, and the numbers do show it, but is there anything else to it that you guys did?

The guys started working with a psychologist. Personally, I said I won't be doing that, hopefully never in my life. Not because of a bad experience; it's just the kind of person I am.

And also luck. A lot of teams pulled out of this tournament because of vacations and all that. Being tired after the Major, needing to catch their breath. But we needed this right now. So that's why we're playing... Well, we already stopped playing.

What are your goals for the rest of the year?

Major, 100%. The organization is from Singapore. We want to go to Singapore badly. We really hope so. Or rather, we won't just hope; we'll do our best to actually make it there.
