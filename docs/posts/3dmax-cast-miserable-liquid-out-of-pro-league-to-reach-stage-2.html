3DMAX cast miserable Liquid out of Pro League to reach Stage 2

3DMAX have advanced to Stage 2 of ESL Pro League Season 23 after a Kévin "⁠misutaaa⁠" Rabier masterclass led his side to a 2-0 win over Liquid (13-5 Nuke, 13-10 Dust2) in the final match of Stage 1.

The Frenchmen are hanging on to their Cologne Major hopes by a thread after a dreary run of results at the start of 2026, and this win was pivotal in ensuring that dream wasn't cut short early. Even with the result, Mischief has them at only an 11% chance of making the Major due to their upcoming tournament schedule, with the team currently only signed up for a single LAN before the April 6 invite cut-off.

Liquid, too, are in dire straits, with the defeat bringing them down to a 13% chance according to Mischief. They will attend BLAST Open Rotterdam and have signed up for WINAMAX, a €15,000 LAN in France that will likely not offer the VRS points needed to jump up the rankings unless the team list changes significantly — which looks unlikely as other contending teams will either attend Urban Riga Open 4 or Stake Ranked Episode 1.

Those odds are also entirely dependent on Liquid getting some wins on the board, and their pithy showings at EPL against Passion UA, Gaimin Gladiators, SemperFi, M80, and 3DMAX in this elimination series offer little hope in that regard.

Questions started in the veto with Liquid picking Nuke, an even map matchup against 3DMAX, instead of Ancient or Anubis, and they were punished for it.

3DMAX never looked back after a Dualies quad-kill from Bryan "⁠Maka⁠" Canda kicked off the proceedings and spurred on a shutout 10-round defensive spree. Liquid dodged a shutout with a solitary T side round and added a few to their tally after a Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek clutch converted a force-buy, but even that did little to ease the burn of a 5-13 defeat on their pick.

misutaaa carried his hot form from Nuke (1.94 rating, 120.6 ADR) into Dust2, where Liquid endured another miserable half despite going 8-4 in opening duels. 3DMAX's newcomer went 15-7 on the offense and put up a 2.23 rating to guide his team to a 9-3 lead, and added further misery to Liquid's day with a double kill in the pistol.

Liquid won a crucial force-buy and capitalized on 3DMAX squandering back-to-back seemingly unlosable 5v2s to begin a comeback, but it proved cosmetic in the end after misutaaa added another double kill to break the streak, Maka converted a 1v2 clutch the round after, and misutaaa and Pierre "⁠Ex3rcice⁠" Bulinge's aggression gave Liquid no breathing room in the final round to close out.
