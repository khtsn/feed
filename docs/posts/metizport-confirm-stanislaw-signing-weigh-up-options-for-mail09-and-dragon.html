Metizport confirm stanislaw signing; weigh up options for MaiL09 and Dragon

Metizport have announced that they have completed the signing of BOSS IGL Peter "⁠stanislaw⁠" Jarguz, who takes charge of the European team after a stand-in spell.

The Canadian temporarily played for Metizport in the aftermath of Jack "⁠Jackinho⁠" Ström Mattsson's benching. During that period, the team finished 5th-8th at ROG JOURNEY Spring and picked up one victory from four matches in CCT S3 Europe Series 20.

On Metizport, the 32-year-old reunites with head coach Vincent "⁠vinS⁠" Jozefiak after the pair worked together on Wildcard for 16 months. The team attended four Big Events during that period, including the Shanghai Major at the end of 2024.

"I’ve heard only great things about Metizport as an organization, and watching the CS project develop from NA has been intriguing," stanislaw said in a statement.

"It is a dream come true to be able to join a project that has high ambitions with a solid foundation beneath them, and reunite with vinS. I hope to achieve big things here as I make this big change in my life, and I promise to give it my all. Thank you for your support!"

With stanislaw's signing, Metizport enter "a period where we need to evaluate and adjust our roster," the Norwegian organization said. As part of this, Nikola "⁠Dragon⁠" Boskovic is stepping down from the starting lineup for the time being as Metizport "explore different options to find the right balance in the team."

Meanwhile, Metizport revealed that Liam "⁠MaiL09⁠" Tügel will be returning from his Alliance loan "soon." The 16-year-old has averaged a 1.20 rating while out on loan, playing a key role as Alliance finished just outside the qualification zone for the IEM Cologne Major, only 11 points shy of SINNERS.

"We are very pleased with his development during his time with Alliance, and the level he has shown, especially with the team coming close to qualifying for the Major," the Norwegian organization said.

"His loan agreement runs until June 30, but includes a recall option. We will engage in a dialogue with Alliance, Liam and his representatives to determine the best solution for all parties moving forward."

Metizport will take the coming weeks and months to "run trials and explore various options" as they look to bounce back from a disappointing first quarter that left them ranked 68th in Europe.

"We are not locking ourselves into one outcome," Metizport said. "This could lead to small changes, or it could lead to a bigger rebuild. What matters is that we make the right decisions for the long term.

"No matter what changes we make, our goal stays the same: We want to compete at the highest level. At the same time, we want to build a team with a strong structure, clear roles, and a positive working environment. This is the start of a new phase for us, and we are fully focused on getting it right."

Metizport currently have:

Nicolas "⁠Plopski⁠" Gonzalez Zamora

Vincent "⁠vinS⁠" Jozefiak (coach)

Jack "⁠Jackinho⁠" Ström Mattsson (benched)
