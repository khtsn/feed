9z consign MOUZ to last-place exit

9z have stunned MOUZ in the lower bracket of BLAST Open Rotterdam, recovering from a narrow map loss on Dust2 (11-13) with victories on Inferno (13-8) and Nuke (13-9) to keep their run alive at the expense of one of the world's top teams.

This stunning result leaves MOUZ with their first group-stage exit from an event in over a year, and their worst placing since adding Lotan "⁠Spinx⁠" Giladi to their ranks. The last time the international side bowed out before playoffs was IEM Katowice 2025, where the team utilized MOUZ NXT player Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze in lieu of the recently cut Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek before exiting in 13-16th place.

Before BLAST Open Rotterdam, MOUZ, ranked third in the Valve Regional Standings (VRS), looked all but certain to secure one of the six direct invitations for European sides to Stage 3 of the IEM Cologne Major. While MOUZ are still in the running for one of the spots, they have now given a chance to the likes of Falcons, Spirit, Natus Vincere, PARIVISION, and Aurora to deprive them of a direct Stage 3 berth.

"It feels sad it goes down this way, and that our brand of Counter-Strike wasn't that good," MOUZ coach Dennis "⁠sycrone⁠" Nielsen shared immediately after the loss. "Just losing a lot of advantageous situations, not thinking that clearly. I think it was obvious that things were not clicking for us at this tournament."

9z will no doubt be pleased with this showing, but they still have an arduous road ahead of them if they want to reach the playoffs in Rotterdam. The team's next challenge in the lower bracket will be against the loser of Spirit's clash with PARIVISION.

The series kicked off on 9z's pick of Dust2, where MOUZ rolled ahead to an 8-4 start on the CT-side thanks to excellent showings from Spinx, Jimi "⁠Jimpphat⁠" Salo, and Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman. A switch flipped for 9z at halftime, as Matias "⁠HUASOPEEK⁠" Ibañez Hernandez and Luciano "⁠luchov⁠" Herrera spearheaded a six-round win streak to kick off their own CT-side.

With the map quickly slipping from their grasp, MOUZ received a saving grace from AWPer Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás, who had just two kills across 20 rounds. The Hungarian sniper secured a crucial 1v3 4k at 9-10, fully swinging the momentum back in MOUZ's favor, allowing them to lock down the map 13-11.

MOUZ got off to a solid start on their own choice of Inferno, winning the pistol round and the first buy round despite suffering a man disadvantage. Ludvig "⁠Brollan⁠" Brolin's T-side quickly stalled out, however, as 9z only let one more round slip away after their 0-4 start, giving them a 7-5 lead heading into the offense.

MOUZ secured the second pistol round, but struggled to find much more beyond the conversion. The two sides traded rounds in the early portion of the half, but a 1v3 4k from luchov dealt a killing blow to MOUZ's disappointing CT-side, allowing 9z to march on to a 13-8 victory and force the Nuke decider.

MOUZ looked like a shell of themselves on Nuke, as 9z romped ahead to a 9-3 lead on the offense, shutting MOUZ out of the first half. MOUZ began a slow crawl back into the match after locking down the second pistol round, but a number of mistakes, including a disastrous 4v3 post-plant, allowed 9z to capitalize on their lead and take the map 13-9.
