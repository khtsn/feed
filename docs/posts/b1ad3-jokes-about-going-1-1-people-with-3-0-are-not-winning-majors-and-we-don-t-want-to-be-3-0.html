B1ad3 jokes about going 1-1: "People with 3-0 are not winning Majors. And we don't want to be 3-0"

Natus Vincere's IEM Cologne Major campaign began with a brutal loss to Spirit, a match where Andrey "⁠B1ad3⁠" Gorodenskiy's troops lost numerous advantages and looked a shadow of themselves after nearly a month away from officials.

"For our system and for our team, how we create knowledge, build knowledge for the team, how we try to update lots of stuff, knowing what to do on the map... for us, without playing, it's hard to implement all the stuff," he told HLTV after Natus Vincere beat Legacy to reach the 1-1 pool.

"This win is important for us because our goal for today was to wake up, first of all, to activate our potential," he added when asked about bouncing back on day two. "Yesterday we didn't play how we can play, and I didn't see us being super fired up, super enthusiastic, like with a lot of vocals, speaking to each other for micro plans, very, very loud, and like I said before, an enthusiastic voice."

Natus Vincere are among the favorites to contest Vitality for the Major title, particularly after breaking a daunting loss streak to Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's men — who, admittedly, weren't practicing beforehand — in Atlanta, but still need two best-of-three wins to secure a playoff berth. When asked where he thinks Natus Vincere stands after Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen's media day comments that the first two days of Stage 3 would be telling of how the top-16 stage would go and who would be favorites for the title, B1ad3 had no trouble putting the opening loss to Spirit behind him.

"I think NAVI is standing on the information that people with 3-0 are not winning Majors," he said in a tongue-in-cheek reference to how only four teams have won the Major after going 3-0 since the Swiss stage was introduced. "This, first of all. And we don't want to be 3-0 (laughs)."

The only teams to win a Major after going 3-0 in the Swiss stage are:

  • Gambit at PGL Major Krakow 2017

  • Astralis at IEM Katowice Major 2019

  • NAVI at PGL Major Stockholm 2021

  • Vitality at BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023

1-1 after today, a bounce back after yesterday, and I'm sure you're happy with that. Just talk me through how you turned things around between yesterday and today.

Yeah, it was a tough match. I think the main issue is that we didn't play official matches for a long time, and for our system and for our team, how we create knowledge, build knowledge for the team, how we try to update lots of stuff, knowing what to do on the map... for us, without playing, it's hard to implement all the stuff, right? You basically need to feel this tournament routine, when you're constantly playing games, constantly inside the gaming process, when you're testing all your stuff for real, and then your brain understands it a lot of times, you more vividly understand what is working, what is not working, and we are very sloppy if we don't play.

Yesterday you saw that: there were some things good, some things bad, and we were doing good entries on Spirit on Dust2, but we couldn't close the rounds. The reason for this is usually that we are not feeling the game, we're not feeling each other in terms of communication on this micro level because it's very crucial to outplay 4vs2s, 5vs3s on A on Dust2. The map works like this, that if you make one mistake, it's very easy to bounce back with entries and play plus one.

So I think it was a tough opponent for us for the first match. They also got confidence when they are smashing us like this, and if it were a different opponent, I'm pretty sure we would bounce back differently, for sure. We tried on Anubis and it was better, but still I felt this shakiness and no confidence sometimes because you're not sure what you should do. So it's good for us to bounce back with this win today, and we are the [kind of] team that will play better and better with each game; the more games we play, the better we will be.

I did want to ask a little bit about those advantages. You said you were sloppy starting out, but I don't think I've ever seen NAVI lose that many rounds that were 3v5s, 2v5s, a lot of sloppy rounds that you guys usually aren't that out of sorts in. What was it like in the team during those? Is it just down to the fact that you guys hadn't played in a while, or what was going on in there?

Yes, there's this aspect, but I explained to you already how it affects us, in what aspects, in what details, and yeah, it's basically what happened. We lost those situations because we didn't feel each other good enough, we didn't feel the moment.

If you played five officials, you can create one decision, but if you played zero and you play a fresh game after one month, you will create another decision

There is this mechanical factor, like how good you are in terms of feeling. You have a game feeling, first of all, game sense, and the feeling of the situation on a mechanical level. When you see something, like three players going out short, you're ramp, your teammate is in CT trying to help you. At this moment, it can look easy, but when we are playing more officials, we have more like, 'flow mode,' and every player is more connected to what is happening in the game on a mechanical level. Like every angle, people check differently-

Like having the full picture?

Picture yes, but at the same time, first of all, if you play a lot, you create confidence. It's an important thing, confidence. In some moments, if you played five officials, you can create one decision, but if you played zero and you play a fresh game after one month, you will create another decision in the same situation. This is, in simple words, how you can understand it.

Do you think the fact that Spirit started in Stage 2 and had a chance to warm up also played into it? That you were very cold, and they've been playing really hot? Because during this event, we're seeing Donk play at insane levels, so when you're approaching them as a competitor in that game, how difficult was it just in the server to prepare against him?

They warmed up pretty good, and also they smashed everyone. They were very confident wins, so I think that it matters for confidence. Like on our tier-one level, confidence is a huge factor. You can stop the confidence with equal confidence, the moves around the map, more precision on a micro level, and also realizing and understanding what you need to do in specific moments, much faster. So many factors work if you play a lot from match to match.

And our prep, we just prepped as usual, I think they played very basic CS. They just manage to execute, and with good firepower, they [were] just executing on a different level, and they were also closing rounds because of this firepower. They're closing rounds very good because they can play good 3vs5, 2v4, not only versus us.

About Legacy here, they'd beaten you twice. Does beating them now give you some of that confidence that you said you were looking for, that you were kinda lacking?

Yes. This win is important for us because our goal for today was to wake up, first of all, to activate our potential. Yesterday we didn't play how we can play, and I didn't see us being super fired up, super enthusiastic, like with a lot of vocals, speaking to each other for micro plans, very, very loud, and like I said before, an enthusiastic voice.

It's very important to activate yourself as a team because this is how we know how we can play; we are not a young team, we've played with this lineup for a long time, so we know our potential. And if we lose, we can always understand why we lose, we are not delusional, we can understand what we need to fix, what didn't work, all this stuff.

For today, like I said, the goal was to show it to ourselves, like we need to see it by ourselves, that we are different, that we are more vocal, that we are more activated, that we are better at creating ideas to be a step ahead constantly. And like I said before, with each match we're going to play better and better.

Lastly, when we did the media day interview with Aleksib, he said that days one and two of Stage 3 are going to tell a lot about both how this stage is going to go, but also who the favorites are heading into the playoffs. How do you feel about where NAVI stands in that after these two days?

I think NAVI is standing on the information that people with 3-0 are not winning Majors. This, first of all. And we don't want to be 3-0 (laughs).
