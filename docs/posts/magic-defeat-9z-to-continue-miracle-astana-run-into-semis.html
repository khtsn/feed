magic defeat 9z to continue miracle Astana run into semis

magic's shock run at PGL Astana continues into the semi-finals with a memorable 2-1 victory over 9z, the underdog side pulling off a remarkable comeback at the end of the deciding map to secure their victory.

The surprise result comes despite 9z's stellar run through the group stage, which featured straight victories over PARIVISION, MOUZ and Falcons. The South American side were seeking to match their best-ever Big Event campaign today, which came in a semi-final run at IEM Dallas 2024.

magic's run in Astana had also been an impressive one, recovering from an 0-2 start in the group stage to make the playoffs despite being one of the lowest-ranked teams in attendance.

magic mo0n multi-kill seals Dust2 for his team

magic's fine form continued at the start of Dust2, their defense shutting down 9z with relative ease. Vitaliy "⁠sFade8⁠" Marushka called excellent rotations to ensure his players were in the right spots, whilst he and Andrey "⁠AW⁠" Anisimov provided the firepower needed for a dominant 9-3 score.

When the sides swapped the map devolved into a messy battle, both teams spending many of their rounds on force-buys as neither could take clean control of the economy. 9z were steadily getting the better of things, surviving a lot of chaotic moments, but magic squeaked through enough low-buys and clever fakes to seal the deal.

meyern bags another multi-kill with smooth 4K

9z righted the ship in emphatic fashion on their opponent's pick of Mirage, largely due to the immense contributions of Ignacio "⁠meyern⁠" Meyer, who posted the best LAN map of his career. The Argentine AWPer posted multi-kill after multi-kill towards the A site to ensure a clean defensive half, before setting his team on their way to victory with a 3K in a disadvantaged situation on their T pistol.

9z's domination continued into the early portion of Ancient, the South American side outclassing magic with ease on their offense. Svyatoslav "⁠MaSvAl⁠" Masko was the man who came up huge when magic needed it, bagging two multi-kills to turn rounds that were firmly in 9z's hands, giving his team five defensive rounds.

tenzy picks up 3K in Mid before awful meyern whiff costs 9z

9z quickly grabbed control as they switched to the defense and seemed set to romp to victory, but magic still had something left in the tank. They switched up their offense to consistently go for uptempo Mid brawls and 9z couldn't find an answer.

magic grew with each round won and became more and more clutch, whilst conversely meyern found himself guilty of multiple poor whiffs on the AWP. Ultimately magic's momentum, and impact plays by AW and Nikita "⁠tenzy⁠" Kochenyuk, ran them all the way to the victory, continuing one of the underdog stories of the year thus far.
