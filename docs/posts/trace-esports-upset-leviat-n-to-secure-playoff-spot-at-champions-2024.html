Trace picked Bind and Leviatán picked Sunset, leaving Ascent as the decider.

Bind began with Trace running their double Sentinel composition with Deadlock, while Leviatán stuck to their Gekko variation of the standard Bind comp with Viper, Skye, Raze, and Brimstone. Starting on attack, Trace won the first pistol round to threaten a lead until Leviatán played meticulously to prevent spike plants. The pistol win and smart post-plant utility were enough for Trace to trade rounds evenly with Leviatán as they entered the half up 7-5.

Trace were up 7-5 at the half, and a classic 3K from Kai confirmed their second pistol round win. It was clear that Leviatán were struggling with planting the spike on time due to the delay ability from the two Sentinels on Trace. Heybay had a great map, with plenty of his utility singlehandedly winning them important rounds.

Heybay wins the round for Trace Esports with the Deadlock ultimate.

Leviatán started to mount a comeback as soon as Trace won 11 rounds, but a fumbled post-plant on B site led to a half-second defuse from LuoK1ng . The next round was a confident win from Trace as they upset the champions of Americas by taking Bind 13-10.

The series moved to Sunset, with Leviatán needing to win on their map pick to stay alive. aspas moved back to Neon while FengF stayed on Raze. Heybay moved to Breach but got three to win Trace their attack side pistol round. The patience and post-plant protocol from Trace shone as they built a 5-1 lead, but Leviatán adapted quickly. A 5-1 lead for Trace disappeared as aspas and C0M helped Leviatán win six rounds in a row to end the half up 7-5.

Leviatán tried to push aggressively into spawn to win their first pistol round of the series, but it didn't work. Trace had an opportunity to halt the momentum that Leviatán built, but aspas' Neon and a tex clutch closed out the map 13-9.

Tex clutches to help secure Sunset for Leviatán.

For Ascent, Leviatán went with the Deadlock and Cypher double Sentinel, while Trace went with the default Ascent comp with Killjoy. For the first time in the series, a team was up significantly at the half, and it was the Chinese third seed up 10-2 over the Americas victors.

Kai and FengF roll over Leviatán to end the half up 10-2.

Leviatán won their second pistol to provide the slightest amount of hope of a comeback, but the team couldn't convert their bonus and Kai buying the Operator again signaled the end of the series. The map ended 13-5, as Trace completed their second upset in a row to secure the first playoff slot in Group C.
