Israeli Troops Scoured a Gaza Hospital for Signs of Hamas

Briefing|Israeli Troops Scoured a Gaza Hospital for Signs of Hamas

https://www.nytimes.com/2023/11/16/briefing/israel-gaza-hospital-george-santos-ethics-report.html

As night fell in the Gaza Strip today, Israeli troops were still searching the territory’s largest hospital, Al-Shifa, which Israel has said concealed a secret Hamas base.

To bolster their case, Israeli officials presented videos showing another cache of weapons that they said were found on the hospital grounds, as well as a doorway that they described as a tunnel entrance. But the images, which could not be independently verified, still have not proven the existence of a sprawling Hamas operation there. Israeli officials warned that the search was bound to be slow, citing the physical difficulties and the risks.

Israel’s military also said that it found the body of one of the hostages that Hamas abducted during its Oct. 7 attack, in a structure “adjacent” to the hospital.

Hamas and the hospital leadership have denied the accusations that the hospital houses a Hamas base, and the Gaza health ministry said that thousands of people remained inside the hospital compound with little food and water.

Biden says the U.S. has ‘real differences’ with China

In a speech at the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation summit today, President Biden said that the U.S. had “real differences” with China, but that the two countries had agreed to improve communication in order to “reduce the risk of miscalculation.”

His comments came a day after he held four hours of talks with President Xi Jinping. Biden said that they reached agreements on curbing fentanyl production and on the resumption of military-to-military communications. But little progress was made around semiconductors, A.I. or the Israel-Hamas war.

My colleague David Sanger wrote that there was a subtle but noticeable shift in the power dynamic between the two countries. For the first time in years, a Chinese leader desperately needed a few things from the U.S.

House investigators found evidence of Santos’s crimes

Representative George Santos, the first-term Republican from New York, said today that he would not seek re-election shortly after the House Ethics Committee said it found “substantial evidence” that he violated federal law. House investigators said Santos used campaign funds for personal purposes, defrauded donors and filed false or incomplete campaign finance and financial disclosure reports.

The committee voted unanimously to refer its findings to the Justice Department. The report could be the catalyst for a third effort to remove Santos from office.
