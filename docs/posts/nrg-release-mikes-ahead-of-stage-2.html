NRG release Mikes ahead of Stage 2

Part of NRG for less than three months, Mikes cited “differences in opinion” as the reason behind his exit from the team.

Following his stint with Envy, Mikes found a new home at 100 Thieves 100 Thieves North America Rank #5 zander Alexander Dituri Cryocells Matthew Panganiban eeiu Daniel Vucenovic Boostio Kelden Pupello Asuna Peter Mazuryk , where he began as an Assistant Coach, was promoted to Head Coach for the 2023 season, and then shifted back to his former role at his last year with the organization in 2024. His tenure with the Thieves saw him make appearances at Champions 2022, LOCK//IN 2023, and Masters Shanghai 2024 before signing with NRG in February.

Mikes leading 100 Thieves at São Paulo's LOCK//IN. (Photo: Lance Skundrich/Riot Games)

NRG have seemingly struggled all throughout their 2025 season. Despite entering the year off the back of two event wins at SEN City Classic and the SOOP Valorant League, the roster helmed by Pujan "FiNESSE" Mehta has only managed to break into the top eight of both Americas Kickoff and Stage 1, missing out on both Masters Bangkok and just recently Masters Toronto.

Now out of competition until Stage 2, the organization has opted to make changes within the team, starting with the release of Mikes. A replacement has not yet been named, but NRG have plenty of time to find a new pickup ahead of their most important stage of the year.

Mikes has stated that he is currently LFT and open to offers from teams interested in bolstering their coaching staff for the rest of the season, implying his intent to stay towards the top of Tier 1. However, he has also announced that this may be his final major esports opportunity, with a potential retirement on the horizon.
