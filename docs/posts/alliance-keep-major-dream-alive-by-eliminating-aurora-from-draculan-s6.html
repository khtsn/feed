Alliance keep Major dream alive by eliminating Aurora from DraculaN S6

Alliance have scored a huge upset win over Aurora in the lower bracket of DraculaN Season 6 to cling on to a chance at qualifying for the IEM Cologne Major.

"Everyone has it in their heads, but we try to say it to ourselves, every game, that we can't think about it," Simon "⁠twist⁠" Eliasson told HLTV after the victory. "The focus is to win DraculaN, that's it. We take each game separately, focus on each game, and hopefully we qualify. But our odds haven't been that big to make it to the Major, so each game, we take it step by step."

The Swedes have been blazing a comeback trail on our predicted invite list ever since their title-winning campaign at ROG Journey, but still had a mountain to climb coming into the Romanian event to have any hope of securing a spot in Cologne.

After beating Inner Circle and fnatic in the lower bracket on day three, Alliance were given their most difficult test yet in a do-or-die battle against Aurora in the lower bracket. The Turks had faltered to M80 to end up staring elimination in the face, and couldn't get back on their feet despite starting the series with a 13-2 win on Inferno and leading on the Overpass decider, 9-4.

"It feels amazing, to be honest," twist said. "This was such an important win, first of all, but it was just so nice to see this team stepping up in clutches. The first map was so rough for us, but it feels amazing that we managed to come back from that stomp because we're not that used to playing against tier-one teams — or at least this squad, I've been around before. But it felt nice, really nice."

It was a golden opportunity for Alliance to make up ground in the race, with Aurora's third-place ranking on the VRS coming into the tournament offering 35 points to the Swedes. The series win has helped Alliance surge into invite range for the Major as they now sit 14th on our predicted list, but only a slim points lead means another do-or-die matchup against Passion UA awaits them in the lower bracket final.
