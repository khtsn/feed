B1ad3: "Some games that I see us lose, we lose because of a lack of firepower"

Natus Vincere have endured a tough year thus far, suffering multiple early exits before bagging a playoff berth in their previous outing. Whilst it wasn't entirely smooth sailing, the team continued to trend in the right direction by picking up another playoff spot in ESL Pro League.

"For sure it's not something that I expected," Andrey "⁠B1ad3⁠" Gorodenskiy told HLTV when reflecting on his team's year thus far. "We were in the peak of our development process during the Budapest Major, and we wanted to bring back that energy and activate that potential that we have."

Natus Vincere have the chance to activate their potential in the playoffs of ESL Pro League, an event without big players like Vitality and Falcons in attendance. B1ad3 if Natus Vincere can "activate ourselves properly" then they could even beat the likes of MOUZ.

Ahead of his team's playoff run B1ad3 went in-depth with HLTV on the differences between online and LAN CS, his team's year thus far, and what they have to do to improve and reach their goals for the season.

Listen to the interview here.

What are your reflections on the group stage? It was a bit up and down, but you got through it in the end.

Online is a very rare experience for us. For example, in the history of this lineup, you can see that it's not very successful. I think we lost in Astralis in Bounty or EPL, then to Monte in Bounty, so we are for sure playing better on LAN. I am glad that we qualified, basically.

We beat G2 the tournament before, in Cluj, and then we lost to them online. Teams like Legacy I think have a little bit of the upper hand in matches vs us online, because they play very confident vs us. I think on LAN this match could be much different.

We focus a lot on unity on LAN, and we always try to build the synergy. Online you don't have all these match routines

Why do you think your team struggles to play as well online?

Maybe because of the change of rituals, circumstances, routine, everything. We focus a lot on unity on LAN, and we always try to build the synergy. Online you don't have all these match routines let's say, for example a morning walk, a mental warmup, discussions together, something in the room. It's a different experience, and I think you need to adapt to this.

It's a very typical thing nowadays, because there are a lot of online tournaments, everyone who plays online tournaments can understand this. You play in your own room, fully isolated, and you don't see your teammates, so you don't feel this team unity at all. You need to basically find the way to activate yourself. Maybe also the seriousness of the value of the match goes down a little bit, you need to force yourself to think differently. It's kind of a natural effect on LAN that usually happens, because it's a different experience. When you play LAN, it comes more naturally, by its own way.

When you play online you need to isolate yourself from your own world to be able to focus on CS. This is the power of a bootcamp, you isolate players from their own problems and connections, and force their focus in only one direction, on the team goal.

One player who had a very good previous stage was makazze. How did you rate his performances, and do you think he found it a bit easier to play online because he was in that world not so long ago?

I'm not sure because we can compare to other online games, like Monte or the Astralis match. For now I cannot say this. But overall his performance was pretty confident, maybe he just became more comfortable day by day, adapting and knowing better what to do. Every match was pretty good for him.

One of the main problems was the map pool change, and you can clearly see this after our bootcamp when we played Krakow

How do you feel about NAVI's year so far? What is your assessment of the way the team has been performing?

For sure it's not something that I expected. We were in the peak of our development process during the Budapest Major, and we wanted to bring back that energy and activate that potential that we have. One of the main problems was the map pool change, and you can clearly see this after our bootcamp when we played Krakow. We lost to FURIA where we could maybe use our map pool differently if there was still Train. So it's slowing us down for sure, and I think maybe for the Major we will be ready much better, like we were at the previous Major, in terms of map pool.

So basically I am not satisfied, first thing. Second, is map pool slowed us down a lot. We should reflect: in Cluj we made the playoffs, in Krakow we lost to FURIA and Spirit, and FURIA was in the grand final after this. What FURIA did in Krakow is what was our goal for this season. They were four rounds from elimination vs. The MongolZ, and then somehow they found the way to activate themselves and find their A game. With that shape they went straight to the finals, and you can see for example FURIA not qualifying now [for EPL playoffs], they also didn't play super good in Cluj and lost the quarter-finals. In this example you can understand how it works, the team can be in finals, but they can also lose if they cannot play their own game. That's our case. When we play our game, we can play really good.

We just played two big tournaments, Krakow and Cluj, that's all by now. Krakow was affected fully by the map pool, and in Cluj we played MOUZ in the playoffs. In the third map that we lost, on Mirage, in MR12 you have crucial rounds that you need to win on T side, and we lost two crucial situations that, in my opinion, we are usually winning when we play our A game. If we won those two, we could go semis and then it's a different story now. Different results and you can analyze much differently, and I would be more satisfied now. We are working on this now, how mentally to be always there.

B1ad3 wants to see his team activate themselves more

Looking forwards to these playoffs, how confident are NAVI feeling?

It's always the same for us. We are a pretty experienced team, we know how CS works, we know not only from my word, but also our experience as a team. We have already done upsets with this lineup, with the previous lineup also. makazze is also the same, he has played some good tournaments with us and he knows that we can beat strong opponents.

At the moment there is no mindset that we cannot beat The MongolZ, we know we can beat them and we know we can even beat MOUZ if we activate ourselves properly. That's now the main goal.

Do you feel more confident for the playoffs knowing that there's no Vitality, no Falcons? Does it perhaps give you a little bit more belief that it's a trophy that you can win?

The interesting thing is that it looks like Falcons are not that big of a threat nowadays, not like Vitality. On paper they are very strong, but it looks like all the material they have, they cannot construct it into something very strong. I can say that six months ago Falcons would be much more dangerous in your head, a team like 'oh we are playing Falcons.' Now the fact is it's a little bit lower. They are still a strong team, but we know they are vulnerable.

The fact that we qualified for this in a hard way, going 3-2, and we can add to this point that there is no Vitality and Falcons, so maybe it would be no playoffs, I don't know, who knows? In the last big events, Krakow and Cluj-Napoca, the playoffs were really strong, and nowadays we can see that Vitality is way above others, let's say they are the S tier team nowadays.

All others can beat each other, we can see this in Spirit for example, they can lose, they can win, they can do both. They can do something unexpected in both ways. Same for Falcons, same for MOUZ, and for other teams, MongolZ, Aurora. You see Astralis, I don't know how they will perform on LAN, but online they beat FURIA in a confident way. There are a bunch of teams that, on their best day, can be a danger. That's why no Vitality is a good thing, but all others now in the playoffs are all dangerous, they are all equal in my opinion.

Also Spirit, with donk they are also much more dangerous, because this player is always an X-factor, no matter how the team is playing he can turn everything in one or two rounds. But they also looked a little bit unexpected. The funny thing is that they said they were discussing six hours per day at bootcamp, doing six pracs per day, and it's curious to understand how it's possible and if it's true. If it is true, imagine how quality their game could be.

Winning trophies, this is the only way we can be satisfied. So at minimum one trophy, it can be Rio, Atlanta, BLAST Texas, any big tournament, not something like StarLadder in the previous year

Looking forward to the rest of NAVI's season, as you said you were not satisfied with the year so far, what would make you satisfied come the end of the season? What do you want NAVI to achieve by the mid-year offseason break?

Winning trophies, this is the only way we can be satisfied. So at minimum one trophy, it can be Rio, Atlanta, BLAST Texas, any big tournament, not something like StarLadder in the previous year. It will show the progress, it will show that we are capable of becoming a team with a future.

What do you think you need to improve on to reach your goals and be satisfied with the season?

I think one of the most important things should be our consistency. We need more consistency, not even as a team but individually. I think from game to game I can notice that each player can play very good, in terms of decision-making, discipline and creating micro-plans during the game, and then the same player can be different in the next game. This is something we are trying to understand the nature of.

Also, I think we need more firepower, that's for sure. Nowadays, some games that I see us lose, we lose because of a lack of firepower. In MR12 you can manipulate your opponent well enough, and create a good gameplan, and with this lineup we have already shown that we can do this and beat good teams, but you will always benefit if you have more firepower. You can analyze teams that have a lot of firepower and what they can achieve sometimes. On the other hand, you can take Falcons, who have a lot of firepower as a team, but they cannot put it into some balanced thing, right? So there's two sides to this.
