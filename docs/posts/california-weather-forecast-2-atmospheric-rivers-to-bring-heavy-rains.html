A storm hitting California through Thursday will set the stage for another week of unsettled weather across the state.

Judson Jones

Judson Jones is a meteorologist and reporter for The Times.

Published Jan. 30, 2024Updated Jan. 31, 2024, 5:28 a.m. ET

Forecasters have warned California residents that unsettled weather through Thursday is likely to bring heavy rains, strong winds and mountain snow as another atmospheric river moves down along the West Coast. Another storm will threaten the region early next week.

While forecasters warned on Tuesday to begin preparing for inclement weather, the National Weather Service’s San Francisco Bay Area office said that the heaviest rainfall will begin around the Wednesday morning commute for some areas and toward the afternoon farther south down the coast. Flooding and downed trees will be the most common hazards.

Flooding concerns, like overwhelmed storm drains that can lead to road closures, will be widespread, and the heavy rain that falls could create localized areas of flash flooding, with urban areas, roads, small streams and burn scars the most vulnerable, forecasters said.

The storm is predicted to be a moderate atmospheric river, and nowhere near the strength of an “Ark storm,” as has been making the rounds on social media in the past few days. Nor is the forecast likely to be anywhere near as severe as the series of back-to-back storms last winter that led to widespread flooding and historic amounts of snow in the region.

This midweek storm, while still having the potential to produce flash flooding and heavy mountain snow, is expected to move quickly, which would reduce the amount of rain that falls over any one location.

A foot or more of snow is forecast over parts of Northern and Central California. The Sierra Nevada will get heavy snow from late Wednesday morning into Thursday.

Farther north, heavy rainfall through Wednesday may lead to increased threats of landslides in Western Washington, forecasters warned in a special weather statement. The consistent rainfall over the last few days, and with more in the forecast, puts extra pressure on soil instability. At least two landslides have been reported thus far in Western Washington, including one in a Seattle neighborhood.

Here’s what to know about the timing.

  • Wednesday: A robust plume of moisture will move over California, with the heaviest rain falling over the northern Bay Area and the Sacramento area Wednesday afternoon before drifting farther south in the evening and overnight.

  • Friday and Saturday: Occasional showers are possible throughout the region.

  • Sunday into Monday: A stronger storm possibly arrives.

The next atmospheric river could be more robust than this one.

It is “increasingly likely” that another strong weather system and possibly a “significant atmospheric river event” will affect California late this weekend into early next week, forecasters with the Weather Prediction Center said.

Despite many possible outcomes, the atmospheric river arriving Sunday into Monday has the potential to produce greater low-elevation rain and mountain snow totals than this week’s event.

A worst-case scenario would be if the storm system stalls off the coast, which could prolong the rain and snow. This scenario would bring a high risk of flooding and heavy mountain snow, forecasters in Los Angeles warned.
