sh1ro on why Spirit struggle vs. Falcons: "Right now, I can genuinely say I have no fucking idea"

Spirit made light work of MOUZ to advance to the semi-finals of IEM Rio, where a rematch against Falcons awaits. The Russian outfit has beaten the Saudi organization's team just once, a crucial win in the quarter-finals of the StarLadder Budapest Major, while losing its four other matchups — including a blowout defeat in the group stage of IEM Rio.

"I think it'll be a different game than in the group stage," Dmitry "⁠sh1ro⁠" Sokolov told HLTV after beating MOUZ. "We'll see. I don't want to say too much — honestly, I just want to win and reach the final. But what happens happens."

The Russian sniper also shared insight into the team's form and how Dmitry "⁠S0tF1k⁠" Forostyanko is helping provide some fresh perspective amidst his stint as a stand-in coach for IEM Rio and PGL Astana.

"We've definitely improved our fundamentals together with him," sh1ro said "When Dima came in, he started to bust our balls regarding our micro: telling us we fucked up here and there. [...] Perhaps at some point we had become too robotic and couldn't adapt during the game... It's like something changed, but nothing really changed, that's one way to put it."

Editor's note:

Questions for this interview were translated into Russian with the help of Spirit's staff, and answers were translated by HLTV staff.

Congratulations, a pretty easy win over MOUZ in the end. Give me your reaction to the match.

Overall it went fine. We were in control of the game, won our map pick confidently. Things went slightly off on Mirage. But that can happen, you lose a pistol and a force buy and the score line can get close. Overall, we played well.

tN1R needs more time. He's been on the team for eight or nine months now and still needs more adaptation to the team and more confidence

The team looks really good right now. For a while you were struggling, but the form at this event is looking up, and tN1R in particular is playing much better. Tell me about you finding your form, and the team starting to click a little bit.

A lot of hard work and a lot of discussions about the problems we had. People in this team want to win here and are trying to find comfort for each other in the game.

As for tN1R — he needs more time. He's been on the team for eight or nine months now and still needs more adaptation to the team and more confidence. He's working on everything, and as you can see, he's getting better with every game. Some chemistry is starting to appear.

donk posted on Telegram after the group stage that he was sick. How's he feeling now? And does it help that others stepped up while he wasn't at his best?

I don't think his gameplay changed much, regardless of how he's feeling physically — he's doing better now with the food poisoning. But overall, just like it was on praccs, when we play well as a team, every player gets the freedom to perform.

I'll use Vitality as an example — everyone clicks in at the right moment. That's the chemistry we're trying to achieve. Looking forward, different players in our team will have their best performances in different matches, and then there will be stability.

We've definitely improved our fundamentals together with S0tF1k. When Dima came in, he started to bust our balls regarding our micro: telling us we fucked up here and there

What has changed since S0tF1k came in as stand-in coach?

I won't reveal everything, but hally and S0tF1k are different coaches. What I can say is we've definitely improved our fundamentals together with him. When Dima came in, he started to bust our balls regarding our micro: telling us we fucked up here and there. It's a fresh outside perspective since he hadn't worked with us before. It's something new for us.

Perhaps at some point we had become too robotic and couldn't adapt during the game... It's like something changed, but nothing really changed, that's one way to put it.

You're playing Falcons tomorrow — historically a tough matchup for you. What's going to be different this time?

I can't say much — I've been asked about Falcons many times already. I think it'll be a different game than in the group stage. We'll see. I don't want to say too much — honestly, I just want to win and reach the final. But what happens happens.

Why do you think you struggle against them so much?

Honestly, at different periods when we've played them, I've had different thoughts about why. Right now, I can genuinely say I have no fucking idea.
