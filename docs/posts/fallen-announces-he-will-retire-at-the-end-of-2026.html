FalleN announces he will retire at the end of 2026

Emotions took over FalleN as he announced he will retire at the end of the year

Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo has announced on the Farmasi Arena stage in Rio de Janeiro that he will be hanging the mouse at the end of 2026, putting an end to a career spanning over 20 years.

"It's 247 more days," he said on the stage. "I've decided that at the end of the year, I'm going to do other things within CS with you all.

"I’m here to invite you to join me on this journey for another 247 days. We still have a lot to accomplish together, and afterwards, I will have the opportunity to do many other things with you, outside the server, but with Counter-Strike in my heart.

"I think we’ll be able to do lots of things to help other players change their lives through the game, just as mine was changed."

From playing at events of the stature of ESWC, WCG, and IEM during the 1.6 days, with teams like Complexity and FireGamers, to breaking out with Luminosity and SK in CS:GO and winning back-to-back Majors in 2015-16, the Brazilian veteran has been a key piece in putting Brazil on the global Counter-Strike map.

FalleN has been more than just a player in some of Brazil's best teams. The 34-year-old has been a popular teacher in his community and has grown the game at the amateur level with his Games Academy project in Latin America, offering instructional videos and platforms for pick-up games and tournaments.

FalleN also donned the MIBR jersey and played internationally with Liquid before he and his Luminosity/SK teammates got back together as Last Dance, a team that would eventually go on to reach the Legends Stage at the PGL Antwerp Major under the Imperial banner while pulling at a whole nation's heart strings.

FalleN will finish his career as an in-game leader and a captain, and it is nowhere more noticeable than in FURIA, with whom he has won trophies BLAST Rivals 2025 Season 2, IEM Chengdu 2025, and FISSURE Playground 2 while also mentoring a young Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko on his way to becoming Rookie of the Year and the sixth best player of 2025.

"I’d like to say a huge thank you to my family — many of them are here today — and to my wife, Leticia, for their unconditional support over the last 23 years," a teary-eyed FalleN said.

"I’d also like to say a huge thank you to my teammates. I’m going to miss you all very much in the future."
