Vitality move past The MongolZ to reach playoffs

Vitality are through to the playoffs of BLAST Open Rotterdam after beating The MongolZ 2-0 in the upper bracket semi-finals (13-4 Mirage, 16-13 Overpass).

Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut and Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan were indomitable on Mirage, the former stealing the show with a few exceptional flicks, but it was the latter who deserves his flowers for completely taking over on Overpass to get a struggling Vitality over the line.

That was, in part, due to some uncharacteristic mistakes from Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's side, who ran out of time after taking too long to retrieve the bomb to hand momentum to The MongolZ and lost a round to pistols in the second half. But when pressure was actually on, Vitality stabilized off of flameZ's excellence to seal victory, earning a seeding match for the semi-finals against PARIVISION for their efforts.

It was business as usual in the largely one-sided rivalry — Vitality were 11-2 in series play against The MongolZ heading into the game — on Mirage, where The MongolZ could do little after converting their pistol win into a 3-0 start.

ZywOo was in terrifying form, winning two clutches, hitting a few stellar flick shots, and repeatedly ripping apart The MongolZ's executes. That, combined with an equally unstoppable flameZ, equated to a tour de force for the French organization as they gave up only one more round while romping to victory.

Things only seemed to go from bad to worse for Garidmagnai "⁠bLitz⁠" Byambasuren's men after they won the first pistol on Overpass, bought four M4s, and then lost to a single Deagle bought by ZywOo. He got the opener before dying, and flameZ picked it up to pull off an unreal 1v3 clutch to allow Vitality up to a 3-1 lead.

But an uncharacteristic mistake from 2025's Team of the Year followed, where they took too long to retrieve the bomb and handed Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar a free 1v4 clutch, and that became the catalyst in The MongolZ turning things around for an 8-4 lead.

flameZ, who was already having a great map, grabbed two in the pistol and farmed an ace against pistols in the next as Vitality began to recover, but a loss to Tec-9s on B proved costly as The MongolZ took momentum from that to hit map point.

But Vitality came alive from there, the same mistakes that plagued them for much of the map evaporating as Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool began to find impact alongside a transcendent flameZ. The Israeli rifler didn't give The MongolZ any time to breathe as he dominated round after round, hitting 30 frags just after Vitality forced overtime, and ended the 16-13 map with 12 multi-kills and a 38-18 K-D to put Vitality through to the playoffs.
