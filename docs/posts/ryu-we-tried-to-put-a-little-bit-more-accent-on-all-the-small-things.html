ryu: "We tried to put a little bit more accent on all the small things"

Astralis enjoyed a successful start to 2026 and crowned it with a third-place finish at EPL S23 and a runner-up placing at PGL Bucharest, but their campaigns at BLAST Rivals and IEM Atlanta didn't go as planned.

"I think it's also a lack of synergy still. I feel like we could be winning clutch situations and more hard rounds. I feel like it did come down a lot to just overall synergy," Gytis "⁠ryu⁠" Glušauskas told HLTV about their recent struggles in the US.

Listen to the interview here.

Congrats on making the Major. How are you feeling ahead of your first appearance?

Feeling good. We had a week of bootcamp prior, so it went pretty well, and of course, very cool to be playing my first Major. It's also in Cologne, it's like a very prestigious event, so it's very nice.

HooXi recently said in an interview with us that it's hard to prepare for a Major until you play a Major. Do you feel there is something special about Majors, or is it similar to other tier-one events that you've kind of gotten used to throughout the year so far?

I mean, I haven't played a Major before, right? But if I were to say, then the Major is obviously the most important event of the year, and I think everyone has always hoped to perform well at the Major. I guess that just adds extra expectations to it, and it makes it different from other tier-one events.

A lot of your teammates in the interviews kind of praised your communication, because usually when players come from lower-tier teams, they are not as good with communicating. Do you feel that's one of your strengths as a player as well?

I mean, it helps the team for sure. It's easier to play with someone who talks more and gives initiative. You could say so, yeah.

About the season you guys had so far, it was a pretty good start. You had a third place and a final in Bucharest, but the last two events didn't go exactly to plan. What do you think happened? Was it just like too many tournaments in a row that meant you guys couldn't really practice during it, or what was the reason for those two results?

For the Rivals event, I think that we just… In the playoffs we choked a 12-4 lead on the second map, so that doesn't help, right? I think it's also a lack of synergy still. I feel like we could be winning clutch situations and more hard rounds. I feel like it did come down a lot to just overall synergy.

Was this synergy something that you worked on during this bootcamp that you mentioned before the Major? Do you feel like you're back on the level that made you reach the final?

We put in all the focus on these kinds of small things, and on stuff like on executing the rounds, clutches. Basically we tried to put a little bit more accent onto all the small things. It's been going well in practice, but we'll see how it goes in an actual game.

Just to also touch on your map pool, you guys played quite a lot of Overpass, and it seemed like you're really enjoying it. Considering so many teams perma-ban it, is it hard to rely on a map like that? Because you might not get to play it in a lot of series.

Well, I think it isn't that big of a pool of people. It's not like we only have Overpass, right? I feel like we don't have to rely on it. I think even recently we've been losing more than winning, so I don't feel like it's a map that we are dependent on. We've been improving our map pool, and I don't think it's that big of an issue. We're happy to play Overpass if we get it, but it's not like the end of the world if we can't.

You're starting Stage 2, where you're one of the favorites to get through. What is something that you guys would be happy with achieving in Cologne?

Well, obviously to make it Stage 3 would already be pretty nice. The playoffs will be really cool, at least from what I've heard from other people. The crowd here is really great, and it would just be really, really nice to experience playing in front of it.
