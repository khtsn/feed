Five biggest storylines of the PGL Cluj-Napoca playoffs

For the first time since their return to hosting events, a non-Major PGL tournament has drawn nearly every heavyweight in the Counter-Strike scene. Just two top-ten teams, Spirit and Liquid, have declined invitations in what is a huge shift towards the Romanian tournament organizer, who are now set to reap the rewards as the competition moves to the BT Arena in Cluj-Napoca.

The eight playoff teams are among the ten highest-ranked in attendance, as we are only missing a FaZe team that isn't playing up to its full potential and G2, who were eliminated by Natus Vincere in a high-octane clash in the 2-2 pool.

Here are the biggest storylines heading into the playoffs, and why this tournament carries extra weight for certain teams.

MOUZ won it last year, can they repeat it against the big dogs?

MOUZ are the reigning PGL Cluj-Napoca champions after winning their only trophy of 2025 in the Romanian city, but the victory came with a fair few caveats.

For starters, last year's tournament only had four top-ten teams in attendance, while their grand final opponents, Falcons, were yet to complete their blockbuster signing of Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov.

This time around, MOUZ's job will be much harder. The quarter-final draw has pitted them against Natus Vincere, and if they are to win that, they will face off against either Falcons or PARIVISION.

On the other side of the bracket are their usual executioners, Vitality, but even earning another swipe at Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut and company will already be a tough task.

Still, the BT Arena remains one of the few coliseums where MOUZ have positive memories, having shaken off their arena struggles in Cluj-Napoca last year. Replicating that run will be difficult, but perhaps familiarity with the stage can help push them across the finish line.

Have TheMongolZ found another star?

When Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold departed The MongolZ, many considered their time as trophy contenders done and dusted.

The Mongolian squad temporarily replaced him with Unudelger "⁠controlez⁠" Baasanjargal, who, to his credit, surprised with what he brought to the table, but the 28-year-old was never a long-term option.

Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar, who is a much easier fit to replace the explosive Senzu and #42 in the top 50 prospects ranking, was selected to fill out the roster. His start has been hot-and-cold, as could be expected from a player with no international experience, but his performances at PGL Cluj-Napoca are showing a glimpse of what the youngster could become.

He had several peaks, such as putting up a staggering 2.61 rating on Dust2 against paiN, and a 1.59 on Nuke against Natus Vincere, while his only quiet game came against the imperious Vitality.

So far, he is leading The MongolZ in the stats department with a team-high 1.15 rating in the group stage. It remains to be seen how he will handle playing in front of a crowd, but The MongolZ might have just begun another journey from pretenders to contenders with their young rifler.

Will Falcons finally break their duck?

On the other side of the trophy-wait spectrum, Falcons are quickly running out of time. The team with possibly the highest expectations put on them by the community has only won one big event trophy in their time in Counter-Strike — and not a particularly elite one, at that —, and none since signing m0NESY.

It's not for lack of opportunity, either. Since their triumph at PGL Bucharest last year, Falcons have reached five grand finals, only to fall at the final hurdle each time.

While some, like the BLAST Bounty final where they used Matej "⁠NucleonZ⁠" Trajkoski instead of Maxim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin, are excusable, no one can deny that a worrying trend is emerging for the star-studded roster.

This time, they will need to start their playoff road by righting their wrongs from Malta. Then, provided they win their semi-final and everything goes according to expectations on the other side of the bracket, the history-making Vitality could await in a best-of-five final.

So far, m0NESY (1.39 rating) and kyousuke (1.35) are looking in top form. The real test will be whether they can keep that level when it matters most.

Can Vitality be stopped?

Debating everyone's title chances is always entertaining, but the reality may be simpler — Vitality could just be a cut above the rest.

We all know what they achieved in 2025, and it doesn't look like this year will be any different for Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's troops. So far, they won 11 out of their 12 matches with an absolutely bonkers map record of 24-5.

Their talisman, ZywOo, who equalled his best-ever tournament at IEM Krakow with a 1.59 average, is as indomitable as ever in Romania and sits at the top of the tournament's stats leaderboard with a 1.41 rating as he looks to lead Vitality's dominating era into another year.

"This tournament, I wouldn't say it's a big priority," Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan admitted to HLTV after he and his team made the playoffs. "But every time we come to a tournament, even though we talk about 'it's not gonna be a priority,' everybody is screaming, hyping, prepping, the same shit, so it's not like anything is different."

Reality is, even if Vitality aren't at their best, they might just be too good for everyone else.

Can one of the underdogs upset the apple cart?

On top of the four already mentioned squads, a further four will enter the crucible of the BT Arena with their own agendas.

Natus Vincere will finally give Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan the chance to appear in front of his home crowd after declining an invitation to the same tournament last year, where the rifler will count on a raucous support from the crowd. Then again, they are playing MOUZ in their first game, so being crowd favorites is a given.

PARIVISION have already won a tier-one tournament this year at BLAST Bounty, and even though Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali likened it to a CCT LAN at the time, it was anything but. Granted, their return to competition at IEM Krakow wasn't as impressive, and they have been given a tough test against Falcons in their Cluj quarter-final, but Jame and his disciples have some miracles up their sleeve.

Aurora are another side with famous arena struggles, something that was exacerbated by their signing of the very inexperienced Caner "⁠soulfly⁠" Kesici. The star-turned-anchor had a stage debut to forget at IEM Krakow, where he and his teammates went missing against FURIA and won just 11 rounds across two maps, but maybe that was the kind of trial by fire required to spark a stronger response.

Aurora's 21-year-old has excelled at times in the group stages, both in Krakow and Cluj, so it may just be a matter of time before the former top 50 prospect, who peaked in 22nd place when the ranking first came out in September 2025, finds his footing and gets comfortable in the high-stakes arena matches.

Sure, calling FURIA an underdog may be a stretch, but on the other hand, every playoff team not named Vitality has a reason to be given that moniker, so bear with me.

FURIA has gone through a restructuring during the winter after ending 2025 on a low at the Budapest Major, and have needed to seriously rely on Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko's brilliance to get them over the line in several games so far.

"I always believe that you've got to be transforming yourselves as players and as a team in order to be finding this chemistry and finding what you need to beat the good teams," Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo told HLTV in Krakow.

"We made [the changes] because we believe that with time, those changes can be more helpful than what might seem at the moment."

FURIA still reached the grand final in Poland despite their ongoing transformation, and even took a lead against Vitality in the best-of-five showdown.

They could well be on a collision course against the apEX-led squad in Romania if both teams win their quarter-final bouts, meaning FURIA would once again get to challenge ZywOo and company, but this time in a best-of-three game.
