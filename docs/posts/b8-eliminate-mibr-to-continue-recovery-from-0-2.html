B8 eliminate MIBR to continue recovery from 0-2

A first-map drubbing did little to dissuade B8 as they enter the deciding 2-2 pool with wind in their sails.

B8 have eliminated MIBR from the IEM Cologne Major to enter the 2-2 pool in Stage 2 off the back of a hard-earned 2-1 victory.

Just like on Sunday against GamerLegion, the Ukrainian side looked down and out after a Mirage drubbing, where a cosmetic second-half comeback masked just how dominant MIBR looked to kick off the series, spearheaded by the support cast of Carlos "⁠venomzera⁠" Eduardo and Breno "⁠brnz4n⁠" Poletto.

But B8 entered their opponents' pick of Nuke with a just as convincing CT side performance with Danylo "⁠s1zzi⁠" Vinnyk making his aggressive AWPing work, and although MIBR made it competitive in the second half, Andrii "⁠npl⁠" Kukharskyi ensured it would go to three with a crucial quad-kill in a 2v4.

It came down to Ancient, where B8 did well to prevent Felipe "⁠insani⁠" Yuji's immense output from having too much of an effect as they exploited the sides of the map in return on the T side, and closed it without too much drama once on the defense.

insani ended the series on top of the charts at a 1.37 rating, but couldn't prevent an early elimination for the Brazilian side, who had hoped to build on their top-four finish at CS Asia Championships.

They were ultimately let down by Klimentii "⁠kl1m⁠" Krivosheev, who got off to a great start in Stage 1 and in their opening Stage 2 win against MIBR, but then disappeared from there on to end the stage with a 0.91 average rating.

The Russian's future with the Brazilian side is up in the air, as G2, who loaned the AWPer to MIBR last year, are prepared to listen to other offers for him.
