MOUZ into semis over B8 after triple OT Ancient

MOUZ have beaten B8 2-0 to set up a semi-final against Falcons in the arena tomorrow.

Dennis "⁠sycrone⁠" Nielsen's steeds looked to be on course for a simple victory after superb CT sides took them from 5-8 to 13-8 on Overpass and to a 9-3 lead on Ancient. As ever, Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman led the way with a 2.04 CT rating in the two-map series, taking his monthly average on defence to 1.60.

But B8 and Andrii "⁠npl⁠" Kukharskyi had other ideas as they launched a CT spree of their own. Star player npl won a 1v3 late in the first half to spark some hope, and it was his pistol 4k that ensured B8 got the right start to their own defensive half.

Clip: npl 1v3s to kick start B8 fightback

Overtime ensued, and a host of clutches went either way as the teams battled through three sets of extra rounds. Justinas "⁠jL⁠" Lekavicius won four clutches himself, the same as B8's entire team, and three were on T side where MOUZ really struggled.

"Losing this game would have been very painful," jL said post-game. "We stayed in the game, our mental is very strong. They had their chances, according to statistics we struggle on this map."

While the focus on MOUZ's new era has understandably been on xertioN's CT ratings, it was also a good sign that Adrian "⁠xelex⁠" Vincze's fragging has improved at CAC. The youngster has a 1.15 rating so far, and was alongside xertioN on CT side as the driving force for MOUZ in the marathon on Ancient.

Clip: One of jL's four clutches on Ancient was this 1v3.

Ahead of the showdown with Falcons tomorrow, MOUZ will know that this series could have been converted in a far less stressful fashion. The game plan simply didn't work on Ancient, and a B rush call in round 24 was illustrative of an in-game leader out of ideas and losing faith in the default.

In the end, jL's clutches and more CT brilliance got them over the line, but this was not the convincing win MOUZ sought ahead of facing down the heavy tournament favorites in the arena.
