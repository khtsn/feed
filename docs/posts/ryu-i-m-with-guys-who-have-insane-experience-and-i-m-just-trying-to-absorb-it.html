ryu: "I'm with guys who have insane experience and I'm just trying to absorb it"

Astralis' latest rebuild has gone from strength to strength in recent months, their latest two results seeing them grab third at ESL Pro League before making it all the way to the final in their last outing at PGL Bucharest.

Gytis "⁠ryu⁠" Glušauskas has been an integral part of that rebuild, a one-time HLTV Prospect before graduating at the end of 2025. The Lithuanian had precious little experience at tier-one before joining Astralis, yet has helped to provide a solid foundation for their recent rise in stature.

"It's been going in an upward trajectory overall," ryu said of his "first real tier-one experience." "It has been a lot of getting used to stuff, like now in America, with jet lag, things you wouldn't usually have to think about when playing online."

"There's a lot of stuff to learn, and you don't have much room for mistakes. You don't have a free game or easy games, so you always need to be ready, always have good prep."

With another LAN campaign in front of him, ryu spoke to HLTV about adjusting to life at tier-one, the development of Astralis' current roster, and his thoughts about the upcoming IEM Cologne Major.

When you came on, Astralis was a bit of a struggling team trying to find an identity. From when you got in until now, how has it been?

It has been nice, it's been my first real tier-one experience. We started the year not the best, we didn't make the LAN at BLAST Bounty and didn't make the playoffs at the first three events we played on LAN, but I think that it has been nice afterwards.

We made a top three at EPL, and then top two in Bucharest, so it's been going in an upward trajectory overall. It has been a lot of getting used to stuff, like now in America, with jet lag, things you wouldn't usually have to think about when playing online.

Regarding your development as a player, you came from Monte and are now playing at events like this Rivals. How has your growth been?

There's a lot of stuff to learn, and you don't have much room for mistakes. You don't have a free game or easy games, so you always need to be ready, always have good prep. When you have a bad start to a game, the other team will take advantage of it, so you always have to try hard.

For me, personally, in my development, the coaching staff and HooXi have been helping me a lot. They're very open to talking, and whenever I ask for feedback, it's always good, so it's been good so far.

What's it like playing for an IGL like HooXi? How has he impacted your play?

He gives a lot of freedom to his players, and the way he calls, he has a lot of trust in you, and he tries to make you feel good in the game. It's kind of similar to what I had in Monte with Gizmy as the IGL. I think he plays a lot into players' strengths, as well, so for me it wasn't the biggest transition, but of course, going into tier one is still a transition, but I wouldn't say it was that drastic.

You're not playing at the level of the top top teams yet, but you're putting up a fight. What do you think is the potential for this roster, and do you think you can become a proper trophy contender?

We can be a hard team to play against, yeah. A lot of times it's up to us. Like any other team, we have issues, and for example, we sometimes have issues with consistency. Like one day you wake up and play really well, and another day it's the other way around, right? That's something we have to work on.

Also, these playoff games. We haven't had a lot of them, but it's about playing the same way as we did in those, and I think that we can be a really annoying team to play against if we just have the consistency there.

You came into Astralis, a huge name in Denmark, after it opened itself up to an international lineup. How has it been with the fans and the organization in this new era? What's the culture like on the team?

I don't know how it was exactly before, but speaking about the culture of the team, it's very nice because everyone is open and putting in a lot of effort. The way we work with each other is very productive, and even if we didn't start the year in the best way, you can see that there's progress from event to event. Overall, we're just very productive in what we're doing.

You're playing with guys who have won trophies. As the new guy, how is it playing with them, and how have they taken you into the team?

I'm with guys who have insane experience, some of them, and I'm just trying to absorb it. I just try to learn from them, right? They're obviously very good at the game and understand it very well. It would be a waste not to, you know?

It's all about improving myself, and I think that the people in the team are very open with this. I can always ask whatever or discuss the game with anyone, and I feel like it's very nice to have because it helps me a lot.

When we played in Bucharest, I feel like in the final there was a lot of pressure. From ourselves, not from anyone else. But I feel like it's just about learning to deal with it.

Gytis "⁠ryu⁠" Glušauskas

You're opening against G2 here at BLAST Rivals. What do you make of that game?

They're a very good team. They recently changed malbsMd for NertZ, and the team looks really solid. I think it'll be a nice game to play. What matters to us is that we show up, play our game, and that we don't lose to ourselves.

Since the qualification for the Major, you're going through some events in the lead-up. What are your expectations as you prepare for the Major and ramp up for it?

If we speak about our gameplay, it's obviously about fixing mistakes, but I think it's also about getting used to play higher-stake matches and learning how to deal with pressure, because when we played in Bucharest, I feel like in the final there was a lot of pressure. From ourselves, not from anyone else. But I feel like it's just about learning to deal with it.

For more result-oriented goals, for this event, it's making the playoffs. There are some teams that have unfortunate situations, like FUT playing with their coach, so for us, it is making the playoffs.

We're working with the staff, with the sports psychologist, and overall, in the game, it's all about getting used to the environment and the pressure, and improving on our mistakes. I don't think there's anything special we're trying to do, just trying to be in the best shape for the Major, so that means playing well individually and as a team. These next events are basically practice for that.
