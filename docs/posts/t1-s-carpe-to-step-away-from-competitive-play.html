T1's carpe to step away from competitive play

Longtime T1 T1 Korea Unranked stax Kim Gu-taek (김구택) Meteor Kim Tae-oh (김태오) BuZz Yu Byeong-cheol (유병철) DH Kang Dong-ho (강동호) iZu Ham Woo-ju (함우주) Munchkin Byeon Sang-beom (변상범) player Lee "carpe" Jae-hyeok (이재혁) will step away from competitive gaming, departing T1 after nearly four years of service for the organization.

"For over 9 years, I have received more love and support than I could ever deserve," carpe said. To all my teammates and staff who worked and suffered alongside me throughout my career, and to my one and only team who always showed unwavering belief in me — thank you from the bottom of my heart. Though we won't see each other for a little while, I will cherish these precious memories deep in my heart. I will look forward to the day I stand before you all again, whether as 'Carpe' or simply as 'Jae-hyeok Lee.' To all the fans who have cheered for me until now, thank you so much."

carpe with T1 at Masters Tokyo in 2023. (Photo by Colin Young-Wolff/Riot Games)

carpe joined T1 in 2022 as the team moved its operations from North America to South Korea. A former Overwatch DPS, carpe competed for FaZe Clan and Philadelphia Fusion in the title, and also won the Overwatch World Cup with South Korea in 2018. He moved into Valorant in 2022 after wrapping up a four-year career in Overwatch. He was primarily T1's Initiator player in his first year with the org, competing alongside former Overwatch players Munchkin and Sayaplayer .

T1 ended that year third in Pacific, qualifying the team for Masters Tokyo and Champions Los Angeles. However, carpe was the sixth man. T1 started the year with Sylvan as its starting Controller player, but was replaced by carpe after three games in Pacific Kickoff. carpe played in the team's opening match at Masters Bangkok, but was soon after replaced by Sylvan as the team went on a trophy-lifting run.

carpe holding the Masters Bangkok trophy. (Photo by Colin Young-Wolff/Riot Games)

Since his match in Bangkok, carpe has been T1's designated sixth-man. Even as the team moved from Sylvan to DH and then DH to the return of Munchkin, carpe has been ever-present in the T1 room.

Currently 27 and turning 28 later this year, carpe is approaching the age at which all Korean males must address mandatory military service, which may be the source of his decision to step away.
