Rare Atom withdraw from Yuqilin PoB S3

Rare Atom have released a statement confirming their withdrawal from Yuqilin Pinnacle of Battle Season 3.

The news comes after the Chinese team forfeited their match against Lynn Vision earlier today, with major ramifications for SemperFi and LVG in their quest to claim a spot at the IEM Cologne Major.

The forfeit has drawn the ire of SemperFi manager BenTheLegend, who accused Rare Atom of "giving" their compatriots VRS points.

However, according to statements from both Rare Atom and tournament operator Yuqilin Esports, the forfeit decision was taken because of a dispute around the rules for when a team arrives late to a match.

"Throughout the event, the organizer only informed teams on the first day of the offline stage that they needed to arrive at the venue 90 minutes before the match," Rare Atom's Weibo statement began, which was translated by HLTV using Deepl. "We complied and arrived on time for previous matches. After the match on April 2, we asked venue staff and were told that we needed to arrive at 13:00 the next day."

That would put the arrival time at one hour before the match, rather than 90 minutes.

"However, at 12:08 today, we were suddenly informed that we needed to arrive at the venue by 12:30. Before arriving, we had not been notified of any details regarding potential penalties."

Due to rain, Rare Atom say they were delayed further, and only arrived at 13:28, 32 minutes before the match was set to start, and were made to forfeit map one for being late as well as losing their pick-ban privileges.

Parts of the rules were unclear and disputable

Rare Atom on why they withdrew from PoB

The crux of the issue comes with the next part of the statement, where Rare Atom complain about a lack of communication from the TO: "Before arriving at the venue, we had not received any official rulebook from the organizer."

They therefore take issue with the double penalty of being made to forfeit map one and losing their pick-ban rights: "We immediately communicated with the event organizers, stating that we had not been informed of the relevant penalties in advance and that parts of the rules were unclear and disputable. As a result, we have decided to forfeit this match and withdraw from the remaining POBS3 tournament."

A Rare Atom spokesperson added: "We believe the penalty was disproportionate to the delay for map veto (not match time), and it is difficult to understand how a punishment not outlined in the rulebook was enforced. [Rare Atom] would like to reiterate that there was absolutely no intentional attempt to give up VRS points."

A translated version of Rare Atom's Weibo statement

Tournament operator Yuqilin Esports released their own statement to HLTV, which conflicts with Rare Atom's account: "Today, April 3rd, RA stated that they were unable to arrive by the check-in time (90 minutes before the match [12:30]) due to weather conditions. Consequently, we consulted with the LVG team and granted RA an additional 30 minutes (13:00) to ensure the match could proceed smoothly."

BoP rule 2.4.5:

If a participating player or team representative is more than 5 minutes late during the designated ban/pick time, their right to make remaining map selections and/or bans will be randomly determined by the tournament management team.

This is a slightly different timeline to Rare Atom, who say they were told to arrive by 13:00 by admins on April 2, only to be "suddenly" told at 12:08 on April 3 that the real deadline was 12:30.

Yuqilin Esports continued: "At 13:25, we notified RA of their default loss on the first map and asked LVG to proceed with the [pick-ban]. After the match maps were finalized, the RA team finally arrived at 13:28. They raised an objection to the penalty, expressed a lack of understanding regarding the default loss rule, and, after further communication, ultimately decided to withdraw from the match."

Rare Atom's decision to withdraw also saw them forfeit a match against Change The Game, who will move on to the consolidation final in their stead.

SemperFi's Major hopes now rest on GangKui Cup, and how well Lynn Vision perform in the rest of PoB.
