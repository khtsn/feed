FaZe humiliate FURIA to make Rivals playoffs

FaZe are through to the quarter-finals of BLAST Rivals Fort Worth after handing FURIA a humbling 2-1 defeat in the lower bracket of Group B (13-4 Dust2, 7-13 Mirage, 13-3 Nuke).

After a somewhat limp debut as FaZe's IGL against Natus Vincere on Wednesday, Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken called a masterclass on both Dust2 and Nuke to overwhelm FURIA on either side of a loss on Mirage, and they got the job done in the most FaZe way possible with a last-second retake in a 2v4 to close out Nuke to add even more insult to injury.

It was the first time FURIA and FaZe faced off since a 2-2 Swiss matchup at the Perfect World Shanghai Major, a time when both teams featured vastly different rosters, and only adds to FURIA's bad fortunes after a lackluster start to the year.

Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo's men have struggled to mirror the form that saw them win four titles in the second half of 2025, and were looking for redemption in Fort Worth after failing to capitalize on directly qualifying for the semi-finals at IEM Rio. But a loss to GamerLegion in their opener, and now to FaZe playing only their second match with Twistzz as in-game leader and Ryan "⁠Neityu⁠" Aubry as a stand-in, can be seen as little other than an embarrassment and leaves the team with much to think about in the next week before PGL Astana.

It helped for FaZe that Helvijs "⁠broky⁠" Saukants had his third-best map at an MVP-eligible event this year, a 1.48 rating with several high-impact openers and six multi-kills, that FaZe narrowly won a few early rounds by way of clutches and disadvantaged recoveries, and David "⁠frozen⁠" Čerňanský looked more like himself after a flat showing against Natus Vincere, but all of that was enabled, in part, by Twistzz's strong calling.

A dismal effort from FURIA played a part, too, with the team looking equally as lackluster as they had in a collapse on Nuke against GamerLegion the day before.

And though there was some redemption for the Brazilian-majority side on Mirage, where excellence from Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko and Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato — including a 1v3 clutch from the latter to win a force in the second half, and a drop-off from broky — helped them to a 13-7 win, the wheels fell off the bus again on the Nuke decider.

Another phenomenal offense from Twistzz, both in calling and individual rounds, saw FaZe romp to a 10-2 lead at the break as the Canadian repeatedly threaded gaps in FURIA's defenses alongside near-flawless Upper executions from his team.

A quad-kill from Jakub "⁠jcobbb⁠" Pietruszewski in the second pistol sealed FURIA's fate, but FaZe rubbed salt in the wound as they closed out with a late retake in a 2v4 to close out the series.
