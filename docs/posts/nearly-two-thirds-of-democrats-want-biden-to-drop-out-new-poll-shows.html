Nearly Two-Thirds of Democrats Want Biden to Drop Out, New Poll Shows

This is not the first survey to find a majority of voters holding this view, but now a sizable majority of voters from the president’s own party are calling for a different nominee.

In early July, just under half of Democrats said Mr. Biden should step aside, according to a New York Times/Siena national survey. A survey taken over the following week found a slightly increased share, 56 percent of Democrats, saying the president should not remain the nominee.

Since Mr. Biden’s halting debate performance in June, the president and top officials in his campaign have contended that, while calls for him to step aside have come from party elites, he still has the support of regular everyday Democrats. But increasingly that does not seem to be borne out in polling data.

Among Democrats, majorities of younger and older voters said Mr. Biden should drop out, according to the AP-NORC poll. In fact, Black Democrats are among the only demographic group within the party where a majority of voters do not say the president should step aside. Black Democrats were split about evenly on whether Mr. Biden should drop out of the race.

At the same time, just one-quarter of Republicans said Mr. Trump should withdraw from the race.
