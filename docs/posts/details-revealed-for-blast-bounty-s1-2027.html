Details revealed for BLAST Bounty S1 2027

BLAST have unveiled the specifics behind the season-opening tournament of 2027.

BLAST has announced the details for BLAST Bounty Season 1 2027, which will open the year from January 11-24 in Malta.

The most notable feature of the new iteration of Bounty, which was announced earlier this year, is that the $1,250,000 tournament will be played entirely on LAN, eliminating the online portion which has been a feature of previous editions of Bounty. The tournament is also set to feature only 16 teams, as opposed to 32.

New format

There will be three stages to the new Bounty format: A play-in, a last chance qualifier, and the playoffs.

The play-in will see the 16 teams split across four groups of four teams, and will play out a double-elimination bracket. Group winners advance to the quarter-finals of the playoffs, second and third place head to the last chance qualifier, whilst fourth place is elimination.

The eight teams who make the last chance qualifier will face off for four playoff spots. The winners move on, the losers are eliminated.

Finally, a single-elimination bracket will take over for the playoffs, which features a best-of-five grand final.

Drafting remains

BLAST Bounty will retain a drafting element within the format. The top four seeds will be placed in separate play-in groups and the rest of the teams will pick their groups, with the lower seeds picking first.

There is also a draft element to the playoffs, with the play-in winners drafting where the last chance qualifier matches are placed in the playoff bracket, starting with the highest seed.

Prize pool breakdown

Separate to the prize pool, BLAST will be handing out acceptance fees to teams that accept their invite to Bounty, based on their ranking at the time of invitation. $60,000 is handed out to ranks 1-4, $30,000 to ranks 5-8, and $5,000 to ranks 9-16.

The prize pool of $1,250,000 is split into three portions: bounties, team payments and prize money.

Each play-in group winner will be slapped with a $40,000 bounty, second -place with a $25,000 bounty, and third-place with a $15,000 bounty, totalling $320,000 in bounty money to be claimed.

$180,000 is set aside for prize money. Top finishers in their play-in group receive $30,000, teams that finish last receive $5,000, and teams that make it through the play-in but exit in the last chance qualifier earn $10,000. Those that make it to the playoffs through the last chance qualifier receive no prize money, but will earn at least $15,000 from a team bounty.
