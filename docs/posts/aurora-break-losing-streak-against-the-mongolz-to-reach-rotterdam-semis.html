Aurora break losing streak against The MongolZ to reach Rotterdam semis

Aurora have put an end to the five-game losing streak they had in head-to-head matches against The MongolZ dating back to May 2025, when they beat them last at PGL Astana, with a convincing 2-0 victory on Inferno and Mirage to secure a spot in the BLAST Open Rotterdam semi-finals.

The series kicked off on Aurora's Inferno pick and the Turkish squad were quick to get ahead despite losing the pistol round as they chained four successful attacks in a row after winning the second-round forcebuy.

The two teams went into the half at a stalemate, but multi-kill rounds by Ismailcan "⁠XANTARES⁠" Dörtkardeş, Özgür "⁠woxic⁠" Eker, and Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın set the tone for Aurora as they went on to have a smooth 7-0 CT side to win it 13-6 and get ahead in the series.

Wicadia downed three in pistols with a dualie spam on Mirage's T ramp, and Aurora went on to dominate the early stages of the second map. Even after The MongolZ won a B hit to get on the board in the sixth round, Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli did so much economic damage that a medium buy was stopped the next round as the Turks cruised to a 7-1 lead.

Aurora increased their 8-4 halftime lead after the second pistol round and hit double digits, but before they could go on and close it out The MongolZ put together a comeback attempt, eventually falling short as the Turkish side sealed it 13-9.
