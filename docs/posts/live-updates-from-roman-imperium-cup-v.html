Bogdan: "HOTU were a class above us"

vs.

Jędrzej "⁠Bogdan⁠" Rokita stopped by HLTV's microphone after Betclic were dismantled by HOTU in a two-map series, with the second map ending in a 13-0 rout.

On what went wrong in the series: "I'm not quite sure, to be honest. From a spectator POV, we just got wrecked in everything we tried. We tried fast things. We tried slow things, and they were just smacking us. They were definitely better than us, to be honest. A class above us. We just got smacked. That's all."

On the main takeaways from this event: "It's hard to say right now. It's hard to say that the tournament was positive after such a beating. But still, our plan was to get out of the hard group that we had. We were facing good opponents and we got out of the group. We got two wins, VRS is on plus, so it was definitely a good tournament from that perspective.

We wish we played better in this match, but it is what it is. It's progress. We set our goal for those tournaments when the roster was created, because we've only been playing together for a month and a half, that we want to be competitive in those tournaments. And we were competitive in the groups. In this match, we weren't competitive at all. They were just a class above us."

On the goals for the next events: "We just want to continue to improve and get experience. We have a 17-year-old guy, and a guy who played two officials before coming to us. It's constant progress. We just want to climb the ranking and play better. That's all."

gla1ve: "If we can do this consistently, I'll be really happy"

On making it through the groups after a 13-2 opening loss:  "Of course, I'm really happy for the boys. We have put a lot of work during this month of practice, and it's starting to show some good results already. We're able to beat a top 20 ranked team. So I'm really, really happy."

On if it's harder to be a coach than a player in these tense games:  "Yeah, it's a bit harder. I mean, I try to take timeouts when I need them, but it's also about finding a balance. Sometimes, they [the opponent] also take a timeout, and I can use that to talk. And it's also about not taking timeouts sometimes, so they don't get to discuss what they want to plan. There are a lot of mind games going on with it, and it's something I have to get used to.  I mean, of course I was thinking about it, but I'm not really used to it."

On the slow start to the tournament: "We misplayed a lot of the rounds that we prepared for NIP on Nuke. That was a bit sad. Because if you misplay, like, three or four rounds on MR12, especially T side Nuke, then you're not going to win. They were also shooting really hard in that first game, while we weren't. I think there was also a bit of nerves because it was the first official on LAN and stuff like this.  I think we started playing much better after this game. I had a good team talk outside, and the boys really stepped up."

On whether this tournament is a success already:  "That's hard to say. Of course, I'm really happy that we beat NIP, but the hardest thing in CS2 is to have consistency. So if we can do this consistently, then I will be really happy, for sure."

pita: "A quick U-turn; it can change so fast in this game"

vs.

EYEBALLERS coach Faruk "⁠pita⁠" Pita stopped by HLTV's microphone after the 2-1 loss to Betclic, which sent his team out of the tournament.

He lamented that the team let a 9-3 lead slip on Inferno against Gentle Mates due to miscommunication.

"Small miscoms, small decisions not going our way," he said, adding that he thinks EYEBALLERS' Major hopes are dead and buried after this match.

On being eliminated from the event on day 1: "I think everyone is very upset, obviously. We had higher hopes, even though, coming into this tournament I think we had a bit of a rocky period. I think we had a pretty decent autumn and winter. We started off the year very well versus FaZe Clan and did pretty okay versus number one in the world. And since then, we've had some... I would say we're in some slump. But when we came into this tournament, I think we were hoping for some redemption.

We started off the day not very well but decently by beating Betclic, and then obviously winning the first map against Gentle Mates and being up 9-3 in the second map. And looking at us right now, we have to leave already after the first day…"

On whether the team got complacent on Inferno against Gentle Mates: "I think there were some miscoms, I would say, on the T side of Inferno. I think the start was... I think they were up 4-0 and our first weapon round was just miscommunication. I think we maybe let it slip a little bit, even though we know Gentle Mates is a really good team on Inferno, especially their CT side is really, really good. Not that easy to play against.

But we had a lot of situations, I would say, on T side Inferno that I think we should have won. A 3v3 on B site afterplant. We also had some rounds where we got into the site and we should just be able to clean that up pretty easy. But small miscoms, small decisions not going our way. So yeah, that sucks. And the third map, Ancient, was super close.

On the impact of this loss on their Major hopes: "We'll see. I don't know if the Major is still a possibility for us. If it is, obviously we'll just try to go all in. But I think after this loss, we can maybe count out the Major and try to maybe decide what we're going to play based on the long term."

On the main lessons from this event: "We need to keep on developing our playbook, I would say. We have a pretty loose style that fits these players very well. But as every time when the loose play style is not working, it's usually because we're not shooting our opponents the way we should. And obviously that makes it difficult. I would have to review the demo to exactly see what we have to keep on working.

A bad day, obviously. I think Betclic played really well. Obviously, Gentle Mates also played super well. So two really tough opponents. We could have been at the restaurant half-celebrating that we made it through as number one in the group. A quick U-turn. It can change so fast in this game. So it is what it is. Sometimes you win, sometimes you lose."
