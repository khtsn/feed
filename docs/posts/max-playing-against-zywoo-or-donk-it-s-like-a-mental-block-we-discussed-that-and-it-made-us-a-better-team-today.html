max: "Playing against ZywOo or donk, it's like a mental block; we discussed that and it made us a better team today"

It has been a historic Major for 9z, from becoming the first non-Brazilian South American team to secure a top-16 spot at a Major to reaching the playoffs after a Stage 3 run that featured a barn-burner series against Spirit and an upset win over Vitality.

Those were both teams that Maximiliano "⁠max⁠" Gonzalez's side suffered landslide losses to at their first Big Event appearance of the year at BLAST Open Rotterdam, but the team learned quickly from those defeats and from a run to the quarter-finals of PGL Astana.

"We knew how we wanted to play CS, but sometimes playing against ZywOo or donk, it's like a mental block," the Uruguayan in-game leader told HLTV on the media day ahead of the playoffs. "We try to take confidence — I don't know how to say it in English — but not strafing, not putting love in the round, just try to go straight, press W. And we discussed that and other things that make us a better team today."

max also spoke about his team's development at the event and upcoming quarter-final match against FURIA, where he hopes 9z will be more prepared for the arena after a dismal showing in Astana.

"The playoffs in Astana helped us, [for this] to not be the first arena for the team," he said. "We are a little bit more experienced, and we experienced things in Astana so that this time, it's not new. We know now how it's going to be. I think mostly it's the calm that we need to work on and pay attention to more."

Media day before the playoffs at the Major. A huge result for you already, so talk to me about that and making this run because what a run it has been.

Yeah, it was an amazing run. We came to this tournament to go to Stage 3, but I didn't think we would make it to the playoffs. For us, it's a dream coming true. Looking forward now.

And we could see how much it meant to you in each match. Some frustration with the Spirit game, but a lot of elation as well with some of the wins, like the Vitality one. Tell me how it's been playing at this Major and leveling up as you go through it.

Yeah, we had very emotional games against Spirit. It was very emotional, and the next day, we played against Aurora, and we couldn't play our best CS. I think it was because we didn't have time to discuss a lot of things that happened in the Spirit match.

But after that, we played against MongolZ, and we could show the most important thing in this team, which is the resilience. It's not easy to go 2-0 to 2-2 and go forward. So for me, I'm very happy with the performance of the boys.

The motivation for the Major for us is everything, and that helps us to communicate more, to take initiative

When you're playing at the Major, the competition gets better and better. How have 9z handled that? How have you changed your approach or changed things as the competition has gotten better?

The most important thing for us is motivation, because we are not accustomed to playing Majors. It's my third in all of my career, and I am 27. The motivation for the Major for us is everything, and that helps us to communicate more, to take initiative, and to make decisions as a group. We have very [high] energy, so that made us look like a good team.

I remember talking to you in Astana, and that was your first Big Event after that Rotterdam run where you lost to Spirit and Vitality. You and BIT both said that you learned a lot from those games, and it felt like we saw it here. How did you adapt so quickly? What did you actually take from those games to be so good here?

I think it's more mental, because we knew how we wanted to play CS, but sometimes playing against ZywOo or donk, it's like a mental block. We try to take confidence — I don't know how to say it in English — but not strafing, not putting love in the round, just try to go straight, press W. And we discussed that and other things that make us a better team today.

luchov has been having an insane tournament. Tell me how it's been seeing him rise to this level and how he's been elevating as he goes through the games.

I saw he had potential years ago, and now he can demonstrate because we are playing really good CS. Not just luchov, but the other ones as well. meyern is playing really good as well, he's in a new role, and dgt comes back stronger in the mental way. He has helped me a lot with the calls on CT side, mostly. And HUASO is always in the same mood; he's always happy. He's a very [vocal] guy, and he's helping a lot with the communication on the team. So not [just] luchov, I think all the team is playing really good.

It's not the same to play in the group stage than with all the crowd and in the stadium, but it's more of the experience that we need

In the quarter-finals, you're going to play FURIA, which is a bit of a South American domestic matchup. There isn't much history between you, so how do you think that's going to go?

It's very funny when the match is between the same region; it's maybe more tight because we know the FURIA people, we know KSCERATO, yuurih, sidde, FalleN. We are not close, but we talk sometimes, so we are kind of close.

Our expectation is to go through to the semi-finals; I think we can do it. It's not the same to play in the group stage than with all the crowd and in the stadium, but it's more of the experience that we need. It's going to be a really good match for the crowd and all the people watching from their houses.

When you played that match in the arena in Astana, there was some nervousness. That was not the 9z that played in the group stage. What's going to be different this time?

The playoffs in Astana helped us [for this] to not be the first arena for the team. We are a little bit more experienced, and we experienced things in Astana so that this time, it's not new. We know now how it's going to be. I think mostly it's the calm that we need to work on and pay attention to more.
