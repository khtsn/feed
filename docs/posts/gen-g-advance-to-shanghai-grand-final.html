Gen.G advance to Shanghai grand final

Gen.G Gen.G Korea Rank #1 Munchkin Byeon Sang-beom (변상범) t3xture Kim Na-ra (김나라) Meteor Kim Tae-O (김태오) Lakia Kim Jong-min (김종민) Karon Kim Won-tae (김원태) have reached their second international grand final in a row after defeating G2 Esports G2 Esports North America Rank #3 icy Jacob Lange trent Trent Cairns valyn Jacob Batio JonahP Jonah Pulice leaf Nathan Orf 2-0. Including Pacific tournaments, Gen.G have participated in four out of four possible grand finals in 2024.

They will next play on Sunday with hopes of earning Pacific's first-ever international title. G2, meanwhile, will go down to the lower bracket, where they will play a best-of-five against either 100 Thieves or Team Heretics to define the second finalist of Masters Shanghai.

Gen.G opened the series with a pistol round win, and continued on to comfortably win the next few rounds. Meteor was a standout player in the beginning of their defense half, securing a series of multikills and first eliminations to help his team win seven rounds in a row. In round five, he found a 4K, which he capped off with a clutch to turn around what appeared to be a G2 stronghold on B. A tactical timeout after that round didn't immediately show results for G2, but they were able to win their first attack on round eight.

G2 maintained the momentum for another seven rounds, but Gen.G made sure to keep their lead for the remainder of the map. A nice thrifty round gave G2 their eighth point, but they were unable to win any rounds afterwards. With four round wins in a row, Gen.G closed off their opponent's map pick 13-8.

Meteor prevents G2 from appearing on the scoreboard.

The Gen.G dominance carried over to Ascent, as they gained an early lead on their offense. Statistically both teams' best map throughout 2024, Ascent slightly favored Gen.G on the attack side compared to G2. Gen.G were unrelenting, as four of their eight round wins in the first half came off the back of quadruple kills. Their round wins were not simply due to mechanical superiority, however. Munchkin and Karon had 11 and 12 assists in the half, respectively, which helped set up the team's entries for success.

As on Lotus, G2 began their recovery towards the end of the first half and the beginning of the second. They were slightly more successful this time around, but it was not enough. An ace against Gen.G's half buy earned icy the tenth round for his team, but Gen.G won the next two rounds to win the map 13-10.

“We're the favorites,” Meteor said after the win. “We'll make sure we prove ourselves [in the grand final].”
