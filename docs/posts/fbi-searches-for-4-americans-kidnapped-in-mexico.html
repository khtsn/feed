March 7, 2023, 8:59 a.m. ET

The F.B.I. is searching for four Americans who were kidnapped in Mexico last week after they crossed into the country from Texas for medical help, according to officials in both countries.

The four Americans drove into the border town of Matamoros, Mexico, from Brownsville, Texas, in a white minivan with North Carolina license plates on Friday when gunmen began firing on their vehicle, the F.B.I. said. The gunmen then put the Americans in another vehicle and drove them away.

During the episode, “an innocent Mexican citizen” was killed, according to Ken Salazar, the U.S. ambassador to Mexico. He said on Monday that multiple U.S. law enforcement agencies were working with the Mexican authorities to find and rescue the missing Americans. The U.S. government is offering $50,000 for information.

President Andrés Manuel López Obrador of Mexico told reporters on Monday that the Americans were in Mexico to “buy medicine.” He said that the governor of Tamaulipas, where the kidnapping happened, was working on the case. “I think it’s going to be solved,” he said. “That’s what I expect, wish.”

One of the Americans kidnapped had a medical appointment in Matamoros the morning of the kidnapping, according to a U.S. official who spoke on the condition of anonymity to discuss an open investigation.

Two people who said they were relatives of the kidnapped Americans told The Associated Press and CNN that the group was going to Mexico for tummy-tuck surgery for one member of the group.

American nationals frequently get entangled in violence in Mexico, crossing the border for cheap medication, medical procedures or even to buy groceries that are sold at lower prices than in the United States. But large swaths of the nearly 2,000 mile border are rife with criminal activity and drug cartels actively push drugs, migrants and even endangered wildlife into the United States for a profit, sometimes with the help of corrupt Mexican authorities.

Just a few days before the four Americans were kidnapped, an American citizen was killed by the Mexican military in the same state, Tamaulipas, as he was driving back from a nightclub with his friends on Feb. 26.
