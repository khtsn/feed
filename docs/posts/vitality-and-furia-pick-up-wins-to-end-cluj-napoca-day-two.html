Vitality and FURIA pick up wins to end Cluj-Napoca day two

Day two of PGL Cluj-Napoca ended with Vitality reverse-sweeping The MongolZ in the final 1-0 matchup, ensuring that all four of the top-ranked teams in the event advanced to the 2-0 qualifying round.

Garidmagnai "⁠bLitz⁠" Byambasuren's troops drew first blood in the series on their Mirage pick, making up for losing both pistols and a hot start by Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire by prevailing in messy mid-round situations.

But that was the only time The MongolZ looked competitive as an insult-to-injury 1v3 nina defuse by Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut saw Vitality extend their unbelievable Dust2 record since the start of 2025 to 37-4 before they made quick work of the Mongolian team on Nuke to close the series.

FURIA were given a run for their money by FUT, only just surviving argely thanks to the latter's inexperience and a late resurgence by Mareks "⁠YEKINDAR⁠" Gaļinskis in overtime on Overpass and on Anubis.

It was the third time the teams faced this year, having traded wins between the online stage of BLAST Bounty and at IEM Krakow, and the matchup delivered yet again. Excellence from Dmytro "⁠dem0n⁠" Myroshnychenko helped FUT win Mirage, but messy late rounds and panic set in when a series win began to look like a reality at 11-10 on Overpass.

YEKINDAR made up for a disastrous map with several entries to rally his team to victory, 22-18, and the Anubis decider was much the same. FUT made up for a slow start to tie the game at the break, but rushed decisions in the late round after the swap and a few excellent multis by Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato and YEKINDAR helped their side seal victory.

Other notable results on day two — that weren't covered in other recaps — saw Astralis send 3DMAX to the 0-2 pool after a two-map series win, Aurora reverse-sweep paiN after losing Nuke to avoid an early trip to the elimination round, and B8's 16-year-old newcomer Daniil "⁠s1zzi⁠" Vinnyk continue to impress as he helped his team battle past HEROIC.
