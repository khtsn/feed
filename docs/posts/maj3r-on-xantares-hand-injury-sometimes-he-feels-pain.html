MAJ3R on XANTARES' hand injury: "Sometimes he feels pain"

Engin "⁠MAJ3R⁠" Küpeli sat down with HLTV before the BLAST Open Rotterdam playoffs, which kick off on Friday with the first round of the six-team bracket. Aurora will have a tall order to make it to the semi-finals as they will face The MongolZ, a team they have failed to beat in their last five matches.

To get this far, Aurora already beat FaZe and FURIA in the group stage at the BLAST Studio before losing to Natus Vincere, another team the Turks have struggled with repeatedly, in the playoff seeding match.

In the interview, MAJ3R revealed more information about how the team prepares for matches now, with pre-match briefings about what some of the possible game calls and situations will be, and how Ismailcan "⁠XANTARES⁠" Dörtkardeş is being affected by a hand injury.

"If one day he tells me that he's really hurting, I will tell him, 'Bro, you need to take care of your health first,'" MAJ3R says regarding the possibility of XANTARES taking time off, "but for now, he's playing, and he feels okay, and if he feels okay, then for us it's okay."

You came in second place in the groups, where you beat FaZe and FURIA and then lost to NAVI. Walk me through the matches. How do you think the team played in the group stage?

We showed the same performance we've been showing for one year. We beat FaZe, but it was more close, like 2-1, then we succeeded in beating FURIA, also a very good team, so we're very happy about the 2-0 and coming to the playoffs.

Then we had a chance to get revenge against NAVI, but we still have some issues to beat them. Overall, I'm okay with the level we've shown, but the most important now will be the playoffs, and hopefully we can show good Counter-Strike there.

You really wanted that revenge against NAVI after losing so many matches against them. Was it painful to lose 2-0 against them? Especially because you couldn't win Anubis, which is one of your best maps.

Yeah, it was painful for me as an in-game leader because we lost on the T side of Anubis, and this is my best T side, but we didn't have a chance to fight back. I think NAVI is a great team, so losing against them... I'm not feeling bad about it, but yeah, I would like to beat them one time.

It was painful for me as an in-game leader because we lost on the T side of Anubis

Just a few days ago, XANTARES revealed he has two cysts in his hand, and that has required him to get some injections. How is he handling that, because it seems a bit serious and quite painful?

I don't have all of the details about that, but he's had an injury for some time, so he got a painkiller for his hand, and the doctor said he has to rest a little, but with all of the tournaments we have and the schedule, that's not easy for him.

It's been like three or four tournaments in a row. We've been on the road for a month now. We bootcamped in Belgrade for seven days for EPL Stage 2, then we went to Stockholm, then to Copenhagen, now here [in Rotterdam], and then we'll go to DraculaN in Romania, so yeah, a lot of tournaments and it's not easy for him, but we'll see.

How is that affecting him in the game?

Sometimes he feels pain, so obviously, when you feel pain, and you're not comfortable with your hand, which is the most important thing when you play Counter-Strike, sometimes he's not playing at the level he should. But like every other problem, he has to fix it, take care, and hopefully it will be better.

If one day he tells me that he's really hurting, I will tell him, 'Bro, you need to take care of your health first.'

Is there a chance that maybe you will go to some event with a stand-in and allow him the chance to get some rest?

For now, we're not talking about this as a team, but health is more important than anything, so if one day he tells me that he's really hurting, I will tell him, 'Bro, you need to take care of your health first.' But for now, he's playing, and he feels okay, and if he feels okay, then for us it's okay.

Whilst MAJ3R said XANTARES sometimes feels pain in his hand, he also added that "for now he feels okay"

Before the match against NAVI, you said that you had a bit of a different calling style and that instead of doing everything on-the-fly, on the morning of the matches, you would talk to the players and tell them five or six calls that maybe you could make during the game. What made you change your system, and do you think this is contributing to the improvements that we're seeing in the team in terms of timings and communication?

Yeah, I feel like last year I was doing the same preparation with my coach. I had a game plan in my mind every time I played against a team, but I was not sharing that with my teammates. I was just calling on the day.

But since this year, we decided, along with my coach, I said to him that I would talk to my teammates before the match about what I would call in the game, because when I don't explain it to them, and I'm just calling, sometimes there are individual mistakes, micro mistakes that I can't fix instantly in the game.

But when I show it in the theory [session] the day before the matches, their mind can be more fresh, and they know what they will do, so they can just focus on their aim, and also I can focus on my aim.

Since this year, we decided, along with my coach, that I would talk to my teammates before the match about what I would call in the game

I did that at ESL Pro League, and we made the final, so I told myself that maybe it's a good thing to do. So I'll keep doing that, and it's the way I approach it now to help myself and my teammates so they can understand my calls better.

You're going up against The MongolZ in the quarter-finals. You lost the last five matches against them. What do you think about them? Are you looking forward to anything in particular?

Yeah, they're like NAVI. There are some teams that we struggle to play. I don't know. Maybe the system, or maybe the game. Or maybe mentally.

So NAVI and The MongolZ are both teams that we struggle to beat, generally, but it's another game, and hopefully, we can beat them. We're really motivated for that, and I hope that we can show good Counter-Strike there.

They will be without their coach for this match. Do you think that can play in your favor?

Yeah, of course. It's not easy to play without a coach in the playoffs in front of a crowd, but they are very good players individually, also, so we'll see.
