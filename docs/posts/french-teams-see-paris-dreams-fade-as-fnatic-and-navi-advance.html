The team knew it needed a deep lower-bracket run to qualify for the event.

The series opened on Karmine Corp's pick of Haven. Last year, the map was one of FNATIC's best, highlighted, of course, by its trend-setting double Duelist Iso-Yoru comp. However, FNATIC opted for a more original comp, featuring Killjoy and Neon. Up against them was KC's own iteration of a double Duelist comp, pitting marteen on his staple Yoru and Avez on Phoenix.

The pair combined for 25 kills on attack, but could only build a 7-5 lead by halftime. That was thanks in part to veteran player crashies 's efforts, as he finished with a team-high 15 kills on that side of the map. FNATIC began its attack side with its usual slow, methodical approach, which got them the first three rounds of the half and an 8-5 lead.

However, a Karmine Corp timeout saw the defenders pivot, shutting down this attack to win four of the next five rounds and threatening to steal FNATIC's map pick. The flash-heavy comp did well to disrupt the sightlines of the slow-paced FNATIC attack.

Facing map point down 12-10, FNATIC fought back to force overtime, but they'd only get that close. Two clean rounds from Karmine Corp in overtime gave them a 14-12 win in a showdown between two pairs of fraggers. marteen and Avez combined for 47 kills for KC, while FNATIC's crashies and Alfajer had a joint 45.

FNATIC's pick of Corrode was next, and while FNATIC had a more meta comp with Omen, Viper, and Vyse, Karmine Corp's agent select raised eyebrows. A zero-Duelist, double Controller, triple Initiator comp displayed one of the most unique Corrode approaches that any team has taken since the map's arrival. Both teams had success on the defender side, with KC jumping out to win eight of the first 10 rounds of the map, entering halftime with an 8-4 lead.

On attack, the lack of a clear-cut Duelist seemed to hinder KC's ability to enter site, which FNATIC capitalized on. They had an eight-win defense half of their own, which brought the two teams to overtime once more. FNATIC winning an attack round to start overtime spelled the beginning of the end. FNATIC dropped just two bodies in two rounds of overtime to seal their map pick 14-12 and move to the decider map of Bind.

Bind has proven to be streaky since its reintroduction to the map pool earlier this year, and this series was no different. Both teams traded punches in the first half, as FNATIC, on attack, won the opening four rounds, only to be met with six straight KC rounds. Switching sides and going up against a zero-Sentinel comp with a 7-5 lead, KC looked to seize the momentum, but it was FNATIC that did just that. FNATIC won eight of the next nine rounds to close the series in dominant fashion, winning 13-8.

On its attacker side, Vitality appeared disconnected, disjointed, and unable to attack. NAVI won seven of the next eight rounds post-halftime to cruise to a 13-7 win, stealing Vitality's map pick away. Duelist player alexiiik was on a heater, averaging a kill per round to finish a map-high 20/13 while Ruxic finished with a 1.39 rating, the game's highest.

The focus then shifted to NAVI's pick of Bind, a map that they have had a love-hate relationship with in Stage 2. A 15-13 series-deciding loss to FNATIC and a 13-5 win over GIANTX marked the team's only two appearances on the map this year. It hasn't been a ban of theirs, however. In six Stage 2 matches, NAVI has picked the map twice, floated it as the decider twice, and had it banned against them twice. In five matches, Vitality has played Bind twice and lost both times. They've also banned it twice.

The map, as the earlier series in the day proved, has been somewhat defender-sided as of late. Both teams went for a double-Controller comp, with NAVI opting for a double-Initiator look and Vitality picking up a Sentinel.

NAVI had a dominant defense in the first half, threatening to make the series a quick one. Ruxic and Hiro, who were heroes on Haven, combined for 31 kills in the half, helping NAVI to a 9-3 lead. The pistol, anti-eco, and bonus rounds to open attack spelled danger for Vitality, but they would not go down without a fight.

Facing match point at 12-3, Vitality fought back. They won eight straight rounds, seemingly heating up at the right time. NAVI had trouble getting into site, only ever planting the spike three times during that run. At 12-11, a handful of crucial duels and a perfect Viper's Pit from hiro spelled the beginning of the end, giving NAVI a 13-11 win.
