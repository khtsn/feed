BLAST Open Rotterdam to stick to old patch

BLAST Open Rotterdam will continue to run on the same patch that was used for the event's opening day, HLTV.org has confirmed.

Valve shipped an update to Counter-Strike 2 on Wednesday evening that overhauled the game's reload mechanics by forcing players to discard unused ammo in a magazine when reloading a weapon.

The change to ammo management adds a layer of complexity to the game and makes reloading a more consequential decision. Spare magazine counts will differ from weapon to weapon, allowing for different play styles.

In order to preserve consistency, BLAST has decided against changing the patch mid-tournament. Not even the playoff stage, scheduled for March 27-29, will be played on the new patch.

This means that Counter-Strike fans will have to wait until PGL Bucharest, which will kick off on April 4, to see the new reload mechanics in use at a tier-1 LAN.
