Parts of the Northeast were under excessive heat advisories as hot and humid conditions could make temperatures feel more like 100 degrees through midweek, in the latest round of scorching heat to bake the region, forecasters said.

The heat advisories in New York City began on Sunday, when the temperature in Central Park reached 92 degrees Fahrenheit, and are in effect through 10 p.m. on Tuesday. The advisories were expected to expand on Monday to most of the New York City region, including parts of New Jersey and Connecticut, according to the National Weather Service office for New York.

“The heat and humidity will continue through Tuesday, and likely continue into Wednesday,” the office said. “It will remain quite uncomfortable with heat indexes exceeding 100 in places,’’ through Wednesday, the office added. The heat index is a measure of how the temperature feels with humidity.

The previous high temperature for the New York region this season was 97 degrees, recorded on June 21 at La Guardia Airport.

The area has endured high temperatures and humidity for much of July. The humidity levels this week are expected to be slightly lower than they were during a heat wave last week, but temperatures will be hotter, forecasters said.

“The dew point will be a little lower, but you are still going to feel warmer,” said John Murray, a meteorologist at the National Weather Service.

The service said that temperatures could feel like 100 degrees in northeast New Jersey and New York City on Monday and into the mid and upper 90s for the rest of the region.

On Tuesday, the heat index could reach between 100 and 104 degrees for northeast New Jersey, New York City and portions of the Lower Hudson Valley. Similar conditions were expected in the region including interior Connecticut for Wednesday.

The high temperatures were forecast between noon and 6 p.m. each day, the Weather Service said, noting that there was “an increased risk of heat-related illness” for vulnerable populations.

Mayor Eric Adams of New York City said in a statement that pools and cooling centers would be open at least through Tuesday.

The city provided a map for residents to locate cooling centers and said several public pools would be open until at least 7 p.m. and in some cases 8 p.m. Con Edison warned of heavy demand for electricity because of air-conditioner use.

Governor Kathy Hochul of New York urged people on Sunday to stay hydrated and to find a cooling center “to help beat the heat” through Wednesday.

After Wednesday, conditions should improve, Mr. Murray said. Forecasts show “a noticeable air mass change,” on Thursday, which should bring cooler temperatures, possibly in the mid to upper 80s, with less humidity.

The Boston office of the National Weather Service also warned of “dangerous heat” and humidity through Wednesday.

The heat index was expected to reach 105 degrees on Tuesday around Boston and Hartford, Conn., and 105 degrees in Providence, R.I. on Wednesday.

“It’s July so this is typically our warmest time of year,” said Kristie Smith, a meteorologist with the Boston office of the National Weather Service, which covers Massachusetts, northern Connecticut and Rhode Island.

“We’ve got a combination of very warm temperatures and very high humidity,” Ms. Smith said.

“It will certainly be uncomfortable,” she added.

A cold front was expected to bring some relief starting on Thursday and lasting through the weekend, the Weather Service office in Boston said.
