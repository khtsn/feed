Gen.G shuts down ZynX for the rest of 2026 due to wrist injury

Gen.G Gen.G Korea Rank #5 Ash Ha Hyun-cheol (하현철) Karon Kim Won-tae (김원태) t3xture Kim Na-ra (김나라) has announced that Kim "ZynX" Dong-ha (김동하) will be taking a break from competition for the second time this year to address a wrist injury.

With two games left in the Pacific Stage 1, ZynX sustained a wrist injury and was replaced in Gen.G's starting lineup by Gen.G Global Academy Gen.G Global Academy Korea Rank #11 Raxcal Kim Min-seok (김민석) KiTae Park Ki-tae (박기태) Fric Jeon Jae-hyeok (전재혁) Sovereign Kim Do-hyun (김도현) RgoN Yong Seung-hyeon (용승현) player KiTae . After Stage 1, ZynX appeared to have recovered, competing in eight matches for Gen.G through its EWC Pacific Qualifier run, where the team came up just short of reaching the event. Gen.G's statement shared that, since ZynX first began experiencing wrist pain, his scrim time was reduced, and that he only competed in EWC matches while sitting out of scrims. Despite those efforts, Gen.G shared that ZynX's condition worsened.

ZynX on stage with Gen.G at Pacific Stage 1. (Photo by Riot Games)

"Following consultations with multiple medical professionals, ZynX was advised to refrain from using his wrist and focus on a full recovery through an extended period of rest," the org said. "After numerous in-depth discussions between ZynX, Gen.G management, and Head Coach solo, we collectively agreed that his long-term health and career must come first. As a result, he will take the remainder of the season to focus entirely on his recovery."

ZynX, who was a winter signing for Gen.G after competing for DRX Academy DRX Academy Korea Rank #7 Kyra Park Jae-young (박재영) Krexx NGen Lee Jae-hong (이재홍) apeX Lee Chan-min (이찬민) XoNs Athan Na Ha-jun (나하준) last year, has been filling Gen.G's secondary Duelist, Sentinel, and secondary Controller roles this year. During the Yoru-heavy meta that started the year, he had the most success on Yoru, but also played Waylay throughout portions of Kickoff. Through three matches in Stage 1, he primarily played Duelist, but also had maps on Sentinel and Initiator.

Gen.G's roles have gone through plenty of mixing in 2026. Karon , once Pacific's Controller of the year, was moved onto Sentinel, while rookie Initiator Ash was moved into the Controller role. After much experimentation, Gen.G seemed to play its best Valorant with t3xture on Duelist with the occasional Chamber map, while Ash's Neon, Karon's Controller, and occasional Phoenix, and ZynX on Sentinel were most reliable.

The team's struggle with roles could be attributed to the return of veteran Initiator player Lakia . In addition to joining as a lifelong Initiator player, Lakia also took on the IGL role, a role that has primarily been assigned to Controller players for Gen.G through its Munchkin -led years. A move away from the Lakia-centric team has already begun, with the team parting ways with him last week. Both moves now open up two roster spots for Gen.G, with a reliable Duelist player and a Sentinel or Controller player likely slated to enter the fray for the team.
