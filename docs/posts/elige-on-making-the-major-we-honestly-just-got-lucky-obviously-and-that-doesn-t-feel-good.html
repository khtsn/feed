EliGE on making the Major: "We honestly just got lucky, obviously, and that doesn't feel good"

Liquid were sent packing from IEM Rio in last place after failing to tally a map win in consecutive losses to Spirit and 3DMAX, fumbling an 11-4 lead on Nuke in the latter series to add insult to injury amidst a torrid run of form.

The Dutch organization just barely qualified for the IEM Cologne Major after a tight race for the final Americas VRS spots, counting on several results from other teams going their way after a dreary 7-8th finish at DraculaN Season 6.

Liquid were criticized for the regional swap, which came after the team was left with little to no chance to qualify through Europe, but even securing a Major ticket is little consolation as Liquid continue to struggle.

"You want to have results that you can feel proud of when you do get them, and it's not like making the Major is like a huge result, especially for an org like Liquid," Jonathan "⁠EliGE⁠" Jablonowski told HLTV after his team's elimination in Rio. "That's like the bare minimum, so we just accomplished the bare minimum and it wasn't really even our accomplishment."

Listen to the interview here.

Commiserations, a hard way to go out of Rio. What's going through your mind right now?

Just frustrating, disappointing. I feel like we haven't improved since Mario [malbsMd] joined too much, and it shows in the results. We didn't win at all here, so it's just like a bad cycle we need to get out of.

I can see the disappointment on your face, and it has definitely been rough. It's hard to get out of that rut sometimes when you're in it. Did you hope that adding Mario would break you out of that, and what do you think you're missing to get over that?

I think that he's been doing a really good job. He plays well, he helps a lot with the style that he brings, the positions that he excels in. It's just an overall team collapse, I don't think it's something specific of him doing or not doing. He could be playing insane, but us as a team, we're not playing well.

Even in this 3DMAX game, we were in a prime posiiton for it to be at least three maps, but we kinda faltered on the first map. We were in a lot of good positions, but... well, we had some good positions, but we were also kinda getting smashed for most of the T side, and we were also really flat on Ancient as well. It's pretty much just back to the drawing board, like we need to start making some improvement because it's pretty much been the same story for most of this year, I'd say.

We need to be playing at a level where we can be proud of what we're displaying, and at this point, obviously none of us are happy

It seemed possible that you'd miss the Major given the way the VRS ranking was going, but you got through in the end. I'm sure it's not the way that you would have wanted to qualify, based on other results, but was that a bit of relief for you? What is your mentality going into the Major with the results around it?

To me, it's just a relief for the org because I think Liquid is a great org, and everyone knows that the Major is very important for organizations. So I'm very happy for them, but, as you said, it's not like it's something that we necessarily earned ourselves. We honestly just got lucky, obviously, and that doesn't feel good.

You want to have results that you can feel proud of when you do get them, and it's not like making the Major is like a huge result, especially for an org like Liquid. That's like the bare minimum, so we just accomplished the bare minimum and it wasn't really even our accomplishment. So we need to be playing at a level where we can be proud of what we're displaying, and at this point, obviously none of us are happy.
