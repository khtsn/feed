wasiNk on 3DMAX's improvement: "Really it was just a question of time"

3DMAX have taken massive strides at PGL Bucharest, recovering from a slow start to the tournament to make it all the way to the semi-finals, doing so via victories over the likes of PARIVISION and, most recently, MIBR.

Coach Damien "⁠wasiNk⁠" Dufour identified the match against Voca as a turning point. "I think the victory against Voca helped us to understand that okay, we should do better, but we are able to take victory with good resilience and a good level," he said. "After that we stepped up game by game."

With a semi-final bout on the horizon, HLTV spoke with recently-reinstalled coach wasiNk to discuss 3DMAX's failed Major qualification campaign and their recent resurgence in Bucharest.

How are you feeling right now?

Really happy. I feel like the work is working (laughs).

I wanted to talk about that series, because on Ancient it looked quite rough for you. What do you think allowed you to turn that map around?

I think we worked a lot firstly on the map because we know we have some weakness, and we tried to develop our CS on this map. On CT we don't change a lot, we just adapt, and on T side we try to perform well and find the best way to play CS on this map, and just do the same we do on the other maps on Ancient.

Today it worked well, it depended a lot on us and on our communication in the server. It was not good enough on Ancient, but a good step-up from the guys.

You must be proud of the resilience they showed mentally to come back on that map?

Yeah. I think the first thing in my value is about resilience, I try to give them the most hype, the most confidence. During the run for the Major we were not confident enough because of the past and everything that happened. Today I think with the process we just put with the staff, with NBK-, AlexTopGoal, we just built a new confidence and trust in ourselves, focus on ourselves. It helped, and resilience is about our DNA.

We just built a new confidence and trust in ourselves, focus on ourselves. It helped, and resilience is about our DNA

When you first came back into the team, was that the biggest problem you saw, confidence and resilience?

There were two big problems. First of all they were not on the same page, my first goal was to put them on the same page. Secondly I feel like every player was underconfident and my goal was to show them they are good players, that's important. I think the victory against Voca helped us to understand that okay, we should do better, but we are able to take victory with good resilience and a good level. After that we stepped up game by game, that's all.

Why do you think you weren't able to show that confidence in your Major qualification run at these smaller LANs?

I think we were really not good, I think there were past demons and I feel like the guys were not ready. The Pro League was a good result of course but it was the hype of the beginning, the fact we did well. When we arrived in this kind of pressure [of the Major qualification run] we were not ready, and we had to just accept it.

Now, with the staff, we have done a new process. It takes time, and what I said to the guys before PGL is that we're about to do a good result but we have to step up. We have to be on the same page, players have to talk between them, because I think communication between the players was not good enough. It was okay, but not good enough, and we fixed it. Now they are able to play all together, they are able to be not in a good situation but come back, like you saw on Ancient.

I think we were really not good, I think there were past demons and I feel like the guys were not ready

So really it was just a question of time. Now we have to keep progressing, it's not finished. I think the level of confidence is good, but like I say, you can go to the bad side really fast so we have to focus and understand why we are good. That's why we did what we did with the staff.

Looking forward it will be either Astralis or EYEBALLERS in the semi-finals, how do you feel about those two teams?

I think both teams are really good. EYEBALLERS are this kind of team who just grind a lot online, they have good resilience. Astralis I think is a better team overall, they have better players and strong technical aspects of the game. Against Astralis it will be a rough match, and the same for EYEBALLERS, but we have to keep stepping up.

I think it's two teams we can beat, but we need to show our face with confidence and still step up. We have some adjustments to make in the game, but I am pretty confident about it.

What are you going to be working on with the team moving forwards? It feels like, in this tournament in particular, you've made a step up and a lot of improvements. What more are you looking to do in the future?

First of all it would be a mistake to just focus on technical aspects overall. There are some adjustments to make, like for example on Ancient, keep going, and on Inferno understand why we don't do everything possible to win and do a good T side, where we were dominant. I think now we just have to understand why we were good, what were the good aspects of our game, and to still improve it. And not focus a lot on the technical, just adjustments.
