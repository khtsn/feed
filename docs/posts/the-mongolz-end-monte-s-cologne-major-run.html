The MongolZ end Monte's Cologne Major run

The MongolZ have eliminated Monte from the IEM Cologne Major after a narrow 2-1 victory in the 1-2 pool of Stage 3 (9-13 Nuke, 13-7 Inferno, 13-11 Dust2).

Garidmagnai "⁠bLitz⁠" Byambasuren's men were playing for more than just their Major lives, as a loss would likely have knocked them out of the top 12 on the July VRS update and left them outside of the invite bubble to tier-one events, and have salvaged that hope alongside giving themselves a chance to reach the LANXESS Arena in the final Swiss round.

Monte's Major run ends in heartbreak, as this narrow loss came the day they pushed Falcons the distance in the 1-1 pool, but the team can overall be satisfied with a run that saw them advance with a 3-2 record through Stage 2 and saw Oscar "⁠AZUWU⁠" Bell step up after a dismal showing in recent months.

Monte were dominant on the offense of Nuke to kick off the series, a masterclass by Jack "⁠Gizmy⁠" von Spreckelsen — both in calling and fragging — leading the charge in a lopsided first half. The British captain got in The MongolZ's head early after slipping the net and sneaking up Heaven for a triple kill, and Aurélien "⁠afro⁠" Drapier stopped Monte from letting a round against pistols slip as they went on to dominate the half, 8-4.

A pistol win from The MongolZ and answer to Monte's first gun round victory brought the map back within reach at 9-9, but a double opener by afro and 1v2 clutch by Aurimas "⁠Bymas⁠" Pipiras to prevent squandering that advantage turned the tides again as Monte won out on their pick.

Monte carried their form into Inferno, The MongolZ struggled for purchase early doors. A quad-kill by Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold to turn a 3v5 was the sole round that went The MongolZ's way out of the first six, but Monte repeatedly squandered their advantages after that.

Despite their early lead and going 10-2 in opening duels in the first half, Gizmy's troops posted only seven rounds by the break, and panic seemed to set in as they failed to post a single round after the swap. bLitz and Usukhbayar "⁠910⁠" Banzragch denied Monte a chance in two of the only moments they could have turned things around, and romped to a 13-7 victory.

Early momentum finally went The MongolZ's way on Dust2, with further multis from bLitz leading his side to a 5-1 start. Bymas and AZUWU stopped the rot for Monte and helped even the score at halftime, but another pistol win by The MongolZ put the European combine on the ropes, 10-6. A few relatively clean rounds from Monte reduced that gap, but just when it looked like a comeback could be a reality, The MongolZ cracked open A twice to hit match point before sealing victory with a B hit in round 24.
