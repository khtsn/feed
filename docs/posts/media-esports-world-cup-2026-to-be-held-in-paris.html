Media: Esports World Cup 2026 to be held in Paris

The 2026 edition of the Esports World Cup will be played in Paris because of the ongoing conflict in Iran, according to a report from GamesBeat's Alexander Lee, who cites "three individuals who work in the esports space and were privy to the Esports World Cup’s communications with stakeholders."

Ralf Reichert, CEO of the Esports World Cup Foundation, had publicly expressed hope that the tournament's third edition would still be held in Riyadh, but the uncertain geopolitical climate in the Middle East seemingly forced the event's organizers to look elsewhere.

The report comes less than two months before the event's start on July 6. It will span seven weeks and 24 different video games, with $75 million on the line.

The Counter-Strike tournament will be held in the final two weeks of the festival, between August 12-23. It will feature 32 teams, double the number in 2025, and a $2 million prize pool — a $750,000 increase compared to last year.

Four of the 32 tournament slots are set to be determined by a non-BYOC LAN open qualifier for up to 128 teams, but it remains unclear whether this qualifier will go ahead if the location is changed.

The global VRS will determine 21 spots, while North America, South America, and Asia will each have two regional invites. The final tournament berth will go to the winner of the Hero Esports Asian Champions League, a $150,000 event that is currently underway in Shanghai.

Per Valve's regulations, EWC will need to be granted an exception from the developer to run the tournament in a new location or with a different format. Valve's last update to the exceptions file was made seven months ago.
