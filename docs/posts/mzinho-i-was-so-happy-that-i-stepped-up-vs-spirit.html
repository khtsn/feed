The MongolZ star Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold has made no secret of his delight at putting in a man-of-the-match performance against Spirit in Group B's lower bracket final at BLAST Open Rotterdam.

The 18-year-old posted a 1.85 rating as his team beat Spirit 2-0 to book the final spot at Rotterdam Ahoy.

"It was a do-or-die match, and I stepped up," he told HLTV on media day ahead of the playoffs. "I was so happy that I stepped up and that we made it to the playoffs."

For the past few weeks, the Mongolian team has had to do without head coach Erdenedalai "⁠maaRaa⁠" Bayanbat, who missed the ESL Pro League 23 Finals in Stockholm and is unable to attend BLAST Open Rotterdam.

"It has been kind of hard [without maaRaa]," mzinho said. "But before every match, the coach is sending us voice messages.

"There is no timeout where the coach says, 'We're making this mistake and we need to focus on that.' No one is saying that, so there is no time to relax. We just have to stay focused."

It has now been three months since Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar was signed to permanently fill the void created by Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold's surprise benching.

After a tough start at IEM Kraków, cobrazera is coming into his own and showing flashes of his potential.

"He's getting better, day by day," mzinho said. "And I think he needs to focus more when playing officials. He usually does well in scrims and practice. I think he has some nerves in official matches, like on stage or something. Maybe it's a bit of a mental thing.

"We are trying to give as much space as we can to him so he can play his game."
