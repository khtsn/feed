latto on game-winning clutch: "It's in the top three plays of my career"

Legacy booked a spot in the semi-final stage of the CS Asia Championships following a hard-fought 2-1 win over The MongolZ, with Bruno "⁠latto⁠" Rebelatto dealing the finishing blow with an ace clutch in the final round of Nuke.

The victory puts Legacy on a collision course with fellow Brazilian side MIBR in the semi-finals.

Speaking to HLTV after the match, latto discussed the incredible clutch, the team's current run of red-hot form, the disappointment of the loss to GamerLegion in Atlanta, and his target for the Cologne Major.

latto, a 2-1 victory over The MongolZ with an ace clutch to close it out. What went through your mind in that final round?

It was a very tough match against The MongolZ. They are a very tough team to play against. And in that 1v3, to be honest, there was not a lot of thinking. I just went and played what was in front of me.

I thought there was a guy in window, I thought there was a guy in decon, I was thinking about those things, I wanted to kill the guys. The moment I made noise there, arT was, like, 'Man, just plant.' After that, I just went with the flow. I didn't think too much, and in the end it worked out.

Would you say it was one of the best plays of your career, also because of the impact it had on the match?

 I think it’s one of the best. I’d say it’s in the top three. Yeah, mostly because of the score, right? If we’d lost, it would’ve been a very tough 12–11 with the economy and everything, so it was a really important round. I definitely think it’s in the top three, for sure.

This is Legacy's second semi-final in a row. How important is it to make such deep runs as we're getting closer to the Major?

I think we have really been gaining a lot of confidence after IEM Rio. I think these two tournaments are really making a big difference for us in terms of confidence. And obviously, we’re gaining experience, right? We hadn’t been playing in many playoffs. This year, we’re playing in more playoffs, so I think we’re gaining experience playing in front of the fans and playing in the playoffs. So I think it’s been really beneficial for us.

Looking back at Atlanta, were you disappointed that you couldn't make the final? You beat Astralis, you beat NAVI — who went on to win the tournament — but you fell to GamerLegion in the semis. How was the mood when you lost that match?

We were really confident, but I think we were a little nervous. I don’t think we had a great day, so I think it’s that experience I told you about that we're still trying to gain, you know? I think we started off a little nervous, we were kind of, like, getting used to this new environment. I think we’re improving on that. And playing these insane matches, I think that helps even more with that.

But I think there was a feeling of sadness. Because we wanted to be in that final, we believed that we had what it took to win the tournament, but we slipped up. I think the tournament, all things considered, was pretty good for us. We won four matches and lost one. I think we’re taking it step by step; we’re improving.

What do you think went wrong in the match against Falcons earlier today?

I think today was a day when we started every match a little slowly, in both the Falcons series and the match against The MongolZ. I think the only map where we had a slightly better start was this Nuke, the last map. We weren’t communicating very well on the server. I think that was it, and against these good teams we’re playing against — teams in the top ten, top five —, if we go in like that, not communicating very well, we’re going to get punished. So I think that’s what happened today.

Next up, you will go up against MIBR, another Brazilian team. What are your thoughts? Do you expect an intense match?

I think so. We’ve already played them once this year, I think. I believe we lost that one in Bucharest. Yeah, they’re playing really well in this tournament; they’re showing some great CS. The good part is that it’s Brazil vs. Brazil, so there’ll be a Brazilian team in the CAC final again. But we're really confident and we want to come out with the win.

And for your team, as the reigning CAC champions, how important would it be to win this match, reach the final, and maybe win that as well?

Going back-to-back here in China would be really special. We love playing here in China — the fans are so passionate; they really root for us, so I think it would be really special for that reason. And also to show that we’re strong, that we can make the playoffs at a Major, that we can make the playoffs in every tournament. So I think this run would be really good for us because of that.

And looking at the team's current run of form, what are your goals for the Major? How far do you think you can go?

The Major is always uncertain, right? All the teams there play really well, everyone’s always fighting tooth and nail to beat everyone else. But I believe that this time we’re going in to try and snatch a spot in the playoffs. It's a dream of mine. I’ve never played in a Major playoff; I’ve always wanted to, so I believe we’re taking it step by step, and if we can get a spot in the playoffs, I think that would already be really good for us.
