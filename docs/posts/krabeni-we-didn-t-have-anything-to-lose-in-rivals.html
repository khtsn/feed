Krabeni: "We didn't have anything to lose in Rivals"

FUT have been on an astronomical rise in 2026, going from a fourth-place finish at ESL Pro League Season 23 to claiming the title at PGL Bucharest over Astralis in Vitality's absence.

The young international side were robbed of the opportunity to carry their momentum into their next outing, BLAST Rivals Season 1 2026, as medical issues forced Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea to take a break, leading coach András "⁠coolio⁠" Fercsák to stand in for the Texas event. FUT departed the tournament after losses to Vitality and Astralis, managing to take a map off the former side despite missing lauNX's presence.

After his team's opening victory over NRG, FUT captain Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija spoke with HLTV regarding the team's lack of practice with lauNX in recent weeks, the team's Ancient comeback, and the team's choice to forgo a bootcamp ahead of the IEM Cologne Major.

Or, you can listen to the interview here.

First, let's talk about Ancient, very difficult CT side. You played against NRG in Bucharest and annihilated them on CT-side Ancient there. What do you think the real difference was here?

I think the difference was that we didn't play together for two weeks or more, because lauNx couldn't travel. I think it was one of the key things. I also think we choked some rounds, like some situations we didn't wait for each other, we had an advantage and didn't try to block or something. I don't remember very well, but I think the difference is that we didn't play CS that much, we had no PCs, and that we also didn't have lauNx with us.

How much time have you had with lauNx since he's been back?

We played only yesterday two praccs, and nothing else.

Is he feeling better?

Yeah, I think he's fresh (laughs).

As far as the Ancient comeback went, you lost the pistol but won the force buy and had pretty smooth sailing from there. When did you realize the game was winnable?

I said when we were losing 8-2, let's just take one or two more rounds and it's going to be same against NAVI, we're going to win on T side. And, of course, we started a bit different, because we lost the pistol here and against NAVI we won pistol, and it was winning round by round. But when we lost the pistol, after winning the second round, their economy is broken for two buy rounds or something like this, and we knew that we could win.

We were thinking before the tournament, only one win is enough

Overpass was going great, but you guys slowed down in the second half and getting those last few rounds was pretty difficult, with you aceing to close it out. Was the issue in that second half?

I don't know. Also, against B8 we lost a lot of rounds on CT, usually our strongest part is the CT, but this game it was the T, somehow. I also think that we didn't play any praccs, anything. This official is good for the next one because we had no time to play or anything, and I just think that was the problem for today, that it was pretty close.

Are you worried, after going from first in Bucharest to last in Rivals without lauNx, that you might lose some of your momentum?

No. When lauNx said that he was diagnosed with this thing, we knew that we didn't have anything to lose in this Rivals tournament, even if we went out in last place it doesn't matter. To be honest with you, we were thinking before the tournament, only one win is enough, kinda, because we're missing the star player, the guy who is taking space on T-side, and it was a bit hard even for coolio to do his role. We weren't sad or something that we lost, we knew that we were coming to this event all together, it's not going to be like in Rivals.

What are the expectations for this event with that in mind?

For me, the goal is always to win, I don't like to lose. The team objective is to go to playoffs, but for me, it's just to win.

We will not have a bootcamp for sure

You guys are up against paiN, who just took down FaZe. Any thoughts on that series?

I just know that they're a good team, when they have a good moment, and it's maybe hard to stop them. We played once against them seven months ago in China. Whenever we play against another region, I like it. I hope it's going to be a great match.

This is the last event you have before the Major, with a few weeks in between. Do you have set plans for Major prep? Is a bootcamp in store?

We will not have a bootcamp for sure. We choose to not have a bootcamp because back then, we had almost three weeks of bootcamp in Germany when we went for Pro League, and it was like three weeks playing, one week home or something like this. Now we will also be here for three weeks kinda, and we want to have maybe some days off, just to practice and play from home.
