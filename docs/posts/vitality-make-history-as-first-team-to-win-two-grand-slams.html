Vitality make history as first team to win two Grand Slams

Vitality won the sixth season of the ESL Grand Slam on Sunday following their 3-0 success over Spirit in the grand final of IEM Rio, earning a $1 million prize in the process.

In doing so, Vitality became the first team to win more than one Grand Slam, cementing their claim as the best team of all time.

Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool also became the player with the most Grand Slam titles with three, surpassing his former teammate Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken, who won gold bars with Liquid in 2019 and then FaZe in 2023.

It took Vitality 11 months to complete the Grand Slam, starting with their victory at IEM Dallas on May 25 last year. FURIA, Spirit and Natus Vincere were the only other teams that picked up wins this season.

"For me, a Grand Slam is harder to win than a Major," Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire said after the final. "There is no doubt that we are the best team in history."

Grand Slam winners:

Season 1 (2017-2018) - Astralis

After the final, Marc Winther, Director of Esports for Counter-Strike at the ESL FACEIT Group, confirmed that a new season of the Grand Slam is set to start.

ESL will run four Grand Slam-eligible events until the end of the year, including an EPT Championship tournament in the Cologne Major. This gives Vitality the chance to accomplish another unprecedented feat: to be the first team to win two Grand Slams inside a calendar year.
