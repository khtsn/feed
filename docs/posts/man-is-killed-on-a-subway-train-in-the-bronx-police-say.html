Man Is Killed on a Subway Train in the Bronx, Police Say

After initially saying the man had been shot in the chest on a D train early Friday, officials later said he had died after a receiving a puncture wound.

Feb. 23, 2024Updated 12:44 p.m. ET

A 45-year-old man was killed on a subway car in Bronx early Friday, the police said. After initially saying the man had been fatally shot in the chest, officials later said he had died from a puncture wound.

The death occurred on a southbound D train as it approached the 182nd-183rd Streets station just after 5 a.m., the police said.

Three men dressed in black who fled from the train were being sought as potential suspects, the police said. No arrests had been made by midday Friday. It was unclear whether the victim, who was pronounced dead after being taken to St. Barnabas Hospital, knew his assailants.

Shortly after the incident, more than a dozen bystanders remained on the station platform and the stopped train. They watched quietly as three emergency medical workers tried unsuccessfully to save the man’s life, their gear scattered around them on the platform floor.

Outside the station, a police officer monitored the cordoned-off entrance. A police cruiser and an ambulance with its lights flashing were parked outside.

Killings on New York’s subway are rare, but the system has been the setting for at least two already this year.

This month, a 35-year-old man was killed and five other people were wounded in a shooting at the Mount Eden Avenue station in the Bronx during the evening rush hour. In January, a 45-year-old father of three was fatally shot aboard a No. 3 train in Brooklyn after intervening in an argument about loud music.

This is a developing story and will be updated.
