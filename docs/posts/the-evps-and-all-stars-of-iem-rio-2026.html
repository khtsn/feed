The EVPs and All-Stars of IEM Rio 2026

Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut's 2026 has been electric, with three MVPs, one top EVP, and a 1.42 rating four months into the year, matching his 2025 rating after Melbourne.

He was simply imperious in Rio, a player of the map on five occasions and an EVP on four more of his 11 maps in total.

That meant, for the first time since he smashed the charts in Krakow, the MVP decision was straightforward as the Frenchman collected another special medal.

1.25 KPRW, 1.56 in playoffs, 90.4 ADR: There weren't many metrics that didn't point in his direction, and for good reason.

Radar Chart Stat Glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • Swing in RW Δ: round swing in round wins

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov

m0NESY was top of Falcons's scoreboard nine times in Rio in 11 maps, which goes to show just how dominant he was in Brazil.

His worst map was a 1.11, and he had eight maps above a 1.42 rating. His opposition included seven maps against the two finalists, and another two against the world number three.

He also had a decent playoff match despite his team's collapse, with a 1.28 rating compared to four teammates at or below 0.80.

Put that together, and it's easy to see why he is the top EVP.

Andrey "⁠tN1R⁠" Tatarinovich

tN1R was Spirit's leading MVP candidate before the final, and picks up his career-first EVP for his role in taking them to the grand final.

It's a long time coming: tN1R joined Spirit last September, but Rio's quarter-final was only the second time he has won a playoff match in their colors.

It will be hoped that he can now kick on, having posted a 1.86 against MOUZ and 1.25 versus Falcons in victorious arena games, and showing flashes in the final on Mirage against Vitality.

If this extra firepower is sustained, Spirit look like a whole different beast.

Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool

ropz, fresh off taking the MVP in Rotterdam, adds another award to his cabinet after starring in yet another grand final.

He posted a 1.38 here, which is only his fourth best in Vitality. That in itself tells you all you need to know about the Estonian's impact when the trophy is on the line.

Along with a good showing against Falcons in groups and a high floor (median map rating of 1.23), that final was enough to get him into EVP contention despite a fairly low 1.15 rating in map wins overall.

Yuri "⁠yuurih⁠" Santos

yuurih's quiet start to 2026 has been one of the main reasons FURIA have dropped off, but he was back to his best in Rio with a team-high 1.34 rating in map wins to take them to semi-finals.

Against Vitality in his home arena, he added to his already solid EVP case with a 1.25 rating, and a poor showing in the low-stakes third-place decider was never going to undo that good work.

With the Falcons game excluded, yuurih averaged a 1.26 rating and 99.1 ADR in round wins, making him the player most important to FURIA's top-four finish and their top EVP.

Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets

Finally, donk squeezes into the EVP list after a 1.40 rating in Spirit's map wins and 1.04 KPRW overall.

His best overall series was the quarter-final against MOUZ, where he finished with 1.55 and 1.31 ratings.

This EVP is not his most convincing given that he had a low floor against top 10 teams (0.30 against Falcons, 0.72 against Vitality). But, if you take the name plate off, a player with a 1.22 rating, superb map wins, and three exceptional arena maps (including one in the final) was always going to get a nod in the end.

Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato

KSCERATO was the edge case in Rio, and nearly missed out because he was only FURIA's fourth-best in the map wins (1.17 rating) that got them into the semi-final.

But he was brilliant in the arena against Vitality, with a player of the map showing on Overpass, and a solid +2% swing on Ancient. And even with the third-place decider at a very low weight, you cannot completely ignore his 1.57-rated series against a top-five team.

When you add those two arena series to a still decent group stage, then, you have enough for the Brazilian's fourth EVP in Rio de Janeiro — though his relatively low contribution to getting them to semis keeps him at the bottom of the list.

Honorable mentions

Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire has now come fairly close to EVPs twice this year, first in Cluj and then having an even better time in Rio. However, his overall 1.04 event rating tells a tale, as good as his quarter-final against Natus Vincere was, and a 0.89 in groups gave him too much work to do in the arena.

Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan also falls short, having a fairly ineffective 0.81 KPRW in playoffs, and not topping the scoreboard once in Rio.

Boris "⁠magixx⁠" Vorobiev, like apEX, had a strong event for an in-game leader but his two player of the map showings in playoffs were the exception, not the rule.

apEX has come close to EVPs at Cluj-Napoca and in Rio

Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri seemed dead set for an EVP after a 1.34 in groups, but dropped the ball with a 0.63 rating in playoffs. Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov and Valeriy "⁠b1t⁠" Vakhovskiy fared better in the arena, but did not do enough to leapfrog him into a EVP placing.

Matúš "⁠MATYS⁠" Šimko was the best player in Brazil to not make it into playoffs, and shone in two standout maps against eventual champions Vitality.

Finally, Dmitry "⁠mizu⁠" Kondratev played well against both Aurora (1.73 Dust2) and NAVI (1.56 Ancient) in only his third-ever Big Event.

All-Star

For once, we don't have three players from the winning team in the all-star list, after ZywOo hoovered up all the frags on Vitality.

Our captain is apEX, who was brilliant on CT side all event long, and contributed plenty in terms of utility as always.

tN1R takes closer over ropz, testament to his jump in form, and teammate donk beats out flameZ and makazze in a fairly weak field for opener.

Finally, yuurih slots in as anchor after by far his best event of 2026 so far.
