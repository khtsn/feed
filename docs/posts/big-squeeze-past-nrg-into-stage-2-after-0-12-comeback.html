BIG squeeze past NRG into Stage 2 after 0-12 comeback

The German team moves to the next stage with a shocking show of resilience.

BIG are through to Stage 2 of the 2026 IEM Cologne Major after a dramatic victory against NRG thanks to a historic 0-12 comeback on the decider map of Mirage.

It's a heartbreaking end to the tournament for NRG, who will be wondering how they let this game slip away after a dominant defensive performance. It was the first time in Major history when a team managed to come back from a 0-12 deficit.

"This is possibly going to be the worst choke of all time if NRG cannot close this game," caster Hugo "⁠Hugo⁠" Byron said late in the second half as BIG continued to close the gap.

"After I won this clutch, I knew we had it," Johannes "⁠tabseN⁠" Wodarz said of his 1v2 at 12-7. "They were really scared. They didn't plant the bomb and overthought [the round]. We also had really good calls. Benjamin [blameF] kept us composed. Everyone did what we were supposed to do.

"I'm so proud that it paid off. We have played this map the most in practice before. Getting smashed 12-0 was not on my cards."

Jon "⁠JDC⁠" de Castro and tabseN were the stars on Dust2, combining for 16 frags on the T side as BIG romped to an 8-4 lead after overcoming an early deficit. One round was all that NRG could muster on the offense as BIG carried their momentum into the second half and calmly finished the game off.

NRG looked much livelier on Nuke, where they put up a respectable five rounds on the T side. After losing the ensuing pistol, they hit back with nothing but USP-s and rode that momentum to a 10-8 lead.

A third map seemed to be on the cards, but then Gleb "⁠gr1ks⁠" Gazin clutched a 1v2 to bring BIG back into the game. A few rounds later, he and tabseN won a 2v3 post-plant, putting BIG on series point, but the German side wouldn't be able to deal the finishing blow as NRG secured the final round of regulation before going on a flawless 4-0 run in overtime.

It would be hard to overstate just how dominant NRG were in the first half of Nuke as they strolled to a flawless 12-0 lead, with IGL Nick "⁠nitr0⁠" Cannella leading the charge for the North Americans.

But the tables turned once the sides switched: BIG's momentum increased with each round won, especially the 1v2 clutches pulled off by Josef "⁠faveN⁠" Baumann and tabseN. NRG looked absolutely deflated and dejected in the final rounds, unable to produce any sort of consistent play as BIG completed a massive 16-0 run.
