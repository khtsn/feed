dastan: "My players are very young, and maybe when I talk with them like I talk with grown people, it will not work. I changed this"

Pressure has been mounting on PARIVISION throughout the season, and IEM Cologne Major is the team's final chance to turn a dreary slump in form around before Dastan "⁠dastan⁠" Akbayev pulls the trigger on changes he has been warning of since PGL Bucharest.

dastan: "Some levels of mistakes are unacceptable. We have the Major, I can't kick them right now, but if they continue, yes"

But the Kazakh coach has taken time to reflect on his approach since his team's group stage exits at PGL Astana and CS Asia Championships, and is trying to be more lenient with the way he talks to and about his players in public.

"Maybe I need to put less pressure," dastan told HLTV on the media day ahead of Stage 3, "because when I see what is happening from my perspective, I [have been around] 10 years already, and when I talk about mistakes to players, it's maybe too much because they really don't have experience; it's just the first months for them. [...] My players are very young, and maybe when I talk with them like I talk with grown people, it will not work. I changed this, the way I speak with them."

Away from the cameras, though, dastan is still trying to toe a difficult line between being strict and disciplining his team for mistakes while giving them space to develop, a situation made even trickier as PARIVISION fight to remain inside the top-12 invite bubble for tier-one events. And with pressure remaining high, he was still critical of what his players need to improve in this lengthy 18-minute sitdown.

Listen to the interview here.

Here ahead of the start of your Major run. A bit of time off since your losses in Astana and China, so what has your preparation been like after you went out from there?

After China, we had two or three days break, then we prepared in a bootcamp in Belgrade. It was a nice bootcamp, we worked on all maps, and the whole preparation was actually good. At the end, the score was 9/10 (smiles). Nothing special. The Major from AVANGAR, Virtus.pro, was always a point where we can see what we do next, and the Major is the place where we need to show ourselves and we need to be ready.

It has been a difficult period for you and for PARIVISION, and you've talked about it a lot. What's changed between the way that you were preparing before to now? You said there might be some differences.

In our working schedule, we changed times. It was more time to players to play individually, first of all. Second, in Astana and China we had private talks with players or with the whole team to talk about what's happening, or what we need to do, and one of my... what I think is that maybe I need to put less pressure.

Because when I see what is happening from my perspective, I [have been around] 10 years already, and when I talk about mistakes to players, it's maybe too much because they really don't have experience; it's just the first months for them. Maybe I need to just play and just not even think about good results. Let's find our game and play good, let's start from that.

My players are very young, and maybe when I talk with them like I talk with grown people, it will not work. I changed this, the way how I speak with them

Now, in the VRS system, I know that it's very important to perform in every tournament because when you are in the tier-one invite bubble, you need to... I don't want to return again to tier-two world. That's why I want good results. At least, my minimum result always is playoffs, it will be nice to go to the stage and it will be a different feeling, even if we are losing. But in Virtus.pro, I think we didn't beat elite teams that much, but we were winning more [against] tier-two and tier-three teams. For example, we go to the quarter-final and we're losing-

You had a better floor.

Yes, yes. And right now, if it was like that, I will be happy. More stable results will be good for us. And also, I hope... my players are very young, and maybe when I talk with them like I talk with grown people, it will not work. I changed this, the way how I speak with them. That's all. We prepared good, I think they are in good form. One player is maybe a bit ill, but it's nothing super strong. I think we will show ourselves.

It's the Major, and maybe because we are in Stage 3 and some teams already played, it will be hard for us to start. But I just forgot about everything, it's the Major, just play, and don't take too many things in your mind, that's what I told to the players, that's all.

It sounds like you've changed your perspective, or outlook, a little bit. For a while there was a lot of frustration from you and a lot of frustration at the mistakes being made, but now it sounds like you're approaching it more with the fact that the team is so young and there isn't a lot of experience. What caused that to change now? You were holding on to it for a long time to try and iron this out.

Because now is the point when the pressure comes to players when they see my interviews. Even in China, in a lot of tournaments, after games we have some discussions, but people start to put more attention on me, like how I'm talking, on Twitter, in the Western community. People think that I'm like a bad person, I don't know, and I don't want to be... I'm strict, but if people watch our vlogs, for example, I'm a different person maybe from how people see.

But of course, there are also young teams in the scene, like FUT for example, B8, maybe Spirit in some way because donk is young, but I just want to be in tier-one zone because I don't want to return [to tier-two]. It takes a lot of time to be here and I hope my guys will play good. I know that it's easier to show results when no one believes in you, but I don't want to talk every time that we don't expect anything. Of course I expect more.

Also, in PARIVISION, to get every player, it also costs money. To buy xiELO, to buy zweih, even buy Jame. It's not because money puts pressure on us, it's just that everyone came to this team to be closer to tier-one, and I expect from my players that they want to win, they need to show energy, they want to be an elite team. That's what I'm talking with my guys. But now, before this tournament, we didn't say anything about expectations; just that we need to first of all find our individual form.

Maybe I need to show less emotions when the cameras are on and the people don't understand the inner situation

Do you have any regret in the way that you handled anything with the players and with the way you were talking before?

Yes, of course I regret that I made some... in the Bucharest interview, for example, I think that it might help my young players to feel that everything is serious around, and maybe I need to show less emotions when the cameras are on and the people don't understand the inner situation.

Because I'll also remind you guys, we are living together for one-and-a-half years, and it was a long journey to be here. What's the point between a super bad mistake, and I'm a coach, it's my job, I need to talk. Maybe I shouldn't talk some mistakes after the map, but there is also my way to keep this feeling that we need to be stronger and play more strict, play moments more...

Disciplined?

Yeah, disciplined. It's not the way that I want to say something bad and make him frustrated, it's more to be strict. Maybe on cameras it shows like that, but also in PARIVISION, it's on me and Jame's shoulders. But I want players to also be involved for the team's success, it's not like me and Jame are creating structure, saying rounds, he's reading it, and go. He also needs to add some ideas, add how he sees our game, and take more initiative.

But they're young, I don't know. Their lives changed too fast. When was the last time a team grew like us? I don't remember. Maybe Spirit with donk three years ago. And now, I think our long-term goal is to be in the top 12 invite zone, and play as much as we can to gain this experience, that's all.

And of course, historically, the Major was the tournament for me and Jame where we showed good results, and I hope it will continue with this team because this journey started from the Budapest Major. We were 1-2 and almost losing to Legacy, and after that we went to Stage 3 and it was the next step of our confidence. The young players need more wins to be more confident, we don't want to be losing too much.

You mentioned taking some of that initiative, and it seems right now the way Jame calls - and I think he even mentioned it in an interview - he's having to call a lot more out of spawn because the players aren't taking that initiative. How are you trying to change that coming into this event?

Because our captain is behind in a lot of situations, because he has AWP, our theory takes a lot of time to teach the guy in front. A lot of situations come from what he sees and he controls the timing. For example, most of the time it's nota, and of course I expect more from nota than from other people because he has options of when to go, where to go, which nades to use, and control the synchronization in rounds.

And in the other way, he is 18 years old, and there are no players that can do better than him. Of course to nota, it puts more pressure and more expectations from me and Jame, and I hope he will find the balance between individual form and to control the round.

Also, as I said before in previous interviews, to be elite-tier, now I think there are five teams that are super-elite. It's Vitality, Spirit, NAVI, Falcons, and I forgot the fifth team. Let's say four or five teams. Others, from number five to, for example, top 15 or top 20, everyone can beat, but these four teams are more stable because they have superstars.

Maybe in this tournament I will tell the guys, 'Even if you're making mistakes, I will not say anything bad to you, you can take risks.' Because it's the only option to win [against] the big dogs in tier-one

We don't have superstars; we need to have more team play. If we make mistakes it costs us more because we don't have a 1.20 rating guy. Everyone is close to 1.00 rating, and it's a bit sad, and I hope that they will. xiELO, in my opinion, can kill more people in the game, his rating should be better. Also, maybe our structure is a bit hard for young players because it needs a lot of skill. We're trying to be effective on every round, we don't play lose.

Maybe in this tournament I will tell the guys, 'Even if you're making mistakes, I will not say anything bad to you, you can take risks.' Because it's the only option to win [against] the big dogs in tier-one, you just don't play scared. But to be stable long-term, to beat tier-two teams, you can't do that every time, and I want to have good structure for the long-term.

You talked about how everyone from the outside sees some of what you say to the players and react to that. Can you tell me how the players have reacted to it? When the cameras aren't on, how are they talking to you about what you've been saying and after you've given them some of this feedback?

(Laughs) 90% [of the time], they agree because I am angry at really stupid mistakes. So when they don't understand simple situations. When the situation is complicated, I don't talk to them that it's something bad. For example, to zweih, I don't [say] anything bad because when he came to the team, I told him that for one year, I will not touch him. He also plays more lurk roles, so it's less connection with other team; he's playing alone, and on CT sides he plays anchor, so it's a lot of alone.

My goal is that they need to always be hungry, because they have money now; they were poor guys, there was nothing in their pocket, and now some money comes to them. And maybe they feel like it will always be [like that]

But a lot of communication goes with nota and BELCHONOKK, and also xiELO. And I think they react normally, you only see from the camera, but when we are in the bootcamp, sometimes it's even harder because it's preparation, I expect more from them.

My goal is that they need to always be hungry, because they have money now; they were poor guys, there was nothing in their pocket, and now some money comes to them. And maybe they feel like it will always be [like that]. I want to tell them that the tier-one scene is hard, you need to always be developing, always create new [things], you always need to update your vision of maps.

Also another point is that because my players are playing two or three months in tier-one, it's a bit hard for them to play on old school maps, like Inferno or Overpass for example, because they didn't even play it before. It's better on Ancient, for example, because they were grown on Ancient. I really don't know what to expect from my team. I hope we will start with a win, and if we will go to LANXESS, I will be very happy.

To bring it back to this Major, there is some pressure regardless. Even if you have changed your approach right now, you've already mentioned that this could be the last event for some of these players, so it is important. As you start this Major, you have this matchup against 9z, a rematch of your Astana game. How are you looking at that match and what are you hoping to see from the team to start off your run here?

9z is also an experienced team. I mean, they're maybe not experienced in terms of playing a lot of international tournaments, but they're 26-years-old average, like max has been playing a lot of years. In Virtus.pro we played against them.

I want to say that we should win, but I want to start hot, you understand me? Like I want it from the first day, because we need that win. If you want to go to playoffs, we need to [beat] 9z tomorrow, but if we start with 0-2 or 0-1, it will be much harder. But it's Swiss system, you can always come back. I really don't feel like we're afraid of someone, and I don't feel like we 100% should win against some teams. Everyone can beat everyone here.
