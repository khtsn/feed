The EVPs and All-Stars of BLAST Rivals 2026 Season 1

In the first half of 2025, IEM Melbourne was the only really close MVP race between Vitality players as they stormed to seven titles in a row.

But that's been different this year, and as they picked up their fifth title in a row of 2026 there was another close race between Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut and Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan for the MVP.

Like in Cluj-Napoca, ZywOo beat out the Israeli in Fort Worth, but it says a lot about Vitality that their individuals have seemingly found a new level on top of an already successful formula.

That causes headaches for those of us who decide the MVP, but it's a luxury for the team itself.

"I thought it was ropz or flameZ who were going to get it," ZywOo said in the mixed zone, but the gap in overall statistics and two 2.00+ rated peaks in the arena just kept the Frenchman clear.

Radar Chart Stat Glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • Swing in RW Δ: round swing in round wins

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan

Close but no cigar in the MVP race once again for flameZ, but he did enough for the top EVP after posting a 1.29 rating in map wins, just 0.02 below ZywOo.

He was magnificent in both maps of the GamerLegion semi-final, and added a player of the map showing on map one of the final to cap a fine playoffs.

In the three Vitality maps that went to overtime, flameZ was top of his team in all of them, which is further proof of his impact.

A quicker start against FUT, or a better finish to the final, and the MVP may have been his — but flameZ's knock on the door is only getting louder.

Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool

ropz may as well be crowned the Prince of Playoffs at this point; he has a 1.22 playoff rating in 2026 compared to 1.08 in group stages.

Like in Rotterdam, he was the best player in the final as he won seven clutches, led Vitality's fightback from 0-11 on Nuke, and picked up map awards for both Anubis (POTM) and Dust2 (EVP).

But his semi-final was not as strong as in the Netherlands, and his group stage was even quieter (0.98 rating), which prevented him from entering the MVP race.

With a 1.42 playoff rating, however, he still shot up the ranks to end as the second EVP.

Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov

w0nderful helped Natus Vincere to the final with three maps above a 1.78 rating, leading to a 1.41 rating in map wins and +11.4% swing in round wins.

His kill differential per round of +0.25 is second only to ZywOo, even eco-adjusted (+0.18), and his raw figures of a 1.27 rating and 1.02 KPRW are very strong indeed.

The final could have gone better, but it was far from a disaster; he was solid on Nuke with 81 ADR and on Anubis with a 1.27 rating, which only added to an already strong case for his second EVP of the year.

Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri

makazze was the other part of Natus Vincere's rush to the final with a 1.40 rating of his own in map wins, and 1.07 KPRW overall.

In all six of those wins, he was above a 1.14 rating. That helped him achieve a median map rating of 1.40, while he was a top three highest-rated player in the server in six out of his nine maps.

In the final, his 27-frag map on Anubis in the final deserved a win and made up for his 10-25 K-D on Nuke to keep him as a strong EVP, even if he ends at the bottom of the list.

Mihai "⁠iM⁠" Ivan had a good showing as Natus Vincere's third piece with 0.97 KPRW, but needed a good final to push into EVP range.

All-Star

ZywOo and flameZ are the instant picks for the all-star team, leaving no room for AWPer w0nderful or opener makazze.

ropz's sheer impact in playoffs made him impossible to ignore, which means there is a vacancy at in-game leader where grenadier-expert Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire would have been if we were not limited to three players per team.

Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken had an outsiders shout for IGL, impressing as skipper with a 55.4% T side win-rate compared to just 42.2% on CT. That stat tells you all about the individuals Twistzz has compared to his peers, even before taking into account the context of how he took the reins from Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen.

But before the grand final, Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen was on a 69.2% win-rate with NAVI on offence and beat Twistzz in the head-to-head twice, so the Finn takes the spot in the end.

Finally, mezii also is hit by our self-imposed rule — the Best Five would have just been Vitality otherwise — so there is a space for HeavyGod as the anchor for the first time since BLAST London.
