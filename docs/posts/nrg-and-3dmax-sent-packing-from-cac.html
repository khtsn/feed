NRG and 3DMAX sent packing from CAC

MOUZ staved off a br0-less NRG to keep their CAC run alive.

NRG and 3DMAX are the first two sides to depart from the CS Asia Championships, bowing out without a series victory to their names. NRG, who were playing with coach Damian "⁠daps⁠" Steele in lieu of Alexander "⁠br0⁠" Bro due to a family emergency, were eliminated by MOUZ after a three-map series. 3DMAX, who were the runners-up in the last iteration of CAC, exit the event after a 1-2 loss at the hands of Liquid.

Despite their eventual victory MOUZ had a difficult start to their elimination series against NRG, fumbling on the American side's pick of Ancient. Aran "⁠Sonic⁠" Groesbeek led the charge for his team on the offense with a 1.96 T-side rating, allowing NRG to clinch a 7-5 lead that they further extended with a CT-pistol win. MOUZ rallied five rounds in a row to briefly claim the lead, but NRG stabilized to lock down their pick 13-10.

MOUZ had a simpler time on their pick of Nuke, taking an early 5-0 lead on the offense that culminated in an 8-4 half at the side swap. NRG snagged the second half pistol, but MOUZ denied the conversion, allowing them to quickly wrap up the map 13-6 to force the Mirage decider.

NRG claimed a trio of rounds to kick off their Mirage T side off the back of the pistol round, but struggled to find momentum for the remainder of the half as Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman and Lotan "⁠Spinx⁠" Giladi repeatedly shut them out, resulting in a 5-7 half. MOUZ further extended their lead with a pistol round win, and while NRG found isolated rounds on the defense, they struggled to string them together. The map concluded with NRG fumbling a 2v5 anti-eco to Spinx and Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás, securing the 13-9 win for MOUZ.

Meanwhile, in Group B's lower bracket, 3DMAX and Liquid clashed with hopes of keeping their CAC runs afloat, kicking off on the former's pick of Nuke. Liquid took an early lead on the defense, racking up two streaks of four rounds to bag an 8-4 scoreline. 3DMAX made up ground with a pistol round win and subsequent conversion of their own, but failed to prevent Liquid from picking up occasional rounds, leading to a 13-11 win for Kamil "⁠siuhy⁠" Szkaradek's men.

3DMAX fared better on Liquid's pick of Dust2, rallying from an early 3-4 deficit on the CT side with a string of five rounds to take an 8-4 lead. Liquid were not content to roll over, tying up the game 9-9 off the back of their own pistol round victory, but 3DMAX regained control in the latter stages of the second half, with a 1v2 from Lucas "⁠Lucky⁠" Chastang effectively putting an end to Liquid's hopes of winning their pick.

The Inferno decider followed the trend of being a close affair; Liquid picked up their CT pistol thanks to a quad kill from siuhy, but had a more difficult time staying in contention once 3DMAX got guns in their hands, resulting in a 6-6 half. Liquid looked poised to end the map quickly in the second half, quickly stringing together six rounds in a row to reach series point, but struggled to find the final round, requiring a triple kill from Jonathan "⁠EliGE⁠" Jablonowski in order to secure the map 13-11 and the series 2-1.
