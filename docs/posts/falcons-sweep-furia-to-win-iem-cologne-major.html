Falcons sweep FURIA to win IEM Cologne Major

More than a decade on from his first Major appearance, NiKo finally leaves the "best players never to win a Major" club.

Falcons added their name to the list of Counter-Strike Major winners by beating FURIA 3-0 in the grand final of IEM Cologne 2026, ending Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač's long quest for his first Major trophy — a stick critics had used to beat him with for years.

"I don't know what to say," NiKo said after the match as the crowd chanted his nickname. "Thank you, guys. Thank you so much. There’s no better place to lift this trophy. I swear to God.

"It definitely was worth the wait. I think I’m the prime example; never give up. Never give up on your dreams, on your life. Keep believing. You’re going to achieve things. It’s going to be hard. It’s going to be painful. But you will get it. You will fucking get it."

He added: "I cannot explain how happy and relieved I am right now."

History was always going to be made in the best-of-five regardless of the outcome, with neither team having won a Major before. In the end, Falcons — riding a wave of momentum after beating Vitality and Spirit — ran out comfortable winners in a lopsided series they dominated from the get-go as their overwhelming firepower was simply too much for their opponents.

The first two map wins were decorated by imperious performances from Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov, who made his impact felt with a 1.80 rating and +10.13% round swing across Mirage and Anubis. Inferno was all about NiKo and Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin as they combined for 33 frags to carry the team across the finish line.

The Bosnian's arrival in January 2025 marked a turning point for the Saudi organization, which has spared no effort to build a championship contender. More superstars came in as the team continued to chase success, but that didn't prevent a string of painful runner-up finishes and radar moments, each of which was immediately ridiculed by the community.

But Falcons' success looked like an eventuality waiting to happen, a case of when, rather than if. Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen's surprise addition — after a failed Cologne qualifying campaign with FaZe — provided that missing oomph to a superstar squad despite his obvious individual struggles.

After a relatively smooth route to the final, FURIA knew they were in for their toughest test yet in Cologne, against a team that had carved its path by surviving extremely close series.

The magnitude of the task was clear to see early on as FURIA found themselves chasing a 0-8 deficit on their pick of Mirage, planting the bomb only once during that dry spell. It was only then that the Brazilians finally found their footing as they won the last four rounds of the half to keep their comeback hopes alive.

After a chaotic start to the second half, during which teams took turns winning rounds, Falcons kept a tight stranglehold on the game and reached map point. FURIA tightened up in search of an equalizer, but a streak of three rounds was all that they could muster before Falcons eventually finished the game off.

karrigan's troops showed no signs of slowing down as Anubis began, with m0NESY kick-starting a 4-0 run with a Dualies 4K in the pistol round. The rest of the half was controlled by FURIA, whose dominant spell was only briefly interrupted by NiKo's individual heroics.

FURIA's momentum quickly evaporated as Falcons took the game by the scruff of the neck once they switched to the T side. kyousuke and m0NESY combined for 22 frags as Falcons overran the Brazilians in an 8-1 run.
