m0NESY on karrigan: "The things we are doing, all the rotations, the chaos we are building on the server, this is me, this is who I am"

Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov walked away from the IEM Cologne Major as its Most Valuable Player, earning his first trophy with Falcons after over 15 months and eight grand final appearances with the Saudi organization.

"I don't know how to explain my feelings, but it feels nice, but I'm just so happy for other things," an elated m0NESY told HLTV after posing with his medal.

"I'm so happy for NiKo, first of all. I am twice as happy for him as I am for myself. Obviously, I'm happy for everyone, but I just kept imagining in my head that NiKo has been going so long for this dream, to achieve this, and it's finally the moment of relieving the stress."

The Russian superstar admitted there is something "magic" about Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen, saying he immediately felt comfortable in his system, and added he took energy from playing in front of the crowd.

"My brother with his wife were in front of the stage today," m0NESY said, "This obviously helped me so much, just to have their energy in front of the stage. I felt... to be honest, today, as soon as I walked out on the stage, I felt like everyone was very calm.

"If I'm talking about myself, I was very calm; I felt like I'm in Nirvana, like this mood where you don't care. You just focus on each round. I felt all the energy in the arena, and I felt like I took it with me into the game."

m0NESY, you are a Major MVP and a Major champion; the IEM Cologne Major at that. This is such a big moment for you, so what is going through your mind?

First of all, I'm very emotional. I'm very tired, and I cannot believe it happened. I cannot believe it happened. We somehow won the Major [m0NESY tears up]. This is what's going on in my head right now.

You've had such a hard run the last couple of months. All these finals where you just couldn't get it over the line. How much does it mean to you to have done it here, at the Major of all places?

First of all, I want to say thanks to all of the team. To the whole team, to the job that we have done, and to the job we have done outside of the server. How many hours we put in the game, and how much time we practiced to achieve this. It means a lot, it means that we never gave up, we kept working, we kept working in the dark, just to shine like this like we did at the Major.

I'm so happy for NiKo, first of all. I am twice as happy for him as I am for myself

I gave you a medal in the last two interviews we did with you, but they were both losing MVPs. I remember your face then, so tell me how it feels to finally get one when you're a champion after so long.

I don't know how to explain my feelings, but it feels nice, but I'm just so happy for other things. I'm so happy for NiKo, first of all. I am twice as happy for him as I am for myself. Obviously, I'm happy for everyone, but I just kept imagining in my head that NiKo has been going so long for this dream, to achieve this, and it's finally the moment of relieving the stress. But I'm so happy, this also means that I did a lot of work, and it paid off.

Talk to me about being there in that moment with NiKo, then. You joined G2 to play with him, he left, and you followed him. So what was that moment like, when you looked at him after you got that final kill and won.

I was just... what was in my mind (laughs). My god, I'm so nervous guys, sorry. I just saw NiKo jumping, I looked at him, he's smiling. I always wanted us to smile after the grand final, and this smile only comes after you win. To be honest, I was just happy for everyone, that's it. Nothing much to say, just only smiles, laughs, only positive energy and... yeah, we did it.

It has been 62 days with karrigan, and you've already won a Major. What is to come after this, what's the ceiling now?

Yes, we made three grand finals, we won one grand final, now we just have to continue doing what we have been doing at this Major because, as I said, the work that we have done, it paid off, and it will pay off in the future. We just need to stay on the ground, continue doing what we are doing, and obviously, there is some magic in karrigan (smiles). Something is... not wrong, but something is good, you know?

So... yeah, sorry for my broken English as well, I hope in the future I will also give better interviews because- well, to be honest, after the grand final it is very emotional, you know? So it's hard to collect all the words that I want to say, but obviously Finn is the best captain I've ever played with, 100%.

I want to pick your brain more on Finn because we haven't talked to you since he came into the team. How has it been playing with him for these couple of months?

Our first tournament was PGL Astana, and we were just trying our things. We wanted to see how we will play with each other because I never played with Finn, only NiKo played, and I mean, he knows him, but I just wanted to find out how we're going to play with each other, how we're going to react, and the things we were going to show on the server.

And to be honest, the playstyle, I immediately understood that I'm very comfortable with it, you know? The things we are doing, all the rotations, the chaos we are building on the server, this is me, this is who I am, and this is where I can help all the time because you are always putting a lot of responsibility on yourself, but you're winning rounds because of that, you know? Because of these chaotic moments. So... Finn is amazing, as I said. Finn is amazing.

You had this player profile that came out before the Major, and there was a lot of talk about your brother and your grandma, how much they supported you. Do you have a message for them after you've had this moment and won this Major trophy?

Yes, first of all, my brother with his wife were in front of the stage today. This obviously helped me so much, just to have their energy in front of the stage. I felt... to be honest, today, as soon as I walked out on the stage, I felt like everyone was very calm.

If I'm talking about myself, I was very calm; I felt like I was in Nirvana, like this mood where you don't care. You just focus on each round. I felt all the energy in the arena, and I felt like I took it with me into the game.

And if I want to say anything to my family, to my close ones: I just want to say, thanks for all of the support. It's everything I can say, I love them so much and I told them so many times, I said it so many times in interviews: they're amazing people, and they are the ones... I wanted to make them proud, and this happened today.

I would rather do it this way than any other way

Lastly, you had one of the hardest Major runs you could have; you beat all of the top teams on your way. How big was that run for you, to have won your Major in this way?

I would rather do it this way than any other way. I would rather beat all the best teams, secure the grand final, play the grand final, and beat the best team as well, than the other way around. If you want to win the Major, you have to beat all the best teams that you have at the tournament, and yes, you need to beat all the teams that you face to win in the grand final.

But what I wanted to say is that this run was very hard, one of the hardest runs I've ever had in a tournament. We had to play against Vitality, Spirit, FURIA, but we were always there. I always was enjoying, and this is the most important: to enjoy, to see the crowd, to see the fans, and the most important comes in the end.

Great, thank you!

Thank you so much! Sorry guys, next time, I promise I will do better interviews. It's just so hard, I'm so emotional (laughs).

You did great.

Thank you, thank you.
