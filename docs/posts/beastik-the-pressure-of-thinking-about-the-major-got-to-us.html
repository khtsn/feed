beastik: "The pressure of thinking about the Major got to us"

SINNERS found themselves in a prime position to make a late charge for a spot at IEM Cologne Major following their recent BC Game Masters win, where they notable took down the likes of G2 and BIG on their way to the title.

To fully capitalize on their success, SINNERS would have hoped to put together another deep run at Roman Imperium Cup VII, but instead had to settle for a 5-8th place finish as BetBoom ended their journey in the quarter-finals.

"In Romania I felt like we were playing without any pressure, we just knew we were the full underdogs," team captain Sebastian "⁠beastik⁠" Daňo told HLTV when comparing his team's BC Game Masters run with their campaign at Roman Imperium. "Here we were thinking about the Major and everything."

After the pressure of the Major race pushed his team to "fall apart" in the "crucial moments" against BetBoom, beastik says his team are happy to let fate decide their future. "There is one LAN in Serbia, the last one actually that we could sign up for, but we won't go there and [instead] leave it to the gods. We will hatewatch other teams and wait."

After winning BC Game Masters, how do you feel about being eliminated just now by BetBoom? What went wrong, especially in the last map during that tense OT? How could this recent loss help you correct your mistakes?

When we knew that we were gonna play BetBoom again in the playoffs, we knew that when playing the same team again very close in time, it's more about the small little mistakes that will change the game. It's not about which team is better, it's more about the little mistakes.

I think it came down to the clutches, we had some rounds that we should have won. Especially on Nuke, I think at 11-10 or something, we lost against s1ren in a 10 HP 1v2. I think that one changed the outcome of the game. Also on Mirage in OT, we had a 1v3 that we lost against Boombl4.

If you don't win these kind of rounds, I don't think you deserve to win the game. I think these were the most crucial ones, but we didn't give up and I like that the guys just went all in, even though we lost some unfortunate rounds.

I think it came down to the clutches, we had some rounds that we should have won... If you don't win these kind of rounds, I don't think you deserve to win the game

Talking about your Major plans, we haven't heard much about you. What's your next move in terms of tournaments, how are you going to grab the last available VRS points? Does the pressure of the race affect you?

I would say that yes, the pressure of us thinking about the Major kind of got to us, especially in the groups when we played the best-of-ones. I felt like we were very stressed compared to when we were playing in Romania because in Romania I felt like we were playing without any pressure, we just knew we were the full underdogs, nobody really expected anything from us, we were playing with zero pressure. Here we were thinking about the Major and everything, I felt it especially in the groups.

Today I didn't feel it that much, I felt it was way better because we were speaking about it and I was pointing out that I felt it a lot, and the guys kinda agreed. So today it was better, but in these crucial moments I think the stress came again, and it fell apart.

Our plans for now, we are leaving it to the gods to be honest. There is one LAN in Serbia, the last one actually that we could sign up for, but we won't go there and leave it to the gods. We will hatewatch other teams and wait.

You once spoke that FURIA was one of your inspirations, because of your similar fighting style. Do you still look up to the Brazilians, and if not, who is your current inspiration?

I don't really know to be honest. I'm trying to develop my style, I don't really try to draw inspiration from anyone. I kinda like apEX right now and we pretty much look up to Vitality, just like every team right now. It's a pretty obvious answer, but I kinda like apEX and I'm trying… not to become him, but draw inspiration from what he is doing, because he is doing really well.

Speaking of style, your play is simple gameplay, more intuitive and following your flow. Do you still have that kind of mindset against bigger teams, or do you adapt your style?

I think we are more of a team that doesn't stick all the time to the identity. I have in mind that sometimes, when I know teams that are preparing a lot like OG or BIG, I know that I need to change something and we need to step out of our comfort zone. So I prefer sometimes to play kind of FACEIT-ish gaming, because teams expect us to do very smart stuff and play with util, and don't expect many contacts. I would say we are 50/50.
