molodoy stars in next IEM Cologne Major player profile

Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko has become the second player featured by ESL in their Major player profile series for IEM Cologne.

The heirs to Valve's own profiles from the 2010s, the video takes viewers to Kazakhstan for interviews with molodoy, his mother, and key teammates like Vitaliy "⁠sFade8⁠" Marushka and Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo.

His journey started with trips to gaming cafes in his home town of Semey, a small city in Eastern Kazakhstan. He would play "25 games a day" as CS quickly overtook football as molodoy's primary passion, and nearly got expelled from school after bunking off to play the game.

The documentary then details how his parents split up when he was 10, and how the young molodoy had to deal with his mother being scammed and losing almost everything she owned.

Eventually, molodoy's grind paid off as he passed a trial with AMKAL and moved to Serbia, arriving with a bag and no suitcase "like in the movies," according to sFade8. When he received his first paycheck, all of it went back to his mother in Semey.

As he started to impress, the rest of the world took note. First came an offer from GamerLegion. But after dominating FalleN in practice, FURIA also took an interest and this was an offer that molodoy could not decline — even though he spoke "zero" English.
