cobrazera stars on arena debut as MongolZ make Cluj semi-final over FURIA

The MongolZ became the final semi-finalists of PGL Cluj-Napoca after recording a 2-1 win over FURIA.

The series began on Mirage, where The MongolZ quickly negated FURIA's pistol-round victory to take the lead early on. Buoyed by several Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar entries, the Mongolian squad ran away to a 9-3 lead before winning four in a row to take their pick by barely breaking a sweat.

cobrazera, who made his arena debut in the game, had a fantastic showing, finishing the map with a 2.10 rating and even got an anti-eco ace late on.

Dust2 was much the same story, as The MongolZ ran away to a dominant 9-3 half thanks to Garidmagnai "⁠bLitz⁠" Byambasuren and Usukhbayar "⁠910⁠" Banzragch locking down the A site, but FURIA came alive when their backs were up against the wall.

They won all but one round on their CT side, displaying the cohesiveness they became known for and even employed an effective double-AWP setup, which allowed them to take the map 13-10.

The MongolZ were back to their best on Nuke, however, as they ran rampant with a 9-3 offense before reaching six match points at 12-6. While they had some trouble closing it out, a two-second ramp hold proved enough for a 13-10 win alongside a semi-final spot.

Mongolia has found another wonderkid

cobrazera led The MongolZ in the stats department with a team-high 1.15 rating in the group stagem putting up a staggering 2.61 rating on Dust2 against paiN and a 1.59 on Nuke against Natus Vincere during the group stage.

Question marks remained on how the youngster would adapt to playing in an arena setting, but the Mongolian rifler delivered in spades.

He topped the board on Mirage with a 2.10 rating before he and his team cooled down on Dust2, but was back to his aggressive self on Nuke to finish the series with an impressive 1.34 rating.
