9z stun Vitality to move into 2-0 pool

9z are through to the 2-0 pool in Stage 3 of the IEM Cologne Major following a 2-1 victory over Vitality, exacting revenge for the 0-2 humbling at BLAST Open Rotterdam less than two months ago.

The South American outfit had to come back from a map down after being outclassed on their pick of Inferno. They were the better team in the remainder of the series as Vitality turned in a lackluster performance — which Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire will be hoping to use as fuel for the rest of their Major campaign.

"Tough to explain," Vitality coach Rémy "⁠XTQZZZ⁠" Quoniam told HLTV after the match. "The first map went pretty well and the plan worked. Then on Mirage, we were not there. We worked a lot on the T side, actually, but we didn't execute properly," the coach said.

"The last map, it was a good start, but then we failed two or three things and of course the second-round forcebuy is a big issue right now for us. Also, they played well on the momentum, so props to them."

The first half of Inferno was a brutal watch for 9z's fans. The South American side mustered three rounds on the T side but stood little chance in the rest of the half, with none of their players reaching more than five frags.

9z did not throw in the towel and hit back in the following pistol as Vitality uncharacteristically lost a 4v3 post-plant, leaving apEX frustrated. The French captain kept his cool, however, and found a hole in 9z's B defense in the next round — which marked the start of a game-winning 4-0 run.

Vitality looked surprisingly disjointed and out of sync in the first half of Mirage, picking up only two rounds — both times thanks to crucial double kills from Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut — as 9z established a commanding lead, with Ignacio "⁠meyern⁠" Meyer posting a 2.26 rating and a +15.85% round swing.

Vitality's comeback hopes took a major blow as the second half kicked off as 9z won the pistol round convincingly. They responded immediately and began to chip away at the deficit, but 9z eventually picked up the two rounds they needed to close out the map and take the series to a decider.

William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman led the charge during the early stages of Dust2, amassing nine frags as his team jumped out to a 4-0 lead. But the landscape of the game changed once 9z had money in the bank as the South American team looked the sharper team for the rest of the half, even winning a 2v4 clutch with two low HP players.

Vitality's struggles continued in the second half as they won the pistol but lost the conversion, with 9z taking control of proceedings. Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool and ZywOo kept their team in the game with a 2v4 clutch against series point, but it was all for naught in the end as 9z killed the game off in the final round of regulation, just when overtime seemed inevitable.
