Russian Attack on Kyiv Injures Dozens

Ukraine’s military said that it had shot down all the ballistic missiles targeting the capital, but that falling debris caused significant damage.

Published Dec. 12, 2023Updated Dec. 13, 2023, 4:23 a.m. ET

Russia launched a barrage of ballistic missiles at Kyiv before dawn on Wednesday, injuring dozens of people and damaging apartment buildings in the third attack on Ukraine’s capital in the past week.

The assault began at about 3 a.m., with a series of explosions heard in Kyiv as the city’s air defenses activated. The Ukrainian military said it had shot down all 10 of the missiles that had targeted the capital, but that falling debris from the interceptions had inflicted heavy damage on apartment blocks in the city’s eastern Dniprovskyi district.

The scale of the damage was evident on Wednesday morning: Windows of some buildings were blown out, several charred cars were in the street and there was a large crater at the foot of one building.

The mayor of Kyiv, Vitali Klitschko, said that 53 people had been wounded. Standing next to the crater, he said that a nearby hospital building also had sustained damage.

A few yards away, residents who had been evacuated from their apartments lined up for food and hot drinks being handed out by a Red Cross team. Many said they were shocked by the attack and were trying to assess the damage, looking up at a building’s shattered facade.

“It’s a nightmare,” Vadim Obremskiy, a resident of one damaged building, said near the scene.

The city’s military administration said that 35 buildings were damaged in the overnight attack and that falling debris had also damaged part of Kyiv’s water supply.

The attack came hours after President Volodymyr Zelensky of Ukraine wrapped up a trip to Washington to appeal for more U.S. military aid. On Wednesday morning, Mr. Zelensky said that President Biden had “agreed to work on increasing the number of air defense systems in Ukraine.”

“It is important for people, it is important for cities and it is important for Ukraine,” he said. “Because it saves lives!”

Andriy Yermak, Mr. Zelensky’s chief of staff, noted that the attack had been successfully repelled thanks to Western-supplied air defense systems.

“The effectiveness of Western weapons in the hands of Ukrainian soldiers cannot be doubted,” Yermak said on the Telegram messaging app.
