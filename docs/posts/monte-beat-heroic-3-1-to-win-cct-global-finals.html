Monte beat HEROIC 3-1 to win CCT Global Finals

Monte have claimed victory at CCT Global Finals 2026 after beating HEROIC 3-1 in the $75,000 event's grand final.

It is the second tournament win for the Ukrainian organization after it lost Gytis "⁠ryu⁠" Glušauskas to Astralis at the start of the year, and comes three weeks after the team went undefeated in the upper bracket of DraculaN Season 6 before falling 1-2 to M80 in the grand final.

The team's run in Bucharest helped cement a Stage 2 invite to the IEM Cologne Major, and this trophy serves as a big confidence boost ahead of their appearance at PGL Astana — a significant step up in competition for a lineup that has spent much of the past year primarily grinding tier-two LANs and online events.

Monte began the eight-team LAN in Portugal with wins over Betclic and Ninjas in Pyjamas, but couldn't keep a clean sheet after losing to HEROIC in the upper final. A second win over Ninjas in Pyjamas earned Monte a rematch in the best-of-five title decider, and this time, a masterclass from Aurélien "⁠afro⁠" Drapier led to a 13-0 drubbing on Nuke to kick off the proceedings.

Another CT masterclass led to a recovery from 4-8 down on Dust2 to put Monte on the brink of victory, but they couldn't get the job done in three. HEROIC struck back on Inferno, only for their work to go to waste after a dismal defense on Ancient left too much of a mountain to climb in the second half and Oscar "⁠AZUWU⁠" Bell took over to drag Monte over the finish line.
