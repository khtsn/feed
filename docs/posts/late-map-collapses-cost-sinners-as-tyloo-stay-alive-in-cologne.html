Late-map collapses cost SINNERS as TYLOO stay alive in Cologne

Zero's impressive performance was key for TYLOO

TYLOO have kept their campaign at IEM Cologne Major alive with a 2-0 series victory over SINNERS, who were in control on both maps but couldn't handle the pressure in the face of TYLOO's late-map comebacks.

"It's even worse than not qualifying, to be able to be here after six-and-a-half years that I've been trying to just go 0-3 showing absolutely nothing," SINNERS captain Sebastian "⁠beastik⁠" Daňo told the broadcast after his team's defeat.

"We just couldn't get our things going on T side. They knew what we were planning to do, it felt like they have either good prep or we were too obvious with the calls."

Zero finishes off 4K with 1v2 clutch

SINNERS ran out to an 8-4 lead off the back of a convincing offense on Ancient, before recovering from a lost pistol to get to 12-7 on their CT side. Overzealous play then began to cost SINNERS, unnecessary peeks being punished and allowing TYLOO back into the map off the back of turning disadvantaged rounds.

It was at this point that TYLOO's newest member, Su "⁠Zero⁠" Jingshen, came online, picking up an ace as he helped drag his team all the way back to OT. The young rifler continued to shine, back-to-back 4Ks grabbing his team the advantage in the extra rounds and they managed to hold on, clinching the map 16-14.

A similar story played out on Inferno, SINNERS seeming the better side for much of the map only to falter right at the death.

Mercury 3K halts the Mid take

TYLOO started hot on their T side thanks to a series of fast-paced plays, but once SINNERS got guns up they began to take control, their map control protocols and crossfires proving too much for their Chinese opponents.

A pistol win in the second half set SINNERS on the path to victory, but once again they couldn't handle the pressure late on the map. Dongkai "⁠Jee⁠" Ji and Jingxiang "⁠Mercury⁠" Wang began to heat up as SINNERS crumbled, the European squad showing the stress on their faces on the player cams, particularly Jordan "⁠stressarN⁠" Manevski. TYLOO duly took advantage, racking up seven rounds in a row to keep their Major dreams alive and send SINNERS home.
