Falcons outlast FURIA to make Rotterdam playoffs

Falcons have secured a spot in the quarter-finals of BLAST Open Rotterdam after beating FURIA 2-1 (13-11 Mirage, 13-16 Dust2, 13-10 Nuke) in the lower bracket final of Group B.

It was a gritty win for Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski's side, who were down 7-11 on Mirage and 3-9 on Nuke, but properly recovered without dropping the kind of advantages that usually plague them when these games come down to the wire.

It was instead FURIA who struggled to hold on in those situations, unable to convert several late-round scenarios on Nuke and wasting away their immense lead with repeated attempts to attack A without changing much up.

It is the second straight event where Gabriel "⁠FalleN⁠" Toledo's men have missed the playoffs after they fell short in Stage 2 of ESL Pro League Season 23, and adds to a strugglesome start to the year. The team has yet to show the kind of winning form they had toward the end of 2025 that made them favorites for the StarLadder Budapest Major, and now have three weeks to turn their form around ahead of their appearance at IEM Rio.

FURIA took control early on Falcons' pick of Mirage, going up 4-1 before Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač came alive late on the offense to help his side recover to post five at the break. Falcons tied after winning the pistol and conversion, but FURIA ran with momentum from winning the first gun round and surged up to an 11-7 lead.

A key adjustment from Falcons to force more aggressive fights in Middle and A Ramp paid dividends and turned the map on its head, allowing them a six-round defensive spree to steal victory, 13-11.

Falcons won the pistol and conversion again on Dust2, but ended up on the losing side of several close rounds that swung momentum in FURIA's favor. They went up 8-4 at the break after a 1v2 ace clutch by Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato, but that lead evaporated with Falcons dominating the early stages of their T side for another six-round spree.

Danil "⁠molodoy⁠" Golubenko came alive late to give FURIA a fighting chance, and a crucial B stack at 9-11 forced Falcons onto an eco and saw FURIA reach map point first. Frustration set in for FURIA after Falcons forced overtime with a Mid to B fake, resulting in Mareks "⁠YEKINDAR⁠" Gaļinskis temporarily breaking his mouse and causing a technical pause.

The Latvian had been struggling on the map until that point, but came alive with an 8-2 run in the extra rounds to force a Nuke decider.

His revival carried into Nuke, where FURIA went down 1-3 early before dominating on the defense for a 9-3 lead at the break. Falcons looked meager for much of that first map, but with their backs against the wall, put up a near-flawless defense of their own with Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin and Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov constantly finding multi-kills and helping pull off retakes to completely steamroll FURIA en route to a 13-10 win.
