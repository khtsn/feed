B1ad3: "Beating Vitality in any condition benefits us hugely"

Throughout 2025 and 2026, Natus Vincere have been subject to a one-sided rivalry with Vitality, failing to take a map off of the side since IEM Cologne 2024, despite facing off in one best-of-three and two best-of-fives in 2026.

After facing off in the quarter-finals of IEM Atlanta, NAVI have finally come out as the victors. Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen's troops recovered from a narrow opening loss on Dust2 with a hard-fought Anubis victory, before clinching the series win with a dominant Inferno showing.

Shortly after his team's quarter-final victory over Vitality, Natus Vincere coach Andrey "⁠B1ad3⁠" Gorodenskiy sat down with HLTV to discuss the series, his team's upcoming game against BetBoom, and their plans ahead of the upcoming IEM Cologne Major.

Here with B1ad3 after a huge win over Vitality. You've finally broken the streak, and you're headed to the semi-finals of IEM Atlanta. Just tell me how you're feeling.

Amazing. It's like an accomplishment, one which we needed for progress. Because we are constantly doing the same mistake, or the same missteps. Always so close to beating them, and then we lose. Now we're just fully focused not to become hesitant or shackled, and to become confident when we have to close a match.

iM: "I think the biggest opponent for us is us, not Astralis, not Legacy, not even Vitality"

When I spoke to iM he said that he felt like the biggest enemy for you guys was yourselves with respect to Vitality. Do you agree with that assessment?

Yeah, obviously they didn't practice a lot. I think after winning Rio, they didn't practice a lot at home, and then they came to Fort Worth, they had some experience of playing CS, and also practice. But then, they didn't practice before this event, and we knew that it will benefit us hugely, we just need to use this moment. They also got in a better shape, beating FaZe, B8, super confident, you know? So we were a little bit scared that they got in shape, and we also lost the first map, Dust2. But, we felt that we could do it.

For me, it's natural that when you're playing the same team constantly, you are learning something constantly, and if you're doing correct conclusions, you can improve every time, and later you will beat the team. For sure, a huge influence was the fact that they are not super hungry. But you can also think to how they did it before; in the previous season, the start of '25, they won all tournaments until the Major. So for me, it was something like, okay, maybe they can do the same here, right? So, with any other team, you can think, okay, now they should go down, but when we face Vitality, I thought, they already did it, they know how to do it, they know that they have the skill. For us, it's good to stop them, not do the same as the previous season.

Maybe if they would ban Inferno, maybe we will consider Nuke or Mirage; I think this was maybe their mistake

Do you feel like this victory is made a little hollow by the fact that they haven't practiced a great deal? Do you think you'd be able to take on peak Vitality?

Well, we played them on Budapest Major, and it was very close, 13-11, 13-11. It felt like... you know any team when it's in prime, you can be scared of it. You can think 'Oh, this team in prime can destroy everyone.' That's not how you should analyze head-to-head, I think, or matchups. Because, you cannot always play prime CS, even if you have good shape to play tournaments. You will not be able to play from day-to-day in the same tournament the best quality. Beating Vitality in any condition is benefiting us hugely. I don't know how you can organize this... can you give us please prime Vitality? It's not possible to do, so we just play.

Of course, they didn't practice, they cannot upgrade their stratbook, refresh everything, work on mistakes, that's true, that's benefiting us a lot. I mean, we benefited from this, and it was easier to play them. Even like Inferno, we decided to play because they didn't play it from Rio, and this map you need to master, from day-to-day, you need to feel the timings, the confidence on positions, the meta also, constantly tracking it. Everything was good, in our favor. We tried to measure everything, and understand what we can do to win, with map pool, with what strats are the best, and so on.

Do you think that the practice aspect was the key reason that Inferno was so one-sided for you guys?

Yeah, for sure. They didn't pick Inferno in Fort Worth; they picked Nuke, and Inferno was fifth, which they usually like to pick this map. This tournament they didn't play it again. We know how it works, so that's why. Also, they banned Nuke, and gave us a choice to pick Mirage or Inferno. Maybe if they would ban Inferno, maybe we will consider Nuke or Mirage; I think this was maybe their mistake, but maybe they also don't want to play Nuke.

How do you feel about the BetBoom matchup in the semi-finals?

Very strong team. There's such a thing when you are not using your mana for a long time, gathering and collecting. This team has a lot of energy, and a lot of mana, and they're cashing in now to do something, working for this moment, I think. Very dangerous team, usually, these kinds of teams. They have not wasted time; they were practicing constantly at home, fixing mistakes.

A lot of tier-three matches, they improve you, because the variety of things that can happen on that level is different, then you can become much more flexible on a lot of stuff. They mastered all that stuff, this core playing together for a long time. Sometimes they also change players; zorte, then zorte left and then went back. Boombl4 knows his player, so it's a pretty strong lineup, and they also like to play Anubis, one of their strongest maps.

Have you given any thought to how your time between now and the Major will be spent? Is there a bootcamp in mind?

Yeah. We always plan everything. It's not random. We ask two months in advance what the schedule is, how we can organize everything. We will do a bootcamp, and we will also come to the Major three days ahead, to get used to the PCs. ESL can organize this if some teams want to do it.
