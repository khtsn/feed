    What We’re Reading

Gilbert Cruz is joined by The Times’s thriller columnist, Sarah Lyall, to talk about some great suspenseful titles to check out this summer. And the editor Joumana Khatib gives her picks for books to look out for between now and Labor Day.

There’s no rule that says you have to read thrillers in the summer — some people gobble them up them year round, while others avoid them entirely and read Kafka on the shore — but on a long, lazy vacation day it’s undeniably satisfying to grab onto a galloping narrative and see where it pulls you. This week, Gilbert Cruz talks to our thrillers columnist Sarah Lyall about some classics of the genre, as well as more recent titles she recommends.

Among the books they discuss is Donna Tartt’s 1992 debut, “The Secret History,” a campus murder mystery and probing psychological study that Tartt has followed up with two more novels in the ensuing 31 years.

“So many thriller writers succumb to the pressure to keep producing year after year,” Lyall says. “There’s all this commercial pressure on the writers, and when there’s too much pressure on a writer, they can’t really let their imagination go. And I think Donna Tartt has resisted that so extraordinarily. She’s probably lost a lot of money because of it. She’s probably given up a lot. But as a result, the books are true to themselves and true to her, and they’re worth the wait.”

Also on this week’s episode, Joumana Khatib offers a preview of some of the biggest books to watch for in the coming season.

Here are the books discussed in this week’s episode:

  • “Rebecca,” by Daphne du Maurier

  • “Presumed Innocent,” by Scott Turow

  • “The Secret History,” by Donna Tartt

  • “Going Zero,” by Anthony McCarten

  • “What Lies in the Woods,” by Kate Alice Marshall

  • “My Murder,” by Katie Williams

  • “The Quiet Tenant,” by Clémence Michallon

  • “All the Sinners Bleed,” by S.A. Cosby

  • “Crook Manifesto,” by Colson Whitehead

  • “Nothing Special,” by Nicole Flattery

  • “Daughter of the Dragon,” by Yunte Huang

  • “The Sullivanians,” by Aledxander Stille

  • “The Heaven and Earth Grocery Store,” by James McBride

  • “Silver Nitrate,” by Silvia Moreno-Garcia
