stavn: "The plan after kicking cadiaN [in HEROIC] was for me to go into IGLing or AWPing"

stavn spoke to the HLTV Confirmed crew about the 'stabbi' drama of 2023 and his taking up of the Big Green in NIP.

Having sat on the sidelines since September of last year, Martin "⁠stavn⁠" Lund is back in action with Ninjas in Pyjamas, making the surprise switch from rifling to take up the AWP for the Ninjas.

With his first tournament in his new surroundings in the books, a third-place finish at CCT Global Finals, the 24-year-old Dane hopped on HLTV Confirmed to discuss a wide range of topics including his departure from Astralis, his return and new role, and his new teammates in Ninjas in Pyjamas.

On revelation made by stavn was that before his departure from HEROIC in 2023, he was already planning to take up the AWP or IGL role. "The plan after kicking cadiaN was for me to go into either IGLing or AWPing in HEROIC," he told the HLTV Confirmed crew.

From an organization's perspective I feel bad for what happened

Martin "⁠stavn⁠" Lund

"That was something I wanted to do as well. One and a half weeks later, I got a message from someone from Astralis." The interest from Astralis caused stavn to ditch the idea of switching roles for the time being.

The debacle surrounding stavn's eventual departure from HEROIC to Astralis was one that caused much debate within the community, the Dane eventually being derisively dubbed "stabbi" alongside his teammate who made the move with him, Jakob "⁠jabbi⁠" Nygaard. stavn admits he harbours some level of regret over the way everything transpired.

"When you look at it objectively, me and jabbi, in a way, fucked up old HEROIC, because cadiaN did get kicked and we left," stavn admitted. "From an organization's perspective I feel bad for what happened. There are things about the way things transpired, but I never wanted to hurt anyone. In the end, it's also a business, and you have to think what's best for your career."

stavn admits he "feels bad" about the way he departed HEROIC

After taking a break to deal with his personal struggles, stavn was ready to return "after the Major last year." Whilst there might have been an expectation that returning on Astralis was an option, stavn felt that was not a good option either for him or the team, partly due to the "stigma" around it.

In the end I didn't want it myself either, to go back

Martin "⁠stavn⁠" Lund on potentially returning to Astralis' lineup

"I did have talks with my agent about potentially AWPing, if it could be a potential for Astralis, but it was never a talk that I had with my ex-teammates or the owners of Astralis, it was just a potential idea I had. In the end I didn't want it myself either, to go back."

Ultimately stavn opted to wield the Big Green for Ninjas in Pyjamas, making the switch to the primary AWP role for the first time in his career. He believes his strengths as a player suit the weapon.

"I know I've never had the aim of a NiKo or ropz," he said. "That is not my strength, my strength is being able to position myself well [...] I like taking control on the CT side, I think you can do this even more with the AWP."

We need a lot more prac, and I need also to learn to play officials again and not let emotions control too much of what's going on

Martin "⁠stavn⁠" Lund on his first days with NIP

stavn also spoke about his first tournament with Ninjas in Pyjamas, the team taking third place at CCT Global Finals, and his initial impressions of his new teammates. "I mean, I would say overall yes," when asked if the event was smooth sailing, before going on to address the problems the team faced.

"We threw so many anti-ecos this event. We need a lot more prac, and I need also to learn to play officials again and not let emotions control too much of what's going on. But overall it was a pretty solid event from our side."

Finally, stavn spoke a little bit about his new team, reserving special praise for Kacper "⁠xKacpersky⁠" Gabara. ""Kacper has really impressed me," the Dane said. "Maybe one thing he can still learn is, he talks [while playing] but he could talk even more, because I think what he has to say is always good."
