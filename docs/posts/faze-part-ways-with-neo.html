FaZe part ways with NEO

FaZe have parted ways with head coach Filip "⁠NEO⁠" Kubski, ending a nearly three-year-long partnership that began in July 2023 after Robert "⁠RobbaN⁠" Dahlström stepped down from the role.

The organization announced that the team's analyst, Dominik "⁠GruBy⁠" Swiderski, will fill in as interim head coach while it explores its options for the future.

The change comes amidst a torrid run of results for FaZe. The team struggled for much of 2025 and eventually swapped Jonathan "⁠EliGE⁠" Jablonowski and Håvard "⁠rain⁠" Nygaard for Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken and Jakub "⁠jcobbb⁠" Pietruszewski, but an improvement in results did not follow.

A miracle run at the StarLadder Budapest Major, where FaZe were milliseconds away from elimination in Stage 1 but rallied all the way back to reach the grand final, turned out to be just that: a miracle.

The team has since been eliminated in the group stage of every 2026 tournament, including 1-3 exits in the Swiss stages of PGL Cluj-Napoca and ESL Pro League Season 23 Stage 2, leaving their overall series record standing at 3-9 for the year.

Such has been their plight that they barely sit on the cusp of an invite to the IEM Cologne Major on our predicted list, but appearances at BLAST Open Rotterdam and DraculaN Season 6 should still offer enough points for them to hold on to that spot.

NEO's departure from FaZe marks the end of his first coaching stint, having transitioned to the role for the first time when uniting with Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen nearly three years prior. Over the years, the team added a few notable pieces of silverware to their cabinet, including trophies at IEM Sydney 2023, CS Asia Championships 2023, and IEM Chengdu.

FaZe also finished as runners-up at three out of the four CS2 Majors in Copenhagen, Shanghai, and Budapest, and fell short of three other big trophies at IEM Katowice 2024 and the BLAST Fall and World Finals in 2023.

FaZe will debut with GruBy as interim head coach at BLAST Open Rotterdam on March 18, where the team is slated to take on EPL S23 runners-up Aurora in their opener.
