Falcons and MIBR clinch CAC semis spots as insani breaks MVP event map rating record

Legacy and B8 will be forced to play another match today in the quarter-finals.

insani's 3.28 rating on Ancient set a new record for highest map rating at an MVP event

Falcons and MIBR have laid claim to places in the semi-finals of the CS Asia Championships, pushing past Legacy and B8 in their respective seeding matches.

While the losers of a seeding match normally still have at least another day before their quarter-final series, the compressed schedule of CAC means that both Legacy and B8 are slated to play their quarter-final matches today. Reigning champions Legacy will face off against the winner of The MongolZ clash with PARIVISION, while B8 will take on the winner of MOUZ's bout with paiN.

Falcons hit the ground running on their opponent's pick of Dust2, romping ahead to a 10-2 lead on the defense thanks to output from AWPer Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov, who posted a 2.10 rating on the CT side. Legacy closed the gap somewhat with a pistol round and subsequent conversion on their own CT side, but Falcons did not take much longer to claim the final three rounds they needed, including one off the back of a Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin 1v3.

Legacy did not fare much better to start on Falcons' pick of Ancient, winning their CT-side pistol before immediately succumbing to Falcons' MAC-10s. Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen's men carried their momentum into a 7-1 lead, later whittled down into an 8-4 scoreline at the half. The two sides traded streaks of rounds in the second half, with René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen stepping up as the hero for Falcons, averaging a 2.34 CT-side rating with 135.8 ADR across 10 rounds, helping to push his team to a 13-9 victory to secure their semi-final berth.

MIBR came out swinging on their pick of Inferno, outright annihilating B8 with a dominant 11-1 T-side that quickly gave way to a 13-1 victory after snagging the second pistol round.

MIBR managed a solid start on B8's choice of Mirage with an early 5-1 lead, but the Ukrainian side managed to find some headway in the latter stages of the first half, finishing their T side tied 6-6. B8 took complete control of the game on the defense, kicking off the second half with a six-round streak that put them within touching distance of the Ancient decider. MIBR managed to make a limited comeback effort with four rounds in a row, but it was not enough to prevent B8 from securing the map 13-10.

On Ancient, MIBR returned to the form they showcased on Inferno, claiming a 10-2 half after yet another stellar T side, this time powered by a show-stopping performance from Felipe "⁠insani⁠" Yuji. The Brazilian rifler posted a 3.28 rating across the map, setting a new record for the highest map rating by a player at an MVP event, and the third-highest-ever on LAN. The rating was warranted: insani had a 25-8 K/D, 163.6 ADR, +21.36% Swing, and six multikills. After a 4k from insani in the second pistol round, MIBR quickly mopped up the map 13-2 to lock in their semi-final spot.
