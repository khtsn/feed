huNter-: "Our mindset and mental strength was much stronger than it was ever before"

We caught up with G2's in-game leader Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač during the IEM Cologne Major's pre-playoff media day, where the caller told us about the hard-fought match against Natus Vincere that stamped their passage to the playoffs.

"We were always present, we didn't think too much about what happened, and we were always trying to focus on the next step," huNter- said about the decider match.

huNter-, a hard-fought match yesterday against NAVI. You lost a lot of clutches, climbed back through a lot of disadvantages, very back-and-forth. Tell me about your emotions through all of that and how managed to finally get it over the line.

We were feeling very confident going into this match against NAVI. We wanted to get revenge for a lot of losses against them. But yeah, I think Dust2 started good, overall we played very well, but after this anti-eco and especially the 1v4 clutch, it was hard to come back into the game mentally.

We were talking after Dust2 that we needed to reset as fast as possible to focus on Inferno, and I think that's what we did. Inferno started again after losing some anti-ecos, but we were always present, we didn't think too much about what happened and we were always trying to focus on the next step. We were really mentally strong and that's what's making me happy. But we should stop losing those stupid rounds so that we don't need to even reset.

Those were going back and forth, I think the opening kill score was something like 49-28 to them, and you had to climb back from so many disadvantages.

Yeah, we played those situations really well and we were calm. Even though it was a 2-2 game and it was a crazy game, the boys stayed calm, they played good in clutch situations overall. After we lost an entry, it was me giving an entry a couple of times, I was listening to what they wanna do and I was trying to help, but they were doing it themselves really well and I'm proud of them. But yeah, 49-28 in entry kills, 6-1 in clutches as well.

I don't know how we won in the end, but I think we deserved this win more than NAVI did. Overall, even that Dust2 we should have won 10/10. But that's why we all love CS. It's an unbelievable game, and anything can happen at any point.

You've been on the losing end of a lot of these matches that end up going super close. What does it mean to finally be able to turn that around and what was different?

I think our mindset and mental strength was much stronger than it was ever before. We were present, doesn't matter what was happening, good or bad, we were always trying to focus on the next step and the next round. That was the most important in the end, and that's why we won.

Spirit up next in the playoffs, a difficult one so far this year. How can you turn it around?

We didn't have a really good time against Spirit lately, but this time will be different. We're playing in front of a big crowd, it's Major playoffs, I think the pressure will be on them. We will be the underdogs there. But we are happy to be in the playoffs, and the only thing I can promise to our fans and everybody cheering for us is that we'll give our best. Anything else is possible, to be honest.
