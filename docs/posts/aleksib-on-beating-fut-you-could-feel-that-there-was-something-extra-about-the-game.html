Aleksib on beating FUT: "You could feel that there was something extra about the game"

Natus Vincere have endured a slow start to the season thus far but have gone a long way to rectifying that issue with a run to the grand final of ESL Pro League, sealing their chance at glory with a hard-fought win over FUT.

NAVI move past FUT to reach EPL final

Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen spoke to HLTV after the game and said that his team knew it was an important semi-final for them. "I do think that for us MOUZ was kind of like a kryptonite team," he said when reflecting on the important of the match. "When we faced FUT in the semi-finals, I think that all of us knew that now was our chance to reach the grand final."

There was not only a grand final berth on the line, however, as FUT's lineup used to play under the Natus Vincere banner as the academy squad, and also used to play with current Natus Vincere rifler Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri. "The vibe that makazze was bringing today on the server, just thinking that he was part of them, he really wanted to show them that he is capable of winning," said Aleksib.

HLTV caught up with Aleksib after the match to get his thoughts on the game, the form of makazze, and the importance of this ESL Pro League run for Natus Vincere's season overall.

Listen to the full interview here.

What are your first reactions after that series? It was a bit back-and-forth.

I'm super glad that we came back into the game on Dust2, because it kinda felt like they brought the momentum from Ancient. We were down 5-2 or 6-2 on Dust2 T side, something like this, and we brought it back and won the CT pistol and much needed anti-force after a sick highlight from Krabeni. So the third map was a good thing.

The CT-side on Ancient was something that I didn't think we could manage to lose, since I think this is one of our strongest maps and we were totally in control it felt like.

What do you put the difficulty of that series down to? What is it about FUT that makes it so difficult.

When I think about the game I just played, and the reactions from my teammates, at times you could feel that there was something extra about the game, knowing that they were your academy team. I'm just so happy that FUT are playing at this level and they are playing under another organization and can show their level now, because they deserved to play in the semi-finals today. They had a chance to win, but in the end we were the better team.

I'm just so happy that FUT are playing at this level and they are playing under another organization and can show their level now

How much did that fact play into it, the fact they used to be your academy team? I assume you scrimmed a lot against them back in the day, so were there any mind games at play?

No, I don't think there were any mind games, we didn't practice them a lot lately, but just the mentality knowing that they were the academy team. The way makazze has been describing it to us, they have been studying us and understand how our game works, and they're trying to replicate a lot of the same stuff we do. Just the vibe that makazze was bringing today on the server, just thinking that he was part of them, but he really wanted to show them that he is capable of winning.

How important was makazze today? Because like you said you were struggling a bit on Dust2, before he came up with that ace to kickstart your comeback.

He's always important, and I think that if he doesn't show up it's a difficulty to our game, because he's a playmaker and playing important zones. The way I see him moving nowadays, the way I see him having the extra confidence… a lot of people might be scared to play their old team, especially a young player like that, but he wasn't feeling the nerves it felt like to me. He was playing amazing today, and he's been playing amazing the whole tournament, so I just hope we will have a nice final.

I would never say it's easy or downplay anything, it's just mentally understanding that the elite teams have fucked up and it's your time now

How important in general for NAVI was this run? Because you had a bit of a slow start to the year, but now you are in a grand final with the chance to pick up a trophy.

Don't get me wrong, I do think that for us MOUZ was kind of like a kryptonite team, so when we faced FUT in the semi-finals, I think that all of us knew that now was our chance to reach the grand final. I would never say it's easy or downplay anything, it's just mentally understanding that the elite teams have fucked up and it's your time now.
