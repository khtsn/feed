SunPayus: "I'm being more curious [...] instead of being so judgmental, and that is really helping"

The Spanish AWPer put up a 1.23 rating in the five maps G2 played in the BLAST Rivals group stage.

We caught up with G2's AWPer, Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García, for a quick comment after a three-map loss to Vitality in the playoff seeding match before BLAST Rivals moves to the arena in front of the American crowd.

The Spaniard has so far had a good start to the event in Texas, averaging a 1.23 rating in the group stage. He had a 1.35 rating in the victory against Astralis and a 1.41 in the two first maps against Vitality before a one-sided Dust2 loss brought his average in the series down to a 1.15.

"I'm feeling better day by day," SunPayus told HLTV. "I'm improving aspects of my life outside of the game that are helping my mentality, and that's really good because I feel like I'm really focused every single round."

You've had some good matches here in the group stage, a 1.35-rated series against Astralis and 1.16 against Vitality. How are you feeling at this event?

I'm feeling better day by day. I'm improving aspects of my life outside of the game that are helping my mentality, and that's really good because I feel like I'm really focused every single round.

I'm never thinking about anything from the past, or getting ahead of myself; I'm focused on the round I'm in. No matter what mistakes I make, or the team makes, I'm being more curious, thinking about how we'll fix it or what I'll learn from this, instead of being so judgmental, and that is really helping me stay focused at all times.

Regarding the game itself, with the Animgraph updates, have you noticed any big difference? Has it had a positive effect on you?

Yeah, it's positive. Not for anything in particular, but I feel like the best improvement has been with the spray. I feel like it's more consistent, more stable. They have to fix the aimpunch, but otherwise I feel like they've finally made a really good update. I'm happy. Regarding the AWP, I haven't noticed it as much. Maybe when players peek, it's not as fast, so that may help a bit, but I haven't really noticed much.

You played Vitality in the playoff seeding match, and it was a contested two maps, but then Dust2 was pretty one-sided, and their T side broke you. What happened there?

huNter- had a bit of a whiff in pistols, they destroyed our setup in the forcebuy, then we had an eco, then in the first gun round they did a five-guy contact play on B and killed us — easy as that —, and we had three players on Long, so the rotation came in really late.

So we were down 0-4, we won a couple of rounds, and we didn't know what their economy was like exactly, and we thought that they may have to forcebuy. They split A against me, alone on A site, and it just snowballed. We also tried to force a lot of aggressive plays, and they had a good game plan because they caught MATYS out alone on Long a couple of times.

What I'm saying is that it was a lot of individual and decision-making mistakes, not so much about the tactical aspect, and it's something we can learn from and improve on. That's just CS2, you make an individual mistake and... Also, it's Vitality, it's not just anyone. They're going to punish you and score nine T rounds on Dust2.

The next game is in the arena. Are you excited about that?

I'm very excited to play with a crowd, it's been a while. I have a lot of confidence in my team, we just need to keep our feet on the ground. We've only had NertZ on the team for a month, but we've been working really hard, and I think our improvement is really noticeable.
