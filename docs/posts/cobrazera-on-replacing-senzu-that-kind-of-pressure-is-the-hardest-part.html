cobrazera on replacing Senzu: "That kind of pressure is the hardest [part]"

The young Mongolian rifler opened up about how he's adjusting to life in his new team.

The MongolZ punched their ticket to the LAN finals of ESL Pro League Season 23 after posting a 3-1 record in the Swiss stage. A 2-0 loss to MOUZ was the only blemish on an otherwise solid online stage as they beat paiN, Aurora and 3DMAX.

In Sweden, The MongolZ will be hoping to build on a top-four finish at PGL Cluj-Napoca, only Anarbileg "⁠cobrazera⁠" Uuganbayar's second event with the team. The 20-year-old acknowledged that there's added pressure from replacing Azbayar "⁠Senzu⁠" Munkhbold, who was the 13th best player of 2025.

"I'm replacing Senzu and that kind of pressure is the hardest [part], I think," he told HLTV on media day.

"He's my close friend and he advises me too, telling me, 'don't try to focus on the bad comments, just play your game and I know you will play well.'"

cobrazera also shared his thoughts on the absence of head coach Erdenedalai "⁠maaRaa⁠" Bayanbat, the upcoming clash with Natus Vincere, whom his team beat last month in Cluj-Napoca, and the feeling of being in a LAN event without Vitality and Falcons.

To talk about your personal journey, you have been with the team for a few months now. What has it been like for you?

It's good, my teammates are good and the vibe is good. All of my teammates are generous, they teach me a lot of things and I am getting up to tier-one. The feeling is good on the stage, it's very great.

Has it been difficult adjusting to playing at a higher level?

It's not that hard, I have my teammates. If I make a mistake, they tell me; they follow up and try to solve my mistakes, so it's not that hard. I'm trying my best on that side.

Is there anything difficult about stepping up?

The pressure. I'm replacing Senzu, and that kind of pressure is the hardest [part], I think.

Do you think about that a lot, the fact you are replacing Senzu? He was not only a good player, but quite popular.

Actually not that much. He's my close friend and he advises me too, telling me, 'don't try to focus on the bad comments, just play your game and I know you will play well.'

Does that make it easier?

Yes, it makes it a lot easier (laughs).

The MongolZ very quickly got to a good level after you joined. Was that surprising for you, how quickly the team adapted?

No actually. They have the core team and it's not a solo game; it's a team game, and they know how to play. I know they will play well, even if they have one player missing. It's the core, they have the same IGL, you know?

Turning the focus on EPL, you played the previous stage without your coach, maaRaa, behind you. How has that been for you and the team? Has it made the games harder to play?

It's kind of harder, it's like a little piece of the puzzle is gone. Usually we are on the server and he is behind us watching all things, so we are missing the third-person view.

What is the hardest part about maara being missing? Is it when you need to make a change in-game, and he can't call a timeout?

Yes, it's kind of hard in that way. The hardest thing is that we are used to going with six, and with one person missing it's different on the feeling side, the vibe is different.

You have NAVI coming up in the playoffs, who you beat in Cluj. Does that give you the confidence you can do it again?

Yeah. We know how they play so we just have to be prepared and do things as we did before.

You will be on stage without maaRaa. Do you think there will be any problems there?

Not that big, but it will affect us a little bit.

Vitality are obviously not at this tournament, nor Falcons. Does that give you more confidence that you might be able to win the trophy?

No. I think we just have to… maybe (laughs). In the back of my mind, it's giving confidence, a little bit.

Looking forward in general for The MongolZ, were any goals set when you joined? A target about where you wanted to be in three months, for example?

No. My personal goal was just to make it easy for them to play, try not to bring a 'new person' vibe, just make the team comfortable. The team goal we haven't really talked about, but the goal is of course playoffs and then trophies, the same as before.
