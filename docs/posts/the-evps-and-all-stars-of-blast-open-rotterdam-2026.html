The EVPs and All-Stars of BLAST Open Rotterdam 2026

It says a lot about Vitality that three of their players could have reasonably been named MVP at BLAST Rotterdam.

In the end, Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool's dominance in playoffs moved the needle his direction despite a slower group stage, but all of he, Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut, and Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan will receive similar credit for the year-end top 20 from their collective fragging bonanza at Open.

It's ropz's first MVP as a Vitality player, a just reward for a player who has transformed the French organization into a winning machine.

Since he arrived, the team has made semi-finals 19 times in a row, gone on a 30-match winning streak last year, and are now on a run of 22 maps undefeated this year at Big Events.

The ability of ropz to provide a stable base for that remarkable ceiling cannot be understated.

Radar Chart stat glossary

  • Rating 3.0: HLTV's composite rating of kills, damage, survival, KAST, multis, and Swing

  • Swing Added: win probability added from positive actions

  • eKPR: eco-adjusted kills per round

  • eADR: eco-adjusted damage per round

  • eKPRW: eco-adjusted kills per round win

  • 2k+: rounds with two or more kills

  • 1k+: rounds with one or more kills

  • KAST: rounds with a kill, assist, survival, or death traded

  • Swing in RW Δ: round swing in round wins

  • eDmg Δ: eco-adjusted damage differential per round

  • eKills Δ: eco-adjusted kill differential per round

  • Round Swing: the win probability sum of a player's actions

Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut

A 1.47 rating at Open has continued ZywOo's scintillating start to 2026, as the four-time Player of the Year aims to go one better and Vitality continue to dominate.

His consistency was unparalleled over the event, having 100% of maps above 1.00 and posting an event-high +5.14% Swing.

1v5ing PARIVISION and posting two maps above a 2.00 rating made it a group stage to remember in Copenhagen's BLAST studio, before a solid 1.28-rated playoffs in the Netherlands confirmed a very high EVP indeed for ZywOo.

The MVP went ropz's way but you cannot charge ZywOo with dropping off; this was as good a performance as many an MVP.

Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan

flameZ's 1.29 rating in 2026 so far is illustrative of a player who has somehow found an extra gear. His last two events, here and PGL Cluj-Napoca 2026, are his strongest-ever performances at Big Events.

That he could not get the MVP on either occasion is desperately unlucky, but also speaks to just how multi-faceted Vitality are when it comes to both their firepower and their utilisation of said firepower.

In Cluj, flameZ's strong final couldn't quite close the gap to ZywOo; in Rotterdam, flameZ's weak final cost him the medal. But if he keeps up this form, one will be going his way sooner rather than later.

Drin "⁠makazze⁠" Shaqiri

Pro League MVP makazze maintained superstar status in Rotterdam with antother exceptionally strong performance.

Natus Vincere's fragging leader averaged a 1.43 rating in the team's map wins, with 1.16 KPRW and a 1.20 rating against top 10 teams only adding to his case.

The final was tough, as expected, but makazze held his own to take his place as the top non-Vitality EVP.

Danil "⁠donk⁠" Kryshkovets

Group stage EVP awards are a rarity for a reason, but donk is not bound by what is achievable by normal players.

Added to his 1.59-rated group stage EVP from BLAST London last year, donk posted a 1.61 here to break his own record as the highest-rated EVP in CS2 history, according to data collected by forum user asyl1m.

donk ended six of his eight maps above a 1.60 rating, averaged 1.51 KPRW, and posted a multi-kill in an absurd 31% of rounds.

This won't matter much to him as Spirit's roster continues to toil below expectations, but at least he departs Brøndby back in an EVP list after missing out at Pro League.

Ali "⁠Wicadia⁠" Haydar Yalçın

It's two EVPs in two events for Wicadia as he builds on Pro League with another strong showing at Open.

The 21-year-old was held at 24th on last year's year-end list, having struggled in playoffs and against top teams, but is on a mission to change that narrative in 2026.

With Ismailcan "⁠XANTARES⁠" Dörtkardeş' injury starting to take a toll, there is a vacuum for Wicadia to fill and he did so admirably here by leading Aurora in map wins (1.34 rating).

It was tough opposition, too, as those wins came against FaZe, FURIA, and The MongolZ, while Aurora's two losses were against the tournament grand finalists.

Honorable mentions

Valeriy "⁠b1t⁠" Vakhovskiy averaged a 1.22 rating in the Ahoy Arena, but fell just shy of an EVP after a 0.96-rated group stage and middling 1.16 rating in map wins overall.

Andrey "⁠BELCHONOKK⁠" Yasinskiy led the way for EVP-less semi-finalists PARIVISION thanks to a great quarter-final, but as always for Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali's side credit was shared in round wins and his peaks were quite low.

XANTARES and Ivan "⁠zweih⁠" Gogin had good group stages, but did not push on in the arena, which was also true for Spirit-sacker Ayush "⁠mzinho⁠" Batbold.

No EVP for PARIVISION

Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov's 1.17 rating is in the range for an EVP, and he was excellent against FURIA, but his 1.25 rating in map wins was actually fourth in Falcons as credit was shared between players.

Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin delivered in playoffs at last, but also fell short overall with a 1.07 rating against top 10 opponents.

Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato nearly added a second group stage EVP, with his high floor and 1.27 rating putting him in contention, but he needed slightly more against Falcons and Aurora to reach that high bar.

Jonathan "⁠EliGE⁠" Jablonowski and Kacper "⁠xKacpersky⁠" Gabara also deserve shoutouts for their work despite early exits for both.

All-Star

Our three MVP candidates had to be in the All-Star side, with Firepower attribute scores of 100, 98, and 98.

That meant no room for Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire as in-game leader, due to a maximum of three players per team, allowing Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen to make it in as credit for Natus Vincere's run to the final.

No anchors made the EVP list, so b1t steps up for the second event in a row as the team's supportive base over BELCHONOKK, mzinho, and René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen.
