As Trump Campaigns, This Group Is Planning His Presidency

Briefing|As Trump Campaigns, This Group Is Planning His Presidency

https://www.nytimes.com/2024/10/24/briefing/trump-campaign-planning-canada-immigration-rules.html

Donald Trump traveled to the Southwest this evening for campaign rallies in the battleground states of Arizona and Nevada. He appeared earlier on a conservative radio show, saying that if elected it would take him only “two seconds” to fire the special counsel Jack Smith, who twice indicted the former president.

But behind the scenes, a prominent think tank is laying the groundwork for a much greater purging of government officials who are not loyal to Trump. The group, America First Policy Institute, didn’t even exist four years ago, but it has installed itself as the Trump campaign’s primary partner in making plans for a potential second term.

A.F.P.I., as the group is known, has received far less scrutiny than Project 2025, a similar right-wing plan made by the Heritage Foundation. But it is poised to be more influential. The group’s vision includes more barriers to abortions; more petroleum production; work requirements for Medicaid recipients; and the elimination of nearly all civil service protections. The U.N. said the world has made no progress on climate

One year after world leaders made a landmark promise to move away from fossil fuels, countries have essentially made no progress in cutting emissions and tackling global warming, according to a U.N. report released today.

The report said that nations have been so slow to curtail their use of oil, gas and coal that it now looks unlikely that countries will be able to hold global warming to the limit they agreed to under the Paris climate agreement in 2015.

Canada tightened immigration after years of expansion

Prime Minister Justin Trudeau announced today that Canada would significantly cut the number of newcomers it allows in. He said that the rapid growth in the country’s immigrant population over the past three years had strained resources like housing and health care.

Its annual target for the number of people made permanent residents, currently at 500,000, will fall to 395,000 next year, 380,000 in 2026 and 365,000 in 2027.

Prosecutors called for Menendez brothers resentencing

The Los Angeles County district attorney announced today that he was requesting the resentencing of Lyle and Erik Menendez, who killed their parents in 1989. The move could free the brothers from prison. They are serving sentences of life without parole.

A new documentary explores an online life lived well

Mats Steen, who died in 2014 at 25, lived out his final years nearly immobilized because he had been born with Duchenne muscular dystrophy. His family knew him as smart and loving, but grieved in part because they believed he would never experience love or friendship.

They were wrong. Mats was a beloved member of a community of World of Warcraft gamers, playing a burly, friendly man called Ibelin. The new film “The Remarkable Life of Ibelin” explores the way Mats’s online life was real in a way his reality couldn’t be.

Do people in ‘blue zones’ live longer?

The concept is simple and alluring: There are special regions around the world — called blue zones — where people remain vibrant and active into their 90s and 100s, thanks to a simple set of behaviors that anyone can follow.

It’s sensible enough to sound convincing and ambiguous enough to support a health and longevity empire. In the 20 years since blue zones were first introduced, the Blue Zones brand has spawned eight books, a Netflix series and several product partnerships. But some experts think the premise might be based on faulty data.

How to find your fufu

Facebook Marketplace is known as the internet’s garage sale because it’s full of sweet deals on used furniture and electronics, as well as a lot of junk. But a growing number of home cooks, many of them immigrants, have turned a corner of the website into a diverse bazaar with options like fufu — a West African dish that you might not find at your local restaurants.

The unlicensed cooks operate in a legal gray area. But several we spoke to said they had dozens of loyal customers, many of whom are thrilled to pick up a tray full of tamales for $22.
