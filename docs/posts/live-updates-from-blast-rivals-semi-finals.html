Live updates from BLAST Rivals semi-finals

Vitality 11-1 GamerLegion

...For the fewest number of kills in a first half by a team in a CS2 arena map, with just 17 across five players in a one-sided first half.

Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut had 16 by himself, just for a reference of how brutal this map has been.

The veto for the second semi-final is in, with GamerLegion looking to begin their hopeful upset campaign against Vitality on Mirage. The best team in the world have opted for Overpass after Dust2 was removed in the first round, with Nuke as a decider if needed.

On Dust2 against NAVI: "I think I was in my own head on T-side. They were making really good calls; makazze is in Pit one round, ghosting Long completely and not resmoking so I thought AWP was there. They had a really good plan, and I think Ancient was much better."

On Ancient loss: "I still think we could have been on a third map if we put together more CT rounds. I got into a good calling spree on T-side, hitting the right momentum calls and the right adaptations. It's really hard to win default against NAVI, lots of Mid control variations, and they were very well prepared for their T-side. T-side, it was working until they adapted and they put one more number at A that last round."

On shift from Liquid calling to now: "From when I stopped calling on Liquid and started being a more secondary helper with siuhy, that's when I took a smaller backseat and focused on developing. Even when I'm not the captain, I'm still watching and trying to learn because this is the role I want to be in in the future, and if I want to be in this role, I need to prove myself. I spent the last year and a half learning, focusing on myself a bit more. Playing with karrigan, I also got to learn a bit more there. Maybe in Liquid I flooded them with too much information, and some players have limitations, and you have to accept that, that's why I was happy with where we got in Liquid. I have a lot of belief in this lineup."

On FaZe's semi-final performance: "I mean, I think they can leave this game with one thing at the back of their heads. I feel like once they got to the T-side, they played completely fearless. They were rushing the sites, then they were creating a fake from it. I think a lot of it was maybe on-the-fly calls as well, they were just thinking we have nothing to lose, let's just play like this. If they play like that without any fear, and any doubts, it will do good for them in the future."

On worries during the comeback: "I'm glad we had plenty of rounds, first of all. w0nderful's clutch at the end of T-side became hugely important in this game, because it gave us more leverage. I do think you can't run away with the game doing the same stuff every round, so we knew they had to slow down and play their playbook, they cannot rush us on the sites, and we were putting more effort into making sure they don't rush us and try to make them play slowly, where we can play our best."

On the biggest change for his team this season: "A lot of teams have made roster changes, I think that's our strength. We're kind of peaking with this lineup, our protocols, our playbook, and the fact that makazze can play the way he plays currently are all the reasons why I think we are where we are right now. I felt we were pretty confident we would reach the final in this tournament, and our biggest challenge will be tomorrow, highly likely against Vitality."

Natus Vincere are the first team bound for the grand finals of BLAST Rivals Season 1, taking down FaZe in straightfoward fashion on their opponent's pick of Dust2, before nearly letting their own map pick of Ancient slip from their grasp after a 9-3 lead turned into a narrow 13-11 victory.
