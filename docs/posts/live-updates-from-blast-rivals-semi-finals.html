Live updates from BLAST Rivals semi-finals

Rémy "⁠XTQZZZ⁠" Quoniam, speaking on the analyst desk after the match:

On Overpass being close: "I think the T side should have been better. We had good situations, and after that it was 10-5, I think, and I don't know, for me, we started to play... we didn't have info on B, we didn't use flashes from Short et cetera. They did well as well, but it was not pretty. I don't know, we were hesitating to do some moves, and it was not the best Vitality on CT for sure."

On Vitality taking a break from practice: "We have to, at some point, make some priorities for tournaments. But I also think it's not the only excuse right now, the lack of practice, sometimes I feel like we took things a bit too easy, in my opinion. Especially on CT side, or when we lost one or two rounds on T side in the group stage, we went down, down, down. It's okay, we cannot be 100% all the time, right? But I don't want to use it as an excuse, we have to improve."

On why the group stage was shaky: "In the group stage, honestly, I felt like we were really tired. The energy was not there. Especially the second game, like we won the first map against G2, it was on Mirage, really tough, and we were outside and I was like, 'Wow, they are really tired right now. We have to fight guys, because it's going to be tough otherwise. I think it's the problem. The advantage last year, when we won the Grand Slam, we had a BLAST tournament instantly in Copenhagen, and this time I think we didn't have time to digest the Grand Slam. It was better last year at least, we had to play instantly, and right now I think it's a bit harder."
