KSCERATO: "No Brazilian team won a tournament in Brazil, so hopefully this will be our time"

FURIA guaranteed themselves an appearance in front of a roaring home crowd arena at IEM Rio after recovering from 2-9 down on the Nuke decider against Natus Vincere in the Group B upper bracket sem-final.

After the match, Kaike "⁠KSCERATO⁠" Cerato explained to HLTV just how much it would mean to the Brazilian-majority team to do what none have done before them and lift a trophy on home soil.

"The motivation I have is different, it's different for other people," he said. "Each of us has a motivation, but my motivation is to win a trophy in Brazil, and also to show the fans and my family that I can do it."

The hope comes amidst an up-and-down start to the season for FURIA, who are still trying to rediscover the form that helped them win four trophies towards the tail end of 2025.

"We've been practicing for two weeks right now, so I think we fixed a lot of stuff," KSCERATO said. "But sometimes the stuff doesn't work as we want, so we just need to keep going, working, and trust ourselves because in this game you cannot lack confidence about the team, about the strat, and all this shit."

Watch the interview here.

Congratulations, you're through to the playoffs in Rio. Crazy match, so just give me your reaction after all of that.

Bro, it was hard as fuck. My neck is tight, it was so tense to play. They played really well in the first half, and then the second, starting on T side, you need the pistol and first gun round to come back, and it's pretty tough to play against them. Right now they are playing really good individually and team-wise, so I think that it was hard as fuck. I'm pretty happy that we won some clutches because it was really important. The game was not easy, and we have to keep going.

My form is good. I fucked up in some situations that I should not, maybe it's a lack of confidence or maybe overconfidence sometimes, but overall I'm doing good

It started out really rough for you on the first map as well, then on the decider you were down 2-9, molodoy saved an AWP and that helped get the last round and get back into it. Talk to me about rallying back like that and how that felt, especially now that you're through to the playoffs.

Well, I think that we made some bad choices on the CT side, and also we just couldn't shoot in the first half against them. They were hitting really hard, like makazze was playing really good outside and b1t did an ace I think? [Editor's note: it was a 4k] So they are really good individually, and if you fuck up something, you're going to die bro, so it was really hard.

CT Mirage, we made bad choices honestly, we chose to be four A and then suddenly they are pushing Short, killing FalleN, and have bomb for free. That's the game playing against them, everything can happen. I think playing against any of these teams, you can get spanked or you can spank them. That's the game, and we have to watch what we did wrong and be better for the next matches.

The Major at the end of last year wasn't a great result, but before that you had a pretty strong period with a few trophies. This year has also started a bit up and down, so what do you make of your form now?

I think my form is good. I fucked up in some situations that I should not, maybe it's a lack of confidence or maybe overconfidence sometimes, but overall I'm doing good. I'm hitting hard when I have to and I'm playing really important roles right now, so being impactful is important for the team. I'm trying my best to be better.

I mean more on the team's form overall.

Ah, the team form overall is good. We've been practicing for two weeks right now, so I think we fixed a lot of stuff. But sometimes the stuff doesn't work as we want, so we just need to keep going, working, and trust ourselves because in this game you cannot lack confidence about the team, about the strat, and all this shit. If FalleN calls it, fucking do it. If he needs me to go down Vents four times, I'll go bro, it doesn't matter. We have to trust each other, and also trust ourselves.

Danil only started playing in tier-one last year, but for sure this one will be the craziest tournament for him

Making the playoffs in Rio is really big for you FURIA and all of you, even molodoy who seems to be a crowd favorite everywhere he goes at this point. How much are you looking forward to playing in front of that crowd? Last time you were in Rio, it ended in the semis for you, so hoping for a better result ths time?

[Yeah], hope for a better result. There is no Brazilian team that won a tournament in Brazil so hopefully this will be our time, but we have to work hard for sure. Danil [molodoy] and Mareks [YEKINDAR]- I think Mareks played a playoffs here once, or... I don't think so, actually.

So it will be really nice for them to see how the crowd is, how the fans are, it'll be pretty loud — you know that (laughs), you can bet on that bro. We cannot even hear the footsteps for sure (laughs), and it will be nice. I love this country, I love this city, the people, the fans, it will be an amazing atmosphere for sure. And Danil only started playing in tier-one last year, but for sure this one will be the craziest tournament for him.
