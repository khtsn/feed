GamerLegion claim Parken Challenger Championship title

GamerLegion have emerged victorious at Parken Challenger Championship Series 1, dropping a single map on their route to the trophy. With their victory GamerLegion have secured a healthy boost to their VRS standing, bagging nearly 100 points and climbing to #14 in the live ranking.

The trophy-winning campaign comes as a welcome boon to a GamerLegion squad that have posted a mixed bag of results early in 2026, taking second at Fragadelphia Miami and making the LAN finals of BLAST Bounty, but exiting CCT Europe Series 13 and IEM Krakow early doors and failing to qualify for BLAST Open Rotterdam.

GamerLegion's run in Denmark began in slow fashion, the European side being taken to overtime by underdogs Avanti and losing to SINNERS before warming into things and picking up three straight wins to secure a playoff berth.

illwill continue making strides up the VRS

illwill are one of the fastest-rising teams in the tier-two scene in Europe, and their run to another top LAN finish saw them gain a 147 point boost to their VRS total, putting them inside the top 30.

In recent weeks the eclectic mix of European talent won NODWIN Clutch Series 4, placed second at Copenhagen Gaming Week Showdown, and second at Parken Challenger Championship. Qualification for IEM Cologne Major is a long shot, but should the team stick together and continue performing well, qualification for PGL Singapore Major may be a more realistic dream.

SINNERS were another team who saw their VRS standing benefit significantly from their Parken run, gaining over 100 points to put them just inside the top 30 in the live ranking. Again, however, Cologne Major qualification will likely prove beyond them.
