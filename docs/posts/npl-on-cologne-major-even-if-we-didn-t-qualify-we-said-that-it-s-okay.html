npl on Cologne Major: "Even if we didn't qualify, we said that it's okay"

B8 came into PGL Bucharest off the back of not only a trophy win at Parken Challenger Championship, but also having secured a place at IEM Cologne Major. The Ukrainian side did so despite a difficult season where they have struggled for results in tier-one events.

"We were happy for sure, but if we even didn't qualify we said that it's okay," Andrii "⁠npl⁠" Kukharskyi told HLTV of his team's Major qualification. "We have a lot of good tournaments in the future... even if we didn't qualify, we could bounce back next season and even make Stage 2, or even better."

The feel-good factor has carried through to their campaign in Bucharest as B8 kicked things off in Romania with a win over Wildcard, setting them up to build some confidence as the Major in Germany looms ever closer.

After beating Wildcard, npl sat down with HLTV for a quick interview to discuss his team's journey to the Major and the progression of their newest addition, Daniil "⁠s1zzi⁠" Vinnyk.

You can listen to the full interview here.

How are you feeling after that match?

Feeling great.

What are your thoughts on that match?

We just played good; we maintained confidence and focus, which we had during our previous tier-two LAN.

I wanted to talk about that previous LAN. You secured your spot at the Major with that LAN win. How is the team feeling now that you've got your spot at the Major?

We were happy for sure, but if we even didn't qualify we said that it's okay, because we have a lot of good tournaments in the future. We have like four or five tier-one invites, and even if we didn't qualify, we could bounce back next season and even make Stage 2, or even better.

How was it playing at that Parken LAN? Did the team feel the pressure? Because that was your last chance to make sure you got to the Major.

No, we didn't feel pressure.

We have like four or five tier-one invites, and even if we didn't qualify, we could bounce back next season and even make Stage 2, or even better

Even after that loss against WAZABI in the group stage? I think that was quite surprising for a lot of people.

No. I don't know what to say. Best-of-one on Inferno is sometimes a lucky map, if you receive a lot of nade damage and if the opponent's gambles work every time, it can be very hard to play. It was just an unlucky match for us, but that's okay.

So was the team okay after that loss, not feeling too upset?

Obviously we lost confidence after that match, but like I said, we had a small talk and said that it was okay, because it's Inferno, as I said before.

Were you proud of the team, that you were able to bounce back after that loss and go on to win the tournament?

Yeah for sure. It was very hard for us to play with confidence in game, because we had a very unlucky season, every time high-seeding team... I mean unlucky seeding for us and we were playing against top teams. It's very hard for a team that changed an important role, in our position it was the AWPer. If you don't win matches and are a young team, it's very hard to show your game every time consistently.

We just managed to beat them and after the match we showed them where they belong

I wanted to talk about you playing against BESTIA in that tournament, the South Amercian team. It seemed like there was a little bit of drama between you two, what was that about?

I don't think we had drama, just they're like… you know SA players, every time they scream a lot. We just managed to beat them and after the match we showed them where they belong, and that's it.

Obviously you have your new AWPer, s1zzi. Now that you've had a bit of time to play with him, how are you feeling about him as an AWPer and his development in the team?

He's becoming better and better with every tournament. Obviously you cannot expect him to show very good numbers on HLTV because he has a big lack of experience. I can say that [compared to] when I was in NAVI, I see that he is sometimes making the same mistakes, and I understand that he just needs time and he will for sure play like a good player.
