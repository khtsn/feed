Firefighters to Search Burned Pennsylvania House Where 2 Police Officers Were Shot

U.S.|Investigators Search Burned House Where 2 Police Officers Were Shot

The authorities said at least six people were unaccounted for at the home in suburban Philadelphia, which burned down after the shooting.

Joel Wolfram

Reporting from East Lansdowne, Pa.

Published Feb. 7, 2024Updated Feb. 8, 2024, 1:01 p.m. ET

An excavator dug through smoldering rubble on Thursday in the Philadelphia suburbs, where officials feared that several people might be dead in the remains of a house that burned down after two police officers were shot.

Arson investigators entered the residence in East Lansdowne, Pa., just before 10 a.m. as firefighters continued to hose down hot spots in the wreckage. The authorities said at least six people, including children, were unaccounted for at the house, which remained unstable.

“I think today might be a grim day,” Jack Stollsteimer, the Delaware County district attorney, said in a television interview on Thursday morning. “We know at least the shooter is in there. But there may be other people from the family as well. That is our fear.”

It remains unclear exactly what happened at the home on Lewis Avenue. Mr. Stollsteimer said at a news conference late Wednesday that the authorities had received a report that an 11-year-old girl had been shot there, and two officers were hit by gunfire when they went to investigate.

Officer John Meehan, 44, of the East Lansdowne Police Department, was taken to Penn Presbyterian Medical Center with a gunshot wound to his left arm, the district attorney’s office said on Thursday, and Officer David Schiazza, 54, of the Lansdowne Police Department, was shot in the leg.

Neither was badly hurt. “They’re doing good,” said Christopher Eiserman, president of the Delaware County Fraternal Order of Police. Officer Schiazza was expected to be released from the hospital on Thursday afternoon, officials said.

A short time after the shooting, as a standoff with the gunman continued, the house went up in flames, Mr. Stollsteimer said, adding that it was unclear what had caused the blaze. Firefighters had to be cautious while trying to extinguish it, he said, because they didn’t know if the gunman was still a threat.

“We had men — police officers with automatic weapons — trained on the house while they were putting out the fire,” the district attorney said.

Deputy Chief Steve Castellano of the East Lansdowne Fire Company said that all three floors of the house had collapsed into the basement, leaving only exterior walls partially standing.

The excavator was helping firefighters peel away layers of the wreckage like an onion, he said. “They’re taking pieces out piece by piece, trying to sift through what debris is left.”

An update from the authorities was expected on Thursday afternoon.

Jacey Fortin, Jesus Jiménez and Victor Mather contributed reporting.
