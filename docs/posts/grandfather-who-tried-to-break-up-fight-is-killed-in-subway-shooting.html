Grandfather Who Tried to Break Up Fight Is Killed in Subway Shooting

The victim, Richard Henderson, “got shot stepping into an altercation that he had nothing to do with,” his wife said. He was killed on a No. 3 train.

Jonah E. Bromwich

Jan. 15, 2024

Next month would have marked the 30th year that Richard and Jakeba Henderson had been together, and March the 20th year they had been married.

But on Sunday, Mr. Henderson, 45, a father of three and a grandfather to two little girls, was fatally shot aboard a No. 3 train in Brooklyn after intervening in an argument between two other passengers over loud music in the car, the police said.

Mr. Henderson, who worked as a crossing guard at a private school in Manhattan, had been watching football with friends, Ms. Henderson said in a phone interview, and was returning home to Crown Heights on the subway. He was shot as the train was nearing the Rockaway Avenue stop in Brownsville, the police said, just three stops from his own.

“He got shot stepping into an altercation that he had nothing to do with,” Ms. Henderson said, adding that her husband was known for standing up to bullies. “He died a hero. He died doing what he did — taking up for the weak.”

The Manhattan-bound train continued on after the shooting, coming to a stop at the Franklin Avenue station in Crown Heights, where the police responded to a 911 call around 8:15 p.m. Mr. Henderson, who was shot in the back and shoulder, was transported to a nearby hospital, where he was pronounced dead.

No arrests have been made in the case. It is not clear whether the gunman was targeting the passenger he was arguing with or Mr. Henderson, the police said.

It was the latest difficult episode for New York’s subway system, the backbone of the city, which has struggled in the early weeks of 2024.

Already this year, there have been two train derailments, one of which injured 26 people and led to significant service disruptions for days.

And last week, a teenage boy was killed in what authorities said was a “subway surfing” incident, in which thrill-seekers ride atop cars. It was the second such death in two months.

“Our hearts go out to the family of Richard Henderson,” the president of New York City Transit, Richard Davey, said in a statement.

“This is another sad reminder that gun violence has no place in this city,” he said. “The M.T.A. is cooperating fully with the investigation to ensure the perpetrator can be brought to justice.”

Shootings on subway trains are rare and make up a fraction of the gun crimes in New York City. In November, two people were shot on a moving subway car in the Bedford-Stuyvesant neighborhood of Brooklyn. They sustained minor injuries.

Overall, shootings in the city, which spiked during the height of the pandemic, have come down. In 2023, about 1,100 people were shot, roughly 400 fewer than in the previous year.

But those statistics mean little to families like the Hendersons, whose lives were transformed Sunday night in the moment it took to fire a gun. In addition to his wife, Mr. Henderson is survived by three children, Richard Jr., Lavina and Janaya, and two young granddaughters.

Mr. Henderson was “the life of the party,” Ms. Henderson said.

“He was the one getting everyone dancing, pulling everyone on to the dance floor to get the party started,” she said. “He liked Biggie Smalls, that was his man.”

The two met when they were young teenagers. Ms. Henderson was out on a date with a friend of Mr. Henderson’s when her future husband’s smile caught her eye.

“He had the prettiest white teeth,” she said. “I stopped talking to his friend, and I started talking to him.”

Maria Cramer contributed reporting. Sheelagh McNeill contributed research.
