After his team's exit from IEM Atlanta, Passion UA captain Johnny "⁠JT⁠" Theodosiou chatted briefly with HLTV regarding the elimination series against SINNERS, the roster instability that has plagued his team this season, and his experience playing alongside stand-in sdy.

Or you can listen to the interview here.

Commiserations on the loss, which has you out of IEM Atlanta and your season all wrapped up. Walk me through that game a little bit; what went wrong on those last two maps?

I think Mirage, we kinda lost our composure, even when we were winning. On the CT-side we had a good half, I think it was 7-5 or something, but we weren't very happ after that, and I think that bled into the T half. We couldn't really get it rolling. I think the ideas we had were incomplete, and our mid rounds were lacking communication. I think it really bit us in the ass, our teamplay and communication, and just in general, the T execution wasn't that good.

Why do you think you guys have had so many difficult results this season?

I think a lot of it, and I've already answered it a few times, is that roster instability has been really hard for us. Lack of time with the same five, we've had stretches where it's been different fives I think three or four times now. Even coming into this tournament, we had I think three days of pracc with the actual team. It's hard to get consistent results like that. I also think the frustrations from the players, even with the circumstances, it's been hard to get into these games. Even when we're winning, we're still lacking comms, getting upset, and still making a lot of mistakes.

I would ask if changes are on the horizon, but it sounds like maybe this five just needs more time. How do you feel about it?

Definitely we need to know the plan for the future, what the set five is, we need to sign some players and complete the roster. From there, we can actually work towards something and actually develop the stratbook and just evolve as players. Because, right now we're kinda just in limbo, and we keep making the same mistakes, starting from stage one, and I think we just need to finalize it, and from there we can evolve into whatever we need to be.

How have you liked working with sdy as piece in your brief time with him?

Honestly, it's been very easy to play with him. He's got great energy, a good understanding of the game. Of course, he was IGLing at certain points of his career, so he's got a lot of good ideas. He was also trying his best to keep us in the game, even now. I think in general, he'll be a good piece for any team. I think he's a really strong player with a good mind for the game.
