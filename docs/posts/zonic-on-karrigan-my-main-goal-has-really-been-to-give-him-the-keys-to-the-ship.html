karrigan interview: Leaving FaZe, joining Falcons, and first impressions

Following their debut victory against K27, the team's coach Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen spoke to HLTV about karrigan's integration and the future of the new-look Falcons.

"Obviously, he brings a lot of energy, a lot of mood, but also leadership, which I think is something that we need with this team," he said.

"We have a lot of players with opinions and you need to have a hard hand on them sometimes, and sometimes it's a balance about giving them freedom as well. I think Finn [karrigan] is the exact guy to fix that."

Congratulations, a nice start to the time with karrigan on the team, but it got close in the end as well. Talk me through that game and what that experience was like with him on the server for the first time.

It was good. Obviously, he brings a lot of energy, a lot of mood, but also leadership, which I think is something that we need with this team. We have a lot of players with opinions and you need to have a hard hand on them sometimes, and sometimes it's a balance about giving them freedom as well. I think Finn [karrigan] is the exact guy to fix that.

He brings a lot of energy, a lot of mood, but also leadership, which I think is something that we need with this team

Yeah, Ancient overall was good, I think on CT side we made some wrong calls, and also a little bit on the discipline department, but overall it was okay. It's a lot of new stuff coming in, of course mainly on the T sides. karrigan is bringing his system, but also for Rene [TeSeS] playing new positions and having to adapt to that is... we haven't had that many days, I would say.

A new experience for you as well, now you're saying, 'Nice Finn!' on top of all the other 'Nices' that we're used to. Reunited with him as well, so what has it been like for you to be back together after so long?

It's great being back with Finn. We worked together 10 years ago in Astralis, a different time, and we kind of progressed out of two different styles, I would say. Me, representing the more structured way, together with gla1ve, and Finn being the more creative, calling on the fly, mid-round calling as he has been.

I will say that I think the game has developed more into his style of play, where you kind of have to play everything, so now I have a little bit of a different role. Maybe sometimes trying to pull back the structure a bit when things get a bit out of hand, but he is definitely what we needed in terms of connecting the players. Even though it's early days, I'm seeing promising signs in practice, and I'm super happy to be back working with him again.

Yeah, I wanted to ask about that philosophy overlap because you did have different philosophies back in the day. You alluded to it a bit, it's moved in his direction, so what has it been like bringing that together now?

It's been important for me to... Finn, but also ourselves, but mainly for him, it's been a rough period. My main goal has really been to give him the keys to the ship, if you can call it that, that he feels in control, he feels really [like we're] really supporting him a lot, and putting him in the driver's seat because he needs that.

I know how it is to be in a rough period, and I think your worst enemy is yourself sometimes, so for me it has just been important that he really feels listened to and respected

He needs to get back into being confident about himself. I know how it is to be in a rough period, and I think your worst enemy is yourself sometimes, so for me it has just been important that he really feels listened to and respected. We have had so many talks since I came home from Rio, and it's just been nice to get together with him, talk CS, talk philosophy, but my main focus has really been to let him know that this is his way of calling that we are following, and I'm supporting him.

You couldn't really talk about it in Rio, but I wanted to pick your brain on this whole decision-making process about bringing him in and moving away from kyxsan. Can you talk me through that, what led to it from your side, and how it came together?

kyxsan has a bright future, I want to state that to begin with. In some way, it wasn't deserved. As I said, he has a bright future, he has a really smart head for the game, but also a tough task. I don't think you can find anyone more experienced than karrigan, who has played in different lineups, on different stages, and having to manage big players.

I would say he is probably the most qualified IGL out there, in terms of experience. I don't want to go too much into details right now, I'm just here, it's obviously a chapter that was before Rio and in Rio, and I just want to give my respects to kyxsan but also super excited to be working with karrigan. That's pretty much all I have to say, I'm just here focusing on this tournament with this roster.

karrigan mentioned the timeline of how this came together so quickly for him in an interview with me. With trying to get that sorted so quickly before the Major and before Astana, what has it been like trying to integrate the team so quickly with him?

I think it was a bit of a shock for the players when they arrived in the first day. I'm super proud of the way karrigan just took charge. We had some talks, and I also told him what we need as a team, that he needs to work with Maxim [kyousuke]'s development, he needs to [make himself respected], he needs to make sure that they listen to his calls.

I'm super proud of the way karrigan just took charge

I had some talks with him regarding this, and he just from the get-go said, 'This is how I want to play.' On the first day, when people were making some mistakes, he was instantly saying, 'Hey guys, this is not what I meant.' Talking communication as well, which has been a key point, so he has just brought in everything that he knows. Especially when it comes to the T sides, it is pretty much his system now.

On the flip side, the CT side, he mentioned in an interview with BanKs that it is NiKo and m0NESY that he is giving the reins to a little bit. What has the dynamic been like between him and them on the server so far?

It's been pretty good. I mean, obviously he doesn't have the most sexy positions on the CT side, so it's pretty much just connecting with his closest teammates. He's still chiming in if he feels like NiKo doesn't have an opportunity, he's at least suggesting stuff around his own side and this I think is good as well, so NiKo gets a second hand.

But on CT side, it's not where we have really been struggling; I think we've been pretty good. Ilya [m0NESY] has a lot of ideas where he wants to peek, NiKo has an overall macro-decision to be made, so on CT side it's been pretty smooth, and also a lower focus point. Which, in the short-term, might be exposed a bit, but it's been mainly the T sides that has been our focus point.

I was watching back through your Rio vlogs, and both in there and something karrigan alluded to in his interview with me, was someone needing to take accoutability for some of these moments that happen, some of the ways that the team falls apart, especially in playoff games. How are you approaching that, to have someone take that accountability? Because karrigan seems like he wants to step into that.

I think everyone is to blame, if you're talking about some of the rounds that we have fucked up in the past or when things don't go well. I also take my part of the blame, and the only thing that I can do is work on it every day and have talks. We did some where we tried to prioritize the players to focus on the team; I think that has solved some of the issues, but they are still there.

But at the end of the day, they also need to play, and also a guy like Maxim, needs to play a lot of CS because this is how all pro players, including myself when I was playing, start out. You grind the game 12 hours a day, and he is still on a very early stage of his career. So it's about taking the temperature of the team, where we are in the season, what we need here now. I can announce here, breaking news for HLTV, that they have been allowed to play some FACEITs before this event. So it's just about taking the temperature and if we feel like the team has to be more on the focus point.

I've had some talks again with Finn, and we are a little more aligned with this, that they just need to understand that the team comes first and we can never sleep on this. We have to be thorough and disciplined in this, and even in this game, some parts of the CT side, we saw a little bit. Whether it's cockiness or [something], we have to step out of this because there are a lot of good teams and you will get punished, and especially in MR12, you can't afford it. So we have to stay disciplined; this is something that we're building, but one step at a time.

It goes back to the whole conversation about needing to rein players in sometimes, step away from the star mentality into the team mentality. Have you noticed karrigan already helping with that, or is it too early to tell?

I will say that the guys have been pretty good in practice, so he hasn't had too much of this. I think everyone has just been excited to play, and some of the players also want to impress him. Especially a guy like Maxim, he has blossomed up a lot.

Communicating more, talking about what he's doing, showing good discipline. He also showed really good discipline this game so he hasn't had too much, but every time there has been a point, he has not been afraid to... not lash out, but at least comment on it, and I think this is really what we need. Be more specific, more direct to the player making the mistake, instead of talking overall where some misunderstandings can occur.

You briefly mentioned it, talking about the objectives and where the team is going to head from here. What are you setting as the expectation? Astana is obviously quite short-term from when karrigan came in, but some people have you as favorites to win it, and then obviously the Major is coming up. So what is the short to long-term outlook for Falcons and for you right now?

With this being such a big change, changing an IGL, I don't want to go into specifics and say, 'We have to do semi-finals.' I've never been a fan of this. I think for me, it's more about the process, that we have to take every game that we're playing seriously, take that as data that we're getting in, and also for Finn, that he's getting in.

He has to, in some sense and even though he's been playing with NiKo, he needs to get to know four players because everyone has developed, including himself. He's a lot different compared to when I had him in Astralis, so it's a new team and he needs to get to know four players. We want to do everything we can in order for us to be ready for the Major, so it's just been focusing more on the overall picture.

If our main goal was Astana, for example, we would probably have focused on some maps more than others, but we have really tried to structure it so that we are as ready as possible for the Major. But I don't want to look at it and say, 'We have to finish like this,' for me it's more the process that we are doing step by step throughout this still short period.

I just wanted to touch on your own personal perspective on this change. Pressure has been mounting on you throughout this whole period, some people thought that you've been given a lot of chances, had all of these superstars to play around, and now karrigan is in here, which seems like the final piece, really. How do you feel about your own situation with where Falcons is heading and how karrigan sets you up for this run towards this Major and the next?

I'm only focusing on these five players, and obviously the organization, when it comes to my own profile, the only thing that I can say is that I am giving my all. Working every day, trying to fix what I can, so as long as they are happy — the players and also Falcons — that means everything to me, of course.

When it comes to the criticism of me, I understand it. Results have not been delivered, and I take responsibility for that. Would I wish that it would have happened earlier? You know, a lot of things could maybe have been different if we had just nailed that trophy last season, but... we didn't get it.

At the end of the day, I only answer to the Falcons management, and any other questions regarding that you have to bring [up] with them

And part of that responsibility is obviously on the head coach, and I take responsibility for that. So yeah. At the end of the day, I only answer to the Falcons management, and any other questions regarding that you have to bring [up] with them.

You're 1-0 now in the Swiss stage, and there are some teams that could be a big challenge for you. If PARIVISION and FURIA win, you could face Spirit in your next game, but still a long ways to go for that. How do you feel about your next round matchup, even though you don't quite know what it is yet?

I mean, it doesn't really matter who we play. I want to see the same energy, the same approach to the game, whether it is against Spirit, FURIA, or K27. I want to see that level of professionalism, also if it is group stage game or a stage game, I want to see the same Falcons every time.
