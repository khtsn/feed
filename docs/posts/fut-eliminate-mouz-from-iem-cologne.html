FUT eliminate MOUZ from IEM Cologne

The youngest team at the Major lives to fight another day.

FUT got the best of MOUZ in a three-map series in the 1-2 pool of the IEM Cologne Major's third stage, eliminating the Hamburg-based organization from the event in Germany and securing a berth in the deciding day of the Swiss group.

The series kicked off on FUT's Ancient pick, a map that MOUZ welcomed, and it turned out to be a nailbiter, going all the way to 24 rounds. Basics ruled the map with trades, plants, and late-round situations galore.

Džiugas "⁠dziugss⁠" Steponavičius's impact for FUT was most notably felt amid the strong team performances as he finished with a 95.8% KAST and +6.11% swing, but the final round came down to a clean A defense that MOUZ could do nothing against.

FUT put up a wall early on the CT side of Nuke, but strong individual performances by Dorian "⁠xertioN⁠" Berman with back-to-back multi-kills and 1v1 clutches turned the tides as MOUZ went on to secure six T-side rounds, enough to settle on the CT side after a USP quad-kill by Ádám "⁠torzsi⁠" Torzsás and win out 13-10 to force the decider.

It all came down to Mirage and FUT, on the attack, showed that they were the superior team, going on a six-round streak after falling behind in a 2-4 start to secure a healthy lead going onto the CT side.

MOUZ got the second pistol round, and a quick triple on B by torzsi followed it up, but FUT were quick to settle into the driver's seat as the xertioN-led squad floundered on the attack and fell apart, unable to threaten FUT's defensive side with Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija and his teammates winning out 13-7.
