Hall of Fame eligibility rules get an overhaul ahead of nominations for class of 2026

The HLTV Hall of Fame presented by 1xBET enters its third year with some updates following the inaugural Swedish class of 2024, which featured four players, and the second class in 2025, which brought the total count of Hall of Famers to seven.

For the 2026 class, with the continued support from 1xBET, the Hall of Fame will return to inducting four players with the goal of recognizing the game's early stars. To do that, a minimum of two and a maximum of three of the four players to be inducted will be from 1.6 to early CS:GO.

These changes come after the Hall of Fame committee met in person for the first time at the HLTV Awards in Belgrade, in which eligibility rules, such as a period of inactivity at the highest level after a player's career, were discussed and updated.

Inactivity rule update

Pre-CS:GO players:

  • 18 months without a Big event played.

  • 1 year inactive from the main roster of any competitive team. (This doesn’t include stand-in appearances, teams without an HLTV ranking, or teams in which the player was not listed on the HLTV team page).

  • June 2028 (18 months since retirement): Eligible to be nominated and voted in if still inactive.

  • January 2029 (two years): Could be inducted into the Hall of Fame if voted in.

Eligibility will also be affected by the elimination of what was colloquially known as the Filip "⁠NEO⁠" Kubski rule, which did not allow a player to be inducted for his playing career while being active in another area of the game, such as coaching.

The Hall of Fame committee itself will also expand this year, as another member has been added: Dmitry "⁠hooch⁠" Bogdanov, a former player, coach, and broadcaster.

The voting process will begin in the summer, following the IEM Cologne Major, when the Hall of Fame committee will meet to discuss this year's nominees. Ten players will be nominated in total, five from the pre-CS:GO era and five from the CS:GO and CS2 era. Four will be voted into the hall.
