Vance to Hit Campaign Trail in an Upended Race

In his first speech as former President Donald J. Trump’s running mate on Wednesday at the Republican National Convention, Mr. Vance barely mentioned Ms. Harris. Instead, he took repeated shots at Mr. Biden, reminding supporters that the president was involved in politics before Mr. Vance was born.

After Mr. Biden’s announcement on Sunday, Mr. Vance previewed his message on social media, drawing a direct link from the president’s policies to Ms. Harris.

“Joe Biden has been the worst President in my lifetime and Kamala Harris has been right there with him every step of the way,” Mr. Vance said before tying her to the Biden administration’s immigration and energy policies. “She owns all of these failures, and she lied for nearly four years about Biden’s mental capacity — saddling the nation with a president who can’t do the job. President Trump and I are ready to save America, whoever’s at the top of the Democrat ticket. Bring it on.”

On Saturday, Mr. Vance joined Mr. Trump for a rally in Grand Rapids, Mich., where he criticized Ms. Harris, a former prosecutor and the first female vice president, for her long career in public office. Ms. Harris, 59, has held office since 2004, when she was the first person of color elected as San Francisco’s district attorney.

“I did serve in the United States Marine Corps and build a business,” Mr. Vance said in Michigan. “What the hell have you done other than collect a check?”
