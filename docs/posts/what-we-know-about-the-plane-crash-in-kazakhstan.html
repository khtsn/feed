What We Know About the Plane Crash in Kazakhstan

The Embraer 190 was traveling to Grozny, Russia, but crashed near Aktau, Kazakhstan, killing 38 people onboard. Investigators are focusing on Russian air defense as the likely cause.

Dec. 27, 2024Updated 3:27 p.m. ET

Two days after a plane bound for Russia crashed in Kazakhstan, killing dozens of passengers, the White House said on Friday that there was reason to believe that Russia was responsible.

In a telephone news conference with reporters, John F. Kirby, a White House national security spokesman, said there were “some early indications that would certainly point to the possibility that this jet was brought down by Russian air defense systems.”

“That said, there’s an ongoing investigation right now,” he added. “Kazakhstan and Azerbaijan are conducting this jointly. We have offered our assistance to that investigation.”

The plane, an Embraer 190 airliner operated by Azerbaijan Airlines, was traveling from Baku, Azerbaijan, to Grozny, Russia, when it crashed on Wednesday on the shore of the Caspian Sea, near Aktau in Kazakhstan. Of the 67 people onboard, 38 died, including passengers and crew members. Most of the plane’s fuselage was severely burned.

As investigators focused on Russian air defenses as the probable cause of Wednesday’s crash, the aviation authorities in Moscow suggested a different culprit. In a statement on Friday, Russia said Ukrainian drone attacks might have played a role. But officials in Azerbaijan dispute that claim.
