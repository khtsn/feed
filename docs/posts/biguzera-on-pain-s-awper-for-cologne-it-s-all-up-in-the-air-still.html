biguzera on paiN's AWPer for Cologne: "It's all up in the air still"

paiN are one match away from securing a playoff berth at CS Asia Championships after battling past BC.Game and TYLOO in Group A's lower bracket, with just MOUZ standing between them and a second consecutive arena appearance after making the quarter-finals at IEM Atlanta.

Speaking to HLTV after beating TYLOO, Rodrigo "⁠biguzera⁠" Bittencourt admitted he looks back on Atlanta with some disappointment.

"It was the perfect event for us to make it to the finals, perhaps even win," he said. "So I think it was a really good event for us to go further. So we were left with a little taste of what we could have achieved."

The Brazilian captain also spoke about his exhaustion from playing back-to-back events amidst a lack of sleep, and touched on Rafael "⁠saffee⁠" Costa's integration in the team with Lucas "⁠nqz⁠" Soares remaining sidelined.

A long day of CS, biguzera. The Nuke loss to TYLOO was perhaps the only downside. Give me your thoughts on these two matches.

To be honest, those were two matches against dangerous teams, teams you can lose to. TYLOO are always a team that surprises you. They're a very tactical team, a team that is hard to face, so you have to study them a lot because it's a lot of tactics, a lot of execs. So it's hard to face them, especially after a tiring day, jet leg, and being awake since midnight.

It was tough, but we managed to find the resilience to finish the day off on a high note.

You were caught off-guard by M80 in your opening game, a BO1. What went wrong?

Besides a BO1 being tricky, I think it's been tough — our run of matches has been hard on some maps. For example, in our previous tournament we didn't play Mirage a single time, so we didn't practice Mirage for ten days, we didn't enter the map.

So then, after a long time, we come in and we play a BO1 on Mirage, and it's hard to just get into the rhythm on the map and everything. So I think it makes it a bit harder. Perhaps we screwed up the veto a bit as well.

I wanted to get your thoughts on your results in Atlanta. You beat FaZe and FUT and then lost to BetBoom and GamerLegion. Are you disappointed you didn't make it further? It seemed like the perfect tournament to reach the semi-finals or even the final.

Yeah, I think it was a tournament where we were in good shape, we were confident, and I think it was the perfect event for us to make it to the finals, perhaps even win. So I think it was a really good event for us to go further. So we were left with a little taste of what we could have achieved.

After you were eliminated, vsm posted on social media that he had been going through a difficult time in his personal life, which had been affecting his performances. How is the team helping him get through this?

I think the way we help each other is always by talking things through, right? We've always been known for this — we're a team built on friendship, where we can say anything. Whatever I have to say, I say it to their faces. rikz is just like that, too, so I think it's something that is already in our DNA. We speak our minds, and I think the truth hurts sometimes, but it can be good.

So I think that, for the team’s long-term success, it’s necessary to have this kind of conversation, this kind of dialogue. So I think that his self-awareness is also important—not just for the team, but for him personally as well. So I think it was really good that he recognized he needed to improve, to step up his game. So it was good.

saffee is a smart guy — he's the kind of guy who picks things up quickly, so you don't have to keep repeating the same thing to him over and over

Talk to me about how saffee is settling into the team. You had played with him in the past, but he was coming from a long break. Has it been easier than expected?

I think I already expected it wouldn't be difficult, because saffee is a smart guy — he's the kind of guy who picks things up quickly, so you don't have to keep repeating the same thing to him over and over. So I think it's something that comes naturally to him. He picks things up fast, so he brings a lot to the table in that regard — in terms of experience, comms, and even helping with mid-round calls and anti-strats.

You're now going up against MOUZ. This is a team with two new players, one of whom is a stand-in. What are your expectations?

To be honest, I haven’t watched anything from MOUZ yet. I only watched a demo from xertioN, because he was playing well, and when the IGL plays well, we, IGLs, like to watch, right? [laughs].

So, to be honest, I haven’t watched anything. It’s been exhausting here in China. I mean, the other night I slept three hours; last night I slept three hours again. And I think it’ll be more about our game than theirs tomorrow. I think we will need to focus on our game. We will wake up early, try to analyze them, and see what we can do.

Last question: any updates about nqz's availability for the Major?

It's all up in the air still. We don’t have any final word on anything. What we always want to is play the tournament we are in and focus on it. We haven't thought much about it, but saffee is someone who has settled in well with the team. I don't know how it's going to turn out, but saffee is doing well, too.

But a decision will have to be made soon, right?

Yes, yes. Probably.
