Short news: Week 9

The four round-robin groups for Roman Imperium Cup VI have been announced ahead of the event's start on March 12.

Several notable teams will compete in Vila Nova de Gaia, Portugal in an effort to bolster their VRS points ahead of the IEM Cologne Major invite cut-off on April 6. The top two teams from each group, which will feature best-of-one play, will advance to the single-elimination, best-of-three playoffs bracket.

The second edition of the Parken Challenger Championship takes place over the next three days with 100 Thieves the most notable team in attendance fighting for a $6,000 first-place prize and a CCT Global Finals spot.
