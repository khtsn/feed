NertZ: "I have full confidence in the players and the system we have"

A rejuvenated NertZ has a 1.24 rating after 17 maps in G2.

Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz's reunion with Eetu "⁠sAw⁠" Saha and Álvaro "⁠SunPayus⁠" García in G2 has 2023's Rookie of the Year smiling again.

His 2.04 series rating against Nemesis in the team's Stake Ranked Episode 1 opener showed the fragging potential NertZ so rarely showed in his stint with Liquid.

"I didn't have the best performance in Liquid," he reflected to HLTV in Barcelona. "I feel like the bigger thing is about personality clashes during my time in Liquid, and I think this is what held us back.

"Liquid is one of the best organizations in the world, so for me, it was just disappointing that we couldn't even make it even to be a good team. Overall, it was a clash of personalities. As flashie said in another interview, they want only two big voices on the team, and that's fair enough."

Tell me a bit about your adaptation with G2 in these first few tournaments.

It's early to say, but I had a good day. I think most of my kills were eco, but for me the vibe in G2 is incredible. I feel like we're on the same page with the jokes, we're all trolling each other, having fun, and this is for me something that's very important in a team because it's something that also affects inside the game.

Also, to come back and play with sAw, that made me happy, and I feel like the whole team is trying to push me to play better and better, which is something that's really important.

Tell me about the roles. It looks like you're getting those aggressive roles and huNter- is taking some of the lurking on the T side, which is strange for an IGL sometimes, but it seems like you're integrating into the team in a way that lets you shine. Would you say that? Are you happy that you're getting some good roles?

Nema [huNter-] sacrifices a lot. Also, MATYS sacrifices a lot because I joined. We have a lot of changes, for them and also for me, to make everyone comfortable. We will try, at least.

About the roles, I'm not even close to being comfortable right now, but we're on the right path. It's a little bit unfortunate that we don't have time to practice since we're on the road with these tournaments, but I feel like from the point we're right now to before the Major, we'll have a lot of changes.

I feel like the bigger thing is about personality clashes during my time in Liquid, and I think this is what held us back

Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz

Tell me a little about being on the road a lot and playing these lower-tier tournaments. How is it like? Smaller teams. Smaller venues. How is it going?

We're doing a decent job. For us, it was a good test about mentality and also being strong mentality because nowadays, in CS2, I feel like most of the teams know how to shoot, and you need to be confident no matter what... with the frustration, with maybe someone that you don't even know swinging on you, and sometimes the confidence. We're talking about how important it is for us to be in a good mental [state] during these games. Good resilience, basically.

For us, during these tournaments, it's true that in some situations we lost not because of a lack of practice or whatever, it's because of our mentality, because we cannot move on from things that happened in the past, and this is something that, as I said, helps us a lot, and I'm just waiting for us to practice online and to have time to fix the things we actually have to fix.

You were taking a bit about how MATYS is sacrificing a lot for you to feel comfortable. It looks like you and him can be a good pair working off of each other, especially as the entry duo on the T side, a good one-two combo. Is that something you noticed already?

I think MATYS is an insane player. He's similar to me outside of the game, and also inside, but for me, we already have a lot of strats in which me and MATYS are going in first and second. I feel like a good duo with firepower, and I want to keep this confidence that he has. I will sacrifice for him, he'll sacrifice for me. It's just a matter of time before I feel it click in this team.

Every team needs a HeavyGod

Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz

You had your breakout when you became Rookie of the Year, and there was the really fun ENCE and Heroic, but then you had the Liquid period, and it feels like you became much smaller. It wasn't the same NertZ as before. Why didn't things work out there for you?

I think it's part of personality. I won't blame anyone, but I didn't have the best performance in Liquid. I feel like the bigger thing is about personality clashes during my time in Liquid, and I think this is what held us back. Accountability...

Liquid is one of the best organizations in the world, so for me, it was just disappointing that we couldn't even make it even to be a good team. Overall, it was a clash of personalities. As flashie said in another interview, they want only two big voices on the team, and that's fair enough. For me, that makes sense, and I hope it will be good for them.

Coming to G2 now, what potential do you see in this team?

It's really hard for me to say because I still haven't had this click. It takes time because it takes a lot of practice, but I see us winning one tournament before the end of the year. I have full confidence in the players that we have, the system we have, and I don't have doubts about any of the players, and that's something that's super important for me. I can promise G2 fans that we'll win a tournament, at least for next year, and it will be amazing because we're planning something really good.

NertZ recommended HeavyGod to Endpoint

You're playing with HeavyGod, another Israeli player coming out of the factory. How is it playing with him, someone you knew well?

We were playing together in Endpoint, I recommended him to Endpoint, and we brought him into the team, and it was amazing. From the point when I was playing with him in Endpoint, to the point he is now, I feel like he's a top-three or top-two Anchor in the world. He's not selfish, and I feel like many people don't see this, but when he has an AK, and he's an Anchor, he's telling me like, 'take the drop, take the drop.' He's doing the sacrifice so I feel comfortable, and he's one of the best talents we have on the scene right now. It's just a blessing to play with a player like him on a team. Every team needs a HeavyGod in the team, 100 percent.

Coming back and playing with SunPayus, it seems like you guys got on very well during your time together in the past. What is reconnecting with him again like?

We always had these good vibes together. We're laughing a lot, he's a super funny guy. But the most important thing is that we're on the same page about almost everything. Like the way he sees CS, I see it the same, and I think it's important that me and my AWPer, the control players, we're on the same page. When you have this on a team, that's what's important. Also, when you have a coach that everyone is aligned with what he's saying, and respect him, that's something that I don't think you see on every team, and it's how it should be.

You went to two of these LANs before and lost to the winners, BetBoom and SINNERS. How do you feel about falling to the eventual winners?

It's a little bit frustrating to lose to these teams. I feel like they're good teams, but for us in G2, we should win, in my opinion. I know for a fact that in the future, if we have time to fix some mistakes and have the strat book that we can have, it can change.

In two weeks, after we have time to practice, we'll be a different team, and it won't even be close to what we are right now. I feel like it's something that was kind of frustrating, even if they won, it may give you a bit of a good feeling, but in the end of the day, it doesn't matter because you lost the tournament and it just gives you more drive to keep improving.

Looking forward to the Major and the end of the season, after these VRS tournaments, what are the goals you have?

As I said at the beginning of the interview, after the cut-off, I think we're going to change a lot of things. We will have talks, and we'll actually have time to change what we wanted to change from the start because, right now, we cannot change everything. This is something that can be a game-changer for us.
