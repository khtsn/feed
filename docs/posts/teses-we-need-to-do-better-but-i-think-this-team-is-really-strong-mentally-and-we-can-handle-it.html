TeSeS: "We need to do better, but I think this team is really strong mentally and we can handle it"

TeSeS put in a 1.24 rating as Falcons beat TYLOO in three maps

Falcons came back from a map down to beat TYLOO 2-1 and progress to Group A's lower bracket final at BLAST Open Rotterdam.

The international team let three map points slip on Inferno but bounced back with statement victories on Ancient and Mirage, dropping only three rounds across those two maps.

"I think this is at least one thing we know we can do in this team, is that we can go from one map playing like shit [laughs], and then we can play super good the next one," René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen told HLTV after the match.

"It also has disadvantages because it can also go the other way, but we know we can play good CS at any moment, and it doesn't matter what happened before this."

The Danish rifler, who is averaging a team-high 1.24 rating, also discussed his rise in form, the pressure to succeed, and Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen's calls for the players to put their egos aside and focus on the team.

A 2-1 victory against TYLOO and you move on in the lower bracket. It looked a bit scary at first, but you were much stronger on Ancient and Mirage. Give me your reactions to the series.

We definitely went into the first map not like how we wanted to, and we struggled a lot. We threw some advantages, and it just seemed like they had a good game plan against us. They were playing very direct, and it was like we were not completely ready for that. So we had to kind of try and adapt, but we didn't succeed at it.

But, then after the first map, we went out and talked about what we needed to fix, and one of the main topics was the mood and energy. It was, like, 'And it's now or fucking never if we want to go to the arena and to Rotterdam, so we need to step up and wake up.' And that's what we did.

You had three map points on Inferno, but you couldn't close it out. It's not an easy situation to be in, especially when it's against an underdog. Was the team confident that you would be able to turn it around?

Yeah, I think this is at least one thing we know we can do in this team, is that we can go from one map playing like shit [laughs], and then we can play super good the next one. It also has disadvantages because it can also go the other way, but we know we can play good CS at any moment, and it doesn't matter what happened before this.

I think the only map in this tournament where we didn't do the things we agreed about was Inferno against NAVI, where we started doing individual things and didn't play that well

René "⁠TeSeS⁠" Madsen

I wanted to get your thoughts on the series against NAVI. You won the first map but they turned it around. What were the main takeaways from that series?

I almost put it in the back of my mind and tried to forget it. But they played really well and they're in a really good period right now, so props to them. I think they played really well. We just struggled throughout the whole series, I think, especially the last two maps, and things didn't go our way, basically.

This has been a good tournament for you individually. Has anything changed about the way you are utilized in the game or the way you prepared for this one?

No, I don't think anything has changed in how I'm used or how I'm thinking about the game or anything like this. For me it's just a process, the whole thing, and right now there are some upward performances from me, and I'm very happy with that because I've really been needing it, and some confidence coming in is really nice.

But I'm just striving for success every single day and trying to do better, game by game, and not trying to make myself comfortable even after a few good performances. So I think mentally what has changed for me is just never making myself comfortable or being satisfied, and striving for success.

One of the biggest talking points about Falcons heading into this event was zonic asking the players to put their egos aside and focus on the team. Do you think the players have been open to that feedback?

Yeah, I really think so. I think the only map in this tournament where we didn't do the things we agreed about was Inferno against NAVI, where we started doing individual things and didn't play that well.

But other than that, I think we have been playing some really good situations, where in the past we have been rushing it and not playing that well. Especially our retakes have been insanely good, I think, most of the time, doing good things together and taking our time with it.

And what is the mood like in the team? Because there is a lot of pressure for the team to not only make playoffs, but make deep runs and get to the finals more consistently.

Right now it's a bit mixed. We all know that we haven't had the season that we want to and the start to the season we want to. So everyone knows that the pressure is on and we want to perform and go deep in tournaments. So everyone has this in the back of their mind, and this is how it's supposed to be, and a big organization like this cannot change it.

We need to do better, but I think this team is really strong mentally, and we can handle it. We just need to do it now.
