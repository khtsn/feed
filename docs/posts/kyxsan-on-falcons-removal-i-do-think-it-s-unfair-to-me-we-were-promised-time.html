kyxsan on Falcons removal: "I do think it's unfair to me, we were promised time"

There's "no bad blood," but kyxsan wishes Falcons had shown more patience with their lineup.

Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski has given his first interview since confirmation of being benched by Falcons in a 45 minute podcast with K Bizzy of Half Time CS.

In it, the 25-year-old in-game leader admitted that the news felt "unfair" and that it came as a surprise when he was told at IEM Rio.

"I arrived, the same day, I got a message from the manager that we needed to talk," he said. "I knew it wouldn't be a nice talk from the way he said it. But it still came as a shock to me because, before this day, there was never a discussion in the team about making changes.

"I am aware, everyone is aware, that we were not playing our best game this year but this is a project that we wanted to build long-term. It wasn't about winning an event now. This team was promised time."

There's no bad blood, I just think that team deserved a bit more time, and a bit more trust

Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski to K Bizzy

kyxsan has commented on the team being given time before, notably in January when Falcons, somewhat surprisingly, went through the transfer window without making any changes.

"Falcons were clear that they still believe in the team, even though the Major was not the best for us," he told HLTV at the time. "I'm really happy that this roster is getting the time that we need because I think we can achieve good things."

Four months later, kyxsan finds himself on the bench. The timing of the move, made at the start of IEM Rio, was also harsh as it made it impossible for the Macedonian to join a new team before the Major roster lock.

In his final vlog at Falcons, kyxsan admitted he struggled to sleep during IEM Rio after receiving the news and struggling with jet lag.

With K Bizzy, kyxsan spoke about how Falcons have operated in this way before, with both Emil "⁠Magisk⁠" Reif and Abdul "⁠degster⁠" Gasanov, but he was still taken off guard when it happened to him. And while "there isn't any bad blood", he went on to say that he does think the move is unfair:

"I understand the decision, that we didn't do the best this year and that at some point something has to change. But I do think it's unfair to me, we were promised time and that this was something we wanted to do long term. I feel like we didn't get that in the end, everything just fell apart."

kyxsan went on to stress why he felt this by highlighting the positives of the seven-month period he had with Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin, a time span where the team won 76 out of 121 maps, only missed playoffs twice, and made three grand finals.

"We didn't win a trophy, but most of the time we were playing good CS," he added. "That's why I think it's a bit unfair because we had shown we were one of the best teams in the world. [...] I understand the decision, there's no bad blood, I just think that team deserved a bit more time, and a bit more trust."
