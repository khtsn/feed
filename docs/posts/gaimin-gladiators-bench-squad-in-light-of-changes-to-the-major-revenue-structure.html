Gaimin Gladiators bench squad "in light of changes to the Major revenue structure"

Gaimin Gladiators have benched the remainder of their roster after a strategic review into their Counter-Strike program.

The team had already announced the exit of stand-in Fernando "⁠fer⁠" Alvarenga — who joined after the sudden termination of João "⁠felps⁠" Vasconcellos' contract, as well as that of Jhonatan "⁠JOTA⁠" Willian and of head coach João "⁠horvy⁠" Horvath.

Now, the remaining core trio have been moved to the bench, of Gabriel "⁠NEKIZ⁠" Schenato, Henrique "⁠HEN1⁠" Teles, and Luca "⁠Luken⁠" Nadotti.

GG's statement pointedly refers to "recent changes to the Major ecosystem and revenue structure" as one of the reasons for the drastic decision.

The quintet had a tough time at the Cologne Major, qualifying as the oldest team at the event but bowing out with a 0-3 record and with a round win rate of just 23.5%.

In Valve's new revenue model, the team that places last, as GG did, receives just 0.72% of the Major's sticker money, while the overall pot of cash to be handed out is said to be far smaller than previous tournaments.

GG say they are "committed to building a competitive and sustainable future," with plans for "next steps" in their Counter-Strike 2 project.
