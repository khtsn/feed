CCT confirm investigation into former Oramond squad

HLTV contacted CCT to confirm the investigation was underway.

The former Oramond squad, now DragonClaw, are currently competing in CCT 2026 Europe Series 3

CCT has confirmed they are investigating the former Oramond squad, now playing as DragonClaw, after an enquiry made by HLTV.

The news comes after CYBERSHOKE's Operations Manager, Emil "VigoV" Khakimov, posted to X stating that CYBERSHOKE's captain, Daniil "⁠alpha⁠" Demin, had conducted an investigation of his own into the former Oramond team.

The X posts included screenshots of discussions of match-fixing and the delivery of direct memory access (DMA) cheats.

alpha later made several posts of his own on X where he explained his investigation in further detail. alpha said he was contacted by someone, whom he labels Person X, who claims they sold radar cheats to current DragonClaw player Miłosz "⁠AntyVirus⁠" Konieczka and provided screenshots of match-fixing discussions.

Also included in alpha's posts were clips of voice chats reportedly containing AntyVirus asking how to use these DMA cheats. alpha claims to have verified it is AntyVirus' voice through several sources, including other players Martin "⁠zur1s⁠" Sláma and Paweł "⁠innocent⁠" Mocek.

CYBERSHOKE's Operations Manager later posted saying that CCT and Thunderpick had committed to investigating the matter, which HLTV were able to confirm with CCT.

AntyVirus, one of the players under suspicion, posted to X in the wake of the accusations calling them "false" and saying "I can cooperate with ESIC."

Members of the DragonClaw roster have found themselves at the centre of controversy in the past. In 2025 ENCE issued a statement raising competitive integrity concerns prior to their YGames Pro Series Season 5 final against M1, with the M1 roster featuring several members of DragonClaw.
