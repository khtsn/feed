magic qualify for PGL Astana as K27's Major bid takes big hit

magic have won the PGL Astana Europe closed qualifier to secure a spot at the Kazakhstani event in May, doing so by besting K27 in the best-of-five grand final.

K27, who are currently emroiled in a last-second dash to the Cologne Major, took a 2-0 lead in the final and even found themselves 11-8 up on the third map but lost in overtime, before faltering on both Overpass, where they had a 10-5 lead, and Anubis to suffer a costly loss in their hunt for VRS points.

PGL Astana will be magic's first tier-one event since their inception at the start of the year.

K27's Major hopes now rest on MPKBK CIS LAN

After an action-packed month, during which K27 played 80 maps across several online and LAN competitions, Aleksey "⁠xeedo⁠" Mikulich and company have just suffered a very costly loss to magic.

The defeat cost them three places in the VRS, meaning they have slipped further from the Major invite cut-off, but their hopes aren't completely dashed.

The majority-Russian squad is competing at the MPKBK CIS LAN, where they have already progressed through to the playoffs with a 2-0 win over 1win, and now requires a deep run in Moscow alongside some favorable results elsewhere to make it to Cologne.
