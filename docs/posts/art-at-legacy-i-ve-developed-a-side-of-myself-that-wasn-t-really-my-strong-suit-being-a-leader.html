arT: "At Legacy I've developed a side of myself that wasn't really my strong suit: being a leader"

Legacy will have the chance to defend their 2025 CAC title against Falcons after beating MIBR 2-0 on Saturday in the semifinals. Andrei "⁠arT⁠" Piovezan's troops came back from 9-3 down on Mirage to win the map in overtime before cruising through Inferno.

The grand final will be a rematch of Friday's Group A upper final, in which Falcons comfortably ran out 2-0 winners. arT said that he expects his players to step up in the final, even though his team is regarded as the underdog.

"I think that if they [Falcons] feel the pressure, it will work in our favor," he told HLTV after the match. "But of course, they’re experienced players—they know they’re the favorites. Maybe they’ll use that to their advantage by playing aggressively in ways we don’t expect, playing more loosely.

"But we’ll see. I think it all depends on how we start the game, too. If we start really well, we might be able to put them on the back foot."

arT also gave his thoughts on how he has evolved as an IGL since his FURIA says and admitted that playing for Legacy has been a rewarding experience.

"Here I can help the kids; I can, you know, make the things they want to happen come true, right?" he said. "The things they’re aiming for in their careers.

"I feel really close to them, and that gives me a sense of comfort, it makes me work with a smile, I get to play the CS I need to play, I do what I have to do. So I think it’s been a really different and very good experience."

Congratulations, arT. The second CAC final for the team, the first one for you. How does it feel?

I'm really happy. I think we worked really hard to get here. We had some pretty poor results leading up to Atlanta and here, so I think it’s a big relief that our hard work is finally paying off.

Bouncing back from a 9-3 deficit on your map pick is never easy, especially with insani playing that well. What did you tell your team at half-time?

I think when freezetime started, we said, 'Let's just win the pistol round, just the pistol round, and then we'll see what happens.' So I think it was just taking it one step at a time; we'd already done something like that in this tournament, so we knew it was possible, and it all started with winning the pistol round.

I think that was the deciding factor; whether we’d have a chance on the map or not. We won the pistol round, and then we started to believe.

I think we have a chance to pull off an upset against those guys

Next up is a final against Falcons, the big boss and the main favorite. You've played them in this tournament and you lost to them 2-0. What expectations do you have for a BO5 final?

I think in the first game we were really feeling the pressure. I think the guys will really step up in the final. They've already had the experience of being up here on stage playing in the playoffs, and they've won this event, so I think they'll be better prepared for the final and more composed. I think we have a chance to pull off an upset against those guys.

Yesterday, latto told me about how your team had had slow starts against Falcons and The MongolZ. Today, it happened again. What does the team need to do to avoid that happening against Falcons tomorrow?

I think we’re too worried about the big picture of the map, you know, not focused on the screen. Even going into Inferno, we were just saying, 'Let's just play what's right in front of us first, then we’ll think about what happens next.' I think it will be a good mindset for tomorrow too. To go in and play round by round, focusing only on what’s in front of us and playing good CS.

There's an important factor at play here: the pressure on Falcons to lift a trophy here. Do you think you can use that to your advantage?

Oh for sure. I think that if they feel the pressure, it will work in our favor. But of course they’re experienced players, and they feel they’re the favorites. Maybe they’ll use that to their advantage by playing aggressively where we don’t expect, by playing more loosely.

But we’ll see. I think it all depends on how we start the game, too. If we start really well, we might be able to put them on the back foot.

FURIA have been fighting for trophies. Legacy are now doing the same. MIBR are playing at a good level. Do you think this can be a good year for Brazilian CS?

For sure. paiN are also making deep runs in tournaments. Now we've made the playoffs in back-to-back tournaments. FURIA have always been FURIA. They still are the best team in Brazil. We've always sort of had one [really good] team; perhaps in the future we'll be able to have more than one.

And now a more personal question: the last few years have been rough for you. You were removed from FURIA, then your Fluxo stint didn't go so well. Perhaps people doubted that you could play at the highest level again. How do you look at your resurgence with Legacy?

It's been an amazing experience. I think at Legacy I’ve managed to develop a side of myself that wasn’t really my strong suit: being a leader. So here I can help the kids; I can, you know, make the things they want to happen come true, right? The things they’re aiming for in their careers. I feel really close to them and that gives me a sense of comfort, it makes me work with a smile, I get to play the CS I need to play, I do what I have to do. So I think it’s been a really different and very good experience.

And why do you think you weren't a true leader back on FURIA? Was it because you were in a different stage in your career and life?

There are lessons that you learn. I think maturity comes with time, too, so these are things you go through and learn along the way. So over time, everyone evolves as a player and as a person. So I think I’m in a much better place today than I was years ago. So it’s a process of evolution, right? Just like it happens inside the server, it does outside of it, too.
