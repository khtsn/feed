The two-map series was played out on Icebox and Abyss. Icebox was Leviátan's map pick, while Abyss was G2's pick.

Leviátan won Icebox 16-14, and then ran away with Abyss 13-5.

Icebox is one of Leviátan's best maps this year. Entering Thursday, they were 12-3 on the map, but two of those three losses came against G2.

G2 came out with the zero-duelist team comp that they've often played on Icebox this year, which puts icy on Gekko and leaf on Killjoy.

The map was razor-thin, with very little separating the two teams. G2 claimed four defensive rounds after their opponents came out with a hot 7-1 start to the half, bringing the two teams to 12-12.

The last time these two teams went into overtime on Icebox, G2 prevailed 14-12.

The trend of this match being attack-sided continued into overtime. In round 29, with the teams knotted at 14 apiece, Leviátan clinched a close round, giving them an upper hand to clinch the map 16-14.

After Leviátan won their opening pistol round on the attacking half, all five of G2's players ran off of the map. The puzzling decision didn't pay off, as Leviátan converted their following bonus round to go up 11-4.

From that point forward, Leviátan was cruising. Despite duelist aspas only finding one first blood across both maps, his supporting cast shone through, guiding the team to a 13-5 victory and a 2-0 series sweep.

G2 head coach JoshRT chalked his team's performance on Abyss to being a byproduct of the Icebox result.

“We lost a close one. I could tell the nerves were a little high. Going into the next game, we tried to mentally reset, and you just forget a few things,” he said.

No members of G2 finished Abyss with more kills than deaths.

“[Leviátan] is such a good team that they'll capitalize and they'll punish you for making those mistakes,” he went on to say.
