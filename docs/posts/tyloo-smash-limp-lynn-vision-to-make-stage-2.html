TYLOO smash limp Lynn Vision to make Stage 2

TYLOO's Major rookie, Zero, was utterly sublime across both maps

TYLOO have won the Chinese derby over Lynn Vision to progress at IEM Cologne Major, sending their countrymen home with dominant victories on Ancient and Inferno.

The trajectory of these two teams carried over into this series, TYLOO recovering admirably after an 0-2 start to the group, whilst Lynn Vision squandered a 2-1 start and were smashed by MIBR in their previous outing.

Thus TYLOO continue their Major journey, powered by the rifle duo of Yi "⁠JamYoung⁠" Yang and Su "⁠Zero⁠" Jingshen, whilst Lynn Vision exit with a whimper off the back of two truly poor performances.

JamYoung 4K with 1v2 clutch

Ancient saw Lynn Vision open proceedings with a ponderous, toothless offensive half. They had few ideas in their default and often found themselves simply waiting around for something to happen, or running through smokes with little oomph behind it.

TYLOO duly feasted, JamYoung and Zero thriving in the face of a weak attack, and they comfortably converted when the sides switched off the back of a pistol win and conversion.

Zero aces Lynn Vision to send the home from Cologne

Lynn Vision were no better in the first half of Inferno, arguably getting worse as they seemed to shrink under the pressure with no one willing to step up and make a play, whilst TYLOO's rifle duo of JamYoung and Zero continued to flourish.

TYLOO raced out to 12 rounds after a strong offense was followed up by a pistol win and conversion on their CT side, and whilst Lynn Vision offered a little bit of resistance to make the score look a little better, Zero made yet another big play to finish the game, acing Lynn Vision on a half-buy to seal the series.
