Starting on Pearl, both teams had slight variations of a similar composition, with Trace opting for a double duelist using Neon and Yoru while DRG ran the double initiator with Fade and KAY/O. DRG's defense side struggled against the explosiveness of FengF on Yoru and Kai on Neon, and some smart thinking from Trace snuck a pistol win out of a tough situation.

Trace sneaks a post-plant defuse with some good teamwork.

Despite losing that pistol round, DRG fought back thanks to big plays from both TvirusLuke and Nicc before Trace secured the map 13-8 thanks to an early collateral from FengF with the Operator.

The teams then went into Abyss with a similar situation to the previous map, as both teams ran similar teams with one key difference. Trace opted for the more defensive composition using Cypher this time, while DRG ran the more traditional double smokes composition. Biank started hot on the attack half, as his Guardian skills helped convert a bonus round. The half was all Trace, as LuoK1ng and Kai took sites with confidence to lead 9-3.

DRG won the pistol round to start a potential comeback, and Nicc cleaned up on DRG's anti-eco. Yet, LuoK1ng was in his element on the retake, hitting some clean shots to not only stop his opponent's momentum but to secure the map 13-7 and a series sweep.

With this win, Trace secured a spot in the upper semis, facing off against the winner of the next game of the day for a chance at qualifying for Masters Bangkok. S1mon showed off the fragging potential of Tejo, as EDG entered the half up 7-5.

Just like the previous half, EDG won pistol and struggled on their anti-eco, but managed to secure the round on the second attempt. ZmjjKK was getting a lot of value off his teammate's utility, especially the near impeccable Tejo play from S1mon, as the lead widened. Fracture was next, with both teams running Neon and Breach, but Nova decided to add Raze and Cypher while EDG selected Fade and Killjoy. Switching from Tejo to Breach, S1mon continued his strong form to help EDG win pistol and not only get EDG's first clean anti-eco win of the day but their first bonus round win. Up 6-0, EDG started to wane as Nova fought back, notably off the Operator play of CB . Just as quickly as it looked like Nova could end the half even, EDG caught fire and was up 9-3.

CHICHOO had a hero round to win EDG's fourth straight pistol round, and that extended the lead to 11-3. It looked to be in the bag for EDG, but Nova won seven rounds in a row thanks to a great run from SWERL on Raze. Just about to lose their lead, S1mon got three from a desperate position on site to get EDG up to 12, then Smoggy had a highlight reel ace in a similar situation. EDG wiped the sweat off their brow as they won the map 13-10 and swept the series to reach the upper semis of Kickoff.
