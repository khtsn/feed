Jimpphat: "If we're not able to [lift trophies] with this roster, there will be some changes at some moment"

MOUZ's losing streak against Spirit was extended to six in a row after a dismal showing in the quarter-finals of IEM Rio, marking another early exit for the German organization amidst a downturn in form.

"I don't think we could fully reset [after losing Dust2]," Jimi "⁠Jimpphat⁠" Salo told HLTV after the match. "And also in the TeamSpeak, we were more quiet and more mad, like more annoyed at the mistakes because it shouldn't happen, but it just kept happening and there was no way to stop it."

Although they have been bereft of important titles, MOUZ were a consistent presence deep into tournaments in 2025, regularly reaching at least the semi-finals before bowing out. Their form of late has not been the same, and has given rise to questions around whether changes are on the horizon.

"Now it has been a little bit silent, to be honest, and we just need to figure out what the reason is as a team," Jimpphat said. "And if we cannot figure it out, then there might be some changes, you know? But it's hard to say now."

Listen to the interview here.

Jimi, commiserations, you're out of Rio. A rough way to go out, so just give me your reaction after that game.

It's really disappointing that we so often go out like this. We go to the playoffs, and then we get completely destroyed and I feel like nothing is connecting. It just really sucks. Not sure how to put it in words, to be honest.

Spirit has been a hard matchup for you; the last time you beat them was almost a year ago at the Major, and you've lost five times in a row to them since then. Why is it so difficult against them for you?

It's a good question. When we look at the matchups, they have our number in a way. I'm not sure exactly what the reason is, but I think they play a little bit of a similar style to us. But of course, they have donk, they have sh1ro, they have really good individuals, and if we are just not sharp, and not ready in the server, they are players who instantly punish you.

Because a player like donk, he never has an off-game, so you really need to play good to beat them. I feel like that has been our problem, that we have not been our sharpest in the moment, and then we just get punished by our mistakes.

I don't think we could fully reset, and also in the TeamSpeak we were more quiet and more mad, like more annoyed at the mistakes because it shouldn't happen

You said before the game that you had 8/10 confidence, but at least on the server, it didn't look like that. What was happening with that?

I'm not sure. Going into the game — at least for me, but I also felt it in the others — I was confident. We felt confident, and then into the game, I think of course we didn't have the best start on Dust2. It affects a lot. We had a lot of good rounds, we had a lot of good situations, but we just couldn't convert them. We somehow overpeeked, or we just did something really stupid, I would say.

Then after all of these situations, it just struck our confidence, and it showed in the next map. I don't think we could fully reset, and also in the TeamSpeak we were more quiet and more mad, like more annoyed at the mistakes because it shouldn't happen, but it just kept happening and there was no way to stop it.

You were booed when you walked out. I mean, I know you're used to it at this point, but it looked like it did hurt. I could see it on your faces, it was like, 'Ah, this sucks that this is always happening,' you know?

Yeah. I mean, I think booing is a part that we've dealt with for a really long time. We know how it is, and to be honest, I'm not sure if it's sad or if it's just like, you don't even care at this point (laughs). But of course, you wish that it would be different, but it doesn't change unless we win the trophies, unless we gain a bigger fanbase, and so far, we haven't been able to do that.

We haven't been able to lift the trophies because I also know the fans want us to lift the trophies, and we haven't managed to do it. So it just sucks. It just sucks, and yeah, there is no other way to change the fans' perspective about us unless we win, and we haven't done it. So we need to be better.

I don't think there will be any changes before the Major

Talking about chasing those trophies and winning: It's just something that doesn't seem to be coming to MOUZ right now. Every time that happens, especially for this kind of stretch, people start wondering if you'll make changes. Obviously there was the Magisk rumor that torzsi said was rubbish, but in terms of that, is that something that you're looking at now? Because the situation looks a bit dire.

It's hard to say for now, of course. I don't know what the future is holding, and there are still three more tournaments [until the break]. I don't think there will be any changes before the Major. I'm not sure, but of course, after all, we want to lift the trophies, we want to win, so if we are not able to do it with this roster, there will be some changes at some moment. If it's after summer or before, but I think not before.

And for us, we are a tier-one team, we want to lift the trophies and beat other tier-one teams, but lately I think our performance has not been the best. Not the MOUZ we are used to, like usually we go at least to the semis, at least to the finals even, and... yeah. Now it has been a little bit silent, to be honest, and we just need to figure out what the reason is as a team, and if we cannot figure it out, then there might be some changes, you know? But it's hard to say now.
