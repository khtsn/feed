Vitality bounce back from loss with NAVI drubbing to reach Rio semis

Vitality breezed past NAVI into the semi-finals

Vitality have locked up a spot in the semi-final stage of IEM Rio 2026 with a quick 2-0 victory against Natus Vincere, extending their win streak against Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen's charges to nine series.

With this victory, Vitality reach their their 20th consecutive semi-final at a Big Event, surpassing the record set by Astralis in 2018-19.

Heading into the series, the question many were asking was how Vitality would respond to Wednesday's close loss to Falcons, which had snapped their 18-match win streak in all competitions. Would the cracks begin to show, or would they come out swinging, intent on proving it was nothing more than a blip?

Only a few rounds into Mirage, it became clear that Vitality looked as strong and dangerous as ever. Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut led the charge on that map with a sensational 2.53 rating — his highest of the year so far —, while Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire topped the scoreboard on Dust2, which was another one-way street.

The victory puts Vitality on a collision course with hometown heroes FURIA in the semi-finals.

"We wanted to prove that we didn't play our best against Falcons," ZywOo said after the match. And we want to enjoy the stage, so it was great to show a great game. We did great today.

"It will be great to play FURIA in front of this amazing crowd. I'm looking forward to playing against FURIA. It's going to be amazing."

The loss was another painful reminder of the gulf between the two teams — something Aleksib had alluded to in the wake of the 3-0 loss in Rotterdam. But back then, NAVI had managed to be competitive during large spells of the best-of-five final. Today, they simply struggled in every department.

"They're showing why they're probably the best team we've ever seen in the history of CS," Aleksib said in a post-match interview. "It's not easy, you go into your map pick, and ZywOo has 30 kills and five deaths. It felt like they were playing with us and it was not very serious.

"We started both games slowly. We won the CT pistol on Dust2. Maybe if we had won the first gun round, we would have made the map competitive, but it didn't feel like that today. The maps didn't matter. They showed up as better individuals and better as team. We played a worse game than in Rotterdam, and even that was 3-0."

ZywOo opened Mirage with a trio of frags while holding the B site on his own, setting Vitality up for a flying start to the game. It took Natus Vincere seven rounds to get on the board, but they were never truly able to impose their will on the game as the half ended with an 8-4 scoreline, with ZywOo leading the charge with 20 frags to his name.

The French AWPer showed no signs of slowing down as he added four more kills to his tally in the ensuing pistol, much to NAVI's chagrin. With a 1v1 in the conversion and multi-kills in the following two rounds, he put Vitality on map point before apEX finished the game off with a crisp 4K.

More misery was to come for NAVI, who found themselves down 7-1 on the T side of Mirage after another disappointing start. They eventually managed to reduce the deficit down to two rounds after a spell of success, but then Vitality went on a 5-0 run to put the series to rest.
