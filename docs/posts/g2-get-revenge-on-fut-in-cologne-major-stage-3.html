G2 get revenge on FUT in Cologne Major Stage 3

The more experienced squad got the better of their young opponents after falling to them in Stage 2.

HeavyGod topped the board with a 1.45 rating in the series

G2 and FUT played each other again in the 0-1 pool of the IEM Cologne Major's Stage 3 after the two teams clashed in the 2-0 pool of Stage 2. FUT's youngsters had the edge in that first match, but this time around it was the more experienced G2 players who prevailed in another three-map series.

The veto was similar to the one from their Stage 2 bout, with Overpass and Ancient making it through as they had in the previous round, where G2 took the former and FUT the latter, but now Mirage was thrown in the mix as the decider.

G2's Overpass was slated first, and a triple by Guy "⁠NertZ⁠" Iluz in the first gun round set the Nemanja "⁠huNter-⁠" Kovač-led squad on the right path as they turned an 0-3 deficit on the attack into a very strong 8-4 lead before switching to the defense on the CT-sided map.

G2 lost the second pistol round, but bounced back quickly and after a second-round forcebuy win went on to win out 13-5.

FUT, the less experienced team, didn't let it get to them, and quickly set off to set the record straight on their Ancient pick despite a 3v5 retake by G2 in the first pistol round. After winning the second round, the team of prospects took control on the attack and posted seven at the half.

G2 once won the second pistol round, but once again could not hold on in the anti-forcebuy as FUT, led by a 2.14-rated Džiugas "⁠dziugss⁠" Steponavičius, evened the series out with a commanding 13-6 scoreline.

The same story repeated on Mirage, with G2 winning the first pistols — this time on the T side — before losing the second round. This time, to three Scout headshots by Nikita "⁠cmtry⁠" Samolotov. A Matúš "⁠MATYS⁠" Šimko double with a Tec-9, the following round didn't let the balance swing, however, and G2 got back to winning ways and off to a 7-2 start before FUT hunkered down to win the remaining three before the switch.

G2 tallied a fourth pistol round on the CT side just to, once again, lose the second round, prompting Hugo "⁠Hugo⁠" Byron, who was calling the match, to react by saying, "No matter how you slice it, G2 suck at these second rounds."

FUT brought it back to 8-8 before G2 retook their lead, but after a pause, FUT once again brought it even. A crucial quad-kill by Nikita "⁠HeavyGod⁠" Martynenko at 10-9 in his team's favor finally cracked it open, as G2 went on to win from there, 13-9.
