FUT have enjoyed largely smooth sailing during their campaign at PGL Bucharest, losing a single map to Inner Circle on their way to the semi-finals. Whilst B8 offered resistance in the quarters, FUT stood firm and swept the Ukrainian side to maintain their excellent map record.

FUT survive fumbled 4v2s to reach Bucharest semi-finals over B8

Considering their current stature and form, FUT are among the favorites to take the trophy, but András "⁠coolio⁠" Fercsák is trying to stay grounded. "We were coming here I would say with higher expectations, but at the same time I am trying to be realistic," he told HLTV following his team's win over B8.

"I don't like to set up high expectations because then you expect more, and then if you lose it's not good. We are just an upcoming team."

With a semi-final matchup with PARIVISION or The MongolZ ahead of him, coolio spent some time talking to HLTV about his team's run thus far in Romania, their chances at taking the trophy, and the future of the roster.

You can listen to the full interview here.

Thoughts on that series?

I think on the first map we started really shaky, we had this issue versus Inner Circle also on Dust and we couldn't come back. Luckily here we could on Ancient, but we have some mental lockdown when we switch to the passive style, when we are not trying to get info on the map, and I think when we are facing that situation our CT side can get killed easily. This is something I was trying to tell them in the pause to switch.

On Overpass we had a really good start and the force-buy round they had we kinda threw away, and I think it was a good moment for them to come back in the game. After we trolled a 3v2, a 4v2, it was really hard mentally, everyone was done. We were trying to cheer each other up and luckily we could come back, and in overtime we kind of knew we were going to win the game because we were back in it [mentally].

After we trolled a 3v2, a 4v2, it was really hard mentally, everyone was done. We were trying to cheer each other up and luckily we could come back

I heard in a timeout towards the end of that game you were telling your players they were trying to do too much, too many people wanted to kill and do things on the map. Does that happen a lot with your team?

I would say it depends. So many people say we are aggressive, we are playing the most aggressive CS, which can be possible in a way but I also disagree with many things, that we are like Bad News Eagles and stuff. We are trying to have a structure, we always have a gameplan, we know what we want to abuse and what we want to punish. Of course it can happen, they are really young and we can troll these situations. Also they [B8] were shooting really hard on Overpass, it happened that we were holding angles where we should get the kill, but they got the kill in the end.

How do you feel about the event overall so far? It's been quite a clean run, youve only lost one map.

We were coming here I would say with higher expectations, but at the same time I am trying to be realistic. I don't like to set up high expectations because then you expect more, and then if you lose it's not good. We are just an upcoming team, but overall I think we knew that we are one of the favorites coming into this tournament. We had two weeks of practice which helps a lot, we can improve on our weaker maps.

We had a slow start against a couple of teams, but then I think we played really good. I'm proud of the boys. I think today also we played good, it was just a mental breakdown for a while. I don't want to say we're gonna win the tournament, I don't like that. I think we already achieved what we want, we wanted to go the semis, I think our goal was to beat teams who are lower ranked than us and finally we did it 2-0. We lost versus Inner Circle, but it was still fine.

I think we have our best chance right now to win

Tomorrow we will face either The MongolZ or PARIVISION, they are the favorites and I think for us it's favorable when we are the underdog on paper. We have the chance to win our first tournament, but if we don't win it we are not going to be sad.

You've got a long time to win a tournament in the future.

Yes, but I think we have the best chance right now to win.

You're a team that are on the up, a lot of people are taking notice of you as a team and your players. Looking a bit further to the future, do you think after the Major it will be hard to keep all of your players?

(laughs) Yeah to be honest, we talk about it. I don't think there are many teams in the world like ours, dzuigss was 15 when I started coaching him, we are more than a family. We share thoughts but I don't know, I think it should be a really good offer for someone.

We have the money, and I think we like to play with each other, so it depends

I think a lot of people also underestimate FUT as an organization from the outside. They are new to CS but they are one of the biggest orgs overall. We have the money, and I think we like to play with each other, so it depends. Of course if Vitality comes for my players it's hard to say no, but I think our plan is to stick together.

You can see with experience, every event we are doing better results and I think I can still say that because we made top four. It's a good question but I hope we can stay together. Like I said, if someone gets a really good offer, we know it can happen.

Like you said you are getting better with each event, constantly improving. Do you have any thoughts about the Major yet, how far you would like to go or what you would like to achieve?

As a joke I said on Steam that we should end this tournament in third place, go to Atlanta and end second, and then we can win the Major. But I don't know, I'm really on the ground and I don't want to set up expectation. With this current form we have I think we have a really good chance to make it to the playoffs. I expect us to be in the playoffs.

We have one month to prepare and we will improve. After PGL we have BLAST and IEM Atlanta, and we are going to gain experience which I think will help a lot. I think we have a good chance. It's hard to say we can win because Vitality are on another level, but the playoffs… I would be really happy to play in Cologne in this arena, for us it's a dream.
