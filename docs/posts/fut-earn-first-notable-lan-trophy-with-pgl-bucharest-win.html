FUT earn first notable LAN trophy with PGL Bucharest win

FUT have laid claim to their first notable LAN trophy since entering the Counter-Strike scene nearly one year ago, taking down Astralis in the best-of-five grand final of PGL Bucharest for the honor. FUT bagged lopsided wins on Ancient (13-5) and Mirage (13-5), with Astralis claiming an overtime Nuke win (16-14) before FUT returned to their winning ways on Dust2 (13-3) to lock down the series 3-1.

FUT have been on a steady rise throughout 2026, making their first Big Event playoff in their last outing at ESL Pro League Season 23. There, the young international side, with an average age of just over 19, fought all the way from Stage 1 of the Swedish event to fourth place, faltering to Astralis in the third-place decider.

FUT kept up their momentum at PGL Bucharest 2026, looking to capitalize on the absence of heavyweights Vitality, MOUZ, FURIA, and Falcons. FUT made the most of their Bucharest run, marching the group stage with a clean 3-0 record, taking down PARIVISION along the way, before moving into the grand final with playoff victories over B8 and The MongolZ.

"A couple of weeks ago, I said I think we will make it through, but I did not fully expect to win the grand final," Laurențiu "⁠lauNX⁠" Țârlea said shortly after the win on home soil.

"I'm feeling blessed that I got the chance to join this team, to be honest. They were such a strong squad, and I got the chance to join them, and now we can all prove to the world together how strong we can be. I can't wait to go to the next tournament."

FUT will now have two other big LANs ahead of them prior to their IEM Cologne Major campaign. The young squad has just over two weeks until their next outing at BLAST 2026 Rivals Season 1, followed shortly thereafter by IEM Atlanta, before heading into the organization's Major debut in Stage 2 of the event.

FUT hit the ground running in the grand final, cruising to a 7-1 lead on the CT side of Astralis' first pick of Ancient before finally having their momentum slowed by a 2v4 clutch from the Danish-majority side. A crisp Victor "⁠Staehr⁠" Staehr multikill helped add to Astralis' tally, but they could only get the deficit down to 4-8 at the side swap.

Astralis' comeback efforts were dashed after missing out on Ancient's second pistol round, which quickly thrust FUT back into the driver's seat. Astralis managed to claim a single buy round win, but it was not enough to halt FUT's dominant 13-5 win.

FUT's pick of Mirage was even more one-sided; yet another pistol round victory for Aulon "⁠Krabeni⁠" Fazlija's troops, stellar individual form from all five of the international squad's members, and numerous successful B hits gave way to a whopping 10-2 T-side scoreline for FUT. Astralis managed to snag their first pistol win of the series to kick off the second half, but FUT got right back to winning ways once guns got in their hands, scooping up another 13-5 victory to go 2-0 up in the series.

FUT clinched yet another pistol round to kick off Astralis' second map pick of Nuke, but Astralis were not keen to simply roll over this time around. Astralis managed to throw FUT's CT-side economy into dire straits early on, but Rasmus "⁠HooXi⁠" Nielsen's men routinely faltered to weak buys, leaving them with a disappointing 4-8 half heading into the defense.

Despite the numerous missed opportunities for Astralis in the first half, the team turned a new leaf on their own CT side. FUT snatched the pistol round to take a 10-4 lead, but Astralis put up a stalwart defense, only letting two more rounds, including an anti-eco, slip from their grasp as they forced overtime. An ace from HooXi defending against an A site pop put Astralis in the driver's seat during the extra rounds, allowing them to push ahead to a 16-14 scoreline and force Dust2.

FUT were clearly unfazed by the manner in which they fumbled Nuke, as they quickly roared into the lead on their second pick of Dust2. Dmytro "⁠dem0n⁠" Myroshnychenko in particular stepped up for his team after the Nuke loss, posting a staggering 2.57 rating with a 25-6 K/D and +15.22% Swing, spearheading a straightforward 13-3 win for his team on Dust2. "A bit lucky," dem0n said after the match. "God was on our side today."
