Aleksib on w0nderful: "He's very hard on himself, so I need to remind him constantly that he's world-class"

Natus Vincere are straight through to the semi-finals of BLAST Open Rotterdam with a 2-0 victory against Aurora in a rematch of the ESL Pro League 23 grand final.

Ihor "⁠w0nderful⁠" Zhdanov had been the hero of the title decider with a series-high 1.56 rating in the best-of-five match. The Ukrainian AWPer has been far from that level in this tournament, prompting Aleksi "⁠Aleksib⁠" Virolainen to offer his teammate some words of support.

"He's very hard on himself, so I need to remind him constantly that he's world-class." Aleksib told HLTV after the match. "Everyone forgot he was the savior of the grand final and he was the reason we won the grand final. He was playing like a superstar, and that's something we needed.

"It's something that I like about him: that when trusts himself and understands that we all have his back, he can pull that kind of performance when it actually matters the most. So that's the number one thing."

Natus Vincere travel to Rotterdam riding a wave of momentum after winning six matches in a row and lifting a trophy. The question many are asking is how they will stack up to Vitality, whom they haven't faced since the Budapest Major.

"It's not going to be easy, but I think we have all the keys to challenge them," Aleksib said.

"Since we have a streak now, we also are showing the confidence of coming off of a tournament win. So these are the keys we need in order to challenge them, and I hope that we can reach the grand final and perhaps play them there."

Aleksi, a 2-0 victory against Aurora, and you're straight to the semi-finals in Rotterdam. Give me your thoughts on the series.

I think [it was a] good level from us. I think that on Anubis, we kind of let it go on the T side. I would say we had the right read of what was about to happen, but maybe we were too much ahead of our plan and they ended up winning, I think, two or three rounds, just straight up pushing us, making us uncomfortable. So they adapted faster than us, and we need to be ready for it next time. But once we got into the CT side of both maps, I just think that we were one level ahead. We were reading a lot of the situations correctly and playing well in clutches, X on X scenarios, and overall just playing some high-level CS today.

Did you expect Nuke to be this hard? It was looking a bit scary towards the end, when they got to double digits before you came back.

I think we understood that we will have a good CT side against them. I think we even said after losing the CT pistol that it's only fair that we are not winning all of the pistols in this series. So they won the pistol, the anti-force and the eco round, and after that it's just gun rounds, and we ended up executing our game plan the best we could in order to come back in that game.

We need to just keep the shape up. It's going to be a lot of days until the semifinals, so that's something to think about

And as for the CT side, we were leading 4-1 and we got punished by a lobby push that we honestly kind of expected. We just had too few people holding the push, we should have had more people there and just shut them down. And that kind of got them back into the game and after that they played kind of good, again pushing us constantly in lobby. So definitely something to think on and to analyze. But overall, I'm just super happy that no matter what happened, we still ended up being the better team today.

In the pre-match interview, you had said that Falcons were a team that you really wanted to beat. You went on to prevent Aurora from getting the revenge. How important were these results not just to keep the win streak going but also to keep the momentum going heading into the playoffs?

I think it's important in every aspect. Aurora really wants to have revenge against us. So it's good that we keep showing this level even though we're fueling their energy, so to speak. Also I think they're a world-class Anubis team, so we kind of know that they're going to pick the map. But our preparation ended up being a little bit better, and we're showing good CS on this map also.

And just going into the semifinals directly, I know we need to just keep the shape up. It's going to be a lot of days until the semifinals, so that's something to think about. But probably we're going to travel tomorrow, have a team dinner, and after that it's just going to be straight up CS and analyzing and grinding and just showing the same level in the semifinals, hopefully.

w0nderful had a great performance now on Nuke but overall he's not been putting up the same numbers that he was putting up in EPL, where he had an amazing performance in the final. As the leader, what has it been like for you to try to extract the most out of him and try to find consistency in his game?

I told him in EPL, when he was a bit sad after the semifinals, 'You need to understand that, as long as we are winning, as long as you are doing your job, everybody's happy, everybody has your back.' No one is thinking that he's playing poorly and that's why we might be losing. He's very hard on himself, so I need to remind him constantly that he's world-class.

Everyone forgot he was the savior of the grand final and he was the reason we won the grand final. He was playing like a superstar, and that's something we needed. It's something that I like about him: that when he trusts himself and he understands that we all have his back, he can pull that kind of performance when it actually matters the most. So that's the number one thing.

Given your recent form and the fact that teams like Spirit, Falcons and MOUZ have dropped off a little bit, a lot of people see you as the biggest threat to Vitality right now. Do you see it that way? Are you looking forward to facing them? Because you haven't played them since Budapest.

Honestly, I'm looking forward to playing against all of the teams you mentioned. That was our goal from the start of the season, to make deep runs to battle these teams in the playoffs. I'm not going to write them off and just look at Vitality. I think it's still a challenge for us that we want to, as a team goal and as individual goals, to have for us. I think Vitality is still playing some world-class CS and not dropping any maps even.

So it's not going to be easy, but I think we have all the keys in order for us to challenge them. Since we have a streak now, we also are showing the confidence of coming off of a tournament win. So these are the keys we need in order to challenge them, and I hope that we can reach the grand final and perhaps play them there.
