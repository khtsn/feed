From survival to improvement: illwill seek backing to continue their rise in the VRS

The introduction of the VRS made launching new projects tougher than ever, but several teams have shown that with the right plan and, more importantly, the right mix of players, anything is possible.

Ninjas in Pyjamas reached the Budapest Major after starting from scratch at the start of 2025, while 100 Thieves are already up to No. 34 in the VRS and surprisingly have a — albeit miniscule — chance to make the Cologne Major thanks to expert maneuvering of the VRS by their Head of Operations Graham "⁠messioso⁠" Pitt.

Both of those projects are backed by some of the biggest organizations esports has to offer, however, making their rise almost par for the course given their sizable financial outlay and the plethora of support staff on offer.

100 Thieves enjoyed a rapid rise at the start of 2026

If even the biggest organizations need ample time to rise up the VRS, an unsalaried team of free agents should, in theory, have next to no chance. Luckily, exceptions that upset the apple cart have always been a part of Counter-Strike, and illwill are the latest feel-good story.

The team came together in October of 2025 with a much different concoction of players than they have now, and progressed through some open qualifiers to earn a VRS rank and some invites to lower-ranked online competitions.

"Woro2k messaged me in October and wanted to create something together. Basically, Woro2k and I took two players from fish123, which was a mixed team created by smooya. We basically stole two players from him," Ali "⁠hAdji⁠" Haïnouss tells HLTV, while stating that the team was created as a long-term project from the start.

Cristian "⁠7kick⁠" Dragos Radut, the second original member still on the roster, admits he is still surprised with the success they have had given their situation. "To be honest, if you had told people back then that we would reach this level in the rankings and become Major [invite] contenders, nobody would have believed it."

hAdji started the illwill project with fellow ex-DRILLAS member Woro2k

The team's results at the start of their time together were good, but nothing special. They attended two DraculaNs, placing 7-8th in the first and 5-6th in the second, the latter under the leadership of in-game leader Nemanja "⁠nEMANHA⁠" Đukić, who had just departed Zero Tenacity.

Those results sent illwill up the rankings due to the importance of LAN matches, and 7kick says Valve's new system is an enjoyable one. "Travelling to different LANs to fight for VRS points has been a great experience," he tells HLTV.

"Personally, I prefer LAN events because they feel more competitive and transparent. Online play will always have its doubts, so proving yourself on LAN means more to me."

The team ended 2025 in 87th place in the VRS and were plagued by instability on their roster. What looked to be one of the last changes came at the start of February, when the mix replaced Stanislav "⁠fostar⁠" Ostashevskiy with Paweł "⁠dycha⁠" Dycha despite winning NODWIN Clutch Series 4, a result that propelled them to the outskirts of the top 40 in the VRS.

illwill wasted no time with their new recruit, signing up for two more open LANs to make the most out of their red-hot streak, and dycha said that he felt at home with the team almost immediately.

"I enjoyed every single match, every single map, which for a long time I missed," he says. "For me, it takes time to find myself in the group, but with them it took hours, so I was kind of surprised."

dycha has already played two LANs with illwill

It was with this five-man lineup that the orgless squad really got on everybody's radar, mostly thanks to their two second-place finishes at Copenhagen Gaming Week Showdown and Parken Challenger Championship S1.

In the former, they beat ENCE and ECSTATIC to make the final before suffering a heartbreaking loss against Monte in a 24-round game on the decider. But they were straight back at LAN just a week later.

This time, they breezed through the group stage with a 5-0 record and most notably beat BC.Game and 9INE to show they need to be considered a trophy threat.

Playoffs went well, too, as they eliminated BC.Game, ECSTATIC, and SINNERS in their lower-bracket run to face GamerLegion in the final, but the Janusz "⁠Snax⁠" Pogorzelski-led team proved too much to handle.

Despite that, 7kick named the two LANs as his best memory in illwill so far, stating that he was "proud of the damage they did to strong teams without any real practice together."

Open LANs, such as Parken Championship, allowed illwill to quickly rise up the rankings

After such a successful period, however, illwill were dealt a cruel blow, as IGL nEMANHA departed the team for Wildcard at the start of March.

"At the beginning we thought that he had a really good offer, from a tier-one team or like a team that is above us in the rankings. So, at least me personally, I accepted that," hAdji shares.

"I was like, 'Yeah, it's fine. You will join a better team, you will have a salary and contract, so I'm fine with that.' Then I saw some leaks connecting him to Wildcard and I was like, 'Damn, you're leaving us for Wildcard?'"

That threw the squad into a search for a new IGL, a position that has historically provided problems to teams much further up the food chain.

"I would say we have few options, but nothing is 100% sure yet. We are talking but soon we might start testing people during practice," dycha says about their ongoing search.

While losing their IGL is a big setback, none of the team's members are losing sight of what they have achieved in such a short time, and are rather setting their sights on repeating that in the coming months.

illwill has recently trialed ex-NIP member MisteM

The team has been given a 4.4% chance of making the Cologne Major by Mischief's calculations due to their recent success, meaning expecting them to appear on the biggest of stages is too optimistic this time around, but 7kick says the team is looking far beyond the Cologne Major.

"We're close [to the Cologne Major], but at the same time still far, so realistically it would take something special for us to make it, especially considering everything that has happened to us recently," he admits.

"That said, we're not focused on short-term rewards. Now our main goal is to prepare for the next Major and secure more invites to tier-one events, and I'm confident that if we stay on this path, we will make the next Major."

hAdji also shares the same view. "If we keep grinding and have the right mentality, I think we are in the right position for the next one. But this one, I don't really think about it," he says, while admitting that the team is currently living on borrowed time if they can't find organizational support soon.

"Right now, the main thing for us is to find stability. We have a good rank, but we really need to find an org as soon as possible so people stay together. I will not go into details, but people in this team get offers right and left," he admits.

"For now, they are believing in this project, and they are refusing, but you don't know, maybe in a few weeks people will accept. There is a risk. You need a contract, you need money to live, so you cannot stay like this. Even though the team is good and we have a good ranking, I just hope we are going to find stability as a roster with an org."

dycha says that free agency is limiting the team's potential

dycha ended the interview with a similar sentiment, saying that the safety net of an organization would not only help the team reach a higher level but also switch their mindset from survival to improvement.

"We need to play LANs to keep our VRS high, but we can't travel all the time using our own money. We are also limited with preparation because we don't have a coach or analyst, so we do everything ourselves. But everyone is putting in a lot of work into making things easier. People don't go in lazy mode just because we have top 25 VRS," he shares about the hardest parts of being an unsalaried team.

"The goal is to get an organisation so we can stay together and move forward with practice without thinking 'Who might be leaving today?' We would also like to bootcamp because you can fix issues and mistakes so much easier in real life. We just want to prove that after signing, we will still have results."
