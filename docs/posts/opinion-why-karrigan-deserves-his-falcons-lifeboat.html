Opinion: Why karrigan deserves his Falcons lifeboat

Finn "⁠karrigan⁠" Andersen's finale at FaZe was not the one he dreamed of. Far from it, in fact, given how the team has collapsed under his stewardship since Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken and Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool left on free transfers.

His later lineups lacked the dynamism and controlled chaos karrigan's leadership was known for. Plenty of that is his responsibility. He's also come under fire for his individual form from the likes of Jacob "⁠Pimp⁠" Winneche, while the perception of him as FaZe's de facto general manager increased scrutiny even as the organization was taking greater control behind the scenes.

But the 'form is temporary, class is permanent' cliche is one for a reason, and it is even more true for in-game leaders than it is for regular players. The qualities that made karrigan great are still there, regardless of FaZe's results: His leadership, his mid-rounding, and, yes, his aura.

At 36, twilight is approaching on one of the great Counter-Strike careers. But it is his right to want to go out on his own terms. That is why, when I was a guest on Sam "⁠Tech Girl⁠" Wright's Feed The Trolls, I proposed this very move for the Dane.

Even if I was half-joking at the time — not many players get a move up the ranking after such poor results — I can see exactly why Falcons' management took the gamble, and why karrigan disavowed FaZe so quickly after the Cologne debacle.

karrigan did not get a send-off befitting his contribution to FaZe

Their project is stalling. Maksim "⁠kyousuke⁠" Lukin's best event might still be his debut at Cologne 2025. Nikola "⁠NiKo⁠" Kovač admitted in Rio that the team are "just not good enough in playoffs right now." It is a win-now project that shows no sign of winning.

There is a heavy dose of irony in the fact that Danny "⁠zonic⁠" Sørensen and NiKo called on karrigan, a player both kicked from their teams to try and get over choking problems, to solve Falcons' own malaise in the playoffs. But that was long ago, and the karrigan that couldn't take TSM past semi-finals or FaZe to Major glory in Boston has evolved into one of the most feared arena callers.

The attraction is more than that, however. karrigan is not just here for the Heimlich; his style of leadership and tactics are perfect for this Falcons side. Damjan "⁠kyxsan⁠" Stoilkovski frequently spoke of his frustration when Falcons' players either did not listen to him, or refused to play as a team.

The Macedonian is a young captain who had plenty of success from Apeks and HEROIC, but a team like Falcons was simply above his pay grade. He does not have the cachet to be the most important voice over a NiKo or Ilya "⁠m0NESY⁠" Osipov. That is not a criticism, because very few captains do, but karrigan does.

kyxsan wanted to rein in some of Falcons' chaotic instincts

It was also evident that kyxsan wanted to reduce the chaos that often cluttered Falcons' mid-rounds. He wanted unpredictable individuals to form a predictable unit. The strength of karrigan, most evident in FaZe's 2022 mini-era, was turning chaos into a strength, and he should be able to balance the team's natural instinct and his own ideas more naturally than kyxsan did.

The most recent FaZe team did not feel like a karrigan team. They lacked the clutch factor, were better in practice than they were in officials, and he was seen in vlogs complaining about a lack of ideas from his teammates in the mid-round.

In comparison, Falcons have the potential to be the third great karrigan side. m0NESY is right up there with Nicolai "⁠device⁠" Reedtz, Ladislav "⁠GuardiaN⁠" Kovács, and prime Helvijs "⁠broky⁠" Saukants in terms of AWPers the 36-year-old has previously set up for success. karrigan understood that broky would overpeek sometimes, in the same way m0NESY does, but allowed him to in the knowledge that a free broky would hit higher peaks than a limited one.

The same goes for kyousuke, who should also benefit from karrigan's selflessness as a captain. He does not need frags to feel confident. He'll go in first on T, take the bad spots on CT, and do all he can to make sure kyousuke has advantageous gunfights.

The two Russians need to feel like they are world beaters, and karrigan has the personality to boost that confidence. kyxsan was a competent tactician, but his energy is from a different planet compared to karrigan. The spell karrigan conjures on crowds, pundits, and his fans is just as strong on his teammates.

NiKo is the known quantity, the CT caller, and as good a vice-captain as karrigan could hope for on T side. This is a duo that has worked together before, and healed their old wounds. It can work again.

The biggest challenge might come out of the server, with karrigan and zonic acting as foils in terms of game style ever since they parted ways in 2016. The loose, free-flowing, high-firepower FaZe of karrigan was the spiritual nemesis of the tactical supremacy of Astralis.

But the zonic of today knows that international CS is different. The Vitality that won the Paris Major was firmly Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire's, a team that embraced a looser style and direct calling. m0NESY and kyousuke do not want the structure that device craved in 2016.

And the former mTw legend was all but soft-launching karrigan in his answer about how to beat Vitality: "They win a lot of games by people already being scared and thinking that they can't win the game. If you do that against Vitality, you're dead.

"You have to disrespect them, take chances, take risks, and go for fights. Even though it's ZywOo around the corner, you have to just swing him and get the trades up."

karrigan and zonic's Astralis tenure didn't go as planned

Too often recently, Falcons have looked scared of losing 4v2s. Their attempts to fix overconfidence have resulted in a far worse problem of underconfidence. It should be simple. You have better players. If you're in a 60-40 duel, go win it. End the round early, don't let the pressure build to breaking point and give away peeker's advantage cowering in a corner.

That lax approach will lose bad rounds, but it will also win unwinnable ones. Banning FACEIT is not going to change the fact that these players need to frag to feel alive. karrigan needs to get the most out of the player's unchangeable instincts, and he has the temperament to do so.

karrigan's age also tips the risk-reward in his favor. It is an open secret that NiKo will take over as in-game leader one day, something that damned a young skipper like kyxsan from the start. But where that fact was a shadow over kyxsan, it can free karrigan up.

I've gone this long without mentioning the 'retirement' word, but you cannot analyse this move without it. karrigan is a short-term signing. A honeymoon is the be-all and end-all.

The dream scenario for both sides is to embrace an entertaining, chaotic style, win some trophies, and see karrigan off when NiKo is ready to take over. It's an urgent operation to undo the damage karrigan has done to his legacy during FaZe's freefall, and to break Falcons' glass ceilings in arenas.

The short-term nature of the move is only exacerbated by the PIF's withdrawal of funds for the LIV golf tour. The football club Ah Ahli has also been sold, with esports-washing projects like Falcons (and ESL) watching on nervously. Is signing karrigan an attempt to grab some trophies before the tap turns off?

karrigan the short-term fix, NiKo the long-term solution?

Regardless of the funding behind Falcons, or the organization's future, ruthlessly cutting kyxsan and signing a veteran like karrigan increases the pressure on a team already under heaps of it. But the good news for them is that he is a leader who can handle it.

I have always compared karrigan to football's Carlo Ancelotti. That's not just because of the expressive eyebrows, either. These are leaders who think players first, and system later. It is about platforming and managing superstars. It's the approach that can make a big ego star player do what you want them to do, all while thinking it was their idea.

Regardless of how bad it got in FaZe, that is still true for karrigan. He has won 25 notable trophies with that approach, and a fire clearly burns inside him to increase that tally.

By parting ways, karrigan and NiKo failed at the start-line when it came to knocking Astralis off their perch in 2018, so it is fitting that the next best thing looms large over this fateful re-union.

The dangers of nostalgia are well-known. But be honest: Even if you don't think it's the right move, you want to see it. Vitality have had it too easy. Handing Counter-Strike's greatest showman the tools to change that can only be a good thing for the neutral.

All this is why the 36-year-old has been given one more chance. It will likely be his last, but there is no doubt in my mind that karrigan has earned it. A decades-long career of brilliance cannot end at HLC Belgrade. I'm glad Falcons agree.
