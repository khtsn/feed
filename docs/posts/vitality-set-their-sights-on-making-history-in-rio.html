Vitality set their sights on making history in Rio

After a stunning string of results in 2025, which included seven consecutive LAN trophies, back-to-back Major wins, and an ESL Grand Slam, Vitality are hungry for more.

Almost one full year after their first Grand Slam victory, Vitality have the opportunity to secure their next big achievement while breaking further records. With wins at IEM Dallas 2025, ESL Pro League Season 22, and IEM Krakow 2026, Vitality are now just a single ESL event victory away from laying claim to the organization's second Grand Slam, a feat accomplished by no other team since the honor's inception in 2017. Vitality would also have the pleasure of hoisting back-to-back ESL Grand Slams, clinching their first one in 2025 after wins at IEM Cologne 2024, IEM Katowice 2025, ESL Pro League Season 21, and IEM Melbourne 2025.

A win in Rio would also shatter individual records, as Robin "⁠ropz⁠" Kool would become the only player to have three Grand Slams to his name, having earned one with FaZe in 2023 in addition to his previous one with Vitality. Meanwhile, teammates Mathieu "⁠ZywOo⁠" Herbaut, Dan "⁠apEX⁠" Madesclaire, William "⁠mezii⁠" Merriman, and Shahar "⁠flameZ⁠" Shushan would join an exclusive club alongside Russel "⁠Twistzz⁠" Van Dulken as being the only players with two Grand Slam victories.

Vitality clinched their first Grand Slam at IEM Melbourne almost exactly a year ago

With ample time until the start of the IEM Cologne Major, Vitality are fully focused on adding more gold bars to their trophy cabinets as quickly as possible. "This is not the key tournament for us," apEX said of Vitality's last outing at BLAST Open Rotterdam. "We want to win the Grand Slam. Doing it in Rio would be the perfect way. For us, [Rotterdam] is training for Rio, seeing what's wrong."

If Vitality were worried about holes in their gameplay, they did not seem to hinder the team in Rotterdam. The international behemoths dominated their sole event appearance of March, going 11-0 in maps, only allowing their opponents to reach double-digit rounds in just four of those maps as they laid claim to their third trophy in a row. Now, Vitality have won a whopping 22 maps at Big Events in a row, eclipsed only by Ninjas in Pyjamas's 34 map streak.

"It feels unbelievable," ropz said of his team's dominant Rotterdam campaign. "We didn't know that we were going to start a map streak, we just know that we want to win tournaments, you know? But if this is the fashion in which we do it, I'm happy."

But still, ropz made clear where the team's sights are set. "We just try to manage [our schedule] so that we can peak again for Rio because that's the goal, to win a Grand Slam before the Major. We have two chances in Rio and Atlanta, but that's the big goal for now."

Since their Rotterdam campaign, Vitality have given themselves two full weeks away from action, opting to skip PGL Bucharest and prioritize their Grand Slam efforts. Even if Vitality come away from Rio without a trophy and Grand Slam in hand, the team will still have another pre-Major opportunity just weeks later at IEM Atlanta. But for apEX, the goal is to "finish the Grand Slam ASAP."

apEX doesn't want to waste any time in pursuit of the Grand Slam

And why shouldn't that be the case? Vitality have looked almost completely unstoppable in 2026, with a semi-final finish at BLAST Bounty 2026 Season 1 Finals being their sole blemish of the year. Outside of that, Vitality are well on pace to match, or possibly even outdo, their jaw-dropping 2025 results.

"We have a really unique team, a really good team, and we don't want to waste any time," ropz said after reaching the playoffs of IEM Krakow. "We've said it many, many times that we want to make the most out of it.

"At some point, when the team will come to an end in a year or two, whenever, we want to look back and have no regrets and make sure we made the most out of it. Because I think we are in a very, very great spot to win everything that's on the plate."

Vitality are keen to end any debates about who the greatest team in Counter-Strike history truly is, and IEM Rio could be one of the difference makers in that endeavor. A dominant run in Rio, aside from giving Vitality their record-breaking second Grand Slam, would also further cement their favorite status for the IEM Cologne Major, which, if they won, would give them their fourth Major title, and their third in a row, tying themselves with fellow greatest team of all time contender Astralis. If Rio can be won without dropping a map, Vitality would also push their map win streak to 33, putting them just a series away from overcoming Ninjas in Pyjamas's Big Event map win streak record of 34.

"We want to create something that has never been done before," apEX shared in Rotterdam. "We want to be better than Astralis. We’ve said it a couple of times, so here we go."

Vitality's IEM Rio campaign will begin in short order, as the Europeans prepare to face off against local side RED Canids in the first match of what could be one of the landmark events for this lineup.
