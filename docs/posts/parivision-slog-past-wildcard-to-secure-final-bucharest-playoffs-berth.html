PARIVISION slog past Wildcard to secure final Bucharest playoffs berth

PARIVISION have locked in the last playoffs spot at PGL Bucharest after a hard-fought 2-0 victory over Wildcard in the final 2-2 group stage matchup.

The BLAST Bounty champions were on their last life in Romania after squandering two chances to reach the playoffs against FUT and 3DMAX, and lost "a lot of rounds like noobs," according to Dastan "⁠dastan⁠" Akbayev, in their last attempt against Wildcard.

The North American team was playing its third straight event since bringing in Nemanja "⁠nEMANHA⁠" Đukić as in-game leader and benefited from a forfeit victory over FaZe to reach the 2-2 pool, but walked away with only a solitary victory over FOKUS after finding themselves unable to convert against PARIVISION when it mattered most.

There is a silver lining in the tenacity Wildcard showed to recover from a 5-11 deficit on Mirage to force overtime, and a brutal back-and-forth, plagued by mistakes from both sides, saw the teams trade blows all the way until PARIVISION emerged the victors, 28-26 after a pair of unlikely double AWP retakes.

The 54-round map is the third-longest on LAN and the longest Mirage since the move to CS2, and the narrow back-and-forth carried on to Dust2. This time, it was Wildcard who dropped a large lead, going up 8-1 after a strong CT side but withering away against Ivan "⁠zweih⁠" Gogin and Dzhami "⁠Jame⁠" Ali after the side swap as PARIVISION narrowly emerged the victors, 13-11.
