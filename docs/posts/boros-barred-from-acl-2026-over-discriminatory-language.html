BOROS barred from ACL 2026 over discriminatory language

Hero Esports has announced that Alter Ego star Mohammad "⁠BOROS⁠" Malhas will not be allowed to participate in the 2026 edition of the Asian Champions League over the use of "racially discriminatory language during the preparation time of 2026 ACL x CS2."

A clip circulating on X appears to show BOROS using derogatory language towards Chinese teammates, though it is unclear whether this is the incident that led to his suspension.

"We do not, and will not, tolerate hate speech, racism, or any form of discrimination. We are committed to fostering a respectful and inclusive environment for all participants and fans," wrote the tournament organizer, which also issued Alter Ego a formal warning for "failure in player supervision."

Neither Alter Ego nor BOROS has publicly commented on the matter.

The Asian Champions League is one of the biggest LAN events of the year in China, with $150,000 on offer. Alter Ego are competing in the closed qualifier for the event with Tomasz "⁠tomiko⁠" Uroda as a stand-in.

TYLOO, Lynn Vision, FlyQuest and SemperFi are among the teams that have been invited to the main event, which will be held in Shanghai later this month.
