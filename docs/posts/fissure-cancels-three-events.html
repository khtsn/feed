FISSURE cancels three events

FISSURE has announced the cancellation of three of its tournaments, which had been planned to take place this April in Shenzhen, China, and in July 2026 and July 2027 at undisclosed locations.

The organizers cited a "logistical conflict" as the reason for the Chinese tournament's cancellation. It was planned to conclude on the day before the kick-off of IEM Rio, making it impossible for any teams who had agreed to play there to attend the FISSURE competition.

As for the July events, FISSURE further said: "Our experience hosting FISSURE Playground 1 showed that tournaments held during the players break do not deliver the level of competition and spectacle that we are committed to providing our audience."

The aforementioned FISSURE Playground 1 was hosted in mid-July 2025 and only attracted one top-ten team in FURIA.

The organizers vowed that the remaining eight events they had previously announced to host over the next three years will go on as planned, despite reports of the company's financial issues.

That includes a $1 million September 2026 event also slated to take place in Shenzhen, China. That may run into similar concerns to do with the level of attendance, as it kicks off on the day following the conclusion of BLAST Open Season 2, a European tournament.
