YEKINDAR: "Our season has been trash and we are focusing more on regaining our identity [than results]"

FURIA arrived in Cologne far from the status of favorites they held at the Budapest Major, reaching the finals only at IEM Krakow, missing playoffs at two BLAST events, and fumbling in Stage 2 of ESL Pro League Season 23.

"Our mindset is that we're not thinking about the placement at the Major, or at any tournament right now, Mareks "⁠YEKINDAR⁠" Gaļinskis told HLTV on the media day ahead of the Cologne Major playoffs. "Our season has been trash, that's just how it is, and we are focusing more on regaining our identity as a team. Right now, we know what it takes from us to have a good game, and mainly that is comms and just making plans with each other and whatever."

The Latvian also spoke about FURIA going 3-0 through Stage 3 for their third straight Major and how they're changing their approach this time around.

"Two of those Majors we lost in the quarter-finals, so we kinda changed our approach a little bit of these couple of days before the games," he said. "Now, we're practicing every day; we only had one free day, which was right after the game. Now we're going to be practicing and playing as much as we can, getting ready for 9z."

Here on the media day ahead of the playoffs. FURIA went through 3-0. and you've had a good streak going through 3-0 at the Majors as well. When you're heading into the playoffs like that, being so dominant, how are you looking at your side of the bracket and how you face up?

I feel like not only the Major, but also after we lost BLAST London... I don't remember who we played, I think we played G2, and then they went to the finals and everything. But we kinda came to the conclusion that the group stage is the group stage, and the playoffs you need to work even harder, prepare even harder, and be ready.

But also, this is our third Major going 3-0, and two of those Majors we lost in the quarter-finals, so we kinda changed our approach a little bit of these couple of days before the games. Now, we're practicing every day; we only had one free day, which was right after the game. Now we're going to be practicing and playing as much as we can, getting ready for 9z.

I feel like even though this side of the bracket is obviously easier, Major magic happens for a reason and you can't underestimate... everybody who is in the playoffs deserves to be there, and you cannot underestimate anybody.

They're a great team, and you can definitely see fire in their eyes

Some people might be surprised to hear that some teams might not practice before going into the arena, so tell me more about that and why you changed that.

It depends on the previous schedule. For example, at the Budapest Major, we had everybody going through the flu during the tournament, and we played many tournaments before the Budapest Major as well. So if you're going to be practicing more during those days, you're going to run out of gas. That's just how it is, and you don't want that to happen.

This time, we feel that we can play more, we don't want to get a little bit rusty, and we want to be warmer for the playoffs. And before this, we had free time at home, so we are kind of refreshed. We've only spent three official matches here, so it's only the start of the tournament for us and we want to play more.

Do you think having only three officials played here leaves you a little vulnerable, in a sense, for your form? Teams like Spirit played in Stage 2, had a lot more time to warm up, so how do you feel about your overall form not having played that many officials here yet?

If you had asked me before Stage 3, I would probably say yeah, but the thing is that even though we came in with no Stage 2, we played against B8, who came from Stage 1, then we played against MOUZ, and then BetBoom, who also qualified from Stage 1, right? So those results showcased that we are warm enough, and it is more about the mindset of everybody in the team, of how we come up for the game.

But of course, we don't want to... mindset is a lot, but of course, half of it is how you aim and how you move and everything. Practice is something where we're just going to keep up the form a little bit, but focus on the mindset.

And how is the mindset in the team? We've talked about this before, it's something you're focusing on, sidde and FalleN said they're trying to clear up some of the frustrations, so what is the mindset like?

Our mindset is that we're not thinking about the placement at the Major, or at any tournament right now. Our season has been trash, that's just how it is, and we are focusing more on regaining our identity as a team. Right now, we know what it takes from us to have a good game, and mainly that is comms and just making plans with each other and whatever.

We are very fluid in our system, and there is a lot of trust between the players about making their decisions on their own, communicating these decisions, making plans with your teammates and everything. This is something that we are going to be focusing on in terms of mindset, like coming into the game not sleepy, or just more about coming into the game ready. Ready to talk, ready to play, ready to make decisions, ready to make moves and risks.

Because even though we are favorites in the quarter-final probably, if we come in thinking that we are favorites and we play at 50%, and 9z are going to be underdogs and play at 80%, we're going to lose. So it's important for us to find this 100%, or if it's impossible to find this 100%, then like 80% or 90%, to play the best we can.

It's not necessarily about who's the favorite or the underdog; we know we can beat anybody, but we know that we can lose to anybody as well

Speaking of being favorites, that kind of pressure does get to teams sometimes, and that kinda felt like the story of Budapest; you were one of the favorites for the title and lost to NAVI in the quarters. This time around you weren't one of the favorites, even though you went 3-0, so how are you feeling actually going into the playoffs being one of the underdogs overall, as a team that wasn't a favorite until now maybe with the way the bracket shaped out?

The thing is, we're always close. Maybe we're not title contenders now, but we were always having close games against Vitality, Falcons, or whatever team you consider title contenders. It's not necessarily about who's the favorite or the underdog or whatever; we don't really care about that much. It's more about we know we can beat anybody, but we know that we can lose to anybody as well.

So it's a matter of finding the balance of confidence, but at the same time, not ego-peeking and losing rounds because of it and whatever. So it's like finding the tightness, but at the same time, being able to make those plays, those arena plays, Major plays, that can win you games.

You've been at quite a few Majors now, but you haven't made it past the quarters yourself. The last four times in a row it was quarters, and in Stockholm it was quarters, so how much of a drive and hope do you have to get further now, just individually?

Of course I would want to get further, but for me, I truly believe in focusing on the present, not thinking about the future, and not thinking about the past. I feel like that's the best psychological mindset that you can have before a game because if your expectations during the game are not met, then you are going to be getting mad. If you're going to be thinking about something like, 'Oh, if we win, we're going to go to the semis, I'm going to break the curse,' or whatever, that is thinking about the future, and then if something goes wrong, it's going to be bad. Some bad round happened before, it's going to be bad.

So we're just staying in the present; we're thinking only about present, and I'm specifically thinking only about the present. I'm focusing on the quarter-final, and if I'm going to lose it, I'm going to lose. If we're going to win, then it's going to be good (smiles).

Your first matchup in the arena will be 9z, and that's a team that FURIA hasn't played since the Americas RMR for the Perfect World Shanghai Major in 2024. You weren't on the team back then, so a lot has changed, including for them. Their form is looking really good, you're on an upward trajectory, so how are you handling that matchup and what has the preparation been like already?

They're a great team, and you can definitely see fire in their eyes and whatever. We also know how much it gives when you beat Vitality, so they're very confident. You can definitely see it by their first game against Spirit in Stage 2 and now their second game against Spirit in Stage 3, so this is a team that we should be careful about.

They're great players, and the interesting part is going to be maps. We are banning Ancient, they are banning Anubis, and their usual picks are Overpass and Nuke, which are very strong maps for us. So it's going to be interesting what they're going to decide on picking against us.

Honestly, this tournament for us was all about momentum, in terms of who was starting the map good that team won the map. So it's going to be important for us to just get a good start, and if we don't get a good start, it's going to be important for us to come back.
