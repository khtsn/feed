GUM on keeping LVG's roster for 1.5 years: "So many players PUG, but nobody wants to go pro"

Lynn Vision haven't made a roster change for a year and three months, and the team are hoping that cohesion will help them at Cologne.

They started with a 8-13 loss to M80, but then bounced back on their home map of Dust2 with a 13-11 win over HEROIC.

The narrow upset — HEROIC were 27th, and Lynn Vision 31st in HLTV rank before the game — is a good sign for a team that impressed at BLAST.tv Austin a year ago but has struggled for form in international events since.

A lack of up-and-comers in the Chinese scene means solutions have to be found internally, with Yifei "⁠GUM⁠" Dai still working on ironing out the rough edges inside the team.

"[Why we haven't made changes is] a question for the whole Asian region, or maybe Chinese-speaking teams," the head coach explained. "Right now, there are not so many choices you can pick. Maybe there are so many players who play PUGs, but nobody wants to go pro.

"It's very hard to go pro in China. Now we have the best players in China and we have to [keep] this roster and try to achieve the best way you can. So I think that's the main reason for it."

Listen to the interview here.

Okay, Gum, congrats. Tight win there. How does it feel to get one over the line?

Relieved. Yeah, that's the word.

Was it coming into today, were you kind of hoping for a particular result? Are you happy getting a win over a team like HEROIC, who are kind of quite established in Europe?

No, actually, I think they are like... HEROIC has actually been on a downfall recently, they lost so many matches in a row. And for best of one today, we had the advantage because of the pick-ban, we can almost see the map will be Dust2, which is our best map. Actually, we think we should beat them easily on Dust2, but we made a mistake from the start, like the Inferno. So today, actually, both maps, we had a slow start. But fortunately, we won this Dust2, so great.

We have the best players in China and we have to [keep] this roster and try to achieve the best way you can.

I saw you speaking with Westmelon after the game. Can you tell us what that was about? Was that about some of the mistakes?

Yeah, I said, who is the stupid one that when you know they had a set up — I mean the pistol round, our first pistol round, you already saw them had set up in Lower Tunnel — 'who is the stupid one who will command your players to actually challenge their setup? Why don't you choose other options?' Oh, I was like talking in a joking way.

And I also wanted to ask, because I think it was in Austin where you guys got Dust2 quite a lot of times in a row, and people kind of kept underestimating your Dust2. Are the team confident on the other maps as well, going into this in case people do start banning it against you?

Yeah, actually, we are pretty comfortable with all the maps. Like today, you can see the Inferno match; No, maybe like the audience cannot see, but I can. Like, we have better preparation. We have the strategy to counter them, but we always stumble over ourselves. So it's like the execution is wrong or the decision is wrong, the mid-round is wrong. So that's what why I say that we had a slow start on both maps. That's it.

This team's been together a year and a half now all together. How do you feel the team has progressed in that time? Is it an advantage to have so much time together now?

Maybe, maybe you can enjoy your vacation. [laughs]. Just kidding. If you played long enough [together], you can like occasionally take two or three days off, then you come back, and you don't need to do a lot of work to get back to the game. So that's the better side that I see.

But I think this team needs the bond like to like every player to play together long enough and to build the bond and to build the relationship, to build their belief in their teammates. So yeah, I think it's an advantage for us when we are in some bad situations.

Is it a conscious decision, like a deliberate move from Lynn Vision to keep the same roster for so long?

Oh, actually, that's a question for the whole Asian region or maybe Chinese-speaking teams. Right now, there are not so many choices you can pick. Maybe there are so many players who play PUGs, but nobody wants to go pro. It's very hard to go pro in China. Now we have the best players in China and we have to [keep] this roster and try to achieve the best way you can. So I think that's the main reason for it.

Maybe people blame us, or they suspect that we have some negotiation or something, but for us, we just try our best and prepare for the event

I also wanted to ask about how the team got here, and winning those three tournaments in a row. But there was the weird thing with the forfeits at PoB and stuff like that. Can you kind of give us your perspective on how you guys kind of got to the major at the end?

On our side, it's very strange, you know, we prepared a lot to play the tournament and to win the tournament. Before Steel Helmet [XSE GangKui Cup] and Yu Qilin PoB [Pinnacle of Battle]. We chose to play only one tournament because after I review all the VRS ranking and the LAN wins, the LAN points. I told my players, we just need to prepare one tournament and try our best to become the champion. And then we can qualify for the major.

You can see the other teams doesn't like think this way. They tried to play both tournaments like TYLOO or Rare Atom. And there's a schedule conflict between these two tournaments. I mean, that's a loophole in the VRS rule book, I think. So they can attend both events and try their best to grind points from both events. And they don't need to try their best or become the champion to actually qualify for the major. So they try it that way. They can grind points from both events. After that, I think we just benefit from the loophole, because we are only trying to prepare for one event.

And as you guys saw, the teams got too tired to play both events. On the midway, like TYLOO, they had already gained enough points to qualify for the major. So, yeah, then we automatically, get their forfeit points and then qualify for the major.

Was it strange for you to kind of qualify in that way?

Yeah. It's very strange. And yeah, maybe people blame us or they suspect that we have some negotiation or something, like scandal or something, but for us, we just try our best and prepare for one event. And the result is we qualify without actually playing. We have played one best of three, then that's it. Yeah. That's quite weird.
